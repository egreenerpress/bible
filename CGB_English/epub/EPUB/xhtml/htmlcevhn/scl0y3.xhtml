Numbers Chapter 2

1 The LORD spoke to Moses and Aaron, saying,

2 Every man among the Israelites shall camp under his own banner, marked by the emblem of his ancestral family. They shall set up their camps at a distance around the tent of meeting.

3 On the eastern side, facing the direction of the sunrise, the divisions of the camp of Judah shall set up their tents, arranged according to their military units. Nahshon, the son of Amminadab, shall be the leader over the people of Judah.

4 His military division, along with those registered in it, numbered seventy-four thousand six hundred.

5 The tribe of Issachar shall camp next to him, and Nethanel son of Zuar shall be the leader of the people of Issachar.

6 His military division, along with those registered in it, numbered fifty-four thousand four hundred.

7 Then comes the tribe of Zebulun, with Eliab, the son of Helon, as the leader of the Zebulunites.

8 His military division, along with those registered in it, numbered fifty-seven thousand four hundred.

9 The total number of those counted in the camp of Judah was 186,400, organized according to their military divisions. They were to set out first.

10 On the southern side, the camp of Reuben shall set up their banner, arranged according to their military divisions. The leader of the Reubenites will be Elizur, the son of Shedeur.

11 His military division, along with those registered in it, totaled forty-six thousand five hundred.

12 The tribe of Simeon will camp next to them, and the leader of the Simeonites will be Shelumiel, the son of Zurishaddai.

13 The total number of men in his division, as recorded in the census, was fifty-nine thousand three hundred.

14 Then comes the tribe of Gad, and the leader of the Gadites will be Eliasaph, the son of Reuel.

15 The total number of men in his division, as recorded in the census, was forty-five thousand six hundred and fifty.

16 The total number of those counted in the camp of Reuben was 151,450, organized according to their military divisions. They were to set out in the second group.

17 Then the tent of meeting shall move forward with the camp of the Levites in the center of the camp. Just as they set up camp, so they shall move forward, each person in their designated position under their respective banners.

18 On the western side, the camp of Ephraim shall set up their banner, arranged according to their military divisions. The leader of the Ephraimites will be Elishama, the son of Ammihud.

19 His military division, along with those who were counted in it, totaled forty thousand five hundred.

20 Next to him will be the tribe of Manasseh, and the leader of the people of Manasseh will be Gamaliel, the son of Pedahzur.

21 The total number of men in his division, as recorded in the census, was thirty-two thousand two hundred.

22 Then comes the tribe of Benjamin, and the leader of the Benjaminites will be Abidan, the son of Gideoni.

23 His military division, along with those registered in it, numbered thirty-five thousand four hundred.

24 The total number of those counted in the camp of Ephraim was 108,100, organized according to their military divisions. They were to set out in the third position when the camps moved forward.

25 The camp of Dan shall position its banner on the northern side, arranged according to their military divisions. Ahiezer, the son of Ammishaddai, shall serve as the leader of the tribe of Dan.

26 His military division, along with those registered in it, numbered sixty-two thousand seven hundred.

27 The tribe of Asher will set up camp next to them, and Pagiel son of Ocran will be the leader of the Asherites.

28 The total number of men in his division, as recorded in the census, was forty-one thousand five hundred.

29 Then comes the tribe of Naphtali, and the leader of the Naphtalites will be Ahira, the son of Enan.

30 The total number of men in his division, as recorded in the census, was fifty-three thousand four hundred.

31 All those counted in the camp of Dan totaled 157,600. They shall march at the rear, following their banners.

32 These are the ones who were counted from the Israelites, according to their ancestral families. The total number of those registered in the camps, organized by their military divisions, was six hundred and three thousand, five hundred and fifty.

33 However, the Levites were not included in the census of the Israelites, just as the LORD had instructed Moses.

34 The Israelites carried out everything that the LORD had commanded Moses. They camped under their respective banners and set out in an orderly manner, each group according to their families and ancestral households.
