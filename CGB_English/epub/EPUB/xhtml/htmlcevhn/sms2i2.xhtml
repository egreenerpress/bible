II Chronicles Chapter 21

1 Jehoshaphat died and was buried with his ancestors in the City of David. His son Jehoram succeeded him as king.

2 He had brothers, the sons of Jehoshaphat: Azariah, Jehiel, Zechariah, Azariah, Michael, and Shephatiah. All of these were the sons of Jehoshaphat, the king of Israel.

3 Their father gave them generous gifts of silver, gold, and valuable possessions, along with fortified cities in Judah. However, he gave the kingdom to Jehoram because he was the firstborn.

4 When Jehoram ascended to the throne of his father, he consolidated his power and executed all his brothers with the sword, as well as several of the leaders of Israel.

5 Jehoram was thirty-two years old when he became king, and he ruled for eight years in Jerusalem.

6 He followed the ways of the kings of Israel, just as the house of Ahab had done, because he had married Ahab’s daughter. He committed what was evil in the sight of the LORD.

7 Nevertheless, the LORD chose not to destroy the house of David, because of the covenant He had established with David and the promise He had made to provide a lasting light for him and his descendants forever.

8 During his reign, the Edomites rebelled against the rule of Judah and established their own king.

9 Then Jehoram set out with his officials and all his chariots. He rose up during the night and attacked the Edomites, who had surrounded him and the commanders of his chariots.

10 At that time, the Edomites rebelled against Judah's rule, and they have remained independent ever since. During the same period, Libnah also revolted against his authority because he had abandoned the LORD, the God of his ancestors.

11 Furthermore, he built shrines on the hills of Judah and led the people of Jerusalem into idolatry, enticing even the people of Judah to participate in these sinful practices.

12 A message came to him from Elijah the prophet, saying, "This is what the LORD, the God of your ancestor David, declares: Because you have not followed the ways of your father Jehoshaphat or the ways of Asa, king of Judah,

13 But you have followed the ways of the kings of Israel and have caused Judah and the people of Jerusalem to engage in idolatry, just as the house of Ahab led Israel into unfaithfulness. Moreover, you have killed your own brothers, members of your father’s family, who were better men than you.

14 Listen carefully: The LORD will strike your people, your children, your wives, and all your possessions with a severe and devastating plague.

15 You will suffer from a severe and debilitating illness affecting your intestines, causing them to waste away and eventually protrude from your body as the sickness progresses day by day.

16 Furthermore, the LORD incited the Philistines and the Arabians who lived near the Cushites to rise up against Jehoram.

17 They invaded Judah, broke through its defenses, and took away all the wealth found in the king’s palace. They also captured his sons and his wives, leaving him with no son except Jehoahaz, his youngest.

18 After all these events, the LORD struck him with a severe and incurable illness in his intestines.

19 As time went on, after two years had passed, his intestines came out because of his illness, and he died from severe diseases. His people did not honor him with a funeral fire, as they had done for his ancestors.

20 He was thirty-two years old when he became king, and he ruled in Jerusalem for eight years. He passed away without being mourned or honored. Although he was buried in the City of David, he was not laid to rest in the tombs of the kings.
