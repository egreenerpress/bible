Numbers Chapter 28

1 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2 Instruct the people of Israel and say to them: You must carefully present to me my offerings—the food for my fire sacrifices, which are a pleasing aroma to me—at their appointed times.

3 You shall instruct them, saying, "This is the offering to be presented by fire, which you are to bring to the LORD: two unblemished year-old lambs each day, as a regular burnt offering."

4 You shall offer one lamb in the morning and the other lamb in the evening.

5 For the grain offering, use one-tenth of an ephah of fine flour, mixed with a quarter of a hin of pressed olive oil.

6 This is a perpetual burnt offering, which was established at Mount Sinai as a pleasing aroma, a fire-offering presented to the LORD.

7 The drink offering for each lamb shall be a quarter of a hin. In the sacred place, you are to pour out the strong wine as a drink offering to the LORD.

8 You shall offer the second lamb in the evening, along with the same grain offering and drink offering as you presented in the morning. It is to be a burnt offering, an offering made by fire, producing a pleasing aroma to the LORD.

9 On the Sabbath day, you shall offer two unblemished one-year-old lambs, along with two-tenths of an ephah of fine flour mixed with oil as a grain offering, and the accompanying drink offering.

10 This is the burnt offering to be presented every Sabbath, in addition to the regular daily burnt offering and its accompanying drink offering.

11 At the start of each month, you are to present a burnt offering to the LORD: two young bulls, one ram, and seven male lambs in their first year, without any defect.

12 For each bull, prepare a grain offering of three-tenths of an ephah of fine flour mixed with oil; and for each ram, prepare a grain offering of two-tenths of an ephah of fine flour mixed with oil.

13 For each lamb, you shall prepare a grain offering consisting of one-tenth of an ephah of fine flour mixed with oil. This is to accompany the burnt offering, a pleasing aroma to the LORD, a sacrifice made by fire.

14 The drink offerings accompanying the sacrifices shall consist of half a hin of wine for a bull, a third of a hin for a ram, and a quarter of a hin for a lamb. This is the regulation for the burnt offering to be presented each month throughout the year.

15 Additionally, one male goat shall be presented as a sin offering to the LORD, in addition to the regular burnt offering and its accompanying drink offering.

16 On the fourteenth day of the first month, the Passover of the LORD is to be observed.

17 On the fifteenth day of this month, the festival begins. For seven days, you must eat bread made without yeast.

18 On the first day, there shall be a sacred assembly; you must not engage in any ordinary labor on that day.

19 You shall present a burnt offering to the LORD, a sacrifice made by fire: two young bulls, one ram, and seven one-year-old lambs. These animals must be without any defect.

20 Their grain offering shall consist of fine flour mixed with oil: you shall offer three-tenths of an ephah for a bull, and two-tenths of an ephah for a ram.

21 You shall offer a tenth of an ephah of grain for each lamb, for all seven lambs.

22 And one goat shall be offered as a sin offering to provide atonement for you.

23 You shall present these offerings in addition to the morning burnt offering, which is a regular and ongoing offering.

24 In this way, you shall present the food offering each day for seven days, as a sacrifice made by fire, a pleasing aroma to the LORD. It shall be offered in addition to the regular burnt offering and its accompanying drink offering.

25 On the seventh day, you are to hold a sacred assembly; you must not do any ordinary work.

26 Also, on the day of the firstfruits, when you present a new grain offering to the LORD during your Festival of Weeks, you are to hold a sacred assembly; you must not do any ordinary work.

27 You shall present the burnt offering as a pleasing aroma to the LORD: two young bulls, one ram, and seven one-year-old lambs.

28 For their grain offering, mix fine flour with oil: three-tenths of an ephah for each bull, and two-tenths of an ephah for each ram.

29 A separate tenth of an ephah of flour shall be offered for each lamb, for all seven lambs.

30 And one male goat, to serve as a sin offering to make atonement for you.

31 You shall present these offerings in addition to the regular burnt offering, along with its grain offering (they must be without defect) and their drink offerings.
