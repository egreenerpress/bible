Ephesians Chapter 4

1 Therefore, I, who am a prisoner because of my service to the Lord, urge you to live in a manner that reflects the dignity of the calling you have received.

2 With complete humility and gentleness, with patience, bearing with one another in love;

3 Striving to maintain the unity brought by the Spirit through the bond of peace.

4 There is one unified body and one Spirit, just as you were called to share in one common hope through your calling.

5 There is one Lord, one true faith, and one act of baptism.

6 There is one God and Father of all, who is supreme over all, works through all, and dwells within all of you.

7 But to each one of us, grace has been given in proportion to the measure of Christ’s gift.

8 Therefore, it is said: "When he ascended to the heights, he took captives as prisoners and gave gifts to people."

9 Now that he has ascended, what does it mean except that he also first descended into the lower regions of the earth?

10 The one who descended is the same one who ascended far above all the heavens, so that he might fulfill all things.

11 He appointed some to be apostles, some to be prophets, some to be evangelists, and some to be pastors and teachers.

12 For the equipping of God’s holy people, for the work of serving in ministry, and for building up the body of Christ.

13 Until we all reach unity in the faith and in the knowledge of the Son of God, becoming mature, attaining to the full measure of the stature of Christ’s completeness.

14 So that we may no longer be like immature children, easily swayed and blown off course by every new teaching that comes along, influenced by the deceptive schemes and clever tricks of those who deliberately mislead others.

15 Instead, by speaking the truth with love, we are to grow in every way into him who is the head—that is, Christ.

16 From Him, the entire body is joined and held together by every supporting ligament, with each part working effectively according to its proper function. This causes the body to grow and build itself up in love.

17 Therefore, I declare and solemnly affirm in the Lord that you must no longer live as the Gentiles do, in the emptiness of their thoughts and reasoning.

18 Their minds have become clouded, and they are cut off from the life of God because of the ignorance within them, which stems from the hardness of their hearts.

19 Having become callous and insensitive, they have abandoned themselves to unrestrained indulgence, engaging in every kind of impurity with an insatiable desire.

20 But this is not how you came to know Christ.

21 If indeed you have heard him and have been taught in him, as the truth is found in Jesus:

22 You must set aside your former way of life, the old self that is corrupted by deceitful desires.

23 And be transformed by the renewing of your inner thoughts and attitudes.

24 And that you clothe yourselves with the new self, which is created according to God’s design in true righteousness and holiness.

25 Therefore, rid yourselves of falsehood and let each one speak truthfully to their neighbor, for we are all interconnected as members of one body.

26 Be angry, but do not sin; do not let the sun set while you are still angry.

27 Do not allow any opportunity for the devil to gain a foothold.

28 Whoever has been stealing must stop stealing. Instead, they should work hard, doing good with their own hands, so that they may have something to share with those in need.

29 Do not let any harmful or worthless words come from your mouth, but only what is good and beneficial for building others up according to their needs, so that your words may bring grace and encouragement to those who hear them.

30 Do not cause sorrow to the Holy Spirit of God, by whom you were marked as His own for the day of redemption.

31 Remove from yourselves all bitterness, rage, anger, shouting, slander, and every form of malice.

32 Be kind and compassionate to one another, forgiving each other just as God, through Christ, has forgiven you.
