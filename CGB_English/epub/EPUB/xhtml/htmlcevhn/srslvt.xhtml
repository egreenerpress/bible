Job Chapter 12

1 And Job responded, saying,

2 Certainly, you must be the ultimate authorities, and wisdom will perish when you are gone.

3 But I possess understanding just as you do; I am not less than you. Indeed, who does not know such things as these?

4 I am like someone mocked by his neighbor, who calls out to God, and He answers him. The righteous and upright man is ridiculed and scorned.

5 A person who is on the verge of stumbling and falling is like a flickering lamp, disregarded in the thoughts of those who live in comfort and security.

6 The dwellings of the wicked thrive, and those who defy God live in safetyâ€”those to whom God grants abundant prosperity.

7 But now, inquire of the animals, and they will instruct you; ask the birds of the sky, and they will inform you.

8 Speak to the earth, and it will instruct you; even the fish of the sea will explain it to you.

9 Who among all these does not recognize that the hand of the LORD has accomplished this?

10 In whose hand is the life of every living creature and the breath of all humanity.

11 Does not the ear test words, just as the mouth tastes its food?

12 Wisdom is found with the aged, and understanding comes with the length of life.

13 With Him reside wisdom and power; He possesses insight and understanding.

14 Look, when He tears something down, it cannot be rebuilt; when He confines someone, there is no way to release them.

15 Observe how God holds back the waters, and they dry up; yet when He releases them, they flood the land and transform the earth.

16 With Him resides both power and understanding; both the one who is led astray and the one who leads astray are under His authority.

17 He causes advisors to be led away as captives, stripped of their wisdom, and turns judges into fools.

18 He releases the authority of kings and binds their waists with a sash.

19 He causes rulers to be taken captive as plunder, and He brings down the powerful.

20 He strips away the words of the trustworthy and deprives the elders of their wisdom.

21 He brings disgrace upon rulers and diminishes the power of the strong.

22 He reveals profound mysteries hidden in darkness and brings to light the deepest shadows of death.

23 He causes nations to grow and prosper, and then he brings them to ruin; he expands their territories, only to reduce them once more.

24 He removes the understanding of the leaders of the earth's people and leads them astray in a trackless wasteland.

25 They stumble in the darkness, unable to see, and He causes them to wander aimlessly, like someone intoxicated.
