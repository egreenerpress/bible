II Chronicles Chapter 8

1 After twenty years had passed, during which Solomon had completed the construction of the house of the LORD and his own royal palace,

2 The cities that Huram had returned to Solomon, Solomon rebuilt them and settled the people of Israel in them.

3 Solomon advanced to Hamath-zobah and successfully conquered it.

4 He constructed Tadmor in the wilderness and all the storage cities that he built in the region of Hamath.

5 He also constructed Upper Beth-horon and Lower Beth-horon as fortified cities, complete with walls, gates, and bars for security.

6 And Baalath, along with all the storage cities that Solomon had built, and the cities for his chariots, and the cities for his cavalry, and everything Solomon desired to construct in Jerusalem, in Lebanon, and throughout the entire territory under his rule.

7 Concerning all the people who remained from the Hittites, the Amorites, the Perizzites, the Hivites, and the Jebusites—those who were not part of Israel—

8 However, concerning the descendants of the peoples who remained in the land after them—those whom the Israelites had not completely driven out—Solomon subjected them to forced labor, and they continued to pay tribute up to the present day.

9 However, Solomon did not conscript any of the Israelites as forced laborers for his projects. Instead, they served as soldiers, commanders of his officers, and leaders of his chariot and cavalry forces.

10 These were the leading officials of King Solomon, numbering two hundred and fifty, who exercised authority over the people.

11 Solomon moved Pharaoh’s daughter from the City of David to the house he had built for her, for he said, “My wife must not live in the house of David, king of Israel, because the places where the ark of the LORD has come are holy.”

12 Then Solomon presented burnt offerings to the LORD on the altar of the LORD, which he had constructed in front of the temple porch.

13 They were to provide a daily offering according to the command of Moses, on the Sabbaths, the new moons, and the appointed festivals—three times each year: during the Feast of Unleavened Bread, the Feast of Weeks, and the Feast of Tabernacles.

14 He established the divisions of the priests for their duties, following the arrangement set by his father David. He also assigned the Levites to their responsibilities—to lead in praise and to assist the priests in their daily tasks as required. Additionally, he organized the gatekeepers by their divisions at each gate, in accordance with the command of David, the man of God.

15 They did not deviate from the king’s command regarding the priests and Levites, whether it concerned any specific matter or the handling of the treasures.

16 All of Solomon's work was carefully prepared from the day the foundation of the house of the LORD was laid until its completion. Thus, the house of the LORD was brought to its full and perfect state.

17 Then Solomon traveled to Ezion-geber and to Eloth, which are located by the seashore in the land of Edom.

18 Huram sent ships and skilled sailors to Solomon by means of his own servants. These men, experienced in seafaring, accompanied Solomon’s servants to Ophir. There, they acquired four hundred and fifty talents of gold and brought it back to King Solomon.
