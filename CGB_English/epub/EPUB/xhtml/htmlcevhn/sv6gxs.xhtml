Mark Chapter 6

1 He left that place and went to his hometown, accompanied by his disciples.

2 When the Sabbath day arrived, He began to teach in the synagogue. Many who heard Him were amazed and asked, "Where does this man get such knowledge? What kind of wisdom has been given to Him? How is it that such powerful deeds are performed by His hands?"

3 Isn’t this the carpenter, the son of Mary, and the brother of James, Joseph, Judas, and Simon? And aren’t his sisters here with us?” And they took offense at him.

4 But Jesus said to them, "A prophet is not without honor, except in his own hometown, among his relatives, and in his own household."

5 And he was unable to perform any significant miracles there, except that he placed his hands on a few sick people and healed them.

6 And he was astonished at their lack of faith. Then he traveled around the nearby villages, continuing to teach.

7 Then Jesus summoned the twelve disciples and began to send them out in pairs, granting them authority to drive out impure spirits.

8 He instructed them to take nothing for their journey except a staff—no bag, no bread, no money in their belts.

9 Wear sandals on your feet, but do not take an extra coat with you.

10 He said to them, "Wherever you enter a house, stay there until you leave that place."

11 If anyone does not welcome you or listen to your message, as you leave that place, shake the dust off your feet as a testimony against them. Truly I tell you, on the day of judgment, it will be more bearable for Sodom and Gomorrah than for that town.

12 They went out and proclaimed that people should turn away from their sins and change their ways.

13 They drove out many demons and anointed many who were sick with oil, bringing them healing.

14 King Herod heard about Jesus, for His name had become widely known. Herod said, "This must be John the Baptist raised from the dead, and that is why such miraculous powers are at work in Him."

15 Some people said, "He is Elijah." Others said, "He is a prophet, or like one of the prophets."

16 When Herod heard about this, he said, "This is John, the one I beheaded. He has been raised from the dead."

17 For Herod himself had sent his men to arrest John and had him bound and imprisoned because of Herodias, the wife of his brother Philip, whom Herod had married.

18 For John had said to Herod, "It is not lawful for you to have your brother's wife."

19 Therefore, Herodias held a grudge against him and wanted to have him killed, but she was unable to do so.

20 For Herod feared John, knowing that he was a righteous and holy man, and he protected him. When Herod listened to John, he was deeply perplexed, yet he heard him gladly and often acted upon his words.

21 When an opportune day arrived, Herod held a banquet on his birthday for his nobles, military commanders, and the leading officials of Galilee.

22 When the daughter of Herodias entered and performed a dance, she delighted Herod and those who were dining with him. The king then said to the young woman, "Ask me for whatever you desire, and I will give it to you."

23 And he swore to her, "Whatever you ask of me, I will give it to you, even up to half of my kingdom."

24 She went out and asked her mother, "What should I request?" Her mother replied, "The head of John the Baptist."

25 She immediately went with haste to the king and made her request, saying, "I want you to give me right now, on a platter, the head of John the Baptist."

26 The king was deeply distressed, but because of his oath and out of respect for those who were dining with him, he did not want to refuse her request.

27 At once, the king sent a guard and ordered him to bring John’s head. The guard went to the prison, beheaded John, and brought his head to the king.

28 They placed his head on a platter and presented it to the young woman, who then gave it to her mother.

29 When his disciples heard about what had happened, they came and took his body, placing it in a tomb.

30 The apostles came together to Jesus and reported to him everything they had done and taught.

31 He said to them, "Come away by yourselves to a secluded place and rest for a while." For there were so many people coming and going that they did not even have time to eat.

32 They left by boat to go to a secluded, deserted place by themselves.

33 The people saw them leaving, and many recognized Jesus. They ran on foot from all the surrounding towns and arrived ahead of them. They gathered together to meet him.

34 When Jesus came out and saw the large crowd, he was deeply moved with compassion for them, because they were like sheep without a shepherd. So he began to teach them many things.

35 As the day was drawing to a close, his disciples approached him and said, "This is a remote place, and the hour is already late."

36 Send them away so they can go into the surrounding countryside and nearby villages to buy food for themselves, because they have nothing to eat.

37 He responded to them, saying, "You give them something to eat." They replied to him, "Should we go and spend two hundred denarii on bread to give them something to eat?"

38 He asked them, "How many loaves do you have? Go and check." After they had counted, they replied, "Five loaves, and two fish."

39 Then he instructed them to have all the people sit down in groups on the green grass.

40 They arranged themselves in groups, sitting in orderly rows, some in groups of a hundred and others in groups of fifty.

41 After taking the five loaves and the two fish, he looked up to heaven, gave thanks, and broke the loaves. He then handed them to his disciples to distribute to the people. He also divided the two fish among them all.

42 And they all ate and were satisfied.

43 They gathered up twelve baskets full of the leftover pieces of bread and fish.

44 The number of those who ate the bread was approximately five thousand men.

45 Immediately, Jesus urged his disciples to get into the boat and go ahead of him to the other side, toward Bethsaida, while he dismissed the crowd.

46 After dismissing them, he went up into the hills to pray.

47 When evening arrived, the boat was in the middle of the sea, and he was alone on the shore.

48 He saw them struggling as they rowed, for the wind was against them. Around the fourth watch of the night, he came to them, walking on the sea, and intended to pass by them.

49 But when they saw him walking on the water, they thought it was a ghost and cried out in fear.

50 For they all saw him and were filled with alarm. But at once he spoke to them and said, "Take courage! It is I. Do not be afraid."

51 He climbed into the boat with them, and the wind stopped blowing. They were utterly astonished and overwhelmed with amazement, marveling at what had happened.

52 They did not understand the significance of the miracle of the loaves, because their hearts were hardened.

53 After they had crossed over, they arrived in the region of Gennesaret and approached the shore.

54 When they got out of the boat, they immediately recognized Him.

55 They went throughout the entire surrounding region and started bringing the sick on mats to wherever they heard he was.

56 Wherever he went—whether into villages, towns, or the countryside—people would bring the sick out into the open spaces and beg him to let them touch even the edge of his clothing. And all who touched him were healed.
