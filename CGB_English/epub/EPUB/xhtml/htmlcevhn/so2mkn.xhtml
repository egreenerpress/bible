Acts Chapter 10

1 In the city of Caesarea, there was a man named Cornelius, who was a centurion in the military unit known as the Italian Regiment.

2 A man of deep faith, who revered God along with his entire household, generously gave to those in need, and continually sought God in prayer.

3 He clearly saw in a vision, around the ninth hour of the day, an angel of God coming to him and saying, “Cornelius.”

4 When he saw him, he was filled with fear and said, “What is it, Lord?” The angel replied, “Your prayers and your acts of charity have ascended as a remembrance before God.”

5 Now, send men to Joppa and summon a man named Simon, who is also called Peter.

6 He is staying with a man named Simon, who is a tanner, and his house is located by the seashore. He will tell you what you need to do.

7 After the angel who had spoken to Cornelius left, he summoned two of his household servants and a devout soldier who was one of his regular attendants.

8 After he had explained all these matters to them, he sent them to Joppa.

9 The next day, as they continued their journey and approached the city, Peter went up to the rooftop to pray at about noon.

10 He became extremely hungry and wanted to eat, but while they were preparing the food, he fell into a visionary state.

11 And he saw heaven opened, and a certain object descending toward him, like a large sheet bound at its four corners, being lowered to the ground.

12 In it were all kinds of four-legged animals of the earth, wild animals, crawling creatures, and birds of the sky.

13 Then a voice spoke to him, saying, "Get up, Peter; slaughter and eat."

14 But Peter replied, "Certainly not, Lord! I have never eaten anything that is considered impure or unclean."

15 And the voice spoke to him a second time, saying, "What God has declared clean, you must not consider unclean."

16 This happened three times, and then the vessel was taken back up into heaven.

17 While Peter was deeply perplexed, wondering what the vision he had seen might mean, suddenly the men sent by Cornelius arrived. They had asked for directions to Simon’s house and were now standing at the gate.

18 They called out and inquired whether Simon, who was also known as Peter, was staying there.

19 As Peter was reflecting on the vision, the Spirit said to him, "Look, three men are searching for you."

20 "Therefore, rise up, go down, and accompany them without hesitation, for I have sent them."

21 Then Peter went down to meet the men who had been sent by Cornelius and said, "I am the one you are looking for. What is the reason for your coming?"

22 They said, "Cornelius, a centurion, a righteous man who reveres God and is well-regarded by the entire Jewish community, received a divine warning through a holy angel to summon you to his home and to listen to what you have to say."

23 Then he invited them in and provided them lodging. The next day, Peter set out with them, and some fellow believers from Joppa went along as well.

24 The next day, they arrived in Caesarea. Cornelius was expecting them and had gathered his relatives and close friends to be there with him.

25 As Peter entered, Cornelius went to meet him, fell at his feet, and showed him reverence.

26 But Peter raised him up, saying, "Stand up; I am only a man like you."

27 As he spoke with him, he entered and found a large group of people gathered together.

28 And he said to them, "You are aware that it is against our customs for a Jewish man to associate with or visit someone from another nation. But God has shown me that I should not consider any person impure or unclean."

29 Therefore, I came to you without hesitation as soon as I was summoned. Now I ask, for what purpose have you called for me?

30 Cornelius replied, "Four days ago, at this very hour, I was fasting. Then, at three in the afternoon, as I was praying in my house, suddenly a man appeared before me, dressed in radiant clothing."

31 The angel said, "Cornelius, your prayers have been heard, and your acts of charity have been remembered before God."

32 Therefore, send to Joppa and summon Simon, who is also called Peter. He is staying at the house of Simon, a tanner, by the seaside. When he arrives, he will speak to you.

33 Therefore, I sent for you right away, and you have done well to come. Now, we are all here in God’s presence, ready to listen to everything the Lord has instructed you to tell us.

34 Then Peter began to speak and said, "Truly, I now understand that God does not show favoritism toward anyone."

35 However, in every nation, anyone who reveres Him and does what is right is acceptable to Him.

36 The message that God sent to the people of Israel, proclaiming peace through Jesus Christ—who is Lord over all.

37 You are aware of that message which was proclaimed throughout all Judea, beginning in Galilee after the baptism that John preached.

38 God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Spirit and with power. He went about doing good and healing all who were oppressed by the devil, for God was with him.

39 We are witnesses to everything He did, both in the land of the Jews and in Jerusalem. They killed Him by hanging Him on a tree.

40 God raised Him up on the third day and made Him visibly known.

41 This message was not revealed to everyone, but to those whom God had previously chosen as witnesses—specifically to us, who shared meals with Him after He rose from the dead.

42 He instructed us to proclaim the message to the people and to bear witness that he is the one appointed by God to be the judge of both the living and the dead.

43 All the prophets testify about him, that through his name, everyone who believes in him will receive the forgiveness of sins.

44 While Peter was still speaking these words, the Holy Spirit came upon all those who were listening to his message.

45 The Jewish believers who had come with Peter were amazed, for the gift of the Holy Spirit had also been poured out on the Gentiles.

46 For they heard them speaking in other languages and praising God. Then Peter responded,

47 Is there any reason to prevent these people from being baptized with water, since they have received the Holy Spirit just as we have?

48 He instructed them to be baptized in the name of the Lord. Afterward, they urged him to stay with them for a few days.
