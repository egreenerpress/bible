Jeremiah Chapter 43

1 When Jeremiah had finished delivering to all the people every word that the LORD their God had commanded him to speak—all these messages—

2 Then Azariah, the son of Hoshaiah, and Johanan, the son of Kareah, along with all the arrogant men, said to Jeremiah, "You are lying! The LORD our God did not send you to tell us, 'Do not go to Egypt to settle there.'"

3 But Baruch, the son of Neriah, is inciting you against us, intending to hand us over to the Chaldeans, so that they may kill us or take us captive to Babylon.

4 So Johanan, the son of Kareah, along with all the military commanders and all the people, refused to obey the voice of the LORD by choosing not to remain in the land of Judah.

5 However, Johanan, the son of Kareah, along with all the military commanders, gathered all the remaining people of Judah who had returned from the various nations where they had been scattered, in order to settle them back in the land of Judah.

6 Men, women, children, the daughters of the king, and every individual whom Nebuzaradan, the captain of the guard, had left in the care of Gedaliah, the son of Ahikam and grandson of Shaphan, along with the prophet Jeremiah and Baruch, the son of Neriah.

7 So they entered the land of Egypt, for they did not heed the voice of the LORD. In this way, they arrived as far as Tahpanhes.

8 Then the word of the LORD came to Jeremiah in Tahpanhes, saying:

9 Take large stones in your hand and bury them in the clay of the brick pavement at the entrance to Pharaoh’s palace in Tahpanhes. Do this in the presence of the people of Judah.

10 Go and tell them this message from the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel: Listen carefully, I am about to summon Nebuchadnezzar, the king of Babylon, my chosen instrument, and I will place his throne upon these stones that I have concealed. He will set up his royal canopy over them.

11 When he arrives, he will strike the land of Egypt, bringing death to those destined for death, captivity to those destined for captivity, and the sword to those destined for the sword.

12 I will set fire to the temples of Egypt’s gods, and it will consume them. The invaders will take the idols as plunder and carry them away. They will strip the land of Egypt bare, as easily as a shepherd wraps himself in his cloak. Then they will depart from there in safety.

13 He will also destroy the idols of Beth-shemesh, which are in the land of Egypt, and he will set fire to the temples of the Egyptian gods, burning them to the ground.
