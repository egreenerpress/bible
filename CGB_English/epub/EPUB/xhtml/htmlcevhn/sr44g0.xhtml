II Timothy Chapter 4

1 I solemnly urge you, in the presence of God and the Lord Jesus Christ—who will judge the living and the dead at his appearing and the establishment of his kingdom—

2 Proclaim the message; be ready at all times, whether convenient or not. Correct, rebuke, and encourage with complete patience and careful instruction.

3 A time will come when people will no longer tolerate sound and wholesome teaching. Instead, driven by their own desires, they will gather around themselves teachers who tell them what they want to hear, catering to their itching ears.

4 They will turn their ears away from the truth and instead turn aside to myths.

5 Be vigilant in all circumstances, endure hardships, carry out the work of spreading the gospel, and fully fulfill the responsibilities of your ministry.

6 For I am now prepared to be poured out as an offering, and the time of my departure is near.

7 I have fought the good fight, I have completed the race, I have remained faithful to what I believed.

8 From now on, there is reserved for me the crown of righteousness, which the Lord, the righteous judge, will award to me on that day—and not only to me, but also to all who have loved and eagerly await his appearing.

9 Make every effort to come to me soon.

10 Demas has abandoned me because he was more devoted to the attractions of this present world, and he has gone to Thessalonica. Crescens has left for Galatia, and Titus has gone to Dalmatia.

11 Only Luke remains with me. Bring Mark along with you, for he is valuable to me in the work of ministry.

12 I have sent Tychicus to Ephesus.

13 When you come, bring the cloak I left with Carpus in Troas, along with the books, and especially the parchments.

14 Alexander, the metalworker, has caused me great harm. May the Lord repay him according to his deeds.

15 Be on your guard against him as well, for he has strongly opposed our message.

16 When I first had to defend myself, no one stood by my side; everyone abandoned me. I pray to God that this will not be held against them.

17 Nevertheless, the Lord stood by me and gave me strength, so that through me the message might be fully proclaimed and all the Gentiles might hear it. And I was rescued from the jaws of danger.

18 The Lord will rescue me from every harmful deed and will keep me safe for his heavenly kingdom. To him be glory forever and ever. Amen.

19 Greet Prisca and Aquila, as well as the family of Onesiphorus.

20 Erastus stayed in Corinth, but I left Trophimus in Miletus because he was ill.

21 Make every effort to come before winter arrives. Eubulus sends you greetings, as do Pudens, Linus, Claudia, and all the brothers and sisters.

22 May the Lord Jesus Christ be present with your spirit. May His grace be with you all. Amen.
