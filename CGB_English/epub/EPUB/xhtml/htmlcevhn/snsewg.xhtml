Song of Songs Chapter 8

1 Oh, if only you were like my brother, who nursed at my mother’s breast! Then, if I found you outside, I could kiss you, and no one would scorn me for it.

2 I would guide you and bring you to my mother’s house, where she would teach me. I would give you spiced wine to drink, made from the juice of my pomegranates.

3 Let his left hand support my head, and let his right hand hold me close.

4 I urge you, daughters of Jerusalem, do not disturb or awaken my beloved until he is ready.

5 Who is this coming up from the wilderness, leaning on her beloved? I awakened you under the apple tree; there your mother went into labor with you, there she who gave birth to you brought you forth.

6 Place me like a seal upon your heart, like a seal upon your arm; for love is as powerful as death, and passion as unyielding as the grave. Its flames are flames of fire, a blazing intensity.

7 No amount of water can extinguish love, nor can floods overwhelm it. Even if someone were to offer all the wealth of their household in exchange for love, it would be utterly scorned.

8 We have a young sister, and her breasts have not yet developed. What shall we do for our sister when the time comes for her to be sought in marriage?

9 If she is like a strong wall, we will build upon her a fortress of silver; but if she is like a door, we will enclose her with panels of cedar.

10 I am like a strong wall, and my breasts are like towers; thus, in his eyes, I became as one who has found favor.

11 Solomon owned a vineyard in Baal-hamon; he entrusted it to caretakers, each of whom was required to bring in a thousand pieces of silver as payment for its produce.

12 My vineyard, which belongs to me, is here before me. As for you, Solomon, you may have a thousand, and those who tend its fruit two hundred.

13 You who reside in the gardens, your companions listen attentively to your voice—let me hear it as well.

14 Hurry, my beloved, and be swift like a gazelle or a young stag on the fragrant mountains.
