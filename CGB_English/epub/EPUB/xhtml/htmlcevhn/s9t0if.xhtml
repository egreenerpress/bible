I Kings Chapter 10

1 When the queen of Sheba heard about Solomon’s reputation, particularly in connection with the name of the LORD, she came to test him with challenging questions.

2 She arrived in Jerusalem with an exceptionally large entourage, accompanied by camels carrying spices, a vast amount of gold, and precious stones. When she met Solomon, she spoke with him about everything that was on her mind.

3 And Solomon answered all her questions; there was nothing so difficult or hidden from the king that he could not explain it to her.

4 When the queen of Sheba witnessed all of Solomon's wisdom and the palace he had constructed,

5 The provisions of his table, the seating of his officials, the service of his attendants and their attire, his cupbearers, and the way he went up to the house of the LORD—all of this left her utterly overwhelmed.

6 The woman said to the king, "The report I heard in my own country about your achievements and your wisdom is true."

7 However, I did not believe the reports until I came and saw it with my own eyes. And now, I realize that not even half of it was told to me. Your wisdom and wealth far surpass the stories I had heard.

8 Blessed are your people, blessed are these servants of yours who continually serve in your presence and listen to your wisdom.

9 Praise be to the LORD your God, who has shown His favor by placing you on the throne of Israel. Because the LORD loves Israel eternally, He has appointed you as king to uphold justice and righteousness.

10 She presented the king with 120 talents of gold, an immense quantity of spices, and precious gemstones. Never again was such an abundance of spices brought as those which the queen of Sheba gave to King Solomon.

11 Additionally, the fleet of Hiram, which transported gold from Ophir, also brought from Ophir a large quantity of almug wood and precious stones.

12 The king used the almug wood to make pillars for the house of the LORD and for his own palace, as well as harps and lyres for the musicians. No such almug trees had ever been brought or seen in the land up to that time.

13 King Solomon granted the queen of Sheba everything she desired, whatever she requested, in addition to what he generously gave her from his royal treasures. Then she and her attendants departed and returned to her own land.

14 The amount of gold that came to Solomon in a single year weighed six hundred and sixty-six talents of gold.

15 In addition to what he received from the merchants and the trade of the spice dealers, as well as from all the kings of Arabia and the regional governors.

16 King Solomon crafted two hundred large shields made of hammered gold, with six hundred shekels of gold used for each shield.

17 He crafted three hundred shields made of hammered gold, with each shield requiring three pounds of gold. The king then placed these shields in the House of the Forest of Lebanon.

18 Furthermore, the king constructed a magnificent throne made of ivory and covered it with the finest gold.

19 The throne had six steps leading up to it, and the back of the throne was rounded at the top. On either side of the seat, there were armrests, and beside each armrest stood a lion.

20 Twelve lions stood there, six on each side of the steps. Nothing like this had ever been made in any other kingdom.

21 All of King Solomon's drinking cups were made of gold, and all the utensils in the House of the Forest of Lebanon were crafted from pure gold. Silver was not used for any of these items, as it was considered of little value during Solomon's reign.

22 The king maintained a fleet of Tarshish ships, operating alongside Hiram's fleet. Every three years, the Tarshish fleet would arrive, carrying cargoes of gold, silver, ivory, apes, and peacocks.

23 King Solomon surpassed all the kings of the earth in both wealth and wisdom.

24 And people from all over the world came to Solomon to listen to the wisdom that God had placed in his heart.

25 Each man brought his tribute: items of silver and gold, clothing, weapons, spices, horses, and mules—a fixed amount every year.

26 Solomon assembled a large force of chariots and horsemen. He acquired 1,400 chariots and 12,000 horsemen, which he stationed in the chariot cities and with the king in Jerusalem.

27 The king made silver as common in Jerusalem as stones, and cedar wood as plentiful as the sycamore trees in the lowlands, because of their great abundance.

28 Solomon acquired horses from Egypt, as well as fine linen. The king’s traders purchased the linen at a set price.

29 A chariot was imported from Egypt for six hundred shekels of silver, and a horse for one hundred and fifty shekels. In the same way, they supplied chariots and horses to all the kings of the Hittites and the kings of Aram through their trading networks.
