Proverbs Chapter 27

1 Do not boast about tomorrow, for you do not know what a single day may bring.

2 Let others commend you, not your own words; let praise come from someone else, not from your own lips.

3 A stone is heavy, and sand is burdensome, but the anger of a fool is more crushing than both.

4 Fierce is wrath, and overwhelming is anger—but who can withstand the destructive force of jealousy?

5 Honest criticism, even if it is openly expressed, is more valuable than love that remains hidden and unspoken.

6 The honest criticism of a true friend is valuable, even if it hurts, but the flattery of an enemy is full of deception.

7 A person who is full despises even honey, but to someone who is hungry, even something bitter tastes sweet.

8 Just as a bird that strays from its nest, so is a person who strays from their rightful place.

9 Fragrant oils and perfumes bring joy to the heart, and in the same way, the heartfelt advice of a friend is deeply pleasing and uplifting.

10 Do not abandon your own friend or your father’s friend, and do not turn to your brother’s house in the day of your trouble. A neighbor who is close by is better than a brother who is far away.

11 My child, act wisely and bring joy to my heart, so that I may have a response for anyone who insults me.

12 A wise person perceives danger ahead and takes cover, but the naive continue forward and suffer the consequences.

13 Seize the garment of one who guarantees a loan for a stranger, and hold it as collateral when he pledges security for an unknown woman.

14 Whoever loudly praises a friend early in the morning, it will be regarded as a curse rather than a blessing.

15 A constant dripping on a rainy day and a quarrelsome woman are much the same.

16 Whoever conceals her conceals the wind, and the perfume of his right hand betrays itself.

17 Just as iron sharpens iron, so one person sharpens the character and understanding of another through their interaction.

18 Whoever tends to the fig tree will enjoy its fruit; in the same way, the one who serves their master faithfully will be honored.

19 Just as water reflects a person’s face, so the heart of one person reflects the heart of another.

20 The grave and the realm of the dead are never filled; in the same way, human desires are never satisfied.

21 Just as a refining pot is used for silver and a furnace for gold, so a person is tested by the praise they receive.

22 Even if you grind a fool in a mortar, crushing him with a pestle along with grain, his foolishness will not leave him.

23 Be diligent in understanding the condition of your flocks, and pay close attention to the care of your herds.

24 Wealth does not last forever, nor does a crown endure through every generation.

25 The grass becomes visible, and the young shoots emerge, while the mountain plants are gathered.

26 The wool from the lambs will provide your garments, and the goats will serve as payment for the field.

27 You will have an abundance of goats’ milk to nourish yourself, provide for your household, and sustain your servants.
