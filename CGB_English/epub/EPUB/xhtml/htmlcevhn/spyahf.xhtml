II Corinthians Chapter 13

1 This is the third time I am coming to you. Every matter must be confirmed by the testimony of two or three witnesses.

2 I have already warned you before, and now I am warning you again, as though I were present with you for the second time. Even though I am absent now, I am writing to those who have sinned in the past and to everyone else, making it clear that if I come again, I will not hold back in dealing with the matter.

3 Since you are looking for evidence of Christ speaking through me, this is not something weak or ineffective in relation to you, but rather it is powerful and active among you.

4 Although he was crucified in a state of human frailty, he now lives by the power of God. In the same way, though we share in his weakness, we will also live with him through the power of God at work in you.

5 Test yourselves to see if you are truly living in the faith. Examine your own hearts and lives. Do you not recognize that Jesus Christ is within you—unless, of course, you fail the test and prove to be unapproved?

6 However, I am confident that you will come to recognize that we are not disqualified or unapproved.

7 I now pray to God that you do no wrong—not so that we may appear to be approved, but so that you may do what is right, even if we seem to be disqualified.

8 We are powerless to act against what is true, but we can only act in support of the truth.

9 We rejoice when we are weak and you are strong, and this is our prayer: that you may be fully mature and complete.

10 Therefore, I am writing these things while I am away, so that when I am present, I will not need to be severe in using the authority that the Lord has given me—authority intended for building you up, not for tearing you down.

11 Finally, my brothers and sisters, I bid you farewell. Strive for maturity, take courage, be united in purpose, and live in harmony. Then the God of love and peace will be with you.

12 Greet each other with a sacred kiss.

13 All the believers send you their greetings.

14 May the gracious favor of the Lord Jesus Christ, the boundless love of God, and the intimate fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with all of you. Amen.
