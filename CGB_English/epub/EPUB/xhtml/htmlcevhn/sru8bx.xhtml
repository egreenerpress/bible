Job Chapter 33

1 Therefore, Job, I urge you to listen to my words and pay close attention to everything I say.

2 Listen now, for I have begun to speak; my words are formed on my tongue and ready to be spoken.

3 My words will reflect the sincerity of my heart, and my lips will speak knowledge with clarity.

4 The Spirit of God has created me, and the breath of the Almighty has granted me life.

5 If you are able to respond to me, arrange your arguments clearly before me, and present your case.

6 Listen, I stand here in God’s place, as you requested, yet I too am shaped from the same clay as you.

7 Listen, my presence will not fill you with fear, nor will my hand weigh heavily upon you.

8 Certainly, I have heard you speak, and I have listened to the words you have said, declaring,

9 I am free from wrongdoing; I am blameless and without guilt. There is no sin or iniquity within me.

10 Look, he finds reasons to accuse me; he considers me his adversary.

11 He confines my feet in shackles; he watches all my ways closely.

12 Look, in this matter you are not right; I will respond to you, for God is greater than any human.

13 Why do you contend with God? He does not explain or give an account of His actions.

14 God speaks not just once, but even a second time, yet humanity fails to grasp it.

15 In a dream, during a night vision, when deep sleep comes upon people, as they lie resting on their beds;

16 Then He opens the understanding of people and confirms the lessons they are taught.

17 So that God may turn a person away from their own plans and conceal pride from them.

18 He preserves his soul from the grave and his life from being cut down by the sword.

19 He is disciplined with pain upon his bed, and his body is racked with constant, severe agony.

20 As a result, he finds even bread loathsome, and his soul detests even the finest food.

21 His body wastes away until it is no longer visible, and his bones, once hidden, now protrude and become visible.

22 Indeed, his soul is nearing the grave, and his life is close to the realm of death.

23 If there is a messenger appointed for him, a mediator, one out of a thousand, to declare to a person what is right and just for them,

24 Then God shows him mercy and declares, "Rescue him from descending to the grave, for I have provided a means of atonement."

25 His body will become healthier than a child’s; he will be restored to the vigor of his youth.

26 A person will pray to God, and He will show them favor; they will see His face with joy, for He will restore to them their righteousness.

27 He observes humanity, and if anyone acknowledges, "I have sinned and twisted what was right, yet it brought me no benefit,"

28 He will rescue his life from descending into the grave, and he will live to see the light of life.

29 Behold, God often works all these things in the lives of people.

30 To rescue his soul from the grave, so that he may be illuminated by the light of life.

31 Pay close attention, Job, and listen to me. Remain silent, and I will speak.

32 If you have anything to say, respond to me; speak, for I wish to vindicate you.

33 If not, listen to me; remain silent, and I will instruct you in wisdom.
