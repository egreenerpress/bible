Leviticus Chapter 9

1 On the eighth day, Moses summoned Aaron, his sons, and the elders of Israel.

2 And he said to Aaron, "Take for yourself a young bull as a sin offering and a ram as a burnt offering, both without defect, and present them before the LORD."

3 You are to speak to the Israelites and say: You must bring a young goat as a sin offering, and a calf and a lamb, both in their first year and without defect, as a burnt offering.

4 Additionally, prepare a bull and a ram as peace offerings to sacrifice before the LORD, along with a grain offering mixed with oil. For today the LORD will reveal Himself to you.

5 The people brought what Moses had instructed to the entrance of the tent of meeting, and the entire assembly gathered and stood in the presence of the LORD.

6 Moses said, "This is what the LORD has commanded you to do, so that the glory of the LORD may appear to you."

7 Moses said to Aaron, "Go to the altar and present your sin offering and your burnt offering. Make atonement for yourself and for the people. Then present the offering of the people and make atonement for them, just as the LORD has commanded."

8 Aaron then approached the altar and slaughtered the calf that was designated as the sin offering for himself.

9 Aaron’s sons brought the blood to him, and he dipped his finger into it. He then applied some of the blood to the horns of the altar and poured out the rest at the base of the altar.

10 However, the fat, the kidneys, and the fatty lobe that is above the liver from the sin offering, he burned on the altar, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

11 He burned the flesh and the hide with fire outside the camp.

12 He slaughtered the burnt offering, and Aaron’s sons brought the blood to him. He then sprinkled the blood all around the altar.

13 They brought the burnt offering to him, including its pieces and the head, and he burned them on the altar.

14 He washed the internal organs and the legs, then burned them on the altar as part of the burnt offering.

15 He presented the people's offering, took the goat designated as the sin offering for the people, slaughtered it, and offered it as a sin offering, just as he had done with the first one.

16 He presented the burnt offering and sacrificed it according to the prescribed procedure.

17 He presented the grain offering, took a handful of it, and burned it on the altar, in addition to the morning burnt offering.

18 He also slaughtered the bull and the ram as a peace offering, which was for the people. Aaron’s sons brought the blood to him, and he sprinkled it around the altar.

19 And the fat of the bull and the ram—the fat tail, the fat covering the entrails, the kidneys, and the fatty lobe attached to the liver—

20 They placed the fat on top of the breasts, and he burned the fat on the altar.

21 Aaron lifted up the breasts and the right shoulder as a wave offering before the LORD, just as Moses had instructed.

22 Aaron raised his hands toward the people and blessed them. Afterward, he descended from the altar, having completed the offerings: the sin offering, the burnt offering, and the peace offerings.

23 Moses and Aaron entered the tent of meeting, and when they came out, they blessed the people. Then the glory of the LORD appeared to all the people.

24 Then fire came forth from the presence of the LORD and consumed the burnt offering and the fat on the altar. When all the people witnessed this, they cried out in awe and fell facedown in reverence.
