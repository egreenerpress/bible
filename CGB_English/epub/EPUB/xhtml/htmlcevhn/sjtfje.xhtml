Nehemiah Chapter 13

1 On that day, they read aloud from the book of Moses in the hearing of the people, and it was found written there that the Ammonite and the Moabite must never be admitted into the assembly of God, not for all time.

2 Because they did not provide the Israelites with bread and water, but instead hired Balaam to oppose them, intending to curse them. However, our God transformed the curse into a blessing.

3 After they had heard the law read to them, they removed from the community of Israel all those who were of foreign descent.

4 Before this time, Eliashib the priest, who was in charge of the storerooms of the house of our God, had formed a close association with Tobiah.

5 He had prepared a large room for him, where previously they stored the grain offerings, the frankincense, the sacred vessels, the tithes of grain, new wine, and olive oil—all of which were designated by command to be given to the Levites, the singers, and the gatekeepers, as well as the contributions for the priests.

6 During this entire period, I was not in Jerusalem, for in the thirty-second year of Artaxerxes, king of Babylon, I went to the king. After some time, I received permission from the king to leave.

7 When I arrived in Jerusalem, I learned about the wrongdoing of Eliashib on behalf of Tobiah, in that he had provided him a room within the courts of the house of God.

8 This deeply troubled me, so I removed all of Tobiah’s belongings from the room and threw them out.

9 Then I gave the order, and they purified the storerooms. Afterward, I returned the sacred vessels of the house of God to their place, along with the grain offerings and the incense.

10 I realized that the portions meant for the Levites had not been given to them, because the Levites and the singers, who were responsible for the work, had each fled to their own fields.

11 Then I confronted the leaders and demanded, "Why has the house of God been neglected?" I gathered the people together and restored them to their proper roles.

12 Then all the people of Judah brought the tithe of their grain, new wine, and olive oil to the storehouses.

13 I appointed overseers for the treasuries: Shelemiah the priest, Zadok the scribe, and Pedaiah from the Levites. Assisting them was Hanan, the son of Zaccur and grandson of Mattaniah, because they were considered trustworthy. Their responsibility was to distribute the supplies to their fellow Levites.

14 O my God, remember me for this, and do not erase the good works I have done for the house of my God and for its service.

15 During that time, I observed in Judah people treading winepresses on the Sabbath, bringing in sheaves of grain, loading donkeys, and transporting wine, grapes, figs, and all kinds of goods into Jerusalem on the Sabbath day. I confronted them and warned them on the very day they were selling food.

16 Men from Tyre also lived there, bringing fish and all kinds of goods, which they sold on the Sabbath to the people of Judah and in Jerusalem.

17 Then I confronted the leaders of Judah and said to them, "What is this wicked thing you are doing, desecrating the Sabbath day?"

18 Didn’t your ancestors act in this same way, and didn’t our God bring all this disaster upon us and upon this city? Yet now you are bringing even greater wrath upon Israel by desecrating the Sabbath.

19 It happened that as the gates of Jerusalem began to grow dark before the Sabbath, I gave orders for the gates to be closed. I instructed that they were not to be opened until after the Sabbath. I also stationed some of my servants at the gates to ensure that no goods or burdens were brought in on the Sabbath day.

20 So the merchants and sellers of all kinds of goods camped outside Jerusalem once or twice.

21 Then I confronted them and said, "Why are you spending the night near the wall? If you do this again, I will take action against you." From that time on, they no longer came on the Sabbath.

22 I instructed the Levites to purify themselves and to come and guard the gates, in order to honor and set apart the Sabbath day as holy. Remember me, O my God, for this as well, and show me mercy according to the abundance of your steadfast love.

23 In those days, I also observed Jews who had taken wives from Ashdod, Ammon, and Moab.

24 The children spoke partly in the language of Ashdod and were unable to speak in the language of the Jews, but instead used the language of each people group they were associated with.

25 I confronted them, rebuked them, and pronounced curses upon them. I struck some of them and pulled out their hair, compelling them to swear an oath before God, saying, "You must not give your daughters in marriage to their sons, nor take their daughters as wives for your sons or for yourselves."

26 Didn’t King Solomon of Israel sin because of these things? Yet among many nations, there was no king like him, for he was dearly loved by his God, and God made him king over all Israel. Even so, foreign women led him into sin.

27 Should we then listen to you and commit this great wrongdoing, sinning against our God by marrying foreign wives?

28 One of the sons of Joiada, who was the son of Eliashib the high priest, had become the son-in-law of Sanballat the Horonite. Because of this, I drove him away from me.

29 Remember them, O my God, for they have dishonored the priesthood and violated the sacred covenant established for the priests and the Levites.

30 In this way, I purified them from all foreign influences and assigned specific duties to the priests and the Levites, each one to their appointed tasks.

31 Concerning the contributions of wood for the altar at the designated times, and for the offering of the firstfruits, please remember me, my God, and show me favor.
