II Corinthians Chapter 2

1 I resolved in my own mind that I would not return to you with sorrow.

2 If I cause you sorrow, then who is there to bring me joy, except the one whom I have made sorrowful?

3 I wrote this to you so that when I come, I would not be grieved by those from whom I ought to find joy. I have full confidence in all of you, knowing that my joy is shared by all of you.

4 I wrote to you out of deep distress and anguish of heart, with many tears, not to cause you sorrow, but to let you know the overflowing love I have for you.

5 But if anyone has caused sorrow, they have not grieved me alone, but to some extentâ€”so that I might not burden all of you excessively.

6 For such a person, this punishment imposed by the majority is sufficient.

7 On the contrary, you should instead forgive him and offer him comfort, so that he is not overwhelmed by excessive sorrow.

8 Therefore, I urge you to reaffirm your love for him.

9 I wrote to you for this very purpose: to test and see whether you are obedient in all things, so that I might know the genuineness of your commitment.

10 To whomever you forgive anything, I also forgive. For if I have forgiven anything, I have done so for your sake, in the presence of Christ.

11 We must be cautious so that Satan does not gain an advantage over us, for we are fully aware of his schemes.

12 Moreover, when I arrived in Troas to proclaim the good news of Christ, the Lord provided me with an opportunity to do so.

13 I felt deeply unsettled in my spirit because I could not find Titus, my brother. So, after saying farewell to them, I departed from there and went into Macedonia.

14 Now we give thanks to God, who always leads us to victory through Christ and makes the fragrance of His knowledge evident through us in every place.

15 To God, we are like the pleasing aroma of Christ, both among those who are being saved and among those who are perishing.

16 To some, we are the aroma of death leading to death; to others, the aroma of life leading to life. And who is capable of handling such a responsibility?

17 For we are not like many others who distort the message of God. Instead, we speak with sincerity, as those sent by God, and in the presence of God we speak in Christ.
