Job Chapter 32

1 So these three men stopped responding to Job, because he considered himself righteous in his own eyes.

2 Then Elihu, the son of Barachel the Buzite, from the family of Ram, became very angry. His anger burned against Job because Job justified himself rather than acknowledging God's righteousness.

3 Moreover, his anger burned against his three friends because they had failed to provide a proper response, yet they had condemned Job.

4 Now Elihu had waited until Job finished speaking, because Job and his friends were older than he was.

5 When Elihu observed that these three men had no response to offer, his anger was stirred.

6 Then Elihu, the son of Barachel the Buzite, responded and said, "I am young in years, and you are much older. That is why I was hesitant and afraid to share my thoughts with you."

7 I declared, "Let the days speak, and let the abundance of years impart wisdom."

8 But there is a spirit within humanity, and the breath of the Almighty gives them understanding.

9 Great individuals are not always wise, nor do the elderly necessarily understand what is just.

10 Therefore, I said, "Listen to me; I too will express my thoughts."

11 Listen, I have been waiting to hear your words; I have paid close attention to your arguments as you carefully considered what to say.

12 Indeed, I paid close attention to you, and I observed that none of you were able to refute Job or provide a proper response to his arguments.

13 Be careful not to claim, "We have discovered wisdom," for it is God who brings him low, not mere human effort.

14 Now, he has not directed his words specifically at me, nor will I respond to him with the same arguments as you.

15 They were astonished and could no longer respond; they ceased to speak.

16 When I waited, (for they did not speak, but remained silent and offered no further response;)

17 I declared, "I will also present my case; I too will share my perspective."

18 For I am filled with thoughts, and the spirit within me compels me to speak.

19 Look, my heart is like wine that has no outlet; it is ready to burst like new wineskins.

20 I will speak so that I may find relief; I will open my mouth and respond.

21 I ask that I may never show partiality to anyone, nor offer flattering words to any person.

22 I do not know how to offer insincere praise or use flattering words; if I were to do so, my Creator would quickly remove me from this life.
