Proverbs Chapter 14

1 Every wise woman strengthens and establishes her household, but a foolish one tears it apart through her own actions.

2 The one who lives with integrity reveres the LORD, but the one whose ways are crooked shows contempt for Him.

3 A foolish person’s words are like a rod that strikes with arrogance, but the speech of the wise protects and guards them.

4 Where there are no oxen, the feeding trough remains clean, but abundant harvests come through the strength of the ox.

5 A trustworthy witness will not deceive, but a dishonest witness will speak falsehood.

6 A mocker seeks wisdom but does not find it, yet understanding comes easily to one who has insight.

7 Leave the company of a foolish person when you realize that their words lack wisdom and understanding.

8 The wisdom of the discerning lies in understanding their path, but the foolishness of fools is rooted in deception.

9 Fools mock the seriousness of sin, but among the upright, there is goodwill and approval.

10 The heart is intimately acquainted with its own sorrow, and no outsider can fully share in its joy.

11 The household of the wicked will be destroyed, but the dwelling of the righteous will thrive.

12 There is a path that appears right to a person, but in the end, it leads to death.

13 Even in moments of laughter, the heart may be filled with sorrow, and the joy that seems so bright will ultimately give way to grief.

14 The one whose heart turns away from faithfulness will be consumed by the consequences of their own choices, but a person of integrity will find fulfillment in their upright character.

15 The naive person believes everything they hear, but the wise individual carefully considers their steps.

16 A wise person is cautious and turns away from wrongdoing, but a foolish person acts recklessly and is overconfident.

17 A person who is quick to anger acts foolishly, and one who schemes with evil intentions is despised.

18 The naive inherit foolishness, but the discerning are adorned with understanding.

19 The wicked will bow down before the good, and the guilty will stand humbled at the entrance of the righteous.

20 A poor person is often despised even by those who live nearby, but a wealthy individual has many companions.

21 Whoever looks down on their neighbor commits a sin, but those who show compassion to the poor are truly blessed.

22 Do not those who plan evil go astray? But kindness and faithfulness belong to those who plan good.

23 In all hard work there is benefit, but mere talk leads only to poverty.

24 The wealth of the wise is their honor, but the foolishness of fools remains nothing but folly.

25 A trustworthy witness rescues lives, but a false witness spreads deceit.

26 Reverence for the LORD brings unwavering assurance, and his children will find a secure refuge.

27 Reverence for the LORD is a wellspring of life, enabling one to turn away from the traps that lead to death.

28 A king's honor is found in the size of his people, but the ruin of a ruler comes when his people are few.

29 A person who is slow to anger demonstrates great wisdom, but one who is quick-tempered magnifies foolishness.

30 A healthy heart brings life to the body, but envy eats away at the bones like decay.

31 Whoever exploits the poor insults their Creator, but whoever shows respect to God demonstrates compassion to the needy.

32 The wicked are swept away by their own wrongdoing, but the righteous hold fast to hope even in death.

33 Wisdom resides in the heart of one who has understanding, but what is within fools becomes evident.

34 Righteousness elevates a nation, but sin brings disgrace to any people.

35 The king’s favor rests on a servant who acts wisely, but his anger is directed toward the one who brings disgrace.
