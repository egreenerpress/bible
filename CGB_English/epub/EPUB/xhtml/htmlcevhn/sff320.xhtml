Matthew Chapter 4

1 Then Jesus was led by the Spirit into the wilderness to be tested by the devil.

2 After he had gone without food for forty days and forty nights, he became hungry.

3 When the tempter approached Him, he said, "If you are truly the Son of God, command these stones to become bread."

4 But he replied, "It is written: 'One does not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of God.'"

5 Then the devil led him to the holy city and placed him on the highest point of the temple.

6 And he said to him, “If you are the Son of God, throw yourself down, for it is written: ‘He will command his angels concerning you,’ and ‘They will lift you up in their hands, so that you will not strike your foot against a stone.’”

7 Jesus replied to him, "It is also written, 'You must not put the Lord your God to the test.'"

8 Once more, the devil took him up to a very high mountain and showed him all the kingdoms of the world and their splendor.

9 And he said to him, "I will give you all these things if you will bow down and worship me."

10 Then Jesus said to him, "Go away, Satan! For it is written: 'You shall worship the Lord your God, and Him alone shall you serve.'"

11 Then the devil left him, and suddenly, angels came and attended to his needs.

12 When Jesus learned that John had been imprisoned, he left for Galilee.

13 After leaving Nazareth, he went and settled in Capernaum, a town by the sea, in the region of Zebulun and Naphtali.

14 This happened to fulfill what was spoken by the prophet Isaiah, who said:

15 The land of Zebulun and the land of Naphtali, along the way of the sea, beyond the Jordan, in Galilee of the Gentiles.

16 The people who lived in darkness have seen a great light; and for those dwelling in the land overshadowed by death, a light has dawned.

17 From that time onward, Jesus began to proclaim His message, saying, "Turn away from your sins and change your hearts, for the kingdom of heaven is near."

18 As Jesus was walking along the shore of the Sea of Galilee, he noticed two brothers, Simon (who was called Peter) and his brother Andrew. They were casting a net into the water, for they were fishermen by trade.

19 And He said to them, "Come, follow me, and I will make you fishers of people."

20 Immediately, they left their fishing nets behind and followed him.

21 Continuing on from there, He saw two other brothers, James the son of Zebedee, and John his brother, in a boat with Zebedee their father, repairing their nets. He called to them.

22 At once, they left the boat and their father behind and followed him.

23 Jesus traveled throughout Galilee, teaching in the synagogues, proclaiming the good news of the kingdom, and healing every kind of illness and disease among the people.

24 His reputation spread throughout all of Syria, and people brought to him all who were suffering from various illnesses and severe pain, those who were demon-possessed, those afflicted with seizures, and those paralyzed. He healed them all.

25 Large crowds of people followed him from Galilee, from Decapolis, from Jerusalem, from Judea, and from the region beyond the Jordan.
