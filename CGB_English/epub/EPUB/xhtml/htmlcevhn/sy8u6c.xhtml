Proverbs Chapter 31

1 These are the words of King Lemuel, the inspired message that his mother taught him.

2 What is it, my son? What concerns you, the child of my own body? What weighs on you, the one I dedicated to God with solemn vows?

3 Do not give your strength to women, nor your ways to that which brings ruin to kings.

4 It is not fitting for kings, O Lemuel, nor is it proper for rulers to indulge in wine or for leaders to crave strong drink.

5 Otherwise, they may drink and forget what the law requires, leading them to distort justice for those who are suffering.

6 Give strong drink to those who are on the verge of death, and wine to those whose hearts are weighed down with sorrow.

7 Let him drink, so that he may forget his poverty and no longer recall his suffering.

8 Speak up for those who cannot speak for themselves, and defend the rights of those who are destined for ruin.

9 Speak boldly, administer justice fairly, and defend the rights of the poor and those in need.

10 Who can find a woman of noble character? Her value is far greater than that of precious jewels.

11 Her husband has complete confidence in her, so that he lacks nothing of value.

12 She will bring him good and not harm all the days of her life.

13 She seeks out wool and flax, and works with willing hands.

14 She is like the trading ships of merchants; she brings her provisions from distant places.

15 She also rises while it is still night, providing food for her household and assigning tasks to her servants.

16 She carefully evaluates a field and purchases it; from the earnings of her labor, she plants a vineyard.

17 She prepares herself with vigor and makes her arms strong.

18 She recognizes that her trade is profitable; her lamp does not go out at night.

19 She reaches out her hands to the spindle, and her fingers grasp the distaff.

20 She extends her hand to the poor; indeed, she reaches out her hands to those in need.

21 She has no fear of the snow for her household, because all her family are dressed in warm, durable garments.

22 She creates beautiful tapestries for her home; her garments are made of fine linen and rich purple cloth.

23 Her husband is well respected at the city gates, where he takes his place among the leaders of the land.

24 She produces fine linen and sells it; she also supplies sashes to the merchants.

25 She is clothed with strength and dignity, and she looks to the future with joy.

26 She speaks with wisdom, and her words are guided by the principle of kindness.

27 She carefully oversees the affairs of her household and does not indulge in laziness.

28 Her children stand and honor her, declaring her blessed; her husband also, and he praises her, saying, "You are truly worthy of admiration."

29 Many women have demonstrated noble character, but you surpass them all.

31 Acknowledge her for the results of her labor, and may her achievements bring her honor in the public assembly.
