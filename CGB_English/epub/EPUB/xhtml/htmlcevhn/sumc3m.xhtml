Job Chapter 16

1 Then Job replied and said,

2 I have heard many things like this before; you are all miserable comforters.

3 Do your empty words ever cease? What gives you the confidence to respond in this way?

4 I could speak just as you do: if I were in your position, I could pile up arguments against you and shake my head in disapproval.

5 But I would encourage you with my words, and the comfort from my lips would ease your suffering.

6 Even if I speak, my suffering is not relieved; and if I remain silent, how am I comforted?

7 But now He has exhausted me; You have devastated all those around me.

8 You have filled me with wrinkles, which stand as evidence against me; and my emaciation, evident within me, testifies openly to my condition.

9 In his fierce anger, he tears me apart, for he despises me; he gnashes his teeth at me in rage. My adversary glares at me with piercing eyes.

10 They have opened their mouths wide against me; they have struck me on the cheek with contempt; they have united together to oppose me.

11 God has handed me over to the wicked and delivered me into the power of the unrighteous.

12 I was living in comfort, but He shattered me completely. He seized me by the neck and crushed me; He made me His target and tore me apart.

13 His archers surround me on every side; he pierces my innermost being without mercy and pours out my lifeblood upon the ground.

14 He shatters me with one blow after another; he charges at me like a mighty warrior.

15 I have clothed myself in sackcloth, covering my skin, and I have humbled my strength, pressing my pride into the dust.

16 My face is disfigured from weeping, and the shadow of death hangs over my eyelids.

17 My hands are free from any wrongdoing, and my prayer is sincere.

18 O earth, do not conceal my blood, and may my cry find no resting place.

19 Behold, even now, my witness is in heaven, and my testimony is recorded in the heights above.

20 My friends mock me, but my eyes overflow with tears to God.

21 Oh, if only someone could argue a person's case before God, just as one might advocate for a friend!

22 When a few more years have passed, I will take the path from which I will not return.
