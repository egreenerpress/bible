Colossians Chapter 4

1 Employers, treat your workers with fairness and equity, recognizing that you too have a Master in heaven.

2 Persist in prayer, staying alert and attentive as you do so, and always express gratitude in your prayers.

3 At the same time, pray for us as well, that God may open a door for us to proclaim the message concerning the mystery of Christ, for which I am also in chains.

4 So that I may make it clear, as I am obligated to proclaim.

5 Live wisely in your interactions with those outside the faith, making the most of every opportunity.

6 Let your words always be gracious, infused with wisdom and thoughtfulness, so that you may know how to respond appropriately to each person.

7 Tychicus will inform you about all my circumstances. He is a dear brother, a trustworthy servant, and a fellow worker in the Lord.

8 I have sent him to you for this very purpose, so that he may learn about your situation and bring encouragement to your hearts.

9 With Onesimus, a faithful and dearly loved brother, who is one of your own. They will inform you about everything that has been happening here.

10 Aristarchus, my fellow prisoner, sends you his greetings, as does Mark, the cousin of Barnabas. (You have received instructions concerning him; if he comes to you, welcome him.)

11 And Jesus, who is called Justus, being among those of the circumcision. These are the only ones who have worked with me for the kingdom of God, and they have been a source of encouragement to me.

12 Epaphras, who is one of your own, a servant of Christ, sends you his greetings. He is always earnestly striving for you in his prayers, asking that you may stand firm, mature, and fully assured in all the will of God.

13 For I can testify about him that he has a deep concern for you and for those in Laodicea and Hierapolis.

14 Luke, the beloved doctor, and Demas send their greetings to you.

15 Greet the brothers and sisters in Laodicea, as well as Nymphas and the church that meets in his home.

16 When this letter has been read among you, make sure it is also read in the church of the Laodiceans, and that you in turn read the letter that comes from Laodicea.

17 And tell Archippus, "Be diligent in carrying out the ministry you have received in the Lord, ensuring that you fulfill it completely."
