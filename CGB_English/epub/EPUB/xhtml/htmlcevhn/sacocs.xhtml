I Peter Chapter 4

1 Since Christ suffered in his physical body for our sake, you too should prepare yourselves with the same resolve, because whoever has suffered in the flesh has turned away from sin.

2 He should no longer spend the remaining time of his earthly life pursuing human desires, but instead live in accordance with the will of God.

3 The time we have already spent in our past lives should be enough for us to have carried out the desires of the Gentiles, when we lived in unrestrained immorality, indulging in lustful passions, excessive drinking, wild parties, drunken feasts, and detestable idol worship.

4 They are surprised that you do not join them in the same reckless and wild behavior, and they insult you because of it.

5 Who will give an account to the one who is prepared to judge the living and the dead?

6 For this reason, the gospel was proclaimed even to those who have died, so that they might be judged in the flesh as humans are, but live in the spirit according to God’s will.

7 The culmination of all things is near. Therefore, maintain self-control and be alert, devoting yourselves to prayer.

8 Above all, maintain a deep and earnest love for one another, because love has the power to cover over a multitude of sins.

9 Show hospitality to one another without complaining or reluctance.

10 Each person has received a gift; use it to serve one another, as faithful stewards of the diverse and abundant grace of God.

11 If anyone speaks, let them speak as one delivering the message of God; if anyone serves, let them do so with the strength that God provides, so that in all things God may be glorified through Jesus Christ. To Him belong all praise and eternal dominion, forever and ever. Amen.

12 Dear friends, do not be surprised or bewildered by the intense trials you are enduring, as if something unusual or unexpected were happening to you.

13 But be glad, because you share in the sufferings of Christ, so that when his glory is revealed, you may also rejoice with overwhelming joy.

14 If you are insulted because of your association with Christ, consider yourselves blessed, for the glorious Spirit of God rests upon you. While others may speak evil of Him, you bring Him honor.

15 However, let none of you endure suffering as a murderer, a thief, a wrongdoer, or as someone who meddles in the affairs of others.

16 If anyone suffers because they are a Christian, they should not feel ashamed, but instead honor God because of it.

17 The time has now come for judgment to begin with the household of God. And if it starts with us first, what will be the outcome for those who refuse to obey the gospel of God?

18 If even the righteous are barely saved, where will the ungodly and the sinner stand?

19 Therefore, let those who endure suffering in accordance with God’s will entrust their lives to Him, their faithful Creator, while continuing to do what is good.
