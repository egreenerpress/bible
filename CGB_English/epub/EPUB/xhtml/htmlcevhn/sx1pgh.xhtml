Ecclesiastes Chapter 3

1 For everything there is an appointed time, and a moment for every purpose under heaven.

2 There is a time to be born and a time to die; a time to plant and a time to uproot what has been planted.

3 There is a time to bring death, and a time to restore life; a time to tear down, and a time to rebuild.

4 There is a time to shed tears and a time to burst into laughter; a time to grieve deeply and a time to leap for joy.

5 There is a time to scatter stones, and a time to gather them; a time to hold close, and a time to let go.

6 A time to gain, and a time to lose; a time to hold on, and a time to let go.

7 A time to tear apart, and a time to mend; a time to remain silent, and a time to speak.

8 There is a season for love and a season for hate; a time for conflict and a time for peace.

9 What benefit does a person gain from all their hard work and labor?

10 I have observed the toil that God has given to humanity to occupy themselves with.

11 God has made everything beautiful in its proper time. Moreover, He has placed eternity in the human heart, yet in such a way that no one can fully comprehend the work God has done from beginning to end.

12 I understand that there is nothing inherently good in people except to find joy and to do what is right throughout their lives.

13 Furthermore, it is fitting for each person to eat and drink, and to find satisfaction in all their laborâ€”this is a gift from God.

14 I understand that whatever God does, it is eternal: nothing can be added to it, nor anything taken away from it. God acts in this way so that people will stand in awe of Him.

15 What has happened before is happening now, and what will happen has already occurred in the past. God calls for an account of what has gone before.

16 Furthermore, I observed under the sun that in the place where justice should be upheld, there was wickedness; and in the place where righteousness should prevail, there was injustice.

17 I thought to myself, "God will judge both the righteous and the wicked, for there is a time appointed for every matter and every deed."

18 I reflected in my heart about the condition of humanity, that God might reveal their true nature, so that they might recognize that they themselves are no different from animals.

19 What happens to human beings also happens to animals; the same fate awaits them both. As one dies, so dies the other. Indeed, they all share the same breath of life, so that a human has no advantage over an animal. For everything is fleeting and meaningless.

20 All people go to the same place; all come from the dust, and all return to dust once more.

21 Who can truly understand the spirit of a human, which ascends upward, or the spirit of an animal, which descends downward to the earth?

22 Therefore, I understand that there is nothing better than for a person to find joy in their own labor, for that is their rightful portion. For who can show them what will happen after they are gone?
