Mark Chapter 11

1 As they approached Jerusalem, near Bethphage and Bethany at the Mount of Olives, he sent two of his disciples ahead.

2 He said to them, "Go into the village ahead of you. As soon as you enter it, you will find a young donkey tied there, one that no one has ever ridden. Untie it and bring it here."

3 If anyone asks you, "Why are you doing this?" reply, "The Lord needs it," and he will immediately send it here.

4 They went on their way and found the colt tied up near a door, at a crossroads outside. They untied it.

5 Some of those standing nearby asked them, "Why are you untying the colt?"

6 They spoke to them exactly as Jesus had instructed, and then they allowed them to leave.

7 They brought the young donkey to Jesus and laid their cloaks on it, and he sat upon it.

8 Many people spread their cloaks on the road, while others cut branches from the trees and scattered them along the path.

9 Those who went ahead of him and those who followed behind cried out, saying, "Hosanna! Blessed is the one who comes in the name of the Lord!"

10 Praise be to the kingdom of our father David, which comes in the name of the Lord! Hosanna in the highest heavens!

11 Jesus entered Jerusalem and went into the temple. After observing everything around him, and as evening had already come, he left with the twelve disciples and went to Bethany.

12 The next day, as they were leaving Bethany, Jesus felt hungry.

13 As Jesus saw a fig tree in the distance with leaves, he approached it, hoping to find some fruit on it. But when he reached it, he found nothing except leaves, because it was not yet the season for figs.

14 And Jesus responded, saying to it, "May no one ever eat fruit from you again." His disciples heard this.

15 They arrived in Jerusalem, and Jesus entered the temple. He began to drive out those who were buying and selling there, overturning the tables of the money changers and the seats of those who sold doves.

16 And He did not allow anyone to carry any kind of container or object through the temple.

17 And he taught them, saying, "Is it not written, 'My house will be called by all nations a house of prayer'? But you have turned it into a hideout for thieves."

18 The scribes and the chief priests heard this and began to plot how they might kill him, for they were afraid of him. The reason for their fear was that all the people were amazed by his teaching.

19 And when evening had come, he left the city.

20 In the morning, as they walked by, they noticed the fig tree had withered completely, all the way down to its roots.

21 Peter, remembering what had happened, said to him, "Teacher, look! The fig tree you cursed has withered and died."

22 And Jesus responded, saying to them, "Put your trust in God."

23 Truly, I tell you this: If anyone says to this mountain, ‘Be lifted up and thrown into the sea,’ and does not doubt in their heart but believes that what they say will happen, it will be done for them. Whatever they declare in faith will come to pass.

24 Therefore, I tell you this: Whatever you ask for in prayer, believe that you have received it, and it will be yours.

25 When you stand to pray, if you hold anything against anyone, forgive them, so that your Father in heaven may also forgive you your wrongdoings.

26 But if you do not forgive others, your Father in heaven will not forgive your sins.

27 They returned to Jerusalem, and as Jesus was walking in the temple, the chief priests, the scribes, and the elders approached him.

28 And say to him, "By what authority are you doing these things? And who gave you this authority to do them?"

29 Jesus responded to them, saying, "I will also ask you one question. Answer me, and then I will tell you by what authority I do these things."

30 Was John's baptism authorized by God, or was it merely a human institution? Give me your answer.

31 They discussed the matter among themselves, saying, "If we say, 'From heaven,' he will ask, 'Then why didn’t you believe him?'"

32 But if we were to say, 'From human authority,' we would fear the people, for everyone regarded John as a true prophet.

33 They replied to Jesus, "We do not know." Jesus responded, "Then I will not tell you by what authority I do these things."
