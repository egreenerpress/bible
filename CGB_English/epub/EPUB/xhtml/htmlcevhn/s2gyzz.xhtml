Hebrews Chapter 12

1 Therefore, since we are surrounded by such a vast multitude of witnesses, let us cast off every burden and the sin that so easily entangles us, and let us run with endurance the race that lies ahead of us.

2 Fix your eyes on Jesus, the one who initiates and perfects our faith. For the joy that lay ahead of him, he endured the cross, disregarding its shame, and now sits at the right hand of the throne of God.

3 Think carefully about Jesus, who endured such hostility and opposition from sinful people, so that you do not grow weary and lose heart in your faith.

4 You have not yet endured to the point of shedding your blood in your struggle against sin.

5 You have forgotten the encouragement that addresses you as children: "My child, do not disregard the Lord's discipline, nor lose heart when He corrects you."

6 For those whom the Lord loves, he disciplines, and he corrects every child whom he accepts.

7 If you endure discipline, God is treating you as his children. For what child is there whom a father does not discipline?

8 But if you are without discipline, which all experience, then you are illegitimate children and not true sons.

9 Moreover, we have had earthly fathers who disciplined us, and we respected them. Should we not then all the more submit to the Father of our spirits and live?

10 For indeed, they disciplined us for a short time as they saw fit, but God does so for our benefit, so that we may share in his holiness.

11 At the time, no discipline seems pleasant, but rather painful. Yet afterward, it produces a harvest of peace and righteousness for those who have been trained by it.

12 Therefore, strengthen the hands that are weak and the knees that are feeble.

13 Ensure that your way of life is upright and straightforward, so that those who are weak or struggling do not stumble and fall away from the right path. Instead, encourage their healing and restoration.

14 Strive to live in peace with everyone, and pursue holiness, for without it no one will see the Lord.

15 Be watchful and careful, so that no one falls short of God’s grace, and so that no bitter root grows up to cause trouble and defile many.

16 Be careful that no one among you becomes sexually immoral or godless, like Esau, who traded his birthright for a single meal.

17 You know that afterward, when he desired to inherit the blessing, he was rejected, for he found no opportunity to change his father's mind, even though he sought it earnestly with tears.

18 For you have not come to a mountain that can be touched, a mountain blazing with fire, nor to gloom, darkness, and storm.

19 And there was the sound of a trumpet and the voice speaking words. Those who heard it begged that no further word be spoken to them.

20 For they could not bear what was commanded: "If even an animal touches the mountain, it must be stoned or pierced with a spear."

21 The sight was so terrifying that Moses said, "I am utterly filled with fear and trembling."

22 But you have come to Mount Zion, to the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem, and to countless multitudes of angels,

23 To the universal gathering and community of those who are God’s firstborn, whose names are recorded in heaven, and to God, the Judge of all, and to the spirits of righteous people who have been made complete and perfect.

24 And to Jesus, the mediator of the new covenant, and to the sprinkled blood, which proclaims a more powerful message than the blood of Abel.

25 Be careful not to reject the one who is speaking to you. For if those who refused to listen to the one who spoke on earth did not escape punishment, how much more will we not escape if we turn away from the one who speaks from heaven?

26 At that time, His voice shook the earth, but now He has promised, saying, "Once more I will shake not only the earth but also the heavens."

27 The phrase, "Yet once more," signifies the removal of things that can be shaken—things that have been created—so that what cannot be shaken may endure.

28 Therefore, since we are receiving a kingdom that cannot be shaken, let us hold on to grace, so that we may serve God in a way that pleases Him, with reverence and awe.

29 For our God is a blazing fire, all-consuming in His power and holiness.
