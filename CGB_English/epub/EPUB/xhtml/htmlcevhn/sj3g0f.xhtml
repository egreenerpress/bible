Philippians Chapter 1

1 Paul and Timothy, servants of Jesus Christ, to all the holy people in Christ Jesus who are in Philippi, along with the overseers and deacons:

2 May grace and peace be given to you from God our Father and from the Lord Jesus Christ.

3 I continually give thanks to my God every time I remember you.

4 In all my prayers for you, I always make my requests with joy.

5 For your partnership in spreading the good news from the very beginning until this present time.

6 I am fully convinced of this: that the One who initiated a good work in you will bring it to completion until the day of Jesus Christ.

7 It is right for me to feel this way about all of you, because I hold you deeply in my heart. Both during my imprisonment and in my efforts to defend and establish the gospel, you have all shared with me in the grace of God.

8 For God is my witness, how deeply I care for all of you with the affection of Christ Jesus.

9 And this is my prayer: that your love may overflow increasingly, accompanied by deep understanding and wise discernment.

10 So that you may discern what is truly excellent and valuable, and that you may be pure and blameless until the day of Christ’s return.

11 Being abundantly filled with the good works that result from living in right relationship with God—works made possible through Jesus Christ—all to bring glory and praise to God.

12 However, I want you to understand, brothers and sisters, that the events that have happened to me have actually served to advance the spread of the gospel.

13 Therefore, my imprisonment for the sake of Christ has become widely known throughout the entire palace guard and to everyone else.

14 And many of the brothers and sisters in the Lord, gaining confidence because of my imprisonment, have become even more courageous in speaking the message of God without fear.

15 Some people proclaim Christ out of jealousy and rivalry, while others do so with genuine goodwill.

16 Some preach Christ out of rivalry and selfish ambition, not with pure motives, but with the intention of causing me more distress in my imprisonment.

17 But the others act out of love, knowing that I have been appointed for the defense of the gospel.

18 What then? Regardless of the circumstances, whether with false motives or genuine intent, Christ is being proclaimed, and in this I rejoice. Indeed, I will continue to rejoice.

19 I am confident that this situation will ultimately result in my deliverance, through your prayers and the support provided by the Spirit of Jesus Christ.

20 With deep anticipation and hope, I am confident that I will not be put to shame in any way. Instead, I will continue to speak boldly and openly, as I always have, so that Christ may be honored and exalted through my life—whether through my continued living or through my death.

21 For me, living means Christ, and dying brings even greater benefit.

22 But if I continue to live in this earthly body, it will mean fruitful labor for me. Yet I am uncertain which to choose.

23 I am torn between two choices: I deeply desire to depart from this life and be with Christ, which is far better.

24 However, remaining alive in the body is more necessary for your sake.

25 With this assurance, I am confident that I will remain and stay with you all to help you progress and find joy in your faith.

26 So that your joy in Jesus Christ may overflow because of my return to you.

27 Let your manner of life be worthy of the gospel of Christ, so that whether I come and see you in person or remain absent, I may hear about your circumstances—that you are standing firm in one spirit, united in purpose as you work together for the faith that comes from the gospel.

28 Do not be intimidated in any way by those who oppose you. For them, this is a clear sign of their coming destruction, but for you, it is evidence of your salvation—and this comes from God.

29 For it has been granted to you on behalf of Christ, not only to believe in Him, but also to endure suffering for His sake.

30 You are experiencing the same struggle that you saw me endure and now hear that I am still facing.
