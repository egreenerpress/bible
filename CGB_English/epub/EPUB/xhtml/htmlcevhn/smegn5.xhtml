Zephaniah Chapter 3

1 Woe to the city that is defiled and corrupt, the city of oppression!

2 She did not heed the voice; she did not accept correction; she did not place her trust in the LORD; she did not draw close to her God.

3 Her leaders within her are like roaring lions; her judges are like wolves that prowl at evening; they do not leave anything for the next day—they devour their prey completely by morning.

4 Her prophets are reckless and deceitful individuals; her priests have defiled the sacred place and have violated the law.

5 The righteous LORD is present within it; He does no wrong. Each morning He faithfully brings His justice to light; He never fails. But the wicked know no shame.

6 I have brought an end to nations; their fortresses are left in ruins. I have turned their streets into desolate places, with no one passing through. Their cities are completely destroyed, devoid of people, with no inhabitants remaining.

7 I said, "Surely you will revere me; you will accept correction." Then your dwelling place would not be destroyed, despite whatever discipline I might bring upon you. But instead, you eagerly rose up and corrupted all your actions.

8 Therefore, wait for me, declares the LORD, until the day I rise up to act. For my purpose is to gather the nations and assemble the kingdoms, so that I may pour out my wrath upon them—my burning anger. For the whole earth will be consumed by the fire of my zeal.

9 At that time, I will give the people a pure and clear way of speaking, so that all of them may call on the name of the LORD and serve Him together in unity and agreement.

10 From beyond the rivers of Cush, my worshipers, the descendants of my scattered people, will bring offerings to me.

11 On that day, you will no longer feel shame for all the actions by which you have rebelled against me. For I will remove from among you those who take pride in their arrogance, and you will no longer act with pride because of my holy mountain.

12 I will also allow a humble and needy people to remain among you, and they will place their trust in the name of the LORD.

13 The remaining people of Israel will no longer commit wickedness or speak lies; no deceitful words will be found in their mouths. For they will graze in peace and rest securely, with no one to cause them fear.

14 Sing joyfully, O people of Zion; shout aloud, O Israel; be glad and rejoice with all your heart, O people of Jerusalem.

15 The LORD has removed your judgments; He has driven out your enemy. The King of Israel, the LORD Himself, is among you; you will no longer experience harm.

16 On that day, it will be said to Jerusalem, "Do not be afraid," and to Zion, "Do not let your hands grow weak."

17 The LORD your God, who is among you, is powerful; He will save you, He will delight in you with great joy; He will be quiet in His love, He will rejoice over you with songs of gladness.

18 I will gather those who grieve over the appointed festivals, those who belong to you, for whom the disgrace of it has become a heavy burden.

19 Listen carefully: At that time, I will deal with all who have oppressed you. I will rescue those who are weak and struggling, and I will bring back those who have been scattered and driven away. I will restore their honor and reputation in every place where they have been disgraced, giving them praise and renown wherever they were once shamed.

20 At that time, I will restore you and gather you together. For I will make you renowned and praised among all the peoples of the earth when I bring back your fortunes before your very eyes, declares the LORD.
