Psalms Chapter 6

1 (For the music director, to be played on stringed instruments, according to the Sheminith style. A psalm of David.) Lord, do not correct me in your anger, nor discipline me in your fierce wrath.

2 O LORD, show me mercy, for I am frail and weary. O LORD, heal me, for my very bones are in anguish.

3 My soul is deeply troubled and distressed; but you, O LORD, how long will this continue?

4 Turn back, O LORD, and rescue my life; save me because of your steadfast love.

5 For in death there is no memory of you; in the realm of the grave, who can praise you?

6 I am exhausted from my constant groaning; throughout the night, I drench my bed with tears; my couch is soaked with my weeping.

7 My eyes are worn out from sorrow; they grow weak and weary because of all my adversaries.

8 Leave me, all of you who practice wickedness, for the LORD has heard the sound of my weeping.

9 The LORD has heard my plea; the LORD will accept my prayer.

10 May all my enemies be disgraced and deeply troubled; may they turn back and be suddenly put to shame.
