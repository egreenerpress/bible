Psalms Chapter 56

1 (For the music director, set to the tune of "The Silent Dove in Distant Lands," a prayer of David, when the Philistines seized him in Gath.) Be gracious to me, O God, for people seek to destroy me; they wage war against me relentlessly, oppressing me day after day.

2 My enemies constantly seek to overwhelm me, for there are many who rise up against me, O Most High.

3 When I am afraid, I will place my trust in you.

4 In God, I will praise His word; in God, I place my trust. I will not fear what mere mortals can do to me.

5 Every day they twist my words; all their intentions are directed toward harming me.

6 They assemble together, they conceal themselves, they observe my every move, lying in wait to take my life.

7 Will the wicked escape through their wrongdoing? In your anger, O God, bring down the nations.

8 You keep track of all my wanderings; collect my tears in your container—are they not recorded in your book?

9 When I call out to you, my enemies will retreat; this I am certain of, because God is on my side.

10 I will praise God for His word; in the LORD, I will exalt His word.

11 I have placed my trust in God; I will not fear what any human being can do to me.

12 Your promises are binding upon me, O God; I will fulfill my commitment to offer you praise.

13 You have rescued my life from death; will you not also keep my feet from stumbling, so that I may live in God’s presence and walk in the light of life?
