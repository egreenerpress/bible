Job Chapter 35

1 Elihu continued speaking and said,

2 Do you consider it just to claim, "My righteousness surpasses God's"?

3 For you said, "What benefit will it bring you?" and, "What gain will I have if I am cleansed from my sin?"

4 I will respond to you, and to your companions who are with you.

5 Gaze up at the heavens and observe; consider the clouds that soar far above you.

6 If you sin, how does that affect Him? Or if your wrongdoings are many, what does that do to Him?

7 If you consider yourself righteous, what do you offer to Him? Or what does He receive from your hand?

8 Your wrongdoing may harm a person like yourself, and your righteous actions may benefit a fellow human being.

9 Because of the overwhelming acts of oppression, the afflicted cry out in distress; they cry out under the weight of the power wielded by the mighty.

10 But no one asks, "Where is God, my Creator, who gives songs in the night?"

11 Who instructs us beyond what the animals of the earth know, and gives us greater understanding than the birds of the sky?

12 There they cry out, but no one answers them, because of the arrogance of the wicked.

13 Certainly, God does not listen to empty or meaningless words, nor does the Almighty pay attention to them.

14 Even though you say you cannot perceive Him, His justice remains steadfast; therefore, place your trust in Him.

15 But now, since this is not the case, He has intervened in His anger; yet He does not act with overwhelming severity.

16 Therefore, Job speaks without understanding; he utters many words, yet lacks true knowledge.
