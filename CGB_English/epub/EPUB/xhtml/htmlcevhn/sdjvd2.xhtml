Jeremiah Chapter 7

1 This is the message that the LORD gave to Jeremiah:

2 Stand at the entrance of the LORD’s temple and declare this message there, saying: "Listen to the word of the LORD, all you people of Judah who come through these gates to worship the LORD."

3 This is what the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel, declares: Change your ways and your actions, and I will allow you to remain in this land.

4 Do not place your trust in deceptive words, repeating, "This is the temple of the LORD, the temple of the LORD, the temple of the LORD!"

5 If you genuinely change your ways and your actions, and if you truly administer justice between one person and another,

6 If you do not oppress the foreigner, the fatherless, or the widow, and if you do not shed innocent blood in this place, nor follow other gods to your own harm,

7 Then I will establish you in this place, in the land I gave to your ancestors, to remain there forever.

8 Look, you are relying on deceptive words that are of no benefit.

9 Do you steal, murder, commit adultery, swear falsely, burn offerings to Baal, and follow after other gods that you do not even know?

10 Come and present yourselves before me in this temple, which bears my name, and then declare, "We are safe to commit all these detestable acts"?

11 Has this house, which bears my name, become in your eyes a hideout for thieves? Truly, I myself have seen it, declares the LORD.

12 Go now to the place in Shiloh, where I first established my presence and made my name known, and see what I did to it because of the wickedness of my people Israel.

13 And now, because you have committed all these deeds, declares the LORD, I spoke to you repeatedly, rising early to warn you, but you did not listen; I called out to you, but you did not respond.

14 Therefore, I will deal with this house, which bears my name and in which you place your trust, and with this place that I gave to you and your ancestors, just as I dealt with Shiloh.

15 I will banish you from my presence, just as I have banished all your relatives, the entire descendants of Ephraim.

16 Therefore, do not pray for this people, nor raise a cry or offer a prayer on their behalf, nor plead with me on their account, for I will not listen to you.

17 Do you not see what they are doing in the cities of Judah and in the streets of Jerusalem?

18 The children collect wood, the fathers light the fire, and the women knead dough to bake cakes for the queen of heaven. They pour out drink offerings to other gods, all to provoke me to anger.

19 The Lord says, "Are they really provoking me to anger? No, they are only bringing harm upon themselves, to their own shame and disgrace."

20 Therefore, this is what the Lord GOD says: "Behold, my anger and wrath will be poured out on this place—on the people, on the animals, on the trees of the field, and on the produce of the ground. It will burn fiercely and will not be extinguished."

21 This is what the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel, says: Combine your burnt offerings with your sacrifices and eat the meat.

22 For I did not speak to your ancestors, nor did I give them any commands on the day I brought them out of the land of Egypt, concerning burnt offerings or sacrifices.

23 This is what I commanded them, saying: "Listen to my voice, and I will be your God, and you will be my people. Walk in all the ways I have instructed you, so that it may go well with you."

24 However, they did not listen or pay attention, but instead followed their own plans and the stubborn desires of their wicked hearts. They turned away and moved backward, rather than progressing forward.

25 From the time your ancestors left the land of Egypt until this very day, I have continually sent you all my servants, the prophets, persistently and faithfully, rising early each day to send them to you.

26 Yet they did not listen to me or pay attention, but instead stubbornly hardened their hearts. They acted even more corruptly than their ancestors.

27 Therefore, you must speak all these words to them, but they will not listen to you. You will call out to them, but they will not answer you.

28 But you shall say to them, "This is a nation that does not heed the voice of the LORD their God, nor accepts discipline. Truth has vanished and is no longer spoken by them."

29 Shave off your hair, O Jerusalem, and throw it away; raise a lament on the barren heights, for the LORD has rejected and abandoned this generation that has provoked His wrath.

30 The people of Judah have committed evil in my sight, declares the LORD. They have placed their detestable idols in the house that bears my name, defiling it.

31 They have constructed the high places of Tophet, which is in the Valley of Ben Hinnom, to burn their sons and daughters in the fire—something I never commanded, nor did it even enter my mind.

32 Therefore, listen carefully, the time is coming, declares the LORD, when this place will no longer be called Tophet or the Valley of Ben Hinnom, but the Valley of Slaughter. They will bury the dead in Tophet until there is no more room.

33 The bodies of these people will become food for the birds of the sky and the wild animals of the earth, with no one to drive them away.

34 At that time, I will bring an end to the sounds of joy and celebration in the cities of Judah and the streets of Jerusalem—the voices of merriment, the shouts of gladness, the calls of the bridegroom, and the songs of the bride. For the land will become a desolate wasteland.
