Genesis Chapter 1

1 In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth.

2 The earth was formless and empty, and darkness covered the surface of the deep. But the Spirit of God was hovering over the surface of the waters.

3 And God said, "Let light come into being," and light came into existence.

4 And God observed the light, seeing that it was good. Then God separated the light from the darkness.

5 And God named the light "Day," and the darkness He named "Night." Then evening came, and morning followed—the first day.

6 And God said, "Let there be an expanse in the midst of the waters, and let it separate the waters from the waters."

7 And God created the expanse, separating the waters beneath the expanse from the waters above the expanse. And it was so.

8 And God named the expanse "Heaven." And there was evening, and there was morning, marking the second day.

9 And God said, "Let the waters beneath the sky be gathered into one place, and let the dry ground become visible." And it happened just as He said.

10 God named the dry ground "Earth," and the collected waters he called "Seas." God observed what he had made and saw that it was good.

11 And God said, "Let the earth produce vegetation: plants yielding seeds, and trees bearing fruit with seeds in them, each according to its kind, upon the earth." And it was so.

12 The earth produced vegetation: plants yielding seed according to their own kinds, and trees bearing fruit with seed in it, each according to its kind. And God saw that it was good.

13 And there was evening, and there was morning—the third day.

14 And God said, "Let there be lights in the expanse of the sky to separate the day from the night. They will serve as signs to mark sacred times, seasons, days, and years."

15 Let them serve as lights in the expanse of the sky to shine upon the earth. And it was so.

16 And God created two great lights: the larger light to govern the day, and the smaller light to govern the night. He also made the stars.

17 And God placed them in the expanse of the sky to shine light upon the earth.

18 And to govern the day and the night, and to separate the light from the darkness. And God saw that it was good.

19 And there was evening, and there was morning—the fourth day.

20 And God said, "Let the waters teem with living creatures, and let birds fly above the earth across the expanse of the sky."

21 And God created the great sea creatures and every living being that moves, which the waters produced in abundance, each according to its kind, and every winged bird according to its kind. And God saw that it was good.

22 And God blessed them, saying, "Be fruitful, increase in number, and fill the waters of the seas. Let the birds also multiply throughout the earth."

23 And there was evening, and there was morning—the fifth day.

24 And God said, "Let the earth produce living creatures according to their kinds—livestock, crawling animals, and wild beasts of the earth, each according to its kind." And it was so.

25 And God created the wild animals of the earth according to their kinds, the livestock according to their kinds, and all the creatures that move along the ground according to their kinds. And God saw that it was good.

26 And God said, "Let us create humanity in our image, reflecting our likeness. Let them rule over the fish of the sea, the birds of the sky, the livestock, all the earth, and every creature that moves upon the ground."

27 So God created humanity in His own likeness, in the likeness of God He created them; male and female He created them.

28 God blessed them and said to them, "Be fruitful and increase in number; fill the earth and bring it under your authority. Rule over the fish in the sea, the birds in the sky, and every living creature that moves on the earth."

29 And God said, "Look, I have given you every plant that bears seed, which is found on the face of the entire earth, and every tree that has fruit with seed in it. They will be your food."

30 And to every animal of the earth, to every bird of the air, and to every creature that moves on the ground—everything that has life—I have given every green plant for food. And it was so.

31 God observed all that He had created, and indeed, it was exceedingly good. And so, the evening passed and the morning came, completing the sixth day.
