Job Chapter 21

1 But Job replied and said,

2 Listen carefully to my words, and let them bring you comfort.

3 Allow me to speak, and after I have spoken, you may continue to mock if you wish.

4 Is my complaint directed toward mere mortals? And even if it were, why should my spirit not be deeply troubled?

5 Pay close attention, and be utterly amazed; place your hand over your mouth in awe.

6 Even when I recall these things, I am filled with fear, and trembling seizes my body.

7 Why do the wicked continue to live, grow old, and even increase in power?

8 Their descendants are firmly established in their presence, and their children are secure before their eyes.

9 Their homes are secure and free from dread, and they do not experience the discipline or judgment of God.

10 Their bull mates successfully and does not fail; their cow gives birth and does not miscarry her calf.

11 They send out their young ones like a flock of sheep, and their children leap and dance with joy.

12 They take up the tambourine and lyre, and they celebrate with the music of the pipe.

13 They live out their days in prosperity, yet in an instant they descend to the grave.

14 Therefore, they say to God, "Leave us alone; we have no interest in understanding your ways."

15 Who is the Almighty, that we should worship him? And what benefit would we gain if we pray to him?

16 Behold, their prosperity is not within their control; the schemes of the wicked are far removed from me.

17 How often is the light of the wicked extinguished! How frequently does their ruin come upon them! God dispenses suffering in His wrath.

18 They are like dry straw swept away by the wind, like husks scattered by a raging storm.

19 God stores up a person's guilt for their children; He will repay them, and they will come to realize it.

20 His own eyes will witness his downfall, and he will experience the full force of the Almighty's wrath.

21 What joy does he have in his household after he is gone, when the number of his months is cut short in the midst of his life?

22 Can anyone impart knowledge to God? For He is the one who judges even the most exalted beings.

23 One person dies in the prime of life, completely at peace and free from distress.

24 His body is strong and nourished, with ample sustenance within him, and his bones are rich and full of vitality.

25 Another person dies with a deeply troubled soul, having never experienced joy or satisfaction in life.

26 They will all lie down together in the dust, and the worms will spread over them.

27 Listen, I am fully aware of your thoughts and the schemes you are plotting against me, even though they are unjust and misguided.

28 You ask, "Where is the house of the ruler? And where are the places where the wicked dwell?"

29 Have you not inquired of those who travel the road? And do you not recognize the signs they bear?

30 Is the wicked person kept for the day of destruction? They will be brought out to face the day of wrath.

31 Who can confront him about his actions? And who can hold him accountable for what he has done?

32 Yet he will be taken to the grave, and his resting place will be in the tomb.

33 The soil of the valley will be pleasing to him, and all people will follow after him, just as countless others have gone before him.

34 How then can you offer me comfort that is meaningless, when your responses are filled with falsehood?
