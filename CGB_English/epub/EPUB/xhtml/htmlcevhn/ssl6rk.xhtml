II Samuel Chapter 19

1 And Joab was informed, saying, "Look, the king is weeping and grieving over Absalom."

2 On that day, the triumph turned into mourning for all the people, for they heard how deeply the king was grieving over his son.

3 And the people entered the city quietly that day, like those who are ashamed and slip away unnoticed when they retreat from battle.

4 But the king covered his face and wept aloud, crying, "Oh, my son Absalom! Oh, Absalom, my son, my son!"

5 Joab entered the king’s house and said, “Today you have brought disgrace upon all your servants who have saved your life, as well as the lives of your sons and daughters, your wives, and your concubines.

6 Because you love your enemies and hate your friends. Today you have made it clear that you care nothing for leaders or servants. For today I see that if Absalom had lived and all of us had died, it would have pleased you greatly.

7 Now then, rise up, go out, and speak kindly to your servants, for I swear by the LORD that if you do not go out, not a single one of them will remain with you tonight—and that will bring you greater harm than all the troubles you have faced from your youth until now.

8 Then the king stood up and took his seat at the city gate. The people were informed, "Look, the king is sitting at the gate!" So all the people came before the king, for every Israelite had returned to their own homes.

9 All the people throughout the tribes of Israel were in conflict, saying, "The king rescued us from the power of our enemies and delivered us from the hand of the Philistines, but now he has fled the land because of Absalom."

10 And Absalom, whom we appointed as our leader, has died in battle. So now, why are you not saying anything about bringing the king back?

11 King David sent a message to Zadok and Abiathar, the priests, instructing them, "Speak to the elders of Judah and say, 'Why are you delaying in bringing the king back to his palace? The entire nation of Israel has already expressed their desire for the king to return to his home.'"

12 You are my brothers, my own flesh and blood; why then are you the last to restore the king to his rightful place?

13 And say to Amasa, “Are you not my own flesh and blood? May God deal with me severely, and even more so, if you do not become the commander of my army in place of Joab, serving before me at all times.”

14 He united the hearts of all the men of Judah as if they were one, so that they sent this message to the king: "Return, you and all your servants."

15 So the king turned back and came to the Jordan River. Meanwhile, the people of Judah went to Gilgal to meet the king and escort him across the Jordan.

16 And Shimei, the son of Gera, a Benjamite from Bahurim, hurried and came down with the men of Judah to meet King David.

17 A thousand men from Benjamin accompanied him, along with Ziba, the servant of Saul’s household, and his fifteen sons and twenty servants. They all crossed the Jordan River ahead of the king.

18 A ferry boat was sent across to transport the king’s household and to carry out whatever he desired. As the king was crossing the Jordan, Shimei, the son of Gera, came and bowed down before him.

19 And he said to the king, "Do not let my lord hold me guilty of wrongdoing, nor remember the offense your servant committed on the day my lord the king left Jerusalem. May the king not dwell on it in his heart."

20 Your servant acknowledges that I have sinned. Therefore, I have come today, the first of all the house of Joseph, to go down and meet my lord the king.

21 But Abishai, the son of Zeruiah, responded, "Shouldn’t Shimei be executed for this? He cursed the Lord’s anointed one!"

22 David said, "What do you have to do with me, you sons of Zeruiah, that you should oppose me today? Should anyone be put to death in Israel today? Don’t I know that I am now king over Israel?"

23 So the king declared to Shimei, "You will not be put to death." And the king made a solemn oath to him.

24 Mephibosheth, the son of Saul, came down to meet the king. He had not cared for his feet, trimmed his beard, or washed his clothes from the day the king had left until the day he returned safely.

25 When he arrived in Jerusalem to meet the king, the king asked him, “Mephibosheth, why did you not come with me?”

26 He replied, "My lord and king, my servant betrayed me. For your servant had said, 'I will saddle a donkey for myself so that I may ride on it and go to the king,' because your servant is lame."

27 He has falsely accused your servant before my lord the king. Yet my lord the king is like an angel of God, discerning good and evil. Therefore, do what seems right and just in your eyes.

28 All of my father’s household were as good as dead in the eyes of my lord the king, yet you have placed your servant among those who eat at your own table. What right, then, do I have to make any further appeal to the king?

29 The king said to him, "Why do you keep talking about this matter? I have already decided: you and Ziba are to divide the land."

30 And Mephibosheth replied to the king, "Let him take everything, for now that my lord the king has returned safely to his own house, it is enough for me."

31 Barzillai the Gileadite came down from Rogelim and crossed the Jordan River with the king to escort him across the Jordan.

32 Now Barzillai was a very old man, eighty years of age. He had supplied the king with provisions while David stayed at Mahanaim, for he was a man of great wealth and influence.

33 The king said to Barzillai, "Come across with me, and I will provide for you and sustain you in Jerusalem."

34 Barzillai replied to the king, "How many years do I have left in my life, that I should go up with the king to Jerusalem?"

35 Today I am eighty years old. Can I still distinguish between good and evil? Can I still savor the taste of what I eat or drink? Can I still hear the voices of men and women singing? Why then should I, your servant, continue to be a burden to my lord the king?

36 Your servant will cross the Jordan with the king for a short distance, but why should the king grant me such a reward for this?

37 Please allow your servant to return, so that I may die in my own city and be buried beside the graves of my father and mother. But as for your servant Chimham, let him go with my lord the king, and do for him whatever you think is best.

38 The king replied, "Chimham will cross over with me, and I will do for him whatever you consider right. Whatever you ask of me, I will do for you."

39 All the people crossed over the Jordan. When the king had crossed over, the king embraced Barzillai, blessed him, and then Barzillai returned to his home.

40 Then the king proceeded to Gilgal, and Chimham accompanied him. All the people of Judah escorted the king, along with half of the people of Israel.

41 Then, all the men of Israel came to the king and said to him, "Why have our brothers, the men of Judah, taken you away secretly and brought you, your household, and all of David’s men across the Jordan?"

42 All the men of Judah responded to the men of Israel, saying, "The king is closely related to us. Why, then, are you so upset about this? Have we taken anything from the king’s provisions? Has he given us any special gift?"

43 The men of Israel responded to the men of Judah, saying, "We have ten shares in the king, and we have a greater claim to David than you do. Why then did you disregard us? Was it not our right to be consulted first in bringing back our king?" But the words of the men of Judah were even harsher than those of the men of Israel.
