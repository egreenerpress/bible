Nehemiah Chapter 12

1 These are the priests and Levites who returned with Zerubbabel, the son of Shealtiel, and Jeshua: Seraiah, Jeremiah, Ezra,

2 Amariah, Malluch, Hattush,

3 Shecaniah, Rehum, and Meremoth.

4 Iddo, Ginnetho, and Abijah.

5 Miamin, Maadiah, Bilgah,

6 Shemaiah, Joiarib, and Jedaiah.

7 Sallu, Amok, Hilkiah, and Jedaiah were the leaders of the priests and their relatives during the time of Jeshua.

8 Furthermore, the Levites—Jeshua, Binnui, Kadmiel, Sherebiah, Judah, and Mattaniah, who was in charge of leading the songs of thanksgiving—along with his fellow Levites.

9 Bakbukiah and Unni, their fellow Levites, stood opposite them during the assigned times of service.

10 Jeshua became the father of Joiakim, Joiakim became the father of Eliashib, and Eliashib became the father of Joiada.

11 And Joiada became the father of Jonathan, and Jonathan became the father of Jaddua.

12 During the time of Joiakim, the leading priests, who were heads of their ancestral families, were as follows: from the family of Seraiah, Meraiah; and from the family of Jeremiah, Hananiah.

13 From the family of Ezra, Meshullam; from the family of Amariah, Jehohanan.

14 From the family of Melicu, Jonathan represented them; from the family of Shebaniah, Joseph represented them.

15 From the family of Harim, Adna; from the family of Meraioth, Helkai.

16 From the family of Iddo, Zechariah; from the family of Ginnethon, Meshullam.

17 From the family of Abijah, Zichri; from the family of Miniamin, Moadiah; from the family of Piltai.

18 From the family of Bilgah, Shammua was chosen; from the family of Shemaiah, Jehonathan was selected.

19 Of the family of Joiarib, Mattenai; of the family of Jedaiah, Uzzi.

20 From the family of Sallai, Kallai; from the family of Amok, Eber.

21 From Hilkiah, Hashabiah; from Jedaiah, Nethaneel.

22 During the time of Eliashib, Joiada, Johanan, and Jaddua, the Levites were recorded as the leaders of their ancestral families. The priests were also recorded, up until the reign of Darius the Persian.

23 The descendants of Levi, who were the leading patriarchs, were recorded in the book of historical records up until the time of Johanan, the son of Eliashib.

24 The leaders of the Levites were Hashabiah, Sherebiah, and Jeshua, the son of Kadmiel, along with their fellow Levites who stood opposite them. They were appointed to lead in praise and thanksgiving, following the command of David, the man of God. Each group took turns in their duties, standing watch opposite one another.

25 Mattaniah, Bakbukiah, Obadiah, Meshullam, Talmon, and Akkub were gatekeepers stationed at the entrances of the gates, responsible for guarding and maintaining security at the thresholds.

26 This took place during the time of Joiakim, the son of Jeshua, who was the son of Jozadak, as well as during the time of Nehemiah, the governor, and Ezra, the priest and scribe.

27 During the dedication ceremony for the wall of Jerusalem, they summoned the Levites from all their locations to bring them to Jerusalem, so they could celebrate the dedication with joy, offering thanksgivings and songs, accompanied by cymbals, harps, and lyres.

28 The descendants of the singers assembled together, coming both from the surrounding countryside near Jerusalem and from the villages of Netophah.

29 Additionally, people came from the house of Gilgal, as well as from the fields of Geba and Azmaveth, for the singers had established villages for themselves in the surrounding areas of Jerusalem.

30 The priests and the Levites consecrated themselves, and they also purified the people, the gates, and the wall.

31 Then I led the leaders of Judah up onto the wall and assigned two large groups to give thanks. One group proceeded to the right along the wall, heading toward the Dung Gate.

32 After them, Hoshaiah and half of the leaders of Judah followed.

33 And Azariah, Ezra, and Meshullam,

34 Judah, Benjamin, Shemaiah, and Jeremiah.

35 Some of the priests’ sons were assigned to play the trumpets: Zechariah, the son of Jonathan, who was the son of Shemaiah, who was the son of Mattaniah, who was the son of Michaiah, who was the son of Zaccur, who was the son of Asaph.

36 And his brothers—Shemaiah, Azarael, Milalai, Gilalai, Maai, Nethaneel, and Judah, along with Hanani—carried the musical instruments of David, the man of God. Ezra the scribe led them.

37 At the Fountain Gate, which was directly in front of them, they ascended the steps leading up to the City of David, following the path along the wall, passing above the house of David, and continuing eastward as far as the Water Gate.

38 The second group of those who gave thanks proceeded in the opposite direction, and I followed them, along with half of the people on the wall. We went from beyond the Tower of the Ovens all the way to the Broad Wall.

39 From above the Gate of Ephraim, and above the Old Gate, and above the Fish Gate, as well as the Tower of Hananeel and the Tower of Meah, all the way to the Sheep Gate, they came to a halt at the Prison Gate.

40 The two groups of those who offered thanksgiving stood in the house of God, along with me and half of the leaders who were with me.

41 The priests—Eliakim, Maaseiah, Miniamin, Michaiah, Elioenai, Zechariah, and Hananiah—were present with trumpets.

42 And Maaseiah, Shemaiah, Eleazar, Uzzi, Jehohanan, Malchijah, Elam, and Ezer were present. The singers raised their voices loudly, with Jezrahiah overseeing them.

43 On that day, they presented abundant offerings and celebrated with great gladness, for God had filled them with immense joy. The wives and children also rejoiced, so that the sound of Jerusalem's joy could be heard far away.

44 At that time, certain individuals were assigned to oversee the storage rooms designated for the treasures, the offerings, the firstfruits, and the tithes. Their responsibility was to collect from the fields of the cities the portions prescribed by the law for the priests and Levites, as the people of Judah were filled with joy because of the priests and Levites who served faithfully.

45 Both the singers and the gatekeepers carried out their duties for their God and the duties of purification, following the commands given by David and his son Solomon.

46 In the days of David and Asaph long ago, there were leaders appointed over the singers, and they composed songs of praise and thanksgiving to God.

47 During the time of Zerubbabel and Nehemiah, all Israel provided daily portions for the singers and gatekeepers, each receiving their allotted share. They also set apart sacred offerings for the Levites, and the Levites, in turn, consecrated these offerings for the descendants of Aaron.
