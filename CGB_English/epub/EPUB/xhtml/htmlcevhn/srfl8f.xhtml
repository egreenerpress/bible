Hebrews Chapter 6

1 Therefore, moving beyond the elementary teachings about Christ, let us press on toward maturity, not laying again the foundation of repentance from lifeless deeds and of faith in God.

2 Concerning the teachings about baptisms, the practice of laying on of hands, the resurrection of the dead, and the eternal judgment.

3 We will proceed with this plan, provided that God allows it.

4 For it is impossible for those who have once been enlightened, who have tasted the heavenly gift, and have shared in the Holy Spirit,

5 And have experienced the goodness of God’s word and the mighty works of the age to come.

6 If they fall away, it becomes impossible to restore them again to repentance, since they are, in effect, re-crucifying the Son of God for themselves and exposing him to public disgrace.

7 For the land that absorbs the frequent rain falling upon it and produces vegetation useful to those who cultivate it receives God's blessing.

8 However, land that produces thorns and thistles is worthless and close to being cursed. In the end, it will be burned.

9 But, dear friends, we are convinced of better things concerning you—things that are connected to salvation—even though we speak in this manner.

10 For God is not unjust; He will not forget your work and the love you have demonstrated for His name as you have served and continue to serve His holy people.

11 We urge each one of you to demonstrate the same earnest commitment, so that you may fully embrace and maintain the certainty of your hope until the very end.

12 Do not become lazy or indifferent, but instead, follow the example of those who, through their faith and steadfast endurance, have received the fulfillment of God's promises.

13 When God made a promise to Abraham, since there was no one greater by whom He could swear, He swore by Himself.

14 He declared, "I will most certainly bless you and greatly increase your number."

15 And so, after he had steadfastly persevered, he received what was promised.

16 Certainly, people swear by someone greater than themselves, and an oath serves as a binding confirmation, putting an end to all disputes.

17 In this matter, God, desiring to demonstrate even more clearly to the heirs of His promise the unchangeable nature of His purpose, confirmed it with an oath.

18 So that by means of two unchangeable realities—in which it is impossible for God to deceive us—we who have sought refuge in Him might be powerfully encouraged to hold firmly to the hope set before us.

19 This hope we possess serves as a secure and steadfast anchor for the soul, firmly fixed and unshakable. It reaches beyond the veil, into the innermost sanctuary where God dwells.

20 The one who has gone before us has entered on our behalf—Jesus, who has been appointed as a high priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek.
