Proverbs Chapter 21

1 The heart of a king is under the control of the LORD, like streams of water; He directs it wherever He desires.

2 A person may consider their own ways to be right, but the LORD examines the intentions of the heart.

3 To act with fairness and uphold justice is more pleasing to the LORD than offering sacrifices.

4 A haughty gaze, an arrogant heart, and the schemes of the wicked are all sinful.

5 The plans of the diligent lead to abundance, but the actions of the hasty lead only to scarcity.

6 Acquiring wealth through deceitful speech is a fleeting and worthless pursuit, like a fleeting vapor pursued by those who are, in truth, seeking their own destruction.

7 The violence of the wicked will bring about their own ruin, for they refuse to act with justice.

8 The conduct of a person can be twisted and perplexing, but the actions of the upright are just and honorable.

9 It is better to live in a small corner on the roof than to share a spacious house with a quarrelsome woman.

10 The heart of the wicked craves wrongdoing; they show no kindness or goodwill toward their neighbor.

11 When the mocker is disciplined, the naive gain wisdom; and when the wise are taught, they acquire understanding.

12 The righteous person carefully observes the household of the wicked, but God brings ruin upon the wicked because of their evil deeds.

13 Whoever refuses to listen to the cries of the poor will themselves cry out, but they will not be answered.

14 A discreetly given gift can calm anger, and a concealed bribe can soothe intense fury.

15 It brings joy to the righteous to act with justice, but ruin awaits those who practice wickedness.

16 The person who strays from the path of wisdom will end up dwelling among those who are spiritually dead.

17 A person who indulges in pleasure will end up in poverty; one who is devoted to wine and luxury will never attain wealth.

18 The wicked will serve as a ransom for the righteous, and the one who breaks the law will be given in place of the upright.

19 It is better to live in a desolate wilderness than to share a home with a quarrelsome and hot-tempered woman.

20 There is valuable treasure and precious oil in the home of the wise, but a foolish person squanders it all.

21 The one who pursues righteousness and kindness will find life, righteousness, and honor.

22 A wise person can overcome a city of the powerful and bring down the strong defenses in which they place their trust.

23 Whoever guards their words and controls their speech protects themselves from many troubles.

24 A proud and arrogant mocker is what he is called—one who acts with overbearing anger.

25 The craving of the lazy person leads to his ruin, for his hands refuse to work.

26 He is consumed by greed all day long, but the righteous person gives generously and without holding back.

27 The offering of the wicked is detestable to the Lord—how much more so when it is brought with evil intent?

28 A false witness will come to ruin, but the one who listens carefully will speak with enduring truth.

29 A wicked person stubbornly persists in their defiance, but the upright person carefully considers and guides their path.

30 No wisdom, no understanding, and no advice can stand against the Lord.

31 The horse is made ready for the day of battle, but true victory and deliverance come from the LORD.
