Psalms Chapter 75

1 (For the director of music, according to the tune "Do Not Destroy," a psalm or song of Asaph.) To you, O God, we give thanks; to you we give thanks. Your name is near, and your marvelous deeds proclaim it.

2 When I gather the assembly, I will administer justice with integrity.

3 The earth and all who dwell upon it are crumbling, but I sustain its very foundations. Selah.

4 I said to the foolish, "Do not act foolishly," and to the wicked, "Do not exalt your power."

5 Do not exalt yourself arrogantly, nor speak with a prideful and defiant attitude.

6 Advancement does not come from the east, nor from the west, nor from the south.

7 However, God is the ultimate judge: He brings down one and raises up another.

8 For the LORD holds a cup in his hand, filled with red wine, blended and fully mixed. He pours it out, but the wicked of the earth will be forced to drain its dregs and drink them to the last drop.

9 I will proclaim it forever; I will sing praises to the God of Jacob.

10 I will cut off all the power of the wicked, but the strength of the righteous will be lifted high.
