Psalms Chapter 65

1 (For the director of music. A psalm and song of David.) O God, in Zion, praise is reserved for you, and to you the vow will be fulfilled.

2 O you who listen to prayer, to you all people will come.

3 My sins overpower me, but you will cleanse us from our transgressions.

4 Blessed is the person you choose and draw near to yourself, allowing them to dwell in your presence. We will be filled with the abundance of your house, the sacredness of your holy temple.

5 Through awe-inspiring acts of justice, you will respond to us, O God of our salvation, you who are the hope of all the farthest reaches of the earth and of those who dwell in distant lands across the sea.

6 He establishes the mountains firmly by His strength, clothed with might.

7 He who quiets the roaring of the seas, the crashing of their waves, and the uproar of the nations.

8 Even those who live in the most distant lands are filled with awe at your signs; you cause the dawn and the dusk to sing for joy.

9 You care for the earth and provide it with water; you abundantly enrich it with the stream of God, which overflows with water. You prepare the grain for the land, having made provision for it in this way.

10 You abundantly water its ridges, settling its furrows; you soften the soil with showers and bless its growth.

11 You crown the year with your abundant blessings; your ways overflow with richness and prosperity.

12 The rain falls upon the wilderness pastures, and the small hills are filled with joy all around.

13 The meadows are adorned with flocks of sheep; the valleys are blanketed with grain. They burst forth in joyful celebration, lifting their voices in song.
