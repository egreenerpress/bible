Genesis Chapter 27

1 When Isaac had grown old and his eyes had become so weak that he could no longer see, he called for Esau, his older son, and said to him, "My son." Esau replied, "Here I am."

2 And he said, "Listen now, I am old, and I do not know when I will die."

3 So now, I ask you, take your weapons—your quiver and your bow—and go out into the open country. Hunt some wild game for me.

4 Prepare for me a delicious meal, the kind I enjoy, and bring it to me so that I may eat. Then, before I die, my soul will bless you.

5 Rebekah overheard Isaac speaking to his son Esau. Esau then went out to the field to hunt game and bring it back.

6 Rebekah spoke to her son Jacob, saying, "Listen, I overheard your father speaking to your brother Esau, saying..."

7 "Prepare some wild game for me and make it into a flavorful dish, so that I may eat it and bless you in the presence of the LORD before I die."

8 Now then, my son, listen to my words and follow the instructions I am giving you.

9 Go now to the flock and bring me two good young goats from there. I will prepare them as a delicious meal for your father, the kind he loves.

10 You shall bring it to your father, so that he may eat and bless you before he dies.

11 Jacob said to his mother Rebekah, "Look, my brother Esau is a hairy man, but I have smooth skin."

12 My father might touch me and realize what I’ve done, and I will appear to him as a deceiver. Then I will bring a curse upon myself instead of a blessing.

13 His mother said to him, "Let the curse fall on me, my son. Just do as I say and go bring them to me."

14 So he went, brought them to his mother, and she prepared a flavorful dish, the kind his father loved.

15 Rebekah took the finest garments of her older son Esau, which were kept in the house, and dressed her younger son Jacob in them.

16 She then took the skins of the young goats and placed them on his hands and on the smooth part of his neck.

17 She handed the flavorful meat and the bread she had prepared to her son Jacob.

18 He approached his father and said, "Father?" His father replied, "Yes, I am here. Who are you, my son?"

19 Jacob said to his father, "I am Esau, your firstborn. I have done as you instructed me. Please, get up, sit, and eat some of the game I have brought, so that you may bless me."

20 Isaac said to his son, “How did you find it so quickly, my son?” He replied, “Because the LORD your God made it happen for me.”

21 Isaac said to Jacob, "Come closer, please, so that I can touch you, my son, and know whether you are truly my son Esau or not."

22 Jacob approached his father Isaac, and Isaac touched him. Then Isaac said, "The voice sounds like Jacob's, but the hands feel like Esau's."

23 He did not recognize him because his hands were hairy, like his brother Esau’s hands. And so he blessed him.

24 And he asked, "Are you truly my son Esau?" And he replied, "I am."

25 And he said, "Bring it here to me, so that I may eat some of my son’s game and then bless you." So he brought it to him, and he ate. Then he brought him wine, and he drank.

26 Then his father Isaac said to him, "Come close now, and kiss me, my son."

27 He approached and kissed him. As he did, he caught the scent of his garments and blessed him, saying, "Ah, the fragrance of my son is like the fragrance of a field that the LORD has blessed."

28 Therefore, may God grant you the dew from heaven, the richness of the earth, and an abundance of grain and wine.

29 May people serve you, and nations bow down before you. Rule over your brothers, and may your mother's sons bow down to you. Cursed be anyone who curses you, and blessed be everyone who blesses you.

30 As soon as Isaac had finished blessing Jacob, and Jacob had just left his father Isaac’s presence, his brother Esau returned from his hunting.

31 He also prepared a delicious meal and brought it to his father. Then he said to him, "Father, please get up and eat some of the game your son has prepared, so that you may bless me with your whole being."

32 And his father Isaac said to him, “Who are you?” He replied, “I am your son, your firstborn, Esau.”

33 Isaac was deeply shaken and said, "Who then was it that hunted game and brought it to me? I ate it all before you came, and I blessed him—and indeed, he will remain blessed."

34 When Esau heard his father's words, he let out a loud and deeply anguished cry, and he said to his father, "Bless me too, my father—please, bless me as well!"

35 And he said, "Your brother came with deceit and has taken away your blessing."

36 And he said, "Is he not rightly named Jacob? For he has deceived me twice: he took away my birthright, and now, look, he has taken away my blessing as well." Then he asked, "Have you not kept any blessing for me?"

37 Isaac responded to Esau, saying, "Listen, I have made him your master, and I have given all his brothers to him as servants. I have provided for him with grain and wine. So now, what can I possibly do for you, my son?"

38 And Esau said to his father, “Do you have only one blessing, my father? Bless me too, my father!” Then Esau raised his voice and wept.

39 And his father Isaac responded to him, saying, "Listen, your home will be in the richness of the earth and the abundance of the dew from heaven above."

40 You will survive by your sword and will serve your brother. But when you grow restless and gain control, you will throw off his yoke from your neck.

41 Esau harbored deep resentment toward Jacob because of the blessing their father had given him. In his heart, Esau thought, "The time of mourning for my father is near; once he is gone, I will kill my brother Jacob."

42 Rebekah was informed of the words spoken by her older son, Esau. She immediately sent for her younger son, Jacob, and said to him, "Listen, your brother Esau is consoling himself with the thought of killing you."

43 Now then, my son, listen to what I say: get up and flee to my brother Laban in Haran.

44 Stay with him for a short while, until your brother's anger subsides.

45 Wait until your brother's anger toward you subsides and he forgets what you have done to him. Then I will send for you and bring you back from there. Why should I lose both of you in a single day?

46 Rebekah said to Isaac, "I am utterly weary of my life because of the Hittite women. If Jacob marries one of the Hittite women, like those who live in this land, what purpose will my life serve?"
