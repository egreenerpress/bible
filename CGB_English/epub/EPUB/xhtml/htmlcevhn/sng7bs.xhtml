Psalms Chapter 2

1 Why do the nations rage and the peoples plot in vain?

2 The rulers of the earth rise up, and the leaders conspire together against the LORD and against his chosen one, declaring,

3 Let us tear apart their restraints and throw off their bonds from us.

4 The One who reigns in heaven will laugh; the Lord will hold them in contempt.

5 Then he will address them in his anger, and in his fierce displeasure he will terrify them.

6 Yet I have established my king upon my holy mountain, Zion.

7 I will proclaim the decree: the LORD has said to me, "You are my Son; today I have become your Father."

8 Request of me, and I will grant you the nations as your inheritance, and the farthest reaches of the earth as your possession.

9 You will shatter them with an iron scepter; you will crush them completely, like a potterâ€™s vessel smashed to pieces.

10 Therefore, act wisely now, you kings; take heed, you rulers of the earth.

11 Worship the LORD with reverence, and celebrate with awe-filled humility.

12 Show reverence and loyalty to the Son, so that he does not become angry, and you are destroyed in your rebellion, for his wrath can flare up quickly. How blessed are all who take refuge in him!
