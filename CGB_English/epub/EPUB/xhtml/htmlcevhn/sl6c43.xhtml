Zechariah Chapter 11

1 Open your gates, O Lebanon, so that the fire may consume your cedar trees.

2 Wail, fir tree, for the cedar has fallen; the mighty ones have been laid waste. Cry out, you oaks of Bashan, for the dense forest has been brought down.

3 The shepherds cry out in anguish, for their splendor has been devastated. The young lions roar fiercely, for the majesty of the Jordan has been laid waste.

4 This is what the LORD my God says: "Take care of the flock marked for slaughter."

5 Those who own them kill them and feel no guilt, and those who sell them say, "Praise the LORD, for I have become wealthy!" Even their own shepherds show them no compassion.

6 The LORD declares, "I will no longer show compassion to the people of this land. Instead, I will hand each person over to the power of their neighbor and to the authority of their king. They will bring devastation upon the land, and I will not rescue them from their hands."

7 I will care for the flock marked for slaughter, especially you, the oppressed and vulnerable among them. I took two staffs for myself: one I named *Favor*, and the other I named *Unity*. And I tended the flock.

8 I also removed three shepherds within a single month, for I grew weary of them, and they, in turn, despised me.

9 Then I said, "I will no longer shepherd you. Let the dying die, and let the one destined for destruction be destroyed. As for the rest, let them devour one another's flesh."

10 I took my staff, named Favor, and broke it in two, thereby annulling the covenant I had established with all the people.

11 On that day, it was shattered, and thus the humble among the flock who faithfully followed me recognized that this was indeed the word of the LORD.

12 I said to them, "If you consider it right, give me my payment; but if not, then withhold it." So they weighed out thirty pieces of silver as my payment.

13 The LORD said to me, "Throw it to the potter"â€”the magnificent value at which they valued me. So I took the thirty pieces of silver and threw them to the potter in the house of the LORD.

14 Then I took my other staff, named Bands, and broke it in two, symbolizing the breaking of the bond of unity between Judah and Israel.

15 The LORD said to me, "Take for yourself the tools of a worthless shepherd."

16 For behold, I will raise up a shepherd in the land who will not care for those who are lost, nor seek the young, nor heal the injured, nor nourish the healthy. Instead, he will devour the flesh of the fattened ones and tear their hooves to pieces.

17 Disaster will come upon the worthless shepherd who abandons the flock! The sword will strike his arm and his right eye: his arm will wither completely, and his right eye will be completely blinded.
