Luke Chapter 20

1 Now it happened that on one of those days, as Jesus was teaching the people in the temple and proclaiming the good news, the chief priests, the scribes, and the elders confronted him.

2 They spoke to him, saying, "Tell us, by what authority are you doing these things? And who is the one who gave you this authority?"

3 He replied to them, "I will also ask you one question; answer me:"

4 The baptism performed by John—was it authorized by God, or was it merely a human institution?

5 They discussed the matter among themselves, saying, "If we say, 'From heaven,' he will ask, 'Why then did you not believe him?'"

6 But if we say, 'From men,' all the people will stone us, because they are convinced that John was a prophet.

7 And they replied that they were unable to determine its origin.

8 And Jesus said to them, "Nor will I tell you by what authority I do these things."

9 Then Jesus began to tell the people this parable: “A certain man planted a vineyard, leased it to tenant farmers, and then went away to a distant country for a long time.”

10 At the appointed time, he sent a servant to the tenants of the vineyard, expecting them to give him a portion of the vineyard's produce. But the tenants seized the servant, beat him, and sent him away empty-handed.

11 Once more, he sent another servant, but they beat him as well, treated him with disgrace, and sent him away with nothing.

12 Once more, he sent a third servant, but they also wounded him and threw him out.

13 Then the owner of the vineyard said, “What should I do? I will send my beloved son. Perhaps they will respect him when they see him.”

14 But when the tenant farmers saw him, they discussed the matter among themselves, saying, “This is the heir. Let’s kill him so that the inheritance will become ours.”

15 So they threw him out of the vineyard and killed him. What, then, will the owner of the vineyard do to them?

16 He will come and put those tenants to death, then give the vineyard to others." When they heard this, they exclaimed, "May such a thing never happen!"

17 He looked at them and said, "What then does this Scripture mean: 'The stone that the builders rejected has become the cornerstone'?"

18 Whoever falls on that stone will be shattered; but whoever it falls upon will be crushed to dust.

19 At that very moment, the chief priests and the teachers of the law sought to seize him, but they were afraid of the crowd. They understood that he had told this parable against them.

20 They kept a close watch on him and sent out spies who pretended to be sincere and upright men. Their goal was to catch him in his words so that they could hand him over to the power and authority of the governor.

21 They questioned Him, saying, "Teacher, we know that You speak and teach truthfully, and that You show no partiality to anyone, but teach the ways of God with complete integrity."

22 Is it permissible for us to pay taxes to Caesar, or not?

23 But Jesus saw through their cunning and said to them, "Why are you trying to test me?"

24 Show me a coin. Whose image and inscription are on it?" They replied, "Caesar's."

25 And he said to them, "Give back to Caesar what belongs to Caesar, and to God what belongs to God."

26 They were unable to trap him in his words in front of the people. They were astonished by his response and remained silent.

27 Then some Sadducees, who deny the reality of resurrection, came to him and posed a question.

28 They said, "Teacher, Moses wrote to us that if a man’s brother dies, leaving a wife but no children, the brother should marry the widow and raise up offspring for his brother."

29 There were seven brothers: the first one married a wife but died without having any children.

30 Then the second brother married her, but he also died without having any children.

31 Then the third brother married her, and in the same way, all seven brothers married her, one after the other. None of them had any children with her, and eventually, they all died.

32 Finally, the woman also died.

33 Therefore, in the resurrection, whose wife will she be? For all seven had her as their wife.

34 Jesus responded to them, saying, "The people of this present age marry and are given in marriage."

35 But those who are deemed worthy to attain that age to come and the resurrection from the dead will neither marry nor be given in marriage.

36 They will no longer be subject to death, for they will be like the angels and will be God’s children, since they are children of the resurrection.

37 The fact that the dead are raised is demonstrated even by Moses in the account of the burning bush, where he refers to the Lord as the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob.

38 For He is not the God of the dead, but of the living, for in His presence all are alive.

39 Then some of the scribes responded, saying, "Teacher, you have spoken rightly."

40 After that, they no longer dared to ask him any more questions.

41 And He said to them, "How can people say that the Christ is David’s son?"

42 David himself declares in the book of Psalms: "The LORD said to my Lord, 'Sit at my right hand.'"

43 Until I make your enemies a place for your feet to rest.

44 David therefore calls him 'Lord,' so how can he be his son?

45 Then, in the presence of all the people, he said to his disciples,

46 Be on guard against the scribes, who seek to walk around in long, flowing robes, who delight in being greeted with respect in the marketplaces, who desire the most prominent seats in the synagogues, and who covet the places of honor at banquets.

47 These are the ones who exploit widows by taking over their homes, and then make a show of offering lengthy prayers to appear righteous. Such people will face a more severe judgment.
