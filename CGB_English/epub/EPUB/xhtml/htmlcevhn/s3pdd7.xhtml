Exodus Chapter 2

1 A man from the tribe of Levi went and married a woman who was also from the tribe of Levi.

2 The woman became pregnant and gave birth to a son. When she saw that he was a beautiful and healthy child, she hid him for three months.

3 When she could no longer conceal him, she prepared a basket made of papyrus reeds, coating it with tar and pitch to make it waterproof. She placed the child inside and set the basket among the reeds along the bank of the river.

4 And his sister stood at a distance, watching to see what would happen to him.

5 The daughter of Pharaoh went down to the river to bathe, while her attendants walked along the riverbank. When she noticed the basket among the reeds, she sent one of her servants to retrieve it.

6 When she opened it, she saw the child, and the baby was crying. Filled with compassion, she said, "This must be one of the Hebrew children."

7 Then his sister said to Pharaoh’s daughter, “Should I go and call for a nursing woman from the Hebrew women to nurse the child for you?”

8 Pharaoh’s daughter said to her, “Go.” So the young woman went and summoned the child’s mother.

9 Pharaoh’s daughter said to her, “Take this child and care for him for me, and I will pay you for your service.” So the woman took the child and nursed him.

10 The child grew, and when the time came, his mother brought him to Pharaoh’s daughter, and he became her son. She named him Moses, saying, “Because I drew him out of the water.”

11 During that time, when Moses had grown to adulthood, he went out to visit his fellow Israelites and witnessed the heavy burdens they were forced to bear. As he observed their suffering, he saw an Egyptian man striking a Hebrew, one of his own people.

12 He glanced around in every direction, and when he saw that no one was present, he struck down the Egyptian and concealed his body in the sand.

13 When he went out the next day, he saw two Hebrew men fighting. He said to the one who was in the wrong, "Why are you striking your fellow Israelite?"

14 And he said, "Who appointed you as a ruler and judge over us? Are you planning to kill me, just as you killed the Egyptian?" Then Moses became afraid and thought, "This matter must surely be known."

15 When Pharaoh learned of this matter, he sought to kill Moses. But Moses fled from Pharaoh’s presence and settled in the land of Midian, where he sat down by a well.

16 The priest of Midian had seven daughters. They came to draw water and filled the troughs to provide water for their father’s flock.

17 The shepherds arrived and chased them away, but Moses rose up, came to their aid, and watered their flock.

18 When they returned to their father Reuel, he asked, "Why have you come back so early today?"

19 They replied, "An Egyptian rescued us from the shepherds and even drew water for us and watered the flock."

20 And he said to his daughters, "Where is he? Why did you leave the man behind? Invite him here, so he can eat with us."

21 Moses agreed to stay and live with the man, who then gave his daughter Zipporah to Moses in marriage.

22 She gave birth to a son, and he named him Gershom, saying, "I have been a foreigner in a foreign land."

23 After some time had passed, the king of Egypt died. The Israelites groaned under the weight of their slavery and cried out in distress. Their cries for help rose up to God because of their oppression.

24 God listened to their cries of distress, and He called to mind His covenant with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

25 And God turned His attention to the Israelites, and He acknowledged them with care and concern.
