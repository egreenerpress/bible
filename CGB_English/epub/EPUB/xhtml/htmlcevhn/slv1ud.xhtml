Romans Chapter 15

1 We who are strong in faith ought to support the weaknesses of those who are less strong, rather than seeking to please ourselves.

2 Let each of us strive to do what is good for our neighbor, aiming to build them up and encourage their growth.

3 For even Christ did not seek to please himself; rather, as it is written, "The insults of those who insulted you have fallen on me."

4 Everything that was written in the past was written to teach us, so that through the endurance and encouragement provided by the Scriptures, we might hold on to hope.

5 May the God of endurance and encouragement enable you to live in harmony with one another, following the example of Christ Jesus.

6 So that you may unite in thought and speech to honor God, the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ.

7 Therefore, accept one another, just as Christ has accepted you, all for the glory of God.

8 Now I affirm that Christ Jesus became a servant to the circumcised, demonstrating God's faithfulness, in order to fulfill the promises made to our ancestors.

9 So that the Gentiles may glorify God for His mercy, as it is written: "For this reason, I will praise You among the Gentiles and sing to Your name."

10 And again it is said, "Rejoice, you nations, together with His people."

11 Once more, it is written: "Praise the Lord, all you nations; and let all peoples honor him."

12 And again, Isaiah says, "There shall come a descendant of Jesse, and he will rise to rule over the nations; in him the nations will place their hope."

13 May the God of hope fill you completely with joy and peace as you trust in him, so that you may overflow with hope by the power of the Holy Spirit.

14 I am fully convinced, my brothers and sisters, that you yourselves are filled with goodness, complete in all knowledge, and well-equipped to instruct and encourage one another.

15 Brothers and sisters, I have written to you with a certain boldness, in part to remind you of the truths you already know, because of the grace that God has given me.

16 I have been appointed as a servant of Jesus Christ to the Gentiles, proclaiming the gospel of God, so that the Gentiles might be presented as an acceptable offering, made holy by the Holy Spirit.

17 Therefore, I have reason to boast in Jesus Christ regarding the things that relate to God.

18 I will not presume to speak of anything except what Christ has accomplished through me, in bringing the Gentiles to obedience—both through my words and my actions.

19 Through powerful signs and miracles, by the authority of the Spirit of God, I have fully proclaimed the gospel of Christ, spreading it from Jerusalem all the way to the region of Illyricum.

20 Indeed, I have made it my aim to proclaim the good news, not in places where Christ’s name was already known, so that I would not be building on someone else’s foundation.

21 But as it is written: "Those to whom no message was sent will see, and those who have not heard will understand."

22 For this reason, I have been greatly hindered from coming to you.

23 But now, since I no longer have any opportunity to work in these regions, and because I have longed for many years to visit you,

24 Whenever I travel to Spain, I will visit you. I hope to see you during my journey and to have your assistance in reaching my destination, after I have first enjoyed spending some time with you.

25 But now I am going to Jerusalem to serve the believers there.

26 For the people of Macedonia and Achaia have been pleased to make a specific contribution for the impoverished believers who are in Jerusalem.

27 They have indeed been pleased to do so, and they are indebted to them. For if the Gentiles have shared in the spiritual blessings of the Jews, then it is only right that the Gentiles should also serve them by meeting their material needs.

28 Therefore, when I have completed this task and have safely delivered this contribution to them, I will travel through your region on my way to Spain.

29 I am confident that when I come to you, I will arrive with the full measure of the blessing that comes from the gospel of Christ.

30 I urge you, my brothers and sisters, by the authority of the Lord Jesus Christ and through the love that comes from the Spirit, to join me earnestly in prayer to God on my behalf.

31 I pray that I may be rescued from those in Judea who do not believe, and that the service I am bringing to Jerusalem may be received favorably by the saints.

32 So that I may come to you with joy, according to God’s will, and together with you find rest and renewal.

33 May the God who brings peace be with all of you. Amen.
