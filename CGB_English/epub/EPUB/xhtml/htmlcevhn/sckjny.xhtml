I Timothy Chapter 4

1 The Spirit clearly declares that in the final days, some will abandon the faith, turning their attention to deceptive spirits and teachings inspired by demons.

2 They speak deceitful lies while pretending to be righteous, and their consciences have been hardened, as if burned with a hot iron.

3 They prohibit marriage and require abstinence from certain foods that God created to be received with gratitude by those who believe and understand the truth.

4 Every creation of God is good, and nothing should be rejected if it is received with gratitude and thanksgiving.

5 For it is made holy through the word of God and prayer.

6 If you remind the fellow believers of these things, you will prove to be a good servant of Jesus Christ, continually nurtured by the teachings of faith and sound instruction, which you have faithfully followed.

7 However, reject irreverent and foolish tales, and instead devote yourself to pursuing godliness.

8 Physical training has some value, but godliness is valuable in every way, holding promise for both the present life and the life to come.

9 This is a trustworthy statement and deserving of full acceptance.

10 This is why we toil and endure criticism, because we place our hope in the living God, who is the Savior of all people, especially of those who believe.

11 Instruct and teach these things clearly.

12 Do not let anyone look down on you because of your youth. Instead, set an example for the believers in your speech, conduct, love, spirit, faith, and purity.

13 Until I arrive, devote yourself to reading Scripture, to encouraging others, and to teaching sound doctrine.

14 Do not disregard the spiritual gift that is within you, which was given to you through prophetic utterance when the elders laid their hands on you.

15 Reflect deeply on these matters; devote yourself entirely to them, so that your progress may be evident to everyone.

16 Be diligent in caring for yourself and in upholding the teachings; remain steadfast in them. By doing so, you will ensure your own salvation and also bring salvation to those who listen to you.
