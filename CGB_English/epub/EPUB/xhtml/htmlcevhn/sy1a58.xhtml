Acts Chapter 6

1 During that time, as the number of disciples continued to grow, a complaint arose among the Greek-speaking believers against the Hebrew-speaking believers. The issue was that their widows were being overlooked in the daily distribution of food and support.

2 Then the twelve apostles gathered the entire group of disciples together and said, "It is not right for us to neglect the ministry of God's word in order to serve meals."

3 Therefore, brothers, select from among yourselves seven men who are well-respected, filled with the Holy Spirit and wisdom, whom we may appoint to oversee this responsibility.

4 However, we will devote ourselves fully to prayer and to the service of proclaiming the message.

5 The proposal pleased the entire assembly, and they selected Stephen, a man full of faith and the Holy Spirit, along with Philip, Prochorus, Nicanor, Timon, Parmenas, and Nicolas, a convert from Antioch.

6 They presented these men to the apostles, who prayed over them and then laid their hands on them.

7 The message of God spread widely, and the number of followers of Jesus grew significantly in Jerusalem. Moreover, a large group of priests embraced the faith and became obedient to it.

8 Stephen, filled with faith and divine power, performed extraordinary signs and miracles among the people.

9 Then some members of the synagogue, known as the Synagogue of the Freedmen (including Cyrenians, Alexandrians, and people from Cilicia and Asia), began to argue with Stephen.

10 They could not withstand the wisdom and the Spirit with which he spoke.

11 Then they secretly persuaded certain men to say, "We have heard him speak blasphemous words against Moses and against God."

12 They incited the people, the elders, and the teachers of the law, and they confronted him, seized him, and brought him before the council.

13 They brought forward false witnesses who claimed, "This man never stops speaking against this sacred place and the law, using blasphemous words."

14 For we have heard him declare that this Jesus of Nazareth will destroy this holy place and alter the traditions that Moses handed down to us.

15 All those who were seated in the council fixed their gaze on him, and they saw that his face was like the face of an angel.
