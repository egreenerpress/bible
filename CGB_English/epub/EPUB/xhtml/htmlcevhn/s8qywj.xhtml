I Chronicles Chapter 27

1 Now, the Israelites were organized according to their numbers, including the heads of families, the commanders of thousands and hundreds, and their officers who served the king in various duties. These groups were assigned to divisions that rotated monthly throughout the year, with each division consisting of twenty-four thousand men.

2 Jashobeam, the son of Zabdiel, was in charge of the first division for the first month. Under his command were twenty-four thousand men.

3 From the descendants of Perez came the leader of all the army commanders for the first month.

4 During the second month, Dodai the Ahohite was in charge, and Mikloth was the leader under his command. In his division, there were also twenty-four thousand men.

5 The third commander of the army for the third month was Benaiah, the son of Jehoiada, who was a leading priest. In his division, there were twenty-four thousand men.

6 This is Benaiah, who was a mighty warrior among the Thirty and even more distinguished than the Thirty. His son Ammizabad served under him in his division.

7 The commander for the fourth month was Asahel, the brother of Joab, and after him, his son Zebadiah took command. In his division, there were twenty-four thousand men.

8 The commander for the fifth month was Shamhuth the Izrahite, and his division consisted of twenty-four thousand men.

9 The commander for the sixth month was Ira, the son of Ikkesh, from Tekoa. He was in charge of a division of twenty-four thousand men.

10 The commander for the seventh month was Helez the Pelonite, from the descendants of Ephraim. His division consisted of twenty-four thousand men.

11 The commander for the eighth month was Sibbecai the Hushathite, from the clan of Zerah. In his division, there were twenty-four thousand men.

12 The commander for the ninth month was Abiezer the Anetothite, from the tribe of Benjamin. In his division, there were twenty-four thousand men.

13 The commander for the tenth month was Maharai the Netophathite, from the clan of Zerah. In his division, there were twenty-four thousand men.

14 The commander for the eleventh month was Benaiah the Pirathonite, from the tribe of Ephraim. He led a division of twenty-four thousand men.

15 The commander for the twelfth month, assigned to the twelfth division, was Heldai the Netophathite, a descendant of Othniel. His division consisted of twenty-four thousand men.

16 Additionally, concerning the leadership of the tribes of Israel: Eliezer, the son of Zichri, was the chief officer over the Reubenites; and Shephatiah, the son of Maachah, was the chief officer over the Simeonites.

17 From the Levites, Hashabiah son of Kemuel; and from the descendants of Aaron, Zadok.

18 From the tribe of Judah, Elihu, one of David's relatives; from the tribe of Issachar, Omri, the son of Michael.

19 From the tribe of Zebulun, there was Ishmaiah, the son of Obadiah; and from the tribe of Naphtali, there was Jerimoth, the son of Azriel.

20 From the descendants of Ephraim, Hoshea the son of Azaziah; and from the half-tribe of Manasseh, Joel the son of Pedaiah.

21 From the half-tribe of Manasseh in Gilead, there was Iddo, the son of Zechariah; and from the tribe of Benjamin, there was Jaasiel, the son of Abner.

22 From the tribe of Dan, Azareel son of Jeroham. These were the leaders of the tribes of Israel.

23 However, David did not count those who were twenty years old or younger, for the LORD had promised to make Israel as numerous as the stars in the sky.

24 Joab, the son of Zeruiah, started to take a census, but he did not complete it, for God’s anger was stirred against Israel because of it. As a result, the final count was never recorded in the official records of King David’s reign.

25 Azmaveth, the son of Adiel, was in charge of the king’s treasuries. Jehonathan, the son of Uzziah, oversaw the storehouses located in the fields, the cities, the villages, and the fortresses.

26 Ezri, the son of Chelub, was in charge of those who worked in the fields, overseeing the cultivation of the land.

27 Shimei the Ramathite was in charge of the vineyards, and Zabdi the Shiphmite was responsible for overseeing the produce of the vineyards that was stored in the wine cellars.

28 Baal-hanan the Gederite was in charge of the olive trees and the sycamore trees in the lowland areas, while Joash was responsible for overseeing the storage of oil.

29 Shitrai the Sharonite was in charge of the herds that grazed in Sharon, and Shaphat son of Adlai was responsible for the herds in the valleys.

30 Obil the Ishmaelite was in charge of the camels, and Jehdeiah the Meronothite was responsible for the donkeys.

31 Jaziz the Hagerite was in charge of the flocks. All these men were the overseers of King David’s property.

32 Jonathan, David’s uncle, was a wise counselor and a scribe. Jehiel, the son of Hachmoni, was entrusted with the care of the king’s sons.

33 Ahithophel served as the king’s advisor, and Hushai the Archite was the king’s close friend.

34 After Ahithophel came Jehoiada, the son of Benaiah, and Abiathar. Joab was the commander of the king’s army.
