Job Chapter 20

1 Then Zophar the Naamathite replied and said,

2 Therefore, my thoughts compel me to respond, and for this reason, I am eager to speak.

3 I have listened to the rebuke that shames me, and the spirit of my insight compels me to respond.

4 Have you not known this from ancient times, ever since humanity was established on the earth?

5 Does not the triumph of the wicked quickly fade, and the joy of the hypocrite last only a moment?

6 Even if his greatness ascends to the heavens and his pride reaches to the clouds,

7 Yet he will be destroyed forever, like his own waste; those who once saw him will ask, "Where has he gone?"

8 He will vanish like a fleeting dream, never to be found again; indeed, he will be swept away like a nighttime vision.

9 The eye that once saw him will see him no longer; nor will his place ever look upon him again.

10 His descendants will seek to help the poor, and his own hands will give back what was taken.

11 His bones are saturated with the guilt of his youthful sins, and they will rest with him in the grave.

12 Even if wrongdoing seems pleasurable to him, even if he savors it and keeps it hidden in his mouthâ€”

13 Even if he holds it back and does not let it go, but keeps it hidden in his mouth,

14 Yet the food in his stomach turns sour; it becomes the venom of serpents within him.

15 He has greedily devoured wealth, but he will be forced to disgorge it; God will drive it out from within him.

16 He will drink the venom of serpents; the fangs of a viper will bring about his death.

17 He will not see the rivers, the streams, or the flowing brooks of honey and cream.

18 He must return what he has gained through his labor and cannot keep it for himself; the restitution will be made according to the value of his possessions, and he will find no joy in it.

19 Because he has oppressed and abandoned the poor; because he has forcefully seized a house he did not build.

20 Certainly, he will find no rest within himself; he will not be able to keep what he longed for.

21 None of his food will remain, and no one will seek after his possessions.

22 When he is at the height of his prosperity, he will find himself in distress; every hand of the wicked will rise against him.

23 When he is about to satisfy his hunger, God will unleash the fury of His wrath upon him, pouring it down on him even as he eats.

24 He will try to escape from the iron weapon, but the steel-tipped bow will pierce him through.

25 The sword is drawn and emerges from its sheath; indeed, the gleaming blade comes forth from his innermost being. Terrors overwhelm him.

26 All that is hidden in darkness will be exposed in his secret places; a fire that no one has kindled will consume him. Disaster will come upon whoever remains in his dwelling.

27 The sky will expose his wrongdoing, and the earth will rise up to testify against him.

28 The wealth of his household will vanish, and his possessions will be swept away on the day of his wrath.

29 This is the fate assigned to the wicked by God, the inheritance decreed for them by the Almighty.
