Job Chapter 8

1 Then Bildad the Shuhite replied and said,

2 How long will you continue to speak like this? How long will the words from your mouth be as forceful and empty as a mighty wind?

3 Does God distort what is right? Or does the Almighty twist what is just?

4 If your children have sinned against Him, and He has rejected them because of their rebellion;

5 If you earnestly seek God early and plead with the Almighty,

6 If you were truly pure and upright, surely God would rise up on your behalf and cause the place where your righteousness dwells to flourish.

7 Though your beginnings were humble, your future will flourish and grow abundantly.

8 I urge you to consider the generations of old, and diligently examine the wisdom of their ancestors.

9 For we are merely creatures of a day, knowing nothing, because our time on earth is fleeting and insubstantial, like a passing shadow.

10 Will they not instruct you, speak to you, and share the sincere thoughts of their hearts?

11 Can the reed grow without marshland? Can the bulrush thrive without water?

12 While it is still fresh and not yet cut down, it withers faster than any other plant.

13 This is the fate of all who forget God; the hope of the hypocrite will come to nothing.

14 Their hope will be utterly destroyed, and their confidence will be as fragile as a spider's web.

15 He will rely on his household for support, but it will not remain stable; he will cling to it tightly, but it will not last.

16 He flourishes like a fresh plant under the sun, and his branches spread out abundantly in his garden.

17 His roots are entwined around the rocky mound, and he looks upon the place of stones.

18 If God removes someone from their place, even their own dwelling will disown them, saying, "I have never known you."

19 Look, this is the fleeting happiness of his lifeâ€™s path, and from the earth, others will rise to take his place.

20 Indeed, God will not reject a blameless person, nor will He support those who do evil.

21 Until he fills your mouth with laughter and your lips with shouts of joy.

22 Those who oppose you will be covered in disgrace, and the abode of the wicked will be utterly destroyed.
