I Chronicles Chapter 13

1 David sought advice from the commanders of the thousands and the hundreds, as well as from every prominent leader.

2 David addressed the entire assembly of Israel, saying, "If this proposal seems good to you and is in accordance with the will of the LORD our God, let us send word to the rest of our people throughout all the regions of Israel, including the priests and Levites in their towns and surrounding areas, inviting them to join us here."

3 Let us bring the ark of our God back to us, for we did not seek guidance from it during the days of Saul.

4 The entire assembly agreed to do as proposed, for the matter seemed just and proper in the view of all the people.

5 So David assembled all Israel, from the Shihor River in Egypt to the entrance of Hamath, to bring the ark of God from Kiriath-jearim.

6 David, along with all Israel, went up to Baalah, that is, Kirjath-jearim, which belongs to Judah, to bring up from there the ark of God, the LORD, who is enthroned between the cherubim and whose name is proclaimed over it.

7 They transported the ark of God on a new cart from the house of Abinadab, with Uzza and Ahio guiding the cart.

8 David and all of Israel celebrated before God with great enthusiasm, accompanied by singing and the music of harps, lyres, tambourines, cymbals, and trumpets.

9 When they arrived at the threshing floor of Chidon, Uzza reached out his hand to steady the ark because the oxen had stumbled.

10 The LORD's anger burned against Uzza, and He struck him down because he reached out his hand to touch the ark. Uzza died there in the presence of God.

11 David was deeply troubled because the LORD had struck Uzzah down. Because of this, that place has been called Perez-uzzah to this day.

12 And David was filled with fear before God that day, saying, "How can I bring the ark of God to my own dwelling?"

13 So David did not bring the ark to his own city, the City of David, but instead took it to the house of Obed-edom the Gittite.
