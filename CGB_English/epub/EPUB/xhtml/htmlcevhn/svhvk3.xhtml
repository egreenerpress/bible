Proverbs Chapter 17

1 It is better to have a simple piece of bread with peace and quiet than a house filled with feasting and conflict.

2 A wise servant will gain authority over a disgraceful son and will share in the inheritance alongside the family members.

3 The crucible is used to refine silver, and the furnace to purify gold, but the LORD tests the motives of the heart.

4 A person who does evil pays attention to deceitful words, and a liar listens eagerly to malicious speech.

5 Whoever mocks the poor insults their Creator, and those who rejoice over another's misfortune will not escape punishment.

6 Grandchildren are the pride and honor of the elderly, and parents are the pride and joy of their children.

7 Eloquent words are not fitting for a fool; even more so, deceitful speech is unbecoming of a leader.

8 A gift is like a precious gem in the eyes of its possessor; wherever it is directed, it brings success.

9 Whoever conceals an offense promotes love, but whoever brings up a past wrong drives close friends apart.

10 A single rebuke makes a deeper impression on a person of understanding than a hundred lashes on a fool.

11 A wicked person is constantly seeking to stir up rebellion; as a result, a harsh messenger will be sent to confront him.

12 It would be better for a man to encounter a bear whose cubs have been taken away than to face a fool caught up in their foolishness.

13 Whoever repays good with evil, evil will never leave their household.

14 The start of conflict is like the first breach in a dam that releases water: so, abandon quarreling before it escalates and becomes uncontrollable.

15 Whoever declares the wicked to be innocent, and whoever condemns the righteousâ€”both of them are detestable to the LORD.

16 Why does a fool have money in hand to gain wisdom, when he has no desire or inclination for it?

17 A true friend shows love in every circumstance, and a brother is meant to stand by you in times of hardship.

18 A person who lacks understanding hastily shakes hands in agreement, pledging to guarantee a debt or obligation in the presence of a friend.

19 Whoever delights in conflict loves wrongdoing, and whoever builds their pride high is seeking ruin.

20 A person with a deceitful heart finds no good, and one who speaks with a twisted tongue falls into trouble.

21 The one who fathers a fool brings grief upon himself, and the father of a fool finds no joy.

22 A joyful heart brings healing like a remedy, but a crushed spirit drains the strength from the body.

23 A corrupt person secretly offers a bribe to distort the course of justice.

24 Wisdom is within reach of those who have understanding, but the eyes of a fool wander aimlessly to the farthest corners of the earth.

25 A foolish child brings sorrow to his father and deep anguish to the mother who gave him life.

26 It is also wrong to punish the innocent, or to strike down leaders who act with integrity.

27 A person who possesses knowledge exercises restraint in speech, and one who has understanding maintains a calm and noble demeanor.

28 Even a fool, when he remains silent, is considered wise; and the one who keeps his mouth closed is regarded as a person of understanding.
