Colossians Chapter 1

1 Paul, an apostle of Jesus Christ by the will of God, and Timothy our brother,

2 To the holy and faithful brothers and sisters in Christ who are in Colossae: May grace and peace be yours from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

3 We give thanks to God, the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, and we continually pray for you.

4 Since we have heard about your faith in Christ Jesus and the love you have for all God’s people,

5 For the hope that is stored up for you in heaven, which you previously heard about through the message of truth, the gospel.

6 This message has reached you, just as it has spread throughout the entire world. It is bearing fruit and growing, just as it has among you, from the day you first heard it and came to truly understand the grace of God.

7 You have also learned from Epaphras, our beloved fellow servant, who is a faithful minister of Christ on your behalf.

8 He has also made known to us your love, which is inspired by the Spirit.

9 For this reason, we too, from the moment we heard about you, have not stopped praying for you. We continually ask that you may be filled with the knowledge of God’s will, accompanied by all wisdom and spiritual insight.

10 Live in a manner that is worthy of the Lord, fully seeking to please Him in every way. Bear fruit in every good work and continually grow in your understanding and knowledge of God.

11 Empowered with all strength, in accordance with his magnificent might, so that you may endure with complete patience and steadfast perseverance, accompanied by joy.

12 We give thanks to the Father, who has qualified us to share in the inheritance of the saints in the realm of light.

13 He has rescued us from the dominion of darkness and brought us into the kingdom of His beloved Son.

14 In Him, we have been set free through His blood, which brings the forgiveness of our sins.

15 He is the visible representation of the invisible God, the one who holds preeminence over all creation.

16 Through him, all things were created—those in heaven and those on earth, both visible and invisible, whether thrones, dominions, rulers, or authorities. Everything was created by him and for him.

17 He existed before all things, and through him all things are held together.

18 He is the head of the body, which is the church. He is the source, the first to rise from the dead, so that in everything he might hold the highest place.

19 It was the Father's will that in Him all completeness should reside.

20 And through the blood of His cross, He established peace, reconciling all things to Himself—whether things on earth or things in heaven—by means of Him.

21 And you, who were once estranged and hostile in your thoughts because of your evil deeds, have now been reconciled by Him.

22 Through His physical body and by His death, He has made it possible to present you as holy, blameless, and beyond reproach in His presence.

23 If you remain steadfast and firmly established in the faith, not shifting away from the hope of the gospel that you have heard—the same gospel that has been proclaimed to every creature under heaven—and of which I, Paul, have been made a servant.

24 I now rejoice in my sufferings for your sake, and I am completing in my own flesh what remains of Christ’s afflictions on behalf of his body, which is the church.

25 I have been appointed as a servant of this message, in accordance with the stewardship entrusted to me by God for your benefit, to fully proclaim the word of God.

26 The hidden truth that was concealed throughout past ages and generations has now been revealed to His holy people.

27 To whom God has chosen to reveal the immeasurable wealth of the glory of this mystery among the nations—which is Christ dwelling within you, the assurance of future glory.

28 We proclaim Him, admonishing everyone and instructing everyone with all wisdom, so that we may present everyone fully mature in Christ Jesus.

29 I also work diligently toward this goal, striving in accordance with His power, which works powerfully within me.
