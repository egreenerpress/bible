I Corinthians Chapter 13

1 Even if I speak with the languages of humans and of angels, but do not have love, I am nothing more than a noisy gong or a clanging cymbal.

2 Even if I have the gift of prophecy and can comprehend all mysteries and possess all knowledge, and even if I have such complete faith that I could move mountains, but do not have love, I am nothing.

3 Even if I were to give away all my possessions to feed the poor, and even if I were to surrender my body to be burned, but did not have love, it would be of no benefit to me at all.

4 Love is patient and kind; love does not envy; love does not boast, nor is it prideful.

5 Does not act improperly, does not pursue selfish interests, is not easily angered, and does not dwell on wrongdoing.

6 It does not take pleasure in wrongdoing, but finds joy in what is true and right.

7 It patiently endures all circumstances, maintains unwavering faith in all situations, holds steadfast hope in all things, and perseveres through every trial.

8 Love never fails. But as for prophecies, they will come to an end; as for tongues, they will cease; as for knowledge, it will pass away.

9 For we understand only in part, and we prophesy only in part.

10 When what is complete and perfect arrives, then what is partial and incomplete will come to an end.

11 When I was a child, I spoke like a child, I reasoned like a child, and I thought like a child. But when I became an adult, I set aside the ways of childhood.

12 At present, we see only a dim reflection, as if looking into a cloudy mirror; but then we will see face to face. Now I know only in part, but then I will know fully, just as I have been fully known.

13 And now these three remain: faith, hope, and love. But the greatest of these is love.
