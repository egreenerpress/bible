Exodus Chapter 10

1 The LORD said to Moses, "Go to Pharaoh, for I have made his heart stubborn, along with the hearts of his officials, so that I may display these miraculous signs of mine before him."

2 And so you may recount to your son and your grandson the mighty deeds I performed in Egypt and the signs I displayed among them, so that you may understand that I am the LORD.

3 Moses and Aaron went to Pharaoh and said to him, "This is what the LORD, the God of the Hebrews, declares: How long will you refuse to submit to me? Let my people go, so that they may worship me."

4 If you refuse to let my people go, then tomorrow I will bring locusts into your territory.

5 They will cover the surface of the land so completely that no one will be able to see the ground. They will devour whatever remains after the hail—what has survived and is left for you in the fields—and they will consume every tree that grows in your fields.

6 They will fill your houses, the houses of all your officials, and the homes of all the Egyptians—something that neither your ancestors nor your ancestors' ancestors have ever seen, from the time they first lived on the earth until this very day." Then he turned and left Pharaoh's presence.

7 Pharaoh's officials said to him, "How long will this man continue to be a trap for us? Let the people go, so they may worship the LORD their God. Do you not yet realize that Egypt is being ruined?"

8 Moses and Aaron were brought back to Pharaoh, and he said to them, "Go, worship the LORD your God. But tell me, who exactly will be going?"

9 Moses said, "We will go with everyone—our young and our old, our sons and our daughters, our flocks and our herds. We must go, for we are to celebrate a festival to the LORD."

10 He said to them, "May the LORD be with you in the same way that I allow you and your children to go. Be warned, for trouble lies ahead of you."

11 No, that will not do. Only the men may go and serve the LORD, as you have requested." And they were expelled from Pharaoh's presence.

12 The LORD said to Moses, "Reach out your hand over the land of Egypt to bring the locusts, so that they may swarm over the land of Egypt and devour every plant in the land—everything that the hail has spared."

13 Moses raised his staff over the land of Egypt, and the LORD caused an east wind to blow across the land all that day and throughout the night. When morning came, the east wind had brought the locusts.

14 The locusts swarmed over the entire land of Egypt and settled in every region of the country. They were extremely severe; never before had there been such a swarm of locusts, nor would there ever be one like it again.

15 The locusts spread across the entire land, covering the face of the earth so completely that the ground was darkened. They devoured every plant in the land and all the fruit on the trees that the hail had spared. Not a single green leaf remained on the trees or on the plants in the fields throughout all the land of Egypt.

16 Then Pharaoh urgently summoned Moses and Aaron and said, "I have sinned against the LORD your God and against you."

17 Now, I beg you, forgive my sin just this once, and plead with the LORD your God to remove this deadly punishment from me.

18 Then he left Pharaoh's presence and pleaded with the LORD.

19 Then the LORD caused a powerful west wind to blow, which swept away the locusts and drove them into the Red Sea. Not a single locust remained anywhere in the entire land of Egypt.

20 However, the LORD caused Pharaoh's heart to become stubborn, so that he refused to allow the Israelites to leave.

21 The LORD said to Moses, "Reach out your hand toward the sky, so that darkness may cover the land of Egypt—a darkness so thick it can be felt."

22 Moses raised his hand toward the heavens, and a deep darkness covered the entire land of Egypt for three days.

23 No one could see another, and no one rose from their place for three days. However, all the Israelites had light in their homes.

24 Pharaoh summoned Moses and said, "Go, worship the LORD. However, your flocks and herds must remain here. Only your children may go with you."

25 Moses said, "You must also provide us with animals for sacrifices and burnt offerings, so that we may present them to the LORD our God."

26 Our livestock must also go with us; not a single hoof will be left behind, for we must take them to serve the LORD our God. We do not yet know what we will need to serve the LORD until we arrive there.

27 However, the LORD caused Pharaoh's heart to become stubborn, and he refused to allow them to leave.

28 Pharaoh said to him, "Leave my presence. Be warned—do not appear before me again, for on the day you see my face, you will die."

29 Moses replied, "You have spoken rightly; I will not see your face again."
