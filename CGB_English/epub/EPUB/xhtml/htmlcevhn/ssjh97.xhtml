Job Chapter 3

1 After this, Job began to speak and expressed his anguish, lamenting the day of his birth.

2 And Job spoke, saying,

3 "May the day of my birth be erased, and the night when it was proclaimed, 'A male child has been conceived.'"

4 May that day be plunged into darkness; may God above not acknowledge it, and may no light shine upon it.

5 Let darkness and the shadow of death claim it; let a cloud settle over it; let the gloom of that day fill it with terror.

6 As for that night, may darkness overtake it; may it not be counted among the days of the year or included in the tally of the months.

7 Behold, may that night be desolate; may no sound of joy be heard in it.

8 Let those who curse days curse it—those who are prepared to summon mourning.

9 May the evening stars of that day grow dark; may it hope for light, but find none; and may it never see the first rays of dawn.

10 Because it did not close the doors of my mother’s womb, nor conceal suffering from my eyes.

11 Why was I not dead from the womb? Why did I not perish as soon as I came forth from the belly?

12 Why did my knees not fail me, or why was I given breasts to nurse?

13 If I had remained silent and stayed still, I would have slept; then I would have been at peace.

14 With rulers and advisors of the earth, who constructed ruined cities for their own purposes.

15 Or with rulers who possessed gold, who filled their homes with silver:

16 Or like a concealed miscarriage, I would not have existed; like infants who never saw the light of day.

17 In that place, the wicked no longer cause distress, and those who are weary find true rest.

18 In that place, the prisoners find peace together; they no longer hear the voice of their oppressor.

19 Both the lowly and the mighty are present there, and the servant is no longer bound to his master.

20 Why is light given to those who are in misery, and life to those whose souls are bitter?

21 They yearn for death, but it does not come; they search for it more eagerly than for hidden treasures.

22 They who are filled with great joy and delight when they finally discover the grave—who are these people?

23 Why is life granted to someone whose path is obscured, and whom God has surrounded with barriers?

24 My groaning comes before I eat, and my cries pour out like rushing waters.

25 What I deeply dreaded has now happened to me, and what I feared has come upon me.

26 I was not secure, nor did I have peace or tranquility; yet still, trouble came upon me.
