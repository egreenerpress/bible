I Corinthians Chapter 1

1 Paul, called to be an apostle of Jesus Christ by the will of God, and Sosthenes, our fellow believer,

2 To the assembly of God that is in Corinth, to those who have been made holy in Christ Jesus, called to be His holy people, along with all who in every place call on the name of our Lord Jesus Christ—both their Lord and ours:

3 May grace and peace be given to you from God our Father and from the Lord Jesus Christ.

4 I continually give thanks to my God for you, because of the grace of God that has been given to you through Jesus Christ.

5 In every way, you have been abundantly supplied by Him, with all forms of speech and every kind of understanding.

6 Just as the testimony about Christ was firmly established among you.

7 Therefore, you lack no spiritual gift as you eagerly await the revelation of our Lord Jesus Christ.

8 He will also strengthen you to the end, so that you may be found without fault on the day of our Lord Jesus Christ.

9 God is trustworthy, and it is through Him that you were called into a shared relationship with His Son, Jesus Christ our Lord.

10 I urge you, brothers and sisters, in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, to all speak in harmony and to avoid divisions among yourselves. Instead, strive to be united in thought and purpose, perfectly joined together in the same mindset and shared understanding.

11 For I have been informed about you, my brothers and sisters, by those from Chloe's household, that there are quarrels and divisions among you.

12 Now I say this because each of you is claiming, 'I belong to Paul,' or 'I belong to Apollos,' or 'I belong to Cephas,' or 'I belong to Christ.'

13 Is Christ fragmented into parts? Was Paul the one crucified for you? Or were you baptized into the name of Paul?

14 I give thanks to God that I did not baptize any of you except Crispus and Gaius.

15 So that no one may claim that I baptized anyone in my own name.

16 I also baptized the household of Stephanas. Beyond that, I do not recall baptizing anyone else.

17 Christ did not send me to baptize, but to proclaim the gospel—and not with eloquent or clever words, so that the power of the cross of Christ would not be diminished.

18 The message of the cross is seen as foolishness by those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved, it is the power of God.

19 For it is written, "I will nullify the wisdom of the wise, and I will render the insight of the discerning as nothing."

20 Where is the wise person? Where is the scholar? Where is the debater of this age? Has not God shown the wisdom of this world to be foolish?

21 For since, in the wisdom of God, the world through its wisdom did not come to know God, God was pleased to save those who believe through what seems like foolishness—the message proclaimed.

22 The Jews demand miraculous signs as proof, and the Greeks pursue philosophical wisdom.

23 We proclaim Christ crucified, which is a cause of offense to the Jews and seems like nonsense to the Gentiles.

24 But to those who are called, both Jews and Greeks, Christ is the power of God and the wisdom of God.

25 For what seems foolish in God surpasses the wisdom of humanity, and what appears weak in God exceeds the strength of humanity.

26 Consider your calling, brothers and sisters: not many of you were wise by human standards, not many were powerful, not many were of noble birth.

27 However, God has selected what the world considers foolish to shame the wise, and He has chosen what the world views as weak to humble what it regards as powerful.

28 God has chosen the lowly and despised things of the world, even things that are considered as nothing, to nullify the things that are significant.

29 So that no human being may boast in God's presence.

30 You are in Christ Jesus because of God’s work, and he has become for us the source of wisdom, righteousness, sanctification, and redemption.

31 As it is written: "Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord."
