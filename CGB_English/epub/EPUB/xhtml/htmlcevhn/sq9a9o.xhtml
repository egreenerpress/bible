Leviticus Chapter 2

1 When anyone presents a grain offering to the LORD, their offering must consist of fine flour. They are to pour oil over it and place frankincense on it.

2 The person shall bring the offering to Aaron’s sons, the priests. The priest will take a handful of the flour, along with its oil and all the frankincense, and burn it as a memorial portion on the altar. It will be an offering made by fire, producing a pleasing aroma to the LORD.

3 The remaining portion of the grain offering shall belong to Aaron and his sons. It is a most sacred part of the offerings presented to the LORD by fire.

4 If you present a grain offering baked in the oven, it must consist of unleavened cakes made of fine flour mixed with oil, or unleavened wafers spread with oil.

5 If your offering is a grain offering baked in a pan, it must be made of fine flour, unleavened and mixed with oil.

6 You shall divide it into pieces and pour oil over it; it is a grain offering.

7 If your offering is a grain offering prepared in a pan, it must be made of fine flour mixed with oil.

8 You shall present the grain offering made from these ingredients to the LORD. When it is brought to the priest, he shall take it to the altar.

9 The priest shall take a portion of the grain offering as a memorial and burn it on the altar. It is an offering made by fire, a pleasing aroma to the LORD.

10 The portion of the grain offering that remains shall belong to Aaron and his sons. It is a most sacred part of the offerings presented to the LORD by fire.

11 No grain offering that you bring to the LORD is to be made with leaven, for you must not burn any leaven or honey as an offering made by fire to the LORD.

12 Concerning the offering of the firstfruits, you shall present them to the LORD, but they are not to be burned on the altar as a pleasing aroma.

13 You must season every grain offering you present with salt. Do not allow the salt of the covenant of your God to be missing from your grain offering. With all your offerings, you are to include salt.

14 If you present a grain offering of your first harvest to the LORD, you must offer for the grain offering fresh heads of grain roasted by fire—that is, crushed grain from fully ripened ears.

15 You shall pour oil on it and place frankincense upon it; it is a grain offering.

16 The priest shall burn a portion of the crushed grain and a portion of the oil, along with all the frankincense, as a memorial offering. This is a fire offering presented to the LORD.
