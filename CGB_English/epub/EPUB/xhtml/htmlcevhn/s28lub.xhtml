Romans Chapter 16

1 I recommend to you our sister Phoebe, who serves the church in Cenchrea.

2 Welcome her in the Lord in a manner worthy of Godâ€™s holy people, and provide her with whatever assistance she may need from you. For she has been a great help to many, including myself.

3 Greet Priscilla and Aquila, my fellow workers in Christ Jesus.

4 They risked their own lives for my sake. I am not only grateful to them, but all the Gentile churches are as well.

5 Also, greet the church that meets in their home. Give my greetings to my dear friend Epaenetus, who was the first convert to Christ in the region of Achaia.

6 Greet Mary, who has worked very hard for us.

7 Greet Andronicus and Junia, my relatives and fellow prisoners, who are highly esteemed among the apostles and who became followers of Christ before I did.

8 Greet Amplias, my dear friend and fellow believer in the Lord.

9 Greet Urbane, our fellow worker in Christ, and Stachys, my dear friend.

10 Greet Apelles, who has proven himself faithful in Christ. Greet those who belong to the household of Aristobulus.

11 Greet Herodion, my relative. Extend my greetings to those in the household of Narcissus who belong to the Lord.

12 Greet Tryphena and Tryphosa, who work diligently in the service of the Lord. Greet Persis, who is dearly loved and has worked tirelessly in the Lord's service.

13 Greet Rufus, who is chosen by the Lord, and his mother, who has been like a mother to me as well.

14 Greet Asyncritus, Phlegon, Hermas, Patrobas, Hermes, and the fellow believers who are with them.

15 Greet Philologus, Julia, Nereus and his sister, Olympas, and all the believers who are with them.

16 Greet one another with a sacred kiss. The congregations of Christ send you their greetings.

17 I urge you, brothers and sisters, to take note of those who create divisions and obstacles that go against the teachings you have received, and stay away from them.

18 For those who act in this way do not truly serve our Lord Jesus Christ, but instead serve their own selfish desires. Through smooth talk and flattering words, they mislead the hearts of the unsuspecting.

19 Your obedience has become widely known to everyone. Therefore, I am glad for your sake. However, I want you to be wise in what is good and innocent in what is evil.

20 The God of peace will soon crush Satan under your feet. May the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you. Amen.

21 Timothy, my fellow worker, and Lucius, and Jason, and Sosipater, my relatives, send their greetings to you.

22 I, Tertius, the one who wrote down this letter, greet you in the name of the Lord.

23 Gaius, who is my host and a host to the entire church, sends you his greetings. Erastus, the city treasurer, also greets you, as does Quartus, our fellow believer.

24 May the gracious favor of our Lord Jesus Christ be with all of you. Amen.

25 Now to Him who has the power to strengthen you according to the good news I proclaim and the message of Jesus Christ, in keeping with the revelation of the mystery that was hidden for ages past but has now been made known.

26 But now it has been revealed, and through the writings of the prophets, in accordance with the command of the eternal God, it has been made known to all nations to bring about the obedience that comes from faith.

27 To the only wise God, be glory forever through Jesus Christ. Amen.
