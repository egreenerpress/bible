II Chronicles Chapter 7

1 When Solomon finished praying, fire descended from heaven and consumed the burnt offering and the sacrifices. Then the glory of the LORD filled the temple.

2 The priests were unable to enter the temple of the LORD because the glory of the LORD had completely filled the LORD’s house.

3 When all the Israelites saw the fire descend and the glory of the LORD fill the house, they knelt down with their faces to the ground on the pavement, worshiping and praising the LORD, saying, "He is good; his steadfast love endures forever."

4 Then the king and all the people presented offerings to the LORD.

5 King Solomon presented a sacrifice of twenty-two thousand oxen and one hundred and twenty thousand sheep. In this way, the king and all the people consecrated the house of God.

6 The priests carried out their duties, and the Levites stood ready with the musical instruments dedicated to the Lord, which King David had provided for praising the Lord, declaring, "His faithful love endures forever." As David had instructed, they offered praise through their service. Meanwhile, the priests blew trumpets before them, and all the people of Israel stood in reverence.

7 Furthermore, Solomon consecrated the central area of the courtyard in front of the house of the LORD, for there he presented the burnt offerings and the fat portions of the peace offerings. This was necessary because the bronze altar that Solomon had made was not large enough to accommodate the burnt offerings, the grain offerings, and the fat.

8 At that same time, Solomon celebrated the festival for seven days, and all Israel joined him—a vast assembly from the entrance of Hamath to the River of Egypt.

9 On the eighth day, they held a sacred assembly, for they had celebrated the dedication of the altar for seven days and observed the feast for another seven days.

10 On the twenty-third day of the seventh month, he dismissed the people to return to their homes, filled with joy and gladness of heart because of the goodness the LORD had shown to David, to Solomon, and to Israel, His people.

11 So Solomon completed the construction of the house of the LORD and the king’s palace. Everything he had desired to accomplish in the house of the LORD and in his own residence, he successfully brought to completion.

12 The LORD appeared to Solomon during the night and said to him, "I have heard your prayer, and I have chosen this place to be My house of sacrifice."

13 If I close the heavens so that no rain falls, or if I command locusts to consume the land, or if I send a plague among my people;

14 If my people, who are identified by my name, will humble themselves, pray earnestly, seek my presence, and turn away from their sinful ways, then I will listen from heaven, forgive their sins, and restore their land.

15 Now my eyes will be open, and my ears attentive to the prayers offered in this place.

16 I have now chosen and set apart this house as holy, so that my name may remain there forever. My presence and my care will be there continually.

17 And as for you, if you will live your life in my presence, as David your father did, and act in accordance with everything I have commanded you, and faithfully keep my statutes and ordinances,

18 At that time, I will establish the throne of your kingdom, just as I made a covenant with your father David, promising: "You will never lack a descendant to rule over Israel."

19 However, if you turn away and abandon my statutes and commandments, which I have presented to you, and instead go and serve other gods, bowing down to worship them,

20 Then I will uproot them completely from the land I have given them, and this house, which I have consecrated for my name, I will cast out of my presence. I will make it an object of scorn and ridicule among all nations.

21 This grand temple, though now exalted, will become a source of shock and amazement to all who pass by it. They will ask, "Why has the LORD treated this land and this temple in such a way?"

22 The response will be: "Because they abandoned the LORD, the God of their ancestors, who brought them out of the land of Egypt, and instead clung to other gods, worshiping and serving them. That is why the LORD has brought all this disaster upon them."
