Romans Chapter 9

1 I speak the truth in Christ—I am not lying—and my conscience confirms it through the Holy Spirit.

2 I experience profound grief and unceasing anguish in my heart.

3 I could almost wish that I myself were cut off from Christ for the sake of my brothers and sisters, my own people according to the flesh.

4 They are the Israelites, to whom belong the privilege of being God’s chosen people, the manifestation of His glory, the covenants He established, the giving of the law, the worship ordained by God, and the promises made to them.

5 The ancestors belong to them, and from their lineage, according to human descent, came the Messiah, who is supreme over all, God blessed forever. Amen.

6 It is not as though God's word has failed. For not all who are descended from Israel are truly part of Israel.

7 Not all who are descended from Abraham are his true children. Rather, it is through Isaac that your offspring will be recognized.

8 This means that those who are born by natural descent are not necessarily children of God; rather, it is the children of the promise who are considered the true descendants.

9 For this is the promise declared: "At this appointed time I will come, and Sarah will bear a son."

10 And not only this, but even when Rebecca conceived children by one man, our father Isaac—

11 Before the children were born, and before they had done anything good or bad—so that God’s purpose in choosing them might stand, not because of their actions but because of his call—

12 It was told to her, "The older will serve the younger."

13 As it is written, "I have loved Jacob, but I have rejected Esau."

14 What then should we conclude? Is God unjust? Certainly not!

15 For God says to Moses, "I will show mercy to whom I choose to show mercy, and I will have compassion on whom I choose to have compassion."

16 Therefore, it does not depend on human desire or effort, but on God, who shows mercy.

17 The Scripture says to Pharaoh, "For this very purpose I have raised you up, so that I might demonstrate my power through you and that my name might be proclaimed throughout the entire earth."

18 Therefore, he shows mercy to those he chooses to show mercy, and he hardens the hearts of those he chooses to harden.

19 You might then say to me, "Why does God still find fault with us? For who can resist His will?"

20 But who are you, O man, to argue against God? Can the created thing say to its creator, "Why have you made me this way?"

21 Does the potter not have authority over the clay, to make from the same lump of clay one vessel for honorable purposes and another for common use?

22 What if God, desiring to demonstrate His wrath and to make His power known, patiently endured those destined for destruction—vessels of wrath prepared for ruin?

23 And this was done so that He might reveal the wealth of His glorious nature to those who are objects of His mercy—those whom He had prepared beforehand to receive His glory.

24 This includes us, whom He has called—not only from among the Jews but also from among the Gentiles.

25 As it is also written in Hosea: "I will call those who were not my people, 'my people,' and her who was not beloved, 'beloved.'"

26 A time will come when in the very place where it was declared to them, "You are not my people," they will be called "children of the living God."

27 Isaiah also proclaims concerning Israel, "Even if the number of the people of Israel is as vast as the sand by the sea, only a remnant will be saved."

28 For He will bring His work to completion and execute it swiftly in justice, because the Lord will carry out His decisive action upon the earth without delay.

29 As Isaiah said long ago, "If the Lord of Hosts had not preserved a remnant for us, we would have become like Sodom and been made similar to Gomorrah."

30 What then can we conclude? That the Gentiles, who were not pursuing righteousness, have obtained righteousness—a righteousness that comes through faith.

31 But Israel, though pursuing the principle of righteousness, has not achieved that righteousness.

32 Why is this so? Because they did not pursue it through faith, but as if it could be achieved by their own works based on the law. They stumbled over the stumbling stone.

33 As it is written: "Look, I am placing in Zion a stone that causes stumbling and a rock that brings offense; but whoever believes in him will not be put to shame."
