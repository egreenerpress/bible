Romans Chapter 12

1 Therefore, I urge you, brothers and sisters, in light of God’s mercies, to offer your bodies as a living sacrifice—holy and pleasing to God. This is your true and proper act of worship.

2 Do not conform to the patterns of this world, but be transformed by the renewal of your mind, so that you may discern what is the good, pleasing, and perfect will of God.

3 I say to each one of you, through the grace that has been given to me, do not think of yourself more highly than you ought to think. Instead, think with sound judgment, in proportion to the measure of faith that God has assigned to each of you.

4 Just as we have many parts in one body, and not all these parts serve the same function,

5 Therefore, though we are many, we form one body in Christ, and each one of us is connected to the others as members of the same body.

6 Since we have been given different gifts according to the grace bestowed upon us, if your gift is prophecy, then prophesy in accordance with the measure of faith you have received.

7 If your role is to serve, then devote yourself to serving. If your responsibility is to teach, then focus on teaching.

8 Or, if someone is called to encourage others, let them focus on encouragement. If someone is called to give, let them give generously and without hidden motives. If someone is called to lead, let them lead with earnest care and dedication. And if someone is called to show mercy, let them do so with a joyful and willing heart.

9 Let your love be genuine and sincere. Reject and despise what is evil; hold firmly to what is good.

10 Show genuine affection and care for one another with the love of family; value and honor each other above yourselves.

11 Do not be lazy in your work; instead, be enthusiastic and diligent in spirit, dedicating yourself wholeheartedly to serving the Lord.

12 Be joyful in the hope you have; remain steadfast during times of trouble; and persist earnestly in prayer.

13 Provide for the needs of fellow believers; be eager to show hospitality.

14 Show kindness to those who mistreat you: offer blessings, and do not speak ill of them.

15 Celebrate with those who are celebrating, and mourn with those who are mourning.

16 Live in harmony with one another. Do not be preoccupied with lofty ambitions, but willingly associate with those who are humble. Do not consider yourselves wiser than you truly are.

17 Do not repay anyone evil for evil. Instead, strive to do what is honorable and right in the eyes of everyone.

18 If it is within your power, and to the extent that you are able, strive to live in peace and harmony with everyone.

19 Beloved, do not seek revenge for yourselves, but instead step aside and allow God’s wrath to act. For it is written, “Vengeance belongs to Me; I will repay,” says the Lord.

20 Therefore, if your enemy is hungry, feed him; if he is thirsty, give him something to drink. For by doing this, you will heap burning coals on his head.

21 Do not let evil defeat you, but instead, conquer evil by doing good.
