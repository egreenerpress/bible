II Corinthians Chapter 11

1 I wish to God you could put up with me for a little while, even in my foolishness—yes, please bear with me.

2 For I am deeply concerned for you with a godly concern, because I have pledged you in marriage to one husband, so that I might present you as a pure and faithful bride to Christ.

3 However, I am concerned that, just as the serpent deceived Eve through his cunning, your thoughts might also be led astray from the pure and sincere devotion that belongs to Christ.

4 For if someone comes and proclaims a different Jesus, one whom we did not proclaim, or if you receive a different spirit, one you did not receive, or a different gospel, one you did not accept, you might easily put up with him.

5 I believe that I am not in any way inferior to the most prominent apostles.

6 Although I may lack eloquence in speaking, I am not lacking in understanding. Indeed, we have been fully revealed to you in every way.

7 Have I done something wrong by humbling myself so that you might be lifted up, because I proclaimed the gospel of God to you without charge?

8 I accepted financial support from other churches, taking wages from them, in order to serve you.

9 When I was with you and in need, I did not burden anyone for support. The brothers who came from Macedonia provided what I lacked. In every way, I have taken care not to be a burden to you, and I will continue to do so.

10 As surely as the truth of Christ is within me, no one will silence me in this boasting throughout the regions of Achaia.

11 Why do I say this? Is it because I do not love you? God knows that is not the case.

12 However, I will continue to do what I am doing, so that I may remove any opportunity for those who are seeking an opportunity to criticize. In this way, they may be exposed as being no different from us in the matters they boast about.

13 For such individuals are false apostles, dishonest workers, disguising themselves as apostles of Christ.

14 And it is no surprise, for Satan himself disguises himself as an angel of light.

15 It is not surprising, then, if his servants also disguise themselves as servants of righteousness. Their final outcome will correspond to their actions.

16 I repeat, let no one regard me as foolish. But even if you do, then accept me as a fool, so that I too may boast a little.

17 What I am saying, I do not say as if it were from the Lord, but rather as if speaking foolishly, in this boldness of boasting.

18 Since many boast about their outward achievements and human accomplishments, I too will boast.

19 You tolerate foolish people with ease, since you yourselves are so wise.

20 You tolerate it when someone enslaves you, exploits you, takes advantage of you, elevates themselves above you, or even strikes you in the face.

21 I am speaking in terms of what others might consider shameful, as if we had been weak. Yet, in whatever way someone else dares to boast—though I am speaking foolishly—I also dare to boast.

22 Are they Hebrews? I am as well. Are they Israelites? I am too. Are they descendants of Abraham? So am I.

23 Are they servants of Christ? (I speak as though I were out of my mind) I am even more so—in far greater labors, in countless beatings, in frequent imprisonments, and often facing death.

24 I was beaten by the Jews five times, each time receiving thirty-nine lashes.

25 Three times I was beaten with rods, once I was stoned, three times I was shipwrecked, and for a full day and night I was adrift in the open sea.

26 I have traveled often, faced dangers from raging waters, threats from robbers, risks from my own people, hazards from foreigners, perils in the city, dangers in the wilderness, threats on the sea, and risks from false believers.

27 Through exhaustion and hardship, through sleepless nights, through hunger and thirst, through frequent times of fasting, through cold and lack of clothing.

28 In addition to all the external challenges I face, there is the constant burden I carry every day—the deep concern for all the churches.

29 Who is struggling, and I do not share in their struggle? Who is led into sin, and my heart does not burn with concern?

30 If I must boast, I will boast about the things that reveal my weaknesses.

31 The God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who is eternally blessed, knows that I am not lying.

32 In Damascus, the governor appointed by King Aretas stationed guards to control the city of the Damascenes, intending to capture me.

33 I was lowered in a basket through an opening in the wall and escaped from his grasp.
