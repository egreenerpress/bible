Job Chapter 30

1 But now, those younger than I am mock meâ€”men whose fathers I would have considered unworthy to tend the dogs of my flock.

2 Indeed, what benefit could their strength bring to me, when their vigor had already faded with age?

3 Because of hunger and severe scarcity, they were left isolated; they fled into the wilderness, a place that was barren and desolate in times past.

4 Who gather wild plants from the bushes for food, and dig up roots of the broom tree to sustain themselves.

5 They were cast out from the company of others, (people shouted after them as they would after a thief).

6 To live in the crevices of the valleys, in the hollows of the earth, and among the rocky cliffs.

7 Among the thorny thickets they cried out in distress; beneath the stinging nettles they huddled together for shelter.

8 They were the offspring of foolish and dishonorable people, born of those without worth or dignity. They were more degraded and contemptible than the dust of the earth.

9 Now I have become the subject of their mocking songs; indeed, I am a common proverb among them.

10 They despise me, they keep their distance from me, and they do not hesitate to spit in my face.

11 Because God has loosened my strength and brought me low, they have cast off all restraint in my presence.

12 Young men rise up against me on my right side; they drive me back and lay out their paths of ruin to attack me.

13 They obstruct my way, they advance my disaster, and they have no one to assist them.

14 They attacked me like a powerful, overwhelming flood; in their devastation, they surged over me.

15 Fears and horrors overwhelm me; they chase after my life like a rushing wind, and my well-being vanishes like a passing cloud.

16 And now my spirit is overwhelmed within me; the days of suffering have seized me.

17 My bones are pierced within me during the night, and my body finds no rest.

18 Because of the severe intensity of my affliction, my clothing has become disfigured; it clings tightly to me like the collar of my garment.

19 He has thrown me into the mud, and I have become like dust and ashes.

20 I call out to you, but you do not answer me; I rise and stand before you, yet you pay no attention to me.

21 You have turned against me with cruelty; with your mighty hand, you rise up against me.

22 You raise me up to the wind; you make me ride upon it, and you scatter my very being.

23 For I am certain that you will lead me to death, to the place destined for all who live.

24 Nevertheless, he will not reach out his hand to the grave, even if they cry out in their ruin.

25 Did I not weep for those in distress? Was my soul not deeply troubled for the needy?

26 When I sought good, evil came to me instead; and when I hoped for light, darkness arrived.

27 My innermost being was in turmoil and found no rest; the days of suffering came upon me unexpectedly.

28 I walked in mourning, deprived of the light of the sun; I rose and cried out in the assembly.

29 I am like a brother to jackals and a companion to owls.

30 My skin has turned black and is peeling away, and my bones are scorched with fever.

31 My harp has been transformed into an instrument of grief, and my flute now echoes the sound of those who weep.
