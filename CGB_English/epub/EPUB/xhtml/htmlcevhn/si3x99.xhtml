Job Chapter 39

1 Do you know the season when the mountain goats give birth? Can you observe the time when the deer bear their young?

2 Can you count the months until they complete their gestation? Do you know the exact time when they give birth?

3 They kneel down, they give birth to their young, they deliver themselves from their labor pains.

4 Their young ones thrive and grow strong, nourished by abundant grain; they go out into the world and do not return to them.

5 Who has set the wild donkey free? Or who has released the bonds of the wild donkey?

6 I have made the wilderness his home, and the desolate land his dwelling place.

7 He shows no respect for the bustling crowds of the city, nor does he pay attention to the shouts of the taskmaster.

8 The expanse of the mountains serves as his grazing land, and he seeks out every bit of green vegetation.

9 Would the wild ox be willing to serve you, or stay by your feeding trough?

10 Can you harness the wild ox with a rope to plow the furrow? Will it follow you to till the valleys?

11 Will you rely on him because his power is great? Or will you entrust your work to him?

12 Will you trust him to bring in your harvest and gather your grain into your storehouse?

13 Did you give the beautiful wings to the peacocks? Or did you provide wings and feathers to the ostrich?

14 She leaves her eggs in the ground and lets them warm in the dirt.

15 And they forget that a foot may trample them, or that a wild animal may crush them.

16 She has become callous toward her own offspring, as if they were not her own; her labor is futile, for she lacks any sense of fear or reverence.

17 Because God has withheld wisdom from her and has not granted her understanding.

18 When she rises up in her might, she mocks the horse and its rider.

19 Have you granted the horse its great strength? Have you adorned its neck with the power to thunder forth?

20 Can you make him tremble like a grasshopper? The majesty of his snorting is terrifying.

21 He charges into the valley, exulting in his power; he advances boldly to confront the warriors.

22 He laughs at danger and is not terrified; he does not retreat from the sword.

23 The quiver clatters against him, along with the gleaming spear and the shield.

24 He charges across the ground with intense power and fury, and he does not pause or hesitate, even at the sound of the trumpet.

25 He exclaims at the sound of the trumpets, "Aha!" He senses the battle from afar, the roar of the commanders, and the clamor of the troops.

26 Does the hawk soar by your understanding, spreading its wings toward the south?

27 Does the eagle soar upward at your command, building its nest in the heights?

28 She lives and remains secure on the rock, on the rugged cliff and the fortress.

29 From there she searches for her prey, and her eyes see far into the distance.

30 Her young ones also drink blood, and wherever the slain are found, there she is.
