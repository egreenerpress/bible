Genesis Chapter 28

1 Isaac summoned Jacob, blessed him, and gave him this instruction: "You must not marry any of the women from the daughters of Canaan."

2 "Get up and go to Paddan-aram, to the household of Bethuel, your mother’s father. From there, choose a wife for yourself from the daughters of Laban, your mother’s brother."

3 And may God Almighty bless you, make you fruitful, and cause you to increase in number, so that you may become a great multitude of people.

4 And I will grant you the blessing of Abraham, both to you and to your descendants after you, so that you may take possession of the land where you now live as a foreigner, the land that God gave to Abraham.

5 Isaac sent Jacob on his way, and Jacob journeyed to Paddan-aram, to Laban, the son of Bethuel the Aramean, who was the brother of Rebekah—the mother of Jacob and Esau.

6 When Esau realized that Isaac had blessed Jacob and sent him away to Paddan-aram to find a wife there, and that as he blessed him, he had given him strict instructions, saying, "You must not marry any of the daughters of Canaan,"

7 Jacob heeded his father and mother and went to Paddan-aram.

8 And when Esau realized that Isaac, his father, was displeased with the Canaanite women he had married,

9 Then Esau went to Ishmael and took Mahalath, the daughter of Ishmael (who was Abraham’s son) and the sister of Nebaioth, to be his wife in addition to the wives he already had.

10 Jacob departed from Beersheba and set out on his journey toward Haran.

11 He came upon a certain place and stayed there for the night because the sun had set. Taking some of the stones from that place, he arranged them as a resting place for his head and lay down there to sleep.

12 He had a dream, and in it he saw a ladder standing on the earth, with its top reaching up to heaven. And there, the angels of God were going up and down on it.

13 And look, the LORD stood above it and said, "I am the LORD, the God of Abraham your father, and the God of Isaac. The land on which you are lying, I will give it to you and to your descendants."

14 Your descendants will be as numerous as the dust of the earth, and you will spread out to the west, the east, the north, and the south. Through you and your descendants, all the families of the earth will be blessed.

15 Listen carefully: I am with you, and I will watch over you wherever you go. I will bring you back to this land, for I will not abandon you until I have fulfilled all that I have promised to you.

16 Jacob woke from his sleep and said, "Truly, the LORD is present in this place, and I was not aware of it."

17 He was filled with awe and said, "How awe-inspiring is this place! This is nothing less than the house of God, and this is the gateway to heaven."

18 Jacob got up early in the morning, took the stone he had used as a pillow, set it upright as a pillar, and poured oil over the top of it.

19 He named that place Bethel, though the city was originally called Luz.

20 Jacob made a solemn promise, saying, "If God remains with me, watches over me on this journey I am taking, provides me with food to eat and clothing to wear,

21 If I return safely to my father’s house, then the LORD will truly be my God.

22 This stone, which I have set up as a pillar, will be God’s house. And of everything you give me, I will faithfully give a tenth back to you.
