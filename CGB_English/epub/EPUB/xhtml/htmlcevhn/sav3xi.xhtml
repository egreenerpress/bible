Hosea Chapter 5

1 Listen to this, you priests; pay attention, you people of Israel; and give ear, you royal house. Judgment is coming upon you because you have become a trap at Mizpah and a net spread out on Tabor.

2 The rebels are deeply entrenched in their ways, eager to carry out violence, even though I have consistently warned and corrected them all.

3 I am fully aware of Ephraim, and Israel is not hidden from me. For now, O Ephraim, you are engaging in idolatry, and Israel has become defiled.

4 They do not direct their actions to turn back to their God, for a spirit of unfaithfulness is deeply rooted within them, and they do not acknowledge the LORD.

5 The arrogance of Israel bears witness against them; therefore, Israel and Ephraim will stumble because of their sin, and Judah too will fall alongside them.

6 They will come with their flocks and herds to seek the LORD, but they will not find Him; He has withdrawn Himself from them.

7 They have acted faithlessly against the LORD, for they have produced illegitimate children. Now, a month will come to destroy them along with their possessions.

8 Sound the horn in Gibeah, and the trumpet in Ramah; raise a loud cry in Beth-aven, for danger is behind you, O Benjamin!

9 Ephraim will be left desolate on the day of judgment. Among the tribes of Israel, I have declared what is certain to come to pass.

10 The leaders of Judah have acted like those who move boundary markers; therefore, I will pour out my anger upon them like a flood of water.

11 Ephraim is crushed and shattered by judgment, because he deliberately followed after human commands.

12 Therefore, I will be like a moth to Ephraim and like decay to the house of Judah.

13 When Ephraim recognized his weakness and Judah became aware of his affliction, Ephraim turned to Assyria for help and sent envoys to the great king. But he could not bring healing to you or relieve you of your suffering.

14 For I will be like a lion to Ephraim, and like a young lion to the house of Judah. I, even I, will tear them apart and depart; I will carry them off, and no one will be able to rescue them.

15 I will depart and return to my dwelling place until they recognize their wrongdoing and earnestly seek my presence. In their time of distress, they will diligently seek me.
