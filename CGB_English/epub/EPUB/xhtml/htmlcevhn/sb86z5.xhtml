Philemon Chapter 1

1 Paul, a prisoner for the sake of Christ Jesus, and Timothy our brother, to Philemon, our beloved friend and fellow worker,

2 And to our dear Apphia, and to Archippus, our fellow soldier, and to the congregation that meets in your home:

3 May grace and peace be yours, given by God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

4 I continually give thanks to my God, always remembering you in my prayers.

5 I have heard about your love and faith, which you demonstrate toward the Lord Jesus and toward all of God’s people.

6 So that the sharing of your faith may become powerful and effective through the recognition of every good thing that is within you in Christ Jesus.

7 We experience great joy and comfort in your love, because through you, the hearts of God’s people have been deeply refreshed, dear brother.

8 Therefore, although I could confidently appeal to you in Christ's name to do what is proper,

9 Yet out of love, I appeal to you instead—I, Paul, an old man and now also a prisoner for the sake of Jesus Christ.

10 I appeal to you concerning my son Onesimus, whom I have spiritually fathered while in chains.

11 In the past, this person was of no benefit to you, but now they are beneficial both to you and to me.

12 Whom I am sending back to you. Therefore, welcome him as you would welcome me, for he is like my very heart.

13 I would have liked to keep him with me, so that he could serve me in your place while I am bound for the sake of the gospel.

14 I would not do anything without your consent, so that your act of kindness would not seem forced, but given freely and willingly.

15 Perhaps he was separated from you for a short time so that you might have him back permanently.

16 No longer as a mere servant, but as more than a servant—as a dearly loved brother, especially to me, and even more so to you, both in a human sense and in the Lord.

17 If you consider me a partner, welcome him as you would welcome me.

18 If he has wronged you or owes you anything, charge it to my account.

19 I, Paul, have written this with my own hand: I will repay it. However, I do not need to remind you that you owe me even your very self.

20 Yes, my brother, let me find joy in you through the Lord; bring comfort to my heart in the Lord.

21 I am confident in your willingness to obey, which is why I have written to you, knowing that you will do even more than what I have asked.

22 At the same time, prepare a place for me to stay, for I am confident that through your prayers I will be granted to you.

23 Epaphras, who is my fellow prisoner because of our shared faith in Christ Jesus, sends you his greetings.

24 Marcus, Aristarchus, Demas, and Luke, my fellow workers.

25 May the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit. Amen.
