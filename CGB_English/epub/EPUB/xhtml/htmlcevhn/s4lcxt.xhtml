Hebrews Chapter 1

1 God, who in various times and in many ways spoke in the past to our ancestors through the prophets,

2 In these final days, God has spoken to us through His Son, whom He has appointed as the heir of all things, and through whom He also created the universe.

3 He is the radiant reflection of God’s glory and the exact representation of His nature, sustaining all things by His powerful word. After He had provided purification for sins through His own sacrifice, He took His seat at the right hand of the Majesty in heaven.

4 He has become far superior to the angels, for he has inherited a name that is more excellent than theirs.

5 To which of the angels did God ever say, "You are my Son; today I have become your Father"? Or again, "I will be a Father to him, and he will be a Son to me"?

6 And once more, when God brings the firstborn into the world, he declares, "Let all God’s angels bow down in worship before him."

7 Concerning the angels, He says, "He makes His angels like winds, and His servants like flames of fire."

8 But concerning the Son, He says: "Your throne, O God, endures forever and ever; a scepter of justice is the scepter of Your kingdom."

9 You have loved what is right and rejected what is wrong; therefore, God, your God, has anointed you with the oil of joy, setting you above your companions.

10 And you, Lord, in the beginning, established the foundation of the earth, and the heavens are the creation of your hands.

11 They will pass away, but you remain forever; and like a garment, they will all wear out and grow old.

12 You will roll them up like a garment, and they will be changed. But you remain the same, and your years will never come to an end.

13 But to which of the angels did God ever say, "Sit at my right hand until I make your enemies a footstool for your feet"?

14 Are not all these spirits sent to serve, appointed to assist those who are destined to inherit salvation?
