Genesis Chapter 7

1 The LORD said to Noah, "You and your entire household must enter the ark, for I have seen that you are righteous before me in this generation."

2 You must take with you seven pairs of every kind of clean animal, a male and its female, and two pairs of every kind of unclean animal, a male and its female.

3 From the birds of the sky, take seven pairs, both male and female, to ensure their offspring survive and thrive across the entire earth.

4 In seven more days, I will send rain upon the earth for forty days and forty nights, and I will wipe out every living creature I have made from the face of the earth.

5 Noah carried out everything exactly as the LORD had instructed him.

6 Noah was six hundred years old when the floodwaters came upon the earth.

7 Noah entered the ark, along with his sons, his wife, and his sons’ wives, to escape the rising floodwaters.

8 Concerning animals that are considered clean and those that are unclean, as well as birds and every creature that moves along the ground...

9 Pairs of animals, both male and female, entered the ark with Noah, just as God had commanded him.

10 After seven days had passed, the floodwaters covered the earth.

11 In the six hundredth year of Noah’s life, on the seventeenth day of the second month, on that very day, all the springs of the vast deep burst forth, and the floodgates of the heavens were opened.

12 The rain fell upon the earth for forty days and forty nights.

13 On that very same day, Noah entered the ark, along with his sons Shem, Ham, and Japheth, as well as Noah’s wife and the three wives of his sons. They all went into the ark together.

14 They, along with every kind of animal, all livestock according to their kinds, every creature that moves along the ground according to its kind, and every bird of every kind—each winged creature of its own kind.

15 And they entered the ark with Noah, two by two of every living creature that had the breath of life within them.

16 Those who entered the ark went in as male and female of every kind of living creature, just as God had commanded Noah. Then the LORD sealed the entrance behind them.

17 The floodwaters remained on the earth for forty days. As the waters rose, they lifted the ark, and it floated high above the ground.

18 The waters surged and rose significantly over the earth, and the ark floated on the surface of the floodwaters.

19 The floodwaters rose overwhelmingly over the earth, and all the high mountains under the entire sky were completely submerged.

20 The waters rose fifteen cubits above the earth, completely covering the mountains.

21 Every living creature that moved on the earth perished—birds, livestock, wild animals, every crawling thing that crawls on the earth, and all humankind.

22 Every living creature that breathed air, all that lived on dry land, perished.

23 Every living creature that existed on the face of the earth was wiped out—humans, livestock, crawling creatures, and birds of the sky. They were all destroyed from the earth. Only Noah and those with him in the ark survived.

24 The floodwaters continued to rise and cover the earth for one hundred and fifty days.
