Job Chapter 19

1 Then Job replied and said,

2 How long will you torment my soul and crush me with your words?

3 You have insulted me ten times over; you show no shame in treating me as a stranger.

4 If it is true that I have made a mistake, then my error is my own to bear.

5 If you truly intend to exalt yourselves above me and use my disgrace as an argument against me,

6 Understand this clearly: God has brought me down and surrounded me with His net.

7 Listen, I cry out about injustice, but no one listens; I shout for help, but there is no justice.

8 He has blocked my way so that I cannot move forward, and He has shrouded my paths in darkness.

9 He has stripped away my honor and removed the crown from my head.

10 He has completely devastated me on all sides, and I am finished; my hope he has uprooted like a tree.

11 He has also ignited His anger against me, and He regards me as one of His adversaries.

12 His forces gather and advance against me; they set up their siege around my dwelling.

13 He has caused my relatives to keep their distance from me, and those who knew me well have truly become strangers to me.

14 My relatives have abandoned me, and my close friends have forgotten me.

15 The people who live in my household, including my servants, regard me as a foreigner; I am treated like a stranger in their eyes.

16 I summoned my servant, but he did not respond; I pleaded with him earnestly, using my own words.

17 My breath has become repulsive to my wife, even though I pleaded with her for the sake of my own children.

18 Even young children looked down on me; when I stood up, they spoke against me.

19 All my closest friends despise me, and those I once loved have turned against me.

20 My bones cling to my skin and flesh, and I have barely escaped with only the skin of my teeth.

21 Show me compassion, show me compassion, my dear friends, for the hand of God has struck me.

22 Why do you pursue me as if you were God, and yet remain unsatisfied even with my suffering?

23 If only my words could be recorded now! If only they could be written down in a book!

24 The words were engraved with an iron tool, filled with lead, and carved into the rock to endure forever!

25 For I know that my Redeemer lives, and in the end, He will rise upon the earth.

26 Even after my skin is destroyed and worms consume this body, yet in my own flesh I will see God.

27 I myself will see Him, and my own eyes will behold Him, and not as a stranger; even though my innermost being is consumed within me.

28 But you should consider, "Why do we pursue him, when the true essence of the matter lies within me?"

29 Be in awe of the sword’s power, for wrath brings the sword’s punishment, so that you may understand there is divine justice.
