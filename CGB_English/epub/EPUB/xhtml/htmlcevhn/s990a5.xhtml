Psalms Chapter 77

1 (For the music director, according to the style of Jeduthun. A psalm of Asaph.) I called out to God with my voice—yes, I cried aloud to God—and He listened to me.

2 In the time of my distress, I sought the Lord; my wound festered through the night and did not stop; my soul could not accept comfort.

3 I thought of God and became deeply troubled; I poured out my distress, and my spirit grew faint. Selah.

4 You keep my eyes from closing; I am so deeply distressed that I cannot find words to speak.

5 I have reflected on the days of old, the years of ancient times.

6 I recall the songs I sang during the night; I reflect deeply within my heart, and my spirit earnestly seeks understanding.

7 Will the Lord reject us forever? Will He no longer show us His favor?

8 Has His mercy completely vanished forever? Does His promise fail to endure for all time?

9 Has God ceased to be gracious? Has He, in His anger, restrained His compassionate mercies? Pause and reflect on this.

10 And I said, "This is my weakness, but I will recall the years of the mighty power of the Most High."

11 I will recall the deeds of the LORD; indeed, I will reflect on your marvelous works from times past.

12 I will reflect deeply on all your works and speak of your mighty deeds.

13 Your ways, O God, are revealed in the holy place. Who is a God as mighty as our God?

14 You are the God who performs extraordinary deeds; you have made your power known among the nations.

15 With your mighty power, you have rescued your people, the descendants of Jacob and Joseph. Selah.

16 The waters saw you, O God, the waters saw you and trembled; even the depths were shaken with fear.

17 The clouds poured down rain; the heavens resounded with thunder; your lightning bolts flashed across the sky.

18 The sound of your thunder echoed through the heavens; the lightning flashes illuminated the world. The earth quaked and trembled.

19 Your way is through the sea, and your path through the vast waters, yet your footprints leave no trace.

20 You guided your people like a flock, leading them by the hand of Moses and Aaron.
