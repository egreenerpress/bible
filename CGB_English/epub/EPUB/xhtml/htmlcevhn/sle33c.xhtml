Micah Chapter 3

1 And I said, "Please listen, you leaders of Jacob and rulers of the house of Israel! Should it not be your responsibility to understand justice?"

2 They despise what is good and embrace what is evil; they strip the skin from people and the flesh from their bones.

3 They even consume the flesh of my people, strip the skin from their bodies, and break their bones into pieces. They chop them up like meat for the pot, like flesh to be cooked in a cauldron.

4 At that time, they will cry out to the LORD, but he will not answer them. He will turn his face away from them, for they have acted wickedly in their deeds.

5 This is what the LORD says concerning the prophets who lead my people astray, who proclaim peace while they bite with their teeth, but declare war against anyone who does not feed them: They cry out "Peace" when they have something to eat, but if someone fails to provide for them, they are quick to stir up conflict.

6 Therefore, night will come upon you, and you will have no vision; darkness will cover you, and you will not be able to practice divination. The sun will set on the prophets, and the day will grow dark for them.

7 At that time, those who claim to see visions will be put to shame, and those who practice divination will be disgraced. Indeed, they will all cover their mouths, for there will be no response from God.

8 Indeed, I am filled with strength by the Spirit of the Lord, with justice, and with courage, to proclaim to Jacob his rebellion and to Israel his sin.

9 Listen carefully, you leaders of the house of Jacob, you rulers of the house of Israel, who despise justice and twist what is right.

10 They construct Zion through violence and establish Jerusalem through wrongdoing.

11 The leaders of the people make decisions for bribes, the priests teach for payment, and the prophets deliver messages for money. Yet they still claim to rely on the LORD, saying, "Isn’t the LORD with us? No harm can come to us."

12 Therefore, because of your actions, Zion will be plowed like a field, Jerusalem will be reduced to ruins, and the mountain of the Lord’s temple will become like the overgrown heights of a forest.
