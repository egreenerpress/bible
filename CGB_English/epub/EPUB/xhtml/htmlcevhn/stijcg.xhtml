Exodus Chapter 17

1 The entire community of the Israelites set out from the wilderness of Sin, continuing their journey as the LORD had commanded. They camped at Rephidim, but there was no water there for the people to drink.

2 So the people complained to Moses, saying, "Give us water to drink." Moses replied to them, "Why are you arguing with me? Why are you testing the LORD?"

3 The people grew thirsty there for water, and they complained against Moses, saying, "Why have you brought us out of Egypt? Was it to let us, our children, and our livestock die of thirst?"

4 Moses cried out to the LORD, saying, "What should I do with these people? They are almost ready to stone me!"

5 The LORD said to Moses, "Go ahead of the people, and take some of the elders of Israel with you. Bring the staff you used to strike the Nile River, hold it in your hand, and go."

6 Listen carefully: I will stand before you there on the rock at Horeb. When you strike the rock, water will flow out of it, and the people will be able to drink. Moses did exactly as he was told, in the presence of the elders of Israel.

7 He named the place Massah and Meribah because of the quarreling of the Israelites and because they tested the LORD by asking, "Is the LORD with us or not?"

8 Then the Amalekites came and attacked Israel at Rephidim.

9 Moses said to Joshua, "Select some men for us and go out to fight against Amalek. Tomorrow, I will stand on the top of the hill with the staff of God in my hand."

10 Joshua did as Moses had instructed him and engaged in battle with the Amalekites. Meanwhile, Moses, Aaron, and Hur ascended to the top of the hill.

11 When Moses raised his hand, the Israelites gained the upper hand in battle; but when he lowered his hand, the Amalekites began to prevail.

12 But Moses' hands grew weary, so they took a stone and placed it under him, and he sat on it. Aaron and Hur supported his hands, one on each side, and his hands remained steady until the sun set.

13 Joshua defeated Amalek and his people with the sword, overwhelming them completely.

14 The LORD said to Moses, "Write this down as a permanent record in a book, and declare it clearly to Joshua, for I will completely erase the memory of Amalek from under heaven."

15 Moses constructed an altar and named it "The Lord is My Banner."

16 For he declared, "Because the LORD has sworn that the LORD will wage war against Amalek throughout every generation."
