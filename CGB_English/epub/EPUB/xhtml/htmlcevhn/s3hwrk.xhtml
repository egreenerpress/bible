I Peter Chapter 5

1 I, who am also an elder and a witness of Christ’s sufferings, as well as a participant in the glory that is to be revealed, urge the elders among you:

2 Care for the flock of God that is entrusted to you, overseeing it not out of obligation, but willingly, as God desires; not for the sake of dishonest gain, but with a sincere and eager heart.

3 Do not act as though you are ruling over those entrusted to God’s care, but instead serve as examples for the flock to follow.

4 And when the supreme Shepherd appears, you will receive an unfading crown of glory.

5 In the same way, you who are younger, submit yourselves to those who are older. Indeed, all of you should be humble toward one another, and clothe yourselves with humility, because God opposes the proud but shows favor to the humble.

6 Therefore, submit yourselves humbly under the powerful hand of God, so that at the proper time he may lift you up.

7 Entrust all your worries and concerns to him, because he deeply cares for you.

8 Stay alert and be self-controlled, for your enemy the devil prowls around like a roaring lion, searching for someone to destroy.

9 Stand firm against him, unwavering in your faith, knowing that the same kinds of suffering are being endured by your fellow believers throughout the world.

10 But the God of all grace, who has called you to share in his eternal glory through Christ Jesus, will himself restore, strengthen, establish, and ground you after you have endured suffering for a little while.

11 To Him belong all glory and sovereign power for all eternity. Amen.

12 Through Silvanus, whom I regard as a faithful brother to you, I have written this brief letter to encourage you and to affirm that the grace of God in which you stand is indeed genuine and true.

13 The church in Babylon, chosen alongside you, sends you greetings, as does Mark, my spiritual son.

14 Greet one another with a loving embrace. May peace be with all of you who are in Christ Jesus. Amen.
