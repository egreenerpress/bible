Psalms Chapter 58

1 (To the director of music, set to the tune of "Do Not Destroy," a sacred poem of David.) Do you truly speak with justice, O assembly? Do you judge fairly, O people?

2 Indeed, in your hearts you devise wickedness; with your hands you carry out violence upon the earth.

3 The wicked are separated from God even from birth; they go astray from the moment they are born, speaking deceit.

4 Their venom is as deadly as that of a serpent; they are like the deaf viper that refuses to listen.

5 Which will not listen to the voice of enchanters, no matter how skillfully they cast their spells.

6 O God, shatter their teeth in their mouths; break the fangs of these young lions, O LORD.

7 Let them dissolve like flowing water that never stops; when he draws his bow to shoot his arrows, may they be shattered into pieces.

8 Like a snail that dissolves as it moves, may each of them fade away; like a stillborn child, may they never see the light of day.

9 Before your cooking pots can even feel the heat of the burning thorns, God will sweep them away like a whirlwindâ€”both the living and the dead, consumed in His fierce anger.

10 The righteous will rejoice when they witness the execution of justice; they will tread upon the blood of the wicked as a sign of triumph.

11 So that people will declare, "Truly, there is a reward for the righteous; indeed, there is a God who judges justly on the earth."
