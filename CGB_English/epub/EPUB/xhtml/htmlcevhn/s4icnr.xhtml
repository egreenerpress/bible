Proverbs Chapter 13

1 A wise son listens to his father’s teaching, but a mocker pays no attention to correction.

2 A person will enjoy the benefits of what they say, but those who act deceitfully will suffer the consequences of their wrongdoing.

3 Whoever guards their words preserves their life, but whoever speaks recklessly will face ruin.

4 The lazy person craves and gets nothing, but the diligent person’s desires are fully satisfied.

5 A person of integrity detests deceit, but a wicked individual is disgraceful and brings shame upon themselves.

6 Righteousness protects those who live with integrity, guiding them on the right path, but wickedness brings ruin to those who choose to sin.

7 Some people appear to be wealthy, yet they possess nothing of true value; others seem poor, yet they hold great and enduring riches.

8 A person's wealth can serve as the price to save their life, but the poor are not subjected to such threats or demands.

9 The light of the righteous brings joy, but the lamp of the wicked will be extinguished.

10 Conflict arises only through pride, but wisdom is found among those who seek good counsel.

11 Wealth acquired through dishonest or empty means will dwindle, but those who earn it through honest labor will see it grow.

12 When hope is delayed, it weighs heavily on the heart, causing sorrow and despair. But when the long-awaited desire is fulfilled, it brings life and joy, like a flourishing tree that sustains and renews.

13 Whoever disregards or rejects the word of God will face ruin, but the one who reverently respects and obeys His commandment will be blessed.

14 The instruction of the wise is like a spring of life-giving water, guiding one away from the traps that lead to death.

15 Good insight brings favor, but the path of those who defy God is filled with difficulty.

16 A sensible person acts with understanding, but a fool exposes their foolishness.

17 A deceitful messenger brings trouble, but a trustworthy envoy promotes well-being.

18 Whoever rejects correction will face poverty and disgrace, but those who heed constructive criticism will gain honor and respect.

19 When a longing is fulfilled, it brings deep satisfaction to the heart; but for fools, turning away from evil is detestable.

20 Whoever associates with the wise will become wise, but whoever keeps company with fools will suffer harm.

21 Evil relentlessly chases after those who sin, but good will be repaid to those who live righteously.

22 A righteous person leaves an inheritance not only for their children but also for their grandchildren, while the wealth accumulated by the wicked is ultimately stored up for the upright.

23 In the fields of the poor, there is often an abundance of food, but it can be ruined due to a lack of sound judgment.

24 Whoever withholds discipline shows hatred toward their child, but the one who loves them is diligent to correct them early on.

25 The righteous eat and find satisfaction for their soul, but the stomach of the wicked will always lack.
