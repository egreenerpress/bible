Deuteronomy Chapter 5

1 Moses summoned all the people of Israel and said to them, "Listen, Israel, to the laws and decrees that I am proclaiming to you today. Learn them well, and be careful to observe and follow them."

2 The LORD our God established a covenant with us at Horeb.

3 The LORD did not establish this covenant with our ancestors, but with us—with all of us who are alive here today.

4 The LORD spoke with you directly, face to face, on the mountain from the midst of the fire.

5 I stood as an intermediary between the LORD and you at that time to declare to you the word of the LORD, because you were afraid of the fire and did not go up the mountain. He said,

6 I am the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, out of the place of slavery.

7 You must not have any other gods besides me.

8 You must not make for yourself any carved image or any likeness of anything that is in the heavens above, or on the earth below, or in the waters under the earth.

9 You must not bow down to them or worship them, for I, the LORD your God, am a God who demands exclusive devotion. I hold accountable the wrongdoing of the parents, bringing consequences upon their children, even to the third and fourth generation of those who reject me.

10 And I will show steadfast love to thousands of those who love me and faithfully observe my commandments.

11 You must not misuse the name of the LORD your God, for the LORD will not consider anyone guiltless who misuses His name.

12 Observe the Sabbath day by keeping it holy, as the LORD your God has commanded you.

13 For six days, you shall work and accomplish all your tasks.

14 The seventh day is the Sabbath dedicated to the LORD your God. On that day, you must not do any work—neither you, nor your son or daughter, nor your male or female servant, nor your ox, nor your donkey, nor any of your livestock, nor the foreigner residing within your gates. This is so that your male and female servants may rest just as you do.

15 Remember that you were once slaves in the land of Egypt, and the LORD your God brought you out from there with great power and a strong, outstretched arm. For this reason, the LORD your God has commanded you to observe the Sabbath day.

16 Respect and honor your father and your mother, as the LORD your God has commanded you, so that your life may be long and prosperous in the land that the LORD your God is giving to you.

17 You must not commit murder.

18 You must not engage in adultery.

19 You must not steal.

20 You must not give false testimony against your neighbor.

21 You must not desire your neighbor’s wife, nor should you covet your neighbor’s house, his field, his male servant, his female servant, his ox, his donkey, or anything that belongs to your neighbor.

22 The LORD spoke these words to your entire assembly on the mountain from the midst of the fire, the cloud, and the deep darkness, with a mighty voice, and He said nothing more. He then inscribed them on two stone tablets and gave them to me.

23 When you heard the voice speaking from the midst of the darkness—while the mountain was ablaze with fire—you approached me, all of you, including the leaders of your tribes and your elders.

24 You declared, "The LORD our God has revealed to us His glory and His majesty, and we have heard His voice from the midst of the fire. Today we have witnessed that God speaks with humanity, and yet we remain alive."

25 Why should we risk death now? This immense fire will utterly consume us. If we continue to hear the voice of the LORD our God, we will surely die.

26 For who among all humanity has heard the voice of the living God speaking from the midst of the fire, as we have, and yet survived?

27 Approach and listen to everything that the LORD our God will say. Then, tell us all that the LORD our God speaks to you, and we will listen and obey.

28 The LORD heard what you said when you spoke to me, and the LORD said to me, "I have heard the words of this people, which they have spoken to you. Everything they have said is right."

29 If only they had such a heart within them—to revere me and to keep all my commandments always, so that it might go well with them and with their children forever!

30 Go and tell them, "Return to your tents."

31 But as for you, stand here beside me, and I will declare to you all the commands, the statutes, and the ordinances that you are to teach them, so that they may observe them in the land I am giving them to possess.

32 You must be careful to carry out everything that the LORD your God has commanded you. Do not deviate from it, turning neither to the right nor to the left.

33 You must follow all the ways that the LORD your God has commanded you, so that you may live, prosper, and enjoy long life in the land that you are about to take possession of.
