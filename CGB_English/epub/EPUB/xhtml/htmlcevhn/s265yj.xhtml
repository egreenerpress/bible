Hosea Chapter 12

1 Ephraim chases after the wind and pursues the east wind; they continually multiply lies and violence. They make alliances with Assyria, while sending olive oil as tribute to Egypt.

2 The LORD also has a dispute with Judah and will hold Jacob accountable for his actions; He will repay him according to his deeds.

3 In the womb, he grasped his brother's heel, and in his strength, he contended with God.

4 Indeed, he struggled with the angel and overcame him; he wept and pleaded with him. He encountered him at Bethel, and there he spoke with us.

5 The LORD, the God of all heavenly armies; the LORD is the name by which He is remembered.

6 Therefore, return to your God; maintain steadfast love and justice, and continually place your hope in your God.

7 He is a merchant, and in his hand are dishonest scales; he delights in exploiting others.

8 And Ephraim said, "Now I have become wealthy; I have gained great possessions. In all my efforts, no one will find any wrongdoing in me that could be called sin."

9 I, the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, will once again cause you to live in temporary shelters, as you did during the days of the sacred festival.

10 I have also communicated through the prophets, and I have given many visions, and employed parables and comparisons, all through the work of the prophets.

11 Is there no wrongdoing in Gilead? Truly, their actions are meaningless and empty. They offer sacrifices of bulls in Gilgal, yet their altars are like piles of stones scattered across the plowed fields.

12 Jacob fled to the land of Aram, and Israel worked in exchange for a wife; indeed, he tended sheep to earn her.

13 The LORD led Israel out of Egypt through the guidance of a prophet, and by that same prophet, He ensured their protection and preservation.

14 Ephraim deeply provoked Him to anger; therefore, the guilt of his bloodshed will remain upon him, and the disgrace he caused will be returned to him by his Lord.
