Deuteronomy Chapter 14

1 You are the children of the LORD your God. You must not cut yourselves or shave your foreheads as a sign of mourning for the dead.

2 For you are a people set apart as holy to the LORD your God, and the LORD has chosen you to be His treasured possession, distinct from all the other nations on the earth.

3 You must not eat anything that is detestable.

4 Here are the animals you are permitted to eat: the ox, the sheep, and the goat.

5 The deer, the gazelle, the fallow deer, the wild goat, the ibex, the antelope, and the mountain sheep.

6 You may eat any animal that has a divided hoof, with the hoof completely split into two parts, and that also chews the cud among the animals.

7 However, among those animals that chew the cud or have a divided hoof, you must not eat the following: the camel, the hare, and the rock badger. Although they chew the cud, they do not have a divided hoof; therefore, they are unclean for you.

8 The pig, though it has a divided hoof, does not chew the cud; it is unclean for you. You must not eat its meat or touch its dead body.

9 You may eat any creatures that live in the waters, but only those that have both fins and scales are permitted for you to eat.

10 You must not eat anything that does not have fins and scales; it is unclean for you.

11 You may eat any bird that is considered clean.

12 But these are the birds you must not eat: the eagle, the bearded vulture, and the osprey.

13 And the red kite, the black kite, and every kind of vulture.

14 And every raven according to its kind,

15 And the owl, the night hawk, the cuckoo, and the hawk, each according to its kind,

16 The little owl, the great owl, and the desert owl...

17 And the pelican, the vulture, and the cormorant.

18 And the stork, the heron according to its kind, the hoopoe, and the bat.

19 And every winged insect that moves along the ground is unclean for you; you must not eat them.

20 You are permitted to eat any bird that is considered clean.

21 You must not eat anything that dies on its own. You may give it to the foreigner living among you, so that they may eat it, or you may sell it to a foreigner. For you are a people holy to the LORD your God. You must not boil a young goat in its mother's milk.

22 You must faithfully set aside a tenth of all the produce from your crops, which the field yields each year.

23 You are to eat in the presence of the LORD your God, in the place where He chooses to establish His name, the tithe of your grain, your wine, and your olive oil, as well as the firstborn of your herds and flocks. This is so that you may learn to revere the LORD your God continually.

24 If the journey is too great for you, so that you are unable to carry it, or if the place the LORD your God chooses to establish his name is too far from you, when the LORD your God has blessed you,

25 Then you shall convert it into money, secure the money in your possession, and go to the place that the LORD your God will choose.

26 You are to use that money for whatever your heart desires—whether for cattle, sheep, wine, fermented drink, or anything else you long for. Then you shall eat there in the presence of the LORD your God, and you shall rejoice, both you and your household.

27 And the Levite who lives within your community—you must not neglect him, for he has no share or inheritance of land among you.

28 At the end of every three years, you must bring out all the tithe of your produce from that year and store it within your towns.

29 The Levite, who has no portion or inheritance with you, as well as the foreigner, the fatherless, and the widow living within your community, shall come and eat until they are satisfied. This is so that the LORD your God may bless you in all the work you do.
