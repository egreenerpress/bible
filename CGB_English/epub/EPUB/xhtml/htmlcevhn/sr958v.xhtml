Psalms Chapter 106

1 Praise the LORD. Give thanks to the LORD, for He is good; His steadfast love endures forever.

2 Who can fully declare the mighty deeds of the LORD? Who is able to proclaim all the greatness of His praise?

3 Blessed are those who uphold justice and continually practice righteousness.

4 Remember me, O LORD, with the kindness you show to your people; come to me with your saving help.

5 Allow me to witness the blessings you bestow upon your chosen ones, so that I may share in the joy of your people and take pride in being part of your treasured possession.

6 We have sinned, just as our ancestors did; we have acted unjustly and done what is evil.

7 Our ancestors failed to comprehend your miraculous works in Egypt; they did not recall the abundance of your steadfast love. Instead, they rebelled against you at the sea, even at the Red Sea.

8 Yet He saved them for the sake of His name, so that He might reveal His great power and make it known.

9 He commanded the Red Sea, and it dried up completely; thus, He guided them through the deep waters as if they were walking through a dry wilderness.

10 He rescued them from the power of those who hated them and delivered them from the grasp of their enemies.

11 The waters overwhelmed their enemies; not a single one remained.

12 Then they trusted in his words; they sang praises to him.

13 They quickly forgot His deeds; they did not wait for His guidance.

14 But in the wilderness, they were consumed by intense cravings and put God to the test in the barren wasteland.

15 He granted their request, but brought a wasting condition upon their inner being.

16 In the camp, they grew envious of Moses, and even of Aaron, who was set apart as holy to the LORD.

17 The ground split open and engulfed Dathan, then closed over the assembly of Abiram.

18 A fire broke out among their assembly; the flames consumed the wicked.

19 They fashioned a calf at Horeb and bowed down to worship an idol made of metal.

20 In this way, they exchanged their glorious God for the likeness of an ox that feeds on grass.

21 They forgot God, their Savior, who had accomplished great deeds in Egypt.

22 He performed amazing deeds in the land of Egypt and awe-inspiring acts by the Red Sea.

23 Therefore, God declared that He would destroy them, had not Moses, His chosen one, stood before Him in the gap to intervene, turning away His wrath so that He would not bring about their destruction.

24 Indeed, they scorned the delightful land and refused to trust in His promise.

25 But they complained in their tents and refused to listen to the voice of the LORD.

26 Therefore, he raised his hand against them, intending to destroy them in the wilderness.

27 To destroy their descendants among the nations and to disperse them across the lands.

28 They aligned themselves with Baal-peor and partook in the offerings made to lifeless idols.

29 In this way, they angered Him with their sinful schemes, and a plague broke out among them.

30 Then Phinehas rose up and carried out justice, and as a result, the plague was halted.

31 And this was credited to him as righteousness, a standing that endures for all generations, lasting forever.

32 They provoked him at the waters of Meribah, and it went badly for Moses because of their actions.

33 Because they provoked his spirit, causing him to speak rashly with his words.

34 They did not annihilate the nations, as the LORD had commanded them to do.

35 However, they associated with the nations and adopted their practices.

36 They worshiped their idols, which became a trap for them.

37 Indeed, they offered their sons and daughters as sacrifices to demons.

38 They spilled innocent blood—the blood of their own sons and daughters—whom they offered as sacrifices to the idols of Canaan. As a result, the land became defiled by this bloodshed.

39 In this way, they became defiled by their own actions and pursued unfaithfulness through their own devising.

40 Therefore, the anger of the LORD burned against His people, to the point that He detested His own chosen possession.

41 He handed them over to the power of foreign nations, and those who despised them became their rulers.

42 Their enemies also oppressed them, and they were subdued under their power.

43 Many times He rescued them, yet they continued to defy Him with their plans and were humbled because of their sin.

44 Yet He took notice of their suffering when He heard their cries for help.

45 And He called to mind His covenant on their behalf, and relented in accordance with the abundance of His steadfast love.

46 He also caused them to receive compassion from all those who had taken them captive.

47 Rescue us, O LORD our God, and bring us back from among the nations, so that we may give thanks to your holy name and celebrate your glory with joyful praise.

48 Praise be to the LORD, the God of Israel, from eternity past to eternity future. Let all the people declare, "Amen!" Give praise to the LORD!
