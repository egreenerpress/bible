Exodus Chapter 37

1 Bezalel constructed the ark out of acacia wood. It was two and a half cubits long, one and a half cubits wide, and one and a half cubits high.

2 He covered it entirely, both inside and out, with pure gold, and fashioned a gold molding around its edges.

3 He made four gold rings for it, placing them at its four corners—two rings on one side and two rings on the opposite side.

4 He made poles from acacia wood and covered them with gold.

5 He inserted the carrying poles into the rings on the sides of the ark, so that it could be transported.

6 He crafted the mercy seat out of pure gold: its length was two and a half cubits, and its width was one and a half cubits.

7 He crafted two cherubim of gold, hammered from a single piece of metal, and placed them at the two ends of the mercy seat.

8 One cherub was placed at one end, and another cherub at the opposite end. He formed the cherubim as part of the mercy seat itself, one at each of its two ends.

9 The cherubim stretched out their wings upward, covering the mercy seat with their wings. Their faces were turned toward each other, and they looked directly toward the mercy seat.

10 He constructed the table out of acacia wood. It was three feet long, one and a half feet wide, and two and a quarter feet high.

11 He covered it entirely with pure gold and crafted a gold molding around its edges.

12 He also crafted a rim around it, about a handbreadth wide, and fashioned a golden molding to encircle the rim.

13 He crafted four gold rings and attached them to the four corners at its base.

14 Opposite the edge were the rings, which served as holders for the poles used to carry the table.

15 He made the poles from acacia wood and covered them with gold, so they could be used to carry the table.

16 He crafted the utensils that were to be placed on the table: its plates, its ladles, its bowls, and its jars for pouring, all made of pure gold.

17 He crafted the lampstand out of pure gold, hammered into shape by skilled hands. The lampstand, including its base, its stem, its cups, its decorative buds, and its blossoms, was all made from a single piece of gold.

18 The lampstand had six branches extending from its sides—three branches on one side and three branches on the other side.

19 Three cups shaped like almond blossoms, each with a bud and a flower, were on one branch; and three cups shaped like almond blossoms, each with a bud and a flower, were on another branch. This pattern was repeated for all six branches extending from the lampstand.

20 The lampstand had four cups shaped like almond blossoms, complete with their ornamental knobs and flowers.

21 And there was a bud beneath each pair of branches extending from the lampstand, a bud beneath the first pair, a bud beneath the second pair, and a bud beneath the third pair—this pattern continued for all six branches that extended from it.

22 The decorative buds and their branches were all of one piece with the lampstand, crafted entirely from a single sheet of pure gold, hammered into shape.

23 He crafted the seven lamps, along with their snuffers and trays, from pure gold.

24 He crafted it from a talent of pure gold, along with all its accompanying vessels.

25 He constructed the incense altar out of acacia wood. It was square, measuring one cubit in length and one cubit in width, and it stood two cubits high. Its horns were made as part of the altar itself, all formed from a single piece of wood.

26 He covered it entirely with pure gold, including the top, the sides all around, and its horns. Additionally, he crafted a gold molding to encircle it.

27 He crafted two gold rings and attached them beneath the crown on its two corners, one on each side, to serve as holders for the poles used to carry it.

28 He made the poles from acacia wood and covered them with gold.

29 He prepared the sacred anointing oil and the pure incense of fragrant spices, skillfully blended as a perfumer would craft them.
