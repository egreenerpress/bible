Job Chapter 37

1 At this, my heart trembles and is shaken from its place.

2 Listen carefully to the roar of His voice, to the sound that comes forth from His mouth.

3 He guides it across the entire sky, and his lightning reaches to the farthest corners of the earth.

4 After it comes the sound of his roaring: he thunders with his majestic voice, and he does not hold back when his voice is heard.

5 God thunders with a wondrous voice; he does mighty works that are beyond our understanding.

6 For He commands the snow, saying, "Fall upon the earth," and likewise the gentle rain and the mighty downpour of His power.

7 He restricts the ability of every person, so that all may recognize His work.

8 Then the wild animals retreat to their dens and stay in their hiding places.

9 From the south comes the whirlwind, and from the north, the biting cold.

10 By the breath of God, frost is formed, and the expanse of the waters is constrained.

11 Moreover, by His power He burdens the thick cloud with moisture; He disperses the cloud of His lightning.

12 The text is turned by His guidance, so that they may carry out all that He commands them across the face of the earth.

13 He brings it about, whether for discipline, for the sake of His land, or as an act of compassion.

14 Listen carefully to this, Job: pause and reflect deeply on the marvelous deeds of God.

15 Do you know when God established these things and made the light of His cloud shine forth?

16 Do you understand how the clouds are balanced in the sky, the marvelous works of the One whose knowledge is perfect?

17 How do your clothes keep you warm when He quiets the earth with the south wind?

18 Have you, alongside Him, stretched out the heavens, firm and unyielding, like a mirror of cast metal?

19 Instruct us on what we should say to Him, for we cannot arrange our words properly because of the profound ignorance that surrounds us.

20 Should I inform Him of what I have to say? If a person were to speak, would they not be overwhelmed?

21 Now, people cannot see the brilliant light that shines through the clouds, but when the wind sweeps by, it clears them away.

22 Clear skies come from the north; with God is awe-inspiring majesty.

23 Concerning the Almighty, we cannot fully comprehend Him; He is supreme in power, perfect in judgment, and abundant in righteousness. He does not act unjustly.

24 Therefore, people should stand in awe of him, for he does not regard those who consider themselves wise in their own understanding.
