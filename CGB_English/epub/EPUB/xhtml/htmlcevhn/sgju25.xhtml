Isaiah Chapter 23

1 Wail, you ships of Tarshish, for Tyre has been destroyed, leaving no house standing, no harbor to enter. This devastation has been made known to them from the land of Chittim.

2 Remain calm, you who dwell on the island; you whom the merchants of Sidon, crossing the sea, have supplied abundantly.

3 The seed of Sihor, the harvest of the river, brought by abundant waters, is her income; and she is a marketplace for the nations.

4 Be ashamed, O Sidon, for the sea has spoken, even the mighty sea, saying, "I do not labor in childbirth, nor do I bring forth children. I do not raise young men, nor do I nurture young women."

5 Just as the news about Egypt brought great distress, so too will the news about Tyre cause them deep anguish.

6 Cross over to Tarshish; wail, you who dwell on the coast.

7 Is this your celebrated city, whose origins stretch back to ancient times? Yet now, her own feet will carry her far away to live as a stranger in a distant land.

8 Who has devised this plan against Tyre, the renowned and majestic city, whose merchants are like nobles and whose traders are among the most esteemed in the world?

9 The LORD of hosts has determined this, to tarnish the arrogance of all that is glorious and to disgrace all who are esteemed on the earth.

10 Flow through your land like a river, O people of Tarshish; your strength has come to an end.

11 He extended his hand over the sea and caused kingdoms to tremble. The LORD has issued a decree against the city of merchants, to demolish its fortified strongholds.

12 And he declared, "You will no longer rejoice, O oppressed virgin, daughter of Sidon. Rise up and cross over to Cyprus; even there you will find no rest."

13 Look at the land of the Chaldeansâ€”this nation did not exist until the Assyrian established it for those who live in the desert. They built its towers and raised up its palaces, but he brought it to destruction.

14 Wail, you ships of Tarshish, for your might has been utterly destroyed.

15 And it will happen in that day that Tyre will be forgotten for seventy years, corresponding to the lifespan of a single king. After the seventy years have ended, Tyre will sing like a prostitute.

16 Take up a harp, walk through the city, you forgotten woman of pleasure; play beautiful music, sing many songs, so that you may be remembered.

17 And it will happen after seventy years have passed that the LORD will take notice of Tyre, and she will return to her trade. She will engage in immoral dealings with all the kingdoms of the world across the face of the earth.

18 Her trade and earnings will be dedicated to the LORD as holy. They will not be stored up or hoarded, for her profits will be used to provide for those who serve in the presence of the LORD, ensuring they have enough to eat and durable clothing to wear.
