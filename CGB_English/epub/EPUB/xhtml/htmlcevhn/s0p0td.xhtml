Hebrews Chapter 13

1 Let the love between fellow believers remain strong and enduring.

2 Do not neglect to show hospitality to strangers, for by doing so, some have unknowingly welcomed angels.

3 Remember those who are in chains, as though you were bound with them, and those who are suffering hardship, as though you yourselves were enduring it in your own bodies.

4 Marriage is to be held in honor by all, and the marriage bed is to remain pure and undefiled. However, God will judge those who engage in sexual immorality and adultery.

5 Let your speech and conduct be free from greed, and be satisfied with what you have. For God himself has said, "I will never abandon you, nor will I ever desert you."

6 Therefore, we can confidently declare, "The Lord is my helper, and I will not be afraid of what any person may do to me."

7 Remember your leaders, who have spoken to you the word of God. Reflect on the outcome of their way of life and imitate their faith.

8 Jesus Christ remains unchanged—yesterday, today, and forever.

9 Do not be swayed by various and unfamiliar teachings. It is beneficial for the heart to be firmly grounded in grace, not in rules about food, which have not brought any real advantage to those who have focused on them.

10 We have an altar from which those who serve in the tabernacle have no right to partake.

11 The bodies of those animals whose blood is brought into the holy place by the high priest as a sin offering are burned outside the camp.

12 Therefore, Jesus also suffered outside the city gate, so that by his own blood he might purify the people and make them holy.

13 Therefore, let us go out to him outside the camp, enduring the disgrace he bore.

14 For we do not have a permanent city here on earth, but we are seeking the one that is to come.

15 Therefore, through Him, let us continually offer to God a sacrifice of praise—that is, the words of our lips giving thanks to His name.

16 Do not neglect to do good and to share with others, for such acts of sacrifice are pleasing to God.

17 Follow the guidance of those who lead you and place yourselves under their authority, for they are responsible for watching over your spiritual well-being, as those who will have to give an account to God. Let them carry out this duty with joy and not with sorrow, for that would be of no benefit to you.

18 Pray for us, for we are confident that we have a clear conscience, desiring to live honorably in every way.

19 I urge you all the more to do this, so that I may be returned to you more quickly.

20 Now may the God of peace, who brought back from the dead our Lord Jesus, the great Shepherd of the sheep, by the power of the blood of the eternal covenant,

21 May God equip you fully for every good work, enabling you to carry out His will. May He work within you what is pleasing in His sight, through Jesus Christ. To Him be glory forever and ever. Amen.

22 I urge you, brothers and sisters, to patiently receive this message of encouragement, for I have written to you briefly.

23 Be aware that our brother Timothy has been released; if he arrives soon, I will see you together with him.

24 Greet all those who are in leadership over you, and all the believers. The believers from Italy send their greetings to you.

25 May grace be with all of you. Amen.
