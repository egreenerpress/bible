Luke Chapter 23

1 The entire crowd rose up and brought him before Pilate.

2 They started to accuse him, saying, "We found this man misleading the nation, telling people not to pay taxes to Caesar, and claiming that he himself is the Messiah, a king."

3 Pilate questioned him, asking, "Are you the King of the Jews?" Jesus replied, "You have said it."

4 Then Pilate said to the chief priests and the crowd, "I find no guilt in this man."

5 They became even more aggressive, declaring, "He is inciting the people, spreading his teachings throughout all Judea, starting from Galilee and continuing to this very place."

6 When Pilate heard the mention of Galilee, he inquired whether the man was from Galilee.

7 When he learned that the man was under Herod’s authority, he sent him to Herod, who was also in Jerusalem at that time.

8 When Herod saw Jesus, he was extremely pleased, for he had wanted to see him for a long time. He had heard many reports about him and hoped to witness some miracle performed by him.

9 Then he questioned him at length, but he gave him no reply.

10 The high priests and religious scholars stood before him, making strong and forceful accusations against him.

11 Herod, along with his soldiers, treated him with contempt and ridiculed him. They dressed him in a splendid robe and sent him back to Pilate.

12 On that same day, Pilate and Herod became friends with each other, for previously they had been hostile toward one another.

13 Pilate then summoned the chief priests, the leaders, and the people together.

14 He said to them, "You have brought this man to me, claiming that he misleads the people. But after examining him in your presence, I have found no basis for the charges you are making against him."

15 No, not even Herod, for I sent you to him, and look, he has found nothing deserving of death in him.

16 I will therefore have him punished and then release him.

17 For it was required that he release one prisoner to them during the festival.

18 And they all shouted together, saying, "Take this man away, and release Barabbas to us!"

19 (He was thrown into prison because he had caused a rebellion in the city and had committed murder.)

20 Pilate, therefore, wanting to set Jesus free, spoke to them again.

21 But they shouted, saying, "Put him to death on the cross! Put him to death on the cross!"

22 And he said to them a third time, "Why? What wrong has he done? I have found no grounds for a death sentence in him. Therefore, I will have him punished and then release him."

23 They pressed urgently with loud voices, demanding that he be crucified. And the voices of the crowd, along with those of the chief priests, won out.

24 Pilate then issued his judgment, granting what they had demanded.

25 Then he released to them the man who had been imprisoned for rebellion and murder, the one they had asked for; but he handed over Jesus to their will.

26 As they took Jesus away, they seized a man named Simon, who was from Cyrene and was coming in from the countryside. They placed the cross on him and made him carry it behind Jesus.

27 A large crowd of people followed him, including many women who were mourning and weeping for him.

28 But Jesus turned to them and said, "Women of Jerusalem, do not weep for me; instead, weep for yourselves and for your children."

29 Listen carefully, for the time is approaching when people will say, "Blessed are those who never had children, the women who never gave birth, and the breasts that never nursed."

30 At that time, people will cry out to the mountains, "Fall on us!" and to the hills, "Cover us!"

31 If such things are done when the circumstances are favorable, what will happen when they are unfavorable?

32 And there were also two others, criminals, who were led with him to be executed.

33 When they arrived at the place called Calvary, they crucified Him there, along with two criminals—one on His right and the other on His left.

34 Then Jesus said, "Father, forgive them, for they do not understand what they are doing." And they divided his clothing among themselves by casting lots.

35 The crowd stood watching, and even the leaders joined in, mocking him. They said, "He saved others; let him save himself, if he is the Messiah, God’s chosen one."

36 The soldiers also ridiculed him, approaching him and offering him sour wine.

37 And they said, "If you are the king of the Jews, save yourself!"

38 Above him, a notice was written in Greek, Latin, and Hebrew: THIS IS THE KING OF THE JEWS.

39 One of the criminals who were being crucified alongside him hurled insults at him, saying, "If you are the Messiah, save yourself—and us as well!"

40 But the other criminal responded, rebuking him, saying, "Don’t you fear God, since you are under the same judgment?"

41 We are receiving what we deserve, for our actions have earned us this punishment. But this man has done nothing wrong.

42 And he said to Jesus, "Lord, remember me when you come into your kingdom."

43 And Jesus said to him, "Truly, I tell you, today you will be with me in paradise."

44 It was around noon, and darkness came over the entire land, lasting until three in the afternoon.

45 The sun became darkened, and the curtain of the temple was torn in two, right down the middle.

46 When Jesus cried out in a loud voice, he said, “Father, into your hands I entrust my spirit.” And after saying this, he breathed his last.

47 When the centurion witnessed what had taken place, he praised God, declaring, "Truly, this man was innocent and righteous."

48 All the people who had gathered to witness these events, seeing what had taken place, were deeply moved and struck their chests in grief, then turned and went away.

49 All those who knew Him, along with the women who had accompanied Him from Galilee, stood at a distance, watching these events unfold.

50 And there was a man named Joseph, a member of the council, who was a good and righteous man.

51 This man had not agreed with their decision or actions. He was from Arimathea, a Jewish town, and he was also one who was eagerly anticipating the kingdom of God.

52 This man went to Pilate and requested the body of Jesus.

53 He took the body down, wrapped it in linen cloth, and placed it in a tomb cut out of rock, where no one had ever been laid before.

54 That day was the day of preparation, and the Sabbath was about to begin.

55 The women who had accompanied Jesus from Galilee also followed behind and observed the tomb, taking note of how his body was placed there.

56 They went back and prepared spices and perfumes. Then they rested on the Sabbath, in keeping with the commandment.
