Exodus Chapter 19

1 In the third month after the Israelites had left the land of Egypt, on that very day, they arrived in the wilderness of Sinai.

2 They had left Rephidim and arrived at the wilderness of Sinai, where they set up camp in the desert. There, the people of Israel encamped in front of the mountain.

3 Moses ascended to meet with God, and the LORD called out to him from the mountain, saying, "This is what you are to say to the house of Jacob and declare to the people of Israel."

4 You have witnessed what I did to the Egyptians, and how I carried you as if on eagles’ wings and brought you to myself.

5 Now, if you truly obey my voice and faithfully keep my covenant, then you will be my treasured possession above all other nations, for the entire earth belongs to me.

6 You shall be to me a kingdom of priests and a holy nation. These are the words you are to speak to the people of Israel.

7 Moses went and summoned the elders of the people. He then presented to them all the words that the LORD had commanded him to speak.

8 All the people responded together, saying, "Everything the LORD has commanded, we will do." Then Moses brought the people's words back to the LORD.

9 The LORD said to Moses, "Listen, I will come to you in a dense cloud so that the people may hear when I speak with you and will trust you forever." Then Moses reported the words of the people to the LORD.

10 The LORD said to Moses, "Go to the people and consecrate them today and tomorrow. Have them wash their clothes."

11 Prepare yourselves by the third day, for on that day the LORD will descend in the presence of all the people on Mount Sinai.

12 You must establish boundaries around the mountain to keep the people at a distance, and warn them, saying, “Be careful not to go up the mountain or even touch its edge. Anyone who touches the mountain must be put to death without exception.”

13 No one is to touch it, or they will surely be put to death by stoning or by being pierced with arrows. Whether it is an animal or a person, they shall not live. When the trumpet sounds a long blast, they are to approach the mountain.

14 Moses descended from the mountain to the people, consecrated them, and they washed their garments.

15 And he said to the people, "Prepare yourselves for the third day. Do not go near your wives."

16 On the third day, as morning dawned, there was thunder and lightning, and a dense cloud covered the mountain. The sound of a trumpet grew louder and louder, causing all the people in the camp to tremble with fear.

17 Moses led the people out of the camp to encounter God, and they took their place at the base of the mountain.

18 Mount Sinai was completely enveloped in smoke because the LORD descended upon it in fire. The smoke rose like the smoke of a furnace, and the entire mountain trembled violently.

19 When the sound of the trumpet grew prolonged and became increasingly louder, Moses spoke, and God responded to him with a voice.

20 The LORD descended upon Mount Sinai, reaching the summit of the mountain. Then the LORD summoned Moses to come up to the top of the mountain, and Moses ascended.

21 The LORD said to Moses, "Go down and warn the people not to force their way through to see the LORD, or else many of them will die."

22 And the priests who approach the LORD must consecrate themselves, so that the LORD does not break out against them.

23 Moses said to the LORD, "The people cannot come up to Mount Sinai, for you commanded us, saying, 'Set boundaries around the mountain and consecrate it.'"

24 The LORD said to him, "Go down, and then you and Aaron are to come back up. But do not let the priests or the people force their way through to come up to the LORD, or else He may break out against them."

25 So Moses went down to the people and spoke to them.
