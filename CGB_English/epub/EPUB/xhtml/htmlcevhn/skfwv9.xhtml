Job Chapter 11

1 Then Zophar the Naamathite replied and said,

2 Should a flood of words go unanswered? Can someone who speaks endlessly be considered righteous?

3 Do your deceitful words silence others? When you mock and ridicule, will no one rebuke you and bring you to shame?

4 For you have declared, "My teaching is flawless, and I am blameless in your sight."

5 Oh, if only God would speak and open his lips to address you directly!

6 And that He would reveal to you the profound mysteries of wisdom, for they are far greater than what you can comprehend! Understand, therefore, that God demands less from you than your wrongdoing truly deserves.

7 Can you, through your own searching, fully comprehend God? Can you uncover the Almighty to the point of complete understanding?

8 It is as vast as the heavens—what can you do? It is deeper than the realm of the dead—what can you truly understand?

9 Its extent is greater than the earth and wider than the sea.

10 If He decides to destroy, or to confine, or to assemble, then who can oppose Him?

11 For He understands the ways of those who are empty and deceitful; He also sees their wickedness. Will He not take notice of it?

12 A foolish person may strive to become wise, yet they are born as untamed and ignorant as a wild donkey's colt.

13 If you set your heart right and reach out to him with open hands;

14 If you hold wrongdoing in your hand, cast it far from you, and do not allow evil to reside in your dwelling places.

15 For then you will hold your head high, free from guilt; indeed, you will stand firm and unshaken, and you will have no fear.

16 Because you will forget your suffering, recalling it only as waters that have flowed away and vanished.

17 Your life will shine brighter than the noonday sun; you will radiate with clarity and be as radiant as the dawn.

18 You will live in security, for there is hope; indeed, you will be settled and at ease, resting safely without fear.

19 You will lie down in peace, with no one to cause you fear; indeed, many will seek your favor and appeal to you.

20 But the eyes of the wicked will grow dim, and they will find no way to escape; their hope will fade away like a dying breath.
