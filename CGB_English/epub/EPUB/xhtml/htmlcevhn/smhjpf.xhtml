Genesis Chapter 23

1 Sarah lived to be one hundred and twenty-seven years old; these were the years of her life.

2 Sarah died in Kiriath-arba, which is Hebron, in the land of Canaan. Abraham came to mourn for Sarah and to weep over her.

3 Abraham rose from where he had been sitting near his deceased wife and addressed the Hittites, saying,

4 I am a foreigner and a temporary resident among you. Please grant me a burial site among you so that I may lay my dead to rest and remove them from my sight.

5 The descendants of Heth replied to Abraham, saying to him,

6 Listen to us, my lord: you are a powerful leader among us. Choose any of our burial sites to lay your dead to rest; none of us will deny you the use of our tomb, so that you may bury your dead.

7 Abraham rose and respectfully bowed before the people of the land, specifically the descendants of Heth.

8 He spoke with them, saying, "If it is your wish that I bury my dead out of my sight, listen to me and plead with Ephron, the son of Zohar, on my behalf."

9 So that he may grant me the cave of Machpelah, which he owns, located at the edge of his field. Let him sell it to me for its full value, so that I may have it as a burial site among you.

10 Ephron lived among the Hittites, and he responded to Abraham in the presence of all the Hittites who had gathered at the city gate, saying,

11 No, my lord, please listen to me: I give you the field, and the cave that is in it—I give it to you. In the presence of my people, I give it to you. Bury your dead.

12 And Abraham humbly lowered himself in respect before the inhabitants of the land.

13 Abraham spoke to Ephron in the presence of the people of the land, saying, "If you are willing to give it, please listen to me: I will pay you the price for the field. Accept it from me, and I will bury my dead there."

14 And Ephron responded to Abraham, saying to him,

15 My lord, please listen to me: the land is worth four hundred shekels of silver. What is that between you and me? So go ahead and bury your dead.

16 Abraham listened to Ephron, and Abraham weighed out to Ephron the amount of silver that Ephron had specified in the hearing of the Hittites—four hundred shekels of silver, according to the standard currency used by merchants.

17 The field of Ephron, located in Machpelah near Mamre—including the field itself, the cave within it, and all the trees throughout the entire area, within all its surrounding boundaries—was legally confirmed as Abraham’s possession.

18 To Abraham as a permanent possession, in the presence of the Hittites and all who had gathered at the entrance of the city gate.

19 After this, Abraham buried his wife Sarah in the cave of the field of Machpelah, near Mamre (which is Hebron) in the land of Canaan.

20 The field and the cave within it were formally secured to Abraham as a burial site by the sons of Heth, ensuring his permanent possession of it.
