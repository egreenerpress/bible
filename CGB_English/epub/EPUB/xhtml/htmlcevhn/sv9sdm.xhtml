Proverbs Chapter 26

1 Just as snow in summer and rain during harvest are out of place, so honor is not fitting for a fool.

2 Just as a bird does not fly aimlessly, and a swallow does not soar without purpose, so a curse without just cause will not take effect.

3 A whip is used to guide a horse, a bridle to direct a donkey, and a rod to correct the back of a fool.

4 Do not respond to a fool in the same foolish manner, or you will become like him.

5 Respond to a fool in a way that matches his foolishness, so that he does not become wise in his own eyes.

6 The one who entrusts a message to a fool is like someone who cuts off their own feet and brings harm upon themselves.

7 The legs of a disabled person are unequal in strength, making walking difficult; similarly, a proverb spoken by a fool is ineffective and lacks wisdom.

8 It is like tying a stone securely in a sling—just as pointless and ineffective as giving honor to a fool.

9 Just as a thorn pierces the hand of a drunkard, so is a proverb in the mouth of a fool.

10 The mighty God, who created all things, both rewards the foolish and deals with those who transgress.

11 Just as a dog goes back to its vomit, so a foolish person repeats their foolishness.

12 Do you see someone who is wise in their own eyes? There is more hope for a fool than for such a person.

13 The lazy person says, "There is a lion in the road! A lion is roaming the streets!"

14 Just as a door swings back and forth on its hinges, so does the lazy person toss and turn in their bed.

15 The lazy person hides their hand in their robe; it wearies them even to lift it back to their mouth.

16 The lazy person considers himself wiser in his own eyes than seven others who can offer sound reasoning.

17 A person who interferes in a conflict that does not concern them is like someone who grabs a dog by the ears—it is unwise and likely to lead to trouble.

18 Like a deranged person who hurls flaming torches, shoots deadly arrows, and spreads destruction.

19 This is how it is with the person who deceives their neighbor and then says, "Wasn’t I just joking?"

20 Where there is no wood, the fire dies out; and where there is no gossip, conflict comes to an end.

21 Just as hot coals fuel a fire and wood keeps it burning, so a quarrelsome person stirs up conflict.

22 The words of a gossip are like sharp wounds, piercing deep into the innermost being.

23 Flattering words spoken with a deceitful heart are like a piece of broken pottery coated with a thin layer of silver—outwardly appealing but worthless and deceptive at their core.

24 A person who harbors hatred conceals it with their words, but within them, they store up deceit.

25 When someone speaks kindly, do not trust them, for their heart is filled with seven detestable things.

26 If someone conceals their hatred through deceit, their wickedness will be exposed before the entire community.

27 Whoever digs a pit will fall into it, and whoever rolls a stone will have it roll back on them.

28 A deceitful tongue harbors hatred for those it harms, and a flattering mouth brings about destruction.
