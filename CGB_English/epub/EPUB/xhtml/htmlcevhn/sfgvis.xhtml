Psalms Chapter 141

1 (A Psalm of David.) Lord, I call out to you; hurry to help me! Listen to my voice when I cry to you.

2 May my prayer rise before you like the fragrance of incense, and may the lifting of my hands be as pleasing to you as the evening offering.

3 O LORD, guard my mouth; watch over the door of my lips.

4 Do not let my heart be drawn toward anything evil, nor lead me to engage in wicked deeds alongside those who practice wrongdoing. And may I not partake in the pleasures or delicacies they offer.

5 Let the righteous correct me—it is an act of kindness; let them rebuke me—it is like precious oil that will not harm me. Indeed, I will continue to pray for them even in their times of trouble.

6 When their leaders are cast down upon the rocky ground, they will listen to my words, for they are pleasing and comforting.

7 Our bones lie scattered at the edge of the grave, like wood that has been chopped and split upon the ground.

8 But my eyes are fixed on you, O GOD the Lord; in you I place my trust. Do not abandon my soul to despair.

9 Protect me from the traps they have set for me, and from the devices of those who do evil.

10 May the wicked be caught in their own traps, while I escape unharmed.
