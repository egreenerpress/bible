Revelation Chapter 11

1 Then I was given a measuring rod resembling a staff, and the angel stood there and said, "Get up and measure the temple of God, the altar, and those who worship there."

2 However, do not measure the outer courtyard of the temple, for it has been given over to the Gentiles. They will trample upon the holy city for forty-two months.

3 I will grant authority to my two witnesses, and they will prophesy for one thousand two hundred and sixty days, dressed in sackcloth.

4 These are the two olive trees and the two lampstands that stand before the Lord of the earth.

5 If anyone seeks to harm them, fire will come forth from their mouths and consume their enemies. And if anyone attempts to harm them, they must be killed in this way.

6 These individuals have the authority to close up the sky so that no rain falls during the time of their prophetic ministry. They also have the power to turn waters into blood and to strike the earth with every kind of plague, as often as they desire.

7 When they have completed their testimony, the beast that rises from the abyss will wage war against them, overpower them, and put them to death.

8 And their lifeless bodies will lie in the public square of the great city, which is symbolically referred to as Sodom and Egypt, the very place where our Lord was also crucified.

9 People from every tribe, language, and nation will see their dead bodies for three and a half days, and they will not allow their bodies to be placed in a tomb.

10 The people living on the earth will rejoice over them, celebrate, and exchange gifts with one another, because these two prophets had brought torment to those who dwelled on the earth.

11 After three and a half days, the breath of life from God entered them, and they stood up on their feet. Those who saw them were filled with great fear.

12 Then they heard a mighty voice from heaven calling to them, "Come up here!" And they rose up to heaven in a cloud, while their enemies watched them.

13 At that very moment, a powerful earthquake struck, and a tenth of the city collapsed. In the earthquake, seven thousand people were killed. The survivors were terrified and gave glory to the God of heaven.

14 The second disaster has passed; and now, the third disaster is coming swiftly.

15 Then the seventh angel blew his trumpet, and loud voices were heard in heaven, proclaiming: "The kingdoms of this world have now become the kingdoms of our Lord and of his Messiah, and he will reign forever and ever."

16 Then the twenty-four elders, who were seated before God on their thrones, fell face down and worshiped God.

17 We say, "We thank you, Lord God Almighty, who is, who was, and who is to come, because you have taken up your great power and have begun to reign."

18 The nations were filled with anger, and your wrath has now come.
