Numbers Chapter 16

1 Now Korah, the son of Izhar, who was the son of Kohath, who was the son of Levi, along with Dathan and Abiram, the sons of Eliab, and On, the son of Peleth—all of whom were descendants of Reuben—gathered a group of men.

2 Then a group of 250 prominent leaders from the community, well-known and respected men, rose up against Moses, along with some of the Israelites.

3 They assembled against Moses and Aaron, and confronted them, saying, "You have gone too far! The entire community is holy, every one of them, and the LORD is present among all of us. Why, then, do you exalt yourselves above the LORD's assembly?"

4 When Moses heard this, he bowed down to the ground in reverence.

5 And Moses spoke to Korah and all his followers, saying, "Tomorrow the LORD will reveal who belongs to Him and who is holy. He will bring that person near to Himself—the one He has chosen, He will allow to come near."

6 Do this: Take censers, Korah, and all your followers.

7 Place fire in the censers and offer incense before the Lord tomorrow. The man whom the Lord chooses will be the one who is holy. You Levites have gone too far in assuming this role for yourselves.

8 And Moses said to Korah, "Listen, I urge you, you sons of Levi:"

9 Do you consider it a trivial matter that the God of Israel has set you apart from the rest of the community of Israel, drawing you near to Himself to perform the work of the LORD’s tabernacle and to stand before the people to serve them?

10 He has brought you close to Himself, along with all your fellow Levites, the descendants of Levi. Yet now you also seek the priesthood?

11 Therefore, both you and all your group have united against the LORD. And who is Aaron, that you should complain against him?

12 Moses sent a message summoning Dathan and Abiram, the sons of Eliab, but they replied, "We will not come."

13 Do you consider it a trivial matter that you have brought us out of a land abundant with milk and honey, only to let us die in this wilderness? Must you also appoint yourself as our ruler?

14 Furthermore, you have not brought us into a land abundant with milk and honey, nor have you granted us possession of fields and vineyards. Are you trying to deceive us? We will not go up.

15 Moses became extremely angry and said to the LORD, "Do not accept their offering. I have not taken a single donkey from them, nor have I harmed even one of them."

16 Moses said to Korah, "You and all your followers are to present yourselves before the LORD tomorrow—you, they, and Aaron."

17 Each of you must take your censer, place incense in it, and bring it before the LORD—all 250 of you, along with Aaron, each holding your own censer.

18 Each man took his censer, placed fire in it, and added incense to it. Then they stood at the entrance of the tent of meeting, alongside Moses and Aaron.

19 Korah assembled the entire community against them at the entrance of the tent of meeting, and the glory of the LORD appeared to the whole assembly.

20 The LORD spoke to Moses and Aaron, saying,

21 Set yourselves apart from this assembly, so that I may destroy them in an instant.

22 They bowed down to the ground and said, "O God, the God who gives life to all living beings, if one person sins, will you be angry with the entire community?"

23 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

24 Speak to the assembly and say, "Move away from the tents of Korah, Dathan, and Abiram."

25 Moses stood up and went to Dathan and Abiram, accompanied by the elders of Israel.

26 And he addressed the assembly, saying, "Please, move away from the tents of these wicked men, and do not touch anything that belongs to them, or you may be swept away in the punishment for all their sins."

27 So they moved away from the area around the tents of Korah, Dathan, and Abiram on all sides. Then Dathan and Abiram came out and stood at the entrance of their tents, along with their wives, their sons, and their young children.

28 Moses said, "This is how you will know that the LORD has sent me to carry out all these works—it is not by my own will or initiative that I have done them."

29 If these men die as all people naturally die, or if they experience the same fate as everyone else, then the Lord has not sent me.

30 But if the LORD does something unprecedented—if the ground opens its mouth and swallows them up, along with everything that belongs to them, and they go down alive into the grave—then you will know that these men have defied the LORD.

31 After he had finished speaking all these words, the ground beneath them split apart.

32 The ground split open and swallowed them up, along with their households, all of Korah’s followers, and all their possessions.

33 They, along with everything connected to them, descended alive into the grave, and the earth closed over them. They were utterly destroyed, vanishing from the midst of the assembly.

34 All the Israelites who were nearby fled when they heard their cries, for they said, "We must escape, or the earth will swallow us too!"

35 Then fire came forth from the LORD and consumed the 250 men who were offering incense.

36 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

37 Tell Eleazar, the son of Aaron the priest, to gather the censers from the midst of the fire, and then scatter the burning coals far away, for these censers have been made holy.

38 The censers used by these sinners, who brought harm upon themselves, must be hammered into broad plates to cover the altar. Because these censers were presented before the LORD, they are now sacred. They will serve as a reminder to the Israelites.

39 Eleazar the priest took the bronze censers that those who had been consumed by fire had used to offer sacrifices. These censers were then hammered into broad sheets to serve as a covering for the altar.

40 This shall serve as a lasting reminder to the people of Israel, so that no outsider—anyone who is not a descendant of Aaron—may approach to burn incense before the LORD. Such a person must not become like Korah and his followers, just as the LORD warned him through Moses.

41 The next day, the entire community of the Israelites complained against Moses and Aaron, saying, "You have caused the death of the LORD's people!"

42 When the community gathered together to oppose Moses and Aaron, they turned their gaze toward the tent of meeting. Suddenly, the cloud covered it, and the glorious presence of the LORD became visible.

43 Moses and Aaron approached the tent of meeting.

44 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

45 "Move away from this assembly, so that I may destroy them in an instant." Then they threw themselves face down on the ground.

46 Moses said to Aaron, "Take a censer, place fire from the altar in it, and add incense. Then go quickly to the assembly and make atonement for them, because the LORD’s anger has been unleashed—the plague has already begun."

47 Aaron did as Moses had instructed him. He hurried into the midst of the assembly, and there he saw that the plague had already begun to spread among the people. He quickly took the incense and offered it, making atonement for the people to stop the plague.

48 And he positioned himself between the dead and the living, and the plague was halted.

49 The number of those who died in the plague was fourteen thousand seven hundred, in addition to those who had died because of the incident involving Korah.

50 Aaron went back to Moses at the entrance of the tent of meeting, and the plague was halted.
