Mark Chapter 14

1 Two days later, the Passover festival and the Feast of Unleavened Bread were about to begin. The chief priests and the teachers of the law were looking for a way to arrest Jesus secretly and have him put to death.

2 But they replied, "Not during the festival, or there might be a riot among the people."

3 While Jesus was in Bethany, at the home of Simon the leper, as he was reclining at the table, a woman came with an alabaster jar containing very expensive perfume made of pure spikenard. She broke the jar and poured the perfume over his head.

4 Some among them were inwardly upset and said, "Why was this ointment wasted in such a way?"

5 For this perfume could have been sold for more than three hundred denarii and the money given to the poor." And they began to criticize her harshly.

6 And Jesus said, "Leave her alone; why are you causing her trouble? She has done a beautiful thing for me."

7 You will always have the poor among you, and whenever you wish, you can do good for them. But you will not always have me with you.

8 She has done what she could; she has come beforehand to anoint my body for burial.

9 Truly, I tell you, wherever this gospel is proclaimed throughout the entire world, what she has done will also be told in memory of her.

10 Then Judas Iscariot, who was one of the twelve disciples, went to the chief priests to arrange how he might betray Jesus to them.

11 When they heard this, they were pleased and agreed to give him money. Then he began looking for an opportune moment to betray him.

12 On the first day of the Festival of Unleavened Bread, when the Passover lamb was to be sacrificed, His disciples asked Him, "Where do You want us to go and make preparations so You can eat the Passover meal?"

13 He sent out two of his disciples and said to them, "Go into the city, and a man carrying a water jar will meet you. Follow him."

14 Wherever he enters, say to the owner of the house, 'The Teacher asks, "Where is the guest room where I may eat the Passover with my disciples?"'

15 And he will show you a large upper room, fully furnished and ready. Prepare everything there for us.

16 His disciples went out and entered the city, where they found everything just as he had told them. Then they prepared the Passover meal.

17 In the evening, he arrived with the twelve.

18 As they were sitting and eating, Jesus said, "Truly I tell you, one of you who is sharing this meal with me will betray me."

19 Then they became deeply distressed and began to ask Him one after another, "Surely it isn’t me, is it?" And another said, "Could it be me?"

20 And he responded to them, saying, "It is one of the twelve—the one who dips bread with me in the bowl."

21 The Son of Man will indeed go, just as it has been written about him. But how terrible it will be for that man who betrays the Son of Man! It would have been better for him if he had never been born.

22 As they were eating, Jesus took bread, gave thanks, broke it, and gave it to them, saying, "Take and eat; this is my body."

23 He took the cup, and after giving thanks, he handed it to them, and they all drank from it.

24 And he said to them, "This is my blood of the new covenant, which is poured out for many."

25 Truly, I tell you, I will not drink again from the fruit of the vine until that day when I drink it anew in the kingdom of God.

26 After they had sung a hymn, they went out to the Mount of Olives.

27 And Jesus said to them, "All of you will fall away because of me this night, for it is written: 'I will strike the shepherd, and the sheep of the flock will be scattered.'"

28 But after I have risen, I will go ahead of you to Galilee.

29 But Peter said to him, "Even if everyone else falls away, I will not."

30 And Jesus said to him, "Truly, I tell you, this very day, before the rooster crows twice, you will deny me three times."

31 But he insisted even more strongly, "Even if I must die with you, I will never deny you in any way." And all the others said the same.

32 They arrived at a place called Gethsemane, and He said to His disciples, "Stay here and wait while I go over there to pray."

33 Then He took Peter, James, and John with Him and began to be deeply distressed and overwhelmed with sorrow.

34 He said to them, "My soul is deeply grieved, even to the point of death. Stay here and keep watch."

35 He went a short distance farther, fell to the ground, and prayed that, if it were possible, this hour might be taken away from him.

36 And he said, "Father, my Father, all things are possible for you. Remove this cup of suffering from me. Yet not what I desire, but what you will."

37 He came and found them sleeping, and said to Peter, "Simon, are you asleep? Could you not stay awake for one hour?"

38 Stay alert and pray, so that you do not fall into temptation. The spirit is indeed willing, but the flesh is weak.

39 Once more, he went away and prayed, repeating the same words.

40 When he returned, he found them asleep once more, for their eyes were heavy with weariness, and they did not know how to respond to him.

41 He came back a third time and said to them, "Are you still sleeping and resting? Enough! The time has come. Look, the Son of Man is about to be handed over into the hands of sinners."

42 "Get up, let us go; look, the one who is betraying me is approaching."

43 And at that very moment, while Jesus was still speaking, Judas, one of the twelve disciples, arrived. With him came a large crowd armed with swords and clubs, sent by the chief priests, the teachers of the law, and the elders.

44 The one who betrayed him had given them a signal, saying, "The man I kiss is the one; arrest him and take him away securely."

45 As soon as he arrived, he went directly to him and said, "Teacher, Teacher!" and then kissed him.

46 They placed their hands on him and seized him.

47 One of those standing nearby drew his sword and struck the servant of the high priest, cutting off his ear.

48 Jesus responded to them, saying, "Have you come out with swords and clubs to arrest me, as though I were a criminal?"

49 I was with you every day in the temple, teaching, and you did not seize me. But this has happened so that the Scriptures may be fulfilled.

50 And they all abandoned him and ran away.

51 A young man was following him, wearing nothing but a linen cloth wrapped around his body. When the men tried to seize him, he slipped out of the linen cloth and ran away naked.

52 He left the linen garment behind and fled from them, completely unclothed.

53 They took Jesus to the high priest, and all the chief priests, elders, and scribes gathered together with him.

54 Peter followed Jesus at a distance, all the way to the high priest’s courtyard. He sat down among the servants and warmed himself by the fire.

55 The high priests and the entire council sought testimony against Jesus in order to condemn him to death, but they could find none.

56 Many people came forward to give false testimony against him, but their statements did not agree with one another.

57 Then some individuals came forward and brought false accusations against him, saying,

58 We heard him say, "I will destroy this temple built by human hands, and in three days I will raise up another, not made by human hands."

59 However, even then their testimonies did not align with one another.

60 The high priest stood up among them and questioned Jesus, saying, "Do you have no response? What do you say about the testimony these witnesses are bringing against you?"

61 But he remained silent and gave no answer. Once more the high priest questioned him, saying, "Are you the Messiah, the Son of the Blessed One?"

62 And Jesus replied, "I am He. And you will see the Son of Man seated at the right hand of divine power and coming with the clouds of heaven."

63 Then the high priest tore his robes and said, "Why do we need any more witnesses?"

64 You have heard this blasphemy. What is your judgment? And they all declared him guilty, deserving death.

65 Then some began to spit on him, and they covered his face. They struck him with their fists and mocked him, saying, "Prophesy!" And the guards slapped him with their hands.

66 As Peter was downstairs in the courtyard, one of the high priest’s servant girls came over.

67 When she noticed Peter warming himself, she looked closely at him and said, "You were also with Jesus of Nazareth."

68 But he denied it, saying, "I do not know or understand what you are talking about." Then he went out to the porch, and the rooster crowed.

69 A servant girl noticed him again and started saying to those standing nearby, "This man is one of them."

70 And he denied it once more. Shortly after, those standing nearby said to Peter again, "You must be one of them, for you are a Galilean, and your way of speaking gives you away."

71 But he began to curse vehemently and to swear, saying, "I do not know this man you are talking about."

72 And the rooster crowed a second time. Then Peter remembered the words Jesus had spoken to him: "Before the rooster crows twice, you will deny me three times." As he reflected on this, he broke down and wept.
