I Chronicles Chapter 23

1 When David had grown old and lived a full life, he appointed his son Solomon as king over Israel.

2 He assembled all the leaders of Israel, along with the priests and the Levites.

3 The Levites were counted from the age of thirty years and older. Their total number, recorded individually, was thirty-eight thousand.

4 Out of these, twenty-four thousand were assigned to oversee the work of the house of the LORD, and six thousand were appointed as officers and judges.

5 Additionally, there were four thousand gatekeepers, and four thousand who praised the LORD with the musical instruments that I, David, provided for giving praise.

6 David organized them into divisions according to the descendants of Levi: Gershon, Kohath, and Merari.

7 From the Gershonites, there were Laadan and Shimei.

8 The descendants of Laadan: the foremost was Jehiel, along with Zetham and Joel—three in total.

9 The descendants of Shimei were Shelomith, Haziel, and Haran—three in total. These were the leaders of the ancestral families of Laadan.

10 The sons of Shimei were Jahath, Zina, Jeush, and Beriah. These four were Shimei’s children.

11 Jahath was the leader, and Zizah was second in rank. However, Jeush and Beriah did not have many sons, so they were counted together as one family unit, according to their father’s household.

12 The descendants of Kohath were Amram, Izhar, Hebron, and Uzziel—four in total.

13 The sons of Amram were Aaron and Moses. Aaron was set apart to consecrate the most sacred things—he and his sons forever—to burn incense before the LORD, to serve Him, and to pronounce blessings in His name for all time.

14 Now regarding Moses, the man of God, his sons were counted among the tribe of Levi.

15 The sons of Moses were Gershom and Eliezer.

16 From the descendants of Gershom, Shebuel was the leader.

17 Eliezer's sons included Rehabiah, who was the foremost among them. Eliezer had no other sons besides Rehabiah, but Rehabiah's descendants were exceedingly numerous.

18 From the descendants of Izhar, Shelomith was the leader.

19 Among the descendants of Hebron, the first was Jeriah, the second was Amariah, the third was Jahaziel, and the fourth was Jekameam.

20 From the descendants of Uzziel: Michah was the first, and Jesiah was the second.

21 The descendants of Merari were Mahli and Mushi. The sons of Mahli were Eleazar and Kish.

22 And Eleazar died without having any sons, only daughters. Their relatives, the sons of Kish, married them.

23 The descendants of Mushi were Mahli, Eder, and Jeremoth—three in total.

24 These were the descendants of Levi, listed according to their ancestral families—the heads of the households. They were registered by name, individually counted, and assigned to carry out the work of serving in the house of the LORD. This included all those twenty years of age and older.

25 David declared, "The LORD, the God of Israel, has granted peace to His people, so that they may settle in Jerusalem permanently."

26 And concerning the Levites, they will no longer need to transport the tabernacle or any of its equipment for its service.

27 The Levites were counted according to the final instructions of David, starting from those who were twenty years old and older.

28 Their duty was to assist the descendants of Aaron in the work of the house of the LORD, serving in the courtyards, the rooms, and in the purification of all sacred objects, as well as in the tasks related to the service of the house of God.

29 For the bread of the Presence, and for the fine flour used in the grain offering, and for the unleavened loaves, and for what is baked in the pan, and for what is fried, and for all measurements of quantity and size;

30 And to stand each morning to give thanks and praise to the LORD, and to do the same in the evening.

31 And to present all burnt offerings to the LORD on the Sabbaths, during the new moons, and at the appointed festivals, in the prescribed number, as commanded, regularly and continually before the LORD.

32 They were to fulfill their responsibilities for the care of the Tabernacle of Meeting, the duties of the holy place, and the obligations toward their fellow descendants of Aaron, their brothers, in the service of the house of the LORD.
