II Samuel Chapter 4

1 When Saul’s son heard that Abner had died in Hebron, he lost all strength, and all the Israelites were deeply distressed.

2 Saul’s son had two men who were leaders of raiding parties. One was named Baanah, and the other was named Rechab. They were the sons of Rimmon, a man from Beeroth, and they belonged to the tribe of Benjamin. (Beeroth was considered part of Benjamin’s territory.)

3 The people of Beeroth fled to Gittaim and have lived there as foreigners to this day.

4 Jonathan, the son of Saul, had a son who was crippled in his feet. He was five years old when the news about Saul and Jonathan came from Jezreel. His nurse picked him up and fled, but in her haste to escape, he fell and was left lame. His name was Mephibosheth.

5 Rechab and Baanah, the sons of Rimmon from Beeroth, set out and arrived at the house of Ish-bosheth during the hottest part of the day. At that time, Ish-bosheth was resting on his bed at noon.

6 They entered the house as if they were coming to get grain, but instead, they struck him in the stomach. Then Rechab and his brother Baanah fled and escaped.

7 When they entered the house, he was lying on his bed in his bedroom. They attacked him, killed him, and cut off his head. Then they took his head and fled through the plain all night.

8 They brought the head of Ish-bosheth to David at Hebron and said to the king, "Here is the head of Ish-bosheth, the son of Saul, your enemy, who sought to take your life. Today the LORD has avenged my lord the king against Saul and his descendants."

9 David replied to Rechab and Baanah, the sons of Rimmon the Beerothite, and said to them, "As surely as the LORD lives, who has delivered me from every trouble..."

10 When someone came to me and said, “Look, Saul is dead,” believing he was bringing good news, I seized him and killed him in Ziklag. He expected me to reward him for his message, but instead, I took his life.

11 How much more, when wicked men have killed a righteous person in his own home, even on his bed? Should I not now demand an accounting for his blood from your hand and remove you from the earth?

12 David gave orders to his young men, and they killed them. They cut off their hands and feet and hung their bodies by the pool in Hebron. However, they took the head of Ish-bosheth and buried it in Abner’s tomb in Hebron.
