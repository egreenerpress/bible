Mark Chapter 13

1 As Jesus was leaving the temple, one of his disciples said to him, "Teacher, look at the size of these stones and the magnificence of these buildings!"

2 Jesus responded to him, saying, "Do you see these massive structures? Not one stone will remain standing on another; every one of them will be torn down."

3 As Jesus was sitting on the Mount of Olives, facing the temple, Peter, James, John, and Andrew came to him privately and asked,

4 Tell us, when will these events take place? And what will be the sign that all these things are about to be fulfilled?

5 And Jesus, responding to them, began to say, "Be careful that no one leads you astray."

6 Many will come claiming to represent me, declaring, 'I am the Messiah,' and they will lead many astray.

7 When you hear about wars and reports of wars, do not be alarmed. These things must happen, but the end is not yet.

8 Nations will rise up against other nations, and kingdoms will war against kingdoms. There will be earthquakes in various places, and there will be famines and unrest. These events mark the beginning of the birth pains.

9 Be careful and watchful, for they will hand you over to local councils, and you will be flogged in the synagogues. You will be brought before governors and kings because of me, as a witness to testify against them.

10 The good news must first be proclaimed to all nations.

11 When they bring you before authorities and hand you over, do not worry beforehand about what you should say or plan your defense. Instead, whatever is given to you in that moment, speak it, for it will not be you speaking, but the Holy Spirit.

12 In this time, a brother will hand over his own brother to be put to death, and a father will do the same to his child. Children will turn against their parents and have them executed.

13 You will be hated by everyone because of your association with me, but whoever remains steadfast and faithful until the end will be saved.

14 But when you see the abomination that causes desolation, which was spoken of by the prophet Daniel, standing in a place where it should not be (let the reader understand), then those who are in Judea must flee to the mountains.

15 And whoever is on the rooftop must not go down into the house, nor enter it, to take anything out of their home.

16 And whoever is in the field must not turn back to retrieve their cloak.

17 But great distress will come upon those who are pregnant and those who are nursing infants in those days!

18 And pray that your escape will not have to happen during the winter.

19 For in those days there will be such intense suffering as has never occurred from the beginning of creation, when God created the world, until now, nor will there ever be anything like it again.

20 If the Lord had not reduced the length of those days, no one would have survived. But for the sake of the chosen ones, whom he has selected, he has shortened those days.

21 And if anyone says to you, "Look, here is the Christ!" or, "There he is!" do not believe it.

22 For false messiahs and false prophets will arise and perform signs and miracles to deceive, if possible, even the chosen ones.

23 But be alert and pay close attention: I have told you everything in advance.

24 But in those days, following that time of great distress, the sun will grow dark, and the moon will no longer shine its light.

25 The stars in the sky will fall, and the celestial forces will be thrown into turmoil.

26 And then they will see the Son of Man coming in the clouds with immense power and splendor.

27 Then he will send out his angels and gather his chosen ones from the four directions, from the farthest reaches of the earth to the farthest limits of the heavens.

28 Now, consider this lesson from the fig tree: When its branch becomes tender and sprouts leaves, you know that summer is near.

29 In the same way, when you see these things happening, understand that it is near, right at the door.

30 Truly I tell you, this generation will not pass away until all these things have taken place.

31 Heaven and earth will come to an end, but my words will never fail or cease to be fulfilled.

32 Concerning that day and hour, no one knows—not even the angels in heaven, nor the Son—but only the Father.

33 Be alert, stay watchful, and pray, for you do not know when the appointed time will come.

34 For the Son of Man is like a man who went on a long journey. He left his house, entrusted authority to his servants, assigned each one his specific task, and instructed the doorkeeper to stay alert.

35 Therefore, stay alert and be watchful, for you do not know when the master of the house will return—whether in the evening, at midnight, when the rooster crows, or at dawn.

36 Be careful, so that when he comes unexpectedly, he does not find you unprepared or negligent.

37 What I say to you, I say to everyone: Be alert and stay watchful.
