Acts Chapter 11

1 The apostles and fellow believers who were in Judea heard that the Gentiles had also accepted the message of God.

2 When Peter arrived in Jerusalem, those who were part of the circumcision group confronted him.

3 They were saying, "You went in to visit uncircumcised men and even ate with them."

4 But Peter explained the entire matter from the start, presenting it to them in an orderly manner, saying,

5 I was in the city of Joppa, praying, when I fell into a trance and saw a vision. A kind of container, like a large sheet, was being lowered from heaven by its four corners, and it came right down to me.

6 When I fixed my gaze upon it, I observed and saw four-legged animals of the earth, wild animals, crawling creatures, and birds of the air.

7 And I heard a voice saying to me, "Get up, Peter; kill and eat."

8 But I replied, "Certainly not, Lord! For nothing impure or unclean has ever entered my mouth."

9 But the voice spoke to me again from heaven, saying, "Do not consider unclean what God has made clean."

10 This occurred three times, and each time everything was brought back up into heaven.

11 Suddenly, three men arrived at the house where I was staying, having been sent to me from Caesarea.

12 The Spirit instructed me to go with them without hesitation. Additionally, these six brothers came with me, and we entered the man’s house.

13 He explained to us how he had seen an angel standing in his house, who said to him, "Send men to Joppa and summon Simon, who is also called Peter."

14 Who will speak to you words by which you and your entire household will be saved?

15 As I began to speak, the Holy Spirit came upon them, just as it had come upon us at the start.

16 Then I recalled the words of the Lord, how He had said, "John baptized with water, but you will be baptized with the Holy Spirit."

17 Since God has given them the same gift as he gave to us, who believed in the Lord Jesus Christ, who was I to stand in God’s way?

18 When they heard these things, they fell silent and praised God, saying, "So then, God has also granted to the Gentiles the opportunity to repent and receive life."

19 Now those who had been scattered due to the persecution that arose over Stephen traveled as far as Phoenicia, Cyprus, and Antioch, proclaiming the message of the gospel, but they spoke only to Jews.

20 Some of them were men from Cyprus and Cyrene. When they arrived in Antioch, they began speaking to the Greek-speaking people there, proclaiming the message about the Lord Jesus.

21 The power of the Lord was present among them, and a large number of people came to believe and turned their hearts to the Lord.

22 News of these events reached the church in Jerusalem, and they sent Barnabas to Antioch.

23 When he arrived and witnessed the grace of God at work, he rejoiced and encouraged them all to remain steadfastly devoted to the Lord with unwavering resolve.

24 For he was a virtuous man, filled with the Holy Spirit and strong in faith, and a great number of people were brought to the Lord.

25 Then Barnabas went to Tarsus to search for Saul.

26 When he found him, he brought him to Antioch. For an entire year, they gathered with the church and taught many people. It was in Antioch that the disciples were first called Christians.

27 During that time, prophets came from Jerusalem to Antioch.

28 Then one of them, named Agabus, stood up and, moved by the Spirit, foretold that a severe famine would spread over the entire world. This prophecy was fulfilled during the reign of Claudius Caesar.

29 Then the disciples, each according to their own means, decided to send aid to the fellow believers living in Judea.

30 They did as instructed and sent the aid to the elders through Barnabas and Saul.
