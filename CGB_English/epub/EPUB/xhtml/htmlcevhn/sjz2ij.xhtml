Genesis Chapter 20

1 Abraham traveled from there toward the southern region and settled between Kadesh and Shur. He stayed for a time in Gerar as a temporary resident.

2 Abraham said of his wife Sarah, "She is my sister." So Abimelech, the king of Gerar, sent for her and took Sarah.

3 But God appeared to Abimelech in a dream during the night and said to him, "You are as good as dead because of the woman you have taken, for she is another man's wife."

4 But Abimelech had not approached her, and he said, "Lord, will you destroy a nation that is innocent?"

5 Did he not say to me, ‘She is my sister’? And she herself also said, ‘He is my brother.’ I acted with integrity in my heart and with innocence in my hands when I did this.

6 And God spoke to him in a dream, saying, "Yes, I know that you acted with sincerity in your heart, for I also prevented you from sinning against me. That is why I did not allow you to touch her."

7 Now, therefore, return the man his wife, for he is a prophet, and he will pray for you, and you will live. But if you do not return her, know for certain that you will surely die—you and all who belong to you.

8 So Abimelech got up early in the morning and summoned all his servants. He told them everything that had happened, and the men were filled with great fear.

9 Then Abimelech summoned Abraham and said to him, "What have you done to us? How have I wronged you, that you have brought such a great sin upon me and my kingdom? You have done things to me that should never be done."

10 And Abimelech said to Abraham, "What did you see or perceive that led you to do this thing?"

11 Abraham said, "Because I thought to myself, 'Surely there is no reverence for God in this place, and they will kill me because of my wife.'"

12 And yet, she is truly my sister; she is the daughter of my father, though not the daughter of my mother, and she became my wife.

13 When God led me to leave my father’s household, I said to her, “This is the favor I ask of you: wherever we go, say of me, ‘He is my brother.’”

14 Abimelech took sheep, cattle, male servants, and female servants, and gave them to Abraham. He also returned Sarah, Abraham’s wife, to him.

15 And Abimelech said, "Look, my land is before you; settle wherever it suits you."

16 And to Sarah he said, "Listen, I have given your brother a thousand pieces of silver. This is to serve as a public vindication for you in the eyes of all who are with you and before everyone else. By this, you are fully cleared of any wrongdoing."

17 So Abraham prayed to God, and God healed Abimelech, his wife, and his female servants, enabling them to have children.

18 For the LORD had completely closed all the wombs in the household of Abimelech because of Sarah, Abraham’s wife.
