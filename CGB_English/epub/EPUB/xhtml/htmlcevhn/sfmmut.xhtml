Job Chapter 29

1 Job continued his discourse and said,

2 Oh, how I wish I could return to the days of old, to the time when God watched over me and kept me safe.

3 When his lamp shone brightly over my head, and by his guiding light I walked through the deepest darkness;

4 In the days of my youth, when Godâ€™s intimate presence rested upon my dwelling,

5 When the Almighty was still present with me, and my children surrounded me;

6 When my path was drenched with abundance, and the rock poured out streams of oil for me;

7 When I went out to the city gate, when I took my place in the public square.

8 When the young men saw me, they would hide themselves, and the elderly would rise and stand in respect.

9 The leaders ceased their speech and placed their hands over their mouths.

10 The leaders remained silent, and their tongues clung to the roofs of their mouths.

11 When people heard about me, they spoke well of me; and when they saw me, they testified to my character.

12 Because I rescued the poor who cried out for help, and the fatherless who had no one to assist them.

13 The blessing of those who were on the brink of destruction came upon me, and I brought joy to the hearts of widows, making them sing with gladness.

14 I have clothed myself with righteousness, and it covers me like a garment; my justice is like a robe and a crown.

15 I served as eyes for those who could not see, and I became feet for those who could not walk.

16 I acted as a father to the poor, and I diligently investigated cases that were unknown to me.

17 I shattered the power of the wicked and snatched the prey from their grasp.

18 Then I thought, "I will die peacefully in my own home, and my days will be as numerous as the grains of sand."

19 My roots reached out to the waters, and the dew settled upon my branches throughout the night.

20 My vigor was fresh within me, and my strength was restored like a new bow in my hand.

21 People listened to me attentively, waited in expectation, and remained silent as I offered my advice.

22 After I spoke, they did not respond further; my words settled upon them like dew.

23 They waited for me as eagerly as people wait for rain; they opened their mouths wide, like thirsty ground longing for the spring showers.

24 If I smiled at them, they could hardly believe it; and the kindness in my expression did not diminish their respect for me.

25 I selected their path and took the lead, presiding as a ruler among the troops, like a king who brings comfort to those who mourn.
