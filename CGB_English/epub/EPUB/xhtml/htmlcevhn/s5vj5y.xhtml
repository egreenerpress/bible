II Chronicles Chapter 15

1 And the Spirit of God came upon Azariah, the son of Oded.

2 He went out to meet Asa and said to him, "Listen to me, Asa, and all Judah and Benjamin: The LORD is with you as long as you remain faithful to Him. If you seek Him, He will be found by you; but if you abandon Him, He will abandon you."

3 For a long time, Israel has been without the true God, without a priest to instruct them, and without the guidance of the law.

4 However, when they, in their distress, turned to the LORD, the God of Israel, and earnestly sought Him, He allowed Himself to be found by them.

5 During that period, there was no safety for anyone, whether they were leaving or entering the land. All the people living in those regions faced constant turmoil and distress.

6 Nation rose against nation, and city against city, for God brought them great distress and turmoil.

7 Therefore, be strong and do not let your hands grow feeble, for your efforts will be rewarded.

8 When Asa heard these words and the prophecy of Oded the prophet, he was strengthened in his resolve. He removed the detestable idols from all the land of Judah and Benjamin, as well as from the cities he had captured in the hill country of Ephraim. He also restored the altar of the LORD that stood in front of the LORD's portico.

9 He assembled all the people of Judah and Benjamin, along with the foreigners who had settled among them from Ephraim, Manasseh, and Simeon. These individuals had come over to him in great numbers from Israel when they saw that the LORD his God was with him.

10 So they assembled together in Jerusalem during the third month, in the fifteenth year of King Asa’s reign.

11 At that time, they presented to the LORD a portion of the plunder they had taken: seven hundred oxen and seven thousand sheep.

12 They made a solemn agreement to wholeheartedly and sincerely seek the LORD, the God of their ancestors, with all their heart and all their soul.

13 Anyone who refuses to seek the LORD, the God of Israel, must be put to death, regardless of their status—whether young or old, male or female.

14 They made a solemn oath to the LORD with a loud voice, accompanied by shouts, the sounding of trumpets, and the blaring of horns.

15 All of Judah celebrated the oath they had taken, for they had pledged it with complete sincerity and sought God with their deepest longing. As a result, God revealed Himself to them, and the LORD granted them peace and security in every direction.

16 Additionally, concerning Maachah, the mother of King Asa, he removed her from her position as queen mother because she had made a detestable idol for Asherah. Asa cut down the idol, crushed it, and burned it in the Kidron Valley.

17 However, the high places were not removed from Israel; yet Asa's heart remained fully devoted to the Lord throughout his life.

18 He brought into the house of God the items that his father had consecrated, as well as those he himself had set apart—silver, gold, and sacred vessels.

19 And there was no more war until the thirty-fifth year of Asa's reign.
