Deuteronomy Chapter 18

1 The priests, who are Levites, and the entire tribe of Levi, will not receive a portion or inheritance along with the rest of Israel. Instead, they shall live on the offerings presented to the LORD by fire, and this will be their inheritance.

2 Therefore, they shall not receive any portion of land among their brothers; the LORD himself is their inheritance, just as he has promised them.

3 This is the portion that the priests are to receive from the people, from those who offer a sacrifice, whether it is an ox or a sheep. They must give the priest the shoulder, the two cheeks, and the stomach.

4 You must give to him the first portion of your grain, your new wine, and your olive oil, as well as the first shearing of your sheep.

5 For the LORD your God has chosen him from among all your tribes to stand and serve in the name of the LORDâ€”both he and his descendants forever.

6 If a Levite comes from any of your towns throughout all of Israel, where he has been living, and arrives with a wholehearted desire to the place that the LORD has chosen,

7 Then he shall serve in the name of the LORD his God, just as all his fellow Levites do, who stand before the LORD to carry out their duties.

8 They shall each receive equal portions of food, in addition to what comes from the sale of their inherited property.

9 When you enter the land that the LORD your God is giving you, you must not adopt the detestable practices of the nations there.

10 You must not allow anyone among you to sacrifice their son or daughter by burning them in fire, nor permit anyone to practice divination, fortune-telling, interpreting omens, sorcery, or witchcraft.

11 Or one who practices sorcery, or seeks guidance from spirits, or consults with mediums, or engages in witchcraft, or calls upon the dead.

12 All who engage in these practices are detestable to the LORD, and it is because of these detestable acts that the LORD your God is driving them out before you.

13 You must be wholehearted and blameless in your devotion to the LORD your God.

14 The nations you are about to possess have listened to those who practice divination and seek omens. But as for you, the LORD your God has not permitted you to act in such a way.

15 The LORD your God will raise up for you a prophet from among your own people, from your fellow Israelites, one like me. You must listen to him.

16 According to everything you requested of the LORD your God at Horeb on the day of the gathering, when you said, "Do not let me hear the voice of the LORD my God again, nor let me see this great fire anymore, or I will die."

17 The LORD said to me, "What they have spoken is right and good."

18 I will raise up for them a prophet from among their own people, like you, and I will place my words in his mouth. He will speak to them everything that I command him.

19 And it will happen that whoever refuses to listen to the words that he will speak in my name, I will hold them accountable.

20 However, if any prophet dares to speak a message in my name that I have not commanded them to speak, or if they speak in the name of other gods, that prophet must be put to death.

21 And if you wonder in your heart, "How can we recognize when a message has not been spoken by the LORD?"

22 When a prophet speaks in the name of the LORD, if what they predict does not happen or come to pass, it is a message the LORD has not spoken. That prophet has spoken presumptuously; you must not fear or be intimidated by them.
