Romans Chapter 3

1 What benefit, then, does the Jew have? Or what value is there in circumcision?

2 There are many reasons, but most importantly, because they were entrusted with the very words of God.

3 What if some did not believe? Does their lack of faith nullify God's faithfulness?

4 Certainly not! Let God be proven true, even if every human being is shown to be false. As it is written: "So that you may be shown to be right in what you say, and prevail when you are judged."

5 But if our wrongdoing highlights God's righteousness, what can we say? Is God unjust when he brings judgment? (I am speaking in human terms.)

6 Certainly not! For if that were the case, how could God judge the world?

7 If God's truth has been magnified and brought greater glory through my falsehood, why then am I still being condemned as a sinner?

8 Should we then say—as some slanderously claim we do and as some assert—"Let us do evil so that good may come"? Such people deserve the condemnation they receive.

9 What then? Are we superior to them? Not at all. For we have already demonstrated that both Jews and Gentiles alike are all under the power of sin.

10 As it is written: "There is no one who is righteous, not even one."

11 No one truly comprehends; no one genuinely seeks after God.

12 All have turned aside; they have collectively become corrupt; there is no one who does good, not even a single one.

13 Their words are like an open grave, full of death and decay; they speak with deceitful tongues, and their lips conceal the venom of deadly snakes.

14 Their speech is filled with curses and bitterness.

15 Their feet are quick to spill blood.

16 Ruin and suffering mark their paths.

17 They have not understood the path of peace.

18 They do not have any reverence or respect for God in their hearts or minds.

19 Now we understand that whatever the law declares, it speaks to those who are subject to the law. This is so that every mouth may be silenced, and the whole world may be held accountable before God.

20 Therefore, no one will be declared righteous in God’s sight by relying on the works of the law, because it is through the law that we become aware of sin.

21 But now, the righteousness of God has been revealed apart from the law, though it is attested by the law and the prophets.

22 The righteousness of God, which comes through faith in Jesus Christ, is given to all and rests upon all who believe. For there is no distinction.

23 All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God.

24 We are declared righteous as a gift, by His grace, through the deliverance and restoration found in Christ Jesus.

25 God has presented him as a means of atonement, effective through faith in his sacrificial death. This was to demonstrate his righteousness, because in his divine patience, God had overlooked previous sins. Through this act, God now declares his righteousness, providing forgiveness for sins committed in the past.

26 To make known, I declare, at this present time his righteousness: so that he may be both just and the one who justifies those who place their faith in Jesus.

27 Where, then, is boasting? It is completely excluded. By what principle? By the principle of works? No, but by the principle of faith.

28 Therefore, we determine that a person is made right with God through faith, apart from works required by the law.

29 Is God the God of the Jews alone? Is He not also the God of the Gentiles? Indeed, He is the God of the Gentiles as well.

30 Since there is one God, who will declare the circumcised righteous by faith and the uncircumcised righteous through faith.

31 Do we, then, nullify the law by means of faith? Certainly not! On the contrary, we uphold the law.
