Nehemiah Chapter 7

1 After the wall had been completed, and I had installed the gates, and the gatekeepers, the singers, and the Levites had been assigned their duties,

2 I appointed my brother Hanani, along with Hananiah the overseer of the palace, to take responsibility for Jerusalem, because he was a trustworthy man who revered God more than most.

3 I instructed them, "Do not open the gates of Jerusalem until the sun is fully up. While the guards are still on duty, have them shut the doors and secure them with bars. Assign guards from the inhabitants of Jerusalem, each to their post, and each stationed near their own house."

4 The city was vast and impressive, but its population was sparse, and the houses had not yet been constructed.

5 And my God placed it in my heart to assemble the nobles, the leaders, and the people so that they could be recorded by their family lineage. I discovered a register of the genealogy of those who had returned at the beginning, and I found written in it the following:

6 These are the descendants of the province who returned from exile—those who had been taken captive by Nebuchadnezzar, the king of Babylon. They came back to Jerusalem and Judah, each one returning to their own city.

7 Who accompanied Zerubbabel, Jeshua, Nehemiah, Azariah, Raamiah, Nahamani, Mordecai, Bilshan, Mispereth, Bigvai, Nehum, and Baanah? The count of the men belonging to the people of Israel was as follows:

8 The descendants of Parosh numbered two thousand one hundred and seventy-two.

9 The descendants of Shephatiah numbered three hundred and seventy-two.

10 The descendants of Arah numbered six hundred and fifty-two.

11 The descendants of Pahath-moab, specifically those from the family lines of Jeshua and Joab, numbered two thousand eight hundred and eighteen.

12 The descendants of Elam numbered one thousand two hundred and fifty-four.

13 The descendants of Zattu numbered eight hundred forty-five.

14 The descendants of Zaccai numbered seven hundred and sixty.

15 The descendants of Binnui numbered six hundred and forty-eight.

16 The descendants of Bebai numbered six hundred and twenty-eight.

17 The descendants of Azgad numbered two thousand three hundred and twenty-two.

18 The descendants of Adonikam numbered six hundred sixty-seven.

19 The descendants of Bigvai numbered two thousand and sixty-seven.

20 The descendants of Adin numbered six hundred and fifty-five.

21 The descendants of Ater, through Hezekiah, numbered ninety-eight.

22 The descendants of Hashum numbered three hundred and twenty-eight.

23 The descendants of Bezai numbered three hundred and twenty-four.

24 The descendants of Hariph: one hundred and twelve.

25 The people from Gibeon numbered ninety-five.

26 The men of Bethlehem and Netophah numbered one hundred and eighty-eight.

27 The men of Anathoth numbered one hundred and twenty-eight.

28 The men from Beth-azmaveth: forty-two.

29 The men of Kiriath-jearim, Chephirah, and Beeroth numbered seven hundred and forty-three.

30 The men from Ramah and Geba numbered six hundred and twenty-one.

31 The men of Michmash: one hundred and twenty-two.

32 The men of Bethel and Ai numbered one hundred and twenty-three.

33 The men from the other Nebo: fifty-two.

34 The descendants of the other Elam numbered one thousand two hundred and fifty-four.

35 The descendants of Harim: three hundred and twenty.

36 The descendants of Jericho numbered three hundred and forty-five.

37 The descendants of Lod, Hadid, and Ono numbered seven hundred and twenty-one.

38 The descendants of Senaah numbered three thousand nine hundred and thirty.

39 The priests, specifically the descendants of Jedaiah from the family of Jeshua, numbered nine hundred and seventy-three.

40 The descendants of Immer: one thousand and fifty-two.

41 The descendants of Pashur numbered one thousand two hundred and forty-seven.

42 The descendants of Harim numbered one thousand and seventeen.

43 The Levites: the descendants of Jeshua, Kadmiel, and the family of Hodevah, totaling seventy-four.

44 The singers, who were descendants of Asaph, numbered one hundred and forty-eight.

45 The gatekeepers included the descendants of Shallum, Ater, Talmon, Akkub, Hatita, and Shobai, totaling one hundred and thirty-eight.

46 The temple servants: the descendants of Ziha, the descendants of Hashupha, the descendants of Tabbaoth.

47 The descendants of Keros, the descendants of Sia, the descendants of Padon.

48 The descendants of Lebana, the descendants of Hagaba, and the descendants of Shalmai.

49 The descendants of Hanan, the descendants of Giddel, the descendants of Gahar.

50 The descendants of Reaiah, the descendants of Rezin, and the descendants of Nekoda.

51 The descendants of Gazzam, the descendants of Uzza, and the descendants of Phaseah.

52 The descendants of Besai, the descendants of Meunim, and the descendants of Nephishesim.

53 The descendants of Bakbuk, the descendants of Hakupha, and the descendants of Harhur.

54 The descendants of Bazlith, the descendants of Mehida, the descendants of Harsha.

55 The descendants of Barkos, the descendants of Sisera, and the descendants of Tamah.

56 The descendants of Neziah, the descendants of Hatipha.

57 The descendants of Solomon's servants: the descendants of Sotai, the descendants of Sophereth, and the descendants of Perida.

58 The descendants of Jaala, the descendants of Darkon, and the descendants of Giddel.

59 The descendants of Shephatiah, the descendants of Hattil, the descendants of Pochereth of Zebaim, and the descendants of Amon.

60 All the temple servants and the descendants of Solomon's servants numbered three hundred and ninety-two.

61 These were the individuals who also came up from Tel-melah, Tel-haresha, Cherub, Addon, and Immer. However, they were unable to provide evidence of their family lineage or their descendants, so it could not be confirmed whether they were truly of Israelite descent.

62 The descendants of Delaiah, the descendants of Tobiah, and the descendants of Nekoda—six hundred and forty-two in total.

63 Among the priests were the descendants of Habaiah, the descendants of Koz, and the descendants of Barzillai. This Barzillai had married one of the daughters of Barzillai the Gileadite and had taken on their family name.

64 These individuals sought to have their names included in the genealogical records of those who were officially recognized as priests, but their names could not be found. As a result, they were considered unclean and excluded from serving in the priesthood.

65 The governor instructed them that they were not to partake of the most sacred offerings until a priest arose who was equipped with the Urim and Thummim.

66 The entire assembly together numbered forty-two thousand three hundred and sixty.

67 In addition to their male and female servants, who numbered seven thousand three hundred and thirty-seven, they also had two hundred and forty-five male and female singers.

68 Their horses numbered 736, and their mules numbered 245.

69 Their camels numbered four hundred and thirty-five, and their donkeys numbered six thousand seven hundred and twenty.

70 And some of the leading family heads contributed to the work. The governor donated to the treasury a thousand gold coins, fifty bowls, and five hundred and thirty garments for the priests.

71 Some of the leading family heads contributed to the treasury for the work: twenty thousand darics of gold and two thousand two hundred minas of silver.

72 The contributions from the rest of the people amounted to twenty thousand darics of gold, two thousand minas of silver, and sixty-seven garments for the priests.

73 So the priests, the Levites, the gatekeepers, the singers, some of the common people, the temple servants, and all the rest of Israel settled in their respective towns. When the seventh month arrived, the people of Israel were living in their towns.
