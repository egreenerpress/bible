Isaiah Chapter 50

1 This is what the LORD says: Where is the certificate of divorce by which I sent your mother away? Or to which of my creditors did I sell you? Look, it was because of your sins that you were sold, and because of your rebellions that your mother was sent away.

2 Why, when I arrived, was there no one present? When I called out, was there no one to respond? Is my power so limited that I cannot save? Or do I lack the strength to rescue? Look, at my command I dry up the sea; I turn rivers into barren wastelands. The fish rot for lack of water and die of thirst.

3 I cover the heavens with darkness, and I make sackcloth their covering.

4 The Lord GOD has granted me the ability to speak with wisdom, so that I may know how to offer timely encouragement to those who are weary. Each morning, He awakens me; He opens my ear to listen and learn, like one who is taught.

5 The Sovereign LORD has opened my understanding, and I did not resist or turn away in defiance.

6 I offered my back to those who struck me, and my cheeks to those who pulled out my beard. I did not hide my face from humiliation and spitting.

7 For the Sovereign LORD will assist me; therefore, I will not be disgraced. For this reason, I have resolved to stand firm, unwavering like flint, and I am confident that I will not be put to shame.

8 The one who declares me righteous is near. Who will dare to oppose me? Let us confront each other. Who is my accuser? Let him approach me.

9 Look, the Lord GOD will come to my aid; who then can condemn me? Indeed, all of them will wear out like a garment; moths will devour them.

10 Who among you fears the LORD and obeys the voice of his servant, yet walks in darkness with no light to guide you? Let such a person trust in the name of the LORD and rely firmly on their God.

11 Listen, all of you who ignite your own fires and surround yourselves with sparks: go ahead, walk by the light of your own flames and the sparks you have lit. But this is what you will receive from my hand: you will lie down in anguish.
