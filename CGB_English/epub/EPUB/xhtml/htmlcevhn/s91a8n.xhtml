Isaiah Chapter 8

1 Furthermore, the LORD said to me, "Take a large scroll and write on it in clear, legible writing concerning Maher-shalal-hash-baz."

2 I appointed trustworthy witnesses to document the matter: Uriah the priest and Zechariah, the son of Jeberechiah.

3 So I went to the prophetess, and she became pregnant and gave birth to a son. Then the LORD said to me, "Name him Maher-shalal-hash-baz."

4 Before the child is old enough to call out "my father" or "my mother," the wealth of Damascus and the plunder of Samaria will be carried off by the king of Assyria.

5 The LORD also spoke to me again, saying,

6 Because this people rejects the gently flowing waters of Shiloah and instead takes delight in Rezin and the son of Remaliah,

7 Now, take notice: the Lord is bringing against them the mighty and abundant waters of the riverâ€”that is, the king of Assyria and all his splendor. He will rise up and overflow all his channels, surging over every boundary.

8 He will sweep through Judah like a flood, rising and overflowing until it reaches up to the neck. The outspread wings of his forces will cover the full breadth of your land, O Immanuel.

9 Come together, all you nations, and you will be shattered; listen closely, all you distant lands. Prepare yourselves for battle, and you will be crushed; arm yourselves, and you will be utterly defeated.

10 Come together and devise your plans, but they will come to nothing; declare your intentions, but they will not succeed, for God is with us.

11 The LORD spoke to me with a powerful and compelling force, instructing me not to follow the ways of this people, saying,

12 Do not join in saying, "Let us form an alliance," with everyone this people calls for an alliance. Do not fear what they fear, nor be filled with dread as they are.

13 Set apart the Lord of hosts as holy; let Him be the one you revere and hold in awe. Let Him be the source of your fear and the object of your deepest respect.

14 He will be a place of refuge and safety, but also a stone that causes people to stumble and a rock that makes them fall. For both the houses of Israel, he will be a trap and a snare to the people of Jerusalem.

15 Many among them will stumble, fall, and be shattered; they will be ensnared and captured.

16 Secure the testimony and safeguard the instruction among my followers.

17 I will wait for the LORD, who has hidden his presence from the house of Jacob, and I will earnestly seek him.

18 Look, I and the children the LORD has given me are meant to be signs and wonders in Israel, appointed by the LORD of hosts, who dwells on Mount Zion.

19 When people say to you, "Consult those who communicate with spirits and seek guidance from sorcerers who whisper and mutter," should not a people inquire of their God? Why turn to the dead on behalf of the living?

20 Turn to the law and the testimony; if they do not speak in accordance with this word, it is because they have no true understanding or enlightenment within them.

21 They will pass through the land, distressed and famished. When they are hungry, they will become enraged and curse their king and their God, turning their gaze upward.

22 They will gaze toward the earth, only to see distress and gloom, a shadow of despair. They will be thrust into deep darkness.
