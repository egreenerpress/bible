II Samuel Chapter 2

1 After these events, David sought guidance from the LORD, asking, "Should I go up to one of the cities of Judah?" The LORD replied to him, "Go up." David then asked, "Where should I go?" And the LORD answered, "To Hebron."

2 So David went up to that place, accompanied by his two wives: Ahinoam from Jezreel and Abigail, who had been the wife of Nabal from Carmel.

3 David brought up all the men who were with him, each one along with his family, and they settled in the cities of Hebron.

4 The men of Judah gathered and anointed David as king over the house of Judah. They also informed David, saying, "It was the men of Jabesh-gilead who buried Saul."

5 David sent messengers to the people of Jabesh-gilead and said to them, "May you be blessed by the LORD, for you have shown such loyalty and kindness to your lord, Saul, by giving him a proper burial."

6 And now, may the LORD show you steadfast love and faithfulness. I, too, will repay you with kindness, because you have done this good deed.

7 Therefore, take courage now and be strong, for your master Saul is dead, and the people of Judah have anointed me as their king.

8 However, Abner, the son of Ner, who was the commander of Saul’s army, took Ish-bosheth, Saul’s son, and brought him to Mahanaim.

9 They appointed him as king over Gilead, the Ashurites, Jezreel, Ephraim, Benjamin, and all of Israel.

10 Ish-bosheth, the son of Saul, was forty years old when he became king over Israel, and he ruled for two years. However, the house of Judah remained loyal to David.

11 The period during which David reigned as king in Hebron over the tribe of Judah lasted seven years and six months.

12 Abner, the son of Ner, along with the servants of Ish-bosheth, the son of Saul, set out from Mahanaim and traveled to Gibeon.

13 Joab, the son of Zeruiah, along with David’s servants, went out and gathered by the pool of Gibeon. They positioned themselves on opposite sides of the pool, sitting down facing one another.

14 Abner said to Joab, "Let the young men come forward and compete before us." Joab replied, "Let them come forward."

15 Then twelve men from Benjamin, who were loyal to Ish-bosheth, the son of Saul, came forward and crossed over, along with twelve of David’s servants.

16 Each man seized his opponent by the head and thrust his sword into his opponent’s side, so they all fell together. Therefore, that place was named Helkath-hazzurim, which means "Field of Swords," and it is located in Gibeon.

17 On that day, a fierce battle took place, and Abner, along with the men of Israel, was defeated by David's forces.

18 There were three sons of Zeruiah present: Joab, Abishai, and Asahel. Asahel was as swift on his feet as a wild gazelle.

19 Asahel pursued Abner relentlessly, not veering to the right or to the left as he followed him.

20 Then Abner turned around and asked, "Is that you, Asahel?" And he replied, "It is I."

21 Abner said to him, "Turn aside to your right or to your left, seize one of the young men, and take his armor for yourself." But Asahel refused to turn aside and continued to pursue him.

22 Abner spoke to Asahel again, saying, "Stop pursuing me! Why should I strike you down and leave you dead? How could I ever face your brother Joab after doing such a thing?"

23 However, Asahel refused to turn away from pursuing Abner. So Abner struck him with the blunt end of his spear, hitting him in the abdomen, and the spear went through him, coming out his back. Asahel collapsed there and died on the spot. And it happened that everyone who came to the place where Asahel had fallen and died stopped and stood still.

24 Joab and Abishai pursued Abner, and as the sun was setting, they reached the hill of Ammah, which lies near Giah along the road to the wilderness of Gibeon.

25 The descendants of Benjamin rallied behind Abner, forming a single united force, and took their position on the summit of a hill.

26 Then Abner called out to Joab and said, "Must the sword continue to destroy forever? Don’t you realize that this will only lead to greater suffering in the end? How long will it be before you command your troops to stop pursuing their own brothers?"

27 Joab declared, "As surely as God lives, if you had not spoken, the people would have continued pursuing their own brothers until morning."

28 So Joab sounded the trumpet, and all the troops halted. They stopped pursuing Israel and ceased fighting altogether.

29 Abner and his men traveled all that night across the plain, crossed the Jordan River, and journeyed through the entire region of Bithron until they arrived at Mahanaim.

30 Joab returned from pursuing Abner, and after he had gathered all the troops together, it was discovered that nineteen of David’s men were missing, along with Asahel.

31 However, David's forces struck down some of Benjamin's men and Abner's troops, resulting in the death of three hundred and sixty men.

32 They lifted up Asahel and buried him in the tomb of his father, which was in Bethlehem. Then Joab and his men traveled through the night and arrived in Hebron at dawn.
