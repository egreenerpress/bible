Psalms Chapter 39

1 (For the music director, according to the style of Jeduthun. A psalm of David.) I resolved, "I will guard my ways so that I do not sin with my words. I will keep a tight rein on my speech, especially when the wicked are near."

2 I remained silent, keeping my mouth shut, even refraining from speaking good words, and my distress only grew more intense.

3 My heart grew fervent within me; as I pondered, a fire kindled within me. Then I spoke out with my words.

4 LORD, help me understand my end and the length of my days, so that I may grasp how fleeting and fragile my life truly is.

5 Look, you have made my days as short as the width of a hand; my lifetime is as nothing in your sight. Truly, even at their best, all people are but fleeting and insignificant. Selah.

6 Certainly, every person goes about their life as if in a fleeting shadow; indeed, they are troubled and restless for no lasting purpose. They accumulate wealth, yet they have no idea who will eventually take possession of it.

7 And now, Lord, what am I waiting for? My hope is entirely in you.

8 Rescue me from all my sins; do not let me become an object of scorn for the foolish.

9 I remained silent; I did not open my mouth, for it was you who brought this about.

10 Stop your punishment from falling upon me; I am overwhelmed by the force of your discipline.

11 When you discipline people for their wrongdoing through correction, you cause their splendor to waste away like a moth consumes fabric. Truly, every person is fleeting and insignificant. Selah.

12 Listen to my prayer, O LORD, and pay attention to my plea; do not remain silent in response to my tears. For I am a foreigner living with you, a temporary resident, just as all my ancestors were.

13 Please spare me, so that I may regain my strength before I depart from this life and cease to exist.
