Jeremiah Chapter 48

1 This is what the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel, says concerning Moab: Woe to Nebo, for it is destroyed! Kiriathaim is disgraced and captured; Misgab is shamed and filled with terror.

2 Moab will no longer be praised. In Heshbon, they have plotted evil against it, saying, "Come, let us destroy it as a nation." You too, O Madmen, will be silenced; the sword will relentlessly pursue you.

3 A cry of anguish will rise from Horonaim, signaling devastation and overwhelming ruin.

4 Moab is utterly devastated; the cries of its children echo far and wide.

5 For on the ascent of Luhith, there will be continuous weeping; and on the descent of Horonaim, the enemies have heard a cry of devastation.

6 Run for your lives, escape and survive, and become like a barren shrub in the desert.

7 Because you have placed your confidence in your own achievements and your wealth, you will indeed be captured. Chemosh, along with his priests and leaders, will be taken into exile together.

8 The destroyer will come against every city, and no city will escape; the valley will be laid waste, and the plain will be ruined, just as the LORD has declared.

9 Grant Moab the means to escape, that it may flee and depart, for its cities will become desolate, with no one left to inhabit them.

10 Cursed is anyone who carries out the Lord’s work with deceit, and cursed is anyone who holds back their sword from shedding blood when it is required.

11 Moab has lived in comfort since its early days, undisturbed and settled like wine left on its sediment. It has not been poured from one container to another, nor has it gone into exile. As a result, its flavor has remained unchanged, and its aroma has not faded.

12 Therefore, listen carefully, the time is coming, declares the LORD, when I will send against him those who will make him wander aimlessly. They will strip him of his possessions, emptying his vessels and shattering his jars.

13 Moab will be disgraced because of Chemosh, just as the people of Israel were disgraced because of Bethel, the object of their misplaced trust.

14 How can you claim, "We are powerful and strong warriors for battle"?

15 Moab has been devastated and has fled from its cities. Its finest young men have been slaughtered, declares the King, whose name is the LORD of Armies.

16 The disaster destined for Moab is close at hand, and its suffering is swiftly approaching.

17 All of you who are close to him, mourn for him; and all of you who know his name, cry out, "How the mighty scepter has been shattered, and the glorious rod destroyed!"

18 You, daughter who dwell in Dibon, descend from your splendor and sit in thirst, for the destroyer of Moab will come against you, and he will demolish your fortresses.

19 You who live in Aroer, stand by the roadside and watch; ask those who are fleeing and those who have escaped, and say, "What has happened?"

20 Moab is utterly dismayed, for it has been devastated. Wail and cry out! Proclaim it along the Arnon River that Moab has been plundered and laid waste.

21 And judgment has come upon the open plains—upon Holon, Jahazah, and Mephaath.

22 And concerning Dibon, and concerning Nebo, and concerning Beth-diblathaim...

23 And concerning Kiriathaim, and concerning Beth-gamul, and concerning Beth-meon,

24 And against Kerioth, and against Bozrah, and against all the cities of the land of Moab, whether distant or nearby.

25 The strength of Moab has been destroyed, and its power has been shattered, declares the LORD.

26 Make him drunk, for he has exalted himself against the LORD. Moab will wallow in his own vomit, and he will become an object of ridicule.

27 Was Israel not an object of mockery to you? Was he treated like a thief? Yet whenever you spoke of him, you leaped with joy.

28 You who live in Moab, abandon your cities and take refuge in the rocky cliffs. Be like the dove that builds its nest in the crevices of the rock, finding safety and shelter there.

29 We have heard of Moab's arrogance—indeed, he is exceedingly proud—his loftiness, his conceit, his overbearing pride, and the haughtiness of his heart.

30 I am fully aware of his anger, declares the LORD, but it will not prevail; his deceitful schemes will not accomplish what he intends.

31 Therefore, I will wail for Moab, and I will cry out in anguish for all of Moab; my heart will grieve deeply for the people of Kir-heres.

32 O vine of Sibmah, I will mourn for you with the same deep sorrow as for Jazer. Your branches have spread across the sea, reaching even to the waters of Jazer. The destroyer has fallen upon your summer fruits and your harvest, leaving them ruined.

33 Joy and gladness have been stripped away from the fertile fields and the land of Moab. I have stopped the flow of wine from the winepresses; no one will tread the grapes with shouts of celebration. Their shouts will no longer be shouts of joy.

34 From Heshbon to Elealeh, and as far as Jahaz, their cries have been heard; from Zoar to Horonaim, they have raised their voices like a young heifer. Even the waters of Nimrim will become a desolate wasteland.

35 Furthermore, declares the LORD, I will put an end in Moab to those who offer sacrifices on the high places and those who burn incense to their gods.

36 Therefore, my heart will mourn for Moab like the sound of pipes, and my heart will lament like pipes for the people of Kir-heres, because the wealth they have acquired has been destroyed.

37 Every head will be shaved, and every beard will be cut short. There will be marks of mourning on all hands, and everyone will wear sackcloth around their waist.

38 There will be widespread mourning on all the rooftops of Moab and in its streets, for I have shattered Moab like a useless vessel, declares the LORD.

39 They will cry out in anguish, saying, "How utterly it has been destroyed! How has Moab turned away in disgrace!" Thus, Moab will become an object of mockery and terror to all those around them.

40 For this is what the LORD says: Look, he will soar like an eagle and stretch out his wings over Moab.

41 Kerioth has been captured, and the fortresses have been seized. On that day, the hearts of Moab’s mighty warriors will tremble like the heart of a woman in the throes of childbirth.

42 Moab will cease to exist as a nation because it has arrogantly exalted itself against the LORD.

43 Terror, the pit, and the trap will come upon you, O inhabitant of Moab, declares the LORD.

44 Whoever tries to escape from terror will fall into a pit, and whoever climbs out of the pit will be caught in a trap. For I will bring upon Moab the time of their judgment, declares the LORD.

45 Those who fled took refuge in the shadow of Heshbon, seeking safety from the advancing force. But a fire will blaze out from Heshbon, and flames will erupt from the midst of Sihon, consuming the outskirts of Moab and the proud leaders of the rebellious ones.

46 How terrible it will be for you, O Moab! The people who worship Chemosh are doomed, for your sons have been taken as captives, and your daughters as well.

47 Nevertheless, I will restore the fortunes of Moab in the days to come, declares the LORD. This concludes the judgment concerning Moab.
