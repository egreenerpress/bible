Isaiah Chapter 64

1 Oh, if only you would tear open the heavens and come down, so that the mountains would melt and flow away at your presence!

2 As when a blazing fire burns, causing the waters to boil, so let your name be made known to your enemies, that the nations might tremble before your presence!

3 When you performed awe-inspiring deeds that we did not expect, you came down, and the mountains melted away at your presence.

4 From the very beginning of time, no one has ever heard, nor has any ear perceived, nor has any eye seen, O God, apart from you, what you have prepared for those who wait for you.

5 You welcome those who delight in doing what is right and who follow your ways. Yet, you are angry, for we have sinned. In your ways, there is lasting hope, and through them, we will find salvation.

6 Yet all of us are like something unclean, and even our best deeds are like soiled garments. We all wither like a leaf, and our sins, like the wind, sweep us away.

7 No one calls on your name or rouses themselves to seek you, for you have hidden your face from us and have allowed us to be consumed because of our sins.

8 But now, O LORD, you are our Father; we are the clay, and you are the potter; we are all the work of your hands.

10 Your sacred cities have become a wasteland; Zion lies in ruins, and Jerusalem is a desolate place.

11 Our sacred and glorious temple, where our ancestors worshiped you, has been consumed by fire; all that we cherished has been reduced to ruins.

12 Will you remain silent in the face of these things, O LORD? Will you restrain yourself and not speak, even as you bring such severe suffering upon us?
