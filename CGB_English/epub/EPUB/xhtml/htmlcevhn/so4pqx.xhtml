Exodus Chapter 20

1 And God spoke all these words, saying,

2 I am the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, out of the place of slavery.

3 You must not worship or place any other gods above me.

4 You must not create for yourself any carved image or any likeness of anything that is in the heavens above, or on the earth below, or in the waters under the earth.

5 You must not bow down to them or worship them, for I, the LORD your God, am a God who demands exclusive devotion. I hold accountable the wrongdoing of the parents, bringing consequences upon their children, even to the third and fourth generation of those who reject me.

6 And I will show steadfast love to thousands of those who love me and faithfully keep my commandments.

7 You must not misuse the name of the LORD your God, for the LORD will not consider anyone guiltless who misuses His name.

8 Set apart the Sabbath day and keep it sacred.

9 You shall work for six days and complete all your tasks.

10 However, the seventh day is a Sabbath dedicated to the LORD your God. On this day, you must not engage in any work—neither you, nor your son or daughter, nor your male or female servant, nor your livestock, nor any foreigner residing within your gates.

11 For in six days the LORD created the heavens, the earth, the sea, and everything in them, and on the seventh day He rested. Therefore, the LORD blessed the Sabbath day and set it apart as holy.

12 Respect and honor your father and your mother, so that you may live a long life in the land that the LORD your God is giving to you.

13 You must not commit murder.

14 You must not engage in adultery.

15 You must not steal.

16 You must not give false testimony against your neighbor.

17 You must not desire your neighbor’s house. You must not desire your neighbor’s wife, his male servant, his female servant, his ox, his donkey, or anything that belongs to your neighbor.

18 All the people witnessed the thunder, the flashes of lightning, the sound of the trumpet, and the mountain enveloped in smoke. When they saw these things, they trembled and moved back, standing at a distance.

19 The people said to Moses, "You speak to us, and we will listen. But do not let God speak directly to us, or we will die."

20 Moses said to the people, "Do not be afraid, for God has come to test you, so that the fear of Him may remain before you, and you may not sin."

21 The people remained at a distance, while Moses approached the dense darkness where God was present.

22 The LORD said to Moses, "This is what you are to say to the Israelites: You have witnessed that I have spoken to you directly from heaven."

23 You must not make any idols of silver to worship alongside me, nor shall you create any idols of gold for yourselves.

24 You shall build an altar of earth for me, and on it you shall offer your burnt offerings and your peace offerings—your sheep and your cattle. In every place where I cause my name to be remembered, I will come to you and bless you.

25 If you choose to construct an altar of stone for me, you must not build it with cut stones. For if you use a tool on it, you will defile it.

26 You must not ascend to my altar by steps, so that your nakedness is not exposed upon it.
