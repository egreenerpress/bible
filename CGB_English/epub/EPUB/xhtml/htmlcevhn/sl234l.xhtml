I Kings Chapter 19

1 Ahab reported to Jezebel everything that Elijah had done, including how he had killed all the prophets with the sword.

2 Then Jezebel sent a messenger to Elijah with this message: "May the gods strike me dead and do even worse if I do not make your life like the life of one of those prophets by this time tomorrow."

3 When he saw this, he got up and fled for his life, traveling to Beersheba, which is in the territory of Judah. There, he left his servant behind.

4 But he himself journeyed a day’s distance into the wilderness, came to a broom tree, and sat down under it. There he prayed for his own death, saying, “It is enough. Now, LORD, take my life, for I am no better than my ancestors.”

5 As he lay sleeping beneath a juniper tree, suddenly an angel came and touched him, saying, "Get up and eat."

6 He looked, and there before him was a cake baked over hot coals, and a jar of water near his head. He ate and drank, then lay down again.

7 Then the angel of the LORD returned a second time, touched him, and said, "Get up and eat, for the journey ahead is too demanding for you."

8 He got up, ate and drank, and then, strengthened by that food, he traveled for forty days and forty nights until he reached Horeb, the mountain of God.

9 He arrived at a cave and stayed there overnight. Then, the word of the LORD came to him, saying, "What are you doing here, Elijah?"

10 And he said, "I have been deeply zealous for the LORD, the God of armies, because the people of Israel have abandoned your covenant, torn down your altars, and killed your prophets with the sword. I alone am left, and now they are seeking to take my life as well."

11 Then he said, "Go out and stand on the mountain in the presence of the LORD." And behold, the LORD passed by, and a mighty and powerful wind tore through the mountains and shattered the rocks before the LORD, but the LORD was not in the wind. After the wind came an earthquake, but the LORD was not in the earthquake.

12 After the earthquake came a fire, but the LORD was not in the fire. And after the fire, there was a gentle, quiet whisper.

13 When Elijah heard this, he covered his face with his cloak, went out, and stood at the entrance of the cave. Suddenly, a voice came to him and said, “What are you doing here, Elijah?”

14 He said, "I have been deeply zealous for the LORD, the God of hosts, because the people of Israel have abandoned your covenant, torn down your altars, and killed your prophets with the sword. I alone am left, and now they are seeking to take my life as well."

15 The LORD said to him, "Go, return on your way to the wilderness of Damascus. When you arrive, anoint Hazael as king over Syria."

16 You are to anoint Jehu, the son of Nimshi, as king over Israel, and Elisha, the son of Shaphat from Abel-meholah, as prophet to succeed you.

17 This is what will happen: those who escape the sword of Hazael will be killed by Jehu, and those who escape the sword of Jehu will be killed by Elisha.

18 Yet I have preserved for myself seven thousand in Israel—all those whose knees have not bowed to Baal, and every mouth that has not kissed him in worship.

19 So Elijah left that place and found Elisha, the son of Shaphat, who was plowing a field with twelve pairs of oxen. Elisha himself was working with the twelfth pair. As Elijah walked by him, he threw his cloak over Elisha.

20 So he left the oxen and ran after Elijah, saying, “Please, let me kiss my father and mother goodbye, and then I will follow you.” But Elijah replied, “Go back, for what have I done to hold you back?”

21 He turned away from him, took a pair of oxen, slaughtered them, and cooked their meat using the oxen's equipment. Then he gave the food to the people, and they ate. Afterward, he got up, followed Elijah, and became his servant.
