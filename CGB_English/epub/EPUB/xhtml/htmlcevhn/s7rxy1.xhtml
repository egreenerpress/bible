Colossians Chapter 3

1 If you have been raised to new life with Christ, set your hearts on the things that are above, where Christ is seated at the right hand of God.

2 Focus your heart and mind on the things of heaven, not on the things of this earthly life.

3 For you have died to your old self, and your true life is now securely united with Christ in God.

4 When Christ, who is the source of our life, is revealed, then you also will be revealed with him in glorious splendor.

5 Therefore, put to death the sinful, earthly desires within you: sexual immorality, impurity, lust, evil desires, and greed, which is a form of idolatry.

6 Because of these things, the wrath of God is coming upon those who are disobedient.

7 At one time, you also lived according to these ways, when you were still immersed in them.

8 Now, you must also rid yourselves of all these things: anger, rage, malice, slander, and obscene speech from your mouth.

9 Do not lie to one another, since you have discarded your former self with its sinful ways.

10 And you have clothed yourselves with the new self, which is being continually renewed in understanding to reflect the likeness of the One who created it.

11 In this context, there is no distinction between Greek or Jew, circumcised or uncircumcised, foreigner or Scythian, slave or free. Instead, Christ is everything and is present in everyone.

12 Therefore, as Godâ€™s chosen people, holy and dearly loved, clothe yourselves with heartfelt compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness, and patience.

13 Be patient with one another, and forgive each other if anyone has a complaint against another. Just as Christ forgave you, so you also must forgive.

14 Above all else, clothe yourselves with love, which binds everything together in perfect unity.

15 Let the peace of God govern your hearts, for you were called to this peace as members of one united body. And always be thankful.

16 Allow the message of Christ to reside deeply within you, abundantly enriching your understanding and wisdom. Instruct and encourage one another through psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs, singing with gratitude in your hearts to the Lord.

17 Whatever you do, whether in speech or action, do it all in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through him.

18 Wives, willingly yield to and respect your own husbands, as is appropriate and fitting in your relationship with the Lord.

19 Husbands, love your wives deeply and do not harbor resentment or act harshly toward them.

20 Children, obey your parents in everything, for this is pleasing to the Lord.

21 Fathers, do not provoke your children to anger, so that they do not become disheartened.

22 Servants, obey your earthly masters in all things, not just when they are watching, as if you were merely trying to please people. Instead, serve with sincerity of heart, out of reverence for God.

23 Whatever you do, do it wholeheartedly, as if you are doing it for the Lord and not merely for people.

24 Be assured that you will receive from the Lord the reward of your inheritance, for you are serving the Lord Christ.

25 However, whoever commits wrongdoing will be repaid for the wrong they have done, and there is no partiality or favoritism.
