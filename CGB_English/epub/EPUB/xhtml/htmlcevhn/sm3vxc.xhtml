Psalms Chapter 84

1 (To the director of music, to be played on the Gittith. A psalm of the sons of Korah.) How lovely are Your dwelling places, O LORD of armies!

2 My soul deeply yearns, even to the point of exhaustion, for the presence of the LORD’s sanctuary; my heart and my very being cry out passionately for the living God.

3 Even the sparrow has found a home, and the swallow a nest for herself, where she may place her young—near your altars, O LORD of hosts, my King and my God.

4 Blessed are those who live in your house; they will continually praise you. Selah.

5 Blessed is the person who finds their strength in you, whose heart is set on the paths of righteousness.

6 As they journey through the Valley of Weeping, they transform it into a place of springs; even the early rains cover it with blessings.

7 They grow from strength to strength; each one of them will appear before God in Zion.

8 O LORD, God of armies, listen to my prayer; pay attention, O God of Jacob. Pause and reflect.

9 Behold, O God, our protector, and turn your gaze upon the face of your chosen one.

10 Spending a single day in your presence is better than a thousand elsewhere. I would rather stand at the threshold of the house of my God than dwell in the homes of the wicked.

11 For the LORD God is like a sun and a shield; the LORD bestows favor and honor. He withholds no good thing from those who live with integrity.

12 O LORD of armies, blessed is the person who places their trust in you.
