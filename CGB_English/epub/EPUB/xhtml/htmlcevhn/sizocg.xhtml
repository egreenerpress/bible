Titus Chapter 1

1 Paul, a servant of God and an apostle of Jesus Christ, sent to proclaim the faith of those chosen by God and to lead them to the knowledge of the truth that leads to godly living.

2 In the confident expectation of eternal life, which God—who cannot deceive or break His word—promised before time itself began.

3 But at the appointed time, he has made his word known through the message proclaimed, which has been entrusted to me by the command of God our Savior.

4 To Titus, my true son in our shared faith: May grace, mercy, and peace be yours from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ, our Savior.

5 This is why I left you in Crete: so that you could put in order what remains to be done and appoint elders in every town, just as I instructed you.

6 If anyone is to be considered blameless, he must be the husband of one wife, with children who are trustworthy and not known for wild behavior or disobedience.

7 For an overseer must be above reproach, as one entrusted with God’s work; not arrogant, not quick-tempered, not addicted to wine, not violent, nor greedy for dishonest gain.

8 But one who is devoted to showing hospitality, who loves what is good, who is self-controlled, upright, godly, and disciplined.

9 Hold firmly to the trustworthy message as it has been taught, so that he may be able to encourage others with sound teaching and refute those who oppose it.

10 For there are many rebellious individuals, empty talkers, and deceivers, particularly those who insist on circumcision.

11 These individuals must be silenced, for they are corrupting entire households by teaching what they should not, all for the sake of dishonest gain.

12 One of their own, a prophet from among them, said, "The people of Crete are always liars, vicious animals, and lazy gluttons."

13 This testimony is trustworthy. Therefore, correct them firmly, so that they may become strong and steadfast in the faith.

14 Do not pay attention to Jewish myths or the rules made by people who have turned away from the truth.

15 To those who are pure, all things are pure; but to those who are corrupted and lack faith, nothing is pure. Indeed, both their minds and their consciences are defiled.

16 They claim to know God, but their actions contradict this, as they reject Him through their detestable and rebellious behavior. They are unfit for any good deed, proving themselves to be morally corrupt and unwilling to obey.
