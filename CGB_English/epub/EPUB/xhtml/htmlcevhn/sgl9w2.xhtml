Proverbs Chapter 30

1 The sayings of Agur, son of Jakeh—an inspired declaration: This man declared his message to Ithiel, yes, to Ithiel and Ucal.

2 Certainly, I am more foolish than any person, and I lack the understanding that a human being should possess.

3 I have not acquired wisdom, nor do I possess the knowledge of the Holy One.

4 Who has gone up to heaven and come back down? Who has gathered the wind in their hands? Who has wrapped up the waters in a cloak? Who has set the boundaries of the earth? What is His name, and what is the name of His Son—if you know?

5 Every word of God is flawless; He is a protective shield for those who place their trust in Him.

6 Do not add to his words, or he will correct you, and you will be exposed as a deceiver.

7 Two things I ask of you; do not refuse me before I die:

8 Keep me far from falsehood and deceit; do not let me experience extreme poverty or excessive wealth. Provide me with the sustenance I need, neither too little nor too much.

9 To prevent me from becoming so satisfied that I might reject you and say, "Who is the Lord?" or from becoming so desperate in poverty that I might steal and dishonor the name of my God.

10 Do not accuse a servant to his master, or he may curse you, and you will be held accountable.

11 There is a generation that speaks ill of their father and fails to honor their mother.

12 There is a generation that considers themselves pure in their own sight, yet they have not been cleansed from their defilement.

13 There is a generation whose eyes are filled with arrogance, and their gaze is haughty and lifted high.

14 There exists a generation whose teeth are like swords, and their fangs like knives, to consume the poor from the earth and the needy from among humanity.

15 The leech has two daughters that cry out, "Give! Give!" There are three things that are never satisfied, and four that never say, "Enough!"

16 The grave and the childless womb; the land that is never satisfied with water; and the fire that never says, "It is enough."

17 The eye that mocks its father and scorns obedience to its mother will be plucked out by the ravens of the valley and devoured by the young eagles.

18 There are three things that are beyond my understanding, and four that I cannot comprehend:

19 The path of an eagle soaring through the sky; the path of a serpent gliding over a rock; the path of a ship cutting through the open sea; and the path of a man pursuing a young woman—these are mysteries beyond full understanding.

20 This is the behavior of an adulterous woman: she indulges in her actions, then casually wipes her mouth and declares, "I have done nothing wrong."

21 The earth is troubled by three things, and there are four that it cannot endure:

22 When a servant becomes king, or a fool is filled with plenty of food—

23 When a hateful woman is married, and a servant girl inherits her mistress's position.

24 Here are four creatures that are small on the earth, yet they are remarkably wise:

25 The ants are a community without great strength, yet they diligently gather their food during the summer.

26 The rock hyraxes are a weak and vulnerable people, yet they make their homes in the cliffs.

27 The locusts have no ruler, yet they advance together in orderly swarms.

28 The spider skillfully grasps with its hands and makes its home even in the palaces of kings.

29 There are three things that move with grace, and even four that are majestic in their movement:

30 A lion, the mightiest among wild animals, does not retreat from any challenge.

31 A swift and graceful greyhound; a strong and commanding male goat; and a king whose authority is unshakable—none can stand against him.

32 If you have acted foolishly by exalting yourself, or if you have entertained wicked thoughts, restrain yourself and hold your tongue.

33 Certainly, just as churning milk produces butter, and squeezing the nose brings forth blood, so too does stirring up anger lead to conflict.
