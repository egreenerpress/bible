Revelation Chapter 5

1 And I observed in the right hand of the one seated on the throne a scroll, inscribed both on the inside and on the back, secured with seven seals.

2 Then I saw a mighty angel proclaiming in a powerful voice, "Who is worthy to open the scroll and break its seals?"

3 No one in heaven, on earth, or under the earth was able to open the scroll or even look at it.

4 I cried deeply, for no one was found worthy to open the scroll, to read it, or even to look at it.

5 Then one of the elders said to me, "Do not weep. Look, the Lion from the tribe of Judah, the Root of David, has triumphed and is worthy to open the scroll and break its seven seals."

6 Then I looked, and there in the center of the throne and among the four living creatures, and among the elders, stood a Lamb that appeared to have been slain. It had seven horns and seven eyes, which are the seven spirits of God sent out into all the earth.

7 He approached and took the scroll from the right hand of the one seated on the throne.

8 When he took the scroll, the four living creatures and the twenty-four elders fell down before the Lamb. Each of them held a harp and golden bowls filled with incense, which represent the prayers of God’s people.

9 They sang a new song, declaring, "You are worthy to take the scroll and to open its seals, because you were slain, and with your blood you purchased people for God from every tribe, language, people, and nation."

10 You have appointed us to be a kingdom and priests to serve our God, and we will reign on the earth.

11 Then I looked, and I heard the sound of countless angels surrounding the throne, along with the living creatures and the elders. Their number was innumerable—tens of thousands multiplied by tens of thousands, and thousands upon thousands.

12 They proclaimed with a loud voice: "The Lamb who was slain is worthy to receive power, wealth, wisdom, strength, honor, glory, and praise!"

13 And I heard every living being—those in heaven, on the earth, beneath the earth, and in the sea, along with all that is in them—declaring, "Praise, honor, glory, and power belong to the one who sits on the throne and to the Lamb, forever and ever!"

14 Then the four living creatures said, "Amen." And the twenty-four elders fell down and worshiped the one who lives forever and ever.
