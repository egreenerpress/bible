Exodus Chapter 31

1 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2 Behold, I have specifically chosen Bezalel, the son of Uri, the son of Hur, from the tribe of Judah.

3 I have filled him with the Spirit of God, granting him wisdom, understanding, and knowledge, as well as skill in every kind of craftsmanship.

4 To create skillful designs, to craft works in gold, silver, and bronze.

5 And in the cutting and setting of stones, and in the carving of wood, to craft every kind of skilled workmanship.

6 And now, I have appointed alongside him Oholiab, the son of Ahisamach, from the tribe of Dan. Moreover, I have endowed all who are skilled with wisdom, so that they may create everything I have commanded you.

7 The tent of meeting, the ark of the covenant, the atonement cover that rests upon it, and all the furnishings of the tabernacle.

8 And the table with all its utensils, the pure lampstand with all its accessories, and the altar of incense.

9 And the altar for burnt offerings, along with all its utensils, and the basin with its stand.

10 The finely woven garments for sacred duties, the holy vestments for Aaron the priest, and the clothing for his sons, to serve in the priestly role.

11 And the sacred anointing oil, as well as the fragrant incense for the holy sanctuary, shall be prepared exactly as I have instructed you. They must carry out everything according to my command.

12 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

13 You must also speak to the Israelites and say to them, "You must certainly observe my Sabbaths, for they are a sign between me and you throughout all your generations, so that you may know that I am the LORD who sets you apart as holy."

14 You must observe the Sabbath, for it is sacred to you. Anyone who desecrates it must surely be put to death. Whoever does any work on that day will be cut off from the community of their people.

15 For six days, work may be done, but the seventh day is a Sabbath of complete rest, set apart as holy to the LORD. Anyone who does any work on the Sabbath day must surely be put to death.

16 Therefore, the people of Israel must observe the Sabbath, keeping it holy throughout their generations as an everlasting covenant.

17 This is a lasting sign between me and the people of Israel forever: for in six days the LORD created the heavens and the earth, and on the seventh day he ceased from his work and was renewed.

18 When Moses had finished speaking with God on Mount Sinai, God gave him two stone tablets inscribed with the terms of the covenant. These tablets, known as the tablets of testimony, were written by the very hand of God.
