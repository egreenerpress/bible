Psalms Chapter 9

1 (For the music director, according to the tune "Muth-labben." A psalm of David.) I will praise you, O LORD, with all my heart; I will declare all your wonderful deeds.

2 I will be filled with joy and delight in you; I will sing praises to your name, O Most High.

3 When my enemies retreat, they will stumble and be destroyed in your presence.

4 For you have upheld my rights and defended my cause; you have sat on the throne, judging with justice.

5 You have rebuked the nations, you have destroyed the wicked; you have erased their name forever and ever.

6 O enemy, your destructions have reached their final end; you have laid waste to cities, and their memory has perished along with them.

7 But the LORD will remain forever; He has established His throne for judgment.

8 He will govern the world with justice and administer fairness to all people with integrity.

9 The LORD will also be a safe haven for those who are crushed by hardship, a place of shelter in times of distress.

10 Those who truly know your name will place their trust in you, for you, O LORD, have never abandoned those who earnestly seek you.

11 Sing praises to the LORD, who dwells in Zion; proclaim His deeds among the nations.

12 When God investigates cases of bloodshed, He takes note of them; He does not overlook the cries of the oppressed.

13 Show me mercy, O LORD; take notice of the distress I endure from those who hate me, you who rescue me from the brink of death.

14 So that I may proclaim all your praises within the gates of Zion, the city of your people, I will rejoice in the deliverance you have brought.

15 The nations have fallen into the pit they dug; their own foot is caught in the net they concealed.

16 The LORD reveals His character through the justice He carries out; the wicked are trapped by the consequences of their own actions. *Pause and reflect.* *Selah.*

17 The wicked will be sent to the grave, and all the nations that disregard God.

18 The oppressed will not always be forgotten; the hope of the afflicted will not be lost forever.

19 Rise up, O LORD; do not allow humanity to triumph. Let the nations be judged before your presence.

20 Cause them to tremble, O LORD, so that the nations may recognize that they are merely human. Selah.
