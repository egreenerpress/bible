Psalms Chapter 22

1 (For the music director, set to the tune of "The Doe of the Dawn." A psalm of David.) My God, my God, why have you abandoned me? Why are you so distant from delivering me, so far from hearing my cries of anguish?

2 O my God, I call out to you during the day, but you do not answer; and in the night, I continue to cry out without ceasing.

3 But you are holy, O you who dwell in the midst of Israel's praises.

4 Our ancestors placed their trust in you; they relied on you, and you rescued them.

5 They called out to you, and you saved them; they put their trust in you, and were not put to shame.

6 Yet I am but a lowly worm, not even a man; scorned by others and despised by the people.

7 Everyone who sees me mocks me; they sneer and shake their heads, saying,

8 He placed his trust in the LORD, believing that He would rescue him. Let the LORD save him now, since He takes pleasure in him.

9 But you are the one who brought me forth from the womb; you made me trust in you even as I lay at my motherâ€™s breast.

10 From the moment I was born, I was placed in your care; you have been my God since before I drew my first breath.

11 Do not stay distant from me, for trouble is close at hand, and there is no one to help me.

12 Many powerful enemies surround me; fierce adversaries, like the mighty bulls of Bashan, have encircled me on every side.

13 They opened their mouths wide against me, like a fierce and roaring lion.

14 I am utterly spent, like water poured out, and all my bones are dislocated. My heart has become like wax; it melts away within me.

15 My strength is completely drained, like a broken piece of pottery; my tongue sticks to the roof of my mouth, and you have laid me in the dust of death.

16 Dogs have surrounded me; a group of evildoers has closed in on me. They have pierced my hands and my feet.

17 I can count all my bones; they gaze and gloat over me.

18 They divide my clothing among themselves and cast lots for my garments.

19 Do not stay distant from me, O LORD; my source of strength, come quickly to my aid.

20 Rescue my life from the sword; save my precious one from the power of the enemy.

21 Rescue me from the lion's jaws, for you have answered me when I cried out from the horns of the wild oxen.

22 I will proclaim your name to my brothers and sisters; in the assembly of your people, I will praise you.

23 You who revere the LORD, praise him; all you descendants of Jacob, honor him; and stand in awe of him, all you people of Israel.

24 For He has not despised nor rejected the suffering of the afflicted; nor has He turned His face away from them. But when they cried out to Him, He listened.

25 I will praise you before the great assembly; I will fulfill my vows in the presence of those who honor you.

26 The humble will eat and be fully satisfied; those who seek the LORD will praise him. Your heart will live forever.

27 All the farthest reaches of the earth will remember and turn to the LORD, and all the families of the nations will bow down in worship before you.

28 The kingdom belongs to the LORD, and He is the one who rules over all the nations.

29 All who prosper on the earth will feast and give praise; all who descend to the grave will bow down before Him. No one can preserve their own life.

30 A future generation will serve him; they will be recognized by the Lord as his people.

31 They will come and proclaim His righteousness to a people yet to be born, declaring that He has accomplished this.
