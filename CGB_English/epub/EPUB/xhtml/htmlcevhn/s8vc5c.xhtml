Lamentations Chapter 5

1 O LORD, take notice of what has happened to us; look closely and see the disgrace we endure.

2 Our inheritance has been handed over to foreigners, and our homes have been taken by outsiders.

3 We are like orphans, without fathers; our mothers are like widows.

4 We have had to pay for the water we drink; even our firewood comes at a price.

5 We are oppressed and burdened; we toil endlessly, finding no relief.

6 We have submitted ourselves to the Egyptians and the Assyrians, seeking only enough bread to satisfy our hunger.

7 Our ancestors sinned, and they are no more; yet we bear the consequences of their wrongdoing.

8 Servants have dominated us; there is no one to rescue us from their control.

9 We obtained our bread at the risk of our lives because of the threat of the sword in the wilderness.

10 Our skin has become scorched and darkened like an oven because of the devastating famine.

11 They violated the women in Zion and the young women in the cities of Judah.

12 Leaders are executed by their own people; the elderly are shown no respect.

13 The young men were forced to work at the grinding mill, and the children stumbled under the weight of the wood.

14 The leaders no longer sit at the city gate to govern, and the young men have stopped their joyful music.

15 The delight of our hearts has come to an end; our joyful dancing has turned into sorrow.

16 The crown has fallen from our heads; how terrible it is for us, for we have sinned!

17 Because of all this, our hearts are overwhelmed; because of these things, our vision grows weak.

18 Because of Mount Zion, which lies in ruins, foxes roam over it.

19 You, O LORD, endure forever; your throne stands firm throughout all generations.

20 Why do you completely forget about us and abandon us for such a long time?

21 Turn us back to you, O LORD, and we will return; restore our lives as they were in former times.

22 But you have completely abandoned us; your anger burns fiercely against us.
