I Corinthians Chapter 3

1 Brothers and sisters, I could not address you as people who are spiritually mature, but as those who are still worldly—mere infants in Christ.

2 I have nourished you with basic teachings, not with deeper truths, because until now you were not ready to receive them, and even now you are still not prepared.

3 For you are still worldly in your thinking. Since there is jealousy, quarreling, and division among you, are you not acting in a worldly manner and living by human standards?

4 For when one person says, "I follow Paul," and another says, "I follow Apollos," are you not acting in a worldly, fleshly manner?

5 Who, then, is Paul, and who is Apollos? They are merely servants through whom you came to believe, as the Lord assigned to each his role.

6 I planted the seeds, Apollos watered them, but it was God who made them grow.

7 Therefore, neither the one who plants nor the one who waters is anything, but only God, who causes the growth.

8 The one who plants and the one who waters share a common purpose, and each will receive their own reward according to their own work.

9 For we are coworkers with God; you are God’s cultivated field, you are God’s construction project.

10 By the grace of God that has been given to me, like a skilled master builder, I have laid the foundation, and someone else is building on it. But let each person be careful how they build upon it.

11 No one can lay any foundation other than the one that has already been established, which is Jesus Christ.

12 If anyone builds on this foundation using gold, silver, precious stones, wood, hay, or straw,

13 Each person's work will be brought to light, for the day will reveal it, because it will be disclosed by fire. The fire will test the quality of each one's work, determining what kind it truly is.

14 If anyone's work endures, being built upon that foundation, they will receive a reward.

15 If someone's work is destroyed by fire, they will suffer loss; yet they themselves will be saved, but only as one escaping through the flames.

16 Do you not realize that you are God’s temple and that the Spirit of God lives within you?

17 If anyone corrupts or desecrates the temple of God, God will bring that person to ruin; for the temple of God is sacred, and you yourselves are that temple.

18 Do not let anyone deceive themselves. If anyone among you considers themselves wise by the standards of this world, they should become willing to be seen as foolish, so that they may truly become wise.

19 The wisdom of this world is foolishness in God's sight. As it is written: "He catches the wise in their own cleverness."

20 And once more, the Lord understands the thoughts of the wise, recognizing that they are futile.

21 Therefore, let no one boast about human leaders. For everything belongs to you.

22 Whether it is Paul, or Apollos, or Cephas, or the world, or life, or death, or the present, or the future—all these things belong to you.

23 You belong to Christ, and Christ belongs to God.
