Romans Chapter 2

1 Therefore, you have no excuse, O man, whoever you are, when you judge others. For in passing judgment on someone else, you condemn yourself, because you, the judge, are doing the very same things.

2 We are certain that God’s judgment is based on truth and is directed against those who practice such things.

3 Do you really think, O man, when you judge those who do such things and yet do the same yourself, that you will escape the judgment of God?

4 Or do you disregard the abundance of His kindness, patience, and forbearance, failing to recognize that God’s kindness is meant to lead you to repentance?

5 But because of your stubbornness and unrepentant heart, you are storing up wrath for yourself against the day of wrath, when God’s righteous judgment will be revealed.

6 Who will give to each person according to what they have done:

7 To those who persistently and patiently continue in doing good, seeking glory, honor, and immortality, they will receive eternal life.

8 But for those who are argumentative, refuse to obey the truth, and instead follow unrighteousness, there will be wrath and fierce anger.

9 Suffering and distress will come upon every person who does evil, both the Jew first and also the Gentile.

10 But glory, honor, and peace will be given to everyone who does what is good—to the Jew first, and also to the Gentile.

11 For God does not show favoritism or partiality toward anyone.

12 All those who have sinned apart from the law will also perish apart from the law, and all those who have sinned under the law will be judged by the law.

13 For it is not those who merely hear the law who are righteous in God’s sight, but those who actually obey the law who will be declared righteous.

14 When the Gentiles, who do not have the law, naturally do what the law requires, they demonstrate that the law is written on their hearts, even though they do not possess it. In this way, they become a law to themselves.

15 This passage reveals that the requirements of the law are inscribed in their hearts, as their conscience also testifies to this truth. At the same time, their thoughts either accuse or defend one another.

16 On the day when God will judge the hidden things of humanity through Jesus Christ, as declared in the message I proclaim.

17 Look, you are called a Jew, and you rely on the law, and you boast in God.

18 You understand God’s will and discern what is right and excellent, having been taught from the law.

19 And are you confident that you yourself are a guide to the blind, a light to those who are in darkness?

20 You are an instructor of the foolish, a teacher of the immature, who possess the outward appearance of knowledge and truth as found in the law.

21 Therefore, you who teach others, do you not teach yourself? You who preach that one should not steal, do you steal?

22 You who declare that a person must not commit adultery, do you commit adultery yourself? You who detest idols, do you engage in sacrilege?

23 You who take pride in the law—do you dishonor God by breaking it?

24 Because of you, the name of God is dishonored among the nations, as it is written.

25 Circumcision indeed has value if you obey the law; but if you break the law, your circumcision becomes as though you were never circumcised.

26 Therefore, if those who are not circumcised uphold the righteous requirements of the law, will not their lack of circumcision be regarded as though they were circumcised?

27 And if those who are uncircumcised by nature yet keep the requirements of the law, will they not condemn you, who, though you have the written law and circumcision, break the law?

28 For a person is not truly a Jew because of outward appearances alone, nor is circumcision merely an external mark on the body.

29 A true Jew is one inwardly, and true circumcision is a matter of the heart, brought about by the Spirit, not by the written law. Such a person’s praise comes not from others, but from God.
