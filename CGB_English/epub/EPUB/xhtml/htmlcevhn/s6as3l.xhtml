Job Chapter 6

1 But Job replied and said,

2 If only my suffering could be fully measured, and all my misery placed on the scales to be weighed!

3 For now, my suffering would outweigh the sand of the sea; that is why my words fail me.

4 The arrows of the Almighty pierce me deeply; their poison drains away my strength. The terrors of God are arrayed against me like an army, overwhelming my spirit.

5 Does the wild donkey bray when it has grass to eat? Does the ox bellow when it has its fodder?

6 Can something tasteless be eaten without salt? Or is there any flavor in the clear part of an egg?

7 The things that my soul once refused to even touch have now become like bitter food to me, filling me with sorrow.

8 Oh, if only my plea could be granted, and if God would fulfill the desire of my heart!

9 If only it would please God to end my lifeâ€”if He would release His hand and bring my suffering to an end!

10 If only I could find some comfort; even in the midst of my suffering, I would endure it bravely. Let God not hold back His hand, for I have not hidden the words of the Holy One.

11 What is my strength, that I should hold on to hope? And what is my future, that I should desire to prolong my life?

12 Is my strength like that of stone? Or is my body made of bronze?

13 Is my strength not within me? Has wisdom been completely taken away from me?

14 To someone who is suffering, compassion should be shown by their friend; yet they abandon reverence for the Almighty.

15 My brothers have acted treacherously, like a seasonal stream, like the fleeting waters of wadis that vanish.

16 They appear dark because of the ice, and the snow conceals what lies beneath.

17 When they grow warm, they disappear; when the heat rises, they are completely consumed from their place.

18 Their paths have veered off course; they wander aimlessly and come to ruin.

19 The warriors of Tema gazed intently, and the caravans of Sheba eagerly anticipated their arrival.

20 They were utterly dismayed because their hopes had failed; they arrived there only to be filled with shame.

21 "Now you are of no help; you see my downfall and are filled with fear."

22 Did I ever ask you to bring me something? Or did I demand that you give me a gift from your wealth?

23 Or, can you rescue me from the grasp of my enemy? Or, can you free me from the power of the mighty?

24 Instruct me, and I will remain silent; help me comprehend where I have gone wrong.

25 How powerful are truthful words! But what does your arguing actually prove?

26 Do you presume to criticize mere words, the utterances of someone in despair, which are as fleeting as the wind?

27 Indeed, you crush the fatherless, and you set a trap for your friend.

28 Now, be satisfied and look at me closely; it will be clear to you whether I am lying.

29 I urge you to turn back, and do not let this be an act of wrongdoing. Yes, return once more, for my integrity is at stake in this matter.

30 Is there any deceit in my words? Can my judgment not distinguish what is corrupt?
