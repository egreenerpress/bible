II Corinthians Chapter 3

1 Are we starting once more to promote ourselves? Or do we need, like some others, letters of recommendation to you or from you?

2 You are like a letter written on our hearts, recognized and understood by everyone.

3 It is evident that you yourselves are a letter from Christ, delivered through our ministry, written not with ink but by the Spirit of the living God—not on tablets of stone, but on tablets of human hearts.

4 We have this confidence through Christ, directed toward God.

5 We are not capable on our own to consider anything as originating from ourselves; rather, our ability comes from God.

6 He has also enabled us to be competent ministers of the new covenant, not of the written code but of the Spirit. For the written code brings death, but the Spirit gives life.

7 Now if the ministry that brought death, inscribed in letters on stone tablets, was so glorious that the Israelites could not gaze steadily at Moses’ face because of the radiance of his countenance—a radiance that was fading—

8 How can the ministry of the Spirit not be even more glorious?

9 For if the service that brings condemnation was glorious, how much more glorious is the service that brings righteousness!

10 Even that which was once glorious has no glory in comparison, because of the far greater glory that surpasses it.

11 For if what was being set aside was glorious, how much more glorious is what remains.

12 Since we have such confidence and assurance, we speak with boldness and clarity.

13 Unlike Moses, who placed a veil over his face so that the Israelites could not gaze intently at the fading glory of what was being set aside,

14 However, their minds were closed and unable to understand, for even to this day, the same veil remains when the old covenant is read. This veil is only removed through Christ.

15 But even to this day, whenever the writings of Moses are read, a veil remains over their hearts.

16 However, when one turns to the Lord, the veil is removed.

17 Now the Lord is the Spirit, and wherever the Spirit of the Lord is present, there is freedom.

18 But all of us, with unveiled faces, gazing upon the glory of the Lord as if reflected in a mirror, are being transformed into the same image, moving from one degree of glory to another, just as this comes from the Spirit of the Lord.
