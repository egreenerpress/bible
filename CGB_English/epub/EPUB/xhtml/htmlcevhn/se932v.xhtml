I Corinthians Chapter 7

1 Regarding the matters you wrote to me about: It is beneficial for a man not to have sexual relations with a woman.

2 To prevent sexual immorality, each man should have his own wife, and each woman should have her own husband.

3 The husband should fulfill his marital duty to his wife, and the wife should do the same for her husband.

4 A wife does not have exclusive authority over her own body, but her husband does. In the same way, a husband does not have exclusive authority over his own body, but his wife does.

5 Do not deprive one another of marital intimacy, unless you both agree to abstain for a limited time, so that you may devote yourselves to prayer and fasting. Then come together again, so that Satan may not tempt you because of your lack of self-control.

6 However, I say this as a concession, not as a command.

7 I wish that everyone could remain as I am. However, each person has their own unique gift from God, one suited to their particular circumstances, and another to theirs.

8 I say this to those who are unmarried and to widows: It is good for them to remain single, as I am.

9 However, if they are unable to exercise self-control, they should marry, for it is better to marry than to be consumed with passion.

10 To those who are married, I give this instruction—not from myself, but from the Lord—a wife must not separate from her husband.

11 However, if she does leave, she must remain unmarried or be reconciled to her husband. Likewise, the husband must not divorce his wife.

12 However, to the others I speak, not as a direct command from the Lord: If any brother has a wife who does not believe, and she is willing to live with him, he should not divorce her.

13 If a woman has a husband who does not believe, and he is willing to live with her, she should not leave him.

14 For the husband who does not believe is set apart through his wife, and the wife who does not believe is set apart through her husband. Otherwise, your children would be considered impure, but as it is, they are holy.

15 However, if the unbelieving partner chooses to leave, let them leave. In such situations, a believing husband or wife is not bound to remain in the marriage. For God has called us to live in peace.

16 How can you be certain, O wife, whether you will bring about the salvation of your husband? Or how can you know, O husband, whether you will bring about the salvation of your wife?

17 However, as God has assigned to each person their role, and as the Lord has called each one, let them live accordingly. This is the instruction I give in all the churches.

18 If someone was called by God while already circumcised, they should not try to reverse it. If someone was called while uncircumcised, they should not seek circumcision.

19 Being circumcised or uncircumcised means nothing; what truly matters is obeying God’s commands.

20 Let each person remain in the situation or role in which they were when they were called.

21 Were you called while you were a servant? Do not let it trouble you. But if you are able to gain your freedom, take advantage of the opportunity.

22 For whoever is called by the Lord while being a servant is now set free by the Lord. In the same way, whoever is called while being free becomes a servant of Christ.

23 You have been purchased at a great cost; do not become slaves to human masters.

24 Brothers and sisters, each person should remain in the situation in which they were called, living in it with faithfulness to God.

25 Regarding the matter of unmarried women, I do not have a direct command from the Lord. However, I offer my opinion as one who has received the Lord’s mercy and is therefore trustworthy.

26 I believe, therefore, that it is beneficial in the current difficult circumstances for a person to remain as they are.

27 Are you married? Do not seek to end the marriage. Are you unmarried? Do not seek a wife.

28 However, if you choose to marry, you have not sinned; and if a young woman marries, she has not sinned. Yet those who marry will face difficulties in this life, but I say this to spare you from unnecessary hardship.

29 But I say this, brothers and sisters: the time is limited. From now on, those who have wives should live as though they do not have them.

30 Those who weep should live as though they are not weeping; those who rejoice should live as though they are not rejoicing; and those who buy should live as though they do not truly own anything.

31 And those who engage with the world should do so as though they are not fully absorbed by it, for the present form and structure of this world is passing away.

32 I want you to be free from anxiety. The unmarried person is concerned about the things of the Lord—how to live in a way that pleases the Lord.

33 However, the one who is married is concerned with worldly matters, seeking to please his wife.

34 There is a distinction between a wife and an unmarried woman. The unmarried woman is devoted to the concerns of the Lord, striving to be holy in both body and spirit. However, the married woman is occupied with the affairs of the world, focusing on how to please her husband.

35 I say this for your benefit, not to impose restrictions on you, but to promote what is proper and to help you focus on serving the Lord without distraction.

36 But if anyone believes he is not acting appropriately toward his unmarried daughter, especially if she is beyond the usual age for marriage and circumstances require it, he is free to do as he sees fit—he does not sin. Let them marry.

37 However, the person who is firmly resolved in their heart, not feeling compelled but having control over their own will, and has decided in their heart to remain unmarried, does well.

38 Therefore, the one who allows her to marry is doing well, but the one who does not allow her to marry is doing even better.

39 A wife is legally bound to her husband as long as he lives. However, if her husband dies, she is free to marry anyone she chooses, provided that the marriage is in accordance with the Lord's will.

40 However, she will be more content if she remains as she is, according to my understanding. And I believe that I, too, have the Spirit of God.
