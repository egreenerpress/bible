Numbers Chapter 25

1 And the Israelites stayed in Shittim, but the people began to engage in immoral acts with the women of Moab.

2 They invited the people to join in the offerings to their gods, and the people ate from the sacrifices and worshiped their gods.

3 And the people of Israel aligned themselves with the worship of Baal of Peor, provoking the LORD's anger to burn fiercely against them.

4 The LORD said to Moses, "Gather the leaders of the people and execute them publicly in the presence of the LORD, under the light of the sun, so that the LORD's intense anger may be turned away from Israel."

5 Moses said to the leaders of Israel, "Each of you must execute those among your people who have aligned themselves with Baal of Peor."

6 Suddenly, one of the Israelites came and brought a Midianite woman to his fellow Israelites in full view of Moses and the entire assembly of the people of Israel, who were weeping at the entrance to the tent of meeting.

7 When Phinehas, the son of Eleazar and grandson of Aaron the priest, saw what was happening, he stood up from among the assembly, took a spear in his hand,

8 He followed the Israelite man into the tent and pierced both of them—the Israelite man and the woman, through her abdomen. In this way, the plague was halted among the Israelites.

9 The number of those who died in the plague was twenty-four thousand.

10 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

11 Phinehas, the son of Eleazar and grandson of Aaron the priest, has turned my anger away from the Israelites. Because he acted with zeal on my behalf among them, I did not destroy the Israelites in my fierce indignation.

12 Therefore, declare this: "Behold, I am granting him My covenant of peace."

13 He and his descendants after him will possess it—the covenant of an eternal priesthood—because he was deeply devoted to his God and made atonement for the people of Israel.

14 The Israelite man who was killed, the one slain alongside the Midianite woman, was named Zimri, the son of Salu. He was a leader from a prominent family among the Simeonites.

15 The name of the Midianite woman who was killed was Cozbi, the daughter of Zur. He was a prominent leader among his people and belonged to a ruling family in Midian.

16 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

17 Trouble the Midianites and strike them down.

18 For they trouble you with their deceitful schemes, by which they led you astray in the incident involving Peor, and in the matter of Cozbi, the daughter of a Midianite leader, their sister, who was killed on the day of the plague because of what happened at Peor.
