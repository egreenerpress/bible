Numbers Chapter 17

1 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2 Speak to the Israelites and instruct them to bring a staff from each of their ancestral families, one from every leader of their ancestral households—twelve staffs in total. Write each man’s name on his staff.

3 You shall write Aaron’s name on the staff of Levi, for there shall be one staff for the leader of each ancestral family.

4 You shall place them inside the tent of meeting, in front of the covenant tablets, where I will meet with you.

5 This is what will happen: the staff of the man I choose will sprout with blossoms. In this way, I will put an end to the complaints of the Israelites, who are grumbling against you.

6 Moses spoke to the Israelites, and each of their leaders gave him a staff—one for each leader, according to their ancestral families, totaling twelve staffs. Aaron’s staff was among them.

7 Moses placed the staffs before the LORD in the tent of meeting, where the testimony was kept.

8 The next day, Moses entered the tent of testimony, and there he saw that Aaron’s staff, representing the house of Levi, had sprouted. It had not only produced buds but had also blossomed and yielded ripe almonds.

9 Moses brought all the staffs out from the presence of the LORD and presented them to the Israelites. Each man looked and took his own staff.

10 The LORD said to Moses, "Take Aaron's staff and place it again before the covenant testimony, to be kept as a sign for the rebellious. This will put an end to their complaints against me, so that they do not die."

11 Moses carried out the task exactly as the LORD had instructed him; he did everything according to the command.

12 The Israelites spoke to Moses, saying, "Look, we are dying, we are perishing, we are all perishing!"

13 Whoever comes too close to the tabernacle of the LORD will die. Are we all destined to perish?
