Song of Songs Chapter 6

1 Where has your beloved gone, most beautiful among women? In which direction has your beloved turned, so that we may search for him with you?

2 My beloved has gone down to his garden, to the beds of fragrant spices, to graze among the gardens and to gather lilies.

3 I belong to my beloved, and my beloved is mine; he grazes among the lilies.

4 You are beautiful, my beloved, as lovely as Tirzah, as radiant as Jerusalem, as awe-inspiring as an army marching with banners.

5 Turn your gaze away from me, for it overwhelms me. Your hair is like a flock of goats streaming down the slopes of Gilead.

6 Your teeth are like a flock of sheep coming up from being washed, each one perfectly paired, and none among them is missing or barren.

7 Your temples, framed by your flowing hair, are like a slice of pomegranateâ€”radiant and beautiful.

8 There are sixty queens and eighty concubines, and young maidens beyond counting.

9 My beloved, my pure one, is unique; she is the only daughter of her mother, the cherished one of the woman who bore her. When the young women saw her, they called her blessed; even the queens and the concubines praised her.

10 Who is this who appears like the dawn, radiant as the moon, pure and bright as the sun, awe-inspiring as a mighty army arrayed with banners?

11 I went down to the nut garden to observe the fruits of the valley, to see if the vines were thriving and if the pomegranates were beginning to bloom.

12 Before I even realized it, my soul had carried me away like the swift chariots of Amminadib.

13 Come back, come back, O Shulamite; come back, come back, so that we may gaze upon you. What do you see in the Shulamite? She is as majestic as a procession of two armies.
