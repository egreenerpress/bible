II Timothy Chapter 2

1 Therefore, my son, be strengthened by the grace that is found in Christ Jesus.

2 And the teachings you have received from me in the presence of many witnesses, entrust these to trustworthy individuals who will be competent to instruct others as well.

3 Therefore, endure hardship like a good soldier of Jesus Christ.

4 A soldier on active duty does not get entangled in the concerns of everyday life, so that he may fully devote himself to pleasing the one who enlisted him.

5 And if someone competes in an athletic contest, they will not receive the victor’s crown unless they compete according to the rules.

6 The farmer who works hard should be the first to receive a share of the harvest.

7 Think carefully about what I am saying, and may the Lord grant you insight and understanding in all matters.

8 Keep in mind that Jesus Christ, who is descended from David, was raised from the dead, as proclaimed in the message I preach.

9 I endure hardship as though I were a criminal, even to the point of being chained. Yet, the word of God cannot be imprisoned.

10 For this reason, I endure all things for the sake of the chosen, so that they too may attain the salvation that is found in Christ Jesus, accompanied by eternal glory.

11 This is a trustworthy statement: If we have died with Him, we will also live with Him.

12 If we endure suffering, we will also reign with him; but if we disown him, he will also disown us.

13 If we are faithless, he remains faithful—for he cannot deny who he is.

14 Remind them of these things, and solemnly urge them in the presence of God to avoid engaging in pointless disputes over words, which only lead to the ruin of those who listen.

15 Strive diligently to present yourself as one approved by God, as a worker who has no cause for shame, accurately handling the word of truth.

16 Avoid engaging in irreverent and empty talk, for such discussions only lead to further ungodliness.

17 Their teaching will spread like a deadly infection. Among them are Hymenaeus and Philetus.

18 Some have strayed from the truth by claiming that the resurrection has already occurred, and in doing so, they are undermining the faith of certain individuals.

19 However, the foundation established by God remains unshakable, bearing this inscription: "The Lord knows those who belong to Him." And, "Let everyone who calls on the name of Christ turn away from wickedness."

20 In a large household, there are not only containers made of gold and silver, but also those made of wood and clay. Some are intended for honorable purposes, while others are for common use.

21 Therefore, if someone cleanses themselves from these things, they will be a vessel of honor, set apart for sacred purposes, suitable for the Master’s use, and ready for every good work.

22 Run away from the desires of youth, but instead pursue righteousness, faith, love, and peace, alongside those who sincerely worship the Lord with a pure heart.

23 Avoid foolish and ignorant debates, for you know that they only breed quarrels.

24 The servant of the Lord must not engage in quarrels or disputes but should instead be gentle and kind toward everyone, skilled in teaching, and patient in enduring difficulties.

25 Gently teach those who are in opposition to themselves, in the hope that God may grant them a change of heart, leading them to recognize and embrace the truth.

26 And so that they may come to their senses and escape from the trap of the devil, who has taken them captive to do his will.
