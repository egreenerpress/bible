Mark Chapter 15

1 Early in the morning, the chief priests met with the elders, the teachers of the law, and the entire council. They bound Jesus, led him away, and handed him over to Pilate.

2 Pilate asked him, "Are you the King of the Jews?" Jesus replied, "You have said it."

3 The chief priests brought many accusations against him, but he did not respond to any of them.

4 Pilate questioned him again, saying, "Do you not offer any response? Consider how many charges they are bringing against you."

5 But Jesus still gave no answer, so that Pilate was amazed.

6 At that festival, it was his custom to release to them one prisoner, whomever they requested.

7 There was a man named Barabbas, who was imprisoned with others who had taken part in a rebellion with him. During that uprising, he had committed murder.

8 The crowd, shouting loudly, began to urge Him to do for them as He had always done before.

9 But Pilate responded to them, saying, "Do you want me to release to you the King of the Jews?"

10 For he understood that the chief priests had handed him over out of jealousy.

11 However, the chief priests persuaded the crowd to ask for Barabbas to be released to them instead.

12 Pilate responded and asked them again, "What then do you want me to do with the man you call the King of the Jews?"

13 And they shouted once more, "Crucify him!"

14 Then Pilate said to them, "Why? What crime has he committed?" But they shouted even louder, "Crucify him!"

15 Therefore, Pilate, wanting to satisfy the crowd, released Barabbas to them. After having Jesus flogged, he handed him over to be crucified.

16 The soldiers took him away into the building known as the Praetorium, and they gathered the entire cohort of soldiers there.

17 They dressed him in a purple robe, twisted together a crown of thorns, and placed it on his head.

18 They began to greet him, saying, "Hail, King of the Jews!"

19 They struck him on the head with a reed, spat on him, and, kneeling down, they mockingly paid him homage.

20 After they had ridiculed him, they removed the purple robe from him, dressed him in his own clothes, and led him away to be crucified.

21 They forced a man named Simon, a native of Cyrene, who was passing by on his way in from the countryside—the father of Alexander and Rufus—to carry the cross for him.

22 They led him to the place called Golgotha, which means "The Place of the Skull."

23 They offered him wine mixed with myrrh, but he refused to drink it.

24 After they had crucified him, they divided his clothing among themselves by casting lots to determine what each person would receive.

25 It was nine o'clock in the morning when they crucified Him.

26 The written statement of the charge against him was displayed above him: "THE KING OF THE JEWS."

27 They crucified two criminals alongside him, one on his right and the other on his left.

28 The scripture was fulfilled, which says, "And he was counted among the lawbreakers."

29 As people walked by, they hurled insults at him, shaking their heads in scorn and saying, "You who claim you can destroy the temple and rebuild it in three days—save yourself!"

30 "Save yourself and come down from the cross!"

31 In the same way, the chief priests, along with the scribes, mocked Him among themselves, saying, "He saved others, but He cannot save Himself."

32 "Let Christ, the King of Israel, come down from the cross now, so that we may see it and believe." Even those who were crucified alongside him insulted him.

33 When it was noon, darkness came over the entire land and lasted until three in the afternoon.

34 At three in the afternoon, Jesus cried out in a loud voice, saying, "Eloi, Eloi, lema sabachthani?" which means, "My God, my God, why have you abandoned me?"

35 Some of those standing nearby, upon hearing this, said, "Look, he is calling for Elijah."

36 Then someone ran and soaked a sponge in sour wine, placed it on a stick, and offered it to him to drink, saying, “Leave him alone; let’s see if Elijah comes to take him down.”

37 And Jesus called out in a loud voice and breathed his last.

38 And the curtain of the temple was torn in two, from top to bottom.

39 When the centurion, who was standing nearby, saw how he cried out and breathed his last, he said, "Truly, this man was the Son of God."

40 There were also women observing from a distance. Among them were Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James the younger and of Joses, and Salome.

41 (These women had also followed him and served him when he was in Galilee,) along with many other women who had come up with him to Jerusalem.

42 As evening approached, since it was the day of preparation—that is, the day before the Sabbath—

43 Joseph of Arimathea, a respected member of the council who was also eagerly anticipating the kingdom of God, came forward and boldly approached Pilate to request the body of Jesus.

44 Pilate was surprised to hear that Jesus had already died. Summoning the centurion, he asked him whether Jesus had been dead for some time.

45 When he learned this from the centurion, he granted the body to Joseph.

46 He purchased fine linen cloth, took the body down, and wrapped it in the linen. Then he placed it in a tomb that had been cut out of the rock, and he rolled a large stone against the entrance of the tomb.

47 Mary Magdalene and Mary, the mother of Joses, carefully observed the place where Jesus was laid to rest.
