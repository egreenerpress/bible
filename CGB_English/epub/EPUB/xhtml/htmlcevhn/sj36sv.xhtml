Isaiah Chapter 32

1 Look, a king will reign with justice, and leaders will govern with fairness.

2 A person will be like a shelter from the wind, a refuge from the storm, like streams of water in a parched land, like the shade of a massive rock in a desolate and weary land.

3 And the eyes of those who see will no longer grow dim, and the ears of those who hear will listen attentively.

4 The reckless heart will gain understanding, and the hesitant tongue will speak with clarity and confidence.

5 The wicked person will no longer be called honorable, nor will the deceitful be described as generous.

6 For the wicked person will speak wickedly, and their heart will devise evil, practicing deceit and spreading lies against the LORD. They deprive the hungry of nourishment and deny the thirsty their drink.

7 The tools of the wicked are corrupt; they plot evil schemes to ruin the poor with deceitful words, even when the needy speak justly.

8 But the generous person devises generous plans, and through their generosity, they will be established.

9 Stand up, you women who live in comfort; listen to my voice, you complacent daughters; pay attention to my words.

10 For many days and years, you complacent women will be distressed, for the grape harvest will fail, and the fruit gathering will not take place.

11 Shudder, you women who live in comfort; be deeply disturbed, you who feel secure. Remove your fine garments, expose yourselves, and wrap sackcloth around your waists.

12 They will mourn for the fertile lands, for the delightful fields, and for the abundant vineyards.

13 Upon the land of my people, thorns and briers will grow; indeed, they will spread over all the houses of celebration in the once joyful city.

14 Because the grand palaces will be abandoned; the bustling city will be deserted; the strongholds and watchtowers will become permanent dens, a place of delight for wild donkeys, and a grazing ground for flocks.

15 Until the Spirit is poured out upon us from heaven, and the barren wilderness becomes a fertile field, and the fertile field is transformed into a flourishing forest.

16 Then justice will reside in the wilderness, and righteousness will abide in the fertile land.

17 The result of living righteously will be peace, and the outcome of righteousness will be lasting tranquility and unshakable confidence.

18 And my people will live in peaceful homes, in secure dwellings, and in tranquil places of rest.

19 When hail falls and descends upon the forest, the city will be brought down to a place of humiliation.

20 Blessed are you who plant seeds near every water source, who allow the ox and the donkey to roam freely to graze there.
