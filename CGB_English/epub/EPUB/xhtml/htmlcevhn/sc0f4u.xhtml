James Chapter 3

1 My brothers and sisters, do not be too eager to become teachers, for you know that we who teach will be judged more strictly.

2 We all stumble in many ways. If someone does not stumble in what they say, they are a mature person, capable of controlling their entire being.

3 Look, we place bits in horses' mouths so that they will obey us, and with them, we can guide their entire body.

4 Consider also the ships: although they are large and driven by strong winds, they are steered by a very small rudder wherever the pilot desires.

5 In the same way, the tongue is a small part of the body, yet it makes grand claims. Consider how a tiny spark can set a vast forest ablaze!

6 The tongue is like a fire, a world of wrongdoing. Among all the parts of the body, the tongue is the one that corrupts the whole person. It ignites the course of life with its destructive power, and it is itself set ablaze by the fires of hell.

7 Every kind of animal—whether beasts, birds, reptiles, or creatures of the sea—has been subdued and brought under control by humanity.

8 No one can fully control the tongue; it is a restless and harmful force, filled with destructive venom.

9 With the same tongue, we bless God, the Father, and with it, we curse people, who are made in the likeness of God.

10 From the same mouth come both words of praise and words of harm. My brothers and sisters, this should not be the case.

11 Does a spring pour out from the same opening both fresh water and bitter?

12 Can a fig tree, my brothers and sisters, produce olives? Or can a grapevine bear figs? In the same way, a spring cannot gush both salty water and fresh.

13 Who among you is wise and understanding? Let them demonstrate it by living a good life, showing their deeds in the humility that comes from wisdom.

14 But if you harbor bitter jealousy and selfish ambition in your hearts, do not boast about it or deceive yourselves by denying the truth.

15 This kind of wisdom does not come from above, but is earthly, driven by natural desires, and influenced by evil.

16 Where jealousy and selfish ambition exist, there you will find disorder and every kind of harmful behavior.

17 The wisdom that comes from above is first and foremost pure; then it is peace-loving, considerate, and open to reason. It is full of mercy and produces good deeds, showing no favoritism and free from pretense.

18 The harvest of righteousness is cultivated by those who promote peace, and it flourishes in an atmosphere of peace.
