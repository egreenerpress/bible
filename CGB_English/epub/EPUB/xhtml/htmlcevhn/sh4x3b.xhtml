Psalms Chapter 5

1 (For the music director, to be played on wind instruments. A psalm of David.) Listen to my words, O LORD; pay attention to my heartfelt thoughts.

2 Listen to the sound of my plea, my King and my God, for it is to you that I bring my prayer.

3 In the morning, O LORD, you will hear my voice; each morning I will bring my prayers before you and eagerly await your response.

4 For you are not a God who takes delight in wickedness; nor will evil find a dwelling place with you.

5 The foolish will not endure in your presence; you detest all who practice wickedness.

6 You will bring to ruin those who speak lies; the LORD detests the violent and deceitful person.

7 As for me, I will enter your house because of your abundant mercy. With reverence and awe, I will bow down in worship toward your holy temple.

8 Guide me, O LORD, in your righteousness because of those who oppose me; make your path clear and direct before me.

9 For there is no truthfulness in what they say; their inner being is filled with corruption; their words are like an open grave, destructive and deceitful; they speak with flattering tongues.

10 O God, bring them to ruin; let them fall by their own schemes. Drive them away because of their many sins, for they have rebelled against you.

11 But let all who place their trust in you be glad; let them continually shout for joy, because you protect them. May those who love your name rejoice in you.

12 For you, LORD, will bless the righteous; with your favor you will surround them like a shield.
