Philippians Chapter 4

1 Therefore, my beloved brothers and sisters, whom I deeply love and long to see, my joy and my crown, stand firm in the Lord, my dearly loved ones.

2 I urge Euodia and I urge Syntyche to be in harmony with one another in the Lord.

3 And I urge you, my faithful companion, to assist those women who have worked alongside me in spreading the gospel, as well as Clement and the rest of my fellow workers, whose names are recorded in the book of life.

4 Always find your joy in the Lord; I repeat, find your joy in Him.

5 Let everyone see your gentleness and self-control. The Lord is near.

6 Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, through prayer and earnest pleading with gratitude, present your requests to God.

7 And the peace of God, which surpasses all comprehension, will guard your hearts and minds through Christ Jesus.

8 Finally, my fellow believers, focus your thoughts on whatever is true, whatever is honorable, whatever is just, whatever is pure, whatever is lovely, and whatever is commendable. If there is anything virtuous or praiseworthy, let these things occupy your mind.

9 Put into practice the things you have learned from me, accepted as true, heard me teach, and observed in my life. If you do this, the God of peace will be with you.

10 But I am filled with great joy in the Lord because your concern for me has finally been renewed. You were indeed concerned before, but you lacked the opportunity to show it.

11 I am not saying this because I am in need, for I have learned to be content in whatever circumstances I find myself.

12 I have learned how to live in humble circumstances, and I have learned how to live in abundance. In every situation and in all circumstances, I have been taught the secret of being contentâ€”whether well-fed or hungry, whether living in plenty or in need.

13 I am able to accomplish all things through Christ, who gives me strength.

14 You have done well by sharing in my suffering and supporting me in my time of trouble.

15 Now, you Philippians are also aware that at the start of the gospel, when I left Macedonia, no church shared with me in the matter of giving and receiving except you alone.

16 Even while I was in Thessalonica, you sent help to meet my needs more than once.

17 I am not saying this because I seek a gift, but because I want to see the spiritual benefit that will grow and be credited to your account.

18 I have everything I need, and more than enough. I am fully satisfied, having received from Epaphroditus the gifts you sent. They are like a fragrant offering, a sacrifice that is pleasing and acceptable to God.

19 But my God will meet all your needs according to the abundance of his glorious riches through Christ Jesus.

20 Now to our God and Father be glory forever and ever. Amen.

21 Greet every holy one who belongs to Christ Jesus. The brothers and sisters who are with me send their greetings to you.

22 All the believers send you their greetings, especially those who belong to Caesar's household.

23 May the gracious favor of our Lord Jesus Christ be with all of you. Amen.
