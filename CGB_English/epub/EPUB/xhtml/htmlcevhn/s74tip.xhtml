Luke Chapter 3

1 In the fifteenth year of the rule of Tiberius Caesar, when Pontius Pilate was governing Judea, Herod was ruling as tetrarch of Galilee, his brother Philip was tetrarch of Ituraea and the region of Trachonitis, and Lysanias was tetrarch of Abilene,

2 During the time when Annas and Caiaphas served as high priests, the word of God came to John, the son of Zacharias, while he was in the wilderness.

3 He traveled throughout the entire region around the Jordan River, proclaiming a baptism of repentance for the forgiveness of sins.

4 As it is written in the book of the words of Isaiah the prophet, saying: "The voice of one calling out in the wilderness, 'Prepare the way of the Lord; make His paths straight.'"

5 Every valley will be raised up, and every mountain and hill will be leveled; the winding paths will be straightened, and the rugged roads will be made smooth.

6 And all humanity will witness the deliverance brought by God.

7 Then he said to the crowd that had come out to be baptized by him, "You brood of vipers! Who warned you to escape from the coming wrath?"

8 Therefore, produce fruit that demonstrates genuine repentance, and do not start saying to yourselves, ‘We have Abraham as our father.’ For I tell you that God is able to raise up children for Abraham from these stones.

9 And now the axe is positioned at the root of the trees. Therefore, every tree that does not produce good fruit will be cut down and thrown into the fire.

10 The crowd then asked him, saying, "What should we do?"

11 He responded and said to them, "Whoever has two coats should share with the one who has none, and whoever has food should do the same."

12 Then tax collectors also came to be baptized and said to him, "Teacher, what should we do?"

13 And he said to them, "Do not demand more than what has been assigned to you."

14 The soldiers also asked him, saying, "What should we do?" He replied to them, "Do not mistreat or falsely accuse anyone, and be satisfied with your pay."

15 As the people were filled with anticipation and everyone was pondering in their hearts about John—wondering whether he might be the Messiah—

16 John responded, addressing everyone, "I baptize you with water, but someone far greater than I is coming. I am not even worthy to untie the straps of his sandals. He will baptize you with the Holy Spirit and with fire."

17 He holds a winnowing fork in his hand, and he will thoroughly clear his threshing floor. He will gather the wheat into his barn, but the chaff he will burn with fire that cannot be extinguished.

18 In his exhortation, he proclaimed many other teachings to the people.

19 But Herod the tetrarch, having been rebuked by him concerning Herodias, his brother Philip’s wife, and for all the wicked deeds Herod had committed,

20 In addition to all this, he also imprisoned John.

21 When all the people had been baptized, it happened that Jesus was also baptized. As He was praying, the heavens opened.

22 And the Holy Spirit came down in a visible form, resembling a dove, and rested upon him. At the same time, a voice spoke from heaven, saying, "You are my beloved Son; in you I take great delight."

23 And Jesus himself was about thirty years old when he began his ministry. He was regarded as the son of Joseph, who was the son of Heli.

24 He was the son of Matthat, who was the son of Levi, who was the son of Melchi, who was the son of Janna, who was the son of Joseph.

25 This was the son of Mattathias, who was the son of Amos, who was the son of Naum, who was the son of Esli, who was the son of Nagge.

26 who was the son of Maath, who was the son of Mattathias, who was the son of Semei, who was the son of Joseph, who was the son of Judah.

27 This was the son of Joanna, who was the son of Rhesa, who was the son of Zerubbabel, who was the son of Shealtiel, who was the son of Neri.

28 He was the son of Melchi, who was the son of Addi, who was the son of Cosam, who was the son of Elmodam, who was the son of Er.

29 who was the son of Jose, who was the son of Eliezer, who was the son of Jorim, who was the son of Matthat, who was the son of Levi.

30 who was the son of Simeon, who was the son of Judah, who was the son of Joseph, who was the son of Jonan, who was the son of Eliakim.

31 He was the son of Melea, who was the son of Menan, who was the son of Mattatha, who was the son of Nathan, who was the son of David.

32 who was the son of Jesse, who was the son of Obed, who was the son of Boaz, who was the son of Salmon, who was the son of Nahshon.

33 He was the son of Aminadab, who was the son of Aram, who was the son of Hezron, who was the son of Perez, who was the son of Judah.

34 who was the son of Jacob, who was the son of Isaac, who was the son of Abraham, who was the son of Terah, who was the son of Nahor.

35 He was the son of Serug, who was the son of Reu, who was the son of Peleg, who was the son of Eber, who was the son of Shelah.

36 who was the son of Cainan, who was the son of Arphaxad, who was the son of Shem, who was the son of Noah, who was the son of Lamech.

37 He was the son of Methuselah, who was the son of Enoch, who was the son of Jared, who was the son of Mahalalel, who was the son of Kenan.

38 who was the son of Enos, who was the son of Seth, who was the son of Adam, who was the son of God.
