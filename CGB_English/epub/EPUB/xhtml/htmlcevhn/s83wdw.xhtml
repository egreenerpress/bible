II Kings Chapter 8

1 Then Elisha spoke to the woman whose son he had brought back to life, saying, "Get up, take your household, and go live wherever you can find a place to stay, because the LORD has decreed a famine, and it will come upon the land for seven years."

2 The woman got up and followed the instructions of the man of God. She left with her household and lived in the land of the Philistines for seven years.

3 At the end of the seven years, the woman returned from the land of the Philistines. She went to appeal to the king for the return of her house and her land.

4 The king spoke with Gehazi, the servant of the man of God, and said, "Please tell me about all the remarkable deeds that Elisha has done."

5 As he was recounting to the king how he had brought a dead person back to life, suddenly the woman whose son had been restored to life came and appealed to the king concerning her house and her land. Gehazi said, "My lord the king, this is the woman, and this is her son, whom Elisha brought back to life."

6 When the king questioned the woman, she explained the situation to him. The king then assigned a specific official to her case, instructing him, "Return everything that belongs to her, including all the produce from her fields from the time she left the land up to the present day."

7 Elisha arrived in Damascus, and at that time, Ben-hadad, the king of Syria, was ill. Someone informed the king, saying, "The man of God has come here."

8 The king instructed Hazael, "Take a gift with you, go and meet the man of God, and seek the LORD's guidance through him. Ask him, 'Will I recover from this illness?'"

9 So Hazael went to meet him, bringing with him a gift consisting of the finest goods from Damascus, carried by forty camels. He came and stood before Elisha and said, "Your son Ben-hadad, king of Syria, has sent me to you, asking, 'Will I recover from this illness?'"

10 Elisha said to him, "Go and tell him, 'You will certainly recover.' However, the LORD has revealed to me that he will indeed die."

11 And he fixed his gaze intently until the man became embarrassed. Then the man of God began to weep.

12 Hazael asked, "Why is my lord weeping?" Elisha replied, "Because I know the harm you will bring upon the people of Israel: you will set their fortified cities on fire, kill their young men with the sword, dash their little ones to pieces, and rip open their pregnant women."

13 Hazael replied, "But how could I, your servant, a mere insignificant person, do such a great thing?" Elisha answered, "The LORD has revealed to me that you will become king over Syria."

14 So Naaman left Elisha and returned to his master. The king asked him, "What did Elisha say to you?" Naaman replied, "He told me that you would certainly recover."

15 The next day, he took a thick cloth, soaked it in water, and laid it over his face, causing him to die. Then Hazael took his place and became king.

16 In the fifth year of Joram, the son of Ahab king of Israel, while Jehoshaphat was still king of Judah, Jehoram, the son of Jehoshaphat king of Judah, began his reign.

17 He was thirty-two years old when he became king, and he ruled for eight years in Jerusalem.

18 He followed the ways of the kings of Israel, just as the house of Ahab had done, because he had married Ahab’s daughter. He committed evil in the eyes of the LORD.

19 However, the LORD chose not to destroy Judah for the sake of David, His servant, just as He had promised to give him and his descendants a lasting dynasty.

20 During his reign, Edom rebelled against Judah's control and established their own king to rule over them.

21 So Joram marched to Zair, accompanied by all his chariots. During the night, he launched an attack against the Edomites who had surrounded him and their chariot commanders. The Edomite forces were defeated, and the survivors fled back to their tents.

22 However, Edom rebelled against Judah's authority, and they remain independent to this day. At the same time, Libnah also rose up in rebellion.

23 The remaining deeds of Joram and everything he accomplished—are they not recorded in the book of the annals of the kings of Judah?

24 Joram died and was buried with his ancestors in the City of David. His son Ahaziah succeeded him as king.

25 In the twelfth year of Joram, the son of Ahab, king of Israel, Ahaziah, the son of Jehoram, king of Judah, began to rule.

26 Ahaziah was twenty-two years old when he became king, and he ruled for one year in Jerusalem. His mother’s name was Athaliah, the daughter of Omri, king of Israel.

27 He followed the ways of the house of Ahab and committed evil in the sight of the LORD, just as the house of Ahab had done, for he was the son-in-law of Ahab's family.

28 He accompanied Joram, the son of Ahab, to wage war against Hazael, the king of Syria, at Ramoth-gilead. During the battle, the Syrians inflicted wounds on Joram.

29 King Joram returned to Jezreel to recover from the wounds inflicted on him by the Arameans at Ramah during his battle against Hazael, the king of Aram. Meanwhile, Ahaziah, the son of Jehoram king of Judah, went down to Jezreel to visit Joram, the son of Ahab, because he was ill.
