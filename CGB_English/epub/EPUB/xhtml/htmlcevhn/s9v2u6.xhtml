Numbers Chapter 13

1 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2 Send out men to explore the land of Canaan, which I am giving to the Israelites. From each of their ancestral tribes, you are to send one man, each one a leader among them.

3 Moses, acting in obedience to the LORD's command, sent them out from the wilderness of Paran. All these men were leaders among the people of Israel.

4 Here are their names: from the tribe of Reuben, Shammua son of Zaccur.

5 From the tribe of Simeon, Shaphat son of Hori.

6 From the tribe of Judah, Caleb son of Jephunneh.

7 From the tribe of Issachar, Igal, the son of Joseph.

8 From the tribe of Ephraim, Hoshea the son of Nun.

9 From the tribe of Benjamin, Palti son of Raphu.

10 From the tribe of Zebulun, Gaddiel son of Sodi.

11 From the tribe of Joseph, specifically from the tribe of Manasseh, Gaddi son of Susi.

12 From the tribe of Dan, Ammiel son of Gemalli.

13 From the tribe of Asher, Sethur son of Michael.

14 From the tribe of Naphtali, Nahbi son of Vophsi.

15 From the tribe of Gad, Geuel son of Machi.

16 Here are the names of the men whom Moses sent to explore the land. Moses gave Hoshea, the son of Nun, the name Joshua.

17 Moses sent them to explore the land of Canaan, instructing them, "Go up this way toward the south and make your way into the hill country."

18 Go and observe the land to see what it is like, and examine the people who live there—whether they are strong or weak, few or many.

19 And what kind of land they live in—whether it is good or bad—and what kind of cities they inhabit, whether they live in tents or in fortified settlements.

20 Assess the quality of the land—whether it is fertile or barren, and whether it has trees or not. Be courageous and bring back some of the fruit of the land. It was the season for the first ripe grapes at that time.

21 So they went up and explored the land from the Wilderness of Zin all the way to Rehob, near the entrance to Hamath.

22 They traveled up through the southern region and arrived at Hebron, where Ahiman, Sheshai, and Talmai, the descendants of Anak, lived. (It is worth noting that Hebron had been established seven years before Zoan in Egypt.)

23 They went to the valley of Eshcol and cut down a branch with a single cluster of grapes from there. They carried it on a pole between two of them, along with some pomegranates and figs.

24 The place was named the Valley of Eshcol because of the cluster of grapes that the Israelites cut down from there.

25 After forty days, they came back from exploring the land.

26 They went and returned to Moses, Aaron, and the entire assembly of the Israelites in the wilderness of Paran, at Kadesh. They brought back a report to them and to the whole community, and they showed them the fruit of the land.

27 They reported to him, saying, "We went to the land where you sent us, and it truly is a land flowing with milk and honey. Here is some of its fruit as evidence."

28 However, the people who live in the land are powerful, and the cities are fortified and extremely large. What’s more, we saw the descendants of Anak there.

29 The Amalekites live in the southern region; the Hittites, Jebusites, and Amorites inhabit the hill country; and the Canaanites reside along the seacoast and near the banks of the Jordan River.

30 Caleb quieted the people in the presence of Moses and said, "Let us go up immediately and take possession of the land, for we are fully capable of conquering it."

31 However, the men who went up with him said, "We cannot attack these people, because they are stronger than we are."

32 They presented a negative report about the land they had explored to the Israelites, saying, "The land we traveled through to investigate is a land that consumes its inhabitants, and all the people we saw there are of enormous size."

33 And there we saw the Nephilim, the descendants of Anak, who were of great stature. In our own eyes, we seemed like grasshoppers, and that is how we appeared to them as well.
