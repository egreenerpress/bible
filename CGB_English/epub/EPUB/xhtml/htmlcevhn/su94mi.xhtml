II Thessalonians Chapter 3

1 Finally, brothers and sisters, pray for us, so that the message of the Lord may spread rapidly and be honored, just as it is among you.

2 And may we be rescued from unreasonable and evil people, for not everyone has faith.

3 But the Lord is faithful; he will strengthen you and protect you from the evil one.

4 We are confident in the Lord concerning you, that you are already doing and will continue to do the things we have instructed you.

5 May the Lord guide your hearts to fully embrace the love of God and to steadfastly endure as you await the return of Christ.

6 We instruct you, brothers and sisters, in the authority of our Lord Jesus Christ, to distance yourselves from any fellow believer who lives in a disorderly manner and does not follow the teachings we passed on to you.

7 You yourselves know how you should follow our example, for we did not act in a disorderly or irresponsible manner among you.

8 We did not accept anyoneâ€™s food without paying for it. Instead, we worked hard day and night with labor and toil so that we would not be a financial burden to any of you.

9 We are not doing this because we lack the authority, but in order to set an example for you to follow.

10 Even when we were with you, we gave you this instruction: if anyone is unwilling to work, they should not eat.

11 We have heard that there are some among you who are living in a disorderly manner, refusing to work and instead meddling in the affairs of others.

12 To those who are acting in such a manner, we instruct and urge in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ that they should work peacefully and provide for themselves by earning their own food.

13 But you, my fellow believers, must not grow tired of doing what is good and right.

14 If anyone does not comply with the instruction given in this letter, take note of that person and do not associate with them, so that they may feel the weight of their actions and be moved to change.

15 Do not regard him as an adversary, but correct and counsel him as a fellow believer.

16 May the Lord of peace himself grant you peace in every circumstance and in every way. May the Lord be present with all of you.

17 This greeting is written by my own hand, Paul, which serves as a distinguishing mark in all my letters. This is how I write.

18 May the gracious favor of our Lord Jesus Christ be with all of you. Amen.
