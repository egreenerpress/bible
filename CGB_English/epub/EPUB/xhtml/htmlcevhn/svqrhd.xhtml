Proverbs Chapter 20

1 Wine is a deceiver, and strong drink leads to turmoil; whoever is led astray by them lacks wisdom.

2 The dread inspired by a king is like the roar of a lion; whoever stirs him to anger endangers their own life.

3 It is honorable for a person to avoid conflict, but every fool is quick to get involved in arguments.

4 The lazy person refuses to plow because of the cold; as a result, they will beg during the harvest season and end up with nothing.

5 A person's thoughts and plans are like deep, hidden waters, but someone with insight and wisdom can bring them to the surface and understand them.

6 Most people are quick to declare their own integrity, but a truly trustworthy person—who can find one?

7 The righteous person lives with integrity, and their children are blessed because of them.

8 A king who sits on the throne of justice dispels all wickedness with his discerning gaze.

9 Who can claim, "I have cleansed my heart; I am free from my sin"?

10 Using different weights and different measures—both are equally detestable to the LORD.

11 Even a child reveals his character through his actions—whether his conduct is pure and whether it is upright.

12 The Lord has created both the ear that hears and the eye that sees—each of them.

13 Do not indulge in excessive sleep, or you will fall into poverty; stay alert and diligent, and you will have enough to sustain yourself.

14 The buyer says, "It’s worthless, it’s worthless," but once he leaves, he boasts about his bargain.

15 Gold and an abundance of rubies are valuable, but the words of wisdom are a priceless treasure.

16 Seize the garment of one who guarantees a loan for a stranger, and hold it as collateral for the debt incurred on behalf of a foreign woman.

17 Dishonestly gained food may seem pleasing to a person, but later it will turn to bitterness and regret.

18 Every plan is established through careful deliberation, and with wise guidance, engage in warfare.

19 A person who spreads gossip reveals confidential matters; therefore, do not associate with someone who speaks deceptively with their words.

20 Whoever curses their father or mother will have their light extinguished, plunging them into utter darkness.

21 An inheritance may be acquired quickly at the start, but its outcome will not bring blessing.

22 Do not say, "I will repay evil"; instead, wait for the LORD, and He will deliver you.

23 Using dishonest weights in trade is detestable to the LORD, and a deceitful scale is utterly wrong.

24 A person's steps are directed by the LORD; how then can anyone fully comprehend their own path?

25 It is a trap for a person to thoughtlessly consume what is sacred, and only afterward, when vows have been made, to reconsider or question their actions.

26 A wise king disperses the wicked and drives the threshing wheel over them.

27 The human spirit is the lamp of the LORD, illuminating the deepest recesses of the heart.

28 Kindness and faithfulness protect the king, and his throne is sustained by steadfast love.

29 The pride of young men lies in their vigor, and the dignity of the elderly is found in their gray hair.

30 The severity of a wound can purify and remove wrongdoing, just as physical discipline can cleanse the innermost depths of the heart.
