Ezekiel Chapter 33

1 Once more, the word of the LORD came to me, saying,

2 "Son of man, speak to your people and say to them: When I bring a sword against a land, and the people of that land choose a man from among them and appoint him as their watchman—"

3 If, when he sees the sword coming upon the land, he blows the trumpet and warns the people,

4 If anyone hears the sound of the trumpet but does not heed the warning, and the sword comes and takes them away, their blood will be on their own head.

5 He heard the blast of the trumpet but paid no attention; his blood will be on his own head. However, whoever heeds the warning will save their life.

6 But if the watchman sees the sword approaching and does not sound the trumpet to warn the people, and the sword comes and takes anyone from among them, that person will perish in their own iniquity. However, I will hold the watchman accountable for their blood.

7 Therefore, you, O mortal, I have appointed you as a watchman for the house of Israel. You must listen to the word that comes from my mouth and give them warning from me.

8 When I say to the wicked, "O wicked one, you will surely die," and you fail to warn the wicked to turn from their ways, that wicked person will die in their sin, but I will hold you accountable for their blood.

9 However, if you warn the wicked person to turn from their evil ways, and they refuse to turn from them, they will die in their sin; but you will have saved your own life.

10 Therefore, O mortal, speak to the house of Israel and say to them: This is what you are saying: "If our transgressions and sins weigh heavily upon us, and we are wasting away because of them, how can we possibly live?"

11 Tell them this: As surely as I live, declares the Lord GOD, I take no pleasure in the death of the wicked. Instead, I desire that the wicked turn from their ways and live. Turn back, turn back from your evil paths! Why should you die, O house of Israel?

12 Therefore, son of man, speak to your people and tell them: The righteous deeds of a righteous person will not save them on the day they sin. Likewise, the wickedness of a wicked person will not cause their downfall on the day they turn away from their wickedness. Nor will the righteous be able to rely on their past righteousness to live when they commit sin.

13 When I declare to the righteous that they will surely live, but then they rely on their own righteousness and commit sin, none of their righteous deeds will be remembered. Because of the sin they have committed, they will die for it.

14 Again, when I say to the wicked, "You will certainly die," but if they turn away from their sin and do what is just and right—

15 If the wicked person returns what was taken as a pledge, repays what was stolen, follows the life-giving statutes, and refrains from committing evil, they will certainly live and not die.

16 None of the sins he has committed will be held against him. He has done what is just and right; he will certainly live.

17 Yet the people of your nation say, "The Lord's way is not fair." But in truth, it is their own ways that are not fair.

18 When a righteous person turns away from their righteousness and commits wrongdoing, they will die as a result of it.

19 But if the wicked person turns away from their wickedness and does what is just and right, they will live because of it.

20 Yet you say, "The way of the Lord is not fair." O house of Israel, I will judge each of you according to your own ways.

21 In the twelfth year of our exile, on the tenth day of the tenth month, a survivor from Jerusalem came to me and said, "The city has been captured."

22 In the evening, before the survivor arrived, the hand of the LORD came upon me and opened my mouth. This continued until he came to me the next morning. At that time, my mouth was opened, and I was no longer unable to speak.

23 Then the word of the LORD came to me, saying,

24 "Son of man, the people living in the desolate places of the land of Israel are saying, 'Abraham was just one man, yet he took possession of the land. But we are many; surely the land has been given to us as our inheritance.'"

25 Therefore, tell them this is what the Lord GOD says: You eat meat with the blood still in it, you lift your eyes toward your idols, and you shed blood. Do you really think you will possess the land?

26 You rely on your weapons, you commit detestable acts, and each of you defiles your neighbor’s wife. Do you really think you will inherit the land?

27 Tell them this: This is what the Lord GOD says: As surely as I live, those who are in the desolate places will fall by the sword, and I will hand over those in the open fields to be devoured by wild animals. Those who are in the strongholds and caves will die from plague.

28 For I will make the land utterly desolate, and the pride of its power will come to an end. The mountains of Israel will be left barren, so that no one will travel through them.

29 Then they will understand that I am the LORD, when I have made the land utterly desolate because of all the detestable acts they have committed.

30 Furthermore, son of man, the people of your nation continue to speak against you near the walls and at the doorways of their houses. They converse with one another, each saying to their fellow, "Please come and listen to the message that comes from the LORD."

31 They come to you as people come, and they sit before you as if they are my people, and they listen to your words, but they do not act on them. Though their mouths speak of great devotion, their hearts are driven by their selfish desires.

32 Behold, you are to them like a beautiful love song, sung by someone with a charming voice and skilled in playing an instrument. They listen to your words, but they do not act on them.

33 When this event takes place—and indeed, it will happen—they will realize that a prophet has been present among them.
