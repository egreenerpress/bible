Psalms Chapter 52

1 (For the director of music. A contemplative psalm of David, written when Doeg the Edomite went to Saul and told him, "David has come to the house of Ahimelech.") Why do you take pride in your wickedness, O powerful one? The steadfast love of God endures forever.

2 Your tongue plans wicked schemes; like a sharp razor, it crafts deceit.

3 You prefer evil over good and deceit over speaking truthfully. Selah.

4 You delight in destructive words, O deceitful tongue.

5 God will utterly destroy you forever; He will remove you, tear you from your dwelling place, and uproot you from the land of the living. Selah.

6 The righteous will witness this and be filled with awe, yet they will also laugh at him in derision.

7 Behold, this is the person who did not make God his refuge; instead, he relied on his great wealth and found security in his own wrongdoing.

8 But I am like a flourishing olive tree in the house of God; I place my trust in the steadfast love of God forever and always.

9 I will praise you forever, for you have accomplished this; and I will trust in your name, for it is good in the presence of your faithful ones.
