Exodus Chapter 28

1 Bring your brother Aaron and his sons—Nadab, Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar—from among the Israelites to serve me as priests. They are to fulfill the duties of the priesthood, representing the people before me.

2 You are to make sacred garments for your brother Aaron, for honor and splendor.

3 You are to speak to all those who are skilled and wise, whom I have endowed with a spirit of wisdom, so that they may make Aaron’s garments to set him apart for consecration, enabling him to serve me in the role of priest.

4 These are the sacred garments they are to make: a breastpiece, an ephod, a robe, an embroidered tunic, a turban, and a sash. They shall create these holy garments for your brother Aaron and his sons, so that they may serve me as priests.

5 They shall gather gold, along with blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, and fine linen.

6 They shall craft the ephod using gold, along with blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, and finely twisted linen, skillfully woven together.

7 The garment shall have its two shoulder pieces attached at their edges, and in this way, it shall be fastened together.

8 The intricately woven band of the ephod, which is attached to it, must be made of the same materials and craftsmanship: gold, blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, along with finely twisted linen.

9 You shall take two onyx stones and engrave on them the names of the sons of Israel.

10 Engrave six of their names on one stone, and the remaining six names on the other stone, arranged in the order of their birth.

11 You shall engrave the two stones with the names of the sons of Israel, as a skilled engraver carves a seal. You shall set them in gold filigree settings.

12 You shall place the two stones on the shoulder pieces of the ephod as memorial stones for the Israelites. Aaron will carry their names before the LORD on his two shoulders as a reminder.

13 You shall make settings of gold.

14 And you shall make two chains of pure gold, crafted in a twisted, cord-like design. Attach these twisted chains to the settings.

15 You shall craft the breastplate of judgment with skillful artistry, following the same design as the ephod. Make it using gold, along with blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, and finely woven linen.

16 The object shall be square and folded double; its length shall be one span, and its width shall also be one span.

17 You shall arrange within it mountings of stones, in four rows: the first row shall be a carnelian, a topaz, and an emerald—this is the first row.

18 The second row shall consist of an emerald, a sapphire, and a diamond.

19 The third row shall have a ligure, an agate, and an amethyst.

20 The fourth row shall have a beryl, an onyx, and a jasper. These stones are to be mounted in gold settings.

21 The stones shall bear the names of the twelve tribes of Israel, each one engraved like a seal with the name of a tribe. There will be twelve stones in total, each corresponding to one of the tribes, with their names inscribed upon them.

22 You shall attach to the breastplate braided chains made of pure gold, crafted with skill and fastened securely at the ends.

23 You shall make two gold rings and attach them to the two ends of the breastplate.

24 You shall place the two braided chains of gold into the two rings that are on the edges of the breastplate.

25 You shall attach the other two ends of the two braided chains to the two settings and secure them to the shoulder pieces of the ephod at the front.

26 You shall make two rings of gold and attach them to the two ends of the breastpiece, on its edge that faces toward the inner side of the ephod.

27 You shall also make two more rings of gold and attach them to the two sides of the ephod, on its underside, near the front, opposite the other joining, above the skillfully woven band of the ephod.

28 They shall attach the breastpiece by its rings to the rings of the ephod using a cord of blue, so that it rests securely above the skillfully woven band of the ephod. This will ensure that the breastpiece remains firmly in place and does not become detached from the ephod.

29 Aaron shall carry the names of the Israelites on the breastpiece of judgment over his heart whenever he enters the holy place, as a continual reminder before the LORD.

30 You are to place the Urim and the Thummim inside the breastpiece of judgment, and they will rest over Aaron’s heart whenever he enters the presence of the LORD. In this way, Aaron will continually carry the responsibility for making decisions on behalf of the Israelites before the LORD, bearing their judgment upon his heart.

31 You shall make the robe of the ephod entirely of blue cloth.

32 There shall be an opening at the top of it, in the center, with a woven edge surrounding the opening, like the collar of a coat of mail, so that it does not tear.

33 And along the lower edge of the robe, you shall make pomegranates of blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, all around the hem. Between these pomegranates, you shall place golden bells, spaced evenly around the entire hem.

34 A golden bell and a pomegranate, alternating with another golden bell and a pomegranate, were to be placed all around the hem of the robe.

35 Aaron shall wear it when he performs his priestly duties, and its sound will be heard when he enters the holy place before the LORD and when he leaves, so that he does not die.

36 You shall make a plate of pure gold and engrave on it, as one engraves a seal, the words: "HOLINESS TO THE LORD."

37 You shall attach it to a blue cord, so that it may be fastened to the turban; it is to be placed on the front of the turban.

38 The plate shall be placed on Aaron’s forehead, so that Aaron may bear the guilt associated with the sacred offerings that the Israelites consecrate in all their holy gifts. It shall remain on his forehead continually, so that they may be accepted before the LORD.

39 You shall skillfully craft the tunic from fine linen, and you shall fashion the turban from fine linen as well. Additionally, you shall make the sash with intricate embroidery.

40 For Aaron's sons, you shall make tunics, and you shall also make sashes for them, and you shall fashion headpieces for them, for dignity and splendor.

41 You shall place these garments on Aaron, your brother, and on his sons as well. Then you shall anoint them, set them apart, and consecrate them, so that they may serve me in the role of priests.

42 You shall make linen undergarments for them to cover their bare skin; these garments shall extend from the waist down to the thighs.

43 These garments shall be worn by Aaron and his sons whenever they enter the tent of meeting or approach the altar to serve in the holy place, so that they do not incur guilt and die. This is to be a permanent regulation for him and his descendants after him.
