Job Chapter 5

1 Call out now, if there is anyone who will respond to you; to which of the holy ones will you turn for help?

2 For anger destroys the foolish person, and jealousy brings ruin to the naive.

3 I have observed the foolish prospering and establishing themselves, but in an instant, I pronounced judgment on their dwelling place.

4 His children are far from security, and they are overwhelmed at the city gate, with no one to rescue them.

5 The hungry devour their harvest, even snatching it from among the thorns, and the thirsty swallow up their wealth.

6 Though suffering does not arise from the earth, nor does trouble sprout from the ground;

7 Yet humanity is destined for hardship, just as surely as sparks naturally rise upward.

8 I would turn to God and entrust my case to Him.

9 He does great and incomprehensible things, marvelous works beyond counting.

10 Who sends rain upon the earth and causes water to flow over the fields:

11 To lift up those who are brought low; so that those who grieve may be raised to a place of security.

12 He frustrates the schemes of the cunning, so that their hands cannot carry out their plans.

13 He catches the clever in their own schemes, and the plans of the deceitful are swiftly brought to ruin.

14 They encounter darkness even in broad daylight and stumble around at noon as if it were night.

15 But He rescues the needy from the sword, from their cries of distress, and from the grasp of the powerful.

16 Therefore, the poor have hope, and injustice is silenced.

17 Consider this: Blessed is the one whom God disciplines. Therefore, do not reject the correction of the Almighty.

18 For He causes pain, but He also heals; He inflicts wounds, but His hands bring restoration.

19 He will rescue you in six troubles; indeed, in seven, no harm will come near you.

20 In times of famine, He will rescue you from death, and in the midst of war, He will deliver you from the threat of the sword.

21 You will be protected from the harm caused by malicious words, and you will not fear devastation when it comes.

22 You will laugh at destruction and famine, and you will not fear the wild animals of the earth.

23 For you will have a covenant with the stones of the field, and the wild animals will live in peace with you.

24 You will come to understand that your dwelling will be secure, and when you inspect your home, you will find it in peace, and you will not fall into wrongdoing.

25 You will also come to understand that your descendants will be numerous, and your offspring will be as abundant as the grass covering the earth.

26 You will come to your grave in a ripe old age, just as a sheaf of grain is gathered in its proper season.

27 Behold, we have examined this thoroughly, and it is indeed true. Listen to it, and take it to heart for your own benefit.
