Matthew Chapter 14

1 At that time, Herod the tetrarch heard about the reputation of Jesus.

2 And he said to his servants, "This is John the Baptist; he has risen from the dead, and that is why miraculous powers are at work in him."

3 For Herod had seized John, bound him, and imprisoned him because of Herodias, the wife of his brother Philip.

4 For John had said to him, "It is against the law for you to have her."

5 When he intended to have him executed, he was afraid of the crowd, because they regarded him as a prophet.

6 When Herod celebrated his birthday, the daughter of Herodias performed a dance in front of the guests, and her performance delighted Herod.

7 Therefore, he swore an oath to give her whatever she might request.

8 And she, having been previously instructed by her mother, said, "Give me here the head of John the Baptist on a platter."

9 The king was deeply troubled, but because of his oath and out of respect for those who were dining with him, he ordered that her request be granted.

10 He sent orders and had John beheaded in the prison.

11 His head was brought on a platter and given to the young woman, who then took it to her mother.

12 Then his disciples came, took the body, buried it, and went to inform Jesus of what had happened.

13 When Jesus heard about it, he left that place by boat to go to a secluded, deserted area. But when the people learned of his departure, they followed him on foot from the towns.

14 Jesus went out and saw a large crowd. He was deeply moved with compassion for them, and he healed those among them who were sick.

15 As evening approached, his disciples came to him and said, "This is a remote place, and the hour is already late. Send the crowds away so they can go to the nearby villages and buy food for themselves."

16 But Jesus said to them, "They do not need to leave; you give them something to eat."

17 They replied to him, "We have only five loaves of bread and two fish here."

18 He said, "Bring them here to me."

19 Then Jesus instructed the crowd to sit down on the grass. He took the five loaves of bread and the two fish, looked up toward heaven, and gave thanks. After breaking the loaves, he handed them to his disciples, who then distributed the bread to the people.

20 And they all ate and were satisfied. Then they gathered up the leftover pieces, filling twelve baskets.

21 The number of those who ate was approximately five thousand men, not including the women and children.

22 Immediately, Jesus urged his disciples to board a boat and go ahead of him to the other side of the lake, while he dismissed the crowds.

23 After dismissing the crowds, Jesus went up on a mountainside by himself to pray. When evening came, he was still there alone.

24 The ship was now far out at sea, being battered by the waves because the wind was blowing against it.

25 And during the final watch of the night, Jesus came to them, walking on the water.

26 When the disciples saw Jesus walking on the water, they were terrified and exclaimed, “It’s a ghost!” And they cried out in fear.

27 Immediately Jesus spoke to them, saying, "Take courage; it is I. Do not be afraid."

28 Peter responded to Him, saying, "Lord, if it is truly You, command me to come to You on the water."

29 And Jesus said, "Come." Then Peter stepped out of the boat and walked on the water, making his way toward Jesus.

30 But when he noticed how strong the wind was, he became frightened, and as he began to sink, he cried out, “Lord, save me!”

31 At once, Jesus reached out his hand, took hold of him, and said, "You who have such small faith, why did you let doubt take over?"

32 When they got into the boat, the wind stopped.

33 Then those who were in the boat approached him and bowed down in reverence, declaring, "Truly, you are the Son of God."

34 After they had crossed over, they arrived in the region of Gennesaret.

35 When the people of that place recognized him, they sent word throughout the surrounding region and brought to him all who were sick.

36 They earnestly pleaded with him, asking simply to touch the edge of his clothing. And all who touched it were completely healed.
