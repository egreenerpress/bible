I Samuel Chapter 13

1 Saul had been king for one year, and after he had ruled over Israel for two years,

2 Saul selected three thousand men from Israel: two thousand remained with him at Michmash and in the hill country of Bethel, while one thousand were under Jonathan’s command in Gibeah of Benjamin. The rest of the people Saul sent back, each to their own home.

3 Jonathan attacked and defeated the Philistine garrison stationed at Geba, and news of this reached the Philistines. In response, Saul had the trumpet sounded throughout the entire land, proclaiming, "Let all the Hebrews hear of this!"

4 The entire nation of Israel heard the news that Saul had attacked and defeated a Philistine garrison, and as a result, the Philistines now regarded Israel with intense hatred. In response, the people rallied behind Saul and gathered together at Gilgal.

5 The Philistines assembled their forces to wage war against Israel, bringing with them thirty thousand chariots, six thousand horsemen, and an army as numerous as the grains of sand on the seashore. They advanced and set up their camp at Michmash, to the east of Beth-aven.

6 When the men of Israel realized they were in a desperate situation—for the people were deeply troubled—they hid themselves in caves, thickets, rocky crevices, high places, and pits.

7 Some of the Hebrews crossed over the Jordan River into the territory of Gad and Gilead. Meanwhile, Saul remained in Gilgal, and all the people who were with him were filled with fear and anxiety.

8 Saul waited seven days, as Samuel had instructed, but Samuel did not arrive at Gilgal. As a result, the people began to disperse and leave Saul’s side.

9 Saul then said, "Bring me the burnt offering and the fellowship offerings." So he presented the burnt offering.

10 As soon as Saul finished presenting the burnt offering, Samuel arrived. Saul went out to greet him, intending to pay his respects.

11 Samuel asked, "What have you done?" Saul replied, "I saw that the people were abandoning me, and you did not come within the appointed time, while the Philistines were assembling at Michmash."

12 Therefore, I thought to myself, "The Philistines will surely come down against me at Gilgal, and I have not yet sought the LORD's favor." So, feeling compelled, I took it upon myself to offer a burnt offering.

13 Samuel said to Saul, "You have acted foolishly. You have not kept the command of the LORD your God, which He gave you. If you had obeyed, the LORD would have established your kingdom over Israel forever."

14 But now your kingdom will not endure. The LORD has sought out a man who aligns with His own heart, and the LORD has appointed him to be leader over His people, because you have not obeyed what the LORD commanded you.

15 Samuel then got up and went from Gilgal to Gibeah in the territory of Benjamin. Saul counted the men who were with him, and they numbered about six hundred.

16 Saul, along with his son Jonathan and the people who were with them, remained in Gibeah, which is in the territory of Benjamin. Meanwhile, the Philistines had set up their camp at Michmash.

17 Then raiding parties came out from the Philistine camp, dividing into three groups. One group turned toward the road leading to Ophrah, in the direction of the land of Shual.

18 Another group took the road toward Beth-horon, while a different group headed in the direction of the border that overlooks the valley of Zeboim, facing the wilderness.

19 At that time, there were no blacksmiths to be found anywhere in the land of Israel, because the Philistines had declared, "We must prevent the Hebrews from making swords or spears."

20 However, all the Israelites would go down to the Philistines to have each man’s plowshare, his mattock, his axe, and his sickle sharpened.

21 Yet they had a file for sharpening their hoes, plowshares, pitchforks, axes, and goads.

22 So it happened that on the day of battle, no sword or spear could be found in the hands of any of the people who were with Saul and Jonathan. However, Saul and his son Jonathan were the only ones who had them.

23 The Philistine military outpost advanced to the pass at Michmash.
