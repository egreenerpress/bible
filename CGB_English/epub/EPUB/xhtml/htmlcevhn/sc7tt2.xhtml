Judges Chapter 10

1 After Abimelech, Tola son of Puah, the son of Dodo, rose up to deliver Israel. He was from the tribe of Issachar and lived in Shamir, in the hill country of Ephraim.

2 He led Israel as judge for twenty-three years, then he died and was buried in Shamir.

3 After him, Jair from Gilead rose to lead and judge Israel for twenty-two years.

4 He had thirty sons, each of whom rode on a young donkey. They controlled thirty towns, which are still known as Havoth-jair to this day, located in the region of Gilead.

5 Jair died and was buried in Camon.

6 The Israelites once again committed evil in the eyes of the LORD. They worshiped the Baals and the Ashtoreths, as well as the gods of Syria, the gods of Sidon, the gods of Moab, the gods of the Ammonites, and the gods of the Philistines. They abandoned the LORD and refused to serve Him.

7 The LORD’s anger burned fiercely against Israel, and He handed them over to the power of the Philistines and the Ammonites.

8 That year, they harassed and oppressed the Israelites for eighteen years—all the Israelites who lived on the east side of the Jordan River in the land of the Amorites, in the region of Gilead.

9 Furthermore, the Ammonites crossed the Jordan River to wage war against Judah, Benjamin, and the house of Ephraim, causing Israel to be in great distress.

10 The Israelites cried out to the LORD, saying, "We have sinned against you, for we have abandoned our God and have worshiped the Baals."

11 The LORD said to the Israelites, "Did I not rescue you from the Egyptians, the Amorites, the Ammonites, and the Philistines?"

12 The Sidonians, the Amalekites, and the Maonites also oppressed you, and you cried out to me. So I rescued you from their power.

13 You have abandoned me and worshiped other gods; therefore, I will no longer rescue you.

14 Go and cry out to the gods you have chosen for yourselves; let them save you in your time of trouble.

15 The Israelites said to the LORD, "We have sinned. Do to us whatever you deem right, but we beg you, save us today."

16 They removed the foreign gods from their midst and devoted themselves to serving the LORD. And His heart was deeply troubled over the suffering of Israel.

17 Then the Ammonites gathered their forces and set up camp in Gilead. In response, the Israelites assembled and made their camp at Mizpah.

18 The people and leaders of Gilead said to one another, "Who is the man who will lead the fight against the Ammonites? He will become the ruler over all the inhabitants of Gilead."
