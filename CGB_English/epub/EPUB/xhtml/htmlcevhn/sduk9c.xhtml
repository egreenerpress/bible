Matthew Chapter 19

1 When Jesus had finished saying these things, he left Galilee and traveled to the region of Judea on the other side of the Jordan River.

2 And large crowds followed him, and he healed them there.

3 The Pharisees also approached Him, testing Him, and asked, "Is it permissible for a man to divorce his wife for any reason at all?"

4 And he responded to them, saying, "Have you not read that the one who created them from the beginning made them male and female?"

5 And he said, "For this reason, a man will leave his father and mother and be united to his wife, and the two will become one flesh."

6 For this reason, they are no longer two separate individuals, but one united entity. Therefore, what God has joined together, no human should separate.

7 They asked him, "Why then did Moses command that a man give his wife a certificate of divorce and send her away?"

8 He said to them, "Moses permitted you to divorce your wives because of the stubbornness of your hearts, but from the beginning, it was not meant to be this way."

9 I tell you this: Anyone who divorces his wife, unless it is due to sexual immorality, and marries another woman commits adultery. Likewise, whoever marries a woman who has been divorced also commits adultery.

10 His disciples said to him, "If this is the situation between a man and his wife, it would be better not to marry."

11 But Jesus said to them, "Not everyone is able to accept this teaching, except those to whom it has been granted."

12 For there are some who are eunuchs from birth, having been born that way from their mother’s womb. There are others who have been made eunuchs by human intervention. And there are those who have chosen to live as eunuchs for the sake of the kingdom of heaven. Whoever is able to accept this, let them accept it.

13 Then some people brought young children to him, asking that he lay his hands on them and pray for them. But the disciples scolded those who brought them.

14 But Jesus said, "Allow the little children to come to me, and do not hinder them, for the kingdom of heaven belongs to such as these."

15 He placed his hands on them and then left that place.

16 Suddenly, a man approached Him and said, "Good Teacher, what good deed must I do to gain eternal life?"

17 And Jesus said to him, "Why do you call me good? No one is truly good except God alone. But if you desire to enter into eternal life, keep the commandments."

18 He asked Him, "Which ones?" Jesus replied, "You shall not murder, you shall not commit adultery, you shall not steal, you shall not give false testimony."

19 Respect and honor your father and mother, and love your neighbor as you love yourself.

20 The young man said to him, "I have kept all these commandments since I was young. What am I still lacking?"

21 Jesus said to him, "If you desire to be complete in your devotion, go and sell all that you possess, and give the proceeds to the poor. Then you will have lasting treasure in heaven. After that, come and follow me."

22 But when the young man heard this, he went away deeply grieved, because he owned many possessions.

23 Then Jesus said to his disciples, "Truly, I tell you, it is difficult for a rich person to enter the kingdom of heaven."

24 And I say to you again: It is easier for a camel to pass through the eye of a needle than for a wealthy person to enter the kingdom of God.

25 When His disciples heard this, they were utterly astonished and said, "Then who can possibly be saved?"

26 But Jesus looked at them and said, "For people, this is impossible, but with God, everything is possible."

27 Then Peter responded to Him, saying, "Look, we have left everything behind and followed You. What will we receive in return?"

28 And Jesus said to them, “Truly I tell you, those of you who have followed me will, in the renewal of all things when the Son of Man sits on his glorious throne, also sit on twelve thrones, judging the twelve tribes of Israel.”

29 And anyone who has left behind houses, or brothers, or sisters, or father, or mother, or wife, or children, or lands, for the sake of my name, will receive a hundred times as much and will inherit eternal life.

30 However, many who are now considered first in importance or status will end up being last, and those who are now regarded as last will become first.
