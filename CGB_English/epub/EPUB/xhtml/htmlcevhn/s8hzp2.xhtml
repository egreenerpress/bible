Mark Chapter 3

1 Once more, Jesus entered the synagogue, and there was a man present whose hand was withered and useless.

2 They were closely observing him to see if he would heal the man on the Sabbath, so that they might have grounds to accuse him.

3 And He said to the man with the withered hand, "Come forward and stand here."

4 And he said to them, "Is it permitted to do good on the Sabbath, or to do harm? To save a life, or to destroy it?" But they remained silent.

5 Then, looking around at them with anger and deeply distressed by the hardness of their hearts, he said to the man, "Stretch out your hand." The man stretched it out, and his hand was completely restored, as healthy as the other.

6 The Pharisees went out and immediately began plotting with the Herodians against him, discussing how they might bring about his destruction.

7 But Jesus withdrew with his disciples to the sea, and a large crowd from Galilee followed him, along with many from Judea.

8 People came to him from Jerusalem, from Idumaea, from the region beyond the Jordan, and from the areas around Tyre and Sidon. When they heard about the remarkable things he was doing, a large crowd gathered to see him.

9 Jesus instructed his disciples to have a small boat ready for him because of the large crowd, so that the people would not press too closely against him.

10 For he had healed many people, so much so that those who were afflicted with diseases crowded around him, eager to touch him.

11 When the unclean spirits saw him, they fell down before him and cried out, saying, “You are the Son of God!”

12 And he sternly instructed them not to reveal his identity to anyone.

13 He went up on a mountainside and summoned those he wanted to join him, and they came to him.

14 He appointed twelve men to be his close companions, so that they could remain with him and he could send them out to proclaim his message.

15 And to have the authority to cure diseases and to drive out evil spirits.

16 And Simon, he gave the name Peter.

17 James, the son of Zebedee, and John, the brother of James, were given the name Boanerges by Jesus, which means "Sons of Thunder."

18 And Andrew, Philip, Bartholomew, Matthew, Thomas, James the son of Alphaeus, Thaddaeus, and Simon the Zealot.

19 And Judas Iscariot, who also betrayed Him, went with them into a house.

20 The crowd gathered once more, to such an extent that they could not even find time to eat.

21 When his family heard about what was happening, they went out to take control of him, because they were saying, "He has lost his senses."

22 The scribes who had come down from Jerusalem said, "He is possessed by Beelzebul, and it is by the power of the ruler of demons that he drives out demons."

23 He called them to himself and spoke to them using parables, saying, "How can Satan drive out Satan?"

24 If a kingdom is divided against itself, that kingdom cannot endure.

25 If a household is divided and in conflict with itself, that household will not be able to endure or remain stable.

26 If Satan were to rise up against himself and become divided, he could not endure; his power would collapse, and his reign would come to an end.

27 No one can enter a strong man’s house and plunder his possessions unless he first overpowers and restrains the strong man. Only then can he ransack the house.

28 Truly, I tell you, all sins and blasphemies, no matter how severe, will be forgiven for the children of humanity.

29 But whoever speaks against the Holy Spirit will never be forgiven; they are guilty of an eternal sin.

30 Because they were saying, "He is possessed by an unclean spirit."

31 Then his brothers and his mother arrived, and standing outside, they sent word to him, calling for him to come out.

32 A large crowd was gathered around Him, and they said to Him, "Look, Your mother and Your brothers are outside, asking for You."

33 And he responded to them, saying, "Who is my mother, or my brothers and sisters?"

34 He looked around at those who were seated around him and said, "Here are my mother and my brothers!"

35 Whoever carries out the will of God is my brother, my sister, and my mother.
