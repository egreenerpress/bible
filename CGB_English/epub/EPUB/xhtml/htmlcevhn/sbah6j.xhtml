Job Chapter 13

1 Behold, my eyes have witnessed all these things, and my ears have heard and comprehended them fully.

2 What you know, I also know; I am not less than you.

3 Certainly, I would address the Almighty; I long to present my case before God.

4 But you are fabricators of falsehood; you are all worthless healers.

5 If only you would remain completely silent! That would be your true wisdom.

6 Listen carefully to my argument, and pay close attention to the words I speak.

7 Would you speak unjustly on God's behalf? Or use deceitful words to defend him?

8 Will you show partiality to him? Will you argue on God's behalf?

9 Is it right for Him to examine you so thoroughly? Or can you mock Him as one person mocks another?

10 He will certainly rebuke you if you show partiality in secret.

11 Should not His majesty fill you with awe? And should not the fear of Him overwhelm you?

12 Your memories are as fleeting as ashes, and your bodies are as fragile as clay.

13 "Be silent and leave me alone, so that I may speak, and let whatever happens to me come to pass."

14 Why should I hold my life between my teeth and risk it with my own hands?

15 Even if he kills me, I will still trust in him; yet I will defend my ways before him.

16 He will also be my deliverance, for no hypocrite can stand in his presence.

17 Listen carefully to my words, and pay close attention to what I am about to say.

18 Listen carefully, I have prepared my case; I am confident that I will be proven right.

19 Who is there that will argue my case? For now, if I remain silent, I will perish.

20 "Do not do two things to me, and then I will not hide myself from you."

21 "Keep your hand far from me, and do not let your terrifying presence fill me with fear."

22 Then you call out, and I will respond; or let me speak, and you reply to me.

23 How many are my wrongdoings and sins? Reveal to me my rebellion and my sin.

24 Why do you hide your face from me and treat me as though I were your enemy?

25 Would you crush a leaf that is tossed by the wind? Would you chase after dry, brittle straw?

26 You record harsh accusations against me and make me bear the guilt of the sins I committed in my youth.

27 You place my feet in shackles and closely examine all my ways; you mark the very steps I take.

28 He decays like something rotten, like a garment consumed by moths.
