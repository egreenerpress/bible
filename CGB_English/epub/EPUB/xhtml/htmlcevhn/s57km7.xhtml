Habakkuk Chapter 3

1 A prayer of Habakkuk the prophet, composed in the style of a *shigionoth* (a passionate, emotional song or lament).

2 O LORD, I have heard your message, and it filled me with awe. O LORD, renew your work in our time, in our time reveal it again; even in your anger, remember to show compassion.

3 God came from Teman, and the Holy One from Mount Paran. Selah. His splendor filled the heavens, and the earth overflowed with his praise.

4 His radiance was like the light; rays of power flashed from his hand, and there his mighty strength was concealed.

5 Before Him, disease and plague advanced, and fiery flames blazed at His feet.

6 He rose and surveyed the earth; he looked and caused the nations to tremble. The ancient mountains were shattered, the eternal hills bowed lowâ€”his ways are eternal.

7 I observed the dwellings of Cushan in distress, and the tent coverings of the land of Midian were trembling.

8 Was the LORD angry with the rivers? Was your fury directed against the waters? Was your wrath aimed at the sea, when you rode upon your horses and your chariots of victory?

9 Your bow was fully prepared, as sworn by the promises of the tribes, in accordance with your word. *Selah.* You split the earth with rivers.

10 The mountains saw you and shook with fear; the surging waters rushed past. The depths of the sea roared aloud and raised their hands on high.

11 The sun and moon halted in their place; at the radiance of your arrows they paused, and at the gleam of your flashing spear.

12 You marched through the land with righteous fury; you crushed the nations in your wrath.

13 You went forth to deliver your people, to bring salvation to your anointed one. You struck the head of the wicked, crushing their power from the foundation to the very top. Selah.

14 You shattered the leaders of his villages with his own weapons; they rushed out like a whirlwind to scatter us. Their delight was like that of those who secretly devour the helpless.

15 You marched through the sea with your horses, through the surging mass of mighty waters.

16 When I heard this, my stomach churned; my lips quivered at the sound. Decay crept into my bones, and I trembled within myself, longing for rest in the day of distress. For when he advances against the people, he will invade them with his forces.

17 Even if the fig tree does not bloom, and no fruit grows on the vines; if the olive crop fails, and the fields produce no food; if the flocks are cut off from the fold, and there are no cattle in the stalls:

18 Yet I will take delight in the LORD; I will rejoice in the God who saves me.

19 The LORD God is my source of strength, and He will make my feet as swift and sure as those of a deer, enabling me to tread upon the heights. To the director of music, for accompaniment with stringed instruments.
