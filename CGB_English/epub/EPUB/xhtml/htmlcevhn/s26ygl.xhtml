Proverbs Chapter 3

1 My child, do not forget my teachings; instead, let your heart hold firmly to my instructions.

2 For they will bring you many days, a long life, and lasting peace.

3 Do not allow kindness and faithfulness to leave you; tie them securely around your neck, and engrave them deeply upon your heart.

4 In this way, you will gain favor and a good reputation in the eyes of both God and people.

5 Place your complete trust in the LORD with your whole heart, and do not rely solely on your own insight or reasoning.

6 In everything you do, recognize and honor Him, and He will guide your steps.

7 Do not consider yourself wise in your own estimation; instead, revere the LORD and turn away from evil.

8 This will bring vitality to your core and strength to your very bones.

9 Honor the LORD with your wealth and with the first and best of all your produce.

10 As a result, your storehouses will be abundantly filled, and your wine vats will overflow with fresh wine.

11 My child, do not reject the discipline of the LORD, nor grow weary when He corrects you.

12 The Lord disciplines those he loves, just as a father corrects the son in whom he takes delight.

13 Blessed is the one who discovers wisdom and the person who gains understanding.

14 The value of wisdom surpasses that of silver, and its profit exceeds even the purest gold.

15 She is more valuable than the finest jewels, and nothing you could ever desire can compare to her worth.

16 In her right hand, she holds a long and fulfilling life; in her left hand, she carries wealth and honor.

17 Her ways are filled with joy, and all her paths lead to peace.

18 She is like a tree of life to those who embrace her, and those who hold her fast are truly blessed.

19 The LORD established the earth through His wisdom; by His profound understanding, He set the heavens in place.

20 Through His wisdom, the deep waters are split open, and the clouds release their dew.

21 My child, do not let these teachings fade from your sight; hold firmly to sound wisdom and thoughtful judgment.

22 They will bring vitality to your inner being and adornment to your character.

23 Then you will walk on your path securely, and your steps will not falter.

24 When you lie down, you will not be afraid; indeed, you will rest, and your sleep will be peaceful.

25 Do not be terrified by sudden calamity, nor dismayed by the destruction that comes upon the wicked.

26 For the LORD will be your source of security and will protect you from stumbling into danger.

27 Do not withhold good from those who deserve it, when you have the ability to act.

28 Do not say to your neighbor, "Go away and come back later; I will give it to you tomorrow," when you already have the means to help them now.

29 Do not plot harm against your neighbor, especially when they live peacefully and trustingly near you.

30 Do not contend with someone without reason, especially if they have done you no wrong.

31 Do not envy those who oppress others, and do not adopt any of their ways.

32 The Lord detests those who are deceitful and perverse, but He shares His intimate counsel with those who live uprightly.

33 The Lord's judgment rests upon the household of the wicked, but He bestows His favor on the dwelling of the righteous.

34 Certainly, God mocks those who mock, but He shows favor to the humble.

35 The wise will receive honor, but disgrace will elevate the foolish.
