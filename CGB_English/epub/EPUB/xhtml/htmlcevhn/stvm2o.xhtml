Psalms Chapter 142

1 (A contemplative psalm of David; a prayer when he was in the cave.) I called out to the LORD with my voice; with my voice I pleaded before the LORD.

2 I laid out my grievances before him; I presented my distress openly to him.

3 When my spirit was utterly overwhelmed within me, you knew the course of my life. In the path where I walked, they secretly set a trap for me.

4 I turned to look to my right, and I saw that there was no one who recognized me or stood by me. All refuge had vanished; no one showed concern for my well-being.

5 I called out to you, O LORD, and said, "You are my shelter and my inheritance in this world of the living."

6 Listen to my plea, for I am in deep distress; rescue me from my persecutors, for they are more powerful than I.

7 Rescue me from my confinement, so that I may give thanks to your name. The righteous will gather around me, for you will show me abundant kindness.
