Psalms Chapter 44

1 (For the director of music. A contemplative song of the sons of Korah.) O God, we have heard with our own ears; our ancestors have recounted to us the mighty deeds you performed in their days, in the times of old.

2 You drove out the nations with your power and established your people in their place. You brought hardship upon those peoples and expelled them from the land.

3 They did not take possession of the land by their own strength, nor was it their own power that delivered them. It was your mighty hand, your powerful arm, and the radiance of your presence, for you showed them your favor.

4 You are my King, O God; decree victories for Jacob.

5 With your help, we will overcome our enemies; by the power of your name, we will defeat those who rise against us.

6 For I will not rely on my bow, nor will my sword deliver me.

7 But you have delivered us from our adversaries and have brought disgrace upon those who despised us.

8 We continually proclaim our trust in God throughout the day, and we will forever honor your name. Selah.

9 But you have rejected us and brought us disgrace; you no longer go out with our armies.

10 You cause us to retreat before our enemies, and those who hate us plunder for themselves.

11 You have handed us over like sheep destined for slaughter, and you have dispersed us among the nations.

12 You sell your people for nothing, and you gain no profit from their price.

13 You have made us an object of disgrace to our neighbors, a subject of mockery and ridicule to those around us.

14 You have made us an object of scorn among the nations, a subject of mocking and head-shaking among the peoples.

15 My distress is always before me, and the disgrace of my situation overwhelms me.

16 For the voice of one who mocks and speaks blasphemy, because of the enemy and the one who seeks revenge.

17 All these things have happened to us, yet we have not forgotten you, nor have we acted deceitfully in keeping your covenant.

18 Our hearts have not turned away, nor have our steps strayed from your path.

19 Though you have severely crushed us in the wilderness, where wild beasts dwell, and have overwhelmed us with the darkness of death.

20 If we have neglected the name of our God or lifted our hands in prayer to a foreign deity,

21 Does not God examine this thoroughly? For He understands the hidden thoughts of the heart.

22 Indeed, because of you, we face death every day; we are regarded as sheep destined for slaughter.

23 Awake! Why do you remain as though asleep, O Lord? Rise up; do not abandon us forever.

24 Why do you hide your face from us and seem to forget our suffering and oppression?

25 For our spirit is utterly crushed, brought low to the ground; our bodies cling to the earth in despair.

26 Rise up to assist us, and deliver us for the sake of your steadfast love and compassion.
