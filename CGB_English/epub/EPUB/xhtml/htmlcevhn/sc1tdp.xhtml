Numbers Chapter 5

1 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2 Instruct the Israelites to remove from the camp anyone who has leprosy, anyone with a bodily discharge, and anyone who has become ritually unclean through contact with a dead body.

3 You must send both men and women outside the camp; you are to expel them so that they do not defile the camp where I dwell among you.

4 The Israelites obeyed and sent them outside the camp, just as the LORD had commanded Moses. This is what the Israelites did.

5 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

6 Speak to the Israelites and say to them: When a man or woman commits any sin that people commonly commit, acting unfaithfully against the LORD, and that person is found guilty,

7 Then they must openly acknowledge the wrong they have committed. They must fully repay the amount of the wrongdoing, adding to it an additional fifth, and give it to the person they have wronged.

8 However, if the man has no relative to whom the restitution for the wrongdoing can be made, then the restitution must be given to the LORD, that is, to the priest. This is in addition to the ram of atonement, which is to be offered to make atonement for him.

9 And every offering of the sacred things brought by the Israelites, which they present to the priest, shall belong to him.

10 Every person’s sacred offerings shall belong to them. Whatever anyone gives to the priest will become the priest’s possession.

11 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

12 Speak to the Israelites and tell them: If a man’s wife turns away and is unfaithful to him, breaking her commitment,

13 If a man has sexual relations with a woman, and it is concealed from her husband, remaining hidden, and she is defiled, but there is no witness against her and she is not caught in the act, then the situation stands as follows:

14 If a husband becomes overcome by a spirit of jealousy and suspects his wife of being unfaithful, whether she has actually been defiled or not, the situation must be addressed. If he is filled with jealousy and believes she has been unfaithful, even if she has not, the matter requires resolution according to the law.

15 Then the man must bring his wife to the priest, along with the offering required for her—a tenth of an ephah of barley flour. He must not pour oil on it or add frankincense, because it is a jealousy offering, a reminder offering, meant to bring wrongdoing to light.

16 The priest shall bring her forward and present her before the LORD.

17 The priest shall take sacred water in a clay jar, and from the dust on the floor of the tabernacle, he shall gather some and mix it into the water.

18 The priest shall present the woman before the LORD, uncover her head, and place the memorial offering in her hands—this is the jealousy offering. The priest shall also hold in his hand the bitter water that brings about the curse.

19 The priest shall then place the woman under oath and say to her, "If no man has slept with you, and if you have not been unfaithful to your husband by becoming impure with another man, then may you be free from the effects of this bitter water that brings a curse.

20 But if you have turned aside to another man instead of your husband, and if you have become defiled, and another man has slept with you besides your husband—

21 Then the priest shall place the woman under a solemn oath of cursing, and the priest shall say to her, "May the LORD make you an object of cursing and a sworn example among your people when the LORD causes your thigh to waste away and your abdomen to swell."

22 This water that brings the curse will enter your body, causing your abdomen to swell and your thigh to waste away. And the woman shall respond, "Amen, amen."

23 The priest shall inscribe these curses in a scroll, and then he shall wash them off into the bitter water.

24 He shall make the woman drink the bitter water that brings about the curse, and the water that brings about the curse will enter her and cause her distress.

25 Then the priest shall take the offering of jealousy from the woman’s hand, lift it up before the LORD, and present it upon the altar.

26 The priest shall take a handful of the grain offering, which serves as a memorial portion, and burn it on the altar. Afterward, he shall have the woman drink the water.

27 When he has made her drink the water, it will come to pass that if she has defiled herself and been unfaithful to her husband, the water that brings the curse will enter her and cause bitter suffering. Her abdomen will swell, her thigh will waste away, and she will become an object of cursing among her people.

28 If the woman is found to be innocent and pure, she will be cleared of any wrongdoing and will be able to conceive children.

29 This is the regulation concerning cases of jealousy, when a wife strays and is unfaithful to her husband, becoming impure.

30 Or when a spirit of jealousy comes over a man, and he becomes suspicious of his wife, he shall bring her before the LORD, and the priest shall carry out for her all the provisions of this law.

31 Then the man will be free from guilt, but the woman must bear the consequences of her wrongdoing.
