Genesis Chapter 25

1 Afterward, Abraham took another wife, and her name was Keturah.

2 And she gave birth to Zimran, Jokshan, Medan, Midian, Ishbak, and Shuah for him.

3 Jokshan became the father of Sheba and Dedan. The descendants of Dedan were the Asshurites, the Letushites, and the Leummites.

4 The sons of Midian were Ephah, Epher, Hanoch, Abida, and Eldaah. All of these were descendants of Keturah.

5 Abraham transferred everything he owned to Isaac.

6 But to the sons of Abraham’s concubines, Abraham gave gifts and sent them away from his son Isaac while he was still alive, directing them eastward to the land of the east.

7 This is the account of the years of Abraham’s life: he lived a total of one hundred and seventy-five years.

8 Then Abraham breathed his last and died at a very old age, having lived a long and fulfilled life. He passed away and was reunited with his ancestors.

9 Isaac and Ishmael, his sons, laid him to rest in the cave of Machpelah, located in the field owned by Ephron, the son of Zohar the Hittite, near Mamre.

10 The field that Abraham bought from the sons of Heth is where Abraham was buried, along with his wife Sarah.

11 After Abraham died, God blessed his son Isaac, and Isaac settled near the well called Lahai-roi.

12 This is the account of the descendants of Ishmael, the son of Abraham, whom Hagar the Egyptian, Sarah’s servant, bore to Abraham.

13 Here are the names of Ishmael’s sons, listed in the order of their generations: Ishmael’s firstborn was Nebaioth, followed by Kedar, Adbeel, and Mibsam.

14 And Mishma, Dumah, and Massa.

15 Hadar, Tema, Jetur, Naphish, and Kedemah:

16 These are the descendants of Ishmael, and these are their names, listed by their settlements and their strongholds. They were twelve tribal leaders, each ruling over their own people.

17 This is the account of the years of Ishmael’s life: he lived 137 years. Then he breathed his last and died, and was gathered to his ancestors.

18 They settled in the region stretching from Havilah to Shur, which lies near Egypt as one travels toward Assyria. He died in the presence of all his relatives.

19 This is the account of Isaac, Abraham’s son: Abraham was the father of Isaac.

20 Isaac was forty years old when he married Rebekah, who was the daughter of Bethuel the Aramean from Paddan-aram and the sister of Laban the Aramean.

21 Isaac prayed earnestly to the LORD on behalf of his wife, because she was unable to conceive. The LORD heard his prayer, and Rebekah, his wife, became pregnant.

22 The babies struggled with each other inside her womb, and she said, “If this is happening, why is it like this for me?” So she went to seek guidance from the LORD.

23 The LORD said to her, "Two nations are within your womb, and two peoples will come forth from your body. One people will be stronger than the other, and the older will serve the younger."

24 When the time came for her to give birth, it was discovered that she was carrying twins in her womb.

25 The first one came out red, his entire body covered with hair like a fur garment; so they named him Esau.

26 Afterward, his brother came out, grasping Esau's heel with his hand; so he was named Jacob. Isaac was sixty years old when Rebekah gave birth to them.

27 The boys grew up, and Esau became a skilled hunter, a man who thrived in the open fields. Jacob, however, was a quiet and settled man, content to live in tents.

28 Isaac favored Esau because he enjoyed the wild game that Esau brought him, but Rebekah loved Jacob.

29 Jacob was cooking a stew, and Esau came in from the field, exhausted.

30 And Esau said to Jacob, "Please let me eat some of that red stew, for I am exhausted." This is why he was also called Edom.

31 Jacob said, "Sell me your birthright today."

32 And Esau said, "Look, I am about to die. What good is this birthright to me?"

33 Jacob said, "Swear to me today." So Esau swore to him and sold his birthright to Jacob.

34 Then Jacob gave Esau some bread and a bowl of lentil stew. Esau ate and drank, then got up and left. In this way, Esau showed how little he valued his birthright.
