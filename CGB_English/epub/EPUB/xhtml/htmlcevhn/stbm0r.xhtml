Job Chapter 31

1 I have made a solemn promise with my eyes; how then could I allow myself to gaze with desire upon a young woman?

2 What share do we have in God who reigns above? What inheritance do we receive from the Almighty on high?

3 Does not disaster await the wicked? And is not a severe judgment destined for those who practice wrongdoing?

4 Does He not observe my conduct and take account of every step I take?

5 If I have lived with falsehood, or if I have eagerly pursued dishonesty;

6 Let me be measured on an accurate scale, so that God may recognize my integrity.

7 If I have strayed from the right path, if my heart has followed the desires of my eyes, or if any wrongdoing has clung to my hands;

8 Let me plant the seeds, and may someone else reap the harvest; yes, may my descendants be utterly destroyed.

9 If my heart has been enticed by a woman, or if I have secretly plotted at my neighborâ€™s door;

10 Then may my wife be forced to serve another man, and may others overpower her.

11 This is a grave and detestable offense; indeed, it is a sin that demands judgment from the authorities.

12 For it is a fire that burns all the way to destruction, and it would completely destroy everything I have gained.

13 If I ever disregarded the rights of my male or female servants when they brought a complaint against me,

14 What then should I do when God arises? And when He comes to judge, how will I answer Him?

15 Did not the same Creator who formed me in the womb also form him? Did not one Maker shape us both in the womb?

16 If I have denied the poor what they needed, or caused the eyes of the widow to grow weary with despair;

17 Have I ever eaten my food all by myself, without sharing any of it with the fatherless?

18 For from my earliest days, he has been like a father to me, and I have cared for her since before I was born.

19 If I have witnessed anyone perishing due to lack of clothing, or any poor person without adequate covering,

20 If he has not been blessed by the work of my hands, and if he has not been kept warm by the wool of my sheep,

21 If I have raised my hand against the fatherless, trusting in my influence at the city gate to protect me,

22 May my arm fall from its socket, and may my arm be shattered at the bone.

23 The threat of destruction from God filled me with dread, and because of His majesty, I could not bear to stand before Him.

24 If I have placed my trust in gold, or declared to pure gold, "You are my security;"

25 If I took delight in the abundance of my possessions or boasted about the wealth I had gained,

26 If I looked with admiration at the sun when it shone brightly, or at the moon as it moved in its radiant splendor;

27 And if my heart has been secretly lured away, or if I have shown reverence by kissing my hand in worship to anything other than God,

28 This would also be a sin deserving judgment, for I would have denied the God who reigns above.

29 If I took pleasure in the downfall of someone who hated me, or gloated when misfortune came upon them:

30 I have not allowed my words to sin by wishing harm upon his life.

31 If the people of my household have not said, "If only we could have some of his meat! We cannot be satisfied!"

32 The foreigner did not have to sleep in the street; instead, I opened my doors to welcome the traveler.

33 If I concealed my sins like Adam, by hiding my wrongdoing within myself:

34 Was I afraid of a large crowd, or did the disdain of entire families frighten me, so that I remained silent and did not step outside my door?

35 If only someone would listen to me! My deepest wish is that the Almighty would respond to me, and that my accuser had written down their charges.

36 Certainly, I would carry it upon my shoulder and fasten it as a crown upon my head.

37 I would recount to him the details of my life; like a nobleman, I would approach him with confidence.

38 If my land cries out against me, or if its furrows also raise their complaint;

39 If I have consumed its produce without paying for it, or if I have caused the owners to lose their lives:

40 Let thorns sprout up instead of wheat, and weeds instead of barley. These are the final words of Job.
