Psalms Chapter 137

1 By the rivers of Babylon, we sat down and wept as we remembered Zion.

2 We hung our harps on the willow trees in the midst of that place.

3 For there, our captors demanded songs from us, and those who tormented us insisted on joy, saying, "Sing us one of the songs of Zion."

4 How can we sing the songs of the LORD while in a foreign land?

5 If I ever forget you, O Jerusalem, may my right hand lose its skill.

6 If I fail to remember you, may my tongue stick to the roof of my mouth; if I do not hold Jerusalem above my greatest joy.

7 Remember, O LORD, the people of Edom on the day Jerusalem fell; they said, "Tear it down, tear it down to its very foundations!"

8 O daughter of Babylon, destined for destruction, blessed will be the one who repays you with the same treatment you have given us.

9 Blessed will be the one who seizes your infants and dashes them against the rocks.
