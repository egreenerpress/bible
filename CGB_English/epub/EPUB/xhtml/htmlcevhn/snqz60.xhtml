II Chronicles Chapter 13

1 In the eighteenth year of King Jeroboam's reign, Abijah became king over Judah.

2 He ruled for three years in Jerusalem. His mother’s name was Michaiah, the daughter of Uriel from Gibeah. During his reign, there was ongoing conflict between Abijah and Jeroboam.

3 Abijah prepared for battle with an army of four hundred thousand courageous and skilled warriors. Jeroboam also arranged his forces against him, leading eight hundred thousand chosen men, all of whom were strong and valiant fighters.

4 Abijah stood on Mount Zemaraim, which is in the hill country of Ephraim, and declared, "Listen to me, Jeroboam, and all Israel!

5 Should you not understand that the LORD, the God of Israel, granted the kingdom over Israel to David forever—to him and to his descendants—through an enduring covenant?

6 Yet Jeroboam, the son of Nebat, who was a servant of Solomon, the son of David, has risen up and rebelled against his master.

7 And worthless men, the sons of Belial, gathered around him and rose up against Rehoboam, the son of Solomon, when Rehoboam was young and inexperienced, and he was unable to resist them.

8 And now you presume to oppose the kingdom of the LORD, which is under the authority of the descendants of David. Though you are a vast multitude and have with you the golden calves that Jeroboam made for you as gods, do you truly believe this will prevail?

9 Have you not driven out the priests of the LORD, the descendants of Aaron, and the Levites, and instead appointed your own priests in the way of the nations of other lands? Now, anyone who comes to dedicate himself with a young bull and seven rams can become a priest for those who are not gods at all.

10 For our part, the LORD is our God, and we have not abandoned Him. The priests who serve the LORD are descendants of Aaron, and the Levites carry out their duties as appointed.

11 They offer burnt sacrifices to the LORD every morning and every evening, along with fragrant incense. They also arrange the showbread on the pure table and light the lamps on the golden lampstand each evening. These are the duties we faithfully carry out to honor the LORD our God. But you have abandoned Him.

12 Listen carefully: God Himself is with us as our leader, and His priests are ready to sound the trumpets to declare war against you. People of Israel, do not fight against the LORD, the God of your ancestors, for you will not succeed.

13 But Jeroboam set an ambush to attack them from behind, so that while the main force of Judah faced them head-on, the ambush lay in wait at their rear.

14 When Judah turned to look, they saw that the battle surrounded them, both in front and behind. In their distress, they cried out to the LORD, and the priests blew the trumpets.

15 Then the men of Judah raised a battle cry, and as they shouted, it happened that God struck down Jeroboam and all Israel before Abijah and Judah.

16 The Israelites fled before the army of Judah, and God handed them over to Judah's forces.

17 Abijah and his forces inflicted a massive defeat upon them, resulting in the death of five hundred thousand of Israel's finest soldiers.

18 At that time, the people of Israel were subdued, but the people of Judah gained the upper hand because they trusted in the LORD, the God of their ancestors.

19 Abijah pursued Jeroboam and captured several cities from him: Bethel along with its surrounding towns, Jeshanah with its towns, and Ephron with its towns.

20 Jeroboam never regained his power during the reign of Abijah. The Lord struck him down, and he died.

21 But Abijah grew powerful and took fourteen wives, who bore him twenty-two sons and sixteen daughters.

22 The remaining events of Abijah's reign, including his actions and his words, are recorded in the chronicles of the prophet Iddo.
