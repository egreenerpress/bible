Acts Chapter 20

1 After the commotion had subsided, Paul summoned the disciples to him, embraced them, and then set out to travel to Macedonia.

2 After traveling through those regions and offering them much encouragement, he arrived in Greece.

3 He stayed there for three months. When he learned that the Jews were plotting against him as he was preparing to sail for Syria, he decided to return by way of Macedonia.

4 Sopater from Berea joined him on the journey to Asia, along with Aristarchus and Secundus from Thessalonica, Gaius from Derbe, Timothy, and Tychicus and Trophimus from Asia.

5 The group that went ahead waited for us at Troas.

6 We set sail from Philippi after the Festival of Unleavened Bread and reached Troas in five days. There we stayed for seven days.

7 On the first day of the week, when the disciples gathered together to share a meal, Paul spoke to them, as he was preparing to leave the next day. He continued his message until midnight.

8 In the upstairs room where they had assembled, there were many lamps burning.

9 A young man named Eutychus was sitting in a window, overcome by a deep sleep. As Paul continued speaking at length, Eutychus, weighed down by sleep, fell from the third story and was picked up dead.

10 Paul went down, fell upon him, and embraced him, saying, "Do not be distressed, for his life is still within him."

11 When he had returned upstairs, he broke bread and ate. After spending a long time in conversation, continuing until daybreak, he then departed.

12 They brought the young man back alive, and this brought them great comfort.

13 We went ahead to the ship and set sail for Assos, where we planned to take Paul on board. He had arranged it this way, intending to travel there on foot.

14 When he joined us at Assos, we welcomed him aboard and continued our journey to Mitylene.

15 We set sail from there and the following day arrived opposite Chios. The day after that, we reached Samos and stayed at Trogyllium. On the next day, we continued our journey and came to Miletus.

16 Paul had decided to bypass Ephesus, as he did not want to spend additional time in Asia. He was eager to reach Jerusalem by the day of Pentecost, if at all possible.

17 From Miletus, he sent a message to Ephesus, summoning the elders of the church.

18 When they arrived to meet him, he said to them, "You know how I have lived among you from the very first day I came to Asia, conducting myself in every situation with integrity and sincerity."

19 I served the Lord with complete humility, often with tears, and through many trials that came upon me because of the plots of the Jews.

20 I have held back nothing that would benefit you, but have openly declared and taught you everything, both in public gatherings and in private homes.

21 He proclaimed this message both to the Jews and to the Gentiles: the need to turn to God in repentance and to place their faith in our Lord Jesus Christ.

22 And now, look, I am compelled by the Spirit to go to Jerusalem, not knowing what will happen to me there.

23 However, the Holy Spirit testifies in every city, declaring that imprisonment and hardships await me.

24 None of these things trouble me, nor do I consider my own life as something of great importance to myself. My only aim is to complete my assigned task with joy and to fulfill the ministry I have received from the Lord Jesus: to bear witness to the good news of God’s grace.

25 And now, listen carefully: I am certain that all of you, among whom I have traveled proclaiming the kingdom of God, will never see me again.

26 Therefore, I declare to you today that I am innocent of any responsibility for the blood of all people.

27 For I have not hesitated to make known to you the entire purpose and will of God.

28 Therefore, be diligent in caring for yourselves and for the entire flock over which the Holy Spirit has appointed you as overseers, to shepherd the church of God, which He acquired with His own blood.

29 I am fully aware that after I am gone, fierce wolves will come among you, showing no mercy to the flock.

30 From among your own group, some will rise up and distort the truth, speaking misleading things in order to lead followers away after themselves.

31 Therefore, be alert and keep in mind that for three years I never stopped warning each of you, both night and day, with heartfelt tears.

32 And now, my brothers and sisters, I entrust you to God and to the message of His grace, which has the power to strengthen you and to grant you a share in the inheritance among all those who are set apart as holy.

33 I have not desired or sought after anyone’s silver, gold, or clothing.

34 Indeed, you yourselves know that these hands of mine have provided for my own needs and for those who were with me.

35 I have demonstrated to you in every way that by working hard in this manner, you should help those who are weak and keep in mind the words of the Lord Jesus, who said, “It is more blessed to give than to receive.”

36 After saying these things, he knelt down and prayed together with all of them.

37 They all wept deeply, embraced Paul, and kissed him affectionately.

38 They were deeply grieved, especially because of what he had said—that they would never see his face again. Then they accompanied him to the ship.
