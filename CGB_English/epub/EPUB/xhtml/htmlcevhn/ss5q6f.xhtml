Ecclesiastes Chapter 11

1 Send out your resources generously, even into uncertain or seemingly unproductive places, for in time you will receive a return.

2 Set aside a portion for seven, and even for eight, because you cannot foresee what troubles may come upon the earth.

3 If the clouds are heavy with rain, they pour it out upon the earth; and if a tree falls to the south or to the north, in the place where it falls, there it will remain.

4 The one who constantly watches the wind will never plant seeds, and the one who keeps staring at the clouds will never harvest.

5 Just as you do not understand the path of the wind or how bones are formed in the womb of a pregnant woman, so you cannot comprehend the works of God, who creates all things.

6 In the morning, plant your seed, and in the evening, do not hold back your effort; for you do not know which will succeedâ€”whether this or that, or if both will turn out equally well.

7 Certainly, light is delightful, and it is a joy for the eyes to see the sun.

8 However, if a person lives many years and finds joy in all of them, they should still remember the days of darkness, for those days will be many. Everything that is to come is fleeting and without lasting substance.

9 Be joyful, young man, in your youth, and let your heart delight in the days of your early life. Follow the desires of your heart and the pursuits that captivate your eyes. Yet remember this: for all these things, God will bring you into judgment.

10 Therefore, banish grief from your heart and cast off trouble from your body, for childhood and youth are fleeting and meaningless.
