Psalms Chapter 51

1 (For the music director. A psalm of David, when the prophet Nathan came to him after he had committed adultery with Bathsheba.) Have mercy on me, O God, in keeping with Your steadfast love; in accordance with the abundance of Your compassion, wipe away my sins.

2 Purify me completely from my wrongdoing, and cleanse me from my sin.

3 I fully recognize my wrongdoing, and my sin is constantly before me.

4 Against you, you alone, have I sinned, and committed this evil in your sight. Therefore, you are justified in your words and blameless in your judgment.

5 Indeed, I was brought forth in a state of moral corruption, and from the moment of my conception, I was marked by sin.

6 Indeed, You desire integrity in the innermost being; and in the depths of my heart, You will teach me wisdom.

7 Cleanse me with hyssop, and I will be purified; wash me, and I will become whiter than snow.

8 Grant me the ability to hear sounds of joy and gladness once more, so that the bones you have crushed may be filled with rejoicing.

9 Turn your gaze away from my sins, and erase all my wrongdoings from your sight.

10 "O God, create in me a pure heart, and restore within me a steadfast and upright spirit."

11 Do not cast me away from your presence, and do not remove your Holy Spirit from me.

12 Grant me once more the joy of your deliverance, and sustain me with a willing and generous spirit.

13 Then I will instruct those who have gone astray in your ways, and those who have sinned will turn back to you.

14 Save me from the guilt of bloodshed, O God, you who are the God of my deliverance; then my tongue will joyfully proclaim your justice.

15 O Lord, open my lips, and my mouth will declare your praise.

16 You do not desire sacrifices, or else I would offer them; you take no pleasure in burnt offerings.

17 The offerings that truly please God are a humble spirit and a repentant heart. O God, you will never reject a heart that is broken and sorrowful for sin.

18 Act according to your gracious will toward Zion; rebuild the walls of Jerusalem.

19 Then you will be pleased with righteous sacrifices, with burnt offerings and whole burnt offerings; then they will offer bulls upon your altar.
