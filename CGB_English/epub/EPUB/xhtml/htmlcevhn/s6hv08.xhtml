Isaiah Chapter 43

1 But now, this is what the LORD, who created you, O Jacob, and who formed you, O Israel, declares: Do not be afraid, for I have redeemed you; I have called you by name—you are mine.

2 When you pass through the waters, I will be with you; and when you cross the rivers, they will not overwhelm you. When you walk through the fire, you will not be burned, nor will the flames consume you.

3 For I am the LORD your God, the Holy One of Israel, your Savior. I gave up Egypt as the price for your freedom, and Ethiopia and Seba in exchange for you.

4 Because you are precious in my sight, you are honored, and I love you. Therefore, I will give people in exchange for you and nations in place of your life.

5 Do not be afraid, for I am with you. I will bring your descendants from the east and gather you from the west.

6 I will command the north, "Release them," and to the south, "Do not hold them back." Bring my sons from distant lands and my daughters from the farthest corners of the earth.

7 Everyone who is called by my name—I have created them for my glory; I have formed them, indeed, I have made them.

8 Bring forward those who are blind yet possess eyes, and those who are deaf yet have ears.

9 Gather all the nations together, and assemble the peoples. Who among them can declare these things and reveal to us what happened in the past? Let them present their witnesses to prove their case, so that they may be justified. Or let them listen and acknowledge, "It is true."

10 You are my witnesses, declares the LORD, and my servant whom I have chosen, so that you may know and trust me, and understand that I am the one and only. Before me, no god was formed, nor will there be any after me.

11 "I alone am the LORD, and apart from me, there is no savior."

12 I have proclaimed, I have delivered, and I have made known—when no foreign god was among you. Therefore, you are my witnesses, declares the LORD, that I am God.

13 Indeed, before the day began, I am He; and there is no one who can rescue anyone from my grasp. When I act, who can reverse it?

14 This is what the LORD, your Redeemer, the Holy One of Israel, says: For your sake, I have sent word to Babylon and brought down all their nobles, along with the Chaldeans, whose pride is in their ships.

15 I am the LORD, your Holy One, the Creator of Israel, your King.

16 This is what the LORD says, who creates a pathway through the sea and a road through the surging waters:

17 He brings forth the chariot and horse, the army and their might; they will lie down together, never to rise again. They are extinguished, snuffed out like a wick.

18 Do not dwell on past events, nor fix your thoughts on what has gone before.

19 Listen carefully, I am about to do something new; even now it is beginning to unfold. Can you not perceive it? I will create a path through the wilderness and cause rivers to flow in the barren desert.

20 The wild animals will honor me, even the jackals and the ostriches, because I provide water in the wilderness and rivers in the desert, to give drink to my people, my chosen ones.

21 I have created this people for myself; they will declare my praise.

22 But you have not called upon me, O Jacob; you have grown weary of me, O Israel.

23 You have not brought me the sheep or goats for your burnt offerings, nor have you honored me with your sacrifices. I did not burden you by demanding grain offerings, nor did I weary you with requests for incense.

24 You have not purchased fragrant cane for me with your money, nor have you satisfied me with the fat of your sacrifices. Instead, you have burdened me with your sins; you have wearied me with your wrongdoing.

25 "I, even I, am the one who wipes away your transgressions for my own sake, and I will not recall your sins."

26 "Remind me of your case; let us argue it together. Present your arguments, so that you may be proven right."

27 Your earliest ancestor sinned, and your leaders have rebelled against me.

28 Therefore, I have disgraced the leaders of the holy place, and I have handed over Jacob to be cursed, and Israel to be scorned.
