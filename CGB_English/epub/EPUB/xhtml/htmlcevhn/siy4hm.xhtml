Exodus Chapter 1

1 Here are the names of the Israelites who entered Egypt, each man accompanied by his family, traveling with Jacob.

2 Reuben, Simeon, Levi, and Judah.

3 Issachar, Zebulun, and Benjamin.

4 Dan, Naphtali, Gad, and Asher.

5 All the descendants who came from Jacob's lineage numbered seventy individuals, for Joseph was already in Egypt.

6 Joseph passed away, along with all his brothers and everyone of that generation.

7 The Israelites became very fruitful and increased greatly in number. They multiplied and grew exceedingly powerful, so that the land was filled with them.

8 Then a new king came to power in Egypt, who did not know about Joseph.

9 And he said to his people, "Look, the people of the Israelites are becoming more numerous and stronger than we are."

10 Come, let us act shrewdly toward them; otherwise, they may increase in number, and if war breaks out, they could join our enemies, fight against us, and then leave the land altogether.

11 So the Egyptians appointed overseers to oppress them with forced labor. They compelled the Israelites to build storage cities for Pharaoh, namely Pithom and Raamses.

12 However, the more the Egyptians oppressed the Israelites, the more they increased in number and flourished. This caused great distress and frustration among the Egyptians because of the Israelites' growing strength and presence.

13 The Egyptians forced the Israelites into harsh and cruel labor.

14 They made the lives of the Israelites bitter through harsh labor, forcing them to work with mortar and bricks and to perform every kind of hard labor in the fields. All the work they imposed on them was carried out with relentless severity.

15 The king of Egypt spoke to the Hebrew midwives, one of whom was named Shiphrah and the other Puah.

16 He said, "When you assist the Hebrew women during childbirth and observe them on the delivery stools, if the child is a son, you must kill him; but if it is a daughter, let her live."

17 However, the midwives revered God and did not follow the command of the king of Egypt. Instead, they allowed the male infants to live.

18 The king of Egypt summoned the midwives and said to them, "Why have you done this? Why have you let the male infants live?"

19 The midwives replied to Pharaoh, "The Hebrew women are not like the Egyptian women; they are vigorous and give birth before the midwives can even reach them."

20 Therefore, God showed kindness to the midwives, and the people increased in number and became very strong.

21 And it happened that because the midwives revered God, He established households for them.

22 Pharaoh commanded all his people, saying, "Every male child who is born must be thrown into the river, but every female child you shall allow to live."
