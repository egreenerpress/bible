I Corinthians Chapter 2

1 And I, my fellow believers, when I came to you, did not come with eloquent speech or superior wisdom as I proclaimed to you the testimony of God.

2 For I resolved to focus on nothing among you except Jesus Christ and his crucifixion.

3 I was with you in a state of weakness, filled with fear and deep trembling.

4 My message and my preaching were not delivered with persuasive words of human wisdom, but with a clear display of the Spirit’s power.

5 Your faith should not rely on human wisdom but on the power of God.

6 We speak wisdom, however, among those who are mature—yet not the wisdom of this age or of the rulers of this age, who are destined to pass away.

7 We proclaim the wisdom of God, which is veiled in mystery—the hidden wisdom that God predetermined before the ages for our ultimate glory.

8 The rulers of this age did not understand this wisdom, for if they had known it, they would not have crucified the Lord of glory.

9 As it is written: "No eye has seen, no ear has heard, nor has it even entered the human heart, the things that God has prepared for those who love Him."

10 However, God has made these things known to us through his Spirit. For the Spirit explores everything, even the profound and hidden matters of God.

11 Who can truly understand the thoughts and inner workings of a person except the spirit within that person? In the same way, no one can fully comprehend the thoughts and ways of God except the Spirit of God.

12 Now we have received, not the spirit of this world, but the Spirit who comes from God, so that we may understand the things freely given to us by God.

13 We communicate these truths not with words taught by human wisdom, but with those taught by the Holy Spirit, explaining spiritual realities to those who are spiritually attuned.

14 However, the person who lives by their natural instincts does not accept the truths that come from the Spirit of God, for such things seem foolish to them. They cannot understand these truths because they can only be grasped through spiritual discernment.

15 However, the one who is guided by the Spirit discerns all matters, yet he himself is not subject to the judgment of anyone.

16 Who has fully understood the thoughts of the Lord, so as to advise Him? Yet, we possess the understanding that comes from Christ.
