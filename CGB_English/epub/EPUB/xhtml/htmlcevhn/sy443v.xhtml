II Samuel Chapter 23

1 Here are the final words of David. David, the son of Jesse, spoke—the man who was exalted to greatness, the one anointed by the God of Jacob, and the beloved psalmist of Israel. He declared:

2 The Spirit of the LORD spoke through me, and His words were on my tongue.

3 The God of Israel declared, the Rock of Israel spoke to me: "Whoever rules over people must be just, governing with reverence for God."

4 He will be like the brightness of the morning, when the sun rises, a morning without clouds; like the fresh grass that springs up from the earth after rain, gleaming in the sunlight.

5 Even though my household may not be perfect in God's sight, He has established with me an everlasting covenant, perfectly arranged and secure in every detail. This covenant is the foundation of my salvation and the fulfillment of all my longing, even if its full realization is not yet evident.

6 But the wicked will all be like thorns to be cast aside, for they cannot be grasped by hand.

7 The person who dares to touch them must be armed with iron and the shaft of a spear, and they will be completely consumed by fire in that very place.

8 These are the names of David’s mighty warriors: The first was Adino the Eznite, chief of the captains, also known as the Tachmonite. He once raised his spear and struck down eight hundred men in a single encounter.

9 After him came Eleazar, the son of Dodo the Ahohite, one of the three mighty warriors who fought alongside David. This was when they confronted the Philistines who had gathered for battle, even though the rest of the Israelite army had retreated.

10 He stood up and struck down the Philistines until his hand grew tired and his hand clung to the sword. The Lord brought about a great victory that day, and the people followed him only to plunder the spoils.

11 After him came Shammah, the son of Agee, from Harar. The Philistines had gathered in a raiding party, and there was a field full of lentils. The people fled from the Philistines.

12 But he took his stand in the middle of the field, defended it, and struck down the Philistines. So the Lord brought about a great victory.

13 Three of the thirty leading warriors went down and came to David during the harvest season at the cave of Adullam, while a company of Philistines had encamped in the Valley of Rephaim.

14 At that time, David was staying in a stronghold, and a Philistine garrison was stationed in Bethlehem.

15 David was deeply thirsty and said, "Oh, if only someone would bring me a drink of water from the well of Bethlehem, the one by the gate!"

16 The three mighty warriors broke through the Philistine forces, drew water from the well near the gate of Bethlehem, and brought it to David. However, he refused to drink it. Instead, he poured it out as an offering to the LORD.

17 And he said, "Far be it from me, O LORD, to do such a thing! Is this not the blood of the men who risked their lives to bring it?" Therefore, he refused to drink it. These were the deeds of these three mighty warriors.

18 Abishai, the brother of Joab and son of Zeruiah, was the leader of the Three. He raised his spear against three hundred men, struck them down, and gained a reputation as one of the Three.

19 Was he not the most honored among the three? Therefore, he became their leader; yet he did not reach the status of the first three.

20 Benaiah, the son of Jehoiada, was a courageous man from Kabzeel who had accomplished many great deeds. He killed two mighty warriors of Moab who were as fierce as lions. Additionally, he went down into a pit on a snowy day and killed a lion there.

21 And he killed an Egyptian, a man of impressive stature. The Egyptian was armed with a spear, but he went down to him with only a staff, wrested the spear from the Egyptian’s hand, and killed him with his own weapon.

22 Benaiah, the son of Jehoiada, accomplished these deeds and gained a reputation as one of the three mighty warriors.

23 He was more distinguished than the thirty, yet he did not reach the status of the top three. David appointed him to be in charge of his personal guard.

24 Asahel, the brother of Joab, was among the thirty mighty warriors. Elhanan, the son of Dodo from Bethlehem, was also one of them.

25 Shammah from Harod, Elika from Harod,

26 Helez from Palti, Ira the son of Ikkesh from Tekoa,

27 Abiezer from Anathoth and Mebunnai from Hushah.

28 Zalmon the Ahohite and Maharai the Netophathite.

29 Heled son of Baanah, from Netophah, and Ittai son of Ribai, from Gibeah in the territory of Benjamin.

30 Benaiah from Pirathon, and Hiddai from the ravines of Gaash.

31 Abi-albon from Arbath, Azmaveth from Barhum.

32 Eliahba from Shaalbim, one of the sons of Jashen, Jonathan,

33 Shammah from Harar, and Ahiam, the son of Sharar from Harar.

34 Eliphelet, the son of Ahasbai, who was from the clan of Maacah, and Eliam, the son of Ahithophel, who was from Gilo.

35 Hezrai from Carmel and Paarai from Arbah.

36 Igal, the son of Nathan from Zobah, and Bani the Gadite.

37 Zelek from Ammon, and Naharai from Beeroth, who was the armor-bearer of Joab, the son of Zeruiah.

38 Ira the Ithrite and Gareb the Ithrite.

39 Uriah the Hittite: thirty-seven in total.
