Proverbs Chapter 24

1 Do not envy wicked people, nor desire to associate with them.

2 For their hearts are set on devising ruin, and their words speak of causing harm.

3 A house is built through wisdom, and by understanding it is firmly established.

4 Through wisdom, the rooms will be filled with every kind of valuable and delightful treasure.

5 A wise person possesses great strength, and one who has understanding gains even greater power.

6 Through wise guidance, you will wage your war effectively, and with the abundance of skilled advisors, there is security.

7 Wisdom is beyond the reach of a fool; he does not even speak wisely in the public assembly.

8 A person who plans to do evil will be known as a troublemaker.

9 The mindset of foolishness is sinful, and the one who mocks and scorns others is detestable in the eyes of people.

10 If you lose heart in times of trouble, your strength is limited.

11 If you refrain from rescuing those who are being led to their death, and those who are on the verge of being killed;

12 If you say, "Look, we were unaware of this," does not the One who examines the heart perceive it? Does not the One who watches over your life know it? And will He not repay each person according to their deeds?

13 My child, eat honey, for it is good; and the honeycomb is sweet to your taste.

14 In this way, the understanding of wisdom will benefit your very being: once you have discovered it, there will be a lasting reward, and your hope will not be disappointed.

15 Do not lie in wait, you wicked person, against the home of the righteous; do not plunder or disturb his place of rest.

16 A righteous person may stumble seven times, yet they will rise again; but the wicked will be brought down by calamity.

17 Do not take joy when your enemy falls, nor let your heart be glad when they stumble.

18 Otherwise, the LORD may see it, and it may displease him, so that he turns his anger away from the one who deserves it.

19 Do not be troubled or angered by those who do evil, nor let yourself become envious of the wicked.

20 For the wicked will receive no lasting benefit; the light of the unjust will be extinguished.

21 My child, revere the LORD and honor the king, and do not associate with those who are quick to rebel or seek upheaval.

22 Their disaster will come upon them suddenly, and who can foresee the destruction that will overtake them both?

23 These principles also apply to the wise. It is not right to show favoritism or partiality when making judgments.

24 Whoever declares the wicked to be righteous will be cursed by the people and despised by the nations.

25 However, those who offer correction with wisdom will find favor, and a gracious blessing will rest upon them.

26 Every person will show respect and approval to the one who gives a wise and fitting response.

27 First, complete your tasks and prepare your resources in the field, ensuring everything is in order. Only after that should you focus on building your house.

28 Do not testify against your neighbor without a valid reason, and do not deceive others with your words.

29 Do not say, "I will repay him in the same way he has treated me; I will give back to the person according to what he has done."

30 I passed by the field of a lazy person and by the vineyard of someone lacking sense.

31 Behold, the land was completely overrun with thorns, and nettles had spread across its surface. Moreover, its stone wall lay in ruins.

32 Then I observed carefully and reflected deeply; I examined it closely and gained understanding.

33 Just a little more sleep, a bit more rest, a moment longer to fold your hands and lie down.

34 Your poverty will come upon you like a traveler, and your need will arrive like a warrior fully armed.
