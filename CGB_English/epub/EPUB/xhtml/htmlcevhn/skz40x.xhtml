Leviticus Chapter 11

1 The LORD spoke to Moses and Aaron, saying to them,

2 Tell the people of Israel: These are the animals you may eat from all the creatures that live on the earth.

3 Any animal that has a divided hoof, with its hooves completely split, and that chews the cud, you may eat.

4 However, you must not eat the following animals, even though they chew the cud or have a divided hoof: the camel, for example, chews the cud but does not have a divided hoof—it is unclean for you.

5 The hyrax, because it chews the cud but does not have a split hoof, is unclean for you.

6 And the hare, because it chews its food again but does not have a split hoof, is unclean for you.

7 And the pig, though it has a divided hoof and its foot is split, it does not chew the cud; it is unclean for you.

8 You must not eat their meat, and you must not touch their carcasses; they are unclean for you.

9 You may eat any creature that lives in the waters—whether in the seas or in the rivers—as long as it has both fins and scales. These are the ones you are permitted to eat.

10 All creatures in the seas or rivers that do not have fins and scales—whether they are swimming in the waters or are any other living thing in the waters—are to be considered detestable to you.

11 You must consider them utterly detestable; you are not to eat their meat, and you must regard their carcasses as repulsive.

12 Any creature in the waters that does not have fins and scales is to be considered detestable to you.

13 These are the birds you are to regard as detestable and not to eat; they are unclean: the eagle, the bearded vulture, and the black vulture.

14 And the vulture, and the kite according to its kind;

15 Every kind of raven, according to its species.

16 And the owl, the night hawk, the cuckoo, and the hawk of every kind...

17 And the little owl, the cormorant, and the great owl.

18 And the swan, the pelican, and the carrion vulture...

19 And the stork, the heron according to its kind, the hoopoe, and the bat.

20 All creatures that move along the ground on all fours, including birds, are to be considered detestable to you.

21 However, you may eat any winged insect that walks on all fours, provided it has jointed legs above its feet for leaping on the ground.

22 You may eat these kinds of insects: the locust according to its kind, the bald locust according to its kind, the cricket according to its kind, and the grasshopper according to its kind.

23 However, all other winged creatures that crawl on all fours are to be detestable to you.

24 And concerning these, you will be considered unclean: anyone who touches their dead body will remain unclean until evening.

25 Anyone who carries any part of the carcass of these animals must wash their clothes and will remain unclean until evening.

26 The carcasses of any animal that has a divided hoof but is not completely split, or does not chew the cud, are unclean for you. Anyone who touches them will become unclean.

27 And any animal that walks on its paws, among all the kinds of creatures that move on four legs, is unclean for you. Whoever touches their dead body will be unclean until evening.

28 Anyone who carries the carcass of these animals must wash their clothes and will remain unclean until evening. These carcasses are unclean to you.

29 Among the small creatures that move about on the ground, the following are also considered unclean for you: the weasel, the mouse, and every kind of lizard.

30 And the gecko, the sand lizard, the wall lizard, the skink, and the mole.

31 Among all the creatures that crawl, these are unclean for you: anyone who touches them when they are dead will be unclean until evening.

32 If any of these creatures dies and falls on something, that object will become unclean. Whether it is a wooden container, clothing, leather, a sack, or any other item used for work, it must be placed in water and will remain unclean until evening. After that, it will be considered clean again.

33 And any clay pot into which any of them falls, whatever is inside it will become unclean, and you must break the pot.

34 Regarding any food that is normally edible, if such water comes into contact with it, the food will become unclean. Similarly, any drink that is normally consumable, if it is in any such vessel, will also become unclean.

35 If any part of a dead animal falls on something, that object will become unclean. Whether it is an oven or a cooking pot, it must be destroyed, because it is unclean and will remain unclean for you.

36 However, a spring or a cistern that holds an ample supply of water remains clean. But anything that comes into contact with the carcass of an unclean animal will become unclean.

37 If any part of a dead animal falls on seed that is to be planted, the seed remains clean.

38 But if water has been poured on the seed and any part of a dead body falls on it, it will be unclean for you.

39 If any animal that you are permitted to eat dies, whoever touches its carcass will be unclean until evening.

40 Anyone who eats the carcass of it must wash their clothes and will remain unclean until evening. Likewise, anyone who carries the carcass must wash their clothes and will remain unclean until evening.

41 Every creature that moves about on the ground is to be considered detestable; you must not eat it.

42 You must not eat any creature that moves on its belly, or any that walks on four legs, or any that has many legs among all the small creatures that crawl on the ground. These are detestable to you.

43 You must not make yourselves detestable by any crawling creature that moves along the ground, nor shall you defile yourselves by them, for doing so would make you unclean.

44 For I am the LORD your God. Therefore, you must consecrate yourselves and be holy, because I am holy. You must not defile yourselves with any kind of creature that crawls on the ground.

45 For I am the LORD who brought you out of the land of Egypt to be your God. You must therefore be holy, because I am holy.

46 This is the regulation concerning animals, birds, all living creatures that move in the waters, and every creature that crawls on the ground.

47 To distinguish between what is unclean and what is clean, and between the animals that are permissible to eat and those that are not.
