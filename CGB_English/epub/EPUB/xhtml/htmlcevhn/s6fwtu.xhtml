I Corinthians Chapter 12

1 Now, brothers and sisters, I want you to understand clearly about spiritual gifts.

2 You are aware that when you were Gentiles, you were led astray and drawn to worship mute idols, following whatever influence directed you.

3 Therefore, I want you to understand this clearly: no one who is speaking by the Spirit of God will ever say, "Jesus is cursed," and no one can sincerely declare, "Jesus is Lord," except by the power of the Holy Spirit.

4 Now there are different kinds of spiritual gifts, but they all come from the same Spirit.

5 There are various kinds of service, but the same Lord.

6 There are various kinds of activities, but it is the same God who empowers and works through all of them in everyone.

7 However, the visible expression of the Spirit is granted to each person for the common good.

8 To one person, the Spirit grants the ability to speak with profound wisdom; to another, the same Spirit gives the capacity to speak with deep understanding.

9 To one person, the same Spirit gives the gift of faith; to another, the same Spirit grants the ability to heal.

10 To one person is given the ability to perform miracles; to another, the gift of prophecy; to another, the ability to distinguish between spirits; to another, the capacity to speak in various kinds of languages; and to another, the ability to interpret those languages.

11 However, all these gifts are activated by one and the same Spirit, who distributes them to each person individually as he determines.

12 For just as the body is one whole, yet has many parts, and all the parts of that one body, though numerous, form a single body, so it is with Christ.

13 For through the work of one Spirit, we have all been united into one body—whether we are Jews or Gentiles, whether we are slaves or free—and we have all been given to partake of the same Spirit.

14 The body is not made up of a single part, but of many parts.

15 If the foot were to say, "Because I am not the hand, I do not belong to the body," would that mean it is no longer part of the body?

16 And if the ear were to say, "Because I am not the eye, I do not belong to the body," would that mean it is no longer part of the body?

17 If the entire body were just an eye, how would it hear? And if the whole body were only an ear, how would it smell?

18 But now God has arranged each of the members in the body exactly as he desired.

19 If the entire body were just one part, where would the rest of the body be?

20 Now, though there are many parts, they form one single body.

21 The eye cannot say to the hand, “I don’t need you,” nor can the head say to the feet, “I don’t need you.”

22 Indeed, those parts of the body that seem to be weaker are even more essential.

23 And those parts of the body that we consider less honorable, we bestow greater honor upon them; and our less presentable parts are treated with even greater care and dignity.

24 Our presentable parts do not require special attention, but God has arranged the body in such a way, giving greater honor to the parts that lacked it.

25 So that there should be no division within the body, but that its members should show equal concern and care for one another.

26 If one part of the body suffers, every part suffers alongside it; and if one part is honored, every part rejoices with it.

27 Now you are the body of Christ, and each one of you is a distinct part of it.

28 God has appointed various roles within the church: first, apostles; second, prophets; third, teachers; then those who perform miracles, those with gifts of healing, those who provide assistance, those who lead, and those who speak in different kinds of tongues.

29 Are all called to be apostles? Are all gifted as prophets? Are all appointed as teachers? Do all possess the ability to perform miracles?

30 Do all possess the ability to heal? Do all speak in different languages? Do all have the gift of interpreting them?

31 Eagerly desire the most valuable spiritual gifts, but I will show you a way that surpasses them all.
