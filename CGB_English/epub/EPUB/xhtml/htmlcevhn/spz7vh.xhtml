Genesis Chapter 39

1 Joseph was taken down to Egypt, where Potiphar, an official of Pharaoh and the captain of the guard, an Egyptian, purchased him from the Ishmaelites who had brought him there.

2 The LORD was with Joseph, and he became a successful man. He lived in the household of his Egyptian master.

3 And his master observed that the LORD was with him, and that the LORD caused everything he did to succeed in his care.

4 Joseph gained favor in his master’s eyes and became his personal attendant. His master appointed him as the manager of his household and entrusted everything he owned into Joseph’s care.

5 From the time that Joseph was appointed as overseer in his master’s household and given charge over everything he owned, the Lord blessed the Egyptian’s household because of Joseph. The Lord’s blessing rested on everything in his house and in his fields.

6 He entrusted everything he owned to Joseph’s care and did not concern himself with anything except the food he ate. Now Joseph was handsome in appearance and had a pleasing demeanor.

7 After these events, the wife of Joseph’s master began to look at him with desire. One day, she said to him, “Come, sleep with me.”

8 But he refused and said to his master’s wife, “Look, my master has entrusted everything in this house to me and has no concern about what happens here.

9 No one in this household holds greater authority than I do, and my master has withheld nothing from me except you, because you are his wife. How then could I commit such a terrible evil and sin against God?

10 And it happened that as she spoke to Joseph day after day, he refused to listen to her, to lie with her or to be in her presence.

11 At that time, Joseph went into the house to attend to his duties, and none of the other men of the household were inside.

12 She grabbed him by his clothing, saying, "Sleep with me." But he left his garment in her hand, fled, and escaped from her.

13 When she realized that he had left his cloak in her hand and had run outside,

14 She called to the men of her household and said to them, "Look, he has brought a Hebrew man here to humiliate us. He came in to me to sleep with me, and I cried out loudly."

15 When he heard me raise my voice and cry out, he left his garment with me, fled, and ran outside.

16 And she kept his garment with her until his master returned home.

17 And she spoke to him, saying these words: "The Hebrew servant you brought to us came to me to humiliate me."

18 And it happened that as I raised my voice and cried out, he left his garment with me and fled outside.

19 When his master heard the words his wife spoke to him, saying, "This is how your servant treated me," his anger burned intensely.

20 Joseph’s master took him and placed him in the prison, the place where the king’s prisoners were confined. And Joseph remained there in the prison.

21 But the LORD was with Joseph, extended kindness to him, and granted him favor in the eyes of the prison warden.

22 The prison warden entrusted Joseph with the responsibility of overseeing all the prisoners who were held in the prison. Whatever tasks needed to be done there, Joseph was the one who carried them out.

23 The prison warden did not concern himself with anything under Joseph's care, because the LORD was with Joseph and caused everything he did to succeed.
