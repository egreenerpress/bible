Hosea Chapter 7

1 When I sought to heal Israel, the sin of Ephraim was exposed, and the wickedness of Samaria was revealed. For they practice deceit; thieves break in, and bands of robbers raid in the open.

2 They do not reflect in their hearts that I remember all their evil deeds. Now their own actions have surrounded them; their sins are right before my eyes.

3 They bring joy to the king through their acts of wickedness, and they delight the rulers with their deceitful words.

4 They are all like adulterers, burning with desire like an oven stoked by the baker, who stops stirring the fire once the dough is kneaded, waiting only for it to rise.

5 On the day of our king's celebration, the leaders have made him ill by overindulging in wine; he has joined hands with mockers.

6 For their hearts are set like an oven, while they lie in ambush. Their baker sleeps through the night, but in the morning, it blazes like a raging fire.

7 They are all inflamed like a blazing oven, and they have consumed their rulers; all their kings have fallen. Not one among them calls out to me.

8 Ephraim has mingled himself among the nations; Ephraim is like a half-baked cake.

9 Foreigners have consumed his resources, and he is unaware of it. Indeed, signs of aging are appearing on him, yet he does not realize it.

10 The arrogance of Israel bears witness against them, yet they refuse to turn back to the LORD their God or seek Him, despite all that has happened.

11 Ephraim is like a senseless dove, lacking understanding: they cry out to Egypt for help, and then turn to Assyria for support.

12 When they go forth, I will cast my net over them; I will bring them down like birds of the sky; I will discipline them, just as their assembly has been warned.

13 How terrible for them! They have turned away from me. Destruction will come upon them because they have rebelled against me. Even though I rescued and redeemed them, they have spoken deceitfully about me.

14 Yet they have not called out to me sincerely from their hearts, even as they wail upon their beds. They gather together for grain and wine, but they turn away from me in rebellion.

15 Even though I have supported and strengthened their power, they still plot evil against me.

16 They turn back, but not to the Most High; they are like a faulty bow that fails to shoot true. Their leaders will fall by the sword because of their reckless words. This will be their shame in the land of Egypt.
