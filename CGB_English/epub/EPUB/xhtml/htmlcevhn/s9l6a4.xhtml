Song of Songs Chapter 2

1 I am the flower of the Sharon plain, the lily of the valleys.

2 Like a lily growing among thorns, so is my beloved among the young women.

3 Like an apple tree standing out among the trees of the forest, so is my beloved among all others. I sat beneath his shade with great joy, and his fruit was sweet to my taste.

4 He led me to the banquet hall, and his banner over me signified love.

5 Sustain me with raisin cakes, refresh me with apples, for I am faint with love.

6 His left hand supports my head, and his right hand holds me close.

7 I solemnly urge you, O daughters of Jerusalem, by the gazelles and the does of the field, do not disturb or awaken my beloved until he is ready.

8 Listen! It is the voice of my beloved! Look, he is coming, bounding over the mountains, leaping across the hills.

9 My beloved is like a gazelle or a young stag. Look, he stands behind our wall, gazing through the windows, peering through the lattice.

10 My beloved spoke and said to me, "Arise, my love, my beautiful one, and come with me."

11 Behold, the winter has ended, the rains have ceased and are gone.

12 The flowers are blooming across the land; the season of birdsong has arrived, and the call of the turtledove echoes through our countryside.

13 The fig tree is producing its early fruit, and the vines, with their delicate blossoms, are spreading their sweet fragrance. Rise up, my beloved, my beautiful one, and come away with me.

14 Oh my beloved, who hides in the crevices of the rock, in the sheltered places of the cliffs, let me see your face, let me hear your voice; for your voice is sweet, and your face is beautiful.

15 Capture for us the foxes, those small foxes that ruin the vineyards, for our vineyards are in bloom with delicate grapes.

16 My beloved is mine, and I am his; he grazes among the lilies.

17 Until the day dawns and the shadows disappear, turn to me, my beloved, and be swift like a gazelle or a young stag on the rugged mountains of Bether.
