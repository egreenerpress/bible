Genesis Chapter 11

1 At that time, the entire earth shared a single language and a common way of speaking.

2 As they traveled from the east, they came upon a plain in the land of Shinar and settled there.

3 Then they said to one another, "Come, let us make bricks and thoroughly bake them." So they used bricks instead of stone, and tar served as their mortar.

4 They said, "Come, let us build a city for ourselves and a tower that reaches to the heavens. Let us make a name for ourselves, so that we will not be scattered across the face of the whole earth."

5 The LORD descended to observe the city and the tower that the descendants of humanity had built.

6 The LORD said, "Look, the people are united as one, and they all speak the same language. This is just the beginning of what they will do; now nothing they plan to accomplish will be beyond their reach."

7 Come, let us go down and confuse their language, so that they will not be able to understand each other’s speech.

8 So the LORD dispersed them from that place across the entire earth, and they ceased building the city.

9 Therefore, it was named Babel, because there the LORD confused the language of the whole earth. And from that place, the LORD scattered them across the face of the entire earth.

10 This is the account of Shem's descendants: Shem was one hundred years old when he became the father of Arphaxad, two years after the flood.

11 After Shem became the father of Arphaxad, he lived for five hundred more years, during which he had other sons and daughters.

12 Arphaxad was thirty-five years old when he became the father of Salah.

13 After Arphaxad became the father of Salah, he lived for four hundred and three more years, and during that time, he had other sons and daughters.

14 Salah was thirty years old when he became the father of Eber.

15 After Salah became the father of Eber, he lived for four hundred and three more years, during which he had other sons and daughters.

16 Eber lived for thirty-four years and then became the father of Peleg.

17 After Eber became the father of Peleg, he lived for four hundred and thirty more years, during which he had other sons and daughters.

18 Peleg was thirty years old when he became the father of Reu.

19 After Peleg became the father of Reu, he lived for two hundred and nine more years, during which he had other sons and daughters.

20 Reu was thirty-two years old when he became the father of Serug.

21 After Reu became the father of Serug, he lived for another two hundred and seven years, during which he had more sons and daughters.

22 Serug was thirty years old when he became the father of Nahor.

23 After Serug became the father of Nahor, he lived for two hundred more years, during which he had other sons and daughters.

24 Nahor was twenty-nine years old when he became the father of Terah.

25 After Nahor became the father of Terah, he lived 119 more years and had other sons and daughters.

26 Terah was seventy years old when he became the father of Abram, Nahor, and Haran.

27 This is the account of Terah’s descendants: Terah was the father of Abram, Nahor, and Haran. Haran, in turn, became the father of Lot.

28 Haran died during his father Terah’s lifetime in the land where he was born, in Ur of the Chaldeans.

29 Abram and Nahor both took wives. Abram’s wife was named Sarai, and Nahor’s wife was named Milcah. Milcah was the daughter of Haran, who was not only her father but also the father of Iscah.

30 But Sarai was unable to conceive; she had no children.

31 Terah took his son Abram, his grandson Lot (the son of Haran), and Sarai, his daughter-in-law, who was Abram’s wife. They all set out together from Ur of the Chaldeans to travel to the land of Canaan. However, they arrived at Haran and settled there.

32 Terah lived a total of two hundred and five years, and he died in Haran.
