I Corinthians Chapter 4

1 Let people regard us in this way: as servants of Christ and entrusted stewards of God's sacred truths.

2 Furthermore, it is essential that those entrusted with responsibility prove themselves to be trustworthy.

3 It matters very little to me whether I am judged by you or by any human court. In fact, I do not even judge myself.

4 For I am not aware of any wrongdoing in myself, yet this does not make me innocent. It is the Lord who examines and judges me.

5 Therefore, do not pass judgment on anything prematurely, before the Lord comes. He will bring to light what is hidden in darkness and will reveal the secret intentions of the heart. At that time, each person will receive the praise that comes from God.

6 Brothers and sisters, I have applied these examples to myself and Apollos for your benefit, so that through us you might learn not to go beyond what is written in Scripture. This is to prevent any of you from becoming arrogant, favoring one person over another.

7 Who makes you different from anyone else? And what do you have that you did not receive? If you received it, why do you boast as though you did not?

8 Now you are satisfied, now you are wealthy, you have ruled as kings without us—and I wish it were true that you did reign, so that we might reign alongside you.

9 For I believe that God has displayed us, the apostles, as though we were last in line, destined for death. We have become a public spectacle to the world, to angels, and to humanity.

10 We may appear foolish for the sake of Christ, but you consider yourselves wise in Christ. We are seen as weak, while you are strong. You are held in honor, but we are treated with contempt.

11 Even up to this very moment, we endure hunger and thirst, lack proper clothing, face physical abuse, and have no permanent place to live.

12 We work hard, laboring with our own hands. When we are insulted, we respond with blessings; when we are persecuted, we endure it patiently.

13 When we are slandered, we respond with kindness. We are treated as the scum of the earth, the refuse of all things, even to this day.

14 I am not writing these things to embarrass you, but as a father who loves his children, I am giving you this warning.

15 Although you may have countless teachers in Christ, you do not have many fathers. For in Christ Jesus, I became your father through the gospel.

16 Therefore, I urge you to imitate me.

17 For this reason, I have sent Timothy to you—my beloved and faithful child in the Lord. He will remind you of my ways in Christ, just as I teach them in every church wherever I go.

18 Now some of you have become arrogant, as if I were not planning to come to you.

19 I will come to you soon, if the Lord wills, and I will not focus on the words of those who are arrogant, but on the power they demonstrate.

20 For the kingdom of God does not consist in mere words, but in genuine power.

21 What do you desire? Should I come to you with a rod of discipline, or with love and a spirit of gentleness?
