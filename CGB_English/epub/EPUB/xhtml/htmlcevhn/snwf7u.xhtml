Nehemiah Chapter 2

1 In the month of Nisan, during the twentieth year of King Artaxerxes’ reign, wine was set before him. I took the wine and presented it to the king. Up to that time, I had never appeared sorrowful in his presence.

2 So the king said to me, "Why do you look so sad, since you are not sick? This can only be the result of a troubled heart." At this, I became deeply afraid.

3 He said to the king, "May the king live forever! Why should I not look sad when the city, the place where my ancestors are buried, lies in ruins, and its gates have been destroyed by fire?"

4 Then the king asked me, "What is it that you are seeking?" At that moment, I prayed to the God of heaven.

5 I said to the king, "If it pleases the king, and if your servant has found favor in your sight, I ask that you send me to Judah, to the city where my ancestors are buried, so that I may rebuild it."

6 The king said to me (with the queen seated beside him), "How long will your journey take, and when will you return?" It pleased the king to send me, so I gave him a specific time for my return.

7 I also said to the king, "If it pleases the king, may letters be granted to me for the governors of the region beyond the Euphrates River, so that they will allow me safe passage until I arrive in Judah."

8 I also wrote a letter to Asaph, the keeper of the king’s forest, requesting that he provide me with timber to make beams for the gates of the palace that belongs to the temple, for the city wall, and for the house I will occupy. The king granted my request, for the gracious hand of my God was upon me.

9 Then I arrived at the governors of the region beyond the Euphrates River and delivered to them the letters from the king. The king had also assigned army officers and cavalry to accompany me.

10 When Sanballat the Horonite and Tobiah the Ammonite official heard about it, they were deeply disturbed and greatly displeased that someone had come to promote the well-being of the people of Israel.

11 So I arrived in Jerusalem and stayed there for three days.

12 I got up during the night, accompanied by a few men, but I did not inform anyone about what my God had placed in my heart to accomplish in Jerusalem. I took no animals with me except the one I was riding.

13 So I went out at night through the Valley Gate, passing by the Dragon Spring and continuing to the Dung Gate. I inspected the walls of Jerusalem, which were in ruins, and its gates, which had been destroyed by fire.

14 Then I proceeded to the Fountain Gate and the King’s Pool, but there was no space for the animal I was riding to pass through.

15 Then I went up during the night along the stream and inspected the wall. Afterward, I turned back, entered through the Valley Gate, and returned to my starting point.

16 The officials did not know where I had gone or what I was doing, for I had not yet informed the Jewish leaders, the priests, the nobles, the officials, or any of the others who were involved in the work.

17 Then I said to them, "You see the trouble we are in—how Jerusalem lies in ruins, and its gates have been destroyed by fire. Come, let us rebuild the wall of Jerusalem, so that we will no longer be a disgrace."

18 Then I explained to them how the gracious hand of my God had been upon me, and I also shared the words that the king had spoken to me. They responded, "Let us rise up and begin building." So they prepared themselves and committed to this noble task.

19 But when Sanballat the Horonite, Tobiah the Ammonite official, and Geshem the Arab heard about it, they mocked and ridiculed us, showing utter contempt. They said, "What is this you are doing? Are you rebelling against the king?"

20 I responded to them, saying, "The God of heaven will grant us success; therefore, we, His servants, will rise up and rebuild. But you have no share, no claim, and no remembrance in Jerusalem."
