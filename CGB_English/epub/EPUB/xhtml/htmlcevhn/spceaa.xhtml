Ecclesiastes Chapter 1

1 The words of the Teacher, the son of David, who ruled as king in Jerusalem.

2 "Utterly meaningless," says the Teacher, "completely meaningless! Everything is meaningless."

3 What benefit does a person gain from all their hard work under the sun?

4 One generation passes away, and another generation arises, but the earth endures forever.

5 The sun rises, and the sun sets, then hurries back to the place where it rises again.

6 The wind blows toward the south, then circles around to the north; it swirls around endlessly, and the wind returns once more along its appointed paths.

7 All the rivers flow into the sea, yet the sea is never filled. To the place from which the rivers originate, there they return once more.

8 Everything is filled with wearisome toil; no one can fully express it. The eye is never satisfied with what it sees, nor is the ear ever content with what it hears.

9 What has happened before will happen again, and what has been done will be done once more. There is nothing truly new under the sun.

10 Is there anything about which one might say, "Look, this is new"? It has already existed in ages past, long before our time.

11 People do not remember the events of the past, nor will future generations remember what is yet to happen, even when it comes to pass.

12 I, the Teacher, was king over Israel in Jerusalem.

13 I devoted myself to seeking and exploring through wisdom everything that is done under heaven. This heavy burden God has given to humanity to occupy themselves with.

14 I have observed all the activities that take place under the sun, and indeed, everything is meaningless and a chasing after the wind.

15 What is bent cannot be straightened, and what is missing cannot be counted.

16 I reflected deeply within myself, saying, "Look, I have attained great wealth and have gained more wisdom than all who were before me in Jerusalem. Indeed, my heart has experienced much wisdom and knowledge."

18 For with great wisdom comes great sorrow, and whoever gains more knowledge also gains more grief.
