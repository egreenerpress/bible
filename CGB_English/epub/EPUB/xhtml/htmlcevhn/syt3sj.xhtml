II Kings Chapter 21

1 Manasseh was twelve years old when he became king, and he ruled for fifty-five years in Jerusalem. His mother’s name was Hephzibah.

2 He committed what was evil in the sight of the LORD, following the detestable practices of the nations that the LORD had driven out before the Israelites.

3 He rebuilt the high places that his father Hezekiah had demolished; he erected altars for Baal and made a sacred pole, just as Ahab, king of Israel, had done. He also worshiped and served all the heavenly host.

4 He constructed altars within the house of the LORD, even though the LORD had declared, "In Jerusalem I will establish my name."

5 He constructed altars dedicated to all the heavenly bodies in both courtyards of the house of the LORD.

6 He caused his son to pass through the fire, practiced divination, sought omens, and consulted mediums and spiritists. He committed great evil in the sight of the LORD, provoking Him to anger.

7 He placed a carved image of Asherah, which he had made, in the house of the Lord, the very house about which the Lord had said to David and to Solomon his son, "In this house and in Jerusalem, which I have chosen out of all the tribes of Israel, I will establish my name forever."

8 I will no longer allow the people of Israel to be driven out of the land I gave to their ancestors, provided they diligently obey everything I have commanded them and faithfully follow all the laws that my servant Moses gave to them.

9 However, they did not listen, and Manasseh led them astray, causing them to commit even greater evil than the nations whom the LORD had destroyed before the people of Israel.

10 The LORD communicated through his servants, the prophets, saying,

11 Because Manasseh, king of Judah, has committed these detestable acts and has acted more wickedly than all the Amorites who lived before him, he has also led Judah into sin by causing them to worship his idols.

12 Therefore, this is what the LORD, the God of Israel, declares: Listen carefully, I am about to bring such disaster upon Jerusalem and Judah that anyone who hears of it will be utterly shocked and horrified.

13 I will measure Jerusalem with the same standard I used for Samaria and the same plumb line I applied to the house of Ahab. I will cleanse Jerusalem as one cleans a dish—thoroughly scrubbing it and then turning it upside down to dry.

14 I will abandon the remaining portion of my inheritance and hand them over to their enemies. They will become plunder and spoil for all their foes.

15 Because they have done what is evil in my eyes and have continually provoked me to anger, from the day their ancestors came out of Egypt up to this very day.

16 Furthermore, Manasseh spilled a great amount of innocent blood, so much that he filled Jerusalem from one end to the other. In addition to this, he led Judah into sin by committing what was evil in the sight of the LORD.

17 The remaining deeds of Manasseh, all that he accomplished, and the sins he committed—are they not recorded in the historical records of the kings of Judah?

18 Manasseh died and was buried in the garden of his own house, in the garden of Uzza. His son Amon succeeded him as king.

19 Amon was twenty-two years old when he became king, and he ruled for two years in Jerusalem. His mother’s name was Meshullemeth, the daughter of Haruz from Jotbah.

20 He committed actions that were wicked in the eyes of the LORD, following the same path of wrongdoing as his father Manasseh had done.

21 He followed the same path as his father, serving the same idols his father had served and bowing down in worship to them.

22 He abandoned the LORD, the God of his ancestors, and did not follow the ways of the LORD.

23 The officials of Amon plotted against him and killed the king in his own palace.

24 The people of the land killed all those who had conspired against King Amon. Then, the people of the land appointed his son Josiah as king in his place.

25 The remaining deeds of Amon and all that he accomplished, are they not recorded in the historical records of the kings of Judah?

26 He was buried in his tomb in the garden of Uzza, and his son Josiah became king in his place.
