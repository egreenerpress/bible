Exodus Chapter 4

1 Moses responded, saying, "But look, they will not believe me or listen to what I say, for they will claim, 'The LORD has not appeared to you.'"

2 The LORD asked him, "What is that in your hand?" He replied, "A staff."

3 And he said, "Throw it on the ground." So he threw it on the ground, and it turned into a snake. Moses fled from it in fear.

4 The LORD said to Moses, "Reach out your hand and grasp it by the tail." So Moses reached out his hand, took hold of it, and it turned back into a staff in his hand.

5 So that they may believe that the LORD, the God of their ancestors—the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob—has truly revealed himself to you.

6 The LORD then said to him further, "Now put your hand inside your robe." So he placed his hand inside his robe, and when he pulled it out, his hand was diseased with leprosy, as white as snow.

7 Then God said, "Put your hand back inside your cloak." So Moses put his hand back inside his cloak, and when he pulled it out, it was restored, as healthy as the rest of his skin.

8 And it will happen that if they do not believe you or listen to the message of the first sign, then they will believe the message of the second sign.

9 If they still refuse to believe these two signs or listen to your voice, then you shall take water from the river and pour it on the dry ground. The water you take from the river will turn into blood when it touches the dry land.

10 Moses said to the LORD, "O Lord, I am not a skilled speaker, neither in the past nor since You have spoken to Your servant. I am slow in speech and struggle to express myself clearly."

11 The LORD said to him, "Who is it that gives humans the ability to speak? Who makes someone mute or deaf, able to see or blind? Is it not I, the LORD?"

12 Now go, and I will be with you as you speak. I will instruct you in what to say.

13 And he said, "O my Lord, please send the message by the hand of the one you choose to send."

14 The LORD’s anger burned against Moses, and He said, “Isn’t Aaron the Levite your brother? I know that he speaks well. In fact, he is already on his way to meet you, and when he sees you, his heart will be filled with joy."

15 You shall speak to him and place the words in his mouth. I will be with your mouth and with his mouth, and I will instruct you both in what you are to do.

16 He will speak to the people on your behalf. Indeed, he will act as your mouthpiece, and you will serve as his guide, like God to him.

17 Take this staff in your hand, for with it you will perform miraculous signs.

18 Moses went back to Jethro, his father-in-law, and said to him, "Please allow me to return to my brothers in Egypt to see if they are still alive." Jethro replied to Moses, "Go in peace."

19 The LORD said to Moses in Midian, "Go, return to Egypt, for all the men who sought to take your life have died."

20 Moses took his wife and his sons, placed them on a donkey, and began his journey back to the land of Egypt. He carried the staff of God in his hand as he went.

21 The LORD said to Moses, "When you go back to Egypt, make sure you perform all the miraculous signs I have given you the power to do, and show them to Pharaoh. But I will harden his heart, so that he will refuse to let the people go."

22 And you shall say to Pharaoh, "This is what the LORD declares: Israel is my son, my firstborn."

23 I declare to you: Release my son so that he may serve me. But if you refuse to let him go, know this—I will strike down your son, your firstborn.

24 As they were traveling and stopped at a lodging place, the LORD confronted him and intended to put him to death.

25 Then Zipporah took a sharp flint, cut off her son’s foreskin, and threw it at Moses’ feet, saying, “You are truly a bridegroom of blood to me!”

26 So he released him. Then she said, "You are a bridegroom of blood to me, because of the circumcision."

27 The LORD instructed Aaron, "Go into the wilderness to meet Moses." So Aaron went and encountered him at the mountain of God, where he greeted him with a kiss.

28 Moses conveyed to Aaron everything the LORD had spoken to him, including all the instructions and the miraculous signs he had been commanded to perform.

29 Moses and Aaron went and assembled all the elders of the Israelites.

30 Aaron spoke all the words that the LORD had given to Moses and performed the signs in the presence of the people.

31 The people believed, and when they heard that the LORD had taken notice of the Israelites and had seen their suffering, they bowed their heads and worshiped.
