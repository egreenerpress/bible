Psalms Chapter 132

1 (A song for pilgrims ascending to Jerusalem.) O LORD, remember David and all the hardships he endured.

2 How he made a solemn oath to the LORD and pledged a vow to the mighty God of Jacob.

3 Certainly, I will not enter my house or lie down on my bed;

4 I will not allow my eyes to rest or grant my eyelids the comfort of sleep.

5 Until I find a dwelling place for the LORD, a sanctuary for the mighty God of Jacob.

6 Behold, we heard of it in Ephrathah; we discovered it in the fields of the forest.

7 We will enter His dwelling place; we will bow down in reverence at the place where His feet rest.

8 Rise up, O LORD, and enter your place of restâ€”you and the ark of your mighty power.

9 May your priests be adorned with integrity, and may your faithful ones rejoice with exuberant joy.

10 For the sake of your servant David, do not reject or turn away from your anointed one.

11 The LORD has made a solemn and truthful promise to David, and He will not go back on it: "I will establish one of your own descendants to sit upon your throne."

12 If your descendants faithfully uphold my covenant and the instructions I will teach them, then their children will also reign on your throne forever.

13 The LORD has chosen Zion; he has desired it as his dwelling place.

14 "This is my resting place forever; here I will reside, for I have chosen it."

15 I will richly bless her with abundant provisions; I will fully satisfy her needy with bread.

16 I will also dress her priests in deliverance, and her faithful ones will sing out with great joy.

17 There I will cause the strength of David to flourish; I have prepared a lasting light for my anointed one.

18 I will clothe his enemies with disgrace, but his own crown will shine brightly.
