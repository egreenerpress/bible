Deuteronomy Chapter 34

1 Moses ascended from the plains of Moab to Mount Nebo, to the summit of Pisgah, which faces Jericho. There, the LORD showed him the entire land of Gilead, extending as far as Dan.

2 All the territory of Naphtali, along with the land of Ephraim and Manasseh, and the entire land of Judah, extending as far as the western sea.

3 And the southern region, along with the plain of the valley of Jericho, the city known for its palm trees, extending as far as Zoar.

4 The LORD said to him, "This is the land I promised with an oath to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, declaring, 'I will give it to your descendants.' I have allowed you to see it with your own eyes, but you will not cross over into it."

5 So Moses, the servant of the LORD, died there in the land of Moab, as the LORD had declared.

6 And he buried him in a valley in the land of Moab, near Beth-peor, but to this day, no one knows the location of his grave.

7 Moses was one hundred and twenty years old when he died. His eyesight had not weakened, nor had his vitality diminished.

8 The Israelites mourned for Moses in the plains of Moab for thirty days. After this, the period of weeping and mourning for Moses came to an end.

9 Joshua, the son of Nun, was filled with the spirit of wisdom because Moses had laid his hands on him. The Israelites listened to Joshua and obeyed him, doing as the LORD had commanded through Moses.

10 Since that time, no prophet has arisen in Israel like Moses, whom the Lord knew intimately, speaking with him directly, face to face.

11 In all the miraculous signs and extraordinary acts of power that the LORD commanded him to perform in the land of Egypt—against Pharaoh, all his officials, and the entire land—

12 And through all the acts of that mighty power, and through all the great and awe-inspiring deeds that Moses performed in the presence of all Israel.
