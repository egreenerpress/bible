James Chapter 2

1 My fellow believers, do not hold the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ, the glorious Lord, while showing favoritism or partiality toward others.

2 If someone comes into your gathering wearing a gold ring and fine clothing, and a poor person enters in shabby, worn-out clothes,

3 And you show favor to the one wearing fine clothes, saying to him, "Sit here in this honorable place," but to the poor person you say, "Stand over there," or, "Sit here at my feet."

4 Are you not showing favoritism among yourselves, and acting as judges with corrupt motives?

5 Listen carefully, my dear brothers and sisters: Has not God chosen those who are poor in the eyes of the world to be rich in faith and to inherit the kingdom He has promised to those who love Him?

6 But you have shown contempt for the poor. Do not the wealthy oppress you and drag you into court?

7 Do they not slander the honorable name by which you are called?

8 If you obey the supreme law as it is written in Scripture, "You shall love your neighbor as yourself," you are doing what is right.

9 However, if you show favoritism or partiality, you are committing sin and are found guilty by the law as lawbreakers.

10 For whoever keeps the entire law but fails in just one point is guilty of breaking all of it.

11 The one who said, "You shall not commit adultery," also said, "You shall not murder." So if you do not commit adultery but do commit murder, you have still become a lawbreaker.

12 Speak and act in a manner consistent with those who will be judged by the law that brings freedom.

13 For those who have shown no mercy will face judgment without mercy; but mercy triumphs over judgment.

14 What good is it, my brothers and sisters, if someone claims to have faith but does not demonstrate it through actions? Can such faith truly save them?

15 If a fellow believer is without clothing or lacks daily food,

16 If one of you says to them, "Go in peace, stay warm, and be well-fed," but does not provide them with what they truly need for their body, what good does that do?

17 In the same way, faith by itself, if it does not have actions to accompany it, is lifeless and ineffective.

18 Indeed, someone might argue, "You have faith, and I have deeds." Show me your faith without any actions, and I will demonstrate my faith through my deeds.

19 You believe that there is one God; that is good. Even the demons believe this, and they shudder in fear.

20 Do you not understand, O misguided person, that faith without corresponding actions is lifeless and ineffective?

21 Was not Abraham, our ancestor, declared righteous by his actions when he offered his son Isaac on the altar?

22 Do you see how faith was active together with his actions, and through those actions, faith was brought to its full expression?

23 The scripture was fulfilled, which says, "Abraham believed God, and it was credited to him as righteousness," and he was called the friend of God.

24 Therefore, you can see that a person is considered righteous by their actions, and not by faith alone.

25 In the same way, was not Rahab the prostitute considered righteous because of her actions when she welcomed the messengers and sent them away by a different route?

26 Just as a body without breath is lifeless, so too is faith without actions devoid of true vitality.
