Psalms Chapter 68

1 (For the director of music. A psalm or song of David.) May God rise up, and may His enemies be scattered; may those who hate Him flee in terror before His presence.

2 Just as smoke is scattered by the wind, scatter them away; as wax melts before the fire, so let the wicked be destroyed in the presence of God.

3 But may the righteous be filled with joy; may they celebrate in the presence of God; indeed, may they rejoice with great gladness.

4 Sing to God, offer praises to his name; honor the one who rides across the heavens by his name, JAH, and celebrate joyfully in his presence.

5 God, in His sacred dwelling, is a protector of the fatherless and a defender of widows.

6 God places the lonely in families; he leads out those who are bound in chains to freedom; but the rebellious live in a desolate land.

7 O God, when You went out before Your people, when You led them through the wilderness; pause and reflect.

8 The earth trembled, and the heavens poured down rain at the presence of God; even Mount Sinai itself quaked before the presence of God, the God of Israel.

9 You, O God, sent abundant rain; by it You strengthened Your inheritance when it was exhausted.

10 Your people have made their home there; you, O God, in your kindness, have provided for those in need.

11 The Lord delivered the message, and a vast multitude proclaimed it.

12 The kings of armies fled in haste, and those who remained at home shared in the spoils.

13 Even though you have lived among the ashes and ruins, you will become like the wings of a dove, radiant with silver, and its feathers gleaming with the brilliance of gold.

14 When the Almighty dispersed kings in that place, it became as white as snow on Mount Zalmon.

15 The mountain of God is like the mountains of Bashan—a majestic mountain, towering like the peaks of Bashan.

16 Why do you exalt yourselves, you lofty mountains? This is the mountain that God has chosen to make His dwelling; indeed, the LORD will reside there forever.

17 The chariots of God are twenty thousand, even thousands upon thousands of angels; the Lord is present among them, as he was at Sinai, in the holy sanctuary.

18 You have ascended to the heights; you have taken captives as your prisoners. You have received gifts to give to people, even to those who were rebellious, so that the LORD God might dwell among them.

19 Praise be to the Lord, who daily provides us with abundant blessings, the God who saves us. Selah.

20 The God who is ours is the God of deliverance; to the LORD, the Sovereign One, belong the ways of escape from death.

21 But God will strike the head of His enemies, crushing the skull of those who persist in their wickedness.

22 The Lord declared, "I will bring them back from Bashan; I will lead my people once more from the depths of the sea."

23 May your feet tread in the blood of your enemies, and may the tongues of your dogs lap it up as well.

24 They have witnessed your majestic presence, O God—the holy processions of my God, my King, within the sanctuary.

25 The singers led the procession, followed by those playing musical instruments; among them were the young women playing tambourines.

26 Praise God in the assemblies, even the Lord, you who are descendants of Israel’s source.

27 Little Benjamin leads the way, with their leader, the rulers of Judah and their assembly, the leaders of Zebulun, and the leaders of Naphtali.

28 Your God has decreed your power; O God, confirm and strengthen what you have accomplished for us.

29 Because of your holy temple in Jerusalem, kings will bring gifts to honor you.

30 Reprimand the assembly of warriors, the throng of mighty bulls, along with the young bulls of the nations, until each one humbly offers tribute with pieces of silver. Scatter the people who take pleasure in war.

31 Rulers will emerge from Egypt; the people of Ethiopia will eagerly extend their hands in worship to God.

32 Sing to God, all you nations of the earth; lift your voices in praise to the Lord. Pause and reflect on this.

33 To the One who rides across the ancient heavens, the highest heavens—behold, He sends forth His voice, a voice of great power.

34 Give honor and power to God, for His majesty reigns over Israel, and His mighty power is displayed in the heavens.

35 O God, you inspire awe from your holy sanctuaries; the God of Israel is the one who grants strength and power to his people. Blessed be God.
