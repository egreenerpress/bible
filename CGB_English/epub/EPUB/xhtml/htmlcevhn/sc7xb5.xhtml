Nehemiah Chapter 10

1 The individuals who sealed the document were Nehemiah, the governor, son of Hachaliah, and Zedekiah.

2 Seraiah, Azariah, Jeremiah,

3 Pashur, Amariah, Malchijah.

4 Hattush, Shebaniah, Malluch,

5 Harim, Meremoth, Obadiah,

6 Daniel, Ginnethon, Baruch,

7 Meshullam, Abijah, Mijamin.

8 Maaziah, Bilgai, and Shemaiah: these were the priests.

9 And the Levites: Jeshua son of Azaniah, Binnui from the descendants of Henadad, and Kadmiel.

10 And their relatives—Shebaniah, Hodijah, Kelita, Pelaiah, Hanan—

11 Micha, Rehob, Hashabiah,

12 Zaccur, Sherebiah, and Shebaniah.

13 Hodijah, Bani, Beninu.

14 The leaders of the people: Parosh, Pahath-moab, Elam, Zattu, Bani,

15 Bunni, Azgad, Bebai,

16 Adonijah, Bigvai, Adin,

17 Ater, Hizkijah, Azzur,

18 Hodijah, Hashum, and Bezai.

19 Hariph, Anathoth, Nebai.

20 Magpiash, Meshullam, Hezir.

21 Meshezabeel, Zadok, and Jaddua.

22 Pelatiah, Hanan, Anaiah,

23 Hoshea, Hananiah, Hashub.

24 Hallohesh, Pileha, Shobek,

25 Rehum, Hashabnah, and Maaseiah.

26 And Ahijah, Hanan, and Anan,

27 Malluch, Harim, Baanah.

28 The rest of the people—the priests, the Levites, the gatekeepers, the singers, the temple servants, and all who had separated themselves from the peoples of the surrounding lands to devote themselves to the law of God—along with their wives, their sons, and their daughters, all who were capable of understanding and discerning,

29 They joined together with their fellow Israelites, their leaders, and solemnly bound themselves with a curse and an oath to follow the law of God, which had been given through Moses, the servant of God. They committed to obey and carry out all the commandments, decrees, and statutes of the LORD our God.

30 We resolved not to give our daughters in marriage to the people of the land, nor to take their daughters as wives for our sons.

31 If the people of the land bring goods or any food to sell on the Sabbath day, we will not buy from them on the Sabbath or on any holy day. Furthermore, we will let the land rest during the seventh year and cancel all debts as required.

32 We also established regulations for ourselves, requiring that each year we contribute a third of a shekel for the upkeep and service of the house of our God.

33 For the bread of the Presence, the regular grain offering, the regular burnt offering, the offerings for the Sabbaths, the new moons, the appointed festivals, the sacred offerings, the sin offerings to make atonement for Israel, and for all the work of the house of our God.

34 We drew lots among the priests, the Levites, and the people to determine who would bring the wood offering into the house of our God, according to our ancestral families, at the appointed times each year. This wood was to be burned on the altar of the LORD our God, as it is prescribed in the law.

35 And to bring the first produce of our land, and the first harvest of every kind of fruit from all our trees, year after year, to the house of the LORD.

36 We also bring the firstborn of our sons and our livestock, as it is written in the Law, along with the firstborn of our herds and flocks, to present them at the house of our God, to the priests who serve in the house of our God.

37 We are to bring the first portion of our dough, our offerings, and the produce of all kinds of trees—wine and oil—to the priests in the storerooms of the house of our God. Additionally, we are to give the tithes from our land to the Levites, so that the Levites may receive the tithes in all the towns where we work the land.

38 The priest, a descendant of Aaron, shall oversee the Levites when they collect the tithes. The Levites, in turn, shall bring a tenth of the tithes they receive to the house of our God, placing it in the storerooms of the treasury.

39 The people of Israel and the descendants of Levi shall bring the offerings of grain, new wine, and olive oil to the storerooms where the sacred vessels are kept, along with the priests who serve, the gatekeepers, and the singers. We will not neglect the house of our God.
