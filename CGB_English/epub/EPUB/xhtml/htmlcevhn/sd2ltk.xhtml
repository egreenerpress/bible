Isaiah Chapter 38

1 During that time, Hezekiah became gravely ill and was at the point of death. The prophet Isaiah, son of Amoz, came to him and said, "This is what the LORD says: Put your affairs in order, for you will not recover; you are going to die."

2 Then Hezekiah turned his face toward the wall and prayed to the LORD, saying,

3 He said, "Please, LORD, remember now how I have lived before you with integrity and a sincere heart, and have done what is good in your sight." And Hezekiah wept bitterly.

4 Then the word of the LORD came to Isaiah, saying,

5 Go and tell Hezekiah this message from the LORD, the God of your ancestor David: I have heard your prayer, and I have seen your tears. Listen carefully: I will add fifteen years to your life.

6 I will rescue you and this city from the power of the king of Assyria, and I will protect this city.

7 And this will serve as a sign to you from the LORD, confirming that the LORD will fulfill what He has promised.

8 Listen carefully: I will cause the shadow on the sundial of Ahaz, which has already moved forward, to go back ten steps. And so, the sun did indeed move backward ten steps, reversing the direction it had previously gone.

9 This is the written account of Hezekiah, king of Judah, when he fell ill and then recovered from his sickness.

10 I declared, "In the midst of my life being cut short, I will enter the gates of the grave; I am being robbed of the remaining years of my life."

11 I said, "I will no longer see the LORD, the LORD Himself, in the land of the living. I will no longer look upon humanity or dwell among those who inhabit the world."

12 My life is fading away and is being taken from me like a shepherd's tent that is dismantled. I have cut off my life like a weaver cuts the thread from the loom. With a wasting illness, God will bring me to an end. From morning until evening, you will bring my life to its conclusion.

13 I thought to myself until morning, "Like a lion, he will crush all my bones; from day to night, you will bring me to an end."

14 Like a crane or a swallow, I cried out; I moaned like a dove. My eyes grow weary as I look upward. O LORD, I am overwhelmed; come to my aid.

15 What can I say? He has both spoken to me and carried out his word. I will walk humbly throughout all my years, bearing the sorrow of my soul.

16 O Lord, it is through these things that people find life, and in all these things lies the vitality of my spirit. Therefore, you will restore me and grant me life.

17 Truly, I experienced deep anguish despite seeking peace, but in your steadfast love for me, you rescued my life from the pit of destruction. You have taken all my sins and cast them far away, out of your sight.

18 The grave cannot offer you praise, nor can death honor you. Those who descend into the pit cannot hold onto the hope of your faithfulness.

19 The living—yes, the living—they will praise you, just as I do today; fathers will tell their children about your faithfulness.

20 The LORD was prepared to deliver me; therefore, we will sing my songs with stringed instruments throughout all the days of our lives in the house of the LORD.

21 For Isaiah had instructed, "Take a lump of figs and apply it as a poultice to the boil, and he will be healed."

22 Hezekiah also asked, "What will be the sign that I will go up to the house of the LORD?"
