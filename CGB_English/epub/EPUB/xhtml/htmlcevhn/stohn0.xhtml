Genesis Chapter 15

1 After these events, the word of the LORD came to Abram in a vision, saying, "Do not be afraid, Abram. I am your protector, and your exceedingly great reward."

2 Abram said, "Lord God, what will you give me, since I remain childless, and the heir of my household is Eliezer of Damascus?"

3 And Abram said, "Look, you have given me no children, and so a servant born in my household will be my heir."

4 Suddenly, the word of the LORD came to him, saying, "This man will not be your heir; instead, the one who will come from your own body will be your heir."

5 Then God brought him outside and said, "Look up at the sky and count the stars, if you are able to number them." And He said to him, "So shall your descendants be."

6 And he placed his trust in the LORD, and the LORD credited it to him as righteousness.

7 And He said to him, "I am the LORD, who brought you out of Ur of the Chaldeans, to give you this land as your inheritance."

8 And he said, "Lord God, how can I be certain that I will possess it?"

9 And He said to him, "Bring me a three-year-old heifer, a three-year-old female goat, a three-year-old ram, a turtledove, and a young pigeon."

10 He took all these animals and birds, cut them in half, and arranged the pieces opposite each other. However, he did not divide the birds.

11 When the birds of prey descended upon the carcasses, Abram drove them away.

12 As the sun was setting, a deep sleep came over Abram, and suddenly, a profound and terrifying darkness enveloped him.

13 And God said to Abram, "You can be certain that your descendants will live as foreigners in a land that does not belong to them. They will be enslaved and oppressed for four hundred years."

14 I will also judge the nation that they serve, and afterward, they will come out with abundant possessions.

15 You will join your ancestors in peace and be buried at a ripe old age.

16 However, in the fourth generation, they will return here, for the wickedness of the Amorites has not yet reached its full measure.

17 As the sun set and darkness fell, a smoking firepot and a flaming torch appeared, moving between the pieces.

18 On that very day, the LORD established a covenant with Abram, declaring, "To your descendants I have given this land, from the river of Egypt to the great river, the Euphrates."

19 The Kenites, the Kenizzites, and the Kadmonites.

20 The Hittites, the Perizzites, and the Rephaim.

21 The Amorites, the Canaanites, the Girgashites, and the Jebusites.
