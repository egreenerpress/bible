Isaiah Chapter 21

1 The prophecy concerning the wilderness by the sea: Like whirlwinds sweeping through the southern desert, it comes from a desolate and fearsome land.

2 A distressing vision has been revealed to me: the betrayer acts deceitfully, and the destroyer brings devastation. Advance, O Elam; lay siege, O Media! I have brought an end to all their groaning.

3 Therefore, my body is filled with anguish, and sharp pains have seized me, like the pains of a woman in labor. I was overwhelmed when I heard it; I was terrified when I saw it.

4 My heart races, and terror overwhelms me; the night that was meant for my joy, He has turned into a time of dread for me.

5 Set the table, keep watch from the lookout post, eat and drink. Rise up, you leaders, and prepare the shields for battle.

6 For this is what the Lord has said to me: "Go, appoint a watchman, and let him report what he sees."

7 He saw a chariot drawn by a pair of horses, a chariot pulled by donkeys, and a chariot led by camels. He listened intently, paying close attention.

8 He cried out, "A lion! My lord, I stand continually on the watchtower during the day, and I remain at my post throughout the night."

9 Look, here comes a chariot with men, accompanied by two horsemen. Then he proclaimed, saying, "Babylon has fallen, has fallen! All the carved images of her gods have been shattered and thrown to the ground."

10 O you whom I have threshed, and the grain of my threshing floor: what I have heard from the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel, I have made known to you.

11 This is the prophecy concerning Dumah. A voice calls to me from Seir, saying, "Watchman, what is left of the night? Watchman, what is left of the night?"

12 The watchman declared, "The dawn is coming, and the night as well. If you wish to seek understanding, then seek earnestly. Turn back, come closer."

13 The prophecy concerning Arabia. In the thickets of Arabia you will spend the night, O caravans of Dedanites.

14 The people of the land of Tema provided water to those who were thirsty; they met the needs of the fugitives by offering them bread.

15 For they fled from the threat of swords, from the sharpened blade, from the drawn bow, and from the relentless horrors of war.

16 For this is what the Lord has said to me: Within one year, measured as a hired worker would count it, all the splendor of Kedar will come to an end.

17 The remaining number of archers, the mighty warriors of the descendants of Kedar, will be reduced in number, for the LORD, the God of Israel, has declared it.
