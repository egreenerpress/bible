Jeremiah Chapter 20

1 Now Pashur, the son of Immer, who was a priest and the chief officer in the house of the LORD, heard that Jeremiah had spoken these prophecies.

2 Then Pashur struck Jeremiah the prophet and placed him in the stocks located at the Upper Gate of Benjamin, near the house of the LORD.

3 The next day, Pashur released Jeremiah from the stocks. Then Jeremiah said to him, "The LORD has not named you Pashur, but rather 'Terror on Every Side.'"

4 For this is what the LORD says: I will make you a source of horror to yourself and to all your friends. They will fall by the sword of their enemies, and you will witness it with your own eyes. I will hand over all of Judah to the power of the king of Babylon. He will take them captive to Babylon and put them to death by the sword.

5 Furthermore, I will hand over all the resources of this city, all its toil, and all its valuable possessions. I will also deliver all the treasures of the kings of Judah into the hands of their enemies. They will plunder them, seize them, and carry them off to Babylon.

6 And you, Pashhur, along with all who live in your household, will be taken into captivity. You will go to Babylon, and there you will die and be buriedâ€”you and all your friends to whom you have prophesied deceit.

7 O LORD, you have misled me, and I allowed myself to be misled. You are stronger than I am, and you have overcome me. I am ridiculed every day; everyone mocks me.

8 Since I began to speak, I have cried out, I have shouted about violence and destruction; for the word of the LORD has become a source of reproach and mockery to me, every single day.

9 Then I declared, "I will no longer speak of him or mention his name again." But his message was like a blazing fire within my heart, trapped in my bones. I grew exhausted from holding it back, and I could no longer contain it.

10 For I have heard the slander of many, terror on every side. They say, "Denounce him! Let us denounce him!" All my close friends watch for my stumbling, saying, "Perhaps he can be lured into error, and we will overpower him; then we will take our revenge on him."

11 But the LORD stands with me like a mighty and fearsome warrior; therefore, those who persecute me will stumble and will not succeed. They will be utterly disgraced, for they will not prosper. Their eternal shame will never be forgotten.

12 But, O LORD of all power, who examines the righteous and sees the innermost thoughts and intentions of the heart, let me witness your judgment upon them; for I have laid my case before you.

13 Sing to the LORD, give praise to the LORD, for He has rescued the life of the needy from the grasp of the wicked.

14 May the day of my birth be cursed, and may the day my mother gave birth to me never be blessed.

15 May that man be cursed who brought news to my father, saying, "A son is born to you," filling him with great joy.

16 May that man be like the cities that the LORD destroyed without relenting. Let him hear the sound of distress in the morning and the clamor of alarm at midday.

17 Because he did not kill me in the womb, or allow my mother to become my grave, leaving her womb perpetually swollen with me.

18 Why was I born from the womb only to experience toil and grief, so that my days would be spent in shame?
