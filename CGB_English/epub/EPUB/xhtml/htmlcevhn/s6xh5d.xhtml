Job Chapter 9

1 Then Job replied and said,

2 I understand that this is indeed true, but how can a person be considered righteous before God?

3 If someone were to argue with God, they could not respond to even one of His thousand challenges.

4 He is wise in understanding and mighty in powerâ€”who has ever defied Him and remained unharmed?

5 He moves mountains without their knowledge, and overturns them in His wrath.

6 Who causes the earth to quake from its foundation, and its supporting columns to shudder.

7 He commands the sun, and it does not rise; he seals up the stars, and they do not shine.

8 He alone stretches out the heavens and walks upon the surging waves of the sea.

9 He is the one who created Arcturus, Orion, and the Pleiades, as well as the constellations of the southern skies.

10 He accomplishes extraordinary deeds beyond comprehension; indeed, He performs countless wonders.

11 Behold, He moves past me, and I do not see Him; He goes by, and I do not perceive Him.

12 Look, when God takes something away, who can stop Him? Who can question Him and say, "What are You doing?"

13 If God does not relent in his wrath, even the mighty allies will bow down beneath him.

14 How can I possibly respond to Him, or carefully select my words to argue my case before Him?

15 Even if I were righteous, I would not dare to argue my case; instead, I would humbly plead for mercy before my Judge.

16 If I had called out and he had answered me, I still would not believe that he had truly listened to my voice.

17 For He overwhelms me with a storm and increases my wounds without reason.

18 He does not allow me to catch my breath; instead, he overwhelms me with bitterness.

19 If I speak of strength, behold, He is mighty; and if of justice, who can summon me to appear?

20 If I attempt to declare myself innocent, my own words would condemn me; if I claim to be blameless, it would only expose my corruption.

21 Even if I were blameless, I still would not understand my own soul; I would reject my life as unworthy.

22 This is one thing I have observed, and so I declare it: He brings ruin both to the blameless and the wicked.

23 If disaster strikes suddenly, he mocks the plight of the innocent.

24 The earth has been handed over to the power of the wicked; he blinds the eyes of its judges. If this is not so, then who is responsible, and where can such a person be found?

25 Now my days pass more swiftly than a messenger; they hurry away and see no prosperity.

26 They vanish swiftly like ships gliding across the waters, like an eagle swooping down swiftly to seize its prey.

27 If I say, "I will set aside my grievance, I will abandon my sorrow, and try to find comfort,"

28 I am overwhelmed by all my suffering; I know that you will not declare me innocent.

29 If I am truly guilty, then why do I struggle to no purpose?

30 Even if I were to cleanse myself with pure snow water and scrub my hands until they were spotless,

31 Yet you will thrust me into the mire, and my own garments will detest me.

32 For He is not a mere man, as I am, that I could answer Him, or that we might stand together in judgment.

33 There is no mediator between us, someone who could place his hand on both of us.

34 Let him remove his rod of discipline from me, and may the dread of his presence no longer overwhelm me.

35 If only I could speak without fear of Him; but that is not my situation.
