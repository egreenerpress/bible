Job Chapter 15

1 Then Eliphaz from Teman replied and said,

2 Would a person of wisdom speak empty, meaningless words, or fill themselves with nothing but hot air?

3 Should he engage in meaningless arguments or use words that bring no benefit?

4 Indeed, you have cast aside reverence and have restrained your prayers before God.

5 Your words reveal your wrongdoing, and you have chosen to speak with the deceitful tongue of the cunning.

6 Your own words condemn you, not I; indeed, your own lips bear witness against you.

7 Were you the first human ever born? Or were you created before the mountains came into existence?

8 Have you received knowledge from God's hidden counsel? Do you claim to possess wisdom exclusively for yourself?

9 What do you know that we do not know? What understanding do you possess that is not also within us?

10 Among us are both the gray-haired and the very old, men who are far older than your father.

11 Do you consider God's comforts to be insignificant? Is there some hidden matter troubling you?

12 Why does your heart lead you astray? And why do your eyes look with such desire?

13 Do you direct your anger against God and allow such words to come from your mouth?

14 What is humanity, that it could be pure? And how can one born of a woman be considered righteous?

15 Indeed, God places no confidence in His holy ones; even the heavens are not pure in His sight.

16 How much more detestable and corrupt is humanity, which consumes wickedness as readily as water?

17 I will instruct you; listen to me, and I will explain what I have witnessed.

18 Wise individuals have passed down knowledge from their ancestors and have not concealed it.

19 To whom alone the earth was granted, and no foreigner walked among them.

20 The wicked person lives in constant distress throughout their days, and the span of years allotted to the oppressor is concealed from them.

21 A terrifying noise fills his ears; even in times of prosperity, the destroyer will suddenly overtake him.

22 He does not believe that he will emerge from the darkness, and he is destined for the sword.

23 He roams about searching for food, asking, "Where can I find it?" He knows that the day of calamity is close at hand.

24 Distress and anguish will overwhelm him; they will overpower him like a king prepared for battle.

25 For he defiantly raises his hand against God and boldly resists the Almighty.

26 He charges at him, striking his neck, thrusting against the sturdy ridges of his shields.

27 Because he covers his face with his excess fat and gathers layers of fat on his sides.

28 He lives in abandoned cities, in houses where no one dwells, places that are on the verge of crumbling into ruins.

29 He will not become wealthy, nor will his possessions endure, nor will he maintain the fullness of his prosperity on the earth.

30 He will not escape from the darkness; the flame will wither his branches, and by the breath of God's mouth, he will perish.

31 Do not let the one who is deceived place their trust in emptiness, for emptiness will be their reward.

32 It will be completed before its appointed time, and its shoot will not flourish.

33 He will shed his unripe grapes like a vine, and he will drop his blossoms like an olive tree.

34 The assembly of hypocrites will be laid waste, and fire will devour the dwellings of those who take bribes.

35 They devise wicked schemes and give birth to emptiness; their hearts are filled with deceit and produce nothing but lies.
