Acts Chapter 2

1 When the day of Pentecost had fully arrived, they were all gathered together in unity in one place.

2 Suddenly, a sound came from heaven like the roar of a powerful, rushing wind, and it filled the entire house where they were sitting.

3 And there appeared to them divided tongues resembling fire, which rested upon each one of them.

4 And they were all filled with the Holy Spirit, and they began to speak in different languages, as the Spirit enabled them to speak.

5 In Jerusalem, there were Jews residing—devout men from every nation under heaven.

6 When news of this spread, the crowd gathered and was utterly amazed, because each person heard them speaking in their own native language.

7 Everyone was astonished and filled with wonder, saying to one another, "Look, aren’t all these people who are speaking Galileans?"

8 How is it that we each hear them speaking in our own native language?

9 Parthians, Medes, Elamites, residents of Mesopotamia, Judea, Cappadocia, Pontus, and Asia.

10 Phrygia and Pamphylia, in Egypt, and in the regions of Libya near Cyrene, as well as visitors from Rome—both Jews and converts to Judaism—

11 People from Crete and Arabia, we hear them declaring in our own languages the mighty and marvelous deeds of God.

12 Everyone was astonished and perplexed, asking one another, "What does this mean?"

13 Others ridiculed them, saying, "These men are drunk!"

14 Peter, standing with the eleven apostles, raised his voice and addressed the crowd, saying, "Men of Judea and all who live in Jerusalem, let this be made clear to you, and listen carefully to my words:

15 These people are not drunk, as you assume, since it is only the third hour of the day.

16 This is what was spoken by the prophet Joel:

17 This is what God declares: "In the final days, I will pour out my Spirit on all people. Your sons and daughters will prophesy, your young men will see visions, and your elders will dream dreams."

18 In those days, I will pour out my Spirit upon my servants, both men and women, and they will prophesy.

19 I will display extraordinary wonders in the heavens above and remarkable signs on the earth below: blood, fire, and billowing smoke.

20 The sun will be darkened, and the moon will turn blood-red, before the arrival of that great and awe-inspiring day of the Lord.

21 And it will happen that everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be delivered.

22 People of Israel, listen to these words: Jesus of Nazareth, a man whom God confirmed among you through miracles, wonders, and signs, which God performed through him in your presence, as you yourselves know.

23 This man, handed over according to the predetermined plan and foreknowledge of God, you seized and, through the actions of lawless men, crucified and killed.

24 God has raised him up, freeing him from the agony of death, because it was impossible for death to keep its hold on him.

25 David speaks about him, saying, "I continually see the Lord before me, for he stands at my right hand, so that I will not be shaken."

26 Therefore, my heart is filled with joy, and my tongue rejoices; furthermore, my body will rest in confident hope.

27 Because you will not abandon my soul to the realm of the dead, nor will you allow your faithful one to experience decay.

28 You have shown me the paths of life; you will fill me with joy in your presence.

29 Brothers and fellow Israelites, allow me to speak openly with you about the patriarch David. He died and was buried, and his tomb remains with us to this very day.

30 Therefore, since he was a prophet and knew that God had sworn to him with an oath that from his descendants, according to the flesh, He would raise up the Messiah to sit on his throne,

31 Foreseeing this, he spoke concerning the resurrection of the Messiah, that his soul would not be abandoned to the realm of the dead, nor would his body undergo decay.

32 God has raised this Jesus from the dead, and we are all witnesses of this fact.

33 Therefore, having been exalted to the right hand of God and having received from the Father the promised Holy Spirit, he has poured out this gift, which you now see and hear.

34 David has not ascended into heaven, but he himself declares, "The Lord said to my Lord, 'Sit at my right hand.'"

35 Until I make your enemies a place for your feet to rest.

36 Therefore, let all the house of Israel know with certainty that God has made this same Jesus, whom you crucified, both Lord and Messiah.

37 When they heard this, they were deeply moved in their hearts and said to Peter and the other apostles, "Brothers, what should we do?"

38 Then Peter said to them, "Turn away from your sins, and let each of you be baptized in the name of Jesus Christ so that your sins may be forgiven. And you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit."

39 The promise is for you, for your children, and for all who are far away—as many as the Lord our God will call to Himself.

40 And with many other words, he earnestly testified and urged them, saying, "Rescue yourselves from this corrupt and misguided generation."

41 Then those who joyfully accepted his message were baptized, and on that very day, about three thousand people were added to their number.

42 They devoted themselves consistently to the teaching of the apostles, to fellowship with one another, to sharing meals together, and to prayer.

43 A deep sense of awe came over everyone, and the apostles performed many extraordinary miracles and signs.

44 All those who believed were united and shared everything they had in common.

45 They sold their property and belongings and distributed the proceeds to everyone, according to each person's needs.

46 They gathered together every day in the temple, united in purpose, and shared meals in their homes with joy and sincerity of heart. They broke bread together, celebrating their fellowship with gladness and genuine devotion.

47 They were continually praising God and enjoying the goodwill of all the people. And the Lord added to their community daily those who were being saved.
