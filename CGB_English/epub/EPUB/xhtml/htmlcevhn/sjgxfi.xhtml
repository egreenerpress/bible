II Chronicles Chapter 2

1 Solomon resolved to construct a temple dedicated to the name of the LORD, as well as a royal palace for his own kingdom.

2 Solomon assigned seventy thousand men to carry loads, eighty thousand to cut stone in the mountains, and three thousand six hundred to supervise them.

3 Solomon sent a message to King Huram of Tyre, saying, "Just as you dealt kindly with my father David by sending him cedar logs to build a house for him to live in, so now deal with me in the same way."

4 Listen, I am building a house for the name of the LORD my God, to dedicate it to Him. It will be a place to burn fragrant incense before Him, to display the bread of the Presence continually, and to offer burnt offerings every morning and evening, as well as on the Sabbath, the new moons, and the appointed festivals of the LORD our God. This is a permanent ordinance for Israel.

5 The house that I am building is magnificent, for our God is greater than all other gods.

6 But who is capable of building a house for Him, when even the highest heavens cannot contain Him? Who am I, then, that I should attempt to build Him a house, except to offer sacrifices before Him?

7 Therefore, send me now a skilled craftsman who is expert in working with gold, silver, bronze, and iron, as well as in weaving purple, crimson, and blue fabrics. He should also be proficient in engraving, working alongside the skilled artisans who are with me in Judah and Jerusalem, whom my father David appointed.

8 Please also send me cedar, fir, and algum wood from Lebanon, for I am aware that your servants are skilled in cutting timber there. My servants will work alongside yours.

9 "Provide me with an ample supply of timber, for the house I intend to build will be exceedingly magnificent and vast."

10 Listen carefully: I will provide your servants, the lumberjacks who cut timber, with twenty thousand measures of ground wheat, twenty thousand measures of barley, twenty thousand baths of wine, and twenty thousand baths of olive oil.

11 Then Huram, the king of Tyre, replied in a written message that he sent to Solomon: "Because the LORD loves His people, He has made you their king."

12 Huram also declared, "May the LORD, the God of Israel, who created heaven and earth, be praised! He has given King David a wise son, endowed with insight and understanding, to build a temple for the LORD and a royal palace for his kingdom."

13 And now I have sent a skilled craftsman, gifted with great understanding, from Huram, my fatherâ€™s associate.

14 The son of a woman from the tribe of Dan, whose father was a man from Tyre, is highly skilled in working with gold, silver, bronze, iron, stone, and wood, as well as in crafting with purple, blue, and fine linen, and crimson fabrics. He is also expert in engraving of all kinds and can devise any intricate design that is given to him. He will work alongside your skilled craftsmen and those of my lord David, your father.

15 Now then, concerning the wheat, the barley, the oil, and the wine that my lord has mentioned, let him send these provisions to his servants.

16 We will cut timber from Lebanon, as much as you require, and transport it to you by sea on rafts to Joppa. From there, you can take it up to Jerusalem.

17 Solomon conducted a census of all the foreigners residing in the land of Israel, following the method his father David had used to count them. The total number was found to be 153,600.

18 He appointed seventy thousand of them to carry burdens, eighty thousand to cut stone in the mountains, and three thousand six hundred overseers to direct the people in their work.
