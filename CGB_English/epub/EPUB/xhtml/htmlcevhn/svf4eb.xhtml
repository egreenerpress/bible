John Chapter 1

1 In the very beginning, the Word already existed. The Word was with God, and the Word was fully divine.

2 This one was with God in the beginning.

3 Everything was created through him, and nothing that exists was made apart from him.

4 In him was the source of life, and this life brought light to humanity.

5 And the light shines in the darkness, but the darkness has not understood it.

6 A man was sent by God, and his name was John.

7 This man came as a witness, to testify about the Light, so that through him everyone might come to believe.

8 He was not the Light himself, but he was sent to testify about the Light.

9 This was the genuine Light, which enlightens every person who comes into the world.

10 He was present in the world, and the world was created through him, yet the world did not recognize him.

11 He came to what was his own, but his own people did not accept him.

12 To all who welcomed him and put their trust in his name, he granted the authority to become children of God.

13 These were born not through human descent, nor by human desire or effort, but through the will and power of God.

14 The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us. We have seen his glory, the glory of the one and only Son, who came from the Father, full of grace and truth.

15 John testified about Him and proclaimed, saying, "This is the one of whom I said, 'The one who comes after me ranks above me, because He existed before me.'"

16 From the abundance of his fullness, we have all received, grace upon grace.

17 The law was given through Moses, but grace and truth came through Jesus Christ.

18 No one has ever seen God at any time; the only Son, who is intimately close to the Father, has made him known.

19 This is the account of John, when the Jewish leaders sent priests and Levites from Jerusalem to question him, saying, "Who are you?"

20 He openly admitted and did not deny, but declared, "I am not the Messiah."

21 They asked him, “Then who are you? Are you Elijah?” He replied, “I am not.” They asked again, “Are you the Prophet?” He answered, “No.”

22 Then they asked him, "Who are you? We need to give an answer to those who sent us. What do you say about yourself?"

23 He said, "I am the voice of one calling out in the wilderness, 'Prepare the way for the Lord,' as the prophet Isaiah said."

24 The individuals who were sent belonged to the Pharisees.

25 They questioned him, saying, "Why then do you baptize, if you are not the Messiah, nor Elijah, nor the Prophet?"

26 John responded to them, saying, "I baptize with water, but among you stands one whom you do not recognize."

27 This is the one who comes after me, yet surpasses me in importance, and I am not worthy even to untie the strap of his sandal.

28 These events took place in Bethany on the far side of the Jordan River, where John was performing baptisms.

29 The following day, John saw Jesus approaching him and declared, "Look, here is the Lamb of God, who removes the sin of the world!"

30 This is the one about whom I said, "After me comes a man who surpasses me, because he existed before me."

31 I did not recognize him, but the reason I came baptizing with water was so that he might be revealed to Israel.

32 John testified, saying, "I saw the Spirit coming down from heaven like a dove, and it remained upon him."

33 I did not recognize him, but the one who sent me to baptize with water said to me, "When you see the Spirit come down and remain on someone, that is the one who will baptize with the Holy Spirit."

34 I observed and testify that this is the Son of God.

35 The next day, John was standing there again with two of his disciples.

36 As John watched Jesus walking by, he said, "Look, there is the Lamb of God!"

37 The two disciples heard him speak, and they began to follow Jesus.

38 Then Jesus turned around and noticed them following Him. He asked them, "What are you looking for?" They replied, "Rabbi" (which means "Teacher"), "where are You staying?"

39 He said to them, "Come and see." They went and saw where he was staying, and they remained with him that day, for it was about the tenth hour.

40 One of the two who heard John speak and then followed him was Andrew, the brother of Simon Peter.

41 He first found his own brother Simon and said to him, "We have found the Messiah," which, when translated, means "the Christ."

42 He brought him to Jesus. When Jesus looked at him, He said, "You are Simon, the son of Jonah. You will be called Cephas," which means "Rock" when translated.

43 The next day, Jesus decided to go to Galilee. He found Philip and said to him, "Follow me."

44 Philip was from Bethsaida, the hometown of Andrew and Peter.

45 Philip found Nathanael and said to him, "We have found the one about whom Moses wrote in the Law and about whom the prophets also wrote—Jesus of Nazareth, the son of Joseph."

46 Nathanael said to him, "Can anything good come from Nazareth?" Philip replied, "Come and see for yourself."

47 Jesus saw Nathanael approaching and said of him, "Here is a true Israelite, in whom there is no deceit!"

48 Nathanael said to him, “How do you know me?” Jesus replied, “Before Philip called you, when you were under the fig tree, I saw you.”

49 Nathanael replied to him, "Teacher, you are the Son of God; you are the King of Israel."

50 Jesus responded to him, saying, "Because I told you that I saw you under the fig tree, do you believe? You will see even greater things than these."

51 And He said to him, “Truly, truly, I tell you, from now on you will see heaven opened, and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of Man.”
