Matthew Chapter 17

1 Six days later, Jesus took Peter, James, and John, his brother, and led them up a high mountain by themselves.

2 He was transformed before them: his face shone like the sun, and his clothing became as bright as light.

3 Suddenly, Moses and Elijah appeared to them, speaking with him.

4 Then Peter responded and said to Jesus, "Lord, it is wonderful for us to be here. If you are willing, let us set up three shelters here—one for you, one for Moses, and one for Elijah."

5 While he was still speaking, suddenly a bright cloud enveloped them, and a voice came from the cloud, saying, "This is my beloved Son, in whom I take great delight. Listen to him."

6 When the disciples heard this, they fell facedown to the ground and were overwhelmed with fear.

7 Jesus approached them, touched them, and said, "Get up, and do not be afraid."

8 When they looked up, they saw no one except Jesus alone.

9 As they were descending from the mountain, Jesus instructed them, saying, "Do not tell anyone about this vision until the Son of Man has risen from the dead."

10 And his disciples asked him, saying, "Why then do the teachers of the law say that Elijah must come first?"

11 And Jesus responded, saying to them, "Elijah will indeed come first and restore all things."

12 But I tell you this: Elijah has already come, and they did not recognize him. Instead, they did to him whatever they pleased. In the same way, the Son of Man will also suffer at their hands.

13 Then the disciples realized that He was speaking to them about John the Baptist.

14 When they arrived at the crowd, a man approached Him, knelt before Him, and said,

15 Lord, have mercy on my son, for he suffers from seizures and is severely tormented. He often falls into the fire and frequently into the water.

16 I brought him to your disciples, but they were unable to heal him.

17 Then Jesus responded, saying, "You unbelieving and corrupt generation, how much longer must I remain with you? How much longer must I endure you? Bring the boy here to me."

18 Jesus sternly commanded the demon to leave, and it came out of the boy. From that very moment, the child was completely healed.

19 Then the disciples approached Jesus privately and asked, "Why were we unable to drive it out?"

20 Jesus replied to them, "It is because of your lack of faith. Truly I tell you, if you have faith even as small as a mustard seed, you can say to this mountain, 'Move from here to there,' and it will move. Nothing will be impossible for you."

21 However, this kind [of demon] does not come out except through prayer and fasting.

22 While they were staying in Galilee, Jesus said to them, "The Son of Man will be handed over to the power of men."

23 They will put him to death, but on the third day he will be raised to life. And they were deeply grieved.

24 When they arrived in Capernaum, the collectors of the temple tax approached Peter and asked, "Does your teacher not pay the temple tax?"

25 He said, "Yes." And when he entered the house, Jesus spoke to him first, saying, "What do you think, Simon? From whom do the kings of the earth collect taxes or tribute—from their own children or from outsiders?"

26 Peter said to him, "From outsiders." Jesus replied to him, "Then the children are exempt."

27 However, to avoid causing offense, go to the sea, cast a hook, and catch the first fish that comes up. When you open its mouth, you will find a coin. Take that and give it to them for both me and you.
