Ecclesiastes Chapter 8

1 Who can compare to the wise person? And who truly understands the meaning of things? A person's wisdom illuminates their countenance, transforming the sternness of their expression into something radiant.

2 I advise you to obey the king's command, especially because of the oath you have made before God.

3 Do not be quick to leave his presence, and do not persist in wrongdoing; for he acts according to his will.

4 When a king speaks, his word carries authority: and who can challenge him by asking, "What are you doing?"

5 Whoever keeps the commandment will experience no harm, and the heart of a wise person understands both the right time and the proper course of action.

6 Because there is a proper time and a divine decree for every matter, the burden of humanity weighs heavily upon them.

7 For he does not know what is to come, for who can inform him of what will happen?

8 No one has authority over the breath of life to keep it from departing, nor does anyone have power over the day of death. There is no escape from this inevitable conflict, and wickedness will not rescue those who are devoted to it.

9 I have observed all these things and carefully considered every activity that takes place under the sun. There is a time when one person exercises power over another, only to bring harm upon themselves.

10 And so I observed that the wicked, who had come and gone from the sacred place, were buried, and they were forgotten in the city where they had carried out their deeds. This too is meaningless.

11 Because the judgment against an evil act is not carried out quickly, the hearts of people become fully determined within them to do evil.

12 Even if a wicked person commits evil a hundred times and still lives a long life, I am certain that it will go well for those who fear God, who stand in awe of Him.

13 However, it will not go well for the wicked, nor will they extend their days, which are as fleeting as a shadow, because they do not stand in awe of God.

14 There is a troubling and perplexing reality that occurs on the earth: there are righteous people who experience what seems like the fate of the wicked, and there are wicked people who receive what appears to be the reward of the righteous. I observed this and concluded that it, too, is meaningless and deeply frustrating.

15 Then I praised joy, because there is nothing better for a person under the sun than to eat, drink, and be glad. This will accompany them in their labor throughout the days of life that God has given them under the sun.

16 When I set my heart to seek wisdom and to observe the activity that takes place on the earth—for there are those who do not rest day or night, their eyes never closing in sleep—

17 Then I observed all the work of God, realizing that no one can fully comprehend the actions that take place under the sun. No matter how hard a person may strive to understand it, they will not succeed. Even if a wise person claims to know it, they will still be unable to grasp it fully.
