Exodus Chapter 39

1 They crafted finely woven garments from blue, purple, and scarlet yarns, designed for performing sacred duties in the holy place. They also made the sacred garments for Aaron, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

2 He crafted the ephod using gold, along with blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, and finely woven linen.

3 They hammered the gold into thin sheets and cut it into fine threads, skillfully weaving it into the blue, purple, and scarlet fabrics, as well as into the fine linen, creating intricate designs.

4 They crafted shoulder pieces for it, designed to join it together; it was fastened at its two edges.

5 The intricately woven band of his ephod, which was attached to it, was made of the same materials and craftsmanship: gold, blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, and finely twisted linen, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

6 They crafted onyx stones, set in gold filigree, engraved like signet rings, each bearing the names of the sons of Israel.

7 He placed them on the shoulder pieces of the ephod, so that they would serve as memorial stones for the Israelites, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

8 He crafted the breastplate with skillful artistry, similar to the design of the ephod. It was made of gold, along with blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, and finely woven linen.

9 The breastplate was square and made double, with each side measuring a span in length and a span in width when folded.

10 They arranged four rows of stones in it: the first row consisted of a carnelian, a topaz, and an emeraldâ€”this was the first row.

11 The second row shall consist of an emerald, a sapphire, and a diamond.

12 The third row shall have a ligure, an agate, and an amethyst.

13 The fourth row contained a beryl, an onyx, and a jasper. Each stone was set in a gold mount, securely fastened in its setting.

14 The stones corresponded to the names of the twelve tribes of Israel, each one engraved like a seal with the name of one of the twelve tribes.

15 They crafted chains for the breastplate, made of twisted cords, fashioned from pure gold, and attached them to its edges.

16 They crafted two gold settings and two gold rings, and they attached the two rings to the two ends of the breastplate.

17 They fastened the two braided chains of gold into the two rings at the edges of the breastplate.

18 They attached the two ends of the twisted chains to the two settings and secured them to the shoulder pieces of the ephod, positioning them at the front.

19 They crafted two rings of gold and attached them to the two ends of the breastpiece, along its edge on the inner side, next to the ephod.

20 They crafted two additional gold rings and attached them to the lower sides of the ephod, near the front, directly opposite the other connecting rings, just above the skillfully woven waistband of the ephod.

21 They fastened the breastplate by its rings to the rings of the ephod using a blue cord, so that the breastplate would remain securely above the skillfully woven waistband of the ephod and would not come loose from it. This was done just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

22 He crafted the robe of the ephod, skillfully woven entirely from blue fabric.

23 There was an opening in the center of the robe, like the opening of a coat of mail, with a woven edge around the opening so that it would not tear.

24 They crafted pomegranates of blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, along with finely twisted linen, and attached them to the hem of the robe.

25 They crafted bells from pure gold and placed them alternately with the pomegranates around the hem of the robe, so that the bells were interspersed among the pomegranates all around the edge.

26 A bell and a pomegranate, alternating all around the hem of the robe, were to be worn while serving in the sanctuary, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

27 They crafted finely woven linen garments for Aaron and his sons.

28 And a turban of fine linen, and elegant headbands of fine linen, and undergarments of finely woven linen.

29 And a sash made of finely woven linen, with blue, purple, and scarlet threads, skillfully embroidered, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

30 They crafted the sacred crown's plate from pure gold and inscribed on it an engraving, similar to the markings of a seal, which read: "HOLINESS TO THE LORD."

31 They attached a blue cord to it, securing it high on the turban, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

32 In this way, all the work for the tabernacle, the tent of meeting, was completed. The Israelites carried out everything exactly as the LORD had commanded Moses; they did it just as instructed.

33 They brought the entire tabernacle to Mosesâ€”the tent and all its furnishings, including its clasps, frames, crossbars, posts, and bases.

34 And the outer covering made of rams' skins dyed red, and the protective covering made of fine leather, and the curtain that served as the innermost covering.

35 The ark containing the covenant tablets, along with its carrying poles, and the atonement cover.

36 The table, along with all its utensils, and the bread of the Presence.

37 The pure lampstand, along with its lamps, arranged properly, and all its utensils, as well as the oil for providing light.

38 And the golden altar for burning incense, the sacred anointing oil, the fragrant incense, and the curtain for the entrance of the tabernacle.

39 The bronze altar, along with its bronze grating, its poles, and all its utensils; the basin and its stand.

40 The curtains surrounding the courtyard, along with their supporting posts and bases, the curtain for the entrance to the courtyard, its ropes, tent pegs, and all the equipment used in the service of the tabernacleâ€”specifically for the tent of meetingâ€”

41 The finely crafted garments for performing sacred duties in the holy place, along with the sacred vestments for Aaron the priest and the garments for his sons, to serve in the priestly office.

42 The Israelites completed all the work exactly as the LORD had commanded Moses.

43 Moses inspected all the work, and indeed, they had completed it just as the LORD had commanded. They had done everything exactly as instructed. So Moses blessed them.
