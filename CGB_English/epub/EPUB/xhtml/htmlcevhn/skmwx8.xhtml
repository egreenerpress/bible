Numbers Chapter 7

1 On the day when Moses had completely set up the tabernacle, anointed it, and consecrated it, along with all its furnishings—including the altar and all its utensils—he also anointed and consecrated them.

2 The leaders of Israel, who were the heads of their ancestral families—the chiefs of the tribes and those in charge of the registered men—brought their offerings.

3 They presented their offering to the LORD: six covered wagons and twelve oxen—one wagon for every two leaders and one ox for each leader. They brought them before the tabernacle.

4 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

5 Take these items from them so that they may be used for the work of the tent of meeting. You shall distribute them to the Levites, assigning to each person according to the duties they are to perform.

6 Moses took the wagons and the oxen and gave them to the Levites.

7 He provided two carts and four oxen to the descendants of Gershon, in accordance with the duties they were assigned.

8 He provided four carts and eight oxen to the descendants of Merari, in accordance with their duties, under the supervision of Ithamar, the son of Aaron the priest.

9 However, to the descendants of Kohath, he gave no carts or wagons, because their responsibility for the sanctuary involved carrying the sacred objects on their shoulders.

10 The leaders of the tribes presented offerings for the dedication of the altar on the day it was anointed. They brought their offerings before the altar.

11 The LORD said to Moses, "Each leader shall present his offering on his appointed day for the consecration of the altar."

12 On the first day, Nahshon, the son of Amminadab, from the tribe of Judah, presented his offering.

13 His offering consisted of one silver platter weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver bowl weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil as a grain offering.

14 One golden spoon weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

15 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

16 One young goat as a sin offering.

17 For the peace offering, Nahshon son of Amminadab presented the following: two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

18 On the second day, Nethanel son of Zuar, the leader of Issachar, presented his offering.

19 He presented as his offering one silver dish weighing one hundred and thirty shekels, and one silver basin weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil as a grain offering.

20 One golden spoon weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

21 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

22 One young goat as a sin offering.

23 For the peace offering, Nethaneel son of Zuar presented two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

24 On the third day, Eliab son of Helon, the leader of the Zebulunites, presented his offering.

25 His offering consisted of one silver platter weighing one hundred and thirty shekels, and one silver basin weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil, to be presented as a grain offering.

26 One golden dish weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

27 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

28 One young goat as a sin offering.

29 For the peace offering, Eliab son of Helon presented two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

30 On the fourth day, Elizur, the son of Shedeur and leader of the Reubenites, presented his offering.

31 His offering consisted of one silver dish weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver bowl weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil, prepared as a grain offering.

32 One golden dish weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

33 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

34 One young goat as a sin offering.

35 For the peace offering, Elizur son of Shedeur presented the following: two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

36 On the fifth day, Shelumiel son of Zurishaddai, the leader of the tribe of Simeon, presented his offering.

37 His offering consisted of one silver platter weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver basin weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil, prepared as a grain offering.

38 One golden bowl weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

39 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

40 One young goat as a sin offering.

41 For the peace offering, Shelumiel son of Zurishaddai presented the following: two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

42 On the sixth day, Eliasaph, the son of Deuel, who was the leader of the tribe of Gad, presented his offering.

43 His offering consisted of one silver platter weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver basin weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil, to be presented as a grain offering.

44 One golden dish weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

45 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

46 One young goat as a sin offering.

47 For the peace offering, he presented two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was the offering brought by Eliasaph, the son of Deuel.

48 On the seventh day, Elishama, the son of Ammihud, who was the leader of the tribe of Ephraim, presented his offering.

49 His offering consisted of one silver platter weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver basin weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil as a grain offering.

50 One golden dish weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

51 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

52 One young goat as a sin offering.

53 For the peace offering, Elishama son of Ammihud presented two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

54 On the eighth day, Gamaliel, the son of Pedahzur, who was the leader of the tribe of Manasseh, presented his offering.

55 His offering consisted of one silver dish weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver basin weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil, to be presented as a grain offering.

56 One golden dish weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

57 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

58 One young goat as a sin offering.

59 For the peace offering, Gamaliel son of Pedahzur presented the following: two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

60 On the ninth day, Abidan son of Gideoni, the leader of the tribe of Benjamin, presented his offering.

61 His offering consisted of one silver platter weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver bowl weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil as a grain offering.

62 One golden dish weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

63 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

64 One young goat as a sin offering.

65 For the peace offering, Abidan son of Gideoni presented the following: two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

66 On the tenth day, Ahiezer, the son of Ammishaddai, who was the leader of the tribe of Dan, presented his offering.

67 His offering consisted of one silver platter weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver bowl weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil as a grain offering.

68 One golden dish weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

69 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

70 One young goat as a sin offering.

71 For the peace offering, Ahiezer son of Ammishaddai presented the following: two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

72 On the eleventh day, Pagiel son of Ocran, the leader of the tribe of Asher, presented his offering.

73 His offering consisted of one silver platter weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver basin weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil as a grain offering.

74 One golden dish weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

75 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

76 One young goat as a sin offering.

77 For the peace offering, Pagiel son of Ocran presented two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

78 On the twelfth day, Ahira, the son of Enan, who was the leader of the tribe of Naphtali, presented his offering.

79 His offering consisted of one silver dish weighing one hundred and thirty shekels and one silver basin weighing seventy shekels, according to the standard shekel of the sanctuary. Both were filled with fine flour mixed with oil, to be presented as a grain offering.

80 One golden dish weighing ten shekels, filled with incense.

81 One young bull, one ram, and one year-old lamb, to be offered as a burnt sacrifice.

82 One young goat as a sin offering.

83 For the peace offering, Ahira son of Enan presented the following: two bulls, five rams, five male goats, and five one-year-old lambs. This was his offering.

84 This was the ceremony for dedicating the altar on the day it was anointed, presented by the leaders of Israel: twelve silver plates, twelve silver basins, and twelve golden dishes.

85 Each silver platter weighed one hundred and thirty shekels, and each bowl weighed seventy shekels. All the silver vessels together weighed two thousand four hundred shekels, according to the standard weight of the sanctuary shekel.

86 There were twelve golden spoons, each filled with incense, and each spoon weighed ten shekels according to the sanctuary shekel. The total weight of the gold for all the spoons was one hundred and twenty shekels.

87 For the burnt offering, there were twelve bulls, twelve rams, and twelve one-year-old lambs, along with their grain offerings. Additionally, there were twelve male goats for the sin offering.

88 The total number of animals offered for the peace offerings was as follows: twenty-four bulls, sixty rams, sixty male goats, and sixty one-year-old lambs. These were presented for the dedication of the altar after it had been anointed.

89 When Moses entered the tent of meeting to speak with the Lord, he heard the voice speaking to him from above the mercy seat that was on the ark of the covenant, between the two cherubim. And the Lord spoke to him there.
