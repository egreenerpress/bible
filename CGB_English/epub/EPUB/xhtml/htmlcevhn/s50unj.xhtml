I Timothy Chapter 2

1 Therefore, I urge that, above all else, petitions, prayers, appeals, and expressions of gratitude be offered on behalf of all people.

2 For rulers and all who hold positions of authority, so that we may live peaceful and tranquil lives, marked by godliness and dignity.

3 This is good and pleasing in the sight of God our Savior.

4 God desires all people to be saved and to come to the knowledge of the truth.

5 For there is only one God, and there is only one mediator between God and humanity—the man Christ Jesus.

6 He gave himself as a ransom for all, a truth to be proclaimed at the appointed time.

7 For this purpose, I have been appointed as a preacher and an apostle—I am speaking the truth in Christ and not lying—a teacher of the Gentiles in faith and truth.

8 I desire, therefore, that in every place, men should pray, lifting up hands that are pure and holy, free from anger and doubt.

9 Similarly, women should dress modestly, with decency and self-control, not with elaborate hairstyles, gold jewelry, pearls, or expensive clothing.

10 However, what is fitting for women who claim to live in reverence for God is to demonstrate their faith through good deeds.

11 Let the woman learn quietly, with full submission.

12 However, I do not permit a woman to teach or to assume authority over a man; rather, she is to remain in quietness.

13 Adam was created first, and then Eve.

14 Adam was not the one who was deceived, but the woman, having been led astray, became involved in the transgression.

15 Nevertheless, she will be preserved through the bearing of children, provided they continue steadfastly in faith, love, and holiness, with self-control.
