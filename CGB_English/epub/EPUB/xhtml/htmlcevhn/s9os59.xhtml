Psalms Chapter 57

1 (For the music director, according to "Do Not Destroy," a poem of David, when he fled from Saul in the cave.) Have mercy on me, O God, have mercy on me, for I place my trust in you. I will take refuge in the shelter of your wings until this time of destruction has passed.

2 I will call out to God, the Most High, to God who accomplishes everything for me.

3 He will send help from heaven and rescue me from the scorn of those who seek to destroy me. Selah. God will demonstrate His steadfast love and faithfulness.

4 My soul is surrounded by fierce enemies; I dwell among those who are consumed with rage, even among people whose words are like spears and arrows, and whose tongues are as sharp as swords.

5 May you be exalted, O God, above the heavens; may your glory be over all the earth.

6 They have set a trap for my path; my spirit is weighed down with distress. They have dug a pit ahead of me, but they themselves have fallen into it. Selah.

7 My heart is steadfast, O God, my heart is steadfast; I will sing and offer praise.

8 Rise up, my soul; awaken, lyre and harp! I will rouse myself at dawn.

9 I will give you praise, O Lord, before all peoples; I will sing of your greatness among the nations.

10 For your steadfast love reaches as high as the heavens, and your faithfulness extends to the skies.

11 May you be lifted high, O God, above the heavens; may your splendor and majesty be exalted over all the earth.
