I Corinthians Chapter 6

1 Does any of you, when you have a dispute with someone, dare to take it before the unrighteous for judgment instead of bringing it before the holy ones?

2 Are you not aware that the holy ones will judge the world? And if the world is to be judged by you, are you not competent to handle even the most trivial disputes?

3 Are you not aware that we will even judge angels? How much more, then, should we be able to handle matters pertaining to this life?

4 If you have disputes concerning matters of this life, appoint those who are least esteemed in the church to judge them.

5 I say this to your shame. Is it really true that there is no one among you who is wise? Not even one person who can settle disputes between fellow believers?

6 But now, one believer is taking another believer to court, and doing so before those who do not share our faith.

7 The situation among you is entirely unacceptable because you are taking legal action against one another. Why not instead choose to endure the wrong done to you? Why not allow yourselves to be wronged or cheated rather than resorting to such disputes?

8 No, you are acting unjustly and cheating, and you are doing this to your own fellow believers.

9 Are you not aware that those who practice unrighteousness will not inherit the kingdom of God? Do not be deceived: neither those who engage in sexual immorality, nor idolaters, nor adulterers, nor those who indulge in perverse behavior, nor those who exploit others for immoral purposes—

10 Thieves, the greedy, drunkards, those who speak abusively, and swindlers will not inherit the kingdom of God.

11 Some of you were once like that, but now you have been cleansed, made holy, and declared righteous in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ and by the Spirit of our God.

12 Everything is permissible for me, but not everything is beneficial. Everything is permissible for me, but I will not allow anything to control me.

13 Food is meant for the stomach, and the stomach for food, but God will do away with both of them. However, the body is not intended for sexual immorality, but for the Lord, and the Lord is for the body.

14 God has raised the Lord from the dead and will also raise us up by His own power.

15 Do you not realize that your bodies are parts of Christ’s own body? How then could I take what belongs to Christ and unite it with a prostitute? Certainly not!

16 Do you not understand that whoever unites with a prostitute becomes one body with her? For it is said, "The two will become one flesh."

17 But whoever is united with the Lord becomes one in spirit with Him.

18 Avoid sexual immorality at all costs. While every other sin a person commits is external to the body, the one who engages in sexual immorality sins against their own body.

19 Do you not realize that your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit, who dwells within you and whom you have received from God? You do not belong to yourselves.

20 You have been purchased at a great cost; therefore, honor God with your body and your spirit, for they belong to God.
