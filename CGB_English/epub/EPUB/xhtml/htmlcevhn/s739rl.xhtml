Deuteronomy Chapter 23

1 A man who has been injured in the testicles or has had his genital organ cut off shall not be admitted into the assembly of the LORD.

2 A child born outside of lawful marriage shall not be admitted into the assembly of the LORD; nor shall any of their descendants, even to the tenth generation, be admitted into the assembly of the LORD.

3 No Ammonite or Moabite may ever be admitted into the assembly of the LORD, nor may any of their descendants, even to the tenth generation, be allowed to join the assembly of the LORD, forever.

4 Because they did not meet you with food and water along the way when you came out of Egypt, and because they hired Balaam son of Beor from Pethor in Mesopotamia to curse you.

5 However, the LORD your God refused to listen to Balaam; instead, the LORD your God transformed the curse into a blessing for you, because the LORD your God deeply loved you.

6 You must not seek their peace or their well-being all your days, forever.

7 You must not despise an Edomite, for he is your brother. You must not despise an Egyptian, because you were a foreigner in his land.

8 The descendants born to them may be admitted into the assembly of the LORD by the third generation.

9 When the army goes out to fight against your enemies, guard yourself against every evil act.

10 If any man among you becomes unclean because of an impurity that happens to him during the night, he must go outside the camp and stay there. He is not permitted to enter the camp.

11 When evening arrives, he must wash himself with water, and once the sun has set, he may return to the camp.

12 You shall designate a place outside the camp where you may go to relieve yourself.

13 You shall have a tool with your equipment; and when you go outside to relieve yourself, you shall dig with it, then turn and cover what comes from you.

14 For the LORD your God moves about within your camp to protect you and to hand your enemies over to you. Therefore, your camp must remain holy, so that He does not see anything indecent among you and turn away from you.

15 You must not return to his master a servant who has escaped from his master and come to you.

16 He shall live with you, among you, in the place he chooses within one of your towns, wherever he prefers; you must not mistreat him.

17 None of the daughters of Israel shall engage in prostitution, nor shall any of the sons of Israel practice male cult prostitution.

18 You must not bring the earnings of a prostitute or the payment of a male prostitute into the house of the LORD your God as an offering for any vow, for both of these are detestable to the LORD your God.

19 You must not charge interest when lending to your fellow Israelite—whether it is interest on money, food, or anything else that may be lent for profit.

20 You may charge interest on a loan to a foreigner, but you must not charge interest on a loan to your fellow Israelite. This is so that the LORD your God may bless you in everything you undertake in the land you are entering to take possession of it.

21 When you make a vow to the LORD your God, you must not delay in fulfilling it, for the LORD your God will certainly demand it from you, and failure to do so would be sin on your part.

22 However, if you choose not to make a vow, it will not be considered a sin against you.

23 You must fulfill and carry out what has come from your lips—the voluntary offering you have vowed to the LORD your God, which you have promised with your own words.

24 When you enter your neighbor’s vineyard, you may eat as many grapes as you desire to satisfy your hunger, but you must not gather any into a container to take with you.

25 When you enter your neighbor’s field of standing grain, you may pluck the heads of grain with your hand, but you must not use a sickle to harvest your neighbor’s standing grain.
