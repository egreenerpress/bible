Matthew Chapter 21

1 As they approached Jerusalem and came to Bethphage, near the Mount of Olives, Jesus sent two of his disciples ahead.

2 He said to them, "Go into the village ahead of you, and right away you will find a donkey tied up, along with a colt beside her. Untie them and bring them to me."

3 If anyone questions you about this, simply say, 'The Lord has need of them,' and they will immediately let you take them.

4 All of this took place so that what was spoken through the prophet might be fulfilled, as it is written:

5 Proclaim to the people of Zion: "Look, your King is coming to you, gentle and humble, riding on a donkey, on a colt, the young offspring of a donkey."

6 The disciples went and carried out exactly as Jesus had instructed them.

7 They brought the donkey and the colt, laid their cloaks on them, and seated him on top.

8 A large crowd spread their cloaks on the road, while others cut branches from the trees and scattered them along the path.

9 The crowds who went ahead of him and those who followed behind cried out, saying, "Hosanna to the Son of David! Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord! Hosanna in the highest!"

10 When he entered Jerusalem, the entire city was stirred up, and people were asking, "Who is this man?"

11 The crowd said, "This is Jesus, the prophet from Nazareth in Galilee."

12 Jesus entered the temple of God and drove out all those who were buying and selling there. He overturned the tables of the money changers and the seats of those who sold doves.

13 And he said to them, "It is written: 'My house will be called a house of prayer,' but you have turned it into a hideout for robbers."

14 And those who were blind and those who were unable to walk came to him in the temple, and he restored their sight and made them whole.

15 When the chief priests and the teachers of the law saw the miraculous works he performed, and heard the children shouting in the temple, "Hosanna to the Son of David!" they were deeply angered.

16 Then they said to Him, "Do You hear what these children are saying?" Jesus replied, "Yes. Have you never read the Scripture: 'From the lips of children and infants You have ordained praise'?"

17 He departed from them and went out of the city to Bethany, where he stayed for the night.

18 Early in the morning, as Jesus was returning to the city, he became hungry.

19 As he approached a fig tree by the road, he went over to it but found nothing on it except leaves. He said to it, "May no fruit ever grow on you again from this time onward." Immediately, the fig tree withered and died.

20 When the disciples saw this, they were amazed and said, "How quickly the fig tree has withered!"

21 Jesus responded to them, saying, "Truly, I tell you, if you have faith and do not doubt, you will not only accomplish what was done to the fig tree, but even if you say to this mountain, 'Be lifted up and thrown into the sea,' it will happen."

22 And whatever you ask for in prayer, if you believe, you will receive it.

23 When Jesus entered the temple and was teaching, the chief priests and the elders of the people approached him and asked, "By what authority are you doing these things? And who gave you this authority?"

24 Jesus responded to them, saying, "I will also ask you one question. If you answer me, I will likewise tell you by what authority I do these things."

25 The baptism performed by John—where did it come from? Was it from heaven, or was it of human origin?" They debated among themselves, saying, "If we say, 'From heaven,' he will ask us, 'Why then did you not believe him?'"

26 But if we say, 'From human authority,' we are afraid of the people, because they all regard John as a prophet.

27 They replied to Jesus, saying, "We do not know." And He said to them, "Then neither will I tell you by what authority I do these things."

28 What do you think about this? A man had two sons. He went to the first and said, “Son, go and work in the vineyard today.”

29 He replied, "I will not go," but later he changed his mind and went.

30 He then went to the second son and said the same thing. The son replied, "I will go, sir," but he did not go.

31 Which of the two did what his father asked? They replied, "The first one." Jesus said to them, "Truly I tell you, the tax collectors and the prostitutes are entering the kingdom of God ahead of you."

32 John came to you, teaching the path of righteousness, but you did not believe him. However, the tax collectors and the prostitutes believed him. Even after witnessing this, you did not change your minds and believe him.

33 Listen to another parable: There was a landowner who planted a vineyard. He put a fence around it, dug a winepress in it, and built a watchtower. Then he leased it to tenant farmers and went away to a distant land.

34 As the season for the fruit approached, he sent his servants to the tenants to collect his share of the harvest.

35 The tenant farmers seized his servants, beat one, killed another, and stoned a third.

36 Once more, he sent additional servants, even more than the first group, but they treated them in the same way.

37 Finally, he sent his own son to them, thinking, "They will respect my son."

38 But when the tenant farmers saw the son, they said to one another, "This is the heir. Let’s kill him and take possession of his inheritance."

39 They seized him, threw him out of the vineyard, and killed him.

40 When the owner of the vineyard comes, what will he do to those tenant farmers?

41 They replied to him, "He will utterly destroy those wicked men and will lease his vineyard to other tenants, who will give him the produce at the proper seasons."

42 Jesus said to them, "Have you never read in the Scriptures: 'The stone that the builders rejected has become the cornerstone. This is the Lord's doing, and it is marvelous in our eyes'?"

43 Therefore, I tell you this: The kingdom of God will be taken away from you and given to a people who will produce its fruit.

44 And whoever stumbles over this stone will be shattered; but whoever it falls upon will be completely crushed.

45 When the chief priests and the Pharisees heard his parables, they realized that he was speaking about them.

46 But when they attempted to seize him, they were afraid of the crowd, because the people regarded him as a prophet.
