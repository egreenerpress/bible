II Corinthians Chapter 8

1 Furthermore, brothers and sisters, we want you to know about the grace of God that has been given to the churches in Macedonia.

2 In the midst of severe suffering and hardship, their overflowing joy and extreme poverty have resulted in an extraordinary generosity.

3 I can testify that they gave according to their means, and even beyond their means. They did so willingly and on their own initiative.

4 They earnestly pleaded with us, urging that we accept the gift and join in the shared effort of supporting the needs of the believers.

5 They did this not merely as we had expected, but they first gave themselves completely to the Lord, and then to us, in accordance with God’s will.

6 We earnestly urged Titus that, just as he had started this work among you, he should also complete this act of grace for you.

7 Therefore, since you excel in every way—in faith, in speech, in knowledge, in all earnestness, and in the love you have shown us—make sure that you also excel in this act of grace.

8 I am not speaking as though I am giving you a command, but rather taking advantage of the eagerness shown by others to test the genuineness of your love.

9 You are aware of the gracious act of our Lord Jesus Christ, that although he was rich, for your sake he became poor, so that through his poverty you might become rich.

10 Here is my advice, which I offer for your benefit: It is appropriate for you, who not only started this work last year but were also eager to do so, to continue now.

11 Now then, carry out the act of giving as you have intended, so that just as there was eagerness in your willingness, there may also be completion according to what you have.

12 If someone is genuinely willing to give, their gift is valued based on what they have, not on what they do not have.

13 I do not intend that others should have relief while you are burdened.

14 Rather, the goal is fairness: that at this present time, your surplus may meet their need, so that in turn their surplus may one day meet your need. In this way, there can be balance and equality.

15 As it is written, "The one who gathered much did not have too much, and the one who gathered little did not have too little."

16 But we give thanks to God, who has placed the same deep concern for you in the heart of Titus.

17 Indeed, he welcomed the encouragement, but being even more eager, he willingly went to you on his own initiative.

18 We have also sent with him a fellow believer who is highly esteemed for his work in spreading the gospel among all the churches.

19 Not only that, but he was also chosen by the churches to accompany us in this act of grace, which we are administering for the glory of the Lord and as a demonstration of your eager willingness.

20 To prevent this, we are taking care that no one can criticize us regarding the generous offering we are managing.

21 Acting with integrity and doing what is right, not only as seen by the Lord but also as observed by others.

22 We have sent with them our brother, whom we have often found to be diligent in many matters, and now even more so because of the great confidence I have in you.

23 If anyone inquires about Titus, he is my partner and fellow worker in serving you. And if our brothers are inquired about, they are representatives of the churches and bring honor to Christ.

24 Therefore, demonstrate to them, and in the presence of the churches, the evidence of your love and the justification for our pride in you.
