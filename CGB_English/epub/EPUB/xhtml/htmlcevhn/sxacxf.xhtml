Job Chapter 36

1 Elihu continued and spoke, saying,

2 Allow me a moment, and I will show you that I still have more to say in defense of God.

3 I will draw my understanding from distant sources and attribute righteousness to my Creator.

4 Certainly, my words will not be untrue; the one who is complete in understanding is present with you.

5 Indeed, God is all-powerful and does not disregard anyone; He is mighty in strength and profound in wisdom.

6 He does not sustain the life of the wicked, but upholds the cause of the oppressed.

7 God does not turn His gaze away from the righteous; He seats them with kings on the throne. Indeed, He establishes them forever, and they are lifted up in honor.

8 If they are bound in chains and held fast by the cords of suffering,

9 Then He reveals to them their deeds and their sins, showing how they have gone beyond what is right.

10 He opens their ears to instruction and directs them to turn away from wrongdoing.

11 If they obey and serve Him, they will live out their days in abundance and their years in contentment.

12 However, if they refuse to obey, they will meet their end by the sword and perish in ignorance.

13 However, those who are deceitful in their hearts store up anger; they do not cry out for help when God disciplines them.

14 They perish in the prime of their lives, and their existence ends in disgrace.

15 He rescues the needy in their distress and opens their ears to hear in times of oppression.

16 In the same way, He would have brought you out of distress into a spacious place, where there is no constraint; and the abundance set before you would be rich and satisfying.

17 But you have carried out the punishment of the wicked; justice and judgment have seized you.

18 Because there is anger, be cautious, or else he may swiftly remove you with his punishment; then even a substantial ransom will not be able to save you.

19 Does he value your wealth? No, not even gold or all the might of your strength.

20 Do not long for the night, when people are suddenly taken from their place.

21 Be careful; do not turn your attention to wrongdoing, for you have chosen this over enduring hardship.

22 Look, God demonstrates His greatness through His mighty power; who can instruct or teach as He does?

23 Who has directed His path, or who can declare, "You have acted unjustly"?

24 Remember to honor and exalt His work, which people observe and witness.

25 Every person can observe it; anyone can perceive it from a distance.

26 Look, God is truly great, and we cannot fully comprehend Him; the extent of His existence is beyond our understanding.

27 For He reduces the drops of water to fine mist, and from the vapor, He pours down rain in abundance.

28 The clouds pour down and drip moisture upon humanity in great abundance.

29 Can anyone comprehend how the clouds spread across the sky, or grasp the thunderous sounds that come from His dwelling place?

30 Look, He spreads His light over it, and He covers the depths of the sea.

31 Through these, He governs the people; He provides them with abundant sustenance.

32 He covers the light with clouds and commands it not to shine, as the cloud passes between.

33 The sound of it reveals its presence, and the livestock also sense the approaching storm.
