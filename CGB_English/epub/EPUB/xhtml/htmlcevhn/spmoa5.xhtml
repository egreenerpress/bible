Haggai Chapter 2

1 On the twenty-first day of the seventh month, the word of the LORD came through the prophet Haggai, saying:

2 Go and speak to Zerubbabel, the son of Shealtiel, who is the governor of Judah, and to Joshua, the son of Josedech, the high priest, and to all the rest of the people, and say to them:

3 Who among you remains who saw this temple in its former splendor? How does it appear to you now? Does it not seem like nothing in comparison to what it once was?

4 The Lord says this: "Now, be strong, Zerubbabel. Be strong, Joshua, son of Jehozadak, the high priest. And be strong, all you people of the land, declares the Lord, and get to work. For I am with you, says the Lord of Hosts."

5 According to the promise I made with you when you came out of Egypt, my Spirit remains among you. Do not be afraid.

6 For this is what the LORD of hosts declares: "In just a little while longer, I will shake the heavens, the earth, the sea, and the dry land.

7 I will cause all nations to tremble, and the one desired by all nations will come. Then I will fill this house with glory, declares the LORD of hosts.

8 The silver belongs to me, and the gold is mine, declares the LORD of hosts.

9 The splendor of this future temple will surpass that of the former one, declares the LORD of hosts. And in this place, I will grant peace, says the LORD of hosts.

10 On the twenty-fourth day of the ninth month, in the second year of King Darius, the word of the LORD came through the prophet Haggai, saying:

11 This is what the LORD of hosts says: "Go and inquire of the priests about the law, asking them this:

12 If someone carries consecrated meat in the fold of their garment and that fold touches bread, stew, wine, oil, or any other food, does that food become holy?" The priests answered, "No."

13 Then Haggai asked, "If someone who is ritually unclean because of contact with a dead body touches any of these things, will they become unclean?" The priests responded, "Yes, they will become unclean."

14 Then Haggai responded and said, "This is how this people and this nation are before me, declares the LORD. The same applies to everything they do and to what they offer—it is unclean."

15 And now, I urge you to reflect on this time, from this day onward, recalling the period before even a single stone was placed upon another in the temple of the LORD.

16 In those days, when someone brought a heap of grain expecting twenty measures, there would only be ten. And when someone went to the winepress to draw out fifty vessels of wine, only twenty would be produced.

17 I struck your crops with scorching heat, mildew, and hail, ruining all the work of your hands; yet you did not return to me, declares the LORD.

18 Reflect carefully from this day forward, from the twenty-fourth day of the ninth month, even from the day when the foundation of the Lord’s temple was laid—give it your full attention.

19 Is there still seed left in the barn? Even now, the vine, the fig tree, the pomegranate, and the olive tree have not yet produced their fruit. But from this day forward, I will bless you.

20 Once more, the word of the LORD came to Haggai on the twenty-fourth day of the month, saying,

21 Tell Zerubbabel, the governor of Judah, that I will cause the heavens and the earth to tremble.

22 I will bring down the thrones of kingdoms and shatter the power of the nations. I will overthrow their chariots and those who ride in them. The horses and their riders will fall, each one by the sword of his fellow.

23 On that day, declares the LORD of hosts, I will take you, Zerubbabel, my servant, son of Shealtiel, says the LORD, and I will make you like a signet ring, for I have chosen you, declares the LORD of hosts.
