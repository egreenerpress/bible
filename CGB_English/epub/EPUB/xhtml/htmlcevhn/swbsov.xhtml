Proverbs Chapter 16

1 The intentions of a person’s heart and the words they speak are both guided by the LORD.

2 A person may consider all their ways to be pure in their own sight, but the LORD examines the motives of the heart.

3 Entrust your actions to the LORD, and your plans will be firmly established.

4 The LORD has created all things for His own purposes; indeed, even the wicked are destined for the day of calamity.

5 Anyone who is arrogant in heart is detestable to the LORD; even if they band together for support, they will not escape punishment.

6 Through acts of kindness and faithfulness, wrongdoing is atoned for, and by revering the LORD, people turn away from evil.

7 When a person’s actions are pleasing to the LORD, He causes even their enemies to live in peace with them.

8 It is better to have little with integrity than to gain great wealth through injustice.

9 A person may plan their course in their heart, but it is the LORD who guides their steps.

10 A king’s words carry divine authority; his mouth does not err when delivering judgment.

11 Accurate scales and honest measures belong to the LORD; every weight in the bag is established by His decree.

12 Kings detest the practice of wickedness, for a throne is firmly established through righteousness.

13 Kings take great pleasure in truthful and upright speech; they value and favor those who speak with integrity.

14 The anger of a king is like a herald of death, but a person of wisdom can calm it.

15 In the radiance of the king's presence, there is life, and his favor is like a cloud bringing the refreshing rain of spring.

16 How much more valuable is it to gain wisdom than to acquire gold! And how much more desirable is it to obtain understanding than to choose silver!

17 The path of the righteous is to turn away from evil; whoever guards their way protects their life.

18 Arrogance precedes ruin, and a proud spirit leads to downfall.

19 It is better to have a humble spirit and associate with those who are modest, than to share in the spoils with those who are arrogant.

20 The one who deals with matters wisely will experience good outcomes, and whoever places their trust in the LORD will be truly blessed.

21 Those who are wise in heart will be recognized as discerning, and gracious speech enhances understanding.

22 Understanding is a fountain of life to those who possess it, but the discipline of fools is nothing but foolishness.

23 A wise person's heart guides their speech, imparting knowledge and insight to their words.

24 Kind and gracious words are like a honeycomb—sweet to the spirit and healing to the body.

25 There is a path that appears right to a person, but in the end, it leads to death.

26 The one who works hard does so for their own benefit, because their own desires drive them to it.

27 A wicked person plots evil schemes, and their words are like a blazing fire, spreading destruction and harm.

28 A deceitful person stirs up conflict, and a gossip drives a wedge between close friends.

29 A person who acts with violence deceives their neighbor, leading them down a path that is harmful and wrong.

30 He closes his eyes to plot wicked schemes; with his lips moving, he carries out evil deeds.

31 Gray hair is a glorious crown, earned by living a life of righteousness.

32 A person who is slow to anger is greater than a mighty warrior, and one who controls their temper is more powerful than one who conquers a city.

33 The lot is cast into the lap, but its entire outcome is determined by the LORD.
