John Chapter 11

1 A man named Lazarus, from Bethany—the village where Mary and her sister Martha lived—was sick.

2 This refers to Mary, the one who anointed the Lord with perfume and wiped his feet with her hair. Her brother Lazarus was the one who became ill.

3 So his sisters sent a message to him, saying, "Lord, the one you love is ill."

4 When Jesus heard this, he said, "This illness will not end in death, but it is for the purpose of bringing glory to God, so that the Son of God may be glorified through it."

5 Now Jesus had a deep affection for Martha, her sister, and Lazarus.

6 When Jesus heard that Lazarus was sick, he stayed where he was for two more days.

7 After this, he said to his disciples, "Let us return to Judea."

8 His disciples said to him, "Teacher, the Jews were just trying to stone you, and yet you are going back there?"

9 Jesus replied, "Are there not twelve hours in the daylight? If someone walks during the day, they do not stumble, because they see the light of this world."

10 However, if someone walks in the darkness, they will stumble, because there is no light within them.

11 After saying these things, Jesus told them, "Our friend Lazarus has fallen asleep, but I am going to wake him from his sleep."

12 Then his disciples said, "Lord, if he is sleeping, he will recover."

13 However, Jesus was speaking about his death, but they thought he was referring to taking a rest in sleep.

14 Then Jesus said to them plainly, "Lazarus has died."

15 I am glad for your sake that I was not there, so that you may come to believe. But now, let us go to him.

16 Then Thomas, who was called Didymus, said to his fellow disciples, "Let us also go, so that we may die with him."

17 When Jesus arrived, he discovered that the man had already been in the tomb for four days.

18 Now Bethany was near Jerusalem, about two miles away.

19 Many of the Jewish people came to Martha and Mary to offer them comfort regarding their brother.

20 When Martha heard that Jesus was approaching, she went out to meet him, but Mary remained seated in the house.

21 Then Martha said to Jesus, "Lord, if you had been here, my brother would not have died."

22 But I am certain that even now, whatever you ask of God, God will grant it to you.

23 Jesus said to her, "Your brother will rise again."

24 Martha said to him, "I know that he will rise again in the resurrection on the last day."

25 Jesus said to her, "I am the resurrection and the life. Whoever believes in me, even if they die, will live."

26 And whoever lives and believes in me will never experience eternal death. Do you believe this?

27 She said to Him, "Yes, Lord; I believe that You are the Messiah, the Son of God, who was to come into the world."

28 After she had spoken these words, she left and privately called her sister Mary, saying, "The Teacher has arrived and is asking for you."

29 Upon hearing this, she immediately got up and went to him.

30 Now Jesus had not yet entered the village but was still in the place where Martha had met him.

31 The Jews who were with her in the house, comforting her, saw Mary get up quickly and go out. They followed her, saying, "She is going to the tomb to weep there."

32 When Mary arrived where Jesus was and saw him, she fell at his feet and said to him, "Lord, if you had been here, my brother would not have died."

33 When Jesus saw her weeping, and the Jews who had come with her also weeping, he was deeply moved in his spirit and greatly distressed.

34 And he asked, "Where have you laid him?" They replied, "Lord, come and see."

35 Jesus shed tears.

36 Then the Jewish people said, "Look how deeply he loved him!"

37 Some of them said, "Could not this man, who opened the eyes of the blind, have also prevented this man from dying?"

38 Jesus, deeply moved within himself once more, went to the tomb. It was a cave, and a stone was lying against it.

39 Jesus said, "Remove the stone." Martha, the sister of the dead man, said to him, "Lord, by now there will be a bad odor, for he has been dead four days."

40 Jesus said to her, "Did I not tell you that if you would believe, you would see the glory of God?"

41 Then they removed the stone from the place where the dead man had been laid. Jesus looked up and said, "Father, I thank you that you have heard me."

42 I knew that you always hear me, but I said this for the sake of the people standing here, so that they may believe that you sent me.

43 After saying these things, he called out in a loud voice, "Lazarus, come out!"

44 The man who had been dead came out, his hands and feet still wrapped in burial cloths, and his face covered with a cloth. Jesus said to them, "Unbind him and let him go."

45 Then many of the Jews who had come to Mary and witnessed the works Jesus had done came to believe in him.

46 However, some of them went to the Pharisees and reported to them what Jesus had done.

47 Then the chief priests and the Pharisees convened a meeting and said, "What should we do? This man is performing many miraculous signs."

48 If we leave him alone like this, everyone will believe in him, and then the Romans will come and take away both our temple and our nation.

49 And one of them, named Caiaphas, who was high priest that year, said to them, "You understand nothing at all."

50 Do you not realize that it is better for one man to die for the sake of the people, so that the entire nation may not be destroyed?

51 He did not say this on his own authority, but because he was high priest that year, he prophesied that Jesus would die for the nation.

52 And this was not only for that nation, but also so that He might unite into one body all the children of God who are scattered throughout the world.

53 From that day onward, they conspired together to put him to death.

54 As a result, Jesus no longer moved openly among the Jewish people. Instead, he left that place and went to a region near the wilderness, to a town called Ephraim, where he stayed with his disciples.

55 The Jewish Passover was approaching, and many people traveled from the countryside to Jerusalem before the festival to undergo the necessary purification rituals.

56 Then they began looking for Jesus and talking among themselves as they stood in the temple, saying, "What do you think? Will he not come to the festival?"

57 Now the chief priests and the Pharisees had issued an order that if anyone knew where Jesus was, they should report it so that He could be arrested.
