Psalms Chapter 60

1 (For the director of music, set to the tune of "Lily of the Covenant," a contemplative poem of David, for instruction; composed when he fought against Aram-Naharaim and Aram-Zobah, and when Joab returned and struck down twelve thousand Edomites in the Valley of Salt.) O God, You have rejected us; You have scattered us; You have been angry with us. Turn back to us and restore us.

2 You have caused the earth to quake; you have shattered it. Repair its fractures, for it is shaking.

3 You have shown your people difficult trials; you have made us drink the wine of bewilderment.

4 You have given a banner to those who revere you, so that it may be raised high for the sake of truth. Selah.

5 So that those you love may be rescued, save them by your power and answer my prayer.

6 God has spoken in His holiness; I will celebrate, I will apportion Shechem, and measure out the Valley of Succoth.

7 Gilead belongs to me, and Manasseh is mine; Ephraim is my stronghold, the source of my power; Judah is the one who upholds my law.

8 Moab is like a basin for me to wash my feet; over Edom I will toss my sandal in triumph. Philistia, shout in celebration because of me.

9 Who will bring me into the fortified city? Who will guide me into the land of Edom?

10 Will you not, O God, who once rejected us, and did not go forth with our armies, now act on our behalf?

11 Grant us deliverance from distress, for human assistance is futile.

12 With God's strength, we will act with courage and power, for He is the one who will crush and subdue our enemies.
