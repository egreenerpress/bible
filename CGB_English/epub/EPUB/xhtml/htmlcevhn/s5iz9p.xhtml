Leviticus Chapter 21

1 The LORD instructed Moses, saying, "Speak to the priests, the sons of Aaron, and tell them: No one among you is to become ceremonially unclean by coming into contact with a dead person, except for his closest relatives."

2 However, for his close relatives—that is, for his mother, father, son, daughter, or brother—

3 And for his sister, a virgin who is closely related to him and has never been married, he may not defile himself by approaching her.

4 However, he must not defile himself, even though he is a prominent leader among his people, by acting in a way that would dishonor or degrade himself.

5 They must not shave their heads bald, nor cut off the edges of their beards, nor make any gashes or marks in their skin.

6 They must be set apart as holy to their God and must not dishonor the name of their God, for they present the offerings made by fire to the LORD, the food of their God. Therefore, they must remain holy.

7 They must not marry a woman who is a prostitute or defiled, nor should they marry a woman who has been divorced from her husband, for the priest is holy to his God.

8 You must set him apart as holy, because he presents the bread of your God. He shall be holy to you, for I, the LORD, who sanctify you, am holy.

9 If a priest’s daughter defiles herself by engaging in prostitution, she dishonors her father. She must be put to death by burning.

10 The high priest, who is the foremost among his brothers, on whose head the anointing oil has been poured and who has been consecrated to wear the sacred garments, must not leave his head uncovered or tear his clothing.

11 He must not come near any dead body, nor make himself ceremonially unclean, even for his father or his mother.

12 The priest must not leave the sanctuary, nor defile the sanctuary of his God, for the sacred anointing oil of his God has consecrated him. I am the LORD.

13 And he must marry a woman who is a virgin.

14 A priest must not marry a widow, a divorced woman, a woman who has been defiled, or a prostitute. Instead, he is to take a virgin from his own people as his wife.

15 He must not dishonor his offspring among his people, for I, the LORD, set him apart as holy.

16 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

17 Tell Aaron this: If any of your descendants, throughout their generations, has a physical defect, he must not come forward to present the food offering to his God.

18 Any man who has a physical defect must not come near to serve: whether he is blind, lame, disfigured in the face, or has any kind of deformity.

19 Or a man who has a crippled foot or a crippled hand.

20 Or anyone who is hunchbacked, or unusually short, or has a defect in their eye, or suffers from a skin disease, or has scabs, or has damaged testicles.

21 No descendant of Aaron the priest who has any physical defect may approach to present the offerings made by fire to the Lord. If he has a defect, he is not to come near to offer the food of his God.

22 He is to partake of the food dedicated to his God, both from the most sacred offerings and from the holy offerings.

23 However, he must not enter behind the curtain or approach the altar, because he has a physical defect. He must not defile my sacred spaces, for I, the LORD, am the one who sanctifies them.

24 Moses relayed this message to Aaron, his sons, and all the people of Israel.
