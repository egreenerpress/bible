Colossians Chapter 2

1 I want you to understand the deep concern and intense struggle I have for you, for those in Laodicea, and for all who have not met me in person.

2 I pray that their hearts may be encouraged, united together in love, and brought to the full wealth of complete understanding, so that they may truly know the mystery of Godâ€”that is, the Father and Christ.

3 In whom are stored all the riches of wisdom and knowledge.

4 I say this so that no one may deceive you with persuasive but misleading arguments.

5 Even though I am not physically present with you, I am with you in spirit, rejoicing as I observe your orderly conduct and the firmness of your faith in Christ.

6 Since you have accepted Christ Jesus as your Lord, continue to live your lives in close relationship with him.

7 Firmly rooted and built up in him, and strengthened in your faith, just as you were taught, overflowing with gratitude.

8 Be careful that no one deceives you with empty philosophies and misleading arguments, which are based on human traditions and the basic principles of this world, rather than on Christ.

9 For in Him resides the complete essence of the divine nature in bodily form.

10 And you are made whole in him, who is the supreme authority over all rulers and powers.

11 In Christ, you have also undergone a spiritual circumcision, one not performed by human hands. This circumcision involves the removal of the sinful nature, accomplished through the work of Christ.

12 You were buried with him in baptism, and through faith in the powerful working of God, who raised him from the dead, you were also raised with him.

13 And you, who were spiritually dead because of your sins and the unregenerate state of your flesh, God has made alive together with Christ, having forgiven all your transgressions.

14 He has canceled the record of the legal demands that stood against us, which were opposed to us. He removed it entirely, nailing it to the cross.

15 And after disarming the rulers and authorities, He publicly displayed them as defeated, triumphing over them through His victory.

16 Therefore, let no one pass judgment on you regarding what you eat or drink, or concerning a religious festival, the observance of a new moon, or the Sabbath days.

17 These things are a foreshadowing of what is to come, but the true substance belongs to Christ.

18 Do not let anyone deceive you and deprive you of your reward by promoting false humility and the worship of angels. Such people delve into matters they have not seen, being arrogantly inflated by their own fleshly thinking.

19 And they are not holding firmly to the Head, from whom the whole body, supported and held together by its ligaments and sinews, grows with the growth that comes from God.

20 Since you have died with Christ and are no longer bound by the basic principles of this world, why do you still live as though you belong to it, submitting to its rules and regulations?

21 Do not touch, do not taste, do not handle.

22 All these things are destined to perish as they are used, based on the commands and teachings of human beings.

23 These practices may appear to be wise because they involve self-imposed worship, false humility, and harsh treatment of the body, but they have no real value in restraining the indulgence of the flesh.
