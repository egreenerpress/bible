Psalms Chapter 42

1 (To the director of music. A contemplative song of the sons of Korah.) Just as a deer longs for flowing streams, so my soul longs for you, O God.

2 My soul deeply longs for God, for the living God. When will I come and stand in His presence?

3 My tears have been my constant sustenance, both day and night, as they repeatedly taunt me, saying, “Where is your God?”

4 When I think about these things, I pour out my heart within me: for I used to go with the crowd, moving with them to the house of God, amid shouts of joy and thanksgiving, among a throng celebrating a sacred festival.

5 Why are you so downcast, my soul? Why are you so troubled within me? Put your hope in God, for I will yet praise him, my Savior and my God, who shows me his favor.

6 O my God, my soul is deeply troubled within me; therefore, I will remember You from the land of the Jordan and the heights of Hermon, from Mount Mizar.

7 The depths cry out to the depths at the roar of your waterfalls; all your breakers and waves have swept over me.

8 Yet the LORD will direct His steadfast love to me by day, and in the night His song will be with me—a prayer to the God who gives me life.

9 I will say to God, my fortress, "Why have you forgotten me? Why must I walk in sorrow because of the enemy's oppression?"

10 My enemies taunt me, cutting deep like a sword through my bones, as they constantly mock me, saying, “Where is your God now?”

11 Why are you so downcast, O my soul? Why are you so troubled within me? Place your hope in God, for I will yet praise Him, my Savior and my God.
