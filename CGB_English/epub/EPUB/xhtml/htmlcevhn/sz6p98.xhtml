John Chapter 16

1 I have told you these things so that you will not fall away or lose faith.

2 They will expel you from the synagogues. Indeed, the time is coming when anyone who kills you will believe they are offering service to God.

3 They will treat you in this way because they have not come to know the Father or me.

4 I have told you these things so that when the time comes, you will remember that I warned you about them. I did not tell you these things from the start, because I was with you.

5 But now I am going to the one who sent me, and none of you asks me, ‘Where are you going?’

6 Because I have told you these things, grief has overwhelmed your heart.

7 Nevertheless, I tell you the truth: It is better for you that I go away. For if I do not leave, the Helper will not come to you. But if I go, I will send Him to you.

8 When he comes, he will expose the world’s guilt concerning sin, righteousness, and judgment.

9 Concerning sin, because they do not believe in me.

10 Concerning righteousness, because I am going to the Father, and you will no longer see me;

11 Concerning judgment, because the ruler of this world has been condemned.

12 "I still have much to tell you, but you are not yet ready to understand it."

13 However, when the Spirit of truth comes, he will guide you into all truth. He will not speak on his own authority, but he will speak whatever he hears, and he will reveal to you what is to come.

14 He will bring me honor, for he will take what is mine and make it known to you.

15 Everything that belongs to the Father is also mine. This is why I said that he will take what is mine and reveal it to you.

16 In a short time, you will no longer see me; and then, after a little while, you will see me again, because I am going to the Father.

17 Then some of His disciples said to one another, "What does He mean when He tells us, 'In a little while, you will not see Me, and then after a little while, you will see Me,' and, 'Because I am going to the Father'?"

18 So they said, "What does he mean by saying, 'a little while'? We don't understand what he is talking about."

19 Jesus, aware that they wanted to ask him about this, said to them, "Are you discussing among yourselves what I meant when I said, 'In a little while, you will no longer see me, and then after a little while, you will see me again'?"

20 Truly, truly, I tell you, you will weep and mourn, but the world will rejoice. You will grieve, but your grief will be transformed into joy.

21 When a woman is in labor, she experiences pain because her time has come. But once she has given birth to the child, she no longer remembers the suffering, because of the joy that a person has been brought into the world.

22 You are now experiencing sorrow, but I will see you again, and your hearts will be filled with joy—a joy that no one can take away from you.

23 On that day, you will no longer need to ask me anything. Truly, I tell you, whatever you ask the Father in my name, he will give to you.

24 Until now, you have not asked for anything in my name. Ask, and you will receive, so that your joy may be complete.

25 I have spoken these things to you in figurative language, but the time is coming when I will no longer speak to you in such terms. Instead, I will reveal the Father to you clearly and openly.

26 On that day, you will ask in my name, and I do not need to tell you that I will plead with the Father on your behalf.

27 For the Father Himself loves you, because you have loved Me and have believed that I came forth from God.

28 I have come from the Father and entered into the world; now, I am leaving the world and returning to the Father.

29 His disciples said to him, "Now you are speaking clearly and not using figurative language."

30 Now we are certain that you know all things and have no need for anyone to question you. Because of this, we believe that you have come from God.

31 Jesus responded to them, "Do you truly believe now?"

32 Listen carefully: the time is coming—in fact, it has already arrived—when you will all be scattered, each going your own way, and you will leave me by myself. Yet I am not truly alone, because the Father is with me.

33 I have told you these things so that in me you may have peace. In the world, you will face trouble and hardship, but take heart and be courageous; I have conquered the world.
