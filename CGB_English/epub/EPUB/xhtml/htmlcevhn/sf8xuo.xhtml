Deuteronomy Chapter 8

1 You must carefully follow all the commands I am giving you today, so that you may live, increase in number, and enter and take possession of the land that the LORD promised to your ancestors.

2 You must remember the entire journey that the LORD your God led you through during these forty years in the wilderness. He did this to humble you, to test you, and to reveal what was in your heart—whether you would keep His commandments or not.

3 He brought you low and allowed you to hunger, then fed you with manna, which neither you nor your ancestors had known, so that He might teach you that human beings do not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of the LORD.

4 Your clothing did not wear out, nor did your feet swell, throughout these forty years.

5 You must also reflect deeply in your heart that just as a father disciplines his son, so the LORD your God disciplines you.

6 Therefore, you must obey the commandments of the LORD your God, living according to His ways and revering Him.

7 For the LORD your God is bringing you into a good land—a land with streams of water, with springs and deep sources that flow from the valleys and hills.

8 A land abundant with wheat, barley, vines, fig trees, and pomegranates; a land rich in olive oil and honey.

9 A land where you will eat bread without scarcity, and where you will lack nothing; a land whose rocks are rich in iron, and from whose hills you can mine copper.

10 When you have eaten and are satisfied, you shall give thanks to the LORD your God for the good land He has given you.

11 Be careful not to forget the LORD your God by failing to observe His commandments, His ordinances, and His statutes, which I am instructing you today.

12 Be careful that when you have eaten and are satisfied, and have built fine houses and settled in them,

13 When your herds and flocks increase, and your silver and gold grow abundant, and all that you possess becomes plentiful,

14 Then, when your heart becomes proud and you forget the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, from the place of slavery;

15 Who guided you through that vast and fearsome wilderness, where there were venomous snakes, scorpions, and parched land with no water? Who brought water out of the solid rock for you?

16 Who provided for you in the wilderness with manna, something your ancestors had never known, in order to humble you and test you, so that he might ultimately bring about your well-being in the days to come.

17 And you may think to yourself, "My own strength and the power of my hands have gained me this wealth."

18 But you must remember the LORD your God, for it is He who gives you the ability to produce wealth, so that He may confirm His covenant, which He swore to your ancestors, as it is today.

19 If you ever forget the LORD your God, turn to follow other gods, serve them, and bow down to them, I solemnly warn you today that you will certainly be destroyed.

20 Just as the nations that the LORD destroyed before you, so too will you perish, because you refused to obey the voice of the LORD your God.
