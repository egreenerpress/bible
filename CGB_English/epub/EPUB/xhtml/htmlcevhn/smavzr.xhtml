Job Chapter 28

1 Certainly, there is a source for silver and a place where gold is refined.

2 Iron is extracted from the earth, and copper is smelted from the rock.

3 He brings an end to darkness and thoroughly examines all that is perfect. He uncovers the deepest mysteries, even the hidden things concealed in the deepest shadows and the gloom of death.

4 A flood bursts forth from where people dwell; even the waters that were once trodden underfoot are forgotten. They dry up, they vanish away from humanity.

5 The earth produces bread from its soil, and beneath its surface, it is as if fire is churning.

6 The stones of that place are the source of sapphires, and its soil contains gold dust.

7 There is a way that no bird has discovered, and that the keen eye of the vulture has never glimpsed.

8 The young lions have not walked upon it, nor has the mighty lion crossed over it.

9 He reaches out his hand to the rock; he uproots the mountains from their foundations.

10 He carves out rivers through the solid rock, and his eye detects every hidden treasure.

11 He restrains the rivers from flooding, and he brings hidden things into the light.

12 But where can wisdom be found? And where does understanding reside?

13 No one knows its true value, nor can it be found anywhere among the living.

14 The deep says, “It is not within me,” and the sea declares, “It is not with me.”

15 It cannot be acquired with gold, nor can its value be measured by weighing out silver.

16 Its worth cannot be measured by the gold of Ophir, nor by the precious onyx or the sapphire.

17 Neither gold nor crystal can compare to it, nor can it be exchanged for ornaments of pure gold.

18 Do not speak of coral or pearls, for the value of wisdom surpasses even the finest rubies.

19 The finest topaz from Ethiopia cannot compare to it, nor can it be appraised with the purest gold.

20 From where does wisdom originate? And where can understanding be found?

21 Since it is concealed from the sight of all living beings and hidden even from the birds of the sky.

22 Destruction and Death declare, "We have heard reports of it with our own ears."

23 God comprehends the path to it, and He knows its exact location.

24 He observes the farthest reaches of the earth and sees everything beneath the entire heavens.

25 He determines the force of the winds and measures out the waters with precision.

26 When He established the laws governing the rain and determined the path for the lightning and thunder...

27 Then he observed it and made it known; he established it, indeed, and thoroughly examined it.

28 And to humanity He declared, "Listen carefully: to revere the Lord—that is true wisdom; and to turn away from evil—that is genuine understanding."
