Genesis Chapter 19

1 As evening fell, two angels arrived in Sodom. Lot was sitting at the city gate, and when he saw them, he stood up to greet them. He bowed low, his face to the ground, showing them deep respect.

2 And he said, “Please, my lords, come into your servant’s house and stay the night. Wash your feet, and then you can rise early and continue on your journey.” But they replied, “No, we will spend the night in the street.”

3 He urged them strongly, and they turned aside to him and entered his house. He prepared a meal for them, baking unleavened bread, and they ate together.

4 Before they went to bed, the men of the city—the men of Sodom—surrounded the house, both young and old, every single person from every part of the city.

5 Then they called out to Lot and said to him, "Where are the men who came to you tonight? Bring them out to us, so that we may have relations with them."

6 Lot stepped outside to meet them and closed the door behind him.

7 And he said, "Please, my brothers, do not act so wickedly."

8 Listen now, I have two daughters who have never been with a man. Please, let me bring them out to you, and you may do with them as you see fit. But do not harm these men, for they have come under the protection of my roof.

9 They said, "Step aside!" Then they added, "This man came here as a foreigner, and now he wants to act as our judge! We’ll treat you worse than we treat them." They aggressively pushed against Lot and moved closer to breaking down the door.

10 But the men reached out their hands, brought Lot inside the house with them, and closed the door.

11 Then they struck the men who were at the entrance of the house with blindness, both the young and the old, so that they grew exhausted trying to find the door.

12 The men said to Lot, "Do you have anyone else here? Bring out your sons-in-law, your sons, your daughters, and anyone else you have in the city. Lead them out of this place."

13 We are going to destroy this place, for the outcry against its people has grown so great before the LORD that He has sent us to bring about its destruction.

14 Lot went out and spoke to his sons-in-law, who were pledged to marry his daughters, saying, "Get up and leave this place, for the LORD is about to destroy this city!" But his sons-in-law thought he was joking and did not take him seriously.

15 When morning came, the angels urgently urged Lot, saying, "Get up, take your wife and your two daughters who are here with you, or you will be destroyed when the city is punished for its wickedness."

16 As Lot hesitated, the men took hold of his hand, his wife’s hand, and the hands of his two daughters—because the LORD was showing him mercy—and led them out of the city, placing them safely outside its limits.

17 When they had brought them outside, he said, "Flee for your lives! Do not look back, and do not stop anywhere in the plain. Escape to the mountains, or you will be destroyed."

18 And Lot pleaded with them, saying, "Please, no, my lords!"

19 Look now, your servant has found favor in your eyes, and you have shown great kindness to me by sparing my life. But I cannot flee to the mountains, for fear that disaster will overtake me and I will die.

20 Look, this city here is close enough to escape to, and it is small. Please, let me flee there—isn’t it just a small place?—so that I may survive and my life will be spared.

21 And He said to him, "Listen, I have granted your request in this matter as well; I will not destroy this city about which you have spoken."

22 Hurry, escape to that place, for I cannot do anything until you arrive there. That is why the city was named Zoar.

23 The sun had risen over the earth when Lot arrived in Zoar.

24 Then the LORD caused burning sulfur and fire to rain down from the heavens upon Sodom and Gomorrah, sent by the LORD Himself.

25 He utterly destroyed those cities, along with the entire plain, all the inhabitants of the cities, and everything that grew on the land.

26 But his wife turned and looked back behind him, and she was transformed into a pillar of salt.

27 Abraham rose early in the morning and went to the place where he had stood in the presence of the LORD.

28 He turned his gaze toward Sodom and Gomorrah, and toward the entire region of the plain, and as he looked, he saw that the smoke from the land was rising like the smoke from a furnace.

29 When God brought destruction upon the cities of the plain, He remembered Abraham and rescued Lot from the midst of the devastation. As God overthrew the cities where Lot had been living, He ensured Lot’s escape from the catastrophe.

30 Lot departed from Zoar and settled in the mountains, taking his two daughters with him, because he was afraid to remain in Zoar. He made his home in a cave, along with his two daughters.

31 The older daughter said to the younger, "Our father is old, and there is no man in the land to marry us, as is the custom everywhere.

32 "Come, let us give our father wine to drink, and then we will lie with him, so that we may keep our father's lineage alive."

33 That night, they gave their father wine to drink. The older daughter went in and lay with her father, but he was unaware when she lay down or when she got up.

34 The next day, the firstborn said to her younger sister, "Listen, last night I slept with our father. Let’s get him to drink wine again tonight, and then you go in and lie with him. This way, we can preserve our father’s lineage through his offspring."

35 That night, they again gave their father wine to drink. The younger daughter went in and slept with him, but he was unaware when she lay down or when she got up.

36 Both of Lot's daughters became pregnant by their father.

37 The firstborn gave birth to a son and named him Moab. He is the ancestor of the Moabites, as they are known to this day.

38 The younger daughter also gave birth to a son and named him Ben-ammi. He became the ancestor of the Ammonites, and his descendants remain to this day.
