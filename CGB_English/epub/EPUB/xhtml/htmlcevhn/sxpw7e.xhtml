Proverbs Chapter 22

1 A good reputation is more desirable than great wealth, and being held in high esteem is better than silver and gold.

2 The wealthy and the poor encounter one another, for the LORD is the Creator of them all.

3 A wise person perceives danger ahead and takes cover, but the naive continue forward and suffer the consequences.

4 Through humility and reverence for the LORD come wealth, honor, and life.

5 Thorns and traps lie in the path of the deceitful; but whoever guards their life will stay far from them.

6 Instruct a child according to the path appropriate for them, and even when they grow old, they will not turn away from it.

7 The wealthy dominate the poor, and the one who borrows becomes subservient to the lender.

8 Whoever plants wrongdoing will harvest emptiness, and the instrument of their wrath will come to nothing.

9 The one who looks upon others with generosity will be blessed, for they share their food with those in need.

10 Remove the one who mocks and ridicules, and conflict will disappear; indeed, quarreling and disgrace will come to an end.

11 Whoever loves purity of heart and speaks with graciousness will find favor with the king and be counted as his friend.

12 The eyes of the LORD watch over knowledge, and He frustrates the words of the deceitful.

13 The lazy person says, "There is a lion outside! I will be killed in the streets!"

14 The words of an immoral woman are like a deep pit; whoever is detested by the LORD will fall into it.

15 Foolishness is deeply rooted in a child's heart, but disciplined correction will remove it from him.

16 Whoever exploits the poor to grow their wealth, and whoever gives gifts to the wealthy, will surely end up in poverty.

17 Listen closely and pay attention to the words of the wise; focus your heart on understanding the wisdom I share.

18 It is a delightful thing when you hold them close within your heart; they will also be ready on your lips.

19 So that your confidence may rest in the LORD, I have made these things known to you today, yes, to you personally.

20 Have I not written to you profound truths, filled with wise counsel and knowledge?

21 I want to make you fully understand the reliability of truthful words, so that you may confidently respond with words of truth to those who inquire of you.

22 Do not take advantage of the poor because they are poor, nor crush the oppressed in the place of judgment.

23 For the LORD will defend their case and take vengeance on those who have plundered them, stripping away the life of those who caused their ruin.

24 Do not form a close friendship with a hot-tempered person, and do not associate with someone prone to rage.

25 Be careful not to adopt his ways, or you will bring a trap upon your soul.

26 Do not be among those who hastily make pledges or those who guarantee the debts of others.

27 If you have nothing to repay, why should he seize your bed from beneath you?

28 Do not move the ancient boundary markers that your ancestors established.

29 Do you see someone who is diligent and skillful in their work? Such a person will rise to stand before kings; they will not remain in the company of insignificant people.
