Isaiah Chapter 56

1 This is what the LORD says: Uphold justice and act with fairness, for my deliverance is close at hand, and my righteousness is about to be made known.

2 Blessed is the person who does this, and the individual who holds firmly to it—who observes the Sabbath without defiling it and refrains from committing any wrongdoing.

3 Do not let the foreigner who has committed themselves to the LORD say, “The LORD has completely excluded me from his people.” Nor let the eunuch say, “Look, I am like a barren tree.”

4 For this is what the LORD says to the eunuchs who honor my Sabbaths, choose what pleases me, and hold firmly to my covenant:

5 To them I will grant within my house and within my walls a memorial and a name more enduring than that of sons and daughters. I will give them an everlasting name that will never be destroyed.

6 Furthermore, the foreigners who unite themselves with the LORD, to serve Him, to love the name of the LORD, and to be His servants—all who honor the Sabbath by not defiling it and who hold firmly to My covenant—

7 I will bring them to my holy mountain and fill them with joy in my house of prayer. Their burnt offerings and sacrifices will be accepted on my altar, for my house will be called a house of prayer for all people.

8 The Lord GOD, who gathers the exiles of Israel, declares: "I will gather still others to them, in addition to those already gathered."

9 All you wild animals of the field, come and devour; yes, all you beasts of the forest, gather and feed.

10 The watchmen are blind; they lack understanding. They are all mute dogs, unable to bark—lying down, sleeping, and loving to slumber.

11 Indeed, they are like ravenous dogs, never satisfied; they are shepherds who lack understanding. Each one turns to their own path, seeking personal gain from every direction.

12 They say, "Come, let us get wine, and we will drink our fill of strong drink. Tomorrow will be just like today, only even better and more plentiful."
