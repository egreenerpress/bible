Hebrews Chapter 3

1 Therefore, holy brothers and sisters, who share in the heavenly calling, fix your thoughts on Jesus, the Apostle and High Priest whom we confess.

2 He was faithful to the one who appointed him, just as Moses was faithful in all of God's household.

3 This man was deemed worthy of greater honor than Moses, just as the one who builds a house deserves more respect than the house itself.

4 Every house is constructed by someone, but the one who built all things is God.

5 Moses was indeed faithful in all God’s household, serving as a steward, to bear witness to what would later be proclaimed.

6 But Christ is faithful as a Son over God’s household. And we are that household, if we hold firmly to our confidence and the hope in which we boast, steadfast to the very end.

7 Therefore, as the Holy Spirit says: "Today, if you hear his voice,

8 Do not harden your hearts, as you did at Meribah, during the time of testing in the wilderness.

9 When your ancestors tested me, challenged me, and witnessed my deeds for forty years.

10 Therefore, I was deeply troubled with that generation, and I declared, "Their hearts are continually led astray; they have not understood my ways."

11 Therefore, I declared in my anger, "They will never enter into my rest."

12 Be careful, brothers and sisters, so that none of you develops a wicked, unbelieving heart that turns away from the living God.

13 Encourage one another every day, as long as it is still called "today," so that none of you may become hardened by sin's deceitful ways.

14 For we become sharers in Christ’s blessings if we firmly maintain our initial confidence and commitment until the very end.

15 As it is written: "Today, if you hear his voice, do not harden your hearts as you did in the rebellion."

16 For some, when they heard, rebelled; however, not all who came out of Egypt under Moses' leadership did so.

17 But with whom was God displeased for forty years? Was it not with those who sinned, whose bodies fell in the wilderness?

18 And to whom did God swear that they would not enter into His rest, if not to those who did not believe?

19 Therefore, we understand that they were unable to enter because of their lack of faith.
