II Chronicles Chapter 11

1 When Rehoboam arrived in Jerusalem, he assembled from the tribes of Judah and Benjamin a force of one hundred and eighty thousand carefully selected men, all skilled warriors, to wage war against Israel and restore the kingdom to Rehoboam.

2 But the word of the LORD came to Shemaiah, the man of God, saying,

3 Go and speak to Rehoboam, the son of Solomon, king of Judah, and to all the people of Israel living in Judah and Benjamin. Tell them this:

4 This is what the LORD says: "You must not go up or fight against your fellow Israelites. Each of you, return to your own home, for this situation has been brought about by me." So they heeded the words of the LORD and turned back from advancing against Jeroboam.

5 Rehoboam settled in Jerusalem and constructed fortified cities throughout Judah for protection.

6 He also built Bethlehem, Etam, and Tekoa.

7 And Beth-zur, and Socoh, and Adullam.

8 And Gath, and Mareshah, and Ziph.

9 And Adoraim, Lachish, and Azekah.

10 And Zorah, Aijalon, and Hebron, which are fortified cities located in the territories of Judah and Benjamin.

11 He strengthened the fortresses, appointed commanders over them, and stored up supplies of food, oil, and wine.

12 In every city, he placed shields and spears, fortifying them greatly, with the support of Judah and Benjamin.

13 And the priests and the Levites from all over Israel came to him from every region where they lived.

14 The Levites abandoned their pasturelands and their property, and they came to Judah and Jerusalem because Jeroboam and his sons had excluded them from serving as priests to the LORD.

15 He appointed priests for the high places, for the worship of idols, and for the calf images he had made.

16 After them, people from all the tribes of Israel who were determined to seek the LORD, the God of Israel, came to Jerusalem to offer sacrifices to the LORD, the God of their ancestors.

17 So they fortified the kingdom of Judah and strengthened Rehoboam, the son of Solomon, for three years. During those three years, they followed the ways of David and Solomon.

18 Rehoboam married Mahalath, the daughter of Jerimoth, who was the son of David. He also married Abihail, the daughter of Eliab, who was the son of Jesse.

19 She bore him children: Jeush, Shamariah, and Zaham.

20 After her, he married Maacah, the daughter of Absalom, who bore him Abijah, Attai, Ziza, and Shelomith.

21 Rehoboam favored Maachah, the daughter of Absalom, more than all his other wives and concubines. (He had taken eighteen wives and sixty concubines, and he fathered twenty-eight sons and sixty daughters.)

22 Rehoboam appointed Abijah, the son of Maacah, as the leader among his brothers, giving him the highest position. He did this because he intended to establish him as king.

23 He acted with great wisdom and strategically placed all his sons throughout the territories of Judah and Benjamin, assigning them to every fortified city. He provided them with ample provisions and supplies. Additionally, he took many wives for himself.
