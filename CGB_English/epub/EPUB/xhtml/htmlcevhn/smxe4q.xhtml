Psalms Chapter 49

1 (To the choirmaster. A psalm for the descendants of Korah.) Listen to this, all you nations; pay attention, all you people of the earth:

2 Both the humble and the exalted, the wealthy and the needy, stand together as equals.

3 I will speak words of wisdom, and the thoughts of my heart will focus on understanding.

4 I will listen attentively to a parable; I will reveal my profound sayings through the music of the harp.

5 Why should I be afraid in times of trouble, when the sins I have committed surround me?

6 Those who place their confidence in their wealth and take pride in the abundance of their riches;

7 No one can ever redeem another person, nor can they offer God a payment to secure their release.

8 "The redemption of their life is of great value, and it will never be sufficient to last forever."

9 That he might continue to live forever and never experience decay.

10 For he observes that even the wise die; the foolish and the senseless also perish, and they leave behind their possessions to others.

11 Deep within their hearts, they believe that their homes will endure forever and their residences will last for all generations. They even name their lands after themselves.

12 Yet, even when a person attains honor and high status, they do not endure; they are like the animals that perish.

13 This is the path of their foolishness, yet their descendants continue to embrace their words. Selah.

14 Like sheep, they are destined for the grave; death will devour them. The upright will prevail over them in the morning, and their splendor will waste away in the grave, far from their former dwelling.

15 But God will rescue my life from the grasp of the grave, for He will take me to Himself. Selah.

16 Do not be afraid or troubled when someone becomes wealthy, or when the splendor of their household grows great.

17 For when he dies, he will take nothing with him; his wealth and honor will not follow him to the grave.

18 Though he lived his life indulging himself and speaking well of his own soul, people will praise you when you prosper and do well for yourself.

19 He will join the company of his ancestors; they will never again see the light of day.

20 A person who attains honor and status but lacks understanding is no different than the animals that perish.
