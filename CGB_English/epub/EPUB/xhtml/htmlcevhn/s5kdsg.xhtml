Job Chapter 41

1 Can you catch Leviathan with a hook or tie down its tongue with a cord you lower?

2 Can you fasten a hook into its nose or pierce its jaw with a thorn?

3 Will he plead with you earnestly? Will he speak to you with gentle words?

4 Will he enter into an agreement with you? Will you take him as a permanent servant?

5 Would you treat him like a plaything, as one might handle a bird? Or would you tie him up for your young women to amuse themselves with?

6 Will his friends feast on him? Will they divide him among the traders?

7 Can you pierce his skin with barbed hooks or his head with fishing spears?

8 Place your hand upon him; consider the struggle, and do not proceed further.

9 Look, anyone who places their hope in him will be disappointed. Will not even the sight of him cause someone to be overwhelmed with fear?

10 No creature is so fierce that it dares to rouse him; who then could possibly stand against me?

11 Who has acted before me, that I should repay them? Everything under the entire heavens belongs to me.

12 I will not hide his attributes, nor his strength, nor his perfect symmetry.

13 Who can uncover the surface of his majestic robe? And who dares to approach him with a double bridle in hand?

14 Who can open the jaws of its mouth? Its teeth are fearsome all around.

15 His scales are his source of pride, tightly bound together as if sealed shut.

16 The stars are so closely positioned that no space exists between them.

17 They are closely connected to one another, firmly bound together, so that they cannot be separated.

18 When he sneezes, a flash of light bursts forth, and his eyes gleam like the first rays of dawn.

19 From his mouth come blazing torches, and fiery sparks shoot forth.

20 Smoke pours from his nostrils, like steam rising from a boiling pot or cauldron.

21 His breath ignites coals, and a flame bursts forth from his mouth.

22 In his neck resides great power, and grief is transformed into joy in his presence.

23 The layers of his flesh are tightly bound together, solid and unyielding; they cannot be shaken or displaced.

24 His heart is as unyielding as a stone, indeed, as hard as the lower millstone.

25 When God rises up in His power, even the mighty are filled with fear; in the face of His overwhelming might, they are driven to purify themselves.

26 The sword of anyone who attacks him will not prevail; neither the spear, the arrow, nor the coat of armor will succeed.

27 He regards iron as if it were straw, and bronze as if it were decayed wood.

28 The arrow cannot force him to flee; slingstones are like mere chaff to him.

29 He regards arrows as mere straw; he scoffs at the brandishing of a spear.

30 Beneath him lie jagged stones; he scatters sharp, piercing objects across the muddy ground.

31 He causes the depths of the ocean to churn like a boiling pot; he makes the sea foam and swirl like a cauldron of bubbling oil.

32 He creates a shining path behind him; one might imagine the depths of the sea to be ancient and gray.

33 On earth, there is none like him, a creature without fear.

34 He observes all that is lofty and exalted; he reigns as king over all who are arrogant and prideful.
