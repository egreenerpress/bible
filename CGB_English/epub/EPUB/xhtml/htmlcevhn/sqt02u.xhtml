Matthew Chapter 10

1 Jesus summoned his twelve disciples and gave them authority over unclean spirits, enabling them to drive them out and to heal every kind of illness and disease.

2 Here are the names of the twelve apostles: First, Simon, also known as Peter, and his brother Andrew; James, the son of Zebedee, and his brother John;

3 Philip and Bartholomew; Thomas and Matthew the tax collector; James the son of Alphaeus, and Lebbaeus, who was also called Thaddaeus.

4 Simon the Zealot, and Judas Iscariot, the one who betrayed him.

5 Jesus sent out these twelve with specific instructions, saying, "Do not go among the Gentiles or enter any town of the Samaritans."

6 Instead, go to the people of Israel who are like lost sheep.

7 As you go, proclaim this message: "The kingdom of heaven is near."

8 Heal those who are ill, cleanse those with leprosy, bring the dead back to life, and drive out evil spirits. Just as you have received these gifts freely, so also give freely.

9 "Do not carry gold, silver, or copper in your money belts."

10 "Do not take a bag for your journey, nor an extra coat, nor sandals, nor a staff; for the worker deserves his provisions."

11 When you enter any city or town, find out who there is worthy of your stay, and remain with them until you leave that place.

12 When you enter a house, greet those who live there.

13 If the household is deserving, may your blessing of peace rest upon it; but if it is not deserving, may your blessing of peace return to you.

14 And if anyone does not welcome you or listen to your message, as you leave that house or town, shake the dust off your feet.

15 Truly, I tell you, it will be more bearable for the land of Sodom and Gomorrah on the day of judgment than for that city.

16 Listen carefully: I am sending you out like sheep among wolves. Therefore, be as shrewd as snakes and as innocent as doves.

17 But be cautious of people, for they will hand you over to the ruling councils, and they will flog you in their places of worship.

18 You will be brought before governors and kings because of your allegiance to me, as a witness to them and to the nations.

19 When they hand you over to the authorities, do not worry about how you should respond or what you should say, for in that very moment, you will be given the words to speak.

20 For it is not you who are speaking, but the Spirit of your Father who speaks through you.

21 A brother will betray his brother to death, and a father will betray his own child. Children will rise up against their parents and have them put to death.

22 You will be hated by everyone because of your association with me, but whoever remains steadfast and faithful until the end will be delivered and saved.

23 But if they persecute you in one city, escape to another. Truly I tell you, you will not have gone through all the towns of Israel before the Son of Man comes.

24 The disciple is not greater than their teacher, nor is the servant superior to their master.

25 A disciple should be content to be like their teacher, and a servant like their master. If they have called the head of the household "Beelzebul," how much more will they insult those who belong to his household?

26 Do not be afraid of them, for there is nothing concealed that will not be brought to light, and nothing hidden that will not be made known.

27 What I reveal to you in private, proclaim openly; and what you hear whispered in your ear, announce boldly for all to hear.

28 Do not be afraid of those who can kill the body but cannot kill the soul. Instead, fear the one who has the power to destroy both soul and body in hell.

29 Are not two sparrows sold for a small coin? Yet not one of them falls to the ground apart from your Fatherâ€™s will.

30 Indeed, every single hair on your head has been counted.

31 Therefore, do not be afraid; you are worth far more than many sparrows.

32 Therefore, whoever acknowledges me before others, I will also acknowledge before my Father in heaven.

33 But whoever disowns me in the presence of others, I will also disown them in the presence of my Father who is in heaven.

34 Do not suppose that I have come to bring peace to the earth. I did not come to bring peace, but division and conflict.

35 For I have come to cause division between a man and his father, and a daughter and her mother, and a daughter-in-law and her mother-in-law.

36 A person's enemies will be those of their own household.

37 Whoever values father or mother more than me is not deserving of me, and whoever values son or daughter more than me is not deserving of me.

38 Whoever does not take up their cross and follow me is not worthy of me.

39 Whoever seeks to hold on to their life will ultimately lose it, but whoever is willing to give up their life for my sake will truly find it.

40 Whoever welcomes you welcomes me, and whoever welcomes me welcomes the one who sent me.

41 Whoever welcomes a prophet because they recognize them as a prophet will receive the same reward as a prophet. Likewise, whoever welcomes a righteous person because they recognize them as righteous will receive the same reward as a righteous person.

42 And whoever offers even a cup of cold water to one of these humble ones, simply because they belong to a disciple, I assure you, they will certainly not miss out on their reward.
