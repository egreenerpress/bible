Mark Chapter 1

1 The origin of the good news about Jesus Christ, the Son of God.

2 As it is written in the prophets: "Look, I am sending my messenger ahead of you, who will prepare the way before you."

3 A voice calls out in the wilderness: "Prepare the way for the Lord; make a straight path for him."

4 John was baptizing in the wilderness and proclaiming a baptism of repentance for the forgiveness of sins.

5 People from all over the land of Judea and those from Jerusalem went out to him. They were baptized by him in the Jordan River, openly confessing their sins.

6 John wore a garment made of camel’s hair and had a leather belt around his waist. His diet consisted of locusts and wild honey.

7 And he proclaimed, saying, "There is one coming after me who is far greater than I am—I am not even worthy to bend down and untie the straps of his sandals."

8 I have baptized you with water, but he will baptize you with the Holy Spirit.

9 During that time, Jesus came from Nazareth in Galilee and was baptized by John in the Jordan River.

10 Immediately, as he came up out of the water, he saw the heavens torn open, and the Spirit descending upon him like a dove.

11 And a voice came from heaven, saying, "You are my beloved Son; in you I take great delight."

12 And at once the Spirit compelled him to go into the wilderness.

13 He remained in the wilderness for forty days, enduring temptation from Satan. During that time, he lived among the wild animals, and angels came to serve and care for him.

14 After John had been imprisoned, Jesus went into Galilee, proclaiming the good news of the kingdom of God.

15 He proclaimed, "The appointed time has now come, and the kingdom of God is near. Turn away from your sins and put your trust in the good news."

16 As Jesus was walking along the shore of the Sea of Galilee, he saw Simon and his brother Andrew casting a net into the water, for they were fishermen.

17 And Jesus said to them, "Follow me, and I will transform you into those who gather people for God’s kingdom."

18 Immediately, they left their nets behind and followed him.

19 After walking a short distance from there, he saw James, the son of Zebedee, and his brother John, who were in the boat repairing their nets.

20 Immediately, Jesus called them, and they left their father Zebedee in the boat with the hired workers and followed him.

21 They went to Capernaum, and immediately on the Sabbath, He entered the synagogue and began to teach.

22 The people were amazed at His teaching, for He instructed them as one who possessed true authority, unlike the teachers of the law.

23 In their synagogue, there was a man possessed by an unclean spirit, and he shouted aloud,

24 They cried out, "Leave us alone! What do you want with us, Jesus of Nazareth? Have you come to destroy us? I know who you are—the Holy One of God!"

25 And Jesus sternly commanded him, saying, "Be silent, and come out of him!"

26 When the unclean spirit violently convulsed the man and shouted with a loud cry, it came out of him.

27 Everyone was utterly astonished, so much so that they began discussing among themselves, saying, "What is this? What kind of new teaching is this? He commands even the unclean spirits with authority, and they obey Him!"

28 And at once his reputation spread far and wide across the entire surrounding region of Galilee.

29 As soon as they left the synagogue, they went into the house of Simon and Andrew, accompanied by James and John.

30 But Simon’s mother-in-law was lying sick with a fever, and immediately they told him about her.

31 He approached her, took her by the hand, and helped her to her feet. At once, the fever left her, and she began to serve them.

32 In the evening, after the sun had set, they brought to Him all who were sick and those who were tormented by evil spirits.

33 The entire town gathered at the door.

34 He healed many who were afflicted with various illnesses and drove out many demons. However, he did not allow the demons to speak, because they recognized who he was.

35 Early in the morning, long before dawn, he got up, left the house, and went to a secluded place, where he prayed.

36 Simon and those who were with him pursued him.

37 When they found him, they said to him, "Everyone is looking for you."

38 And he said to them, "Let us go to the nearby towns so that I may proclaim the message there as well, for this is the very reason I have come."

39 He proclaimed his message in their synagogues across the whole region of Galilee and drove out evil spirits.

40 A man with leprosy came to him, pleading earnestly, and kneeling before him, said, "If you are willing, you have the power to make me clean."

41 And Jesus, filled with compassion, reached out his hand, touched him, and said, "I am willing; be made clean."

42 As soon as he finished speaking, the leprosy immediately left him, and he was made clean.

43 And he sternly warned him and immediately sent him away.

44 And He said to him, "Make sure you don’t tell anyone about this. Instead, go on your way, present yourself to the priest, and offer the sacrifices that Moses commanded for your cleansing. This will serve as evidence to them."

45 However, he went out and began to proclaim it widely, spreading the news far and wide. As a result, Jesus could no longer enter the city openly but stayed in remote, deserted areas. Yet people came to him from every direction.
