Psalms Chapter 7

1 (A lament of David, which he sang to the LORD, concerning the words of Cush the Benjamite.) O LORD my God, in You I place my trust; rescue me from all who pursue me, and deliver me.

2 Otherwise, he might tear my soul apart like a lion, ripping it to pieces, with no one to rescue me.

3 O LORD, my God, if I have committed this wrongdoing, if there is guilt in my actions,

4 If I have repaid harm to someone who was at peace with me, or if I have handed over my enemy without just cause,

5 May the enemy pursue my life and seize it; yes, let him crush my existence to the ground and reduce my dignity to dust. Selah.

6 Rise up, O LORD, in your anger; stand up against the fury of my enemies. Awaken on my behalf to carry out the judgment you have decreed.

7 Therefore, the assembly of the people will surround you; for their sake, rise up and return to your place of authority.

8 The LORD will judge the people; judge me, O LORD, according to my righteousness and the integrity within me.

9 Oh, may the wickedness of the wicked come to an end, but uphold the righteous. For the God of justice examines the hearts and innermost thoughts.

10 My protection comes from God, who delivers those who are sincere and upright in heart.

11 God upholds justice for the righteous, and His anger burns against the wicked continually.

12 If he does not turn back, he will sharpen his sword; he has drawn his bow and prepared it for action.

13 He has also prepared deadly weapons for himself; he readies his arrows to strike down those who pursue him.

14 Look, he labors to produce wickedness, conceives trouble, and gives birth to deceit.

15 He dug a pit and hollowed it out, but he has fallen into the hole he himself prepared.

16 The harm he intended will fall back on his own head, and his violent actions will descend upon his own skull.

17 I will give praise to the LORD because of His perfect righteousness, and I will sing praises to the name of the LORD, the Most High.
