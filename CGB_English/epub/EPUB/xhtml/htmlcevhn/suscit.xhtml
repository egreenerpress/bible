Isaiah Chapter 3

1 Listen carefully, for the Lord, the Almighty God of all armies, is about to remove from Jerusalem and Judah every source of support and sustenance—the entire supply of bread and the entire supply of water.

2 The mighty warrior, the skilled soldier, the judge, the prophet, the wise counselor, and the elder—all these will be taken away.

3 The commander of fifty, the respected leader, the wise advisor, the skilled craftsman, and the persuasive speaker.

4 I will appoint children to be their leaders, and infants will govern them.

5 The people will be oppressed, each by another and each by their neighbor. The young will act arrogantly toward the elderly, and the lowly will disrespect those who are honorable.

6 When someone takes hold of their brother from their father’s household and says, “You have clothing, so you should be our leader, and let this ruin be under your authority,”

7 On that day, he will declare with an oath, saying, "I will not act as a healer, for in my house there is neither food nor clothing. Do not make me a leader of the people."

8 Jerusalem lies in ruins, and Judah has fallen, for their words and their actions stand in opposition to the LORD, provoking the gaze of His glorious presence.

9 The expression on their faces testifies against them; they openly proclaim their sin like Sodom, making no attempt to conceal it. How terrible it will be for them! For they have brought harm upon themselves.

10 Tell the righteous that it will go well with them, for they will enjoy the rewards of their deeds.

11 Trouble awaits the wicked! They will face calamity, for they will receive the consequences of their own actions. What their hands have done will be repaid to them.

12 Concerning my people, their oppressors are mere children, and women govern over them. O my people, those who guide you are leading you astray, and they are ruining the direction of your ways.

13 The LORD rises to present His case and takes His stand to judge the people.

14 The LORD will bring judgment against the elders and leaders of His people, for you have devoured the vineyard; the plunder taken from the poor is stored in your houses.

15 Why do you crush my people and oppress the poor, grinding their faces into the dust? This is what the Lord GOD Almighty declares.

16 Furthermore, the LORD declares: Because the women of Zion are arrogant, walking with heads held high and eyes full of pride, moving with deliberate steps and swaying as they go, and making a jingling sound with their feet:

17 Therefore, the Lord will afflict with sores the scalps of the women of Zion, and the Lord will expose their private shame.

18 On that day, the Lord will remove the pride of their jingling anklets, their headbands, and their crescent-shaped ornaments.

19 The chains, the bracelets, and the veils.

20 The head coverings, the anklets, the headbands, the perfume boxes, and the earrings.

21 The earrings and nose rings,

22 The various sets of fine clothing, the cloaks, the shawls, and the decorative hair accessories.

23 The mirrors, the fine linen garments, the head coverings, and the veils.

24 This is what will happen: instead of a pleasant fragrance, there will be a foul stench; instead of a fine belt, there will be a torn rag; instead of well-groomed hair, there will be baldness; instead of elegant clothing, there will be rough sackcloth; and instead of beauty, there will be scorching and ruin.

25 Your men will die by the sword, and your warriors will fall in battle.

26 And her gates will weep and grieve; she, now desolate, will sit on the ground in sorrow.
