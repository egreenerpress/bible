Joshua Chapter 19

1 The second allotment of land was drawn for Simeon, that is, for the tribe of the descendants of Simeon according to their clans. Their allotted territory lay within the inheritance of the descendants of Judah.

2 They received as their inheritance Beersheba (also called Sheba) and Moladah.

3 And Hazar-shual, Balah, and Azem.

4 And Eltolad, Bethul, and Hormah.

5 And Ziklag, Beth-marcaboth, and Hazar-susah.

6 And Beth-lebaoth, and Sharuhenâ€”thirteen cities along with their surrounding villages.

7 Ain, Rimmon, Ether, and Ashan; four cities along with their surrounding villages.

8 All the surrounding villages near these cities, extending as far as Baalath-beer and Ramath of the Negev, were included. This was the inheritance allotted to the tribe of the descendants of Simeon, distributed according to their clans.

9 The inheritance of the tribe of Simeon was taken from the portion allotted to the tribe of Judah, because the share given to Judah was more than they needed. As a result, the tribe of Simeon received their inheritance within the territory of Judah.

10 The third allotment of land fell to the descendants of Zebulun, divided according to their clans. The boundary of their inheritance extended as far as Sarid.

11 Their boundary extended toward the west, passing by Maralah and reaching as far as Dabbasheth, then continuing to the stream that flows near Jokneam.

12 The boundary then turned from Sarid eastward toward the sunrise, reaching the border of Chisloth-tabor. From there, it extended to Daberath and continued upward to Japhia.

13 And from there it continues eastward to Gittah-hepher, then to Ittah-kazin, and extends out to Remmon-methoar as far as Neah.

14 The boundary then turns northward toward Hannathon and ends at the valley of Jiphthah-el.

15 And Kattath, Nahallal, Shimron, Idalah, and Bethlehem: twelve cities along with their surrounding villages.

16 This is the inheritance allotted to the descendants of Zebulun, divided according to their clans, including these cities and their surrounding villages.

17 The fourth allotment of land was assigned to Issachar, for the descendants of Issachar according to their clans.

18 Their territory extended toward Jezreel, Chesulloth, and Shunem.

19 And Haphraim, Shion, and Anaharath.

20 And Rabbith, and Kishion, and Abez,

21 And Remeth, and En-gannim, and En-haddah, and Beth-pazzez;

22 The boundary extends to Tabor, Shahazimah, and Beth-shemesh, and its farthest limit reaches the Jordan River. This includes sixteen cities along with their surrounding villages.

23 This is the allotted inheritance for the tribe of the descendants of Issachar, divided according to their clans, including the cities and their surrounding villages.

24 The fifth portion of land was allotted to the tribe of Asher, divided according to their clans.

25 Their territory included Helkath, Hali, Beten, and Achshaph.

26 And Alammelech, Amad, and Misheal; it extends westward to Carmel and to Shihor-libnath.

27 The boundary then turns eastward toward Beth-dagon, extends to Zebulun, and reaches the valley of Jiphthah-el on the northern side of Beth-emek and Neiel. From there, it continues to Cabul on the left side.

28 And Hebron, Rehob, Hammon, and Kanah, extending as far as Great Sidon;

29 Then the boundary turns toward Ramah and extends to the fortified city of Tyre; it then turns toward Hosah, and its limits reach the sea at the region of Achzib.

30 Ummah, Aphek, and Rehob: twenty-two cities, along with their surrounding villages.

31 This is the portion allotted to the tribe of the descendants of Asher, divided according to their clans, including these cities and their surrounding villages.

32 The sixth portion of land was allotted to the descendants of Naphtali, distributed according to their clans and families.

33 Their territory extended from Heleph, from the oak in Zaanannim, and included Adami, Nekeb, and Jabneel, as far as Lakum. Its boundary ended at the Jordan River.

34 Then the boundary turns westward toward Aznoth-tabor and continues from there to Hukkok. It reaches the territory of Zebulun on the south and extends to Asher on the west, and to Judah at the Jordan River on the east.

35 The fortified cities are Ziddim, Zer, Hammath, Rakkath, and Chinnereth.

36 And Adamah, and Ramah, and Hazor.

37 And Kedesh, and Edrei, and En-hazor.

38 And Iron, Migdal-el, Horem, Beth-anath, and Beth-shemeshâ€”nineteen cities along with their surrounding villages.

39 This is the allotted inheritance for the tribe of Naphtaliâ€™s descendants, divided according to their clans, including the cities and their surrounding villages.

40 The seventh portion of land was allotted to the tribe of Dan, divided according to their clans.

41 The territory allotted to them as their inheritance included Zorah, Eshtaol, and Ir-shemesh.

42 And Shaalabbin, Ajalon, and Jethlah.

43 And Elon, Timnah, and Ekron...

44 And Eltekeh, Gibbethon, and Baalath.

45 And Jehud, Bene-berak, and Gath-rimmon.

46 And Me-jarkon, and Rakkon, along with the boundary near Japho.

47 The territory allotted to the descendants of Dan was too small for them. As a result, the Danites went up and attacked Leshem, captured it, and struck it down with the sword. They took possession of it, settled there, and renamed Leshem as Dan, after their ancestor Dan.

48 This is the allotted inheritance for the tribe of the descendants of Dan, distributed according to their clans, including these cities and their surrounding villages.

49 When they had finished dividing the land according to its boundaries as their inheritance, the people of Israel gave a portion of the land to Joshua son of Nun among them.

50 In accordance with the command of the LORD, they granted him the city he requested, which was Timnath-serah in the hill country of Ephraim. He rebuilt the city and settled there.

51 This is the inheritance that Eleazar the priest, Joshua son of Nun, and the leaders of the ancestral families of the tribes of Israel distributed by casting lots in Shiloh in the presence of the LORD, at the entrance to the tent of meeting. In this way, they completed the division of the land.
