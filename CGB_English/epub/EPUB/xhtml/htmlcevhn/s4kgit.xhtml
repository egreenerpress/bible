Psalms Chapter 40

1 (For the director of music. A Psalm of David.) I waited patiently for the LORD, and He turned to me and heard my cry.

2 He lifted me out of a terrifying pit, from the clinging mud and mire, and placed my feet on solid ground, making my steps secure.

3 He has placed a new song in my mouth, a hymn of praise to our God. Many will witness this, be moved with reverence, and place their trust in the LORD.

4 Blessed is the person who places their trust in the LORD, who does not look to the arrogant for guidance, nor turns to those who embrace deceit.

5 O LORD my God, how many are the marvelous works you have done, and the thoughts you have directed toward us! They are beyond counting; they cannot be fully recounted. Even if I tried to declare and speak of them, they are too numerous to be numbered.

6 You did not desire sacrifices or offerings; you have opened my ears to listen and obey. You have not demanded burnt offerings or sin offerings.

7 Then I said, "Here I am—I have come! It is written about me in the scroll of the book."

8 I find joy in doing your will, O my God; indeed, your instruction is deeply rooted within my heart.

9 I have proclaimed your righteousness before the great assembly; indeed, I have not held back my words, O LORD—you know this to be true.

10 I have not kept your righteousness hidden in my heart; I have proclaimed your faithfulness and your salvation. I have not withheld your steadfast love and your truth from the great assembly.

11 Do not withhold your compassionate mercies from me, O LORD; may your steadfast love and faithfulness always protect and sustain me.

12 Countless troubles have surrounded me; my sins have overwhelmed me, so that I cannot even lift my head. They are more numerous than the hairs on my head, and because of this, my courage has failed me.

13 O LORD, be willing to rescue me; O LORD, hurry to assist me.

14 May those who seek to take my life and destroy me be utterly disgraced and dismayed; may those who desire harm for me be turned back and covered in shame.

15 Let them be brought to ruin as the consequence of their disgrace, those who mock me, saying, "Aha! Aha!"

16 May all who seek you be filled with joy and gladness because of you; may those who cherish your deliverance always proclaim, "Let the LORD be exalted!"

17 Though I am poor and in need, the Lord still cares for me. You are my help and my rescuer; do not delay, O my God.
