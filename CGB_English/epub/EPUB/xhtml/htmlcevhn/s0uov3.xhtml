Acts Chapter 9

1 Saul, still fiercely threatening and intent on violence against the Lord’s disciples, went to the high priest.

2 He asked him for letters to the synagogues in Damascus, so that if he found any who belonged to this movement—whether men or women—he might bring them as prisoners to Jerusalem.

3 As he traveled and approached Damascus, suddenly a brilliant light from heaven flashed all around him.

4 And he fell to the ground and heard a voice saying to him, "Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting me?"

5 And he asked, “Who are you, Lord?” The Lord replied, “I am Jesus, the one you are persecuting. It is difficult for you to resist and struggle against what is meant to guide you.”

6 Trembling and overwhelmed, he said, “Lord, what do you want me to do?” The Lord replied, “Get up and go into the city, and you will be told what you must do.”

7 The men who were traveling with him stood there, utterly speechless. They heard the sound of a voice but saw no one there.

8 Saul got up from the ground, but when he opened his eyes, he could not see anything. So they took him by the hand and led him into Damascus.

9 For three days, he was unable to see and did not eat or drink.

10 There was a disciple in Damascus named Ananias. The Lord spoke to him in a vision, calling, "Ananias!" He replied, "Here I am, Lord."

11 The Lord said to him, "Get up and go to the street called Straight. Inquire at the house of Judas for a man named Saul, from Tarsus, for he is there praying."

12 In a vision, he saw a man named Ananias coming to him and placing his hand on him so that he might regain his sight.

13 Then Ananias replied, "Lord, I have heard from many about this man and all the harm he has done to your holy people in Jerusalem."

14 And here he has been given authority by the chief priests to arrest all who invoke your name.

15 But the Lord said to him, "Go on your way, for he is a chosen instrument of mine to carry my name before the Gentiles, kings, and the people of Israel."

16 For I will reveal to him the many hardships he must endure because of my name.

17 Ananias went on his way and entered the house. He placed his hands on Saul and said, "Brother Saul, the Lord—Jesus, who appeared to you on the road as you were traveling—has sent me so that you may regain your sight and be filled with the Holy Spirit."

18 At once, something like scales fell from his eyes, and his sight was restored immediately. He got up and was baptized.

19 After he had eaten, he regained his strength. Saul then stayed with the disciples in Damascus for several days.

20 Immediately, he began proclaiming Christ in the synagogues, declaring that he is the Son of God.

21 All who heard him were astonished and said, "Isn’t this the man who caused havoc in Jerusalem for those who called on this name? Didn’t he come here with the purpose of arresting them and bringing them bound to the chief priests?"

22 But Saul grew even more powerful and baffled the Jews living in Damascus by demonstrating that Jesus was indeed the Messiah.

23 After many days had passed, the Jewish leaders plotted to kill him.

24 However, Saul became aware of their plot. They kept watch at the gates both day and night, intending to kill him.

25 That night, the disciples took him and lowered him down along the city wall in a basket.

26 When Saul arrived in Jerusalem, he attempted to associate himself with the disciples. However, they were all afraid of him and could not believe that he was truly a disciple.

27 Barnabas took him and brought him to the apostles. He explained to them how Saul had seen the Lord on the road and that the Lord had spoken to him. He also described how Saul had boldly preached in Damascus in the name of Jesus.

28 And he stayed with them, moving freely in and out of Jerusalem.

29 He spoke courageously in the name of the Lord Jesus and debated with the Hellenistic Jews, but they plotted to kill him.

30 When the believers learned of this, they took him down to Caesarea and sent him on his way to Tarsus.

31 After this, the churches throughout all Judea, Galilee, and Samaria experienced peace and were strengthened. Living in reverence for the Lord and encouraged by the Holy Spirit, they grew in number.

32 As Peter traveled through various regions, he also went down to visit the believers who lived in Lydda.

33 And there he came upon a man named Aeneas, who had been confined to his bed for eight years, suffering from paralysis.

34 Peter said to him, "Aeneas, Jesus Christ heals you. Get up and prepare your bed." Immediately, Aeneas got up.

35 All the people who lived in Lydda and Sharon saw what had happened, and they turned to the Lord.

36 In Joppa, there was a disciple named Tabitha, which translates to Dorcas. She was known for her many good deeds and acts of charity, which she consistently carried out.

37 During that time, she became ill and passed away. After they had prepared her body, they placed her in an upstairs room.

38 Since Lydda was close to Joppa, and the disciples had heard that Peter was there, they sent two men to him, urging him not to delay in coming to them.

39 Then Peter got up and went with them. When he arrived, they took him to the upstairs room. All the widows stood beside him, weeping and showing him the robes and garments that Dorcas had made while she was still with them.

40 Peter asked everyone to leave the room, then knelt down and prayed. Turning toward the body, he said, “Tabitha, get up.” She opened her eyes, and when she saw Peter, she sat up.

41 He took her by the hand and helped her to her feet. Then, after summoning the believers and the widows, he presented her to them alive.

42 The news spread throughout all of Joppa, and many people came to believe in the Lord.

43 And it happened that he stayed many days in Joppa with a man named Simon, who was a tanner.
