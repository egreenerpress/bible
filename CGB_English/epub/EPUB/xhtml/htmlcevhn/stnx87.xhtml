Mark Chapter 2

1 After several days, Jesus returned to Capernaum, and word spread that he was staying in a house.

2 Immediately, a large crowd gathered, so many that there was no space left to accommodate them, not even near the doorway. And he began to teach them the word of God.

3 They came to him, bringing a man who was paralyzed, carried by four people.

4 When they were unable to reach him because of the crowd, they removed part of the roof above where he was. After making an opening, they lowered the mat on which the paralyzed man was lying.

5 When Jesus saw their faith, he said to the paralyzed man, "My son, your sins are forgiven."

6 But some of the teachers of the law were sitting there, questioning in their minds,

7 Why does this man speak in such a way, as if he is committing blasphemy? Who has the authority to forgive sins except God alone?

8 At once, Jesus, aware in his spirit that they were debating these matters among themselves, said to them, "Why are you turning these things over in your minds?"

9 Which is easier to say to the paralyzed man: ‘Your sins are forgiven,’ or to say, ‘Get up, take your mat, and walk’?

10 "But so that you may understand that the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins," (he then said to the paralyzed man,)

11 I say to you, get up, pick up your mat, and go home.

12 At once, the man got up, picked up his mat, and walked out in front of everyone. They were all astonished and began to praise God, saying, "We have never seen anything like this before!"

13 He went out again by the seashore, and the entire crowd gathered around him, and he began to teach them.

14 As Jesus was walking along, he saw Levi, the son of Alphaeus, sitting at the tax collector’s booth. He said to him, “Come, follow me.” Levi got up and followed him.

15 One day, as Jesus was dining in his home, many tax collectors and sinners were also sitting at the table with him and his disciples. There was a large crowd, and they had all come to follow him.

16 When the scribes and Pharisees saw Jesus eating with tax collectors and sinners, they asked his disciples, "Why does he eat and drink with tax collectors and sinners?"

17 When Jesus heard this, he said to them, "Those who are healthy do not need a doctor, but those who are sick. I did not come to call those who consider themselves righteous, but to call sinners to turn from their ways and return to God."

18 The disciples of John and the Pharisees regularly observed fasting. They came to Jesus and asked, "Why do John’s disciples and the Pharisees fast, but your disciples do not fast?"

19 Jesus said to them, "Can the wedding guests fast while the bridegroom is still with them? As long as the bridegroom remains with them, they cannot fast."

20 However, a time will come when the bridegroom will be taken from them, and in those days, they will fast.

21 No one sews a patch of new, unshrunk cloth onto an old garment. If they do, the new patch will shrink and pull away from the old fabric, making the tear even worse.

22 No one pours new wine into old wineskins. If they do, the new wine will burst the skins, the wine will be spilled, and the wineskins will be ruined. Instead, new wine must be poured into fresh wineskins.

23 One Sabbath day, as Jesus was passing through the grain fields, his disciples began to pick some heads of grain as they walked along.

24 The Pharisees said to him, "Look, why are they doing what is not permitted on the Sabbath day?"

25 And He said to them, "Have you not read what David did when he was in need and hungry, he and those who were with him?"

26 How he entered the house of God during the time of Abiathar the high priest and ate the consecrated bread, which is not lawful for anyone to eat except the priests, and also gave some to those who were with him?

27 And He said to them, "The Sabbath was established for the benefit of humanity, not humanity for the sake of the Sabbath."

28 Therefore, the Son of Man is also the Lord over the Sabbath.
