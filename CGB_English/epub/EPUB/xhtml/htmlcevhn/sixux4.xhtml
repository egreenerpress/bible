Proverbs Chapter 5

1 My child, pay close attention to my wisdom, and listen carefully to my understanding.

2 So that you may carefully consider wisdom, and that your words may preserve understanding.

3 The words of an adulterous woman may seem as sweet as honey, and her speech as smooth as oil.

4 However, her outcome is as bitter as wormwood, as piercing as a double-edged sword.

5 Her steps lead her toward death; her path firmly grasps the realm of the grave.

6 Be careful, or you might lose sight of the path of life. Her ways are unstable and shifting, making it difficult for you to fully understand them.

7 Listen to me now, my children, and do not turn away from the words I speak.

8 Stay far away from her path, and do not go near the entrance of her house.

9 Be careful not to give away your dignity to others, or your years of life to those who are cruel.

10 Be careful, or else outsiders will take possession of your wealth, and the fruit of your hard work will end up in the house of a foreigner.

11 And you will grieve in the end, when your flesh and your body are wasted away.

12 And say, "How I have despised correction and rejected discipline, and my heart has turned away from rebuke!"

13 I have refused to listen to the guidance of my teachers and have turned a deaf ear to those who sought to instruct me!

14 I was on the verge of falling into every kind of wrongdoing, even in the presence of the gathered community and assembly.

15 Draw water from your own source, and let flowing streams come from your own spring.

16 Let your springs flow freely, and streams of water run through the public squares.

17 Let them belong solely to you, and not be shared with outsiders.

18 May your source of life and vitality be blessed, and find joy in the companionship of the wife you married in your youth.

19 Let her be like a graceful doe, a gentle and cherished companion; may her affection continually bring you joy and fulfillment. May you always be captivated by her love, finding deep delight and contentment in her presence.

20 Why, my son, would you be captivated by an unfaithful woman and cling to the embrace of a stranger?

21 The actions and paths of humanity are fully visible to the LORD, and He carefully considers every step they take.

22 A person's own wrongdoing will ensnare the wicked, and they will be bound by the chains of their sins.

23 He will perish for lack of discipline, and in the abundance of his foolishness, he will wander aimlessly.
