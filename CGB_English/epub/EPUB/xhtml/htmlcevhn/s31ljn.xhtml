Proverbs Chapter 29

1 The one who, despite frequent warnings, stubbornly refuses to listen will be suddenly broken beyond repair.

2 When those who are upright and just hold positions of leadership, the people find joy and celebration. But when the wicked rise to power, the people are filled with sorrow and lamentation.

3 Whoever loves wisdom brings joy to their father, but one who associates with promiscuous women wastes their wealth.

4 A king secures the stability of a nation through just governance, but one who accepts bribes brings about its ruin.

5 A person who flatters their neighbor is setting a trap for their own steps.

6 When an evil person falls into sin, it becomes a trap that ensnares them, but the righteous person finds joy and sings with gladness.

7 The righteous person carefully considers the plight of the poor, but the wicked show no concern to understand it.

8 Arrogant and mocking individuals lead a city into ruin, but those who are wise and discerning have the power to calm anger and avert disaster.

9 If a wise person argues with a foolish one, whether the fool becomes angry or mocks, there will be no resolution or peace.

10 Violent and bloodthirsty people despise those who live with integrity, but those who are righteous deeply care for and seek the well-being of the upright.

11 A fool expresses every thought that comes to mind, but a wise person holds back and considers when to speak.

12 If a leader listens to lies, all those who serve him will become corrupt.

13 The poor and the deceitful encounter one another, but the LORD gives sight to the eyes of both.

14 A king who judges the poor with fairness and integrity will have his throne firmly established forever.

15 Discipline and correction impart wisdom, but a child who is allowed to act without guidance brings disgrace to his mother.

16 When the number of wicked people grows, wrongdoing increases as well; but the righteous will witness their downfall.

17 Discipline your child, and he will bring you peace; indeed, he will bring joy to your heart.

18 When there is no divine revelation or guidance, the people lose direction and fall into ruin; but those who uphold and follow the law, they are truly blessed.

19 A servant cannot be effectively disciplined by mere words; even if they understand, they will not respond.

20 Do you see someone who speaks without thinking? There is more hope for a fool than for such a person.

21 Whoever indulges and pampers their servant from a young age will eventually find that servant behaving like a member of the family, expecting the privileges of a son.

22 A person who is quick to anger stirs up conflict, and one who is hot-tempered multiplies wrongdoing.

23 A person's arrogance will lead to their downfall, but those who are humble in spirit will be honored and upheld.

24 Whoever associates with a thief harms their own life; they hear the call to testify but refuse to reveal the truth.

25 The fear of others leads to entrapment, but whoever places their trust in the LORD will find security.

26 Many people strive to gain the favor of those in authority, but true justice and judgment for every individual come from the LORD.

27 A person who acts unjustly is detestable to those who are righteous, and those who live with integrity are detestable to the wicked.
