Genesis Chapter 45

1 Joseph could no longer control his emotions in the presence of all those standing around him, so he cried out, "Have everyone leave the room!" When no one else remained with him, Joseph revealed his true identity to his brothers.

2 Then he wept with loud sobs, and the Egyptians heard it, as did the household of Pharaoh.

3 Joseph said to his brothers, "I am Joseph. Is my father still alive?" But his brothers could not answer him, for they were deeply shaken and overwhelmed by his presence.

4 Joseph said to his brothers, "Please come closer to me." So they approached him. Then he said, "I am Joseph, your brother—the one you sold into Egypt."

5 So now, do not be distressed or angry with yourselves for selling me here, because God sent me ahead of you to save lives.

6 For the past two years, the famine has afflicted the land, and there are still five more years to come during which there will be no plowing or harvesting.

7 God sent me ahead of you to ensure that you would have descendants on the earth and to save your lives through a mighty act of rescue.

8 So now, it was not you who sent me here, but God. He has appointed me as a father figure to Pharaoh, as master over his entire household, and as a ruler over all the land of Egypt.

9 Hurry and go to my father, and tell him, 'This is what your son Joseph says: God has made me ruler over all of Egypt. Come down to me without delay.'

10 You will settle in the land of Goshen, and you will be close to me—you, your children, your grandchildren, your flocks, your herds, and all that you possess.

11 "I will provide for you there, because there are still five years of famine to come. This way, you, your household, and all that you have will not fall into destitution."

12 And now, your eyes can see, and the eyes of my brother Benjamin as well, that it is truly my own voice speaking to you.

13 You must tell my father about all the honor and greatness I have attained in Egypt, and about everything you have witnessed here. Then, hurry and bring my father down to this place.

14 Then he embraced his brother Benjamin, holding him tightly, and wept with deep emotion; and Benjamin, in turn, wept as he held him.

15 Then Joseph embraced each of his brothers and wept over them. After this, his brothers began to speak openly with him.

16 The news reached Pharaoh’s palace, with reports saying, “Joseph’s brothers have arrived.” This pleased Pharaoh greatly, as well as his officials.

17 Pharaoh said to Joseph, "Tell your brothers this: Load your animals and return to the land of Canaan."

18 Bring your father and your entire household, and come to me. I will provide you with the best of the land of Egypt, and you will enjoy the richest produce of the land.

19 Now you are instructed to do this: Take wagons from the land of Egypt for your children and your wives, and bring your father here with you.

20 Do not worry about your belongings, for the best of all the land of Egypt is yours.

21 The Israelites did as they were instructed. Joseph provided them with wagons, as Pharaoh had commanded, and also supplied them with provisions for their journey.

22 To each of them, he gave a set of new garments, but to Benjamin he gave three hundred pieces of silver and five sets of new garments.

23 To his father, he sent gifts in this way: ten donkeys loaded with the finest goods of Egypt, and ten female donkeys carrying grain, bread, and provisions for his father’s journey.

24 So he sent his brothers away, and they departed. He said to them, "Make sure you do not quarrel with one another on the way."

25 They left Egypt and traveled to the land of Canaan, where they returned to their father Jacob.

26 They told him, saying, "Joseph is still alive, and he is ruler over all the land of Egypt." But Jacob's heart grew faint, for he could not believe them.

27 They recounted to Jacob all the words Joseph had spoken to them. When he saw the wagons that Joseph had sent to transport him, the spirit of Jacob, their father, was renewed, and his strength returned.

28 And Israel declared, "It is enough; my son Joseph is still alive. I will go and see him before I die."
