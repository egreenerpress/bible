Hosea Chapter 8

1 Blow the trumpet as a warning! An enemy will swoop down like an eagle against the house of the LORD, for they have broken my covenant and rebelled against my law.

2 Israel will call out to me, saying, "You are our God; we acknowledge you."

3 Israel has rejected what is good; therefore, their enemies will pursue them.

4 They have appointed kings without my consent; they have established rulers without my knowledge. With their silver and gold, they have crafted idols for themselves, leading to their own destruction.

5 Your calf idol, O Samaria, has rejected you; my anger burns against them. How long will it be before they turn to purity?

6 This also comes from Israel: a craftsman made it, so it cannot be God. Indeed, the calf of Samaria will be shattered into pieces.

7 Because they have sown the wind, they will reap the whirlwind. Their crops will have no stalk; their buds will produce no grain. Even if they do yield something, foreigners will devour it.

8 Israel has been utterly consumed; now they will be scattered among the nations like a worthless vessel that no one desires.

9 They have gone up to Assyria, like a wild donkey wandering alone; Ephraim has paid for the affection of lovers.

10 Even though they have sought help from foreign nations, I will now gather them together, and they will experience some sorrow because of the burden imposed by the king and rulers.

11 Because Ephraim has built many altars for sinful purposes, those very altars will become a source of sin for him.

12 I have written for him the great principles of my law, but they have been regarded as something foreign and unfamiliar.

13 They offer meat as sacrifices to me, presenting it as offerings, and then they eat it; but the Lord does not accept them. Now he will remember their wrongdoing and punish their sins. They will be sent back to Egypt.

14 The people of Israel have forgotten their Creator and have turned to building temples, while Judah has constructed many fortified cities. Yet I will send fire upon their cities, and it will consume their palaces.
