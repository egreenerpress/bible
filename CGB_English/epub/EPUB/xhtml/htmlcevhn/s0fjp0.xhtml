I John Chapter 5

1 Whoever believes that Jesus is the Messiah has been born of God; and everyone who loves the Father also loves the one who is born of Him.

2 This is how we know that we truly love God's children: when we love God and obey his commands.

3 This is what it means to love God: that we obey his commandments. And his commandments are not burdensome.

4 For everyone who is born of God overcomes the world. And this is the victory that conquers the world—our faith.

5 Who is it that conquers the world, if not the one who believes that Jesus is the Son of God?

6 This is the one who came through water and blood—Jesus Christ. He did not come by water alone, but by water and blood. And the Spirit is the one who testifies to this, because the Spirit is truth.

7 For there are three who testify in heaven: the Father, the Word, and the Holy Spirit; and these three are united as one.

8 There are three that testify on earth: the Spirit, the water, and the blood; and these three are in agreement as one.

9 If we accept the testimony of people, the testimony of God is far greater, for this is the testimony that God has given about His Son.

10 Whoever believes in the Son of God has the testimony within themselves. But whoever does not believe God has made Him out to be a liar, because they have not believed the testimony that God has given concerning His Son.

11 This is the testimony: God has given us eternal life, and this life is found in His Son.

12 Whoever has the Son has life; but whoever does not have the Son of God does not have life.

13 I have written these things to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, so that you may know with certainty that you possess eternal life, and so that you may continue to place your faith in the name of the Son of God.

14 This is the assurance we have in Him: that if we ask anything in alignment with His will, He listens to us.

15 And if we are confident that he listens to us, whatever we ask, we can be assured that we have received what we requested from him.

16 If anyone sees a fellow believer committing a sin that does not lead to death, they should pray, and God will grant life to the one who has sinned—provided it is not a sin leading to death. There is a sin that leads to death; I am not saying that one should pray about that.

17 All wrongdoing is sin, but there is a sin that does not lead to death.

18 We understand that whoever is born of God does not continue in a life of sin; rather, the one who is born of God guards himself, and the evil one cannot harm him.

19 We understand that we belong to God, and the entire world is under the power of evil.

20 We know that the Son of God has come and has given us the ability to understand, so that we may know the one who is true. We are united with him who is true—through his Son, Jesus Christ. He is the true God and the source of eternal life.

21 Dear children, guard yourselves against idols. Amen.
