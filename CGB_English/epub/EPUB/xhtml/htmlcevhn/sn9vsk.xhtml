Judges Chapter 3

1 Here are the nations that the LORD allowed to remain, in order to test Israel through them—specifically, those among the Israelites who had not experienced all the wars of Canaan.

2 This was done so that the generations of the Israelites might learn and understand warfare, especially those who had no prior experience with it.

3 Specifically, the five rulers of the Philistines, all the Canaanites, the Sidonians, and the Hivites who lived in the mountains of Lebanon, from Mount Baal-hermon to the entrance of Hamath.

4 The purpose of these nations was to test Israel, to determine whether they would obey the commandments of the LORD, which He had given to their ancestors through Moses.

5 The Israelites lived among the Canaanites, Hittites, Amorites, Perizzites, Hivites, and Jebusites.

6 They married their daughters to foreign men and took foreign women as wives for their sons, and they began to worship the gods of those nations.

7 The Israelites committed evil in the eyes of the LORD. They abandoned the LORD their God and worshiped the Baals and the Asherahs.

8 Therefore, the LORD’s anger burned against Israel, and He handed them over to the power of Cushan-Rishathaim, the king of Mesopotamia. As a result, the Israelites were subjected to Cushan-Rishathaim’s rule for eight years.

9 When the people of Israel cried out to the LORD, the LORD appointed a deliverer for them, who saved them. This deliverer was Othniel, the son of Kenaz, who was Caleb’s younger brother.

10 Then the Spirit of the LORD came upon him, and he took on the role of judging Israel. He went out to battle, and the LORD handed over Chushan-rishathaim, the king of Mesopotamia, into his power. His strength overcame Chushan-rishathaim, and he achieved victory over him.

11 The land enjoyed peace for forty years. Then Othniel, the son of Kenaz, passed away.

12 Once again, the Israelites did what was evil in the sight of the LORD. Because of their wrongdoing, the LORD empowered Eglon, the king of Moab, to rise up against Israel. This happened as a consequence of their evil deeds in the LORD's eyes.

13 He assembled the Ammonites and Amalekites, and they marched out to attack Israel. They defeated them and took control of the city of palm trees.

14 The Israelites were subject to Eglon, the king of Moab, for eighteen years.

15 When the Israelites cried out to the LORD, the LORD raised up a deliverer for them: Ehud, the son of Gera, a Benjamite, who was left-handed. Through him, the Israelites sent tribute to Eglon, the king of Moab.

16 Ehud crafted a double-edged dagger, about a cubit in length, and fastened it securely under his clothing on his right thigh.

17 He brought the tribute to Eglon, the king of Moab. Now Eglon was an exceedingly obese man.

18 After he had finished presenting the offering, he dismissed the people who had carried it.

19 But he himself turned back from the stone quarries near Gilgal and said, "I have a confidential matter to discuss with you, O king." The king replied, "Keep it quiet." Then all those who were standing around him withdrew and left.

20 Ehud approached him while he was sitting in a private upper room, which he used exclusively for himself. Ehud said, "I have a message from God for you." At this, the king rose from his seat.

21 Ehud reached out with his left hand, took the dagger from his right thigh, and plunged it into the king's belly.

22 The handle also went in after the blade, and the fat closed around the blade, so that he could not pull the dagger out of his belly; and the filth came out.

23 Then Ehud went out through the porch, closed the doors of the upper room behind him, and locked them.

24 When Ehud had left, his servants came and saw that the doors of the upper room were locked. They said, "He must be relieving himself in the inner chamber."

25 They waited until they were embarrassed, for the doors of the upper room remained closed. So they took a key and unlocked them, only to find their master lying dead on the ground.

26 Ehud fled while they delayed, and he went beyond the stone quarries, escaping to Seirath.

27 When he arrived, he sounded a trumpet on the mountain of Ephraim, and the Israelites came down from the hill country to join him, with him leading the way.

28 He said to them, "Follow me, because the LORD has given your enemies, the Moabites, into your hands." So they followed him, captured the crossings of the Jordan River leading to Moab, and did not allow anyone to pass through.

29 At that time, they killed about ten thousand Moabite men, all of them strong and courageous warriors; not a single man escaped.

30 So on that day, Moab was brought under the control of Israel. After this, the land experienced peace for eighty years.

31 After him came Shamgar, the son of Anath, who killed six hundred Philistines using an ox goad. In this way, he also delivered Israel.
