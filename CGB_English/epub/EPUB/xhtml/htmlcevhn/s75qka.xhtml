Proverbs Chapter 6

1 My child, if you have pledged yourself as a guarantee for your friend, if you have made a binding agreement with a stranger by shaking hands,

2 You are trapped by the words of your own mouth; you are ensnared by the words you have spoken.

3 Act now, my son, and free yourself, for you have fallen into the power of your neighbor. Go, humble yourself, and plead earnestly with your friend.

4 Do not allow your eyes to close in sleep, nor let your eyelids grow heavy with slumber.

5 Save yourself like a gazelle escaping from the hunter’s grasp, or like a bird fleeing from the snare of the trapper.

6 Go to the ant, you lazy one; observe its ways and learn wisdom.

7 Though it has no guide, overseer, or ruler,

8 She prepares her provisions in the summer and gathers her sustenance during the harvest.

9 How long will you lie there, you lazy person? When will you get up from your sleep?

10 "Just a little more sleep, a bit more rest, a moment longer to fold your hands and doze."

11 Your poverty will come upon you like a traveler, and your need will arrive like a warrior fully armed.

12 A deceitful and corrupt individual, a person of wickedness, speaks with a perverse and twisted tongue.

13 He signals with his eyes, he communicates with his feet, and he instructs with his fingers.

14 Perversity fills his heart; he continually plans evil and spreads conflict.

15 Therefore, his disaster will come upon him unexpectedly; in an instant, he will be shattered beyond recovery.

16 The LORD detests these six things, and seven are utterly abhorrent to Him:

17 A haughty demeanor, a deceitful tongue, and hands that spill the blood of the innocent.

18 A heart that plans evil schemes, and feet that are quick to rush into wrongdoing.

19 A deceitful witness who spreads lies, and one who stirs up conflict among brothers.

20 My child, hold fast to your father's instruction, and do not abandon the teachings of your mother.

21 Keep these teachings always close to your heart and let them guide your thoughts and actions. Wear them as a constant reminder, like a treasured ornament around your neck, so they may shape your life and decisions.

22 When you walk, it will guide you; when you lie down, it will watch over you; and when you rise, it will speak with you.

23 The commandment is like a guiding lamp, and the law is a source of light; the corrections and teachings of discipline lead to the path of life.

24 To protect you from the immoral woman, from the seductive words of a foreign woman.

25 Do not desire her beauty in your heart, nor allow her to captivate you with her alluring glances.

26 For through the seduction of an immoral woman, a man is reduced to utter poverty, and the adulteress preys upon the most valuable possession—his very life.

27 Can a man hold fire close to his chest without his clothes being burned?

28 Can someone walk on hot coals without their feet being burned?

29 Therefore, anyone who has sexual relations with his neighbor’s wife—whoever engages in such an act will not remain guiltless.

30 People do not despise a thief if he steals to satisfy his hunger when he is starving.

31 If he is discovered, he must repay seven times what he took; he must give up everything he owns in his house.

32 Whoever commits adultery with a woman shows a lack of wisdom; the one who does such a thing brings ruin upon his own life.

33 He will receive injury and disgrace, and his shame will remain forever, never to be erased.

34 For jealousy ignites a man’s fury, and he will show no mercy when the time comes for retribution.

35 He will not accept any payment to spare you; no matter how many gifts you offer, he will not be satisfied.
