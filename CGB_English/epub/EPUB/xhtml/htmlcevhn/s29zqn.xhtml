Hosea Chapter 10

1 Israel is like a vine that bears no fruit for others, producing only for itself. As its harvest increased, so did its altars; as its land prospered, so did its idols, crafted into splendid images.

2 Their loyalty is divided; now they will be held guilty. He will demolish their altars and destroy their sacred images.

3 Now they will say, "We have no king, because we did not fear the LORD. So what could a king do for us?"

4 They have spoken deceitful words, making false oaths when forming agreements. As a result, justice grows like poisonous hemlock in the plowed rows of a field.

5 The people of Samaria will tremble because of the idolatrous calves of Beth-aven. The inhabitants will mourn over it, and the priests who once rejoiced in its glory will grieve, for its splendor has been taken away.

6 It will also be taken to Assyria as a tribute to the great king. Ephraim will be disgraced, and Israel will be put to shame because of their own plans.

7 Concerning Samaria, its king has been utterly destroyed, vanishing like foam upon the surface of the water.

8 The sacred high places of Aven, which symbolize Israel's sin, will be utterly destroyed. Thorns and thistles will overgrow their altars. In their despair, the people will cry out to the mountains, "Cover us!" and to the hills, "Fall on us and hide us!"

9 O Israel, you have been sinning since the days of Gibeah. There they took their stand, but the battle in Gibeah against the wicked did not overcome them.

10 It is my intention to discipline them, and the nations will be assembled against them when they tie themselves to their double rebellion.

11 Ephraim is like a well-trained heifer that enjoys threshing grain, but I will place a yoke upon her fair neck. I will harness Ephraim to labor; Judah will plow, and Jacob will break up the hard ground.

12 Plant seeds of righteousness within yourselves, and you will harvest the fruit of steadfast love. Prepare your hearts, like breaking up unplowed soil, for now is the time to seek the LORD, until he comes and pours down righteousness upon you like rain.

13 You have plowed wickedness, you have harvested injustice; you have consumed the fruit of deceit. This is because you relied on your own ways and placed your trust in the strength of your many warriors.

15 In the same way, Bethel will deal with you because of your great evil. In the morning, the king of Israel will be completely destroyed.
