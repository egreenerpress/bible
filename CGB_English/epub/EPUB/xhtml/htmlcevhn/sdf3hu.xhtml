Job Chapter 7

1 Does not humanity have a set time on earth? Are not our days like those of a hired worker, with a fixed duration and purpose?

2 Just as a servant eagerly longs for the shade to rest, and as a hired worker eagerly anticipates the payment for his labor,

3 Therefore, I am allotted months of futility, and nights of misery are assigned to me.

4 When I lie down, I think, "When will I get up, and when will the night be over?" But I am restless, tossing and turning until the break of day.

5 My body is covered with worms and hardened crusts of dirt; my skin is cracked and festering, becoming repulsive.

6 My days pass more quickly than a weaverâ€™s shuttle, and they come to an end without any hope.

7 Please remember that my life is fleeting, like a passing breeze; my eyes will never again see happiness.

8 The eye of the one who has seen me will see me no longer; your eyes are fixed on me, yet I am no more.

9 Just as a cloud dissipates and vanishes from sight, so too does the one who descends to the grave; they will not rise again.

10 He will not return to his home again, and his place will no longer recognize him.

11 Therefore, I will not hold back my words; I will speak out in the distress of my spirit; I will express my anguish in the bitterness of my soul.

12 Am I like the raging sea or a great sea monster, that you set a guard over me?

13 When I think to myself, "My bed will bring me comfort, and my couch will ease my suffering,"

14 Then you frighten me with dreams and terrify me through visions.

15 Therefore, I would rather choose suffocation and death than continue living this life.

16 I detest it; I do not wish to live forever. Leave me alone, for my days are meaningless.

17 What is humanity, that You should honor them so greatly? Why do You set Your heart upon them?

18 And that You should attend to him each morning, testing him at every instant?

19 How long will you not leave me alone, nor give me a moment's rest, even long enough for me to swallow my saliva?

20 I have sinned; what should I do before You, O Guardian of humanity? Why have You made me a target for Your scrutiny, so that I have become a burden even to myself?

21 Why do you not forgive my wrongdoing and remove my guilt? For soon I will lie down in the dust, and when you seek me in the morning, I will no longer be here.
