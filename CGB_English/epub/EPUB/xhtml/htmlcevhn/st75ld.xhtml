Psalms Chapter 73

1 (A Psalm of Asaph.) Indeed, God is good to Israel, especially to those who are pure in heart.

2 But as for me, my feet nearly stumbled; I was on the verge of slipping.

3 For I became deeply troubled and resentful when I observed the success and ease of the wicked, even though their ways were foolish and without understanding.

4 For they are not bound by the chains of death; their vigor remains unshaken.

5 They do not face the same hardships as others; nor are they afflicted in the same way as the rest of humanity.

6 Therefore, pride surrounds them like a chain; violence envelops them like a garment.

7 Their eyes bulge from excess; they have more than their hearts could ever desire.

8 They are morally corrupt and speak maliciously about oppression; they speak arrogantly and with pride.

9 They speak arrogantly against the heavens, and their words spread across the earth.

10 Therefore, his people turn back to this place, and they are forced to drink the bitter cup of suffering to its fullest measure.

11 And they say, "How can God truly know? Does the Most High have understanding?"

12 Look, these are the wicked, who thrive in this life and grow ever more wealthy.

13 Truly, I have purified my heart for nothing, and washed my hands in innocence to no avail.

14 All day long I have been afflicted, and every morning I have been disciplined.

15 If I were to say, "I will speak in this manner," I would surely be acting unfaithfully toward the generation of your children.

16 When I sought to understand this, it became overwhelming and distressing to me.

17 Until I entered the sanctuary of God; then I truly grasped their ultimate fate.

18 Certainly, you placed them on unstable ground; you caused them to fall into ruin.

19 How suddenly they are brought to ruin, destroyed in an instant! They are completely overwhelmed by terror.

20 Like a fleeting dream that vanishes upon waking, so too, O Lord, when You arise, You will dismiss their empty illusions.

21 So my heart was deeply troubled, and I was pierced with anguish within.

22 I was so foolish and lacking in understanding; I was like a senseless animal in your presence.

23 Yet I am always with you; you hold me securely by my right hand.

24 You will guide me with your wisdom, and afterward, you will bring me into your glorious presence.

25 Who do I have in heaven but you? And on earth, there is no one I desire more than you.

26 My body and my heart may grow weak and fail, but God is the strength of my heart and my eternal portion.

27 Indeed, those who distance themselves from you will come to ruin; you have brought to an end all who abandon you and turn to unfaithfulness.

28 It is truly beneficial for me to draw close to God; I have placed my confidence in the Lord GOD, so that I may proclaim all of your mighty deeds.
