Psalms Chapter 55

1 (For the director of music, to be accompanied by stringed instruments, a contemplative psalm of David.) Listen to my prayer, O God; do not turn away from my plea for help.

2 Pay attention to me and listen to my plea. I am deeply troubled as I pour out my complaint; I cry out in distress.

3 Because of the clamor of my foes, because of the cruelty of the wicked—they heap wrongdoing upon me and in their anger harbor hatred against me.

4 My heart is deeply anguished within me, and the horrors of death have overwhelmed me.

5 Fear and trembling have taken hold of me, and a deep sense of dread has completely overwhelmed me.

6 And I said, "If only I had wings like a dove! Then I would fly away and find a place of rest."

7 Behold, I would then flee far away and dwell in the wilderness. Selah.

8 I would quickly seek refuge and escape from the fierce, howling storm and violent tempest.

9 O Lord, scatter and confuse their speech, for I have witnessed violence and conflict within the city.

10 Day and night, they move about upon its walls; wickedness and trouble are within it.

11 Wickedness dwells within her; deceit and treachery never leave her streets.

12 It was not an enemy who insulted me—if it had been, I could have endured it. Nor was it someone who hated me who rose up against me—if it had been, I could have hidden from them.

13 But it was you—a man like myself, my close companion, my trusted friend.

14 We shared heartfelt fellowship together and journeyed as companions to the house of God.

15 May death overtake them suddenly, and may they descend swiftly to the grave, for wickedness dwells in their homes and within their midst.

16 As for me, I will cry out to God, and the LORD will deliver me.

17 In the evening, in the morning, and at midday, I will pray and cry out earnestly, and He will hear my voice.

18 He has rescued my life in safety from the conflict that surrounded me, for many were arrayed against me.

19 God will hear and bring judgment upon them—He who has existed from eternity. Selah. Because they remain unchanged and unrepentant, they do not fear God.

20 He has raised his hand against those who were at peace with him; he has violated his covenant.

21 His speech was as smooth as butter, yet conflict filled his heart; his words were as soothing as oil, but they were like sharpened swords.

22 Entrust your cares to the LORD, and He will support you; He will never allow the righteous to be shaken.

23 But you, O God, will bring them down to the pit of destruction. Violent and deceitful people will not live out even half their days. Yet I will place my trust in you.
