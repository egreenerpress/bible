Luke Chapter 14

1 Now it happened that as he entered the house of one of the leading Pharisees to share a meal on the Sabbath, they were closely observing him.

2 And look, there was a man standing before him who was suffering from swelling due to fluid retention.

3 And Jesus responded, speaking to the legal experts and Pharisees, saying, "Is it permitted to heal on the Sabbath day?"

4 They remained silent. Then he took the man, healed him, and sent him on his way.

5 And he responded to them, saying, "Which one of you, if your donkey or ox falls into a pit on the Sabbath day, will not immediately pull it out?"

6 And they were unable to offer any reply to him regarding these matters.

7 Then Jesus told a parable to the guests who had been invited, after noticing how they were selecting the places of honor for themselves. He said to them,

8 When you are invited by someone to a wedding celebration, do not take the seat of honor for yourself. Otherwise, a more distinguished guest than you may arrive, and the host might come and say to you, 'Give your place to this person.' Then, in shame, you would have to move to a lower seat. Instead, when you are invited, go and sit in the least prominent place, so that when the host comes, he may say to you, 'Friend, move up to a better position.' Then you will be honored in the presence of all the guests. For everyone who exalts themselves will be humbled, and those who humble themselves will be exalted.

9 The host who invited both you and him will come and say to you, 'Give this man your seat.' Then, humiliated, you will have to take the lowest place.

10 But when you are invited, go and take the least prominent seat, so that when the one who invited you arrives, he may say to you, ‘Friend, move up to a better place.’ Then you will be honored in the presence of all the guests dining with you.

11 For anyone who elevates themselves will be brought low, and whoever humbles themselves will be lifted up.

12 Then he also said to the one who had invited him, "When you host a dinner or a banquet, do not invite your friends, your brothers, your relatives, or your wealthy neighbors. Otherwise, they might invite you in return, and you would be repaid for your kindness."

13 But when you host a banquet, invite the poor, the disabled, the crippled, and the blind.

14 You will be blessed because they cannot repay you; for you will be repaid at the resurrection of the righteous.

15 As one of those reclining at the table with Him heard these words, he said to Him, "How fortunate is the one who will share in the feast of God’s kingdom."

16 Then he said to him, "A certain man prepared a grand banquet and invited many guests."

17 At the time of the evening meal, he sent his servant to tell those who had been invited, “Come, for everything is now prepared.”

18 And they all, in unison, began to make excuses. The first one said to him, "I have purchased a field, and I must go and inspect it. Please excuse me."

19 Another person said, "I have purchased five pairs of oxen, and I am going to test them. Please excuse me."

20 And another said, "I have just married a wife, and for that reason, I cannot come."

21 So the servant went and reported these things to his master. Then the owner of the house, filled with anger, said to his servant, “Go out at once into the streets and alleys of the town and bring in here the poor, the disabled, the lame, and the blind.”

22 The servant replied, "Master, everything has been done as you instructed, but there is still space available."

23 The master said to the servant, "Go out to the roads and the outskirts of the town, and urge the people there to come in, so that my house may be filled."

24 For I tell you, not one of those men who were invited will partake of my feast.

25 A large crowd followed him, and he turned to them and said:

26 If anyone comes to me and does not prioritize me above their father, mother, wife, children, brothers, sisters, and even their own life, they cannot be my disciple.

27 And whoever does not take up their own burden of sacrifice and follow me cannot be my disciple.

28 Which one of you, planning to build a tower, does not first sit down and calculate the cost to determine whether you have enough to complete it?

29 Otherwise, if he lays the foundation but is unable to complete it, everyone who sees it will begin to ridicule him.

30 They said, "This man started to build but was unable to complete the work."

31 Or what king, when he sets out to wage war against another king, does not first sit down and consider whether he is capable with ten thousand soldiers to confront the one who comes against him with twenty thousand?

32 Alternatively, while the other is still far away, he sends a delegation and seeks terms of peace.

33 In the same way, whoever among you does not give up everything they possess cannot be my disciple.

34 Salt is valuable, but if the salt loses its flavor, how can its taste be restored?

35 This substance is not suitable for the soil, nor even for the manure pile; people simply throw it away. Whoever has the ability to understand, let them pay attention.
