Proverbs Chapter 15

1 A gentle response defuses anger, but harsh words provoke it.

2 The speech of the wise conveys knowledge appropriately, but the mouth of fools gushes out folly.

3 The eyes of the LORD are everywhere, observing both the wicked and the righteous.

4 A wholesome tongue is a tree of life, bringing healing and nourishment, but deceitful speech causes a fracture in the spirit.

5 A foolish person rejects their father's guidance, but whoever heeds correction shows wisdom.

6 In the home of the upright, there is abundant wealth, but in the gains of the wicked, there is only distress.

7 The words of the wise spread understanding, but the heart of the foolish lacks such discernment.

8 The offering brought by the wicked is detestable to the LORD, but the prayer of the righteous brings him joy.

9 The actions of the wicked are detestable to the LORD, but He deeply loves those who pursue righteousness.

10 Discipline is deeply painful to those who abandon the right path, and whoever despises correction will face destruction.

11 The realm of the dead and the abyss of destruction lie open before the LORDâ€”how much more so the hearts of human beings!

12 A mocker does not appreciate those who correct him, nor will he seek guidance from the wise.

13 A joyful heart brings a bright and cheerful expression, but when the heart is filled with sorrow, the spirit is crushed.

14 A person with understanding seeks knowledge with their whole heart, but the words of fools are consumed by their own foolishness.

15 Every day brings hardship to those who suffer, but a joyful heart experiences an unending celebration.

16 It is better to have little and live in reverence for the LORD than to possess great wealth along with the turmoil it brings.

17 A simple meal of vegetables shared in love is far better than a feast of fattened oxen served with bitterness.

18 A person who is quick to anger provokes conflict, but one who is patient and slow to anger calms disputes.

19 The path of the lazy person is like a hedge of thorns, full of obstacles and difficulties, but the way of the righteous is smooth and clear.

20 A wise son brings joy to his father, but a foolish man shows contempt for his mother.

21 Foolishness brings delight to one who lacks wisdom, but a person of understanding lives with integrity.

22 Without guidance, plans fail, but with the input of many advisors, they succeed and are firmly established.

23 A person finds joy in giving a fitting reply, and how good it is to speak the right words at the right time!

24 The path of life leads upward for the wise, so that they may turn away from the grave below.

25 The LORD will bring ruin to the house of the arrogant, but He will secure the boundaries of the widow.

26 The intentions and schemes of the wicked are utterly detestable to the LORD, but the words of those who are pure and upright bring Him delight.

27 A person who is driven by greed for profit brings trouble upon their own household, but whoever rejects dishonest gain will find life.

28 The heart of the upright person carefully considers how to respond, but the mouth of the wicked overflows with harmful words.

29 The LORD remains distant from the wicked, but He listens to the prayers of those who live in righteousness.

30 The brightness of the eyes brings joy to the heart, and good news strengthens the body.

31 The ear that listens to life-giving correction will remain among the wise.

32 Whoever rejects correction shows contempt for their own well-being, but the one who listens to rebuke gains wisdom.

33 Reverence for the LORD is the foundation of true wisdom, and humility comes before honor.
