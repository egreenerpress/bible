II Samuel Chapter 13

1 After these events, Absalom, the son of David, had a beautiful sister named Tamar. Amnon, another son of David, fell in love with her.

2 Amnon became so deeply troubled that he grew physically ill because of his sister Tamar, for she was a virgin, and it seemed impossible for him to act on his desire toward her.

3 Amnon had a companion named Jonadab, the son of Shimeah, who was David’s brother. Jonadab was an exceedingly shrewd and cunning man.

4 And he said to him, "Why are you, as the king's son, growing thinner day by day? Won't you tell me what is wrong?" Amnon replied, "I am in love with Tamar, my brother Absalom's sister."

5 Jonadab said to him, "Lie down on your bed and pretend to be ill. When your father comes to see you, say to him, 'Please let my sister Tamar come and prepare some food for me. Let her cook it in my presence so I can watch and then eat it from her hand.'"

6 So Amnon went to bed and pretended to be ill. When the king came to visit him, Amnon said to the king, "Please, let my sister Tamar come and prepare a couple of cakes in front of me, so that I may eat from her hand."

7 Then David sent a message to Tamar, saying, "Go now to your brother Amnon's house and prepare a meal for him."

8 So Tamar went to her brother Amnon’s house, where he was lying down. She took flour, kneaded it, and prepared cakes right in front of him, then baked them.

9 She took a pan and served the food before him, but he refused to eat. Then Amnon said, "Send everyone away from me." So all the men left the room.

10 Amnon said to Tamar, "Bring the food into the room so I can eat from your hand." Tamar took the cakes she had prepared and brought them into the room to her brother Amnon.

11 When she brought the food to him and he began to eat, he grabbed her and said, "Come, sleep with me, my sister."

12 She replied to him, "No, my brother, do not force me; for such a thing should never be done in Israel. Do not commit this disgraceful act."

13 And I, where can I take my disgrace? And as for you, you will be regarded as one of the fools in Israel. Now, therefore, I beg you, speak to the king, for he will not refuse to give me to you.

14 However, he refused to listen to her pleas. Being stronger than she was, he overpowered her, forced himself upon her, and had sexual relations with her.

15 Then Amnon felt an intense hatred toward her, so much so that his hatred was even greater than the love he had once felt for her. Amnon said to her, "Get up and leave."

16 She said to him, "There is no reason for this. The wrong you are doing by sending me away is worse than the harm you have already caused me." But he refused to listen to her.

17 Then he summoned the servant who attended to him and said, "Remove this woman from my presence at once, and lock the door behind her."

18 She was wearing a richly ornamented robe, for such garments were reserved for the king’s virgin daughters. Then his servant led her out and locked the door behind her.

19 Then Tamar took ashes and sprinkled them on her head, tore the richly ornamented robe she was wearing, put her hand on her head, and walked away, weeping aloud.

20 And her brother Absalom said to her, "Has Amnon your brother been with you? But now, keep quiet, my sister. He is your brother; do not dwell on this matter." So Tamar lived in isolation in her brother Absalom's house.

21 But when King David heard about all these events, he became extremely angry.

22 And Absalom did not speak to his brother Amnon, whether kindly or harshly, for Absalom harbored hatred toward Amnon because he had violated his sister Tamar.

23 After two full years had passed, Absalom held a sheep-shearing celebration in Baal-hazor, near Ephraim, and he invited all the king’s sons to join him.

24 Then Absalom approached the king and said, "Please, my lord the king, your servant has sheepshearers working. I humbly ask that the king and his attendants accompany your servant."

25 The king said to Absalom, "No, my son, we should not all go now, or we might become a burden to you." But Absalom insisted, though the king still refused to go. Instead, he blessed him.

26 Then Absalom said, "If not, I ask you, please let my brother Amnon come with us." The king replied to him, "Why should he go with you?"

27 But Absalom insisted, urging him until he allowed Amnon and all the king’s sons to go with him.

28 Now Absalom had instructed his servants, saying, "Pay close attention when Amnon's heart is cheerful from wine, and when I say to you, 'Strike Amnon,' then kill him. Do not be afraid, for have I not commanded you? Be strong and act with courage."

29 Then Absalom’s servants did to Amnon exactly as Absalom had ordered. At this, all the king’s sons got up, mounted their mules, and fled.

30 As they were on their journey, news reached David with this report: "Absalom has killed all the king's sons, and not one of them remains."

31 Then the king stood up, tore his clothes, and lay down on the ground. All his servants stood nearby, their own garments torn as well.

32 Jonadab, the son of Shimeah, David’s brother, responded, saying, “Do not let my lord assume that all the king’s sons have been killed. Only Amnon is dead. This has been Absalom’s plan ever since the day Amnon violated his sister Tamar.”

33 Therefore, my lord the king, do not let this matter weigh heavily on your heart, as though all your sons have died. Only Amnon is dead.

34 But Absalom fled. The young man who was keeping watch raised his eyes and looked, and behold, a large group of people was approaching along the path on the hillside behind him.

35 And Jonadab said to the king, "Look, the king’s sons are coming. Just as your servant said, so it has happened."

36 As soon as he finished speaking, the king’s sons arrived, and they raised their voices and wept. The king and all his servants also wept bitterly.

37 But Absalom fled and went to Talmai, the son of Ammihud, who was the king of Geshur. And David grieved for his son every day.

38 So Absalom fled and went to Geshur, where he stayed for three years.

39 King David’s heart yearned to reach out to Absalom, for he had found some comfort over Amnon’s death.
