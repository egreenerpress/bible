Acts Chapter 7

1 Then the high priest asked, "Is this true?"

2 He said, "Men, brothers, and fathers, listen to me: The God of glory appeared to our ancestor Abraham while he was still in Mesopotamia, before he settled in Haran."

3 And He said to him, "Leave your country and your relatives, and go to the land that I will show you."

4 Then he left the land of the Chaldeans and settled in Haran. After his father died, God led him to move to this land where you now live.

5 God did not grant him any inheritance in the land, not even a small portion to stand on. Yet He promised that He would give it to him as a permanent possession and to his descendants after him, even though at that time he had no child.

6 God spoke in this manner: that his descendants would live as foreigners in a land not their own, where they would be enslaved and mistreated for four hundred years.

7 God declared, "I will judge the nation that enslaves them, and afterward, they will come out and worship me in this place."

8 God established the covenant of circumcision with him. As a result, Abraham became the father of Isaac and circumcised him on the eighth day, as the covenant required. Isaac later became the father of Jacob, and Jacob became the father of the twelve patriarchs.

9 The patriarchs, driven by jealousy, sold Joseph into slavery in Egypt; yet God remained with him.

10 God rescued him from all his troubles and granted him favor and wisdom in the eyes of Pharaoh, the king of Egypt. As a result, Pharaoh appointed him as ruler over Egypt and entrusted him with authority over his entire household.

11 Now a severe famine spread throughout all the land of Egypt and Canaan, bringing great hardship. Our ancestors could find no food to sustain themselves.

12 When Jacob learned that there was grain in Egypt, he sent our ancestors there first.

13 On the second occasion, Joseph revealed his identity to his brothers, and his family lineage was made known to Pharaoh.

14 Joseph then sent word and summoned his father Jacob and all his relatives to come to him, totaling seventy-five people.

15 So Jacob journeyed to Egypt, and there he died, as did our ancestors.

16 They were taken to Shechem and placed in the tomb that Abraham had purchased for a sum of money from the sons of Hamor, the father of Shechem.

17 But as the time approached for the fulfillment of the promise that God had sworn to Abraham, the people increased in number and multiplied greatly in Egypt.

18 Until a new king came to power who did not acknowledge or recognize Joseph.

19 This man acted deceitfully toward our people and mistreated our ancestors, forcing them to abandon their newborn children so that they would not survive.

20 At that time, Moses was born, and he was exceptionally beautiful. He was cared for in his father’s house for three months.

21 When he was abandoned, the daughter of Pharaoh found him and took him in, raising him as her own son.

22 Moses was thoroughly educated in all the knowledge and learning of the Egyptians, and he was powerful both in his speech and in his actions.

23 When he reached the age of forty, it stirred within him to go and see how his fellow Israelites were doing.

24 When he saw one of his fellow Hebrews being mistreated, he came to his defense and stood up for the oppressed man. In his anger, he struck down the Egyptian.

25 For he assumed that his fellow Israelites would realize that God was going to deliver them through his actions, but they did not grasp this understanding.

26 The following day, he appeared to them again as they were arguing, and he tried to reconcile them, saying, "Gentlemen, you are brothers. Why are you harming each other?"

27 But the one who had wronged his neighbor pushed him away, saying, "Who appointed you as a ruler and judge over us?"

28 "Are you going to kill me, just as you killed the Egyptian yesterday?"

29 Upon hearing this, Moses fled and became a foreigner in the land of Midian, where he fathered two sons.

30 After forty years had passed, an angel of the Lord appeared to him in the wilderness near Mount Sinai, within the flames of a burning bush.

31 When Moses saw it, he was amazed at the sight. As he approached to take a closer look, the voice of the Lord spoke to him.

32 He said, "I am the God of your ancestors, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob." At this, Moses trembled with fear and did not dare to look.

33 Then the Lord said to him, "Take off your sandals from your feet, for the place where you are standing is sacred ground."

34 I have observed, I have truly witnessed the suffering of my people who are in Egypt, and I have heard their cries of distress. I have come down to rescue them. Now, go, for I am sending you to Egypt.

35 This Moses, whom they rejected, saying, "Who appointed you as a ruler and judge?"—this same man God sent to be both a ruler and a deliverer, through the guidance of the angel who appeared to him in the bush.

36 He led them out after demonstrating extraordinary miracles and signs in the land of Egypt, at the Red Sea, and throughout the wilderness for forty years.

37 This is the same Moses who said to the Israelites, "The Lord your God will raise up for you a prophet from among your own people, just like me. You must listen to him."

38 This is the one who was among the assembly in the wilderness, accompanied by the angel who spoke to him on Mount Sinai, and with our ancestors. He received the living words of God to deliver to us.

39 The people to whom our ancestors refused to listen, rejecting him and turning away in their hearts, longing to return to Egypt.

40 They said to Aaron, "Make us gods who will lead us, because we don’t know what has happened to this Moses, the man who brought us out of the land of Egypt."

41 In those days, they crafted a calf, presented offerings to the idol, and took delight in what their own hands had made.

42 Then God turned away and allowed them to worship the heavenly bodies, as it is written in the book of the prophets: "House of Israel, did you bring me slaughtered animals and sacrifices for forty years in the wilderness?"

43 Indeed, you carried the shrine of Moloch and the star of your god Rephan, the idols you made to worship. Because of this, I will exile you beyond Babylon.

44 Our ancestors had the tabernacle of testimony in the wilderness, just as God had commanded when He spoke to Moses, instructing him to construct it according to the pattern he had been shown.

45 This is also what our ancestors, who came after, brought with Joshua into the land they took possession of from the Gentiles, whom God drove out before the presence of our ancestors. This continued until the time of David.

46 Who gained favor in the sight of God and earnestly sought to establish a dwelling place for the God of Jacob.

47 However, Solomon constructed a house for him.

48 However, the Most High does not reside in temples built by human hands, as the prophet has said:

49 Heaven is my throne, and the earth is my footstool. What kind of house could you possibly build for me? says the Lord. Where could you find a place for me to rest?

50 Has not my hand created all these things?

51 You are stubborn and unyielding, with hearts and ears that remain closed and unresponsive. You continually oppose the Holy Spirit, just as your ancestors did before you.

52 Which of the prophets did your ancestors not persecute? They even killed those who foretold the coming of the Righteous One, and now you have become His betrayers and murderers.

53 You received the law through the arrangement of angels, yet you have not obeyed it.

54 When they heard these words, they were deeply pierced in their hearts and ground their teeth at him in anger.

55 But he, filled with the Holy Spirit, gazed intently into heaven and saw the glory of God, with Jesus standing at the right hand of God.

56 And he said, "Look, I see the heavens opened, and the Son of Man standing at the right hand of God."

57 Then they shouted at the top of their voices, covered their ears, and all rushed at him together.

58 They threw him out of the city and stoned him to death. The witnesses who testified against him laid their outer garments at the feet of a young man named Saul.

59 As they were stoning Stephen, he called out to God, saying, "Lord Jesus, receive my spirit."

60 He knelt down and cried out in a strong voice, "Lord, do not hold this sin against them." After saying this, he died.
