Matthew Chapter 27

1 When morning arrived, all the chief priests and the elders of the people gathered together to deliberate and plot against Jesus, intending to have him put to death.

2 After they had bound him, they took him away and handed him over to Pontius Pilate, the governor.

3 Then Judas, who had betrayed Him, when he saw that Jesus was condemned, felt deep remorse and returned the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests and elders.

4 He said, "I have sinned by betraying innocent blood." But they replied, "What does that have to do with us? That is your responsibility."

5 Then he threw the silver pieces down in the temple, left, and went away to hang himself.

6 The chief priests took the silver coins and said, "It is not lawful to put this money into the temple treasury, because it is blood money."

7 They deliberated and decided to use the money to purchase the potter’s field as a burial place for foreigners.

8 Therefore, that field has been called the Field of Blood to this day.

9 Then what was spoken by the prophet Jeremiah was fulfilled: "They took the thirty pieces of silver, the price set on him by the people of Israel, the value placed on the one they valued."

10 And they used it to buy the potter's field, just as the Lord had directed me.

11 Jesus stood before the governor, and the governor questioned him, saying, "Are you the King of the Jews?" Jesus replied, "You have said it."

12 When the chief priests and elders brought accusations against him, he remained silent and offered no response.

13 Then Pilate said to him, "Do you not hear all the accusations they are bringing against you?"

14 And he did not respond to him with a single word, so much so that the governor was utterly astonished.

15 At that festival, it was the governor's custom to release to the people any prisoner they chose.

16 At that time, they were holding a notorious prisoner named Barabbas.

17 So when the crowd had assembled, Pilate asked them, "Who do you want me to release to you? Barabbas, or Jesus, who is called the Messiah?"

18 For he understood that they had handed him over out of jealousy.

19 When he took his seat on the judgment bench, his wife sent him a message, saying, “Do not involve yourself with that innocent man, for today I have endured great distress in a dream because of him.”

20 However, the chief priests and the elders convinced the crowd to demand Barabbas be released and to call for Jesus' execution.

21 The governor responded and said to them, "Which of the two do you want me to release to you?" They replied, "Barabbas."

22 Pilate said to them, "What then should I do with Jesus, who is called the Christ?" They all replied, "Let him be crucified."

23 The governor asked, "What wrong has he done?" But they shouted even louder, "Crucify him!"

24 When Pilate realized that he could not achieve anything and that instead a disturbance was growing, he took water and washed his hands in front of the crowd, saying, "I am innocent of this righteous man’s blood. You are responsible for this."

25 Then all the people responded, saying, "Let the responsibility for his blood be on us and on our descendants."

26 Then Pilate released Barabbas to them, and after having Jesus flogged, he handed him over to be crucified.

27 Then the governor's soldiers took Jesus into the palace courtyard and assembled the entire company of soldiers around him.

28 They removed his clothes and dressed him in a robe of scarlet.

29 After weaving a crown of thorns, they placed it on his head and put a reed in his right hand. Then they knelt before him in mockery, saying, "Hail, King of the Jews!"

30 They spat on him, took the reed, and struck him on the head.

31 After they had finished mocking him, they removed the robe from him, put his own clothes back on, and led him away to be crucified.

32 As they were leaving, they encountered a man from Cyrene named Simon. They forced him to carry the cross for Jesus.

33 When they arrived at a place called Golgotha, which means "the place of the skull,"

34 They offered him wine mixed with bitter herbs to drink, but after tasting it, he refused to drink it.

35 They nailed him to the cross and divided his clothing among themselves by casting lots. This happened to fulfill what was spoken through the prophet: "They divided my garments among themselves, and for my clothing they cast lots."

36 They sat down and kept watch over him there.

37 They placed above his head the written charge against him, which read: "THIS IS JESUS, THE KING OF THE JEWS."

38 At that time, two criminals were crucified alongside him, one on his right side and the other on his left.

39 As they walked by, they hurled insults at him, shaking their heads in scorn.

40 They mocked him, saying, "You who claim to destroy the temple and rebuild it in three days, save yourself! If you are truly the Son of God, come down from the cross!"

41 In the same way, the chief priests, along with the scribes and elders, mocked him, saying,

42 He has saved others, but he cannot save himself. If he is truly the King of Israel, let him come down from the cross now, and then we will believe in him.

43 He placed his trust in God; let God rescue him now, if He desires him, for he claimed, "I am the Son of God."

44 The criminals who were crucified alongside him also hurled insults at him, mocking him in the same way.

45 From noon until three in the afternoon, darkness covered the entire land.

46 Around three in the afternoon, Jesus cried out in a loud voice, saying, "Eli, Eli, lema sabachthani?" which means, "My God, my God, why have you abandoned me?"

47 Some of those who were standing nearby, upon hearing this, said, "This man is calling for Elijah."

48 Immediately, one of them ran, took a sponge, filled it with sour wine, placed it on a stick, and offered it to him to drink.

49 The others said, "Leave him alone; let's see if Elijah comes to rescue him."

50 Jesus, after crying out once more in a loud voice, gave up his spirit.

51 And suddenly, the curtain of the temple was torn in two from top to bottom; the earth shook, and the rocks split apart.

52 And the tombs were opened, and many bodies of the holy ones who had died were raised to life.

53 And after His resurrection, they came out of the tombs, entered the holy city, and appeared to many people.

54 When the centurion and those with him, who were guarding Jesus, witnessed the earthquake and the events that took place, they were filled with great awe and said, "Truly, this man was the Son of God!"

55 Many women were present, watching from a distance. They had followed Jesus from Galilee, serving and supporting him.

56 Among them were Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James and Joseph, and the mother of Zebedee’s sons.

57 As evening approached, a wealthy man from Arimathea named Joseph arrived. He was also a disciple of Jesus.

58 He went to Pilate and requested the body of Jesus. Pilate then ordered that the body be given to him.

59 After Joseph took the body, he wrapped it in a clean linen cloth.

60 And he placed it in his own new tomb, which he had carved out of the rock. Then he rolled a large stone in front of the entrance to the tomb and went away.

61 Mary Magdalene and the other Mary were seated near the tomb, facing it.

62 On the day after the day of preparation, the chief priests and the Pharisees gathered together and went to Pilate.

63 They said, "Sir, we recall that this deceiver claimed while he was still alive, 'After three days, I will rise again.'"

64 Therefore, give the order to secure the tomb until the third day, so that his disciples do not come at night, steal his body, and then tell the people, ‘He has risen from the dead.’ If this happens, the final deception will be worse than the first.

65 Pilate said to them, "You have a guard. Go and secure the tomb as best as you can."

66 So they went and secured the tomb by sealing the stone and posting a guard.
