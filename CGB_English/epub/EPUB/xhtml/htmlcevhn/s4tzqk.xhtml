Proverbs Chapter 19

1 It is better to be poor and live with integrity than to be deceitful in speech and act foolishly.

2 Furthermore, it is not good for a person to lack understanding, and whoever rushes ahead with their actions makes mistakes.

3 A person's foolishness distorts their path, and their heart becomes resentful toward the LORD.

4 Wealth attracts many companions, but the poor are often abandoned even by their closest neighbors.

5 A false witness will not go unpunished, and whoever spreads lies will not escape judgment.

6 Many will seek the favor of a person in authority, and everyone is drawn to someone who gives generously.

7 All the relatives of the poor person despise him; how much more do his friends distance themselves from him! He pleads with them, using words, but they are nowhere to be found.

8 The one who gains wisdom shows love for their own soul; the one who holds onto understanding will discover what is truly good.

9 A false witness will not escape punishment, and whoever spreads lies will face destruction.

10 It is not fitting for a fool to enjoy luxury, nor is it proper for a servant to rule over princes.

11 A person's wisdom leads them to control their anger, and it brings them honor to overlook an offense.

12 The king's anger is like the fierce roar of a lion, but his approval is as refreshing as dew on the grass.

13 A foolish son brings disaster to his father, and a quarrelsome wife is like a constant dripping of water.

14 Houses and wealth are passed down from parents, but a wise and discerning wife is a gift from the LORD.

15 Laziness plunges one into a deep slumber, and the idle person will endure hunger.

16 Whoever obeys the commandment preserves their own life, but whoever disregards their ways will face death.

17 Whoever shows compassion to the poor is lending to the LORD, and the LORD will repay them for what they have done.

18 Discipline your child while there is still opportunity, and do not let your heart be swayed by his tears.

19 A person who is quick to anger will face consequences: even if you rescue them, you will have to do so repeatedly.

20 Listen to advice and accept instruction, so that you may gain wisdom for your future.

21 A person's heart may devise many plans, but it is the Lord's purpose that will ultimately prevail.

22 A person's true character is revealed through their acts of kindness, and it is better to be poor and honest than to be deceitful and untrustworthy.

23 Reverence for the LORD leads to life; those who possess it will dwell in contentment and will not be overtaken by harm.

24 A lazy person tucks their hand into their robe and is too weary to even lift it back to their mouth.

25 Strike a mocker, and the naive will take warning; rebuke someone with understanding, and they will gain insight.

26 A child who squanders their father's resources and drives away their mother is a disgraceful offspring, bringing shame and dishonor.

27 Stop, my child, from listening to teachings that lead you astray from the truths of wisdom.

28 A dishonest witness mocks justice, and the words of the wicked eagerly consume wrongdoing.

29 Punishment is prepared for mockers, and beatings for the backs of fools.
