Proverbs Chapter 23

1 When you sit down to eat with a ruler, pay close attention to what is set before you.

2 And exercise self-control, even to the point of restraint, if you are prone to overindulgence.

3 Do not crave his delicacies, for they are deceptive food.

4 Do not strive to gain wealth; abandon reliance on your own understanding.

5 Would you fix your gaze on something that has no substance? For wealth is fleetingâ€”it sprouts wings and soars away like an eagle into the sky.

6 Do not eat the food of someone who is stingy or greedy, nor crave their fancy dishes.

7 For as a person thinks within their heart, so they truly are. They may say to you, "Eat and drink," but their heart is not genuinely with you.

8 The food you have eaten, you will vomit up, and your pleasant words will be wasted.

9 Do not speak your words of wisdom in the presence of a fool, for he will only scorn and disregard the value of what you say.

10 Do not move the ancient boundary markers, and do not encroach upon the fields of the fatherless.

11 For their defender is powerful; he will argue their case against you.

12 Direct your heart toward learning, and focus your attention on the words of understanding.

13 Do not withhold discipline from a child, for if you correct them with the rod, they will not perish.

14 You should discipline him with the rod, and in doing so, you will rescue his soul from destruction.

15 My child, if your heart is wise, my own heart will also rejoice.

16 Indeed, my innermost being will rejoice when your words speak what is just and true.

17 Do not let your heart be envious of those who do wrong, but instead, maintain a continual reverence for the LORD throughout each day.

18 Certainly, there is a future outcome, and your hope will not be disappointed.

19 Listen, my child, and gain wisdom; direct your heart along the right path.

20 Do not associate with those who drink excessively or with those who indulge in gluttonous feasting.

21 For those who indulge in excessive drinking and overeating will end up in poverty, and laziness will leave a person clothed in nothing but rags.

22 Listen to your father, who gave you life, and do not disregard your mother when she grows old.

23 Acquire truth and never let it go; likewise, seek wisdom, discipline, and insight, and hold them firmly.

24 The father of a righteous person will rejoice greatly, and the one who fathers a wise child will find deep joy in him.

25 Your father and your mother will be filled with joy, and the one who gave birth to you will celebrate with gladness.

26 My child, dedicate your heart to me, and let your eyes carefully follow my ways.

27 A prostitute is like a deep pit, and a promiscuous woman is a treacherous, narrow well.

28 She lies in wait like a predator, seeking to ensnare her victims, and she multiplies the number of those who turn away from what is right, leading more people into wrongdoing.

29 Who experiences misery? Who knows deep sorrow? Who is caught up in constant quarrels? Who is filled with meaningless chatter? Who suffers injuries for no reason? Whose eyes are bloodshot and weary?

30 Those who linger over their wine; those who go in search of spiced and mixed drinks.

31 Do not gaze at wine when it is red, when it sparkles in the cup, when it flows smoothly.

32 In the end, it bites like a snake and strikes like a venomous serpent.

33 Your eyes will gaze upon forbidden women, and your heart will speak corrupt and twisted thoughts.

34 Indeed, you will be like someone lying down in the middle of the sea, or like one who reclines on the top of a shipâ€™s mast.

35 You might say, "They have struck me, but I was not hurt; they have beaten me, but I felt no pain. When will I wake up? I will go after it again."
