Job Chapter 24

1 Why is it that, although the Almighty is fully aware of all times and seasons, those who claim to know Him fail to recognize the days of His intervention?

2 Some people move boundary markers; they seize flocks by force and graze them for themselves.

3 They seize the donkey of the fatherless and take the widow's ox as collateral.

4 They drive the needy from their path; the poor of the land are forced into hiding together.

5 Look, like wild donkeys in the desert, they go out to their labor; they rise early to seek their prey. The wilderness provides food for them and for their children.

6 They each harvest their own grain in the field, and they gather the produce of the wicked.

7 They force the destitute to sleep without clothing, leaving them with no covering against the cold.

8 They are drenched by the mountain rains and cling to the rocks, desperate for shelter.

9 They snatch infants from their mothers' arms and seize the poor as collateral for debts.

10 They force him to go about naked, without any clothing, and they seize the grain from the hungry.

11 They produce oil within their walls and tread their winepresses, yet they still suffer from thirst.

12 People cry out in anguish from within the city, and the souls of the wounded plead for help. Yet God does not charge them with wrongdoing.

13 They are among those who defy the light; they do not understand its ways, nor do they remain on its paths.

14 The murderer rises at dawn to kill the poor and needy, and by night he acts like a thief.

15 The eye of the adulterer watches for the twilight, thinking, "No one will see me," and he covers his face to hide his identity.

16 In the darkness, they break into houses that they had scouted and marked during the day; they are strangers to the light.

17 For them, the morning is like the shadow of death; if they recognize it, they are overwhelmed by the terrors of death's darkness.

18 He moves as swiftly as flowing waters; their allotted portion is cursed upon the earth, and he does not turn his attention to the paths of the vineyards.

19 Drought and scorching heat dry up the waters from melted snow; in the same way, the grave consumes those who have sinned.

20 The womb will forget him; the worm will feast on him with delight; he will be remembered no more; and wickedness will be shattered like a tree.

21 He mistreats the childless woman who cannot bear children, and shows no kindness to the widow.

22 He brings down the powerful by His strength; He rises up, and no one can be certain of their life.

23 Even if he is granted safety and security, and he feels at ease, his eyes are still fixed on their actions and paths.

24 They are lifted up for a brief time, but soon they vanish and are brought down; they are removed like all others, cut off like the tops of the grain stalks.

25 If this is not the case now, who can prove me wrong and show that my words are meaningless?
