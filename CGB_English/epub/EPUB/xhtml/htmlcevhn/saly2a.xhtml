Job Chapter 40

1 Then the LORD responded to Job and said,

2 Can someone who argues with the Almighty correct Him? Let the one who accuses God provide an answer.

3 Then Job responded to the LORD and said,

4 Look, I am unworthy; how can I respond to you? I will cover my mouth and remain silent.

5 I have spoken once, and I will not reply again; indeed, I have spoken twice, but I will go no further.

6 Then the LORD responded to Job from the whirlwind and said,

7 Prepare yourself now like a strong and resolute person; I will question you, and you must answer me.

8 Do you intend to nullify my justice? Will you condemn me so that you may appear righteous?

9 Do you possess strength comparable to God's? Can you thunder with a voice as mighty as His?

10 Clothe yourself now with grandeur and dignity; adorn yourself with splendor and magnificence.

11 Pour out the fury of your anger; look upon all who are arrogant and bring them low.

12 Observe all who are arrogant and humble them; crush the wicked where they stand.

13 Bury them together in the dust, and shroud their faces in the hidden place.

14 In that case, I would also acknowledge to you that your own strength and ability can deliver you.

15 Consider now the behemoth, which I created along with you; it feeds on grass like an ox.

16 Behold now, his strength resides in his loins, and his power is centered in the muscles of his abdomen.

17 He swings his tail like a mighty cedar; the muscles of his thighs are tightly knit together.

18 His bones are like solid bronze; his limbs are as sturdy as iron rods.

19 He is the foremost of God's creations; the one who made him can also cause his sword to come against him.

20 Certainly, the mountains provide food for Him, where all the wild animals of the field roam and thrive.

21 He lies down beneath the shade of the trees, in the shelter of the reeds and the marshes.

22 The dense trees provide him with their shade; the willows by the stream surround him.

23 Look, he drinks up a river without hesitation; he is confident that he can even swallow the Jordan River whole.

24 He observes it keenly with his eyes; his nostrils detect the hidden traps.
