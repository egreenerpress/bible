Psalms Chapter 78

1 (A Psalm of Asaph, for instruction.) Listen, my people, to my teaching; pay close attention to the words I speak.

2 I will speak using parables; I will share profound and ancient mysteries.

3 We have heard and come to understand these things, just as our ancestors have recounted them to us.

4 We will not conceal these truths from our children, but will declare to the coming generation the praises of the LORD, His mighty power, and the wondrous deeds He has accomplished.

5 For He established a covenant witness in Jacob and set a decree in Israel, which He commanded our ancestors to teach to their children, so that the next generation might know them.

6 This is so that the next generation might know them, even the children yet to be born, who will rise up and recount them to their own children.

7 So that they might place their trust in God, and not overlook the mighty deeds He has done, but faithfully obey His commands.

8 And so they would not be like their ancestors—a stubborn and defiant generation, a generation whose hearts were not loyal to God, and whose spirits were not faithful to him.

9 The descendants of Ephraim, though equipped with weapons and armed with bows, retreated and turned away on the day of battle.

10 They did not uphold God's covenant and chose not to follow His law.

11 And they forgot His deeds, and the miraculous wonders He had shown them.

12 He performed extraordinary wonders in the presence of their ancestors, in the land of Egypt, in the region of Zoan.

13 He parted the sea and led them through it; He made the waters rise up like a wall.

14 During the day, He guided them with a cloud, and throughout the night, He led them with the light of fire.

15 He split the rocks in the wilderness and provided them with water as if from vast, deep springs.

16 He also made water flow from the rock, bringing forth streams, and caused waters to pour down like rivers.

17 They continued to sin against Him even more by rebelling against the Most High in the wilderness.

18 And they tested God in their hearts by demanding food to satisfy their cravings.

19 Indeed, they spoke against God, saying, "Can God prepare a feast in the wilderness?"

20 Look, he struck the rock, and water poured out, and streams overflowed abundantly. But can he also provide bread? Can he supply meat for his people?

21 Therefore, the LORD heard this and was filled with wrath; so a fire was ignited against Jacob, and His anger rose against Israel.

22 Because they did not have faith in God, nor did they rely on His deliverance.

23 Though He had given orders to the clouds above and opened the gates of the heavens,

24 He provided them with manna to eat, raining it down from above, and gave them grain from heaven.

25 The people ate the food of heaven; God provided them with abundant nourishment to satisfy their hunger.

26 He made an east wind blow across the sky, and by His might, He brought forth a south wind.

27 He sent down meat for them like a cloud of dust, and winged birds as plentiful as the sand by the sea.

28 And He caused it to fall in the center of their camp, surrounding their dwellings on all sides.

29 So they ate and were fully satisfied, for he granted them what they longed for.

30 They did not turn away from their desires. Even while the food was still in their mouths,

31 God's anger rose against them, and He struck down the strongest among them, cutting down the finest warriors of Israel.

32 Despite all this, they continued to sin and did not trust in His marvelous works.

33 As a result, He caused their days to be spent in futility and their years to be filled with distress.

34 When He caused their death, they sought Him; they turned back and earnestly sought God.

35 And they recalled that God was their steadfast foundation, and the Most High God their deliverer.

36 Yet they spoke to him with flattering words and deceived him with their tongues.

37 For their hearts were not truly devoted to him, nor were they faithful in upholding his covenant.

38 Yet, overflowing with compassion, he forgave their wrongdoing and did not destroy them. Time and again, he held back his anger and did not unleash the full force of his wrath.

39 For He remembered that they were merely mortal—a fleeting breath that passes away and does not return.

40 How often they rebelled against Him in the wilderness and caused Him sorrow in the desert!

41 Indeed, they turned away and tested God, and set boundaries for the Holy One of Israel.

42 They failed to remember His mighty power, nor did they recall the day when He rescued them from their foes.

43 He performed his miraculous signs in Egypt and displayed his wonders in the region of Zoan.

44 He turned their rivers into blood, and their streams became undrinkable.

45 He sent various kinds of flies among them, which consumed them, and frogs, which brought ruin upon them.

46 He also handed over their crops to the caterpillar and the fruit of their labor to the locust.

47 He devastated their grapevines with hailstones and ruined their sycamore fig trees with freezing frost.

48 He also surrendered their livestock to the destructive hail and their flocks to the scorching bolts of lightning.

49 He unleashed upon them the full intensity of his anger, fury, and righteous indignation, bringing distress and calamity by dispatching destructive messengers among them.

50 He paved a path for His wrath; He did not spare their lives from death, but handed them over to the plague.

51 He struck down all the firstborn in Egypt, the most vital of their strength in the dwellings of Ham.

52 But He led His own people out like a flock of sheep, guiding them through the wilderness as a shepherd guides his flock.

53 He guided them securely, so that they had no fear; yet the sea engulfed their enemies.

54 He led them to the boundary of His holy place, to this mountain, which His mighty hand had secured.

55 He drove out the nations before them and allotted their land as an inheritance, apportioning it by boundary lines. He settled the tribes of Israel in their dwelling places.

56 Yet they tested and angered the Most High God, and did not keep His decrees.

57 However, they turned away and acted treacherously, just as their ancestors had done; they were like a faulty bow that fails to shoot straight.

58 They angered Him by building worship sites on high places and aroused His jealousy by crafting carved idols.

59 When God heard this, he was filled with anger and utterly rejected Israel.

60 Therefore, He abandoned the tabernacle at Shiloh, the dwelling place He had established among His people.

61 He allowed his power to be taken captive, and his splendor to fall into the hands of the enemy.

62 He handed over his people to the sword and poured out his anger upon his chosen possession.

63 The fire destroyed their young men, and their young women were left without the opportunity to marry.

64 The priests were slain by the sword, and their widows did not weep or mourn.

65 Then the Lord arose as if from sleep, like a mighty warrior roused by the strength of wine.

66 He struck his enemies on their backsides, inflicting on them a lasting disgrace.

67 Furthermore, He rejected the dwelling place of Joseph and did not select the tribe of Ephraim.

68 However, he chose the tribe of Judah and Mount Zion, which he cherished.

69 He constructed his sanctuary as lofty as the heavens, like the earth which he has firmly established to endure forever.

70 He also selected David, his servant, and brought him from tending the flocks.

71 He took him from tending the ewes with their nursing lambs to shepherd Jacob, his own people, and Israel, his chosen inheritance.

72 So he cared for them with a pure and upright heart, and led them with the wisdom and skill of his hands.
