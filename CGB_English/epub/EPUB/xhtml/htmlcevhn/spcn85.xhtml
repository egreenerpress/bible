I Kings Chapter 17

1 Elijah the Tishbite, who was from the settlers of Gilead, said to Ahab, "As surely as the LORD, the God of Israel, lives—the God before whom I stand—there will be neither dew nor rain during these coming years, except at my command."

2 The LORD spoke to him, saying,

3 Go from here and turn eastward, and conceal yourself by the brook Cherith, which is east of the Jordan.

4 You will drink from the stream, and I have directed the ravens to provide food for you there.

5 So he went and acted in accordance with the word of the LORD. He went and settled by the Wadi Cherith, which is east of the Jordan.

6 The ravens brought him bread and meat in the morning, and bread and meat in the evening, and he drank water from the stream.

7 After some time had passed, the stream dried up because there had been no rain in the land.

8 The LORD spoke to him, saying,

9 "Get up and go to Zarephath, a town in the region of Sidon, and stay there. I have directed a widow in that place to provide for you."

10 So he got up and went to Zarephath. When he arrived at the city gate, he saw a widow there gathering sticks. He called out to her and said, “Please, bring me a little water in a container so I can drink.”

11 As she was going to get it, he called out to her and said, "Please bring me a piece of bread in your hand."

12 She replied, "As surely as the LORD your God lives, I have no bread—only a handful of flour in a jar and a little oil in a jug. Look, I am gathering a couple of sticks so I can go in and prepare it for myself and my son. We will eat it and then die."

13 Elijah said to her, "Do not be afraid. Go and do as you have said, but first prepare a small loaf of bread for me and bring it to me. Afterward, you may make some for yourself and your son."

14 For this is what the LORD, the God of Israel, declares: The jar of flour will not run out, and the jug of oil will not run dry, until the day the LORD sends rain upon the earth.

15 She went and acted according to Elijah's instruction. She, her household, and Elijah had enough food to eat for many days.

16 The jar of flour did not run out, nor did the jug of oil become empty, just as the LORD had promised through Elijah.

17 After these events, the son of the woman who owned the house became ill. His illness was so severe that he stopped breathing, and there was no life left in him.

18 She said to Elijah, "What do I have to do with you, O man of God? Have you come to me to remind me of my sin and to cause the death of my son?"

19 And he said to her, "Give me your son." Then he took the child from her arms, carried him up to the upper room where he was staying, and laid him on his own bed.

20 Then he called out to the LORD and said, "O LORD my God, have you also brought calamity upon the widow with whom I am staying, by causing her son to die?"

21 Then he stretched himself out over the child three times and called out to the LORD, saying, "O LORD my God, I ask you, please let this child’s life return to him."

22 The LORD listened to Elijah’s plea, and the child’s life returned to him, and he was restored to life.

23 Elijah took the child and carried him down from the upper room into the house. He handed the boy to his mother and said, "Look, your son is alive."

24 The woman said to Elijah, "Now I know for certain that you are a man of God, and that the word of the LORD spoken through you is true."
