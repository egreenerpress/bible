Genesis Chapter 2

1 In this way, the heavens and the earth were completed, along with everything in them.

2 On the seventh day, God completed the work He had done; and He ceased from all His labor on that day, resting from the entire work He had accomplished.

3 And God blessed the seventh day and set it apart as holy, because on it He rested from all His work of creation that He had done.

4 This is the account of the heavens and the earth at the time of their creation, when the LORD God formed the earth and the heavens.

5 At that time, no plants of the field had yet sprouted on the earth, and no herbs of the field had begun to grow, because the LORD God had not yet sent rain upon the earth, and there was no one to cultivate the ground.

6 But a mist rose up from the earth and moistened the entire surface of the ground.

7 Then the LORD God shaped humanity from the dust of the earth and breathed into their nostrils the breath of life, and the human became a living being.

8 The LORD God planted a garden in Eden, toward the east, and there He placed the man He had created.

9 Then the LORD God caused every kind of tree to grow out of the groundâ€”trees that were pleasing to look at and suitable for food. In the middle of the garden, He also placed the tree of life, along with the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.

10 A river flowed out of Eden to water the garden, and from there it divided into four main branches.

11 The name of the first river is Pishon; it flows around the entire land of Havilah, where gold is found.

12 The gold of that land is of excellent quality; there is also bdellium and onyx stone.

13 The name of the second river is Gihon; it is the one that flows around the entire land of Cush.

14 The name of the third river is the Tigris; it is the one that flows east of Assyria. The fourth river is the Euphrates.

15 The LORD God took the man and placed him in the garden of Eden to cultivate it and care for it.

16 And the LORD God instructed the man, saying, "You are free to eat from any tree in the garden."

17 However, you must not eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, for on the day you eat from it, you will certainly die.

18 And the LORD God said, "It is not good for the man to be alone; I will create a companion suitable for him."

19 Then the LORD God formed from the ground every wild animal of the field and every bird of the air, and He brought them to the man to see what he would name them. Whatever the man called each living creature, that became its name.

20 Adam assigned names to all the livestock, the birds of the sky, and every wild animal of the field. However, no suitable companion was found for Adam.

21 Then the LORD God caused a deep sleep to come over Adam, and he fell asleep. God took one of his ribs and closed up the flesh in its place.

22 Then the LORD God took the rib He had removed from the man and fashioned it into a woman. He brought her to the man.

23 Adam said, "This one is now bone from my bones and flesh from my flesh. She will be called Woman, because she was taken out of Man."

24 For this reason, a man will leave his father and mother and be united to his wife, and the two will become one flesh.

25 The man and his wife were both naked, yet they felt no shame.
