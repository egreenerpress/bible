Exodus Chapter 6

1 Then the LORD said to Moses, "Now you will see what I am about to do to Pharaoh. By a mighty display of power, he will let them go, and by a mighty display of power, he will drive them out of his land."

2 And God spoke to Moses, saying to him, "I am the LORD."

3 I revealed myself to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob as God Almighty, but by my name, the LORD (Yahweh), I did not make myself fully known to them.

4 I have also confirmed my covenant with them, to grant them the land of Canaan, the land where they lived as foreigners and sojourners.

5 I have also heard the cries of the Israelites, whom the Egyptians are holding as slaves, and I have remembered my covenant with them.

6 Therefore, tell the Israelites: I am the LORD, and I will deliver you from the oppression of the Egyptians. I will free you from their slavery and rescue you with mighty power and acts of great judgment.

7 I will take you as my own people, and I will be your God. You will come to know that I am the LORD your God, who delivers you from the oppression of the Egyptians.

8 I will bring you into the land that I solemnly promised to give to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, and I will give it to you as your inheritance. I am the LORD.

9 Moses relayed these words to the Israelites, but they did not listen to him because of their overwhelming despair and the harshness of their slavery.

10 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

11 Go in and speak to Pharaoh, the king of Egypt, and tell him to let the Israelites leave his land.

12 Moses spoke before the LORD, saying, "Look, the Israelites have not listened to me. How then will Pharaoh listen to me, especially since I am not a skilled speaker?"

13 The LORD spoke to Moses and Aaron, giving them a command for the Israelites and for Pharaoh, the king of Egypt, to lead the Israelites out of the land of Egypt.

14 These are the leaders of their ancestral families: The sons of Reuben, the firstborn of Israel, are Hanoch, Pallu, Hezron, and Carmi. These are the clans of Reuben.

15 The sons of Simeon were Jemuel, Jamin, Ohad, Jachin, Zohar, and Shaul, whose mother was a Canaanite woman. These are the clans of Simeon.

16 Here are the names of Levi’s sons, listed by their generations: Gershon, Kohath, and Merari. Levi lived for one hundred and thirty-seven years.

17 The descendants of Gershon were Libni and Shimei, grouped according to their family lines.

18 The sons of Kohath were Amram, Izhar, Hebron, and Uzziel. Kohath lived for one hundred and thirty-three years.

19 The sons of Merari were Mahli and Mushi. These are the clans of Levi, listed according to their family lines.

20 Amram married Jochebed, his father’s sister, and she bore him Aaron and Moses. Amram lived for one hundred and thirty-seven years.

21 The descendants of Izhar were Korah, Nepheg, and Zichri.

22 The sons of Uzziel were Mishael, Elzaphan, and Zithri.

23 Aaron married Elisheba, the daughter of Amminadab and sister of Naashon. She bore him four sons: Nadab, Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar.

24 The descendants of Korah were Assir, Elkanah, and Abiasaph. These are the clans of the Korahites.

25 Eleazar, the son of Aaron, married one of the daughters of Putiel, and she bore him a son named Phinehas. These are the leaders of the ancestral families of the Levites, listed according to their clans.

26 These are Aaron and Moses, to whom the LORD said, "Lead the Israelites out of the land of Egypt, organized according to their divisions."

27 These are the ones who spoke to Pharaoh, the king of Egypt, to bring the Israelites out of Egypt: they are Moses and Aaron.

28 And it happened on the day when the LORD spoke to Moses in the land of Egypt,

29 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying, "I am the LORD. Speak to Pharaoh, the king of Egypt, everything that I command you to say."

30 Moses said to the LORD, "Look, I am not eloquent in speech, so why would Pharaoh listen to me?"
