Matthew Chapter 22

1 And Jesus responded and spoke to them again using parables, saying,

2 The kingdom of heaven is like a certain king who prepared a wedding feast for his son.

3 He sent out his servants to summon those who had been invited to the wedding feast, but they refused to come.

4 Once more, he sent out other servants, instructing them, "Go and tell those who were invited, 'Look, I have prepared my feast: my oxen and fattened cattle have been slaughtered, and everything is ready. Come to the wedding banquet.'"

5 However, they treated the invitation with indifference and went about their own business—one to his farm, and another to his trade.

6 The remaining people seized his servants, mistreated them harshly, and killed them.

7 When the king heard about this, he was furious. He sent out his armies, destroyed those murderers, and burned their city to the ground.

8 Then he said to his servants, "The wedding feast is prepared, but those who were invited have proven themselves unworthy."

9 Therefore, go out to the main roads and invite to the wedding feast all whom you find.

10 So those servants went out into the main roads and gathered everyone they could find, both the unworthy and the worthy. And the wedding hall was filled with guests.

11 When the king entered to observe the guests, he noticed a man who was not wearing wedding attire.

12 The king said to him, "Friend, how did you come in here without wearing wedding clothes?" The man was left speechless.

13 Then the king said to his attendants, "Tie him up, hands and feet, and throw him out into the darkest place, where there will be weeping and grinding of teeth."

14 Many are invited, but few are selected.

15 Then the Pharisees went and plotted together how they might trap Him in His words.

16 They sent their disciples to him, along with the Herodians, saying, "Teacher, we know that you are truthful and teach the way of God with integrity, and that you are not swayed by anyone’s status or position, for you do not show partiality to people."

17 Tell us, then, what is your opinion? Is it right to pay taxes to Caesar, or not?

18 But Jesus, aware of their malicious intent, said, "Why are you testing me, you hypocrites?"

19 "Show me the coin used for the tax." So they brought him a denarius.

20 And He said to them, "Whose likeness and inscription is this?"

21 They answered him, "Caesar's." Then he said to them, "So give back to Caesar what belongs to Caesar, and to God what belongs to God."

22 When they heard these words, they were astonished, left him, and went on their way.

23 On that same day, the Sadducees, who deny the reality of resurrection, came to him and posed a question.

24 They said, "Teacher, Moses instructed that if a man dies without having children, his brother should marry the widow and father offspring on behalf of his deceased brother."

25 There were seven brothers among us. The first one married a wife but died without having any children, so he left his wife to his brother.

26 In the same way, the second brother married her, and then the third, and so on, up to the seventh.

27 Finally, the woman also died.

28 Therefore, in the resurrection, whose wife will she be among the seven? For all of them were married to her.

29 Jesus responded to them, saying, "You are mistaken because you do not understand the Scriptures or the power of God."

30 For in the resurrection, people do not marry nor are they given in marriage, but they are like the angels of God in heaven.

31 But concerning the resurrection of the dead, have you not read what God said to you, declaring,

32 I am the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob. God is not the God of the dead, but of the living.

33 When the crowd heard this, they were amazed by his teaching.

34 When the Pharisees heard that Jesus had silenced the Sadducees, they gathered together.

35 Then one of them, who was an expert in the law, posed a question to test Him, saying,

36 "Teacher, which commandment in the law is the most important?"

37 Jesus said to him, "You must love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, and with all your mind."

38 This is the foremost and most important commandment.

39 The second commandment is similar to the first: You must love your neighbor as yourself.

40 The entirety of the Law and the teachings of the prophets depend on these two commandments.

41 While the Pharisees were assembled, Jesus posed a question to them,

42 He asked them, "What do you think about the Messiah? Whose son is he?" They replied, "He is the son of David."

43 He said to them, "How then does David, speaking by the Spirit, call him 'Lord,' saying,

44 The LORD said to my Lord, "Sit at my right hand until I make your enemies a footstool under your feet."

45 If David refers to him as 'Lord,' how can he also be his son?

46 No one could offer a response to him, nor did anyone dare to ask him any further questions from that day onward.
