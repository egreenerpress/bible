Exodus Chapter 5

1 Afterward, Moses and Aaron went to Pharaoh and said to him, "This is what the LORD, the God of Israel, declares: 'Release my people, so that they may go and celebrate a festival in my honor in the wilderness.'"

2 Pharaoh responded, "Who is this LORD, that I should listen to His command and release Israel? I do not recognize this LORD, nor will I allow Israel to go."

3 They said, "The God of the Hebrews has confronted us. Please allow us to go on a three-day journey into the wilderness to offer sacrifices to the LORD our God. Otherwise, He may strike us with a plague or with the sword."

4 The king of Egypt said to them, "Why are you, Moses and Aaron, distracting the people from their work? Go back to your labor!"

5 Pharaoh said, "Look, the people of the land are now numerous, and you are letting them stop working and rest from their labor."

6 On that same day, Pharaoh gave orders to the taskmasters overseeing the people and to their officers, saying,

7 You will no longer provide the people with straw for making bricks, as you have done before. Instead, they must go and gather straw for themselves.

8 The quota of bricks that they were required to make before, you shall now impose on them without reducing it in any way. They are lazy, and that is why they are crying out, saying, ‘Let us go and offer sacrifices to our God.’

9 Increase the workload for the men, so that they must focus on their labor and not pay attention to empty promises or deceptive words.

10 The overseers of the people went out, along with their officials, and they addressed the people, saying, "This is what Pharaoh declares: 'I will no longer provide you with straw.'"

11 Go and gather straw for yourselves wherever you can find it, but your required amount of work will not be reduced.

12 So the people were dispersed across the entire land of Egypt to collect stubble for use instead of straw.

13 The overseers pressed them urgently, saying, "Complete your work—your daily quota—just as you did when straw was provided."

14 The overseers of the Israelites, whom Pharaoh’s taskmasters had appointed over them, were beaten and questioned, “Why have you not completed your required quota of bricks, both yesterday and today, as you did before?”

15 Then the overseers of the Israelites came and pleaded with Pharaoh, saying, "Why are you treating your servants this way?"

16 Your servants are not being provided with straw, yet they tell us, "Make bricks!" Look, your servants are being beaten, but the fault lies with your own people.

17 But he replied, "You are lazy, you are lazy! That is why you say, 'Let us go and offer sacrifices to the LORD.'"

18 "Go now and get to work. You will not be given any straw, but you must still produce the full quota of bricks."

19 The overseers of the Israelites realized they were in a dire situation after being told, "You must not reduce the number of bricks required for your daily quota."

20 Moses and Aaron encountered them as they were leaving Pharaoh’s presence, standing in their path.

21 They said to them, "May the LORD take notice of you and judge you, for you have made us utterly repulsive in the eyes of Pharaoh and his officials, putting a sword in their hands to kill us."

22 Moses went back to the LORD and said, "Lord, why have you brought such trouble upon this people? Why did you send me?

23 Since I went to Pharaoh to speak in your name, he has only brought more harm to this people, and you have not rescued your people at all.
