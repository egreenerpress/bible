Genesis Chapter 9

1 And God blessed Noah and his sons, saying to them, "Be fruitful, increase in number, and fill the earth."

2 And the fear and awe of you will be upon every animal of the earth, every bird of the sky, every creature that moves on the ground, and every fish of the sea. They are placed under your authority.

3 Every living creature that moves shall be food for you; just as I have given you the green plants, I now give you all things.

4 However, you must not eat the flesh with its life, that is, its blood.

5 I will certainly demand an accounting for your lifeblood—your very life. From every animal I will require it, and from every human being as well. I will hold each person accountable for the life of another human being.

6 Whoever takes a human life must have their own life taken by human hands, for God created humanity in His own image.

7 And you, be fruitful and increase in number; multiply abundantly on the earth and fill it.

8 And God spoke to Noah and his sons who were with him, saying,

9 And now, I am establishing my covenant with you and with your descendants after you.

10 And with every living creature that is with you—the birds, the livestock, and all the wild animals of the earth—every living thing that came out of the ark, I establish this covenant with all the creatures of the earth.

11 I will establish my covenant with you: never again will all living beings be destroyed by the waters of a flood, nor will there ever again be a flood to devastate the earth.

12 And God said, "This is the sign of the covenant I am establishing between me and you, and every living creature with you, for all generations to come:

13 I place my rainbow in the clouds, and it will serve as a sign of the covenant between me and the earth.

14 And it will happen that when I cause a cloud to cover the earth, the rainbow will appear in the cloud.

15 I will remember the covenant I have established between myself and you, as well as every living creature of all flesh. Never again will the waters become a flood to destroy all life.

16 The rainbow will appear in the clouds, and when I see it, I will remember the eternal covenant between God and every living creature of all flesh that is on the earth.

17 And God said to Noah, "This is the sign of the covenant that I have established between myself and all living creatures on the earth."

18 The sons of Noah who came out of the ark were Shem, Ham, and Japheth. Ham was the father of Canaan.

19 These are the three sons of Noah, and from them the entire earth was populated.

20 Noah began to work the land as a farmer, and he planted a vineyard.

21 He drank the wine, became intoxicated, and lay exposed inside his tent.

22 And Ham, the father of Canaan, saw his father’s nakedness and went out to tell his two brothers about it.

23 Shem and Japheth took a garment, placed it over both their shoulders, and walked backward to cover their father’s nakedness. They turned their faces away so that they did not see their father’s exposed state.

24 When Noah awoke from his wine, he realized what his youngest son had done to him.

25 And he declared, "Cursed be Canaan; he shall become the lowest of servants to his brothers."

26 And he declared, "May the LORD, the God of Shem, be praised! And may Canaan become his servant."

27 God will cause Japheth to prosper, and he will live in the dwellings of Shem; Canaan will become his servant.

28 And Noah lived for three hundred and fifty years after the flood.

29 Noah lived a total of nine hundred and fifty years, and then he died.
