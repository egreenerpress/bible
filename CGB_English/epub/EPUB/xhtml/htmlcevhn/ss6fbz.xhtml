Luke Chapter 24

1 On the first day of the week, at the earliest light of dawn, they went to the tomb, carrying the spices they had prepared, along with some others who accompanied them.

2 They discovered that the stone had been rolled away from the tomb.

3 They went inside and discovered that the body of the Lord Jesus was not there.

4 As they stood there, deeply puzzled about what had happened, suddenly two men appeared beside them, dressed in radiant clothing.

5 As they were filled with fear and bowed their faces to the ground, the men said to them, "Why are you searching for the living among the dead?"

6 "He is not here; he has risen. Remember what he told you while he was still in Galilee."

7 He said, "The Son of Man must be handed over to sinful people, be crucified, and on the third day rise again."

8 And they recalled what he had said.

9 They returned from the tomb and reported all these things to the eleven apostles and to all the others.

10 The women who reported these things to the apostles were Mary Magdalene, Joanna, Mary the mother of James, and the other women who were with them.

11 Their words appeared to them as meaningless stories, and they did not believe what was said.

12 Then Peter got up and ran to the tomb. Bending down to look inside, he saw the linen burial cloths lying there by themselves. He left, marveling to himself about what had happened.

13 And look, two of them were traveling that same day to a village called Emmaus, which was about seven miles from Jerusalem.

14 They discussed among themselves all the events that had taken place.

15 As they were talking together and discussing these things, Jesus himself approached and began walking with them.

16 But their eyes were kept from recognizing him.

17 And He said to them, "What kind of conversation is this that you are having with each other as you walk along, looking so downcast?"

18 One of them, named Cleopas, responded, saying to Him, "Are You the only visitor in Jerusalem who is unaware of the events that have taken place there in recent days?"

19 He asked them, "What things are you discussing?" They replied, "We are talking about Jesus of Nazareth, who was a prophet powerful in both action and speech in the sight of God and all the people."

20 And how the chief priests and our leaders handed him over to be sentenced to death and had him crucified.

21 We had hoped that he was the one who would redeem Israel. Moreover, today is the third day since all these events took place.

22 Indeed, some women from our group also amazed us. They went to the tomb early in the morning.

23 When they could not find his body, they returned and reported that they had seen a vision of angels, who declared that he was alive.

24 Some of those who were with us went to the tomb and found it exactly as the women had described, but they did not see him.

25 Then He said to them, "You are foolish and slow to believe all that the prophets have declared."

26 Was it not necessary for the Christ to endure these sufferings and then enter into his glory?

27 Starting with the writings of Moses and continuing through all the prophets, he explained to them the passages in all the Scriptures that referred to himself.

28 As they approached the village where they were heading, Jesus acted as if he intended to continue traveling further.

29 But they urged him strongly, saying, "Stay with us, for it is nearly evening, and the day is almost over." So he went in to stay with them.

30 As he was sitting at the table with them, he took bread, gave thanks for it, broke it, and gave it to them.

31 Then their eyes were opened, and they recognized him; and he disappeared from their sight.

32 Then they said to each other, "Were not our hearts deeply stirred within us as he spoke with us on the road and explained the Scriptures to us?"

33 At that very moment, they got up and returned to Jerusalem, where they found the eleven disciples gathered together with those who were with them.

34 They were saying, "The Lord has truly risen, and he has appeared to Simon."

35 They recounted the events that had taken place along the way and how they had recognized him when he broke the bread.

36 As they were speaking these things, Jesus himself appeared among them and said to them, "May peace be with you."

37 But they were filled with fear and alarm, thinking they had seen a ghost.

38 And He said to them, "Why are you distressed? And why do doubts and questions arise in your hearts?"

39 Look at my hands and my feet—it is truly me. Touch me and see for yourselves, because a spirit does not have flesh and bones as you see that I have.

40 After he had finished speaking, he showed them his hands and his feet.

41 While they were still disbelieving because of their joy and amazement, He asked them, "Do you have anything here to eat?"

42 They offered him a portion of grilled fish and some honeycomb.

43 He took it and ate it in their presence.

44 And He said to them, "These are the words I spoke to you while I was still with you: everything written about Me in the Law of Moses, the Prophets, and the Psalms must be fulfilled."

45 Then He opened their minds, enabling them to comprehend the Scriptures.

46 He said to them, "This is what is written: The Messiah had to suffer and rise from the dead on the third day."

47 And it should be proclaimed in His name to all nations, starting from Jerusalem, that people must turn from their sins and receive forgiveness.

48 You are witnesses to these events.

49 Listen carefully: I am sending upon you what my Father has promised. But remain in the city of Jerusalem until you are clothed with power from heaven.

50 He led them out as far as Bethany, then raised his hands and blessed them.

51 As he was blessing them, he was taken from their presence and ascended into heaven.

52 They worshiped him and went back to Jerusalem filled with overwhelming joy.

53 They were constantly in the temple, giving praise and honor to God. Amen.
