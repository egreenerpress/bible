I Timothy Chapter 3

1 This statement is trustworthy and true: If anyone aspires to the role of overseer, they are seeking a noble and honorable task.

2 A church leader must be above reproach, faithful to one spouse, self-controlled, sensible, respectable, hospitable, and skilled in teaching.

3 He must not be given to excessive drinking, nor prone to violence, nor driven by a desire for dishonest gain. Instead, he should be gentle, not quarrelsome, and free from the love of money.

4 A person who manages their own household well, keeping their children under control with proper dignity and respect.

5 For if someone does not know how to manage their own household, how will they be able to oversee the community of God?

6 Do not appoint someone who is new to the faith, for if they become prideful, they may fall into the same judgment as the devil.

7 Furthermore, he must have a good reputation among those outside the faith, so that he does not fall into disgrace and become ensnared by the devil.

8 In the same way, deacons must be dignified, not deceitful in speech, not indulging in excessive wine, and not pursuing dishonest gain.

9 Maintaining the profound truth of the faith with a clear and upright conscience.

10 First, these individuals must be tested and proven worthy; only then should they be allowed to serve as deacons, provided they are found to be without fault.

11 In the same way, their wives must be dignified, not given to malicious gossip, self-controlled, and trustworthy in every matter.

12 Deacons must each be married to only one wife, managing their children and their households with excellence.

13 For those who have served well as deacons gain a position of honor and confidence, along with great assurance in the faith that is rooted in Christ Jesus.

14 I am writing these things to you, with the hope of coming to you soon.
