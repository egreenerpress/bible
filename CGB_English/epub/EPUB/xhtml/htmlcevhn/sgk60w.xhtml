Job Chapter 18

1 Then Bildad the Shuhite replied and said,

2 How long will you continue talking without stopping? Pay attention, and then we will speak.

3 Why are we treated like animals and regarded as worthless in your eyes?

4 He tears himself apart in his rage: should the earth be abandoned for your sake? Should the rock be dislodged from its place?

5 Indeed, the light of the wicked will be extinguished, and the glow of their fire will no longer shine.

6 The light within his dwelling will turn to darkness, and the lamp that once shone for him will be extinguished along with his life.

7 The path of his power will be constrained, and his own schemes will bring about his downfall.

8 For he is caught in a net by his own actions, and he walks straight into a trap.

9 The trap will seize him by the heel, and the plunderer will overpower him.

10 A hidden snare is set for him on the ground, and a trap lies in wait for him along the path.

11 Fears will surround him on every side, overwhelming him with terror, and they will force him to flee in haste.

12 His strength will be weakened by hunger, and calamity will be poised to strike him at every turn.

13 It will consume the vigor of his flesh; even the most deadly of plagues will devour his strength.

14 His sense of security will be uprooted from his dwelling, and it will lead him to the ruler of all that is dreadful.

15 It will remain in his dwelling place, for it no longer belongs to him; sulfur will be scattered over his home.

16 His roots will wither beneath the ground, and his branches will be cut off above.

17 His memory will fade from the earth, and his name will be forgotten in the streets.

18 He will be forced from the realm of light into utter darkness and banished from the world entirely.

19 He will have no son or descendant among his people, nor anyone left to continue his lineage in the places where he once lived.

20 Those who come after him will be utterly appalled at his fate, just as those who came before were filled with dread.

21 Indeed, such are the homes of the wicked, and this is the dwelling place of those who do not acknowledge God.
