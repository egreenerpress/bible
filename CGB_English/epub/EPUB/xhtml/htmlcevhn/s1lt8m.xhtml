Job Chapter 22

1 Then Eliphaz from Teman replied and said,

2 Can a person truly bring benefit to God, as someone who is wise might bring benefit to themselves?

3 Does the Almighty find any delight in your righteousness? Does He gain anything when you strive to make your ways blameless?

4 Will he correct you because he fears you? Will he bring you into judgment to contend with you?

5 Is not your wrongdoing immense, and your sins without limit?

6 You have taken a pledge from your fellow Israelite without cause and stripped the clothing from those who have nothing to wear.

7 You have not provided water for the weary to drink, and you have withheld bread from the hungry.

8 But as for the powerful and influential man, he possessed the land, and the respected and honorable man lived securely in it.

9 You have turned away widows with nothing, and you have crushed the strength of the fatherless.

10 Therefore, traps surround you on every side, and sudden terror overwhelms you.

11 Or the darkness, which prevents you from seeing, and the overwhelming flood of waters that engulf you.

12 Is not God in the highest heavens? Look at the stars, how lofty they are!

13 And you say, "How can God know? Can He truly judge through the thick darkness?"

14 Dense clouds envelop Him, so that He cannot be seen; yet He moves throughout the expanse of the heavens.

15 Have you considered the ancient path that wicked people have followed?

16 These were swept away before their time, their very foundation washed away by a flood.

17 They said to God, 'Leave us alone! What can the Almighty do for us?'

18 Yet He filled their homes with abundance and good things; but the plans and schemes of the wicked are far removed from me.

19 The righteous observe it and rejoice, while the innocent mock them with derision.

20 While our possessions remain intact, the fire consumes what little is left of them.

21 Take the time to truly know Him, and you will find peace; in doing so, blessings will come to you.

22 Please accept the instruction from his mouth, and store his words deeply within your heart.

23 If you return to the Almighty, you will be restored; you will remove wrongdoing far from your dwelling places.

24 Then you will store up gold as if it were mere dust, and the gold of Ophir as plentiful as the stones of the streams.

25 Indeed, the Almighty will be your protection, and you will have an abundance of wealth.

26 For then you will find joy in the Almighty, and you will confidently lift your face to God.

27 You should direct your prayer to Him, and He will listen to you; then you must fulfill the vows you have made.

28 You will declare a matter, and it will be fulfilled for you; and light will shine upon your path.

29 When people are brought low, you will say, "There is hope for restoration," and He will deliver those who are humble.

30 He will rescue the innocent in their time of need, and their deliverance is secured through the integrity of your actions.
