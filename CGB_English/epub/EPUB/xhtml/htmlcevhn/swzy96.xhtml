Genesis Chapter 3

1 Now the serpent was more cunning than any other creature of the field that the LORD God had created. He said to the woman, "Did God really say, 'You must not eat from any tree in the garden'?"

2 The woman replied to the serpent, "We are allowed to eat the fruit from the trees in the garden."

3 But concerning the fruit of the tree that is in the middle of the garden, God has said, “You must not eat from it, nor should you even touch it, or else you will die.”

4 The serpent said to the woman, "You certainly will not die."

5 For God knows that on the day you eat from it, your eyes will be opened, and you will become like divine beings, knowing both good and evil.

6 When the woman saw that the tree was good for food, pleasing to the eyes, and desirable for gaining wisdom, she took some of its fruit and ate it. She also gave some to her husband, who was with her, and he ate it as well.

7 Then the eyes of both of them were opened, and they realized that they were naked. So they sewed fig leaves together and made coverings for themselves.

8 They heard the sound of the LORD God moving about in the garden during the evening breeze. Then Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the LORD God among the trees of the garden.

9 And the LORD God called out to Adam, saying to him, "Where are you?"

10 And he replied, "I heard your voice in the garden, and I was afraid because I was naked, so I hid myself."

11 And He said, "Who told you that you were naked? Have you eaten from the tree about which I commanded you not to eat?"

12 The man replied, "The woman you gave to be with me—she gave me fruit from the tree, and I ate it."

13 The LORD God said to the woman, "What have you done?" The woman replied, "The serpent deceived me, and I ate."

14 The LORD God said to the serpent, "Because you have done this, you are cursed more than all livestock and every wild animal. You will crawl on your belly, and you will eat dust all the days of your life."

15 I will create hostility between you and the woman, and between your offspring and hers. Her descendant will crush your head, and you will strike his heel.

16 To the woman, He said, "I will greatly increase your pain in childbearing; with painful labor you will give birth to children. Your longing will be for your husband, yet he will have authority over you."

17 And to Adam, He said, "Because you listened to your wife's voice and ate from the tree about which I commanded you, saying, 'You must not eat from it,' the ground is now cursed because of you. You will toil in hardship to eat from it all the days of your life."

18 The ground will produce thorns and thistles for you, and you will eat the plants of the field.

19 You will labor and toil to produce food, working by the sweat of your brow, until you return to the ground from which you were taken. For you were formed from dust, and to dust you will return.

20 Adam named his wife Eve, because she would become the mother of all living beings.

21 The LORD God made garments of animal skins for Adam and his wife, and He clothed them.

22 The LORD God said, "Now the man has become like one of us, knowing good and evil. Therefore, to prevent him from reaching out his hand, taking fruit from the tree of life, eating it, and living forever—"

23 Therefore, the LORD God banished him from the Garden of Eden, to work the soil from which he had been formed.

24 So God expelled the man from the garden, and He stationed cherubim to the east of the Garden of Eden, along with a flaming sword that moved back and forth, to guard the way to the tree of life.
