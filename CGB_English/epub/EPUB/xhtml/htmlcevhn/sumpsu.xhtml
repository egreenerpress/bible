Deuteronomy Chapter 9

1 Listen, Israel: Today you are about to cross the Jordan River to enter and take possession of nations larger and stronger than you, with cities that are great and fortified, reaching up to the sky.

2 A people great and mighty, the descendants of the Anakim, whom you know well and about whom you have heard it said, "Who can stand against the descendants of Anak?"

3 Recognize today that the LORD your God is the one who goes ahead of you. Like a blazing fire, he will consume them and bring them down before you. You will drive them out and swiftly destroy them, just as the LORD has promised you.

4 Do not say in your heart, after the LORD your God has driven them out before you, “Because of my righteousness the LORD has brought me in to take possession of this land.” Rather, it is because of the wickedness of these nations that the LORD is driving them out before you.

5 It is not because of your righteousness or the integrity of your heart that you are going in to take possession of their land. Rather, it is because of the wickedness of these nations that the LORD your God is driving them out before you, and to fulfill the promise that the LORD swore to your ancestors—Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

6 Therefore, understand this: the LORD your God is not giving you this good land to possess because of your righteousness, for you are a stubborn and rebellious people.

7 Remember, and do not forget, how you provoked the LORD your God to anger in the wilderness. From the day you left the land of Egypt until you arrived in this place, you have continually rebelled against the LORD.

8 At Horeb, you provoked the Lord to anger, so much so that the Lord was furious with you and intended to destroy you.

9 When I went up the mountain to receive the stone tablets—the tablets of the covenant that the LORD made with you—I stayed on the mountain for forty days and forty nights. During that time, I did not eat bread or drink water.

10 The LORD gave me two stone tablets inscribed by the finger of God. On them were written all the words that the LORD had spoken to you on the mountain from the midst of the fire on the day of the gathering.

11 At the conclusion of forty days and forty nights, the LORD gave me the two stone tablets, which were the tablets of the covenant.

12 The LORD said to me, "Get up and go down from here quickly, for the people you brought out of Egypt have become corrupt. They have quickly turned away from the path I commanded them to follow; they have made for themselves a cast idol."

13 Moreover, the LORD spoke to me, saying, "I have observed this people, and indeed, they are a stubborn and obstinate people."

14 Leave me alone, so that I may destroy them and erase their name from under heaven. Then I will make of you a nation stronger and greater than they are.

15 So I turned and descended from the mountain, and the mountain was ablaze with fire. The two tablets of the covenant were in my hands.

16 I observed, and indeed, you had sinned against the LORD your God. You had made for yourselves a cast metal calf and had quickly turned away from the path that the LORD had commanded you to follow.

17 I took the two stone tablets and threw them from my hands, shattering them before your eyes.

18 I prostrated myself before the LORD, just as I had done before, for forty days and forty nights. During that time, I neither ate bread nor drank water because of all the sins you had committed by acting wickedly in the sight of the LORD, provoking Him to anger.

19 For I was fearful of the intense anger and burning wrath with which the LORD was enraged against you, intending to destroy you. But the LORD listened to me at that time as well.

20 The LORD was extremely angry with Aaron, to the point of intending to destroy him; and at that same time, I also prayed on Aaron's behalf.

21 I took the object of your sin—the calf you had made—and burned it in the fire. I crushed it, grinding it into fine powder until it was as fine as dust. Then I threw the dust into the stream that flowed down from the mountain.

22 At Taberah, at Massah, and at Kibroth-hattaavah, you stirred the LORD to anger.

23 Similarly, when the LORD sent you out from Kadesh-barnea, instructing you, "Go up and take possession of the land I have given you," you rebelled against the command of the LORD your God. You did not trust Him or listen to His voice.

24 You have been defiant against the LORD from the very day I first knew you.

25 So I prostrated myself before the LORD for forty days and forty nights, just as I had done before, because the LORD had declared his intention to destroy you.

26 So I prayed to the LORD and said, "O Lord GOD, do not destroy your people, your own possession, whom you have redeemed by your great power and brought out of Egypt with a mighty hand."

27 Remember your servants Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. Do not focus on the defiance of this people, their wickedness, or their sin.

28 Otherwise, the land from which you brought us out might say, "Because the LORD was not able to bring them into the land he promised them, and because he despised them, he led them out to kill them in the wilderness."

29 Yet they are your people and your inheritance, whom you brought out with your great power and your outstretched arm.
