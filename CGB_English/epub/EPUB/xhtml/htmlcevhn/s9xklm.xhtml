Proverbs Chapter 18

1 A person, driven by their own desires, may isolate themselves and pursue every form of wisdom, delving deeply into matters beyond their understanding.

2 A fool takes no pleasure in gaining wisdom, but only in expressing whatever thoughts come to mind.

3 When the wicked arrive, contempt follows closely behind, and disgrace brings shame with it.

4 A person's words are like deep waters, and the source of wisdom is like a bubbling, ever-flowing stream.

5 It is wrong to show favoritism to the wicked or to undermine the righteous in a legal decision.

6 A fool's words lead to conflict, and his speech invites punishment.

7 A fool's words bring about their own ruin, and their speech becomes a trap that ensnares their very life.

8 The words of a gossip are like sharp wounds, piercing deep into the innermost being.

9 A person who is lazy in their work is akin to one who is a great destroyer.

10 The name of the LORD is a mighty fortress; the righteous flee to it and find protection.

11 The wealth of a rich person serves as his fortified city, and in his own imagination, it stands as a towering wall of security.

12 Before disaster strikes, a person's heart is often filled with pride, but humility comes before honor.

13 Whoever responds to a matter before fully understanding it acts foolishly and brings disgrace upon themselves.

14 The inner strength of a person can endure physical weakness, but who can endure a spirit that is crushed or broken?

15 The discerning heart acquires understanding, and the attentive ear of the wise seeks out knowledge.

16 A person's gift opens doors for them and gains them access to influential people.

17 The one who presents his case first appears to be in the right, but then his opponent comes forward and examines him closely.

18 The casting of lots puts an end to disputes and settles matters even among the powerful.

19 A brother who has been wronged is more difficult to win back than a fortified city, and disputes between siblings are as unyielding as the bars of a fortress.

20 A person will be satisfied by the fruit of their words; they will be filled with the results of what they speak.

21 The ability to bring life or death lies within the power of speech, and those who cherish it will reap the consequences of their words.

22 Whoever finds a wife finds something truly good and receives favor from the LORD.

23 The poor person pleads with humility, but the wealthy respond with harshness.

24 A person who desires to have friends must demonstrate kindness and goodwill. Yet, there is a friend whose loyalty and devotion surpass even that of a brother.
