Exodus Chapter 11

1 The LORD said to Moses, "I will bring one more plague upon Pharaoh and upon Egypt. After this, he will let you go from here. In fact, when he finally releases you, he will drive you out completely."

2 Speak now to the people, and let each man ask his neighbor, and each woman ask her neighbor, for articles of silver and gold.

3 The LORD caused the Egyptians to regard the Israelites favorably. Additionally, Moses himself was held in high esteem throughout the land of Egypt—both by Pharaoh’s officials and by the Egyptian people.

4 And Moses declared, "This is what the LORD says: Around midnight, I will pass through the heart of Egypt."

5 Every firstborn in the land of Egypt will die, from the firstborn of Pharaoh who sits on his throne to the firstborn of the servant girl who works at the mill, and even the firstborn of the livestock.

6 And there will be a loud and anguished outcry across the entire land of Egypt, unlike anything that has ever happened before or will ever happen again.

7 But not even a dog will growl or bark against any of the Israelites, whether against a person or an animal. This is so you may understand how the LORD makes a distinction between the Egyptians and the Israelites.

8 All your officials will come to me, bow down before me, and plead, ‘Leave, you and all the people who follow you.’ After that, I will depart.” Then Moses left Pharaoh’s presence in fierce anger.

9 The LORD said to Moses, "Pharaoh will not listen to you, so that I may perform even more extraordinary acts in the land of Egypt."

10 Moses and Aaron performed all these miraculous signs in the presence of Pharaoh, but the LORD caused Pharaoh’s heart to become stubborn, so that he refused to allow the Israelites to leave his land.
