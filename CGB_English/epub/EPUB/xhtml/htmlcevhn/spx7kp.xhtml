Song of Songs Chapter 4

1 Look, you are beautiful, my beloved; truly, you are beautiful. Your eyes are like doves, shining through the veil of your hair. Your hair flows like a flock of goats descending from the slopes of Gilead.

2 Your teeth are like a flock of sheep that have just been shorn, coming up from the washing; each one perfectly matched, and none among them is missing or barren.

3 Your lips are like a scarlet ribbon, and your words are lovely. Your cheeks are like a slice of pomegranate, glowing through the veil of your hair.

4 Your neck is like the tower of David, built as an armory, adorned with a thousand shields, all belonging to mighty warriors.

5 Your two breasts are like two young gazelles, twins that graze among the lilies.

6 Until the day dawns and the shadows disappear, I will go to the mountain of myrrh and to the hill of frankincense.

7 You are entirely beautiful, my beloved; there is no flaw in you.

8 Come with me from Lebanon, my bride, come with me from Lebanon. Gaze from the heights of Amana, from the peaks of Shenir and Hermon, from the lairs of lions, from the mountains where leopards roam.

9 You have captured my heart, my sister, my bride; you have captivated me with just one glance from your eyes, with a single strand of your necklace.

10 How beautiful is your love, my sister, my bride! Your love is more delightful than wine, and the fragrance of your perfumes surpasses all spices!

11 Your words, my beloved, are as sweet as the honeycomb; honey and milk flow from your speech. The fragrance of your clothing is like the scent of the forests of Lebanon.

12 My beloved is like a secluded garden, my bride, a private spring, a fountain kept under seal.

13 Your garden is like a lush orchard of pomegranates, filled with delightful fruits, along with henna and spikenard.

14 Fragrant spikenard and saffron; aromatic calamus and cinnamon, along with all varieties of frankincense trees; myrrh and aloes, together with the finest and most precious spices.

15 A spring of gardens, a source of fresh, flowing waters, and streams cascading from Lebanon.

16 Arise, O north wind; and come, O south wind; breathe upon my garden, so that its fragrant spices may be released. May my beloved enter his garden and partake of its delightful fruits.
