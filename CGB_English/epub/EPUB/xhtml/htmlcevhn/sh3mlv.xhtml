Deuteronomy Chapter 6

1 Here are the commands, decrees, and laws that the LORD your God has instructed me to teach you, so that you may follow them in the land you are about to enter and take possession of.

2 So that you may revere the LORD your God, obey all His laws and commandments, which I am instructing you—you, your children, and your grandchildren—throughout your entire life, and so that your days may be long.

3 Listen carefully, O Israel, and be diligent to obey these instructions, so that it may go well with you and that you may prosper greatly, just as the LORD, the God of your ancestors, has promised you, in the land that is rich and abundant, flowing with milk and honey.

4 Listen, Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one.

5 You must love the LORD your God with all your heart, with all your being, and with all your strength.

6 And these words that I am commanding you today must remain deeply rooted in your heart:

7 You must diligently teach these things to your children, speaking of them when you are at home, when you are traveling, when you lie down, and when you rise up.

8 You shall bind them as a sign on your hand, and they shall be like reminders on your forehead.

9 You shall write these words on the doorposts of your house and on your gates.

10 When the LORD your God brings you into the land he promised to your ancestors—Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob—to give you, you will find large and prosperous cities that you did not build.

11 You will enter houses filled with every good thing, which you did not provide, and drink from wells you did not dig, and enjoy vineyards and olive groves you did not plant. When you have eaten your fill and are satisfied,

12 Be careful not to forget the LORD, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, from the place of slavery.

13 You must revere the LORD your God, worship Him, and make your oaths in His name alone.

14 You must not follow after other gods, the gods of the peoples who live around you.

15 For the LORD your God is a God who demands exclusive devotion among you, so that the anger of the LORD your God does not flare up against you and wipe you out from the face of the earth.

16 You must not test the LORD your God, as you tested him at Massah.

17 You must carefully observe the commands of the LORD your God, along with His decrees and laws, which He has given to you.

18 You must do what is right and good in the eyes of the LORD, so that it may go well with you, and that you may enter and take possession of the good land the LORD promised to your ancestors.

19 To drive out all your enemies from before you, just as the LORD has promised.

20 When your child asks you in the future, saying, “What is the meaning of the decrees, laws, and rulings that the LORD our God has commanded you?”

21 You shall then say to your son, "We were slaves to Pharaoh in Egypt, and the LORD brought us out of Egypt with great power and strength."

22 The LORD performed extraordinary and severe signs and wonders in Egypt, against Pharaoh, and against all his household, right before our eyes.

23 He led us out from that place so that He might bring us into the land He had promised with an oath to our ancestors, to give it to us as our possession.

24 The LORD instructed us to follow all these decrees and to revere the LORD our God, for our lasting benefit, so that He might sustain our lives, as He has done to this day.

25 And it will be considered our righteousness if we are careful to obey all these commandments before the LORD our God, just as He has commanded us.
