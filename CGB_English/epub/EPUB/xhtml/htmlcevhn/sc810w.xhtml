II Corinthians Chapter 12

1 It is not beneficial for me to boast, but I will proceed to speak of visions and revelations from the Lord.

2 I know a man in Christ who, over fourteen years ago—whether in the body or out of the body, I cannot say; God knows—was caught up to the third heaven.

3 I knew a man like this—whether he was in his body or outside of it, I cannot say; only God knows—

4 He was taken up into paradise and heard inexpressible words, which no one is permitted to speak.

5 I will boast about such a person, but I will not boast about myself, except in my weaknesses.

6 Even if I wanted to boast, I would not be foolish, because I would only be speaking the truth. But I refrain from doing so, so that no one may think more highly of me than what they observe in my actions or hear from my words.

7 To prevent me from becoming overly proud because of the extraordinary revelations I received, I was given a thorn in my flesh—a messenger from Satan to torment me—so that I would not become conceited.

8 Regarding this matter, I pleaded with the Lord three times, asking that it might be taken away from me.

9 And he said to me, "My grace is enough for you, for my power is made complete in weakness." Therefore, I will most gladly boast all the more about my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may dwell in me.

10 Therefore, I find joy in weaknesses, in insults, in hardships, in persecutions, and in difficulties endured for the sake of Christ. For when I am weak, then I am truly strong.

11 I have been made to look foolish by boasting, but you forced me into it. I should have been commended by you, for I am not inferior to the most eminent apostles, even though I am nothing in myself.

12 Certainly, the marks of an apostle were demonstrated among you with great endurance, through miraculous signs, wonders, and powerful acts.

13 In what way were you less significant than the other churches, except that I did not burden you financially? Please forgive me for this perceived wrong.

14 Look, this is the third time I am preparing to come to you, and I will not be a burden to you. For I am not seeking what is yours, but you yourselves. After all, it is not the responsibility of the children to save up for their parents, but the parents for their children.

15 I am willing to give everything I have and exhaust myself completely for your sake. Even though my love for you grows ever greater, the love I receive in return seems to grow less.

16 But even if it is so, I did not impose any burden on you. Yet, being shrewd, I used wisdom to win you over.

17 Did I take advantage of you through any of those I sent to you?

18 I asked Titus to go, and I sent a brother with him. Did Titus take advantage of you in any way? Did we not act in the same spirit? Did we not follow the same path?

19 Once more, do you think we are defending ourselves to you? We speak before God in the presence of Christ, but everything we do, beloved, is for your strengthening and growth.

20 I am concerned that when I come to you, I may not find you as I hope, and that you may not find me as you expect. I fear there may be quarrels, jealousy, anger, rivalry, slander, gossip, arrogance, and disorder among you.

21 And to prevent this, when I come again, my God may humble me before you, and I will have to mourn over many who have already sinned and have not turned away from the impurity, sexual immorality, and indecent behavior they have practiced.
