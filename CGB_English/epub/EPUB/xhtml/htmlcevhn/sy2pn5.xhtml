Matthew Chapter 11

1 After Jesus had finished giving instructions to his twelve disciples, he left that place to go and teach and proclaim his message in their towns.

2 Now when John, who was in prison, heard about the deeds of Christ, he sent two of his disciples to Him.

3 And they asked him, "Are you the one who is to come, or should we expect someone else?"

4 Jesus responded to them, saying, "Go back and report to John what you hear and see:

5 The blind are given their sight, the lame are able to walk, those with leprosy are healed, the deaf can hear, the dead are raised to life, and the good news is proclaimed to the poor.

6 And fortunate is the one who does not take offense at me or stumble because of me.

7 As they were leaving, Jesus began to speak to the crowds about John, saying, "What did you go out into the wilderness to see? A reed swaying in the wind?

8 But what did you go out to see? A man dressed in fine clothes? Look, those who wear luxurious clothing are found in royal palaces.

9 But what did you go out to see? A prophet? Yes, I tell you, and even more than a prophet.

10 For this is the one about whom it is written: "Look, I am sending my messenger ahead of you, who will prepare the way before you."

11 Truly I tell you, among those born of women, no one greater than John the Baptist has ever arisen. Yet even the least significant person in the kingdom of heaven is greater than he.

12 From the time of John the Baptist until now, the kingdom of heaven has been forcefully advancing, and determined people are seizing it with urgency.

13 All the prophets and the Law foretold events up until the time of John.

14 And if you are willing to accept it, this is Elijah, the one who was destined to come.

15 Whoever has the ability to understand, let them pay close attention and truly comprehend.

16 To what shall I compare this generation? They are like children sitting in the marketplace, calling out to their friends,

17 And they said, "We played joyful music for you, but you did not dance; we sang mournful songs, but you did not grieve."

18 John came neither eating bread nor drinking wine, and people said, "He has a demon."

19 The Son of Man came eating and drinking, and they say, "Look at this man! He is a glutton and a drunkard, a friend of tax collectors and sinners!" Yet wisdom is proven right by the actions of those who follow it.

20 Then Jesus began to rebuke the cities where most of his powerful deeds had been performed, because they had not turned from their ways in repentance.

21 How terrible it will be for you, Chorazin! How terrible it will be for you, Bethsaida! For if the powerful miracles performed among you had been done in Tyre and Sidon, they would have repented long ago, showing their sorrow by wearing sackcloth and sitting in ashes.

22 But I tell you this: On the day of judgment, it will be more bearable for Tyre and Sidon than for you.

23 And you, Capernaum, who have been lifted up to the heights, will be brought down to the depths. For if the powerful deeds performed in you had been done in Sodom, it would have remained standing to this day.

24 But I tell you this: On the day of judgment, it will be more bearable for the land of Sodom than for you.

25 At that time, Jesus responded and said, "I thank you, Father, Lord of heaven and earth, because you have hidden these things from the wise and learned, and have revealed them to the humble and childlike."

26 Yes, Father, for this was pleasing and fitting in your sight.

27 Everything has been entrusted to me by my Father. No one truly knows the Son except the Father, and no one truly knows the Father except the Son and those to whom the Son chooses to reveal him.

28 Come to me, all of you who are weary and burdened, and I will provide you with rest.

29 Take my guidance upon you, and learn from me; for I am gentle and humble in heart, and you will find rest for your souls.

30 For the yoke I offer is gentle, and the load I give is light.
