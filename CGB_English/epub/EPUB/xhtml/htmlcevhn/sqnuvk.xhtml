Job Chapter 10

1 I am utterly exhausted with my life; I will freely express my grievances. I will speak out of the deep anguish of my soul.

2 I will say to God, "Do not condemn me; show me why you are contending with me."

3 Is it right for you to oppress, to reject the work of your own hands, while favoring the schemes of the wicked?

4 Do you see with human eyes? Or do you perceive things as a mortal perceives them?

5 Are your days like those of a human? Are your years like the span of a mortal's life?

6 Are you now investigating my wrongdoing and examining my sin?

7 You know that I am not guilty of wrongdoing, and there is no one who can rescue me from your power.

8 Your hands have shaped me and formed me completely, yet now you would destroy me.

9 Please consider that you have formed me like clay; will you now return me to dust?

10 Have you not poured me out like milk and caused me to congeal like cheese?

11 You have clothed me with skin and flesh, and you have knit me together with bones and sinews.

12 You have given me life and shown me kindness, and your watchful care has sustained my spirit.

13 You have kept these things hidden in your heart; I am aware that this is within you.

14 If I sin, you take notice of it, and you will not clear me of my guilt.

15 If I am guilty, then disaster will surely come upon me; but even if I am innocent, I cannot hold my head high. I am overwhelmed with despair and turmoil; therefore, look upon my suffering and see my distress.

16 For it grows worse. You pursue me like a fierce lion, and once more you display your power against me in astonishing ways.

17 You bring forth new accusations against me and intensify your anger toward me; you oppose me with constant changes and conflict.

18 Why then did you bring me out of the womb? If only I had died at birth, and no eye had ever seen me!

19 I would have been as if I had never existed; I would have been taken from the womb straight to the grave.

20 Are my days not already few? Stop now, and leave me alone, so that I may find a little comfort.

21 Before I depart to the place from which I will not return, to the land of deep darkness and the shadow of death;

22 A land of deep darkness, like the blackest night; a place of death's shadow, where there is no order, and where even the light is as dark as the grave.
