Numbers Chapter 10

1 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2 "Make for yourself two trumpets of silver; you shall craft them from a single piece of metal. These shall be used to summon the community and to signal the movement of the camps."

3 When they sound the trumpets, the entire community is to gather before you at the entrance to the tent of meeting.

4 If they sound only one trumpet, then the leaders, who are the heads of the thousands of Israel, shall assemble before you.

5 When you sound the alarm, the camps positioned to the east shall advance.

6 When you sound the alarm a second time, the camps positioned on the south side shall set out on their journey; they are to sound the alarm to signal their departure.

7 When the assembly is to be gathered, you must blow the trumpets, but you are not to sound a call to war.

8 The sons of Aaron, the priests, shall sound the trumpets, and this shall be a lasting statute for you throughout all your generations.

9 If you go to war in your land against an enemy who oppresses you, you must sound the alarm with the trumpets. Then you will be remembered before the LORD your God, and you will be delivered from your enemies.

10 Likewise, on your days of joy, during your appointed festivals, and at the start of each new month, you are to sound the trumpets over your burnt offerings and over the sacrifices of your fellowship offerings. These acts will serve as a reminder before your God. I am the LORD your God.

11 On the twentieth day of the second month, in the second year, the cloud lifted from above the tabernacle of the covenant.

12 The Israelites set out from the wilderness of Sinai and continued their journey. The cloud of God's presence, which guided them, came to rest in the wilderness of Paran.

13 They began their journey as the LORD had commanded, following the instructions given through Moses.

14 At the forefront, the banner of the camp of the tribe of Judah led the way, organized by their military divisions. Nahshon, the son of Amminadab, was the commander over his army.

15 And Nethaneel, the son of Zuar, was appointed as the leader over the army of the tribe of Issachar.

16 And Eliab, the son of Helon, was the leader over the army of the tribe of Zebulun.

17 The tabernacle was dismantled, and the descendants of Gershon and Merari moved forward, carrying the tabernacle.

18 The camp of Reuben moved forward under their banner, arranged according to their divisions. Elizur, the son of Shedeur, was the commander of their army.

19 Shelumiel, the son of Zurishaddai, was the leader in charge of the division of the tribe of Simeon.

20 And in command of the division of the tribe of Gad was Eliasaph, the son of Deuel.

21 The Kohathites moved forward, carrying the sacred objects of the sanctuary, while the others set up the tabernacle in preparation for their arrival.

22 The camp of the tribe of Ephraim set out next, arranged according to their military divisions. Elishama, the son of Ammihud, was the commander of their forces.

23 And in charge of the army division of the tribe of Manasseh was Gamaliel, the son of Pedahzur.

24 And Abidan, the son of Gideoni, was the leader in charge of the army of the tribe of Benjamin.

25 The division of the camp of the Danites set out, positioned as the rear guard for all the camps as they moved forward with their armies. Ahiezer, the son of Ammishaddai, was in command of his army.

26 And Pagiel, the son of Ocran, was appointed as the leader over the forces of the tribe of Asher.

27 Ahira, the son of Enan, was the leader in charge of the forces of the tribe of Naphtali.

28 This is how the Israelites traveled, organized by their divisions, whenever they set out on their journey.

29 Moses said to Hobab, the son of Reuel the Midianite, who was Moses' father-in-law, "We are traveling to the place the LORD has promised to give us. Come with us, and we will treat you well, for the LORD has promised blessings for Israel."

30 And he said to him, "I will not go; instead, I will return to my own land and to my family."

31 And he said, "Please do not leave us, for you know how we are to set up camp in the wilderness, and you can serve as our guide."

32 If you choose to accompany us, then whatever favor and blessing the LORD grants to us, we will extend the same to you.

33 They set out from the mountain of the LORD and traveled for three days. During those three days, the ark of the LORD's covenant went ahead of them to find a place for them to rest.

34 And the cloud of the LORD was present over them during the day as they journeyed out from the camp.

35 When the ark began to move forward, Moses declared, "Arise, O LORD, and may Your enemies be scattered; may those who oppose You flee in Your presence."

36 When the cloud came to rest, Moses would say, "Return, O LORD, to the countless multitudes of Israel."
