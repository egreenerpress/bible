Job Chapter 27

1 Job continued his discourse and said,

2 As surely as God lives, who has denied me justice, and the Almighty, who has deeply troubled my soul.

3 "As long as I have breath within me, and the Spirit of God remains in my nostrils;"

4 My lips will not speak evil, nor will my tongue craft lies.

5 Far be it from me to declare you innocent! Until the day I die, I will never abandon my integrity.

6 I firmly hold onto my integrity and will not let it slip away; my heart will not accuse me as long as I live.

7 May my enemy be treated like the wicked, and may the one who opposes me be regarded as the unrighteous.

8 What hope does the hypocrite have, even if he gains wealth, when God takes away his life?

9 Will God listen to his plea when distress overtakes him?

10 Will he find joy in the Almighty? Will he continually seek God in prayer?

11 I will instruct you with the guidance of God; I will not hide what comes from the Almighty.

12 Look, you yourselves have witnessed this; why then do you persist in such empty and futile thinking?

13 This is the fate appointed for the wicked by God, and the inheritance that oppressors will receive from the Almighty.

14 If his children increase in number, they will fall by the sword, and his descendants will not have enough to eat.

15 Those who survive him will perish in death, and his widows will not mourn.

16 Even if he amasses silver like dust and gathers clothing as plentiful as clay,

17 The wicked may prepare their schemes, but the righteous will wear them as a garment, and the innocent will divide the spoils.

18 He constructs his house like a moth's fragile dwelling, or like a temporary shelter that a watchman hastily sets up.

19 The wealthy man will lie down to rest, but he will not be gathered to his ancestors; he opens his eyes, and he is no more.

20 Sudden terrors overwhelm him like a flood; a storm sweeps him away in the darkness of the night.

21 The east wind sweeps him away, and he is gone; like a tempest, it hurls him from his place.

23 People will clap their hands in scorn at him and mockingly drive him from his place.
