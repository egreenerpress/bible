Amos Chapter 3

1 Listen to this message that the LORD has spoken against you, O people of Israel, against the entire family I brought out of the land of Egypt. He declares:

2 Out of all the families of the earth, I have chosen you alone to be in a special relationship with me. Because of this, I will hold you accountable and bring judgment upon you for all your sins.

3 Can two people walk together unless they are in agreement?

4 Does a lion roar in the forest when it has no prey? Does a young lion growl from its den if it has caught nothing?

5 Can a bird be caught in a trap on the ground if no snare has been set for it? Can a trap spring up from the earth and capture nothing at all?

6 If a trumpet is sounded in the city, will the people not tremble? If disaster strikes a city, has the Lord not caused it?

7 Certainly, the Lord GOD does not act without first revealing His plans to His servants, the prophets.

8 The lion has roared—who will not be afraid? The Lord GOD has spoken—who can refrain from proclaiming His message?

9 Proclaim this message in the palaces of Ashdod and in the palaces throughout the land of Egypt, saying: Gather together on the mountains of Samaria and witness the great turmoil within it, as well as the oppression taking place in its midst.

10 For they do not understand how to act justly, declares the LORD, as they hoard violence and plunder within their palaces.

11 Therefore, this is what the Lord GOD says: An enemy will surround the land, and he will strip away your strength from you. Your fortified palaces will be plundered.

12 This is what the LORD says: Just as a shepherd rescues from the lion’s mouth two legs or a piece of an ear, so will the people of Israel be rescued—those who live in Samaria, lounging on the edge of their beds, and those in Damascus, reclining on their couches.

13 Listen closely, and bear witness in the house of Jacob, declares the Lord GOD, the God of all armies.

14 On the day when I punish Israel for its sins, I will also bring judgment upon the altars of Bethel. The horns of the altar will be cut off and will fall to the ground.

15 The LORD declares: "I will strike down both the winter house and the summer house; the houses adorned with ivory will be destroyed, and the grand mansions will come to an end."
