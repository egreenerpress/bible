Job Chapter 38

1 Then the LORD responded to Job from the whirlwind and said,

2 Who is this that obscures understanding by speaking without true insight?

3 Prepare yourself and stand firm like a man, for I will question you, and you must answer me.

4 Where were you when I established the foundations of the earth? Tell me, if you possess understanding.

5 Who has determined its dimensions, if you know? Or who has marked out its boundaries?

6 Upon what are its foundations set? And who laid its cornerstone?

7 When the morning stars sang in unison, and all the heavenly beings cried out with joy?

8 Who enclosed the sea behind barriers when it burst forth, as though it were emerging from the womb?

9 When I made the clouds its covering and thick darkness its swaddling cloth,

10 And I established its fixed boundaries, setting bars and gates in place.

11 And He declared, "You may come this far, but no farther; here is where your mighty waves will be halted."

12 Have you ever given orders to the morning or shown the dawn its place since the beginning of your days?

13 So that it may grasp the farthest reaches of the earth, causing the wicked to be shaken loose from it?

14 The text is transformed like clay under a seal, taking on its impression, and they remain fixed like a garment, firmly in place.

15 And from the wicked, their light is withheld, and their strength, which they raise high, will be shattered.

16 Have you ventured into the sources of the sea, or have you explored the depths of the ocean?

17 Have the gates of death been revealed to you? Or have you glimpsed the entrance to the realm of deep darkness?

18 Have you comprehended the vast expanse of the earth? If you understand it fully, declare it.

19 Where is the path where light resides? And as for darkness, where is its dwelling place?

20 "Can you guide it to its boundary, or do you understand the way to its dwelling place?"

21 Do you know this because you were present at creation? Or is it because you have lived so many years that you claim such understanding?

22 Have you entered the storehouses of the snow, or have you seen the storehouses of the hail?

23 This is what I have stored up for the time of distress, for the day of conflict and warfare.

24 By what means is the light divided, and how is the east wind dispersed across the earth?

25 Who has created channels for the torrents of water, or made a path for the thunderbolt's flash?

26 To send rain upon the earth where no one lives, upon the wilderness where no human dwells;

27 To provide for the barren and desolate land, and to make the delicate shoots of new plants sprout and grow?

28 Does the rain have a father? Or who has brought forth the drops of dew?

29 From whose womb does the ice emerge? And who gives birth to the frost of heaven, that white covering of the skies?

30 The waters are concealed as if by a solid barrier, and the surface of the deep is locked in ice.

31 Can you restrain the harmonious movements of the Pleiades, or release the binding cords of Orion?

32 Can you make the constellations appear at their appointed times? Or can you direct the movement of Arcturus and its accompanying stars?

33 Do you understand the laws that govern the heavens? Can you establish their authority over the earth?

34 Can you raise your voice to the clouds so that an overwhelming flood of water might cover you?

35 Can you command the lightning bolts to go forth and then have them report back to you, saying, "Here we are"?

36 Who has placed wisdom within the depths of a person? Or who has granted understanding to the human heart?

37 Who can count the clouds with true understanding? Or who can restrain the waterskins of the heavens?

38 When the dust hardens into solid ground, and the clumps of earth cling tightly together?

39 Will you hunt prey for the lion or satisfy the hunger of the young lions?

40 When they lie down in their dens and stay hidden in their shelters to ambush their prey?

41 Who provides food for the raven? When its young cry out to God, they wander about, desperate for nourishment.
