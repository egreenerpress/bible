Proverbs Chapter 7

1 My child, hold fast to my teachings and treasure my instructions within you.

2 Obey my commandments, and you will live; guard my teachings as carefully as you would protect the pupil of your eye.

3 Engrave these teachings deeply within your being; let them guide your actions as if they were tied to your fingers, and inscribe them permanently upon the core of your heart.

4 Tell wisdom, "You are like a sister to me," and regard understanding as your close relative.

5 So that they may guard you from the immoral woman, from the outsider who seduces with her smooth words.

6 From the window of my house, I gazed out through the lattice.

7 As I observed among the inexperienced, I noticed among the young men a youth who lacked understanding.

8 As he walked along the street near her corner, he turned and took the path leading to her house.

9 In the fading light of dusk, in the evening hours, in the depth of the dark and shadowy night:

10 Suddenly, a woman approached him, dressed like a prostitute and with a cunning heart.

11 She is loud and defiant; her feet do not stay at home.

12 Now she is outside, now in the streets, and she lies in wait at every corner.

13 So she grabbed him, kissed him, and with a bold and shameless expression said to him,

14 I have brought peace offerings with me; today I have fulfilled the vows I made.

15 Therefore, I have come out to meet you, earnestly seeking your presence, and now I have found you.

16 I have adorned my bed with richly embroidered coverings, with intricately carved designs, and with the finest linen from Egypt.

17 I have scented my bed with myrrh, aloes, and cinnamon.

18 Come, let us indulge in love until the morning; let us delight ourselves in affection.

19 For the master of the house is not at home; he has gone on a distant journey.

20 He has taken a bag of money with him and will return home on the appointed day.

21 With her persuasive words, she led him to give in; through the smooth talk of her lips, she compelled him.

22 He follows her immediately, like an ox led to the slaughter, or like a fool rushing to the punishment of the stocks.

23 Until an arrow pierces his liver; like a bird rushing into a trap, unaware that it will cost him his life.

24 Listen to me now, therefore, O children, and pay close attention to the words I speak.

25 Do not let your heart be drawn toward her ways, and do not wander into her paths.

26 For she has caused many to fall, grievously wounded; indeed, numerous mighty men have been destroyed by her.

27 Her home is a path leading to the grave, descending to the depths of death.
