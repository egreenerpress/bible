Psalms Chapter 90

1 (A Prayer of Moses, the man of God.) Lord, you have been our eternal refuge throughout every generation.

2 Before the mountains were created, or before you shaped the earth and the world, from eternity past to eternity future, you are God.

3 You cause humanity to return to dust, and you declare, "Come back, O mortals."

4 In your sight, a thousand years are like a single day that has already passed, or like a brief watch during the night.

5 You sweep them away like a flood; they are like a dream—they vanish quickly. In the morning, they are like grass that sprouts and grows.

6 In the morning it springs up and thrives, but by evening it is cut down and withers away.

7 For we are utterly overwhelmed by your anger, and deeply shaken by your fierce wrath.

8 You have placed our wrongdoings before you, and our hidden sins are exposed in the brightness of your presence.

9 All our days pass away under your anger; our years come to an end like a fleeting whisper.

10 The length of our lives is seventy years—or eighty, if we are strong—yet even their span is filled with toil and trouble; for life passes quickly, and then we are gone.

11 Who can fully comprehend the intensity of your anger? Your wrath is as great as the awe and reverence you inspire.

12 Instruct us, then, to recognize the brevity of our lives, so that we may devote our hearts to gaining wisdom.

13 Turn back, O LORD—how long must this continue? Have compassion and show mercy to your servants.

14 Grant us the fullness of your steadfast love in the morning, so that we may sing for joy and be glad throughout all our days.

15 Grant us joy in proportion to the days you have caused us to suffer, and the years in which we have endured hardship.

16 May your deeds be revealed to your servants, and your splendor be shown to their children.

17 May the splendor of the LORD our God rest upon us, and may He establish the work of our hands for us; yes, may He firmly establish the work of our hands.
