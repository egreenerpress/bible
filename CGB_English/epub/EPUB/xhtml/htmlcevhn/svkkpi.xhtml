Exodus Chapter 9

1 Then the LORD said to Moses, "Go to Pharaoh and say to him, 'This is what the LORD, the God of the Hebrews, declares: Release my people, so that they may worship me.'"

2 If you refuse to let them go and continue to hold them captive,

3 Look, the hand of the LORD will strike your livestock in the fields—your horses, donkeys, camels, cattle, and sheep—with a severe and deadly plague.

4 The LORD will make a distinction between the livestock of Israel and the livestock of Egypt, so that none of the animals belonging to the Israelites will die.

5 The LORD set a specific time, declaring, "Tomorrow the LORD will accomplish this thing in the land."

6 The LORD carried out this act the next day, and all the livestock of Egypt died, but not a single animal belonging to the Israelites perished.

7 Pharaoh sent messengers to investigate, and indeed, not a single animal belonging to the Israelites had died. Yet Pharaoh’s heart remained stubborn, and he refused to let the people go.

8 The LORD said to Moses and Aaron, "Take handfuls of soot from the furnace, and have Moses scatter it toward the sky in Pharaoh's presence."

9 The dust will spread throughout the entire land of Egypt, and it will cause painful boils to break out, forming sores on both people and animals across all of Egypt.

10 Then they took soot from the kiln and stood before Pharaoh. Moses tossed it into the air, and it caused festering boils to break out on both people and animals.

11 The magicians could no longer stand before Moses because of the boils, for the boils afflicted both the magicians and all the Egyptians.

12 The LORD caused Pharaoh's heart to become stubborn, and he refused to listen to them, just as the LORD had told Moses would happen.

13 The LORD said to Moses, "Get up early in the morning, present yourself before Pharaoh, and say to him, 'This is what the LORD, the God of the Hebrews, declares: Let my people go, so that they may worship me.'"

14 For at this time, I will bring all my plagues upon you, your officials, and your people, so that you may understand that there is no one like me in all the earth.

15 Now I will extend my hand to strike you and your people with a deadly plague, and you will be removed from the earth.

16 Indeed, for this very purpose I have raised you up, to demonstrate my power through you, and so that my name may be proclaimed throughout the entire earth.

17 Are you still arrogantly opposing my people, refusing to let them go?

18 Listen carefully: Tomorrow at this same time, I will send a devastating hailstorm, the likes of which Egypt has never seen from the day it was founded until now.

19 Therefore, act now and bring in your livestock and everything you have in the field to safety. For every person and animal that remains in the field and is not brought back to shelter will be struck by the hail and will die.

20 Those among Pharaoh’s officials who respected the word of the LORD hurried to bring their servants and livestock inside their homes for safety.

21 And whoever paid no attention to the warning of the LORD left his servants and his livestock out in the open field.

22 The LORD said to Moses, "Reach out your hand toward the sky, so that hail may fall across the entire land of Egypt—on people, on animals, and on every plant in the fields throughout the land of Egypt."

23 Moses raised his staff toward the sky, and the LORD sent thunder and hail, with fire flashing down to the earth. The LORD caused hail to fall upon the land of Egypt.

24 Then hail fell, accompanied by blazing fire, so severe that nothing like it had ever occurred in the entire land of Egypt since it first became a nation.

25 The hail struck throughout the entire land of Egypt, destroying everything in the open fields—both people and animals. It battered every plant in the fields and shattered every tree.

26 Only in the region of Goshen, where the Israelites lived, was there no hail.

27 Pharaoh sent for Moses and Aaron and said to them, "This time I have sinned. The LORD is just, but I and my people are guilty."

28 Plead with the LORD, for this is more than enough—that there be no more violent thunderings and hail. I will let you go, and you will not have to stay any longer.

29 Moses said to him, "As soon as I leave the city, I will lift up my hands in prayer to the LORD. The thunder will stop, and there will be no more hail, so that you may know the earth belongs to the LORD."

30 But concerning you and your servants, I am aware that you still do not truly revere the LORD God.

31 The flax and the barley were destroyed, for the barley was in the ear and the flax was in bloom.

32 However, the wheat and the rye were not destroyed, because they had not yet reached maturity.

33 Moses left the city and went out from Pharaoh’s presence. He lifted his hands in prayer to the LORD, and the thunder, hail, and rain stopped; no more rain fell upon the earth.

34 When Pharaoh saw that the rain, hail, and thunder had stopped, he continued to sin and stubbornly hardened his heart, as did his officials.

35 Pharaoh’s heart remained stubborn, and he refused to let the Israelites go, just as the LORD had said through Moses.
