I Samuel Chapter 31

1 The Philistines engaged in battle against Israel, and the Israelite forces retreated before the Philistines, suffering heavy casualties as they fell slain on Mount Gilboa.

2 The Philistines pursued Saul and his sons relentlessly, and they killed Jonathan, Abinadab, and Malchi-shua, the sons of Saul.

3 The battle turned fiercely against Saul, and the archers struck him; he was severely wounded by their arrows.

4 Then Saul said to his armor-bearer, "Draw your sword and run me through with it, so that these uncircumcised men do not come and kill me, and then mock me." But his armor-bearer refused, for he was deeply afraid. So Saul took his own sword and fell upon it.

5 When Saul’s armor-bearer saw that Saul was dead, he also threw himself on his sword and died with him.

6 So Saul died, along with his three sons, his armor-bearer, and all his men—all of them perished on that same day.

7 When the Israelites who were on the opposite side of the valley and those beyond the Jordan saw that the Israelite army had fled, and that Saul and his sons had died, they abandoned their cities and fled as well. Then the Philistines came and occupied those cities.

8 The next day, when the Philistines came to plunder the dead, they discovered Saul and his three sons lying dead on Mount Gilboa.

9 They severed his head, removed his armor, and sent messengers throughout the land of the Philistines to announce the news in the temples of their idols and among their people.

10 They placed his armor in the temple of Ashtaroth, and they fastened his body to the wall of Beth-shan.

11 And when the people of Jabesh-gilead heard what the Philistines had done to Saul,

12 All the courageous men rose up and journeyed through the night. They retrieved the body of Saul and the bodies of his sons from the wall of Beth-shan, brought them to Jabesh, and cremated them there.

13 They gathered their bones and buried them beneath a tree in Jabesh, then observed a fast for seven days.
