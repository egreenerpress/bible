Song of Songs Chapter 7

1 How lovely are your feet in sandals, O noble daughter! The curves of your thighs are like finely crafted ornaments, the exquisite work of a skilled artisan.

2 Your navel is like a rounded bowl, never lacking its drink; your belly is like a mound of wheat, encircled with lilies.

3 Your two breasts are like two young gazelles, twins that graze among the lilies.

4 Your neck is like a tower of ivory; your eyes are like the pools of Heshbon, by the gate of Bath-rabbim. Your nose is like the tower of Lebanon, facing toward Damascus.

5 Your head crowns you like the majestic Mount Carmel, and your flowing hair is as rich and royal as purple cloth; the king is captivated by your beauty, enthralled as he gazes upon you.

6 How beautiful and delightful you are, my love, a source of great joy!

7 Your figure is like a palm tree, and your breasts are like clusters of fruit.

8 I said, "I will climb the palm tree and take hold of its branches. May your breasts be like clusters of grapes on the vine, and the fragrance of your breath like the scent of apples."

9 And the roof of your mouth is like the finest wine, flowing smoothly for my beloved, gliding gently, stirring the lips of those who sleep to speak.

10 I belong to my beloved, and his longing is for me.

11 Come, my beloved, let us go out into the countryside; let us spend the night in the villages.

12 Let us rise early and go to the vineyards; let us see if the vines are blossoming, if the young grapes are appearing, and if the pomegranates are in bloom. There I will give you my love.

13 The mandrakes release their fragrance, and at our gates are all kinds of delightful fruits, both fresh and stored, which I have set aside for you, my beloved.
