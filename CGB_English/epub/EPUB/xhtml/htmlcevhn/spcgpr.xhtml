Genesis Chapter 22

1 After these events, God tested Abraham and called to him, saying, "Abraham!" And Abraham replied, "Here I am."

2 Then God said, "Take your son, your only son Isaac, whom you love, and go to the land of Moriah. There, offer him as a burnt offering on one of the mountains that I will show you."

3 Abraham got up early in the morning, prepared his donkey, and took two of his servants with him, along with his son Isaac. He split the wood for the burnt offering, then set out and went to the place God had directed him.

4 On the third day, Abraham looked up and saw the place in the distance.

5 Abraham said to his servants, "Stay here with the donkey. The boy and I will go over there to worship, and then we will return to you."

6 Abraham gathered the wood for the burnt offering and placed it on his son Isaac. He then took the fire and the knife in his hand, and the two of them walked on together.

7 Isaac spoke to his father Abraham and said, "Father?" Abraham replied, "Yes, my son, I am here." Isaac then said, "Look, here is the fire and the wood, but where is the lamb for the burnt offering?"

8 Abraham said, "My son, God Himself will provide the lamb for the burnt offering." So the two of them continued on together.

9 They arrived at the place God had designated to him. There, Abraham built an altar, arranged the wood, and bound his son Isaac. He then placed Isaac on the altar, on top of the wood.

10 Abraham reached out his hand and took the knife, preparing to sacrifice his son.

11 Then the angel of the LORD called to him from heaven, saying, "Abraham, Abraham!" And he replied, "Here I am."

12 The angel said, "Do not harm the boy or do anything to him. Now I know that you truly revere God, because you have not withheld your son, your only son, from me."

13 Abraham raised his eyes and looked, and there behind him was a ram, its horns tangled in a thicket. Abraham went over, took the ram, and offered it as a burnt offering in place of his son.

14 Abraham named that place "The LORD Will Provide," as it is still said to this day, "On the mountain of the LORD, it will be provided."

15 Then the angel of the LORD called to Abraham from heaven a second time, saying,

16 And He said, "I swear by Myself, declares the LORD, because you have done this and have not withheld your son, your only son..."

17 I will surely bless you and greatly increase your descendants, making them as numerous as the stars in the sky and as the sand on the seashore. Your descendants will take possession of the gates of their enemies.

18 And through your descendants, all the nations of the earth will be blessed, because you have obeyed my command.

19 So Abraham went back to his servants, and they got up and traveled together to Beersheba. There, Abraham settled and made his home.

20 After these events, Abraham was informed, saying, "Listen, Milcah has also borne children to your brother Nahor."

21 Huz, his firstborn son, and Buz, his brother, along with Kemuel, the father of Aram.

22 And Chesed, Hazo, Pildash, Jidlaph, and Bethuel.
