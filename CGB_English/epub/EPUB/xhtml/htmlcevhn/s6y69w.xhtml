Genesis Chapter 17

1 When Abram was ninety-nine years old, the LORD appeared to him and said, "I am God Almighty. Live in my presence and be blameless."

2 I will establish my covenant between me and you, and I will greatly increase your numbers.

3 Abram bowed down with his face to the ground, and God spoke to him, saying,

4 As for me, my covenant is with you, and you will become the father of many nations.

5 Your name will no longer be Abram, but your name will be Abraham, for I have made you the father of many nations.

6 I will cause you to be abundantly fruitful, and I will make nations arise from you, and kings will descend from your lineage.

7 I will establish my covenant between me and you, and your descendants after you throughout their generations, as an everlasting covenant. I will be your God and the God of your descendants after you.

8 I will grant to you and to your descendants after you the land where you now live as a foreigner—the entire land of Canaan—as an eternal possession, and I will be their God.

9 And God said to Abraham, "You must keep my covenant—you and your descendants after you, throughout all generations."

10 This is my covenant, which you are to uphold, between me and you and your descendants after you: Every male among you must be circumcised.

11 You must circumcise the flesh of your foreskin, and this will serve as a sign of the covenant between me and you.

12 Every male child among you must be circumcised when he is eight days old, throughout your generations. This applies to those born within your household as well as those purchased with money from any foreigner who is not of your own lineage.

13 Anyone born in your household or purchased with your money must be circumcised. My covenant will be marked in your flesh as an everlasting covenant.

14 And any male who is not circumcised, whose foreskin remains intact, that person shall be cut off from his community; he has violated my covenant.

15 And God said to Abraham, "Regarding Sarai, your wife, you shall no longer call her by the name Sarai, but her name shall be Sarah."

16 I will bless her, and I will also give you a son through her. Yes, I will bless her, and she will become the mother of nations; kings of peoples will come from her.

17 Then Abraham bowed low to the ground, laughed to himself, and thought, "Can a child truly be born to a man who is a hundred years old? And can Sarah, who is ninety years old, really give birth?"

18 And Abraham said to God, "If only Ishmael could live in your presence!"

19 God said, "Your wife Sarah will indeed bear you a son, and you are to name him Isaac. I will establish my covenant with him as an everlasting covenant, and with his descendants after him."

20 Regarding Ishmael, I have heard your plea. Listen, I have blessed him and will cause him to prosper. I will greatly increase his descendants, and he will father twelve rulers. Moreover, I will make him the founder of a great nation.

21 However, I will establish my covenant with Isaac, whom Sarah will bear for you at this appointed time next year.

22 After finishing His conversation with him, God departed from Abraham's presence.

23 Abraham took his son Ishmael, all the males born in his household, and all the male servants he had purchased—every male in Abraham's household—and on that very same day, as God had commanded him, he circumcised the flesh of their foreskins.

24 Abraham was ninety-nine years old when he underwent circumcision, the removal of the flesh of his foreskin.

25 Ishmael, his son, was thirteen years old when he was circumcised, removing the flesh of his foreskin.

26 On that very same day, Abraham was circumcised, along with his son Ishmael.

27 All the men of his household, whether born in his house or purchased from a foreigner, were circumcised along with him.
