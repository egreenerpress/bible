Matthew Chapter 18

1 At that moment, the disciples approached Jesus and asked, "Who is the most important in the kingdom of heaven?"

2 Jesus called a young child to come to Him and had the child stand among them.

3 And He said, "Truly I tell you, unless you turn away from your current ways and become like little children, you will never enter the kingdom of heaven."

4 Therefore, whoever humbles themselves like this little child is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven.

5 And whoever welcomes one of these little children in my name welcomes me.

6 But if anyone causes one of these little ones who believe in me to stumble, it would be better for them to have a large millstone tied around their neck and be drowned in the depths of the sea.

7 How terrible it will be for the world because of the things that cause people to stumble! Such things are inevitable, but how terrible it will be for the person through whom they come!

8 Therefore, if your hand or your foot causes you to sin, cut it off and throw it away. It is better for you to enter life crippled or maimed than to have two hands or two feet and be thrown into the eternal fire.

9 If your eye causes you to sin, tear it out and throw it away. It is far better for you to enter into life with only one eye than to have both eyes and be thrown into the fire of hell.

10 Be careful not to look down on any of these little ones, for I tell you that in heaven their angels continually see the face of my Father who is in heaven.

11 For the Son of Man has come to rescue what was lost.

12 What do you think? If someone has a hundred sheep and one of them wanders away, won’t he leave the ninety-nine on the hillside and go search for the one that has gone astray?

13 And if he happens to find it, I tell you the truth, he rejoices more over that one sheep than over the ninety-nine that did not wander away.

14 In the same way, it is not the desire of your Father in heaven that even one of these little ones should be lost.

15 Furthermore, if your brother wrongs you, go and point out his fault privately, just between the two of you. If he listens to you, you have won back your brother.

16 But if he refuses to listen to you, then bring along one or two others, so that every matter may be confirmed by the testimony of two or three witnesses.

17 If he refuses to listen to them, bring the matter before the congregation. But if he still refuses to listen even to the congregation, then treat him as you would a Gentile or a tax collector.

18 Truly, I tell you, whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you release on earth will be released in heaven.

19 I tell you again: If two of you on earth agree together about anything you ask for, it will be done for you by my Father in heaven.

20 For wherever two or three come together in my name, I am there among them.

21 Peter approached Jesus and asked, "Lord, how many times should I forgive my brother when he sins against me? Up to seven times?"

22 Jesus said to him, "I do not say to you, up to seven times, but up to seventy times seven."

23 Therefore, the kingdom of heaven can be compared to a certain king who decided to settle accounts with his servants.

24 When he began to settle the accounts, a man was brought before him who owed ten thousand talents.

25 However, since he did not have the means to repay the debt, his master ordered that he, along with his wife, children, and all his possessions, be sold to settle the payment.

26 The servant then fell to the ground and bowed before him, pleading, "Master, please be patient with me, and I will repay everything I owe."

27 Then the master of that servant was filled with compassion, released him, and canceled his debt.

28 However, that same servant went out and found one of his fellow servants who owed him a hundred denarii. He grabbed him, seized him by the throat, and demanded, "Pay back what you owe me!"

29 Then his fellow servant fell down at his feet and pleaded with him, saying, "Be patient with me, and I will repay you everything."

30 But he refused and instead had the man thrown into prison until he could repay the debt.

31 When his fellow servants saw what had happened, they were deeply troubled. They went and reported everything to their master.

32 Then his master, after summoning him, said to him, "You wicked servant! I forgave you the entire debt because you pleaded with me.

33 Shouldn’t you also have shown mercy to your fellow servant, just as I showed mercy to you?

34 And his master was furious, and handed him over to the jailers until he could repay everything he owed.

35 In the same way, my heavenly Father will also deal with you if you do not sincerely forgive each one of your brothers and sisters for their wrongdoings.
