James Chapter 5

1 Listen now, you who are wealthy, weep and cry out in anguish over the calamities that are about to overtake you.

2 Your wealth has decayed, and your clothes have been eaten by moths.

3 Your gold and silver have corroded, and their rust will testify against you, consuming your flesh like fire. You have stored up wealth for the final days.

4 Listen! The wages you have withheld from the workers who harvested your fields are crying out against you. The cries of those who reaped have reached the ears of the Lord of Hosts.

5 You have indulged in luxury and self-gratification on earth, living in reckless excess. You have fattened your hearts as if preparing for a day of feasting and celebration.

6 You have condemned and put to death the innocent one, and he does not oppose you.

7 Therefore, brothers and sisters, be patient as you wait for the coming of the Lord. Consider the farmer: he waits with great anticipation for the valuable harvest from the earth, enduring with patience until the land receives the early and late rains.

8 You must also remain patient; strengthen your hearts, for the coming of the Lord is near.

9 Do not harbor resentment or hold grudges against one another, my brothers and sisters, or you will face judgment. Take note: the Judge is already standing at the door.

10 My brothers and sisters, consider the prophets who spoke in the name of the Lord as examples of enduring hardship and demonstrating patience.

11 Consider this: we regard those who endure as blessed. You have heard of Job’s perseverance and have witnessed the outcome the Lord brought about—how the Lord is deeply compassionate and full of tender mercy.

12 Above all, my brothers and sisters, do not swear oaths—not by heaven, nor by the earth, nor by any other pledge. Instead, let your "yes" mean yes, and your "no" mean no, so that you do not fall under judgment.

13 If anyone among you is suffering, they should pray. If anyone is joyful, they should sing praises.

14 Is anyone among you sick? Let them call for the elders of the church, and have them pray over the person, anointing them with oil in the name of the Lord.

15 The prayer offered in faith will bring healing to the sick, and the Lord will restore them to health. Moreover, if they have committed sins, those sins will be forgiven.

16 Admit your wrongdoings to each other, and pray for one another, so that you may be restored to health. The heartfelt, earnest prayer of a person who lives rightly before God is powerful and effective.

17 Elijah was a man with the same human weaknesses as we have, and he prayed fervently that it would not rain. As a result, no rain fell on the land for three and a half years.

18 He prayed once more, and the skies poured down rain, causing the earth to produce its harvest.

19 Brothers and sisters, if any of you strays from the truth and someone helps them return to it,

20 Let it be understood that whoever turns a sinner away from the error of their ways will rescue a soul from death and cover over a great number of sins.
