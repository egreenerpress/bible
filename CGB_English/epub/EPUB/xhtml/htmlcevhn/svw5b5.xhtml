Isaiah Chapter 15

1 This is a prophecy concerning Moab. In a single night, the city of Ar in Moab has been devastated and reduced to silence. Likewise, in that same night, the city of Kir in Moab has been destroyed and left in utter stillness.

2 He has gone up to the temple and to Dibon, to the high places, to mourn. Moab will wail over Nebo and over Medeba; on all their heads there will be shaved baldness, and every beard will be cut off.

3 In their streets, they will wrap themselves in sackcloth as a sign of mourning. On the rooftops of their houses and in the public squares, everyone will wail loudly, weeping profusely.

4 Heshbon and Elealeh will cry out, and their voices will be heard as far as Jahaz. Because of this, the warriors of Moab will shout in distress, and their courage will fail them; they will be overwhelmed with anguish.

5 My heart cries out for Moab; its refugees will flee to Zoar, like a young heifer of three years old. They will ascend the slopes of Luhith with weeping, and on the road to Horonaim, they will raise a desperate cry of devastation.

6 The waters of Nimrim will become a wasteland; the hay has dried up, the grass has perished, and no green plants remain.

7 Therefore, the wealth they have acquired and the possessions they have stored up, they will carry away to the stream of the poplars.

8 The outcry has spread throughout the borders of Moab; their wailing reaches as far as Eglaim, and their mournful cries extend to Beer-elim.
