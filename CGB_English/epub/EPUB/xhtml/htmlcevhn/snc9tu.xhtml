Isaiah Chapter 20

1 In the year when Tartan, sent by Sargon, the king of Assyria, came to Ashdod, he waged war against the city and captured it.

2 At that time, the LORD spoke through Isaiah, the son of Amoz, saying, "Go and remove the sackcloth from your waist, and take off your sandals from your feet." Isaiah obeyed, walking about naked and barefoot.

3 The LORD declared, "Just as my servant Isaiah has walked naked and barefoot for three years as a sign and a warning concerning Egypt and Ethiopia..."

4 In this way, the king of Assyria will take the Egyptians as prisoners and the Ethiopians as captives, both young and old. They will be led away naked and barefoot, with their buttocks exposed, bringing disgrace and humiliation upon Egypt.

5 They will be filled with fear and shame because of their reliance on Ethiopia, in which they placed their hope, and because of Egypt, in which they took pride.
