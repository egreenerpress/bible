Leviticus Chapter 24

1 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2 Instruct the people of Israel to bring you pure, pressed olive oil for the light, so that the lamps may burn continuously.

3 Outside the curtain that shields the Ark of the Testimony, in the Tent of Meeting, Aaron and his descendants must keep the lamp burning from evening until morning continually in the presence of the LORD. This is to be a permanent statute for all generations to come.

4 He shall arrange the lamps on the pure lampstand so that they burn continually in the presence of the LORD.

5 You shall take fine flour and bake twelve loaves from it. Each loaf shall contain two-tenths of an ephah of flour.

6 You shall arrange them in two rows, six in each row, on the pure table before the presence of the LORD.

7 You shall place pure frankincense on each row of bread, so that it may serve as a memorial portion on the bread, an offering made by fire to the LORD.

8 Every Sabbath, he shall arrange it before the LORD regularly, as a perpetual offering taken from the Israelites by an eternal covenant.

9 This portion shall belong to Aaron and his sons, and they are to eat it in a sacred place, for it is most holy to them, being part of the offerings to the LORD made by fire. This is a permanent regulation.

10 A man, the son of an Israelite woman (whose father was an Egyptian), went out among the Israelites. This son of the Israelite woman and an Israelite man got into a dispute in the camp.

11 The son of an Israelite woman blasphemed the name of the Lord and uttered a curse. They brought him to Moses. (His mother’s name was Shelomith, the daughter of Dibri, from the tribe of Dan.)

12 They placed him in custody so that the will of the LORD might be revealed to them.

13 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

14 Take the one who has uttered the curse outside the camp, and let all who heard him place their hands on his head. Then the entire community is to stone him.

15 You shall speak to the Israelites and say: Anyone who blasphemes their God will be held accountable for their sin.

16 Anyone who blasphemes the name of the LORD must surely be put to death. The entire community is to stone that person—whether they are a foreigner or a native of the land. When someone blasphemes the name of the LORD, they are to be executed.

17 Anyone who kills another person must certainly be put to death.

18 If someone kills another person's animal, they must replace it—an animal for an animal.

19 If someone injures or disfigures another person, whatever harm they have caused must be repaid to them in the same manner.

20 Injury for injury, eye for eye, tooth for tooth: just as someone has inflicted harm on another, so the same harm shall be done to them in return.

21 If someone kills an animal, they must make restitution for it; but if someone kills a person, they must be put to death.

22 You shall have the same law for both the foreigner and the native-born citizen, for I am the LORD your God.

23 Moses instructed the Israelites to bring the one who had uttered the curse out of the camp and to execute him by stoning. The Israelites carried out what the LORD had commanded Moses.
