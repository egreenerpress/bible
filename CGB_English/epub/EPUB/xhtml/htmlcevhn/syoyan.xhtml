Ecclesiastes Chapter 10

1 Just as dead flies spoil the perfumer's ointment, causing it to emit a foul odor, so a small act of folly can tarnish the reputation of someone known for wisdom and honor.

2 A wise person's heart is inclined toward what is right and honorable, guiding them toward sound judgment and virtuous actions. In contrast, a fool's heart is drawn toward what is misguided and improper, leading them to folly and error.

3 Indeed, even when a foolish person walks along the road, their lack of understanding becomes evident, and they proclaim to everyone that they are a fool.

4 If a ruler becomes angry with you, do not abandon your position; for calmness can resolve serious offenses.

5 I have observed a troubling injustice under the sun, a mistake that stems from those in authority.

6 Folly is exalted to positions of great honor, while the wealthy are seated in humble places.

7 I have observed servants riding on horses, while princes walk on the ground like common servants.

8 Whoever digs a pit will fall into it, and whoever breaks through a wall will be bitten by a serpent.

9 Whoever moves stones will be injured by them, and whoever splits wood will be put at risk by it.

10 If the blade is dull and one does not sharpen it, more effort will be required to use it. But wisdom is valuable because it provides the right guidance.

11 Certainly, a snake will strike without being charmed, and a gossip is just as harmful.

12 The speech of a wise person is marked by grace and kindness, but the words of a fool bring about his own ruin.

13 The first words that come from his mouth are foolishness, and the conclusion of his speech is reckless and destructive nonsense.

14 A fool is always full of words, yet no one can predict what will happen in the future. And who can tell a person what will come after them?

15 The toil of the foolish exhausts them completely, for they do not know how to find their way to the city.

16 How tragic it is for a land when its ruler is inexperienced and its leaders indulge in feasting from the early hours of the day!

17 Blessed are you, O land, when your king is of noble birth, and your leaders eat at the proper timeâ€”for nourishment and strength, not for indulgence and excess!

18 Through constant laziness, the structure deteriorates, and due to the neglect of idle hands, the house begins to collapse.

19 A feast is prepared for joy, and wine brings cheer, but wealth addresses every need.

20 Do not speak ill of the king, not even in your thoughts, and do not curse the wealthy in the privacy of your bedroom. For a bird of the sky may carry your words, and a winged creature may reveal what you have said.
