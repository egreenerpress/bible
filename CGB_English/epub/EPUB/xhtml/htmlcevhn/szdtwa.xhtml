Genesis Chapter 4

1 Adam had intimate relations with his wife Eve, and she became pregnant and gave birth to Cain. She declared, "With the LORD's help, I have brought forth a man."

2 Then she gave birth to his brother Abel. Abel became a shepherd, tending flocks of sheep, while Cain worked the soil as a farmer.

3 As time went on, it happened that Cain brought an offering to the LORD from the produce of the ground.

4 Abel also brought an offering from the firstborn of his flock, including the choicest portions. The LORD looked favorably upon Abel and his offering.

5 However, the Lord did not regard Cain or his offering with favor. Cain became intensely angry, and his face grew downcast.

6 The LORD said to Cain, "Why are you so angry? Why has your face become downcast?"

7 If you do what is right, will you not be accepted? But if you do not do what is right, sin is crouching at your door. It desires to control you, but you must master it.

8 Cain spoke with his brother Abel, and when they were in the field, Cain attacked Abel, his brother, and killed him.

9 The LORD said to Cain, "Where is your brother Abel?" Cain replied, "I do not know. Am I supposed to be my brother’s guardian?"

10 And He said, "What have you done? The voice of your brother's blood is crying out to me from the ground."

11 Now you are cursed from the ground, which has opened its mouth to receive your brother’s blood from your hand.

12 When you work the ground, it will no longer yield its full produce for you; you will be a restless wanderer, moving from place to place on the earth.

13 And Cain said to the LORD, "My guilt and the consequences of my sin are more than I can endure."

14 Look, today you have driven me away from the land, and I will be hidden from your presence. I will become a restless wanderer on the earth, and whoever finds me will kill me.

15 The LORD said to him, "Therefore, whoever kills Cain will face sevenfold retribution." Then the LORD placed a protective mark on Cain, so that no one who encountered him would kill him.

16 Cain departed from the presence of the LORD and settled in the land of Nod, which lies to the east of Eden.

17 Cain had relations with his wife, and she became pregnant and gave birth to Enoch. Cain then built a city and named it after his son, calling it Enoch.

18 Enoch became the father of Irad; Irad became the father of Mehujael; Mehujael became the father of Methusael; and Methusael became the father of Lamech.

19 Lamech married two women: one was named Adah, and the other was named Zillah.

20 And Adah gave birth to Jabal; he became the ancestor of those who live in tents and raise livestock.

21 And his brother’s name was Jubal; he became the ancestor of all who play the lyre and the flute.

22 And Zillah also gave birth to Tubal-cain, who became a skilled craftsman, working with bronze and iron. He was a teacher of all who practiced these crafts. Tubal-cain’s sister was named Naamah.

23 Lamech said to his wives, Adah and Zillah, "Listen to my words; wives of Lamech, pay attention to what I say: I have killed a man for wounding me, a young man for injuring me."

24 If Cain is to be avenged seven times over, then surely Lamech will be avenged seventy-seven times over.

25 Adam was intimate with his wife once more, and she gave birth to a son. She named him Seth, saying, "God has granted me another child in place of Abel, since Cain killed him."

26 To Seth, a son was also born, and he named him Enosh. At that time, people began to invoke the name of the LORD.
