Jeremiah Chapter 9

1 If only my head were filled with water, and my eyes a flowing spring of tears, so that I could weep day and night for the slain of my people, the children of my nation!

2 How I wish I had a shelter in the wilderness, a place for travelers to rest, so I could leave my people and go far away from them! For they are all unfaithful, a gathering of deceitful and treacherous people.

3 They aim their tongues like a bow to shoot lies, but they lack courage to stand for the truth in the land. They move from one wicked act to another, and they do not acknowledge me, declares the LORD.

4 Be cautious of everyone around you, and do not place your trust in any brother, for every brother is likely to deceive, and every neighbor will spread slander.

5 Each person will deceive their neighbor and will not speak truthfully. They have trained their tongues to speak lies and exhaust themselves in doing wrong.

6 Your dwelling is surrounded by deception; through their deceit, they have chosen not to acknowledge me, declares the LORD.

7 Therefore, this is what the LORD of hosts declares: "Behold, I will refine them and test them, for what else can I do for the sake of my people?

8 Their words are like a sharp arrow, aimed to wound; they speak with deception. With their mouths, they speak kindly to their neighbors, but in their hearts, they plot harm.

9 "Should I not punish them for these deeds?" says the LORD. "Should I not avenge myself on a nation like this?"

10 I will weep and mourn for the mountains, and I will lament for the pastures of the wilderness, for they have been scorched and laid waste, leaving no one able to pass through. The sound of livestock is no longer heard; both the birds of the sky and the wild animals have fled and disappeared.

11 I will reduce Jerusalem to ruins, a place overrun by wild creatures; and I will make the cities of Judah desolate, leaving them without a single inhabitant.

12 Who is the wise person who can understand this? And who is the one to whom the LORD has spoken, so that they may explain it? Why has the land been ruined and laid waste like a desert, so that no one passes through it?

13 The LORD declares, "Because they have abandoned the law I gave them and have not listened to my voice or followed it in their lives..."

14 But they have followed the desires of their own hearts and have pursued the worship of Baal, as their ancestors taught them to do.

15 Therefore, this is what the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel, declares: Listen carefully, I will provide this people with bitter hardship, like feeding them wormwood, and I will give them poisoned water to drink.

16 I will disperse them among the nations, peoples whom neither they nor their ancestors have ever known. I will pursue them with the sword until I have completely destroyed them.

17 This is what the LORD of hosts says: "Think carefully about this, and summon the professional mourners to come; send for the skilled women who wail, so they may come as well."

18 Hurry and raise a lament for us, so that our eyes may overflow with tears, and our eyelids pour out streams of water.

19 A cry of mourning rises from Zion: "How utterly we are ruined! We are overwhelmed with shame, for we have abandoned the land, and our homes have driven us away."

20 Listen to the word of the LORD, you women, and let your ears take in the message from His mouth. Teach your daughters how to mourn, and each of you instruct your neighbor in lamentation.

21 For death has climbed in through our windows and entered our homes, destroying the children outside and the young men in the streets.

22 Proclaim this message: Thus says the LORD, "The dead bodies of people will fall like dung on the open fields, like cut grain left behind by the reaper, with no one to gather them."

23 This is what the LORD says: "The wise must not boast in their wisdom, nor the strong in their strength, nor the wealthy in their riches."

24 However, let the one who boasts boast in this: that they understand and know me, for I am the LORD who acts with steadfast love, justice, and righteousness in the earth. Indeed, I delight in these things, declares the LORD.

25 Listen, the time is coming, declares the LORD, when I will bring judgment on all who are circumcised in the flesh but remain uncircumcised in heart.

26 Egypt, Judah, Edom, the Ammonites, Moab, and all those who live in the farthest regions, those who dwell in the wilderness—all these nations are uncircumcised, and even the entire house of Israel is uncircumcised in their hearts.
