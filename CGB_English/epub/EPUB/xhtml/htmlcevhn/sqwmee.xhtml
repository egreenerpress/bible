Hebrews Chapter 9

1 Certainly, the first covenant also had regulations for worship and an earthly sanctuary.

2 A tabernacle was constructed, with the first section containing the lampstand, the table, and the bread of the Presence. This area is referred to as the Holy Place.

3 And beyond the second curtain was the sacred tent called the Most Holy Place.

4 This area contained the golden incense altar and the ark of the covenant, which was entirely covered with gold. Inside the ark were the golden jar holding the manna, Aaron’s staff that had budded, and the stone tablets inscribed with the terms of the covenant.

5 Above it were the glorious cherubim overshadowing the atonement cover; concerning these things, we cannot now speak in detail.

6 Now when these arrangements had been established, the priests would regularly enter the first section of the tabernacle to carry out their duties in serving God.

7 But into the second room, the high priest entered alone once a year, and not without blood, which he offered both for his own sins and for the unintentional wrongdoings of the people.

8 The Holy Spirit was indicating through this that the way into the Most Holy Place had not yet been revealed as long as the first tabernacle was still standing.

9 This was a symbolic representation for the present time, during which both gifts and sacrifices were offered, yet these could not fully cleanse the conscience of the one performing the service or make them spiritually complete.

10 These regulations were concerned only with food and drink, various ceremonial washings, and external rules for the body, which were imposed until the time of restoration and renewal.

11 But when Christ came as the high priest of the good things that were to come, he entered through a greater and more perfect tabernacle, one not made by human hands—that is, not part of this created world.

12 Not by means of the blood of goats and calves, but through his own blood, he entered once for all into the Most Holy Place, securing eternal redemption for us.

13 For if the blood of bulls and goats, and the ashes of a heifer sprinkled on those who are ceremonially unclean, sanctify them so that their bodies are purified,

14 How much more, then, will the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without blemish to God, cleanse our consciences from acts that lead to death, so that we may serve the living God?

15 For this reason, he is the mediator of the new covenant, so that through his death—bringing redemption for the sins committed under the first covenant—those who are called may receive the promised eternal inheritance.

16 For when a will is established, it is necessary that the death of the one who made the will be confirmed.

17 A will only takes effect after the death of the person who made it; it has no force or validity while the one who made it is still alive.

18 Therefore, even the first covenant was not established without the shedding of blood.

19 For when Moses had fully declared every commandment of the law to all the people, he took the blood of calves and goats, along with water, scarlet wool, and hyssop, and sprinkled both the scroll of the covenant and all the people.

20 He said, "This is the blood of the covenant that God has established with you."

21 Furthermore, he sprinkled blood on both the tabernacle and all the sacred utensils used in worship.

22 Nearly everything is purified according to the law through the shedding of blood, and without the shedding of blood, there can be no forgiveness.

23 It was necessary, therefore, for the earthly representations of the heavenly realities to be purified by these sacrifices, but the heavenly realities themselves require far greater sacrifices than these.

24 For Christ did not enter into holy places made by human hands, which are merely representations of the true ones, but into heaven itself, where he now appears in the presence of God on our behalf.

25 Nor does it mean that he should repeatedly offer himself, as the high priest enters the Most Holy Place every year with the blood of others.

26 For if that were the case, he would have had to suffer repeatedly since the creation of the world. But now, at the culmination of the ages, he has appeared once for all to remove sin by offering himself as a sacrifice.

27 Just as it is destined for all people to die once, and after that comes the judgment.

28 Christ was offered once to take upon himself the sins of many; and to those who eagerly await him, he will appear a second time, not to deal with sin, but to bring salvation.
