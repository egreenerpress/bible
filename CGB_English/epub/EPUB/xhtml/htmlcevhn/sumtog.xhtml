II Corinthians Chapter 10

1 Now I, Paul, personally appeal to you by the gentleness and kindness of Christ—I who am humble when face to face with you, but bold toward you when I am away.

2 I urge you, so that when I am present with you, I may not have to act boldly with the confidence I intend to show toward those who regard us as if we live by worldly standards.

3 For although we live in the physical world, we do not wage war according to human methods.

4 For the weapons we use in our spiritual battle are not of this world, but they are empowered by God to demolish strongholds.

5 We demolish every false argument and every lofty idea that sets itself up against the true knowledge of God, and we take captive every thought to make it obedient to Christ.

6 And we stand prepared to address and correct all acts of disobedience once your obedience has been fully demonstrated and established.

7 Do you judge things based on outward appearances? If anyone is confident in themselves that they belong to Christ, they should consider this again: just as they belong to Christ, so do we.

8 Even if I were to boast a bit more about the authority the Lord has given us—an authority meant to build you up, not to tear you down—I would have no reason to be ashamed.

9 I say this so that it does not appear as though I am trying to intimidate you with my letters.

10 People say that his letters are forceful and powerful, but his physical appearance is unimpressive, and his speaking ability is lacking.

11 Let such a person consider this: just as we are in our words through letters when we are absent, so we will also be in our actions when we are present.

12 We do not presume to classify or compare ourselves with those who promote themselves. However, when they measure themselves by their own standards and compare themselves to one another, they demonstrate a lack of wisdom.

13 However, we will not boast beyond proper limits, but within the boundaries of the responsibility God has assigned to us—a responsibility that extends even to you.

14 We do not overextend ourselves or claim authority beyond what is appropriate, as if we had not reached you. For we have indeed come to you in our mission to proclaim the gospel of Christ.

15 We do not boast about accomplishments beyond our proper scope, particularly in areas where others have done the work. Instead, we hold to the hope that as your faith grows, our influence among you will expand greatly, in accordance with the boundaries God has set for us.

16 To proclaim the good news in the areas beyond your region, and not to take pride in work already accomplished within someone else's sphere of influence.

17 However, let the one who boasts boast only in the Lord.

18 A person is not proven genuine by praising themselves, but by being commended by the Lord.
