I Samuel Chapter 12

1 Samuel addressed all Israel, saying, "Listen, I have carefully considered everything you have said to me and have acted according to your wishes by appointing a king to rule over you."

2 And now, look, the king leads you; I am old and gray-haired, and my sons are among you. I have guided you from my youth until this very day.

3 Here I stand before you now. Bring your accusations against me in the presence of the LORD and His anointed. Whose ox have I taken? Whose donkey have I seized? Whom have I cheated? Whom have I oppressed? From whose hand have I accepted a bribe to close my eyes to wrongdoing? If I have done any of these things, I will make it right and restore what was taken.

4 They replied, "You have not cheated us, nor have you mistreated us, nor have you taken anything from anyone by force."

5 And he said to them, "The LORD is a witness against you, and His anointed one is a witness today, that you have found nothing in my possession." And they replied, "He is witness."

6 Samuel said to the people, "It is the LORD who raised up Moses and Aaron, and who brought your ancestors out of the land of Egypt."

7 Now then, stand still so that I may present my case before the LORD and remind you of all the righteous deeds the LORD has done for you and your ancestors.

8 When Jacob entered Egypt, and your ancestors cried out to the LORD, the LORD sent Moses and Aaron, who led your ancestors out of Egypt and brought them to settle in this land.

9 When they forgot the LORD their God, He handed them over to Sisera, the commander of Hazor’s army, and to the Philistines, and to the king of Moab, and these enemies waged war against them.

10 They called out to the LORD and said, "We have sinned, for we have abandoned the LORD and served the Baals and the Ashtoreths. But now, rescue us from the power of our enemies, and we will serve you."

11 The LORD raised up Jerubbaal, Bedan, Jephthah, and Samuel, and through them He rescued you from the power of your enemies on every side, so that you lived in safety.

12 When you saw that Nahash, the king of the Ammonites, was advancing against you, you said to me, "No! We must have a king to rule over us," even though the LORD your God was already your king.

13 Now, look at the king you have chosen, the one you asked for! See, the LORD has appointed a king to rule over you.

14 If you will revere the LORD, serve Him, obey His voice, and not defy His commands, then both you and the king who rules over you will remain faithful to the LORD your God.

15 But if you refuse to listen to the voice of the LORD and instead rebel against His command, then the LORD’s power will turn against you, just as it did against your ancestors.

16 Now then, stand firm and witness this extraordinary act that the LORD is about to perform right before your eyes.

17 Isn’t today the time of the wheat harvest? I will call out to the LORD, and He will send thunder and rain. Then you will realize and understand how great your wickedness is—what you have done in the sight of the LORD by demanding a king for yourselves.

18 So Samuel cried out to the LORD, and the LORD sent thunder and rain that very day. As a result, all the people were filled with great awe and reverence for the LORD and for Samuel.

19 And all the people said to Samuel, "Please pray to the LORD your God on behalf of your servants, so that we may not die. For we have added to all our sins this great wrongdoing by asking for a king."

20 Samuel said to the people, "Do not be afraid. It is true that you have committed all this evil, yet do not turn away from following the LORD. Instead, serve the LORD wholeheartedly."

21 Do not turn away, for if you do, you will pursue worthless things that cannot benefit or save you; they are utterly empty.

22 For the LORD will not abandon His people, for the sake of His great name, because it has been His will to make you His own people.

23 Furthermore, as for me, far be it from me to sin against the LORD by failing to pray for you. Instead, I will instruct you in the good and proper way.

24 Revere the LORD alone, and serve Him sincerely with your whole heart. Reflect on the remarkable deeds He has done for you.

25 However, if you persist in acting wickedly, both you and your king will be utterly destroyed.
