Joshua Chapter 15

1 This was the portion assigned to the tribe of Judah, divided according to their clans. Their territory extended southward to the border of Edom, with the wilderness of Zin marking the farthest limit of the southern boundary.

2 Their southern boundary began at the shore of the Salt Sea, from the bay that extends toward the south.

3 The boundary extended to the southern slope of Maaleh-acrabbim, continued through Zin, and ascended on the southern side to Kadesh-barnea. From there, it passed by Hezron, went up to Adar, and curved around to Karkaa.

4 From there, the boundary extended toward Azmon and reached the Wadi of Egypt, ending at the Mediterranean Sea. This will be your southern border.

5 The eastern boundary extended to the Salt Sea, reaching as far as the mouth of the Jordan River. Their northern border began at the bay of the sea, at the farthest point of the Jordan.

6 The boundary line extended up to Beth-hogla and continued along the northern side of Beth-arabah. From there, it ascended to the Stone of Bohan, which belonged to Reuben’s son.

7 The boundary extended upward toward Debir from the Valley of Achor, then turned northward toward Gilgal, which lies opposite the ascent of Adummim on the south side of the river. From there, the boundary continued toward the waters of En-shemesh and ended at En-rogel.

8 The boundary line extended upward through the Valley of Ben Hinnom to the southern slope of the Jebusite territory, which is Jerusalem. From there, the boundary ascended to the top of the mountain that faces the Valley of Hinnom to the west, at the northern end of the Valley of Rephaim.

9 The boundary extended from the top of the hill to the spring of the waters of Nephtoah and continued to the cities of Mount Ephron. From there, the boundary was drawn to Baalah, which is Kirjath-jearim.

10 The boundary extended from Baalah westward to Mount Seir, then continued along to the side of Mount Jearim (also known as Chesalon) on the northern side. From there, it descended to Beth-shemesh and proceeded onward to Timnah.

11 The boundary extended to the northern side of Ekron, then curved toward Shikron, continued along to Mount Baalah, and reached as far as Jabneel. The boundary's endpoint was at the sea.

12 The western boundary extended to the Mediterranean Sea, including its coastline. This was the boundary surrounding the territory of the descendants of Judah, allocated according to their clans.

13 And to Caleb, the son of Jephunneh, he granted a portion among the people of Judah, as the LORD had commanded Joshua. This was the city of Arba, the ancestor of Anak, which is Hebron.

14 Caleb drove out from there the three sons of Anak: Sheshai, Ahiman, and Talmai, who were descendants of Anak.

15 He then went up from there to the inhabitants of Debir. Previously, the name of Debir had been Kirjath-sepher.

16 Caleb declared, "To the man who attacks and captures Kirjath-sepher, I will give my daughter Achsah as his wife."

17 Othniel, the son of Kenaz and Caleb’s younger brother, captured it. In response, Caleb gave his daughter Achsah to Othniel as his wife.

18 As she approached him, she urged him to ask her father for a field. Then she dismounted from her donkey, and Caleb said to her, "What do you want?"

19 The one who replied said, "Grant me a special favor; since you have given me land in the southern region, also give me springs of water." So he gave her the upper springs and the lower springs.

20 This is the portion of land allotted to the tribe of Judah’s descendants, divided according to their clans.

21 The farthest cities of the tribe of Judah, located toward the border of Edom in the south, were Kabzeel, Eder, and Jagur.

22 And Kinah, Dimonah, and Adadah...

23 And Kedesh, Hazor, and Ithnan.

24 Ziph, Telem, and Bealoth.

25 And Hazor, Hadattah, Kerioth, and Hezron (that is, Hazor)—

26 Amam, Shema, and Moladah.

27 And Hazar-gaddah, Heshmon, and Beth-palet.

28 And Hazar-shual, and Beersheba, and Bizjothjah.

29 Baalah, Iim, and Azem.

30 And Eltolad, and Chesil, and Hormah.

31 And Ziklag, Madmannah, and Sansannah...

32 Lebaoth, Shilhim, Ain, and Rimmon—all these cities, along with their surrounding villages, total twenty-nine in number.

33 In the valley were Eshtaol, Zoreah, and Ashnah.

34 Zanoah, En-gannim, Tappuah, and Enam.

35 Jarmuth, Adullam, Socoh, and Azekah.

36 Sharaim, Adithaim, Gederah, and Gederothaim—fourteen cities in total, along with their surrounding villages.

37 Zenan, Hadashah, and Migdal-gad.

38 And Dilean, Mizpeh, and Joktheel.

39 Lachish, Bozkath, and Eglon.

40 And Cabbon, Lahmam, and Kithlish.

41 And Gederoth, Beth-dagon, Naamah, and Makkedah—sixteen cities along with their surrounding villages.

42 Libnah, Ether, and Ashan.

43 And Jiphtah, and Ashnah, and Nezib.

44 Keilah, Achzib, and Mareshah—nine cities in total, along with their surrounding villages.

45 Ekron, along with its surrounding towns and villages.

46 From Ekron all the way to the sea, including all the areas near Ashdod, along with their surrounding villages.

47 Ashdod, along with its surrounding towns and villages; Gaza, along with its surrounding towns and villages, extending as far as the River of Egypt, the Mediterranean Sea, and the boundary of that region.

48 In the hill country, there are Shamir, Jattir, and Socoh.

49 And Dannah, and Kirjath-sannah (which is Debir),

50 And Anab, Eshtemoh, and Anim.

51 And Goshen, Holon, and Giloh—eleven cities along with their surrounding villages.

52 Arab, Dumah, and Eshean.

53 And Janum, Beth-tappuah, and Aphekah...

54 And Humtah, Kirjath-arba (that is, Hebron), and Zior—nine cities in total, along with their surrounding villages.

55 Maon, Carmel, Ziph, and Juttah.

56 And Jezreel, Jokdeam, and Zanoah...

57 Cain, Gibeah, and Timnah—ten cities in total, along with their surrounding villages.

58 Halhul, Beth-zur, and Gedor.

59 Maarath, Beth-anoth, and Eltekon—six cities in total, along with their surrounding villages.

60 Kirjath-baal, also known as Kirjath-jearim, and Rabbah—these two cities, along with their surrounding villages.

61 In the wilderness, there are Beth-arabah, Middin, and Secacah.

62 And Nibshan, the City of Salt, and En-gedi—six cities along with their surrounding villages.

63 Concerning the Jebusites, who were the inhabitants of Jerusalem, the people of Judah were unable to drive them out. As a result, the Jebusites have continued to live alongside the people of Judah in Jerusalem up to this day.
