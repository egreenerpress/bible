Psalms Chapter 139

1 (For the music director, a psalm of David.) O LORD, You have examined me and know me completely.

2 You are aware of when I sit down and when I rise up; you perceive my thoughts even from a distance.

3 You surround my path and my resting place, and You are intimately familiar with all my ways.

4 For there is not a word on my tongue, but behold, O LORD, you know it completely.

5 You have surrounded me, both behind and before, and placed Your hand upon me.

6 This understanding is beyond my grasp; it is so profound, I cannot reach it.

7 Where can I go to escape your Spirit? Where can I flee from your presence?

8 If I rise to the heavens, you are there; if I make my resting place in the realm of the dead, you are present even there.

9 If I were to rise on the wings of the dawn and settle in the farthest reaches of the sea,

10 Even in that place, your hand will guide me, and your strength will sustain me.

11 If I say, "Surely the darkness will hide me, and the night will become light around me."

12 Indeed, darkness cannot conceal anything from you; even the night shines as brightly as the day. To you, darkness and light are the same.

13 For you have formed my innermost being; you have woven me together in my motherâ€™s womb.

14 I will praise you, for I am made with awe-inspiring intricacy; your works are marvelous, and my soul knows this very well.

15 You were fully aware of my being, even when I was formed in secret, intricately crafted in the hidden depths of the earth.

16 Your eyes saw my unformed body; all the days ordained for me were written in your book before any of them came to be.

17 How deeply cherished are your thoughts toward me, O God! How vast is their total sum!

18 If I were to try to count them, they would outnumber the grains of sand. Even when I wake, I am still in your presence.

19 Certainly, you will destroy the wicked, O God; therefore, stay away from me, you violent and bloodthirsty people.

20 For they speak maliciously against you, and your enemies misuse your name without reverence.

21 Do I not despise those, O LORD, who despise you? And do I not feel deep sorrow for those who rise up against you?

22 I despise them with complete disdain; I regard them as my adversaries.

23 Examine me, O God, and understand my innermost being; test me, and discern my thoughts.

24 Examine me and see if there is any harmful or sinful path within me, and guide me in the eternal way.
