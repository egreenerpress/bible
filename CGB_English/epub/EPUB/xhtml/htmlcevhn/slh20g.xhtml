I Chronicles Chapter 10

1 The Philistines engaged in battle against Israel, and the Israelite forces retreated before them, suffering heavy casualties as they fell slain on Mount Gilboa.

2 The Philistines pursued Saul and his sons relentlessly, and they killed Jonathan, Abinadab, and Malchi-shua, the sons of Saul.

3 The battle turned fiercely against Saul, and the archers struck him, causing him to be severely wounded by their arrows.

4 Then Saul said to his armor-bearer, "Draw your sword and run me through with it, so that these uncircumcised men do not come and humiliate me." But his armor-bearer refused, for he was deeply afraid. So Saul took his own sword and fell upon it.

5 When Saulâ€™s armor-bearer saw that Saul was dead, he also threw himself on his sword and died.

6 So Saul died, along with his three sons, and his entire household perished at the same time.

7 When all the men of Israel who were in the valley saw that their army had fled and that Saul and his sons were dead, they abandoned their cities and fled as well. Then the Philistines came and occupied those cities.

8 The next day, when the Philistines came to plunder the dead, they discovered Saul and his sons lying slain on Mount Gilboa.

9 After they stripped him of his armor, they cut off his head and took his weapons. They then sent messengers throughout the land of the Philistines to announce the news to their idols and to their people.

10 They placed his armor in the temple of their gods and mounted his head in the temple of Dagon.

11 When all the people of Jabesh-gilead heard everything the Philistines had done to Saul,

12 All the courageous men rose up, took the body of Saul and the bodies of his sons, and carried them to Jabesh. There, they buried their bones beneath the oak tree in Jabesh and observed a fast for seven days.

13 So Saul died because of his unfaithfulness to the LORD. He had disobeyed the LORD's command and had also sought guidance from a medium, consulting her instead of turning to the LORD.

14 He did not seek guidance from the LORD, so the LORD caused his death and transferred the kingdom to David, the son of Jesse.
