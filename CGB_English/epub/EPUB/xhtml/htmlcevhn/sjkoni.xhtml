Psalms Chapter 109

1 (For the music director, a Psalm of David.) Do not remain silent, O God, my source of praise;

2 The words of the wicked and the speech of the deceitful are directed against me; they have spoken lies about me with a false tongue.

3 They surrounded me with hateful words and attacked me without any reason.

4 Though they oppose me because of my love, I devote myself to prayer.

5 They have repaid me with evil in return for my good deeds, and with hatred in response to my love.

6 Appoint a wicked person to oppose him, and let an adversary stand at his right side.

7 When he is brought to judgment, may he be found guilty, and may his prayers be regarded as sinful.

8 May his life be short, and may someone else take over his position.

9 May his children be left without a father, and may his wife become a widow.

10 May his children be perpetual wanderers, forced to beg for their sustenance; may they search for their food in desolate and ruined places.

11 May the oppressor seize everything he possesses, and may outsiders plunder the fruits of his labor.

12 May there be no one to show him mercy, and may no one show kindness to his fatherless children.

13 May his descendants be completely removed, and in the next generation, may their name be erased from memory.

14 May the guilt of his ancestors be brought to the LORD's attention, and may the sin of his mother never be erased from remembrance.

15 Let them remain continually in the presence of the LORD, so that He may erase their memory from the earth.

16 Because he failed to show mercy and instead pursued the poor and needy, seeking even to destroy those who were already crushed in spirit.

17 Since he loved to curse others, may his own curses return to him. Since he took no pleasure in blessing, may blessings remain distant from him.

18 As he has wrapped himself in cursing as if it were his clothing, so may it seep into his innermost being like water, and penetrate his bones like oil.

19 May it be to him like the clothing he wears, like the belt that is always wrapped around him.

20 May this be the recompense from the LORD for my adversaries and for those who speak harm against my life.

21 Act on my behalf, O LORD God, for the sake of your name; because your steadfast love is good, rescue me.

22 For I am afflicted and in need, and my heart is deeply wounded within me.

23 I fade away like a shadow at dusk; I am shaken and scattered like a locust in the wind.

24 My knees have grown weak from fasting, and my body has lost its strength and vitality.

25 I also became an object of scorn to them; when they saw me, they shook their heads in contempt.

26 Assist me, O LORD my God; deliver me according to your steadfast love and compassion.

27 So that they may recognize that this is your doing, that you, O LORD, have accomplished it.

28 Let them curse, but You, O Lord, bless. When they rise up, let them be put to shame, but let Your servant be glad.

29 May my enemies be overwhelmed with disgrace, and may they wrap themselves in their own humiliation as if it were a cloak.

30 I will wholeheartedly praise the LORD with my words; indeed, I will honor Him in the presence of many.

31 For he will stand beside the needy, to rescue them from those who seek to condemn their lives.
