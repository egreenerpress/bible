Judges Chapter 12

1 The men of Ephraim assembled and marched northward, confronting Jephthah with these words: "Why did you cross over to fight against the Ammonites without calling us to join you? We will burn your house down with you in it!"

2 Jephthah replied to them, "My people and I were engaged in a fierce conflict with the Ammonites. When I called for your help, you did not come to rescue us from their hands."

3 When I realized that you would not come to my aid, I risked my life and crossed over to confront the Ammonites, and the LORD handed them over to me. So why have you now come up against me today to fight me?

6 Then they said to him, "Say now, 'Shibboleth.'" But he said, "Sibboleth," because he could not pronounce it correctly. So they seized him and killed him at the crossings of the Jordan. At that time, forty-two thousand Ephraimites fell.

7 Jephthah led Israel as judge for six years. Afterward, Jephthah the Gileadite died and was buried in one of the cities of Gilead.

8 After him, Ibzan from Bethlehem led and governed Israel.

9 He had thirty sons and thirty daughters. He sent his daughters to marry outside his family and brought in thirty daughters from other families to marry his sons. He led and governed Israel for seven years.

10 Then Ibzan died and was buried in Bethlehem.

11 After him, Elon from the tribe of Zebulun led Israel as judge, and he governed Israel for ten years.

12 Elon the Zebulonite died and was buried in Aijalon, in the territory of Zebulun.

13 After him, Abdon son of Hillel, from Pirathon, served as a judge over Israel.

14 He had forty sons and thirty grandsons, who rode on seventy donkeys. He led Israel as a judge for eight years.

15 And Abdon, the son of Hillel from Pirathon, died and was buried in Pirathon, in the land of Ephraim, in the hill country of the Amalekites.
