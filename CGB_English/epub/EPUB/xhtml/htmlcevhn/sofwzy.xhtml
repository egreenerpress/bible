Psalms Chapter 74

1 (A contemplative psalm of Asaph.) O God, why have you completely rejected us? Why does your anger burn so fiercely against the flock of your own care?

2 Remember the people you have chosen long ago, the community you acquired as your own; the tribe that is your special possession, which you have rescued. Recall Mount Zion, the place where you have made your dwelling.

3 Rise up and march toward the everlasting ruins; see all the destruction the enemy has wrought in the sanctuary.

4 Your adversaries raise a clamor in the midst of your sacred assemblies; they display their banners as symbols of their defiance.

5 A person gained renown based on how skillfully they wielded axes against the dense trees.

6 But now they demolish its intricate carvings all at once, using axes and hammers.

7 They have set fire to your holy sanctuary; they have desecrated it by tearing down the place where your name dwells, reducing it to the ground.

8 They thought to themselves, "Let us completely destroy them." They have set fire to all the places where God is worshiped throughout the land.

9 We no longer see the signs of Your presence; there are no prophets among us, and none of us knows how long this will last.

10 O God, how long will the adversary mock and insult? Will the enemy continue to dishonor your name without end?

11 Why do you hold back your hand, even your mighty hand? Draw it forth from within your cloak and act!

12 For God has been my sovereign from ancient times, bringing about deliverance throughout the earth.

13 You divided the sea by your mighty power; you shattered the heads of the sea monsters in the waters.

14 You shattered the heads of Leviathan, breaking them into pieces, and gave him as food to the people dwelling in the wilderness.

15 You split open the spring and the torrent; you dried up the ever-flowing rivers.

16 The day belongs to you, and the night is also yours; you have established both the light and the sun.

17 You have established all the boundaries of the earth; you have created both summer and winter.

18 Keep this in mind, O LORD: the enemy has mocked you, and a senseless people has spoken disrespectfully against your name.

19 O Lord, do not surrender the life of your beloved to the power of the wicked; do not abandon your afflicted people forever.

20 Pay attention to the covenant, for the shadowed regions of the earth are filled with places where cruelty dwells.

21 Do not allow the oppressed to turn back in disgrace; may the poor and needy glorify your name.

22 Rise up, O God, and defend your cause; remember how the foolish constantly mock you.

23 Do not forget the clamor of your enemies; the uproar of those who rise against you grows louder without ceasing.
