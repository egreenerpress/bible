Zechariah Chapter 3

1 Then he showed me Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the LORD, with Satan standing at his right side to accuse him.

2 The LORD said to Satan, "May the LORD rebuke you, O Satan—yes, the LORD who has chosen Jerusalem rebuke you! Is this not a burning stick rescued from the fire?"

3 Now Joshua was dressed in filthy garments and stood before the angel.

4 He responded and spoke to those who stood before him, saying, "Remove the filthy garments from him." Then he said to him, "Look, I have taken away your guilt, and I will dress you in fresh, clean clothing."

5 I said, "Let them place a clean turban on his head." So they placed a clean turban on his head and dressed him in clean garments. And the angel of the LORD stood nearby.

6 And the angel of the LORD solemnly declared to Joshua, saying,

7 This is what the LORD of hosts says: If you walk in my ways and faithfully carry out my responsibilities, then you will govern my house and oversee my courts. I will grant you the privilege of walking among those who serve here.

8 Listen now, Joshua the high priest, you and your companions who sit before you, for you are men who are a sign of wonder. Behold, I am about to bring forth my servant, the Branch.

9 Behold, the stone that I have set before Joshua—on this single stone are seven eyes. I, the LORD of hosts, declare that I will engrave an inscription upon it, and in a single day, I will remove the guilt and sin of this land.

10 On that day, declares the LORD of hosts, each person will invite their neighbor to sit together in peace under their own vine and fig tree.
