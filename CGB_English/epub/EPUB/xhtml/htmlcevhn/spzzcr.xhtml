Luke Chapter 2

1 In those days, a decree was issued by Caesar Augustus that a census should be taken of the entire Roman world.

2 This census was first conducted when Quirinius was governing Syria.

3 And everyone went to their own town to register for the census.

4 Joseph also traveled from Galilee, leaving the town of Nazareth, and went to Judea, to the city of David, which is called Bethlehem. This was because he was from the family and lineage of David.

5 To register for the census with Mary, his betrothed wife, who was expecting a child.

6 And so it happened that while they were there, the time came for her to give birth.

7 She gave birth to her firstborn son, wrapped him in strips of cloth, and laid him in a feeding trough, because there was no space for them in the guest room.

8 In that same region, shepherds were staying out in the fields, keeping watch over their flock during the night.

9 Suddenly, the angel of the Lord appeared to them, and the radiant glory of the Lord surrounded them. They were filled with great fear.

10 The angel said to them, "Do not be afraid, for I bring you good news of great joy that will be for all people."

11 Today, in the city of David, a Savior has been born for youâ€”he is the Messiah, the Lord.

12 This will be the sign for you: You will find the baby wrapped in strips of cloth and lying in a feeding trough.

13 And suddenly, a great assembly of the heavenly army appeared with the angel, praising God and proclaiming,

14 Glory to God in the highest heavens, and on earth peace, and favor extended to humanity.

15 After the angels had left them and returned to heaven, the shepherds said to one another, "Let us go to Bethlehem right away and see this event that has taken place, which the Lord has revealed to us."

16 They hurried and found Mary, Joseph, and the baby lying in a feeding trough.

17 After they had seen it, they spread the word far and wide about what had been told to them concerning this child.

18 All who heard these things were amazed at what the shepherds told them.

19 But Mary treasured all these things and deeply reflected on them in her heart.

20 The shepherds went back to their fields, giving glory and praise to God for everything they had heard and seen, just as it had been told to them.

21 When eight days had passed and it was time to circumcise the child, he was given the name Jesus, the name the angel had given before he was conceived in the womb.

22 When the days of her purification, as required by the law of Moses, were completed, they brought the child to Jerusalem to present him to the Lord.

23 As it is written in the law of the Lord, "Every firstborn male shall be consecrated to the Lord."

24 And to present a sacrifice, as prescribed in the law of the Lord: a pair of turtledoves or two young pigeons.

25 There was a man in Jerusalem named Simeon, who was righteous and devout. He was eagerly awaiting the comfort and deliverance of Israel, and the Holy Spirit was upon him.

26 The Holy Spirit made it known to him that he would not experience death until he had seen the Messiah of the Lord.

27 Moved by the Spirit, he entered the temple. When the parents brought in the child Jesus to fulfill the customary requirements of the law,

28 Then he took the child in his arms, praised God, and said,

29 Lord, now you are allowing your servant to depart in peace, according to your promise.

30 For my eyes have witnessed your deliverance,

31 Which you have prepared in the presence of all people.

32 A light to bring revelation to the nations, and the glory of your people Israel.

33 Joseph and his mother were amazed at the things that were said about him.

34 Simeon blessed them and said to Mary, his mother, "Listen, this child is destined to cause the falling and rising of many in Israel. He will be a sign that will be opposed and spoken against."

35 Indeed, a sword will pierce your own soul as well, so that the innermost thoughts of many hearts may be laid bare.

36 There was also a woman named Anna, a prophetess, the daughter of Phanuel, from the tribe of Asher. She was very old, having lived with her husband for seven years after her marriage, and then remaining a widow.

37 She was a widow, about eighty-four years old, who never left the temple but worshiped God continually with fasting and prayer, both day and night.

38 At that very moment, she came forward and also gave thanks to the Lord. She spoke about the child to everyone who was waiting expectantly for the redemption of Jerusalem.

39 After they had fulfilled everything required by the law of the Lord, they returned to Galilee, to their hometown of Nazareth.

40 The child grew and became strong in spirit, filled with wisdom, and the favor of God rested upon him.

41 Every year, his parents would travel to Jerusalem to observe the Passover festival.

42 When he was twelve years old, they traveled to Jerusalem, as was the customary practice during the festival.

43 After they had completed the required days of the festival and were returning home, the boy Jesus stayed behind in Jerusalem without Joseph and his mother realizing it.

44 But they, assuming he was with the group, traveled for a day before they began searching for him among their relatives and friends.

45 When they could not find him, they returned to Jerusalem to search for him.

46 After three days had passed, they found him in the temple, sitting among the teachers, listening to them and asking them questions.

47 All who heard him were amazed at his insight and the wisdom of his responses.

48 When they saw him, they were astonished. His mother said to him, "Son, why have you treated us this way? Your father and I have been anxiously searching for you, filled with worry."

49 And He said to them, "Why were you searching for Me? Did you not know that I must be occupied with My Fatherâ€™s work?"

50 They did not comprehend the meaning of what he had said to them.

51 He went down with them and came to Nazareth, where he lived in obedience to them. His mother, however, treasured all these things in her heart.

52 And Jesus grew in wisdom and physical maturity, gaining favor both with God and with people.
