II Corinthians Chapter 6

1 As fellow workers with God, we urge you not to accept His grace in a way that proves meaningless or without effect.

2 For God says, "I listened to you at the time I chose to show favor, and on the day of deliverance I came to your aid. Look, now is the time of my favor; now is the day of salvation!"

3 Doing nothing that would cause others to stumble or find fault, so that our ministry may not be discredited.

4 In every situation, we demonstrate that we are servants of God: through great endurance, in times of hardship, in moments of need, and in periods of distress.

5 Through beatings, through imprisonments, through riots, through exhausting labor, through sleepless nights, and through times of fasting.

6 Through purity, through understanding, through patience, through kindness, through the Holy Spirit, through genuine love,

7 Through the message of truth, by the power of God, and with the weapons of righteousness in both hands,

8 Through both honor and disgrace, through slander and praise; regarded as impostors, yet genuine;

9 Though we are regarded as unknown, we are in fact well-known; though we appear to be dying, yet we live; though we are disciplined, we are not destroyed.

10 Though we are often sorrowful, we are always filled with joy; though we may appear poor, we enrich many; though we seem to have nothing, we truly possess everything.

11 To the Corinthians: We speak openly to you, and our hearts are wide open toward you.

12 You are not restricted by us, but you are constrained by your own affections.

13 Now, as a fitting response in kind—I speak to you as my own children—open your hearts wide as well.

14 Do not form close partnerships with those who do not share your faith. For what connection can exist between righteousness and unrighteousness? What harmony is there between light and darkness?

15 What agreement can there be between Christ and Belial? Or what does a believer have in common with an unbeliever?

16 What connection can there be between the temple of God and idols? For you are the temple of the living God, as God has said: "I will dwell among them and walk with them; I will be their God, and they will be my people."

17 Therefore, separate yourselves from them, says the Lord, and do not associate with what is impure. Then I will welcome you.

18 I will be a Father to you, and you will be my sons and daughters, says the Lord Almighty.
