Luke Chapter 17

1 Then Jesus said to his disciples, "It is inevitable that stumbling blocks will arise, but woe to the one through whom they come!"

2 It would be better for him to have a heavy millstone tied around his neck and be thrown into the sea than to cause harm or lead astray one of these little ones.

3 Be careful and watchful in your conduct: If your brother wrongs you, confront him directly; and if he expresses genuine remorse, forgive him.

4 And if he wrongs you seven times in a single day, and each time returns to you, saying, ‘I repent,’ you must forgive him.

5 The apostles said to the Lord, "Strengthen our faith."

6 The Lord said, "If you had faith even as small as a mustard seed, you could say to this mulberry tree, 'Be uprooted and planted in the sea,' and it would obey you."

7 Which of you, if you have a servant plowing the fields or tending the livestock, would say to him as soon as he comes in from his work, ‘Go ahead and sit down to eat’?

8 Would he not instead say to him, ‘Prepare what I need for my meal, dress yourself properly, and serve me while I eat and drink; after that, you may eat and drink yourself’?

9 Does the master thank the servant for simply doing what he was commanded to do? I think not.

10 In the same way, when you have done everything that you were commanded to do, you should say, "We are unworthy servants; we have only done what was our duty to do."

11 As he was traveling to Jerusalem, he journeyed through the region between Samaria and Galilee.

12 As he entered a certain village, ten men who were afflicted with leprosy approached him, but they stood at a distance.

13 They cried out loudly, saying, "Jesus, Teacher, have compassion on us!"

14 When Jesus saw them, he said to them, "Go and present yourselves to the priests." And as they went on their way, they were healed.

15 One of them, realizing that he had been healed, turned back and praised God in a loud voice.

16 He prostrated himself at Jesus' feet, expressing his gratitude. This man was a Samaritan.

17 Jesus responded, "Were not ten made clean? But where are the other nine?"

18 No one else came back to give praise to God except this foreigner.

19 And He said to him, "Get up and go on your way; your faith has healed you."

20 When the Pharisees asked Jesus when the kingdom of God would come, he replied to them, "The kingdom of God does not come in a way that can be visibly observed."

21 Do not say, ‘Look, it is here!’ or ‘See, it is there!’ For truly, the kingdom of God is present among you.

22 And he said to his disciples, "The time will come when you will long to see even one of the days of the Son of Man, but you will not see it."

23 And they will say to you, "Look here!" or "Look there!" Do not go after them or follow them.

24 For just as the lightning flashes from one part of the sky and shines to the other part of the sky, so will the Son of Man be in His day.

25 But first, he must endure many hardships and be rejected by this generation.

26 And just as it was in the days of Noah, so will it also be in the days of the Son of Man.

27 They ate, they drank, they married, and were given in marriage, until the day Noah entered the ark. Then the flood came and destroyed them all.

28 Just as it was in the days of Lot: they were eating, drinking, buying, selling, planting, and building.

29 On the very day that Lot left Sodom, fire and sulfur rained down from heaven and completely destroyed them all.

30 In the same way, it will be on the day when the Son of Man is revealed.

31 On that day, whoever is on the rooftop, with their belongings inside the house, must not go down to retrieve them. Likewise, whoever is out in the field must not turn back.

32 Remember what happened to Lot's wife.

33 Whoever strives to save their own life will end up losing it, but whoever is willing to let go of their life for a greater purpose will truly preserve it.

34 I tell you, on that night there will be two people lying in the same bed; one will be taken, and the other will be left behind.

35 Two women will be grinding grain together; one will be taken, and the other will be left behind.

36 Two people will be working in the field; one will be taken, and the other will be left behind.

37 They replied, "Where, Lord?" He answered them, "Wherever the dead body lies, there the vultures will gather."
