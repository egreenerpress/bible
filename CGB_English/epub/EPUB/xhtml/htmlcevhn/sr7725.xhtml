Luke Chapter 6

1 On the second Sabbath after the first, Jesus was passing through the grain fields. His disciples began to pick some heads of grain, rub them in their hands, and eat the kernels.

2 Some of the Pharisees said to them, "Why are you doing what is not permitted on the Sabbath?"

3 Jesus responded to them, saying, "Have you not even read what David did when he and those with him were hungry?"

4 How he entered the house of God, took and ate the consecrated bread, and also gave it to those who were with him—bread which is not lawful to eat except for the priests alone?

5 And He said to them, "The Son of Man is also Lord over the Sabbath."

6 It also happened on another Sabbath that he went into the synagogue and began to teach. There was a man there whose right hand was shriveled and useless.

7 The scribes and Pharisees were closely observing Jesus to see if he would heal someone on the Sabbath day, hoping to find grounds to accuse him.

8 But Jesus, aware of their thoughts, said to the man with the withered hand, "Get up and step forward into the center." So the man stood up and came forward.

9 Then Jesus said to them, "Let me ask you something: Is it permitted to do good or to do harm on the Sabbath? To save a life or to destroy it?"

10 Jesus looked around at all of them and then said to the man, "Stretch out your hand." The man did as he was told, and his hand was completely restored, as healthy as the other.

11 They were overcome with rage and began discussing among themselves what they could do to Jesus.

12 During that time, he went out to a mountain to pray and spent the entire night in prayer to God.

13 When morning came, He summoned His disciples and chose twelve from among them, whom He also designated as apostles.

14 Simon (whom He also named Peter) and his brother Andrew, James and John, Philip and Bartholomew...

15 Matthew and Thomas, James the son of Alphaeus, and Simon, who was called the Zealot.

16 And Judas, the brother of James, and Judas Iscariot, who also became the betrayer.

17 He descended with them and stood on a level place, accompanied by a gathering of his disciples and a large crowd of people from all over Judea, Jerusalem, and the coastal regions of Tyre and Sidon. They had come to listen to him and to be healed of their illnesses.

18 And those who were tormented by impure spirits were healed.

19 The entire crowd pressed forward, eager to touch Him, for power flowed out from Him, and He healed every one of them.

20 Jesus looked at his disciples and said, "Blessed are you who are poor, for the kingdom of God belongs to you."

21 Blessed are you who hunger now, for you will be satisfied. Blessed are you who weep now, for you will rejoice.

22 You are blessed when people hate you, when they exclude you from their gatherings, insult you, and slander your name as evil because of your allegiance to the Son of Man.

23 Be glad on that day and leap with joy, for look—your reward is great in heaven. This is how their ancestors treated the prophets in the same way.

24 But how terrible it will be for you who are wealthy! For you have already received your comfort and reward.

25 How terrible it will be for you who are satisfied now, for you will go hungry. How terrible it will be for you who laugh now, for you will grieve and weep.

26 How terrible it will be for you when everyone speaks well of you, for their ancestors did the same to the false prophets.

27 But I say to you who are listening: Love your enemies, do good to those who hate you.

28 Bless those who speak ill of you, and pray for those who mistreat you or act with hostility toward you.

29 And to the one who strikes you on the cheek, offer the other as well; and to the one who takes your cloak, do not refuse your coat also.

30 Give to everyone who asks of you; and if someone takes what belongs to you, do not demand it back.

31 And just as you would want others to treat you, treat them in the same way.

32 If you love only those who love you, what credit is that to you? Even sinners love those who love them.

33 If you only do good to those who do good to you, what credit is that to you? Even sinners act in the same way.

34 If you lend to those from whom you expect repayment, what credit is that to you? Even sinners lend to sinners, expecting to be repaid in full.

35 But you must love your enemies, do good to them, and lend to them without expecting anything in return. Then your reward will be great, and you will be called children of the Most High, for he is kind to the ungrateful and the wicked.

36 Therefore, show mercy, just as your Father is merciful.

37 Do not judge others, and you will not be judged. Do not condemn others, and you will not be condemned. Forgive others, and you will be forgiven.

38 Give generously, and you will receive generously in return. People will pour into your life a full measure—pressed down, shaken together, and overflowing. For the measure you use to give will be the measure used to give back to you.

39 Jesus told them a parable: "Can someone who is blind guide another blind person? Won’t both of them fall into a pit?"

40 A disciple is not greater than their teacher, but everyone who is fully trained will become like their teacher.

41 Why do you focus on the tiny speck in your brother’s eye, yet fail to notice the large beam in your own eye?

42 How can you say to your brother, “Brother, let me remove the speck from your eye,” when you yourself fail to notice the log in your own eye? You hypocrite! First, take the log out of your own eye, and then you will see clearly to remove the speck from your brother’s eye.

43 A healthy tree does not produce rotten fruit, nor does a rotten tree produce good fruit.

44 Every tree is recognized by its own fruit. People do not pick figs from thornbushes, nor do they gather grapes from bramble bushes.

45 A good person, from the good stored up in their heart, produces good things; and an evil person, from the evil stored up in their heart, produces evil things. For what fills the heart overflows through the words they speak.

46 Why do you call me "Lord, Lord," yet fail to do what I tell you?

47 Whoever comes to me, listens to my words, and puts them into practice, I will show you what such a person is like:

48 He is like a man who built a house, digging deep and laying the foundation on solid rock. When the floodwaters rose and the torrent rushed against that house, it could not be shaken, because it was firmly established on the rock.

49 However, the one who listens but does not act is like a person who built a house directly on the ground without any foundation. When the floodwaters rose and crashed against it, the house collapsed at once, and its destruction was complete.
