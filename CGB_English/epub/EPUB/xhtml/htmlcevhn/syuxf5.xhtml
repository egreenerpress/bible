Ezra Chapter 2

1 These are the descendants of the province who returned from exile—those who had been taken captive by Nebuchadnezzar, the king of Babylon, and brought to Babylon. They later came back to Jerusalem and Judah, each returning to their own city.

2 Those who returned with Zerubbabel were: Jeshua, Nehemiah, Seraiah, Reelaiah, Mordecai, Bilshan, Mispar, Bigvai, Rehum, and Baanah. This is the count of the men from the people of Israel:

3 The descendants of Parosh numbered two thousand one hundred and seventy-two.

4 The descendants of Shephatiah numbered three hundred and seventy-two.

5 The descendants of Arah numbered seven hundred and seventy-five.

6 The descendants of Pahath-moab, specifically from the families of Jeshua and Joab, numbered two thousand eight hundred and twelve.

7 The descendants of Elam numbered one thousand two hundred and fifty-four.

8 The descendants of Zattu numbered nine hundred and forty-five.

9 The descendants of Zaccai numbered seven hundred and sixty.

10 The descendants of Bani numbered six hundred and forty-two.

11 The descendants of Bebai numbered six hundred and twenty-three.

12 The descendants of Azgad numbered one thousand two hundred and twenty-two.

13 The descendants of Adonikam numbered six hundred and sixty-six.

14 The descendants of Bigvai numbered two thousand and fifty-six.

15 The descendants of Adin numbered four hundred and fifty-four.

16 The descendants of Ater, through Hezekiah, numbered ninety-eight.

17 The descendants of Bezai numbered three hundred and twenty-three.

18 The descendants of Jorah numbered one hundred and twelve.

19 The descendants of Hashum: two hundred and twenty-three.

20 The descendants of Gibbar numbered ninety-five.

21 The descendants of Bethlehem numbered one hundred and twenty-three.

22 The men of Netophah: fifty-six.

23 The men from Anathoth numbered one hundred and twenty-eight.

24 The descendants of Azmaveth numbered forty-two.

25 The people from Kirjath-arim, Chephirah, and Beeroth numbered seven hundred and forty-three.

26 The people from Ramah and Geba numbered six hundred and twenty-one.

27 The men of Michmash: one hundred and twenty-two.

28 The men from Bethel and Ai numbered two hundred and twenty-three.

29 The descendants of Nebo, fifty-two in number.

30 The descendants of Magbish numbered one hundred and fifty-six.

31 The descendants of the other Elam numbered one thousand two hundred and fifty-four.

32 The descendants of Harim: three hundred and twenty.

33 The descendants of Lod, Hadid, and Ono numbered seven hundred and twenty-five.

34 The descendants of Jericho numbered three hundred and forty-five.

35 The descendants of Senaah numbered three thousand six hundred and thirty.

36 The priests: the descendants of Jedaiah, from the family of Jeshua, numbered nine hundred seventy-three.

37 The descendants of Immer numbered one thousand and fifty-two.

38 The descendants of Pashur numbered one thousand two hundred and forty-seven.

39 The descendants of Harim numbered one thousand and seventeen.

40 The Levites: the descendants of Jeshua and Kadmiel, from the line of Hodaviah, numbered seventy-four.

41 The singers: the descendants of Asaph, one hundred twenty-eight in number.

42 The descendants of the gatekeepers: the descendants of Shallum, the descendants of Ater, the descendants of Talmon, the descendants of Akkub, the descendants of Hatita, and the descendants of Shobai—in total, one hundred and thirty-nine.

43 The temple servants: the descendants of Ziha, the descendants of Hasupha, the descendants of Tabbaoth.

44 The descendants of Keros, the descendants of Siaha, the descendants of Padon.

45 The descendants of Lebanah, the descendants of Hagabah, the descendants of Akkub.

46 The descendants of Hagab, the descendants of Shalmai, the descendants of Hanan.

47 The descendants of Giddel, the descendants of Gahar, and the descendants of Reaiah.

48 The descendants of Rezin, the descendants of Nekoda, and the descendants of Gazzam.

49 The descendants of Uzza, the descendants of Paseah, and the descendants of Besai.

50 The descendants of Asnah, the descendants of Mehunim, and the descendants of Nephusim.

51 The descendants of Bakbuk, the descendants of Hakupha, and the descendants of Harhur.

52 The descendants of Bazluth, the descendants of Mehida, the descendants of Harsha.

53 The descendants of Barkos, the descendants of Sisera, and the descendants of Thamah.

54 The descendants of Neziah, the descendants of Hatipha.

55 The descendants of Solomon's servants: the descendants of Sotai, the descendants of Sophereth, and the descendants of Peruda.

56 The descendants of Jaalah, the descendants of Darkon, and the descendants of Giddel.

57 The descendants of Shephatiah, the descendants of Hattil, the descendants of Pochereth of Zebaim, and the descendants of Ami.

58 All the temple servants and the descendants of Solomon's servants numbered three hundred and ninety-two.

59 These were the people who came up from Tel-melah, Tel-harsa, Cherub, Addan, and Immer. However, they were unable to prove their family lineage or demonstrate whether they were descendants of Israel.

60 The descendants of Delaiah, the descendants of Tobiah, and the descendants of Nekoda numbered six hundred and fifty-two.

61 Among the descendants of the priests were the families of Habaiah, Koz, and Barzillai. This Barzillai had married one of the daughters of Barzillai the Gileadite and had taken on their family name.

62 These individuals sought to have their names included in the genealogical records of those who were officially recognized as priests, but their names could not be found. As a result, they were considered unclean and excluded from serving in the priesthood.

63 The governor instructed them that they were not to partake of the most sacred offerings until a priest arose who was equipped with the Urim and Thummim.

64 The entire assembly together numbered forty-two thousand three hundred and sixty.

65 In addition to their male and female servants, who numbered seven thousand three hundred and thirty-seven, there were also among them two hundred men and women who were skilled in singing.

66 Their horses numbered seven hundred and thirty-six, and their mules numbered two hundred and forty-five.

67 Their camels numbered four hundred thirty-five, and their donkeys totaled six thousand seven hundred and twenty.

68 And some of the leading family heads, when they arrived at the house of the LORD in Jerusalem, gave willingly to support the rebuilding of God’s house, so that it could be restored to its proper place.

69 They contributed according to their means to the treasury for the work: sixty-one thousand drachmas of gold, five thousand pounds of silver, and one hundred priestly garments.

70 So the priests, the Levites, some of the people, the singers, the gatekeepers, and the temple servants settled in their respective cities, and all the rest of Israel also lived in their own cities.
