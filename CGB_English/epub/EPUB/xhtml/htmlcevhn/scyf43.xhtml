Psalms Chapter 79

1 (A Psalm of Asaph.) O God, the nations have invaded Your inheritance; they have defiled Your holy temple and reduced Jerusalem to ruins.

2 The bodies of your faithful servants have been left as food for the birds of the sky, and the flesh of your devoted ones has been given to the wild beasts of the earth.

3 They have poured out their blood like water all around Jerusalem, and there was no one to bury them.

4 We have become an object of disgrace to our neighbors, a source of mockery and ridicule to those around us.

5 How long, O LORD? Will you remain angry forever? Will your intense zeal continue to burn like fire?

6 Bring your judgment upon the nations that do not acknowledge you, and upon the kingdoms that refuse to call on your name.

7 For they have utterly consumed Jacob and devastated the place where he dwells.

8 Do not hold against us the sins of our past; may your compassionate mercies come quickly to meet us, for we are in desperate need and utterly humbled.

9 Save us, O God, our deliverer, for the honor of your name. Rescue us and cleanse us from our sins, for the sake of your name.

10 Why should the nations say, "Where is their God?" Let Him be made known among the nations before our eyes by avenging the blood of Your servants that has been shed.

11 May the cries of the prisoners reach you; by the greatness of your power, save those who are destined for death.

12 Repay our neighbors sevenfold into their own hearts for the disgrace they have brought upon you, O Lord.

13 Therefore, we, your people and the flock under your care, will forever give you thanks; we will declare your praise throughout all generations.
