Deuteronomy Chapter 27

1 Moses, along with the elders of Israel, instructed the people, saying, "Observe and follow all the commands that I am giving you today."

2 On the day you cross the Jordan into the land that the LORD your God is giving you, you shall set up large stones and coat them with plaster.

3 You are to inscribe on them all the words of this law after you have crossed over, so that you may enter the land that the LORD your God is giving you—a land flowing with milk and honey, just as the LORD, the God of your ancestors, has promised you.

4 Therefore, when you have crossed the Jordan, you shall set up these stones, which I command you today, on Mount Ebal, and you shall coat them with plaster.

5 You shall build an altar there to the LORD your God, an altar made of stones. Do not use any iron tool on them.

6 You shall construct the altar of the LORD your God using uncut stones, and on it you shall present burnt offerings to the LORD your God.

7 You shall present fellowship offerings, and you shall eat there and celebrate joyfully in the presence of the LORD your God.

8 You shall inscribe on the stones all the words of this law clearly and distinctly.

9 Moses and the Levitical priests addressed all Israel, saying, "Pay attention and listen, O Israel: Today you have become the people of the LORD your God."

10 Therefore, you must listen to the voice of the LORD your God and carry out His commandments and decrees, which I am giving to you today.

11 On that same day, Moses gave this instruction to the people, saying:

12 When you have crossed the Jordan, these tribes shall stand on Mount Gerizim to pronounce blessings over the people: Simeon, Levi, Judah, Issachar, Joseph, and Benjamin.

13 The following tribes shall stand on Mount Ebal to pronounce the curses: Reuben, Gad, Asher, Zebulun, Dan, and Naphtali.

14 The Levites shall proclaim and declare to all the people of Israel in a loud voice, saying:

15 May the person who creates any carved or cast idol—a detestable thing to the LORD, the work of a craftsman’s hands—and then hides it in a secret place be cursed. And all the people shall respond by saying, “So be it.”

16 "May anyone who dishonors or disrespects their father or mother be cursed." And all the people will respond, "Amen."

17 "May anyone who moves their neighbor’s boundary marker be cursed." And all the people shall respond, "Amen."

18 May anyone who causes a blind person to go astray be cursed. And all the people will respond, "Amen."

19 May anyone who distorts justice for the foreigner, the fatherless, or the widow be cursed. And all the people shall respond, "Amen."

20 Cursed is anyone who has sexual relations with his father’s wife, for he dishonors his father’s intimate relationship. And all the people shall respond, “Amen.”

21 "May anyone who engages in sexual relations with any kind of animal be cursed." And all the people shall respond, "Amen."

22 "May anyone who engages in sexual relations with his sister—whether she is the daughter of his father or the daughter of his mother—be cursed. And all the people shall respond, 'Amen.'"

23 "May anyone who sleeps with his mother-in-law be cursed." And all the people shall respond, "Amen."

24 "May anyone who strikes down their neighbor in secret be cursed." And all the people shall respond, "Amen."

25 "May anyone who accepts a bribe to kill an innocent person be cursed." And all the people shall respond, "Amen."

26 "May anyone who fails to uphold and carry out all the words of this law be cursed." And all the people shall respond, "Amen."
