John Chapter 7

1 After these events, Jesus traveled and ministered in Galilee, for He chose not to go about in Judea because the Jewish leaders were seeking to kill Him.

2 The Jewish Festival of Tabernacles was approaching.

3 So his brothers said to him, "Leave this place and go to Judea, so that your followers there may also see the works you are doing."

4 For no one acts in secret while desiring to be known publicly. If you are doing these things, reveal yourself openly to the world.

5 Even his own brothers did not believe in him.

6 Then Jesus said to them, "My appointed time has not yet arrived, but your time is always ready."

7 The world has no reason to hate you, but it hates me because I expose its actions as evil.

8 "Go up to this feast yourselves; I am not going up to this feast yet, because the right time for me has not yet arrived."

9 After he had spoken these things to them, he remained in Galilee.

10 But when his brothers had gone up to the feast, he also went up, not publicly, but in a manner that was concealed, as if in secret.

11 Then the Jewish leaders were looking for him at the festival and asking, "Where is he?"

12 There was a great deal of whispering and debate among the people about him. Some were saying, "He is a good man," while others argued, "No, he is misleading the people."

13 However, no one spoke openly about Him because they were afraid of the Jewish leaders.

14 About halfway through the festival, Jesus went up to the temple and began to teach.

15 The Jews were amazed and said, "How does this man know so much about the Scriptures, since he has never been formally taught?"

16 Jesus responded to them, saying, "What I teach is not my own, but comes from the one who sent me."

17 If anyone is willing to do God's will, they will come to understand whether this teaching is from God or whether I am speaking on my own authority.

18 The one who speaks on his own authority seeks his own honor, but the one who seeks the honor of the one who sent him is truthful, and there is no falsehood in him.

19 Did not Moses give you the Law, yet none of you keeps it? Why are you seeking to kill me?

20 The people responded, "You are out of your mind! Who is trying to kill you?"

21 Jesus responded to them, saying, "I have performed one act, and you are all astonished."

22 Moses gave you the practice of circumcision—though it did not originate with him but with the patriarchs—and you circumcise a man even on the Sabbath.

23 If a man undergoes circumcision on the Sabbath so that the law of Moses is not violated, why are you angry with me for making a man completely well on the Sabbath?

24 Do not make judgments based solely on outward appearances, but instead render decisions that are just and righteous.

25 Then some of the people from Jerusalem said, "Isn’t this the man they are trying to kill?"

26 But look, he speaks openly and confidently, and they say nothing to stop him. Could it be that the rulers truly know this is indeed the Messiah?

27 However, we know where this man comes from; but when the Messiah comes, no one will know where he comes from.

28 Then Jesus called out in the temple as he was teaching, saying, "You both know who I am, and you know where I come from. Yet I have not come on my own authority, but the one who sent me is true—the one you do not know."

29 But I know him, because I come from him, and he is the one who sent me.

30 At that moment, they tried to seize him, but no one laid a hand on him, because his appointed time had not yet arrived.

31 And many of the people came to believe in him, saying, "When the Messiah comes, will he perform greater signs than these that this man has done?"

32 The Pharisees heard that the people were whispering these things about him, so the Pharisees and the chief priests sent officers to arrest him.

33 Then Jesus said to them, "I will be with you only a little longer, and then I will go to the one who sent me."

34 You will search for me, but you will not find me; and where I am, you cannot come.

35 Then the Jews said to one another, "Where does he intend to go that we will not be able to find him? Does he plan to go to the Jewish people scattered among the Gentiles and teach the Gentiles?"

36 What kind of statement is this that He made: "You will seek Me, but you will not find Me; and where I am, you cannot come"?

37 On the final day, the most important day of the festival, Jesus stood up and called out, saying, "If anyone is thirsty, let them come to me and drink."

38 The one who believes in me, as the Scripture has declared, will have streams of living water flowing from within them.

39 (But he said this concerning the Spirit, whom those who believed in him were to receive; for the Holy Spirit had not yet been given, because Jesus had not yet been glorified.)

40 Therefore, when many of the people heard these words, they said, "Truly, this man is the Prophet."

41 Some people said, "This is the Messiah." But others questioned, "Can the Messiah really come from Galilee?"

42 Has not the Scripture declared that the Christ comes from the lineage of David and from the town of Bethlehem, where David lived?

43 As a result, the people were divided in their opinions about him.

44 Some of them wanted to seize him, but no one actually laid a hand on him.

45 Then the officers returned to the chief priests and Pharisees, who asked them, "Why didn’t you bring him here?"

46 The officers replied, "No one has ever spoken the way this man speaks."

47 The Pharisees responded to them, "Have you also been led astray?"

48 Have any of the leaders or the Pharisees put their faith in him?

49 But these people, who do not understand the law, are under a curse.

50 Nicodemus, who had come to Jesus at night and was one of their own, said to them,

51 Does our law condemn anyone without first hearing their case and understanding what they have done?

52 They replied to him, "Are you also from Galilee? Look into it and see for yourself: no prophet has ever come from Galilee."

53 And each person returned to their own home.
