Matthew Chapter 24

1 Jesus left and walked away from the temple. His disciples came to him, pointing out the temple buildings to him.

2 And Jesus said to them, "Do you not see all these things? Truly I tell you, not one stone here will remain standing on another; every one will be torn down."

3 As Jesus was sitting on the Mount of Olives, his disciples came to him privately and asked, "Tell us, when will these things happen? And what will be the sign of your coming and of the end of the age?"

4 And Jesus responded, saying to them, "Be careful that no one leads you astray."

5 Many will come claiming to represent me, declaring, 'I am the Messiah,' and they will lead many astray.

6 You will hear about wars and reports of wars; do not be alarmed, for these things must happen, but the end is not yet.

7 Nations will rise up against other nations, and kingdoms will wage war against other kingdoms. There will be widespread famines, deadly diseases, and earthquakes in various places.

8 All these events mark the start of the troubles to come.

9 At that time, they will hand you over to suffer persecution and will put you to death. You will be hated by all nations because of your allegiance to my name.

10 At that time, many will fall away, turn against each other, and harbor hatred for one another.

11 And many false prophets will arise and lead many astray.

12 And because lawlessness will increase, the love of many will grow cold.

13 However, the one who remains steadfast and perseveres until the end will be delivered.

14 This message of the kingdom will be proclaimed throughout the entire world as a testimony to all nations, and then the end will come.

15 When you see the abomination that causes desolation, which was spoken of by the prophet Daniel, standing in the holy place—let the reader understand—

16 Then those who are in Judea must escape to the mountains.

17 Let the one who is on the rooftop not come down to retrieve anything from their house.

18 Do not let the one who is in the field turn back to retrieve their cloak.

19 And great distress will come upon those who are pregnant and those who are nursing infants in those days!

20 But you should pray that your escape will not happen in winter or on the Sabbath day.

21 For at that time there will be great distress, unlike anything that has happened from the beginning of the world until now, nor will there ever be anything like it again.

22 If those days were not cut short, no one would survive. But for the sake of the chosen ones, those days will be shortened.

23 If anyone tells you, "Look, the Messiah is here!" or "He is over there!" do not believe it.

24 For false messiahs and false prophets will rise up and perform great signs and miracles, so convincing that, if it were possible, even the chosen ones would be led astray.

25 Listen carefully, I have already warned you about this beforehand.

26 Therefore, if they say to you, “Look, he is in the wilderness,” do not go out; or, “Look, he is in the inner rooms,” do not believe it.

27 For just as lightning flashes from the east and is visible as far as the west, so will the coming of the Son of Man be.

28 Wherever the dead body lies, there the vultures will gather.

29 Right after the suffering of those days, the sun will grow dark, the moon will no longer shine, the stars will fall from the sky, and the forces of the heavens will be shaken.

30 Then the sign of the Son of Man will appear in the sky, and all the peoples of the earth will mourn. They will see the Son of Man coming on the clouds of heaven with great power and glory.

31 He will send out his angels with the loud blast of a trumpet, and they will gather his chosen ones from the four directions, from one extremity of the sky to the other.

32 Now, consider this lesson from the fig tree: when its branch becomes tender and sprouts leaves, you know that summer is near.

33 In the same way, when you see all these things happening, understand that it is near, right at the threshold.

34 Truly I tell you, this generation will not pass away until all these things have taken place.

35 The heavens and the earth will perish, but my words will never perish.

36 Concerning that specific day and hour, no one knows—not even the angels in heaven—but only my Father.

37 Just as it was in the days of Noah, so will it be at the coming of the Son of Man.

38 Just as in the days before the flood, people were eating and drinking, marrying and being given in marriage, right up until the day Noah entered the ark,

39 They were completely unaware until the flood came and swept them all away. In the same way, the coming of the Son of Man will be.

40 At that time, two people will be working in the field; one will be taken, and the other will be left behind.

41 Two women will be grinding grain at the mill; one will be taken, and the other will be left behind.

42 Therefore, stay alert, because you do not know the specific time when your Lord will arrive.

43 But understand this: if the owner of the house had known at what time of night the thief was coming, he would have stayed alert and would not have allowed his house to be broken into.

44 Therefore, you must also be prepared, for the Son of Man will come at an hour when you do not expect Him.

45 Who, then, is a trustworthy and prudent servant, whom his master has placed in charge of his household to provide them with food at the proper time?

46 Blessed is that servant whom his master, upon returning, finds faithfully carrying out his duties.

47 Truly, I tell you, he will appoint him as the overseer of all his possessions.

48 But if that wicked servant says to himself, "My master is taking a long time to return,"

49 And he will start to beat his fellow servants and to feast and drink with those who are drunk.

50 The master of that servant will arrive on a day when he is not expecting him and at an hour he does not anticipate.

51 He will be cut to pieces and assigned a place with the hypocrites, where there will be weeping and grinding of teeth.
