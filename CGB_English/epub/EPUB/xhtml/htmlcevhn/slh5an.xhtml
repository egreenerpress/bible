Titus Chapter 2

1 But you must teach what aligns with wholesome and healthy instruction.

2 Older men should be clear-minded, dignified, self-controlled, and strong in faith, love, and endurance.

3 Older women, in the same way, should conduct themselves in a manner worthy of their sacred calling. They must not engage in slander or be enslaved to excessive drinking, but should instead model what is good and teach others to do the same.

4 They should instruct the younger women to be self-controlled, to love their husbands, to love their children,

5 To act with wisdom and self-control, to be pure, to manage their households well, to be kind, and to submit to their own husbands, so that the word of God may not be dishonored.

6 Encourage young men in the same way to exercise self-control and maintain sound judgment.

7 In every way, demonstrate yourself as an example of good deeds. In your teaching, show integrity, dignity, and sincerity.

8 Speak in a manner that is wholesome and beyond reproach, so that anyone who opposes you may be put to shame, finding no grounds to accuse you of wrongdoing.

9 Encourage servants to be obedient to their own masters, striving to please them in every way, without talking back or being argumentative.

10 Do not steal, but instead demonstrate complete honesty and integrity, so that in every way they may bring honor to the teaching about God our Savior.

11 The grace of God, which brings salvation, has been revealed to all people.

12 This passage instructs us to reject ungodliness and worldly desires, and instead to live with self-control, righteousness, and devotion to God in the present age.

13 We eagerly await the blessed hope and the glorious manifestation of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ.

14 He gave himself for us, so that he might rescue us from all wickedness and cleanse us to be his own special people, devoted to doing good works.

15 Speak these things, encourage others, and correct them with full authority. Do not let anyone disregard or disrespect you.
