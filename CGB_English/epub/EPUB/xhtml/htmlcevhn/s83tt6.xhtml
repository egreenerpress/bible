James Chapter 1

1 James, a servant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, to the twelve tribes dispersed among the nations: Greetings.

2 My brothers and sisters, consider it pure joy whenever you face trials of various kinds.

3 Understand this: the testing of your faith produces endurance.

4 But allow perseverance to complete its work, so that you may become mature and complete, lacking in nothing.

5 If any of you lacks wisdom, let them ask God, who gives generously to all without finding fault, and it will be given to them.

6 However, when someone asks, they must do so with unwavering faith. For the one who doubts is like a wave of the sea, blown and tossed by the wind.

7 Do not let that person believe they will receive anything from the Lord.

8 A person who is divided in their thoughts and loyalties is unreliable and inconsistent in everything they do.

9 Let the humble brother rejoice in being lifted up.

10 However, the wealthy will be humbled, for like the flower of the grass, they will fade and vanish.

11 As soon as the sun rises with its scorching heat, it causes the grass to wither, its flower to fall, and the beauty of its appearance to fade away. In the same way, the rich person will fade away in the midst of their pursuits.

12 Blessed is the person who perseveres through trials, for when they have stood the test, they will receive the crown of life that the Lord has promised to those who love Him.

13 No one should say, when they are tempted, “I am being tempted by God,” for God cannot be tempted by evil, nor does he tempt anyone.

14 However, each person is tempted when they are lured away and enticed by their own desires.

15 When desire takes hold and is allowed to grow, it gives birth to sin. And when sin is fully developed, it produces death.

16 Do not be deceived or led astray, my dearly loved brothers and sisters.

17 Every good and perfect gift comes from above, descending from the Father of heavenly lights, who does not change like shifting shadows or experience any variation.

18 By his own deliberate choice, he brought us into being through the message of truth, so that we might become, as it were, the first and most significant evidence of his new creation.

19 Therefore, my dearly loved brothers and sisters, let each of you be quick to listen, slow to speak, and slow to become angry.

20 For human anger does not produce the righteousness that God desires.

21 Therefore, rid yourselves of all moral impurity and excessive wickedness, and humbly accept the implanted word, which has the power to save your souls.

22 However, you must not merely listen to the word, but put it into practice. If you only listen and do not act, you are deceiving yourselves.

23 For if someone listens to the word but does not put it into practice, they are like a person who looks at their own face in a mirror:

24 For when he looks at himself and then goes away, he immediately forgets what kind of person he was.

25 However, whoever carefully examines the perfect law that brings freedom and remains committed to it, not merely listening and forgetting but actively putting it into practice—such a person will be blessed in what they do.

26 If anyone among you appears to be devout but does not control his tongue, deceiving his own heart, that person's devotion is worthless.

27 Genuine and faultless religion in the sight of God the Father is this: to care for orphans and widows in their distress and to remain unstained by the corrupt influences of the world.
