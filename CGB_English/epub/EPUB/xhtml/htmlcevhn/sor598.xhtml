Genesis Chapter 29

1 Then Jacob continued on his journey and arrived in the land of the eastern peoples.

2 He looked and saw a well in the field, and there were three flocks of sheep lying beside it, for the flocks were watered from that well. A large stone covered the opening of the well.

3 All the flocks were gathered there, and they would roll the stone away from the mouth of the well to water the sheep. Afterward, they would return the stone to its place over the well's opening.

4 Jacob asked them, "My friends, where are you from?" They replied, "We are from Haran."

5 And he asked them, "Do you know Laban, the son of Nahor?" They replied, "Yes, we know him."

6 He asked them, "Is he doing well?" They replied, "He is well, and look, Rachel his daughter is coming with the sheep."

7 He said, "Look, it is still the middle of the day, and it is not yet time to gather the livestock. Water the sheep, and then take them out to graze."

8 They replied, "We cannot do that until all the flocks are gathered here and the stone is rolled away from the mouth of the well. Only then will we water the sheep."

9 While he was still speaking with them, Rachel arrived with her father’s sheep, for she was the one who tended them.

10 When Jacob saw Rachel, the daughter of Laban, his mother’s brother, along with Laban’s flock of sheep, he stepped forward and rolled the stone away from the mouth of the well. Then he watered the flock belonging to Laban, his mother’s brother.

11 Jacob kissed Rachel, then raised his voice and wept.

12 Jacob explained to Rachel that he was her father’s relative and the son of Rebekah. Upon hearing this, she hurried to inform her father.

13 When Laban heard the news about Jacob, his sister’s son, he hurried out to meet him. He embraced Jacob, kissed him, and brought him to his home. There, Jacob told Laban everything that had happened.

14 Laban said to him, "You are truly my own flesh and blood." So Jacob stayed with him for a full month.

15 Laban said to Jacob, "Just because you are my relative, does that mean you should work for me without pay? Tell me, what should your wages be?"

16 Laban had two daughters: the older one was named Leah, and the younger one was named Rachel.

17 Leah had delicate eyes, but Rachel was strikingly beautiful and had a lovely appearance.

18 Jacob loved Rachel deeply and said, "I will work for you for seven years in exchange for Rachel, your younger daughter."

19 Laban replied, "It is better for me to give her to you than to another man. Stay here with me."

20 Jacob worked for seven years to earn Rachel’s hand in marriage, but to him, it felt like only a few days because of the deep love he had for her.

21 Jacob said to Laban, "Give me my wife, for the time I agreed to work for her has been completed, so that I may be united with her as her husband."

22 Laban assembled all the men of the area and prepared a feast.

23 In the evening, he took his daughter Leah and brought her to him, and he slept with her.

24 Laban gave his daughter Leah a servant named Zilpah to be her maid.

25 The next morning, Jacob discovered that it was Leah. He confronted Laban, saying, "What have you done to me? Did I not work for you in order to marry Rachel? Why have you deceived me like this?"

26 Laban replied, "It is not customary in our land to give the younger in marriage before the firstborn."

27 Complete the week of celebration for this one, and we will give you the other as well, in return for the work you will do for me during another seven years.

28 Jacob did as he was asked and completed the required week of celebration for Leah. Afterward, Laban gave him Rachel, his daughter, to be his wife as well.

29 Laban gave his daughter Rachel a servant named Bilhah to be her attendant.

30 He also went in to Rachel, and he loved Rachel more than Leah. So he served with Laban for another seven years.

31 When the LORD saw that Leah was unloved, He enabled her to conceive, but Rachel remained unable to bear children.

32 Leah became pregnant and gave birth to a son. She named him Reuben, saying, "The LORD has seen my suffering; surely now my husband will love me."

33 She became pregnant again and gave birth to a son. She said, "Because the LORD has heard that I am unloved, He has given me this son as well." So she named him Simeon.

34 She became pregnant again and gave birth to a son. She said, "This time my husband will be united with me, because I have borne him three sons." So he was named Levi.

35 She became pregnant again and gave birth to a son. She said, "This time I will praise the LORD." So she named him Judah. After that, she stopped having children.
