Galatians Chapter 3

1 O foolish Galatians! Who has deceived you and led you astray from obeying the truth? Was it not clear to you how Jesus Christ was vividly portrayed as crucified before your very eyes?

2 I simply want to ask you this: Did you receive the Spirit by doing the works required by the law, or by believing what you heard through faith?

3 Are you so unwise? You began your journey by the power of the Spirit; are you now trying to complete it by relying on human effort?

4 Have you endured so much for nothing? If indeed it was not for nothing.

5 So then, the one who supplies you with the Spirit and works miracles among you—does he do this by your observance of the law, or by your belief in the message you heard?

6 Just as Abraham believed God, and his faith was credited to him as righteousness.

7 Understand this, then: those who live by faith are the true descendants of Abraham.

8 The Scripture, foreseeing that God would declare the Gentiles righteous through faith, proclaimed the good news in advance to Abraham, saying, "Through you, all the nations will be blessed."

9 Therefore, those who live by faith are blessed together with Abraham, who was faithful.

10 All who rely on the works of the law are under a curse, for it is written: "Cursed is everyone who does not continue to do everything written in the book of the law."

11 It is clear that no one is made right with God by following the law, as Scripture declares: "The righteous will live by faith."

12 The law is not based on faith; rather, it is as it is written: "The one who does these things will live by them."

13 Christ has freed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse in our place, as it is written: "Cursed is everyone who is hung on a tree."

14 So that the blessing given to Abraham might extend to the Gentiles through Jesus Christ, and that we might receive the promised Spirit by faith.

15 Brothers and sisters, I am speaking in human terms: Even when it is only a human covenant, once it is established, no one can cancel it or add to it.

16 The promises were given to Abraham and his descendant. It does not say, "and to descendants," referring to many, but rather, "and to your descendant," referring to one—who is Christ.

17 And this is what I mean: the covenant that God previously established and confirmed in Christ cannot be nullified by the law, which came four hundred and thirty years later, so as to invalidate the promise.

18 For if the inheritance comes through the law, it no longer depends on the promise; but God granted it to Abraham through the promise.

19 Why, then, was the law given? It was established because of transgressions, serving as a temporary measure until the arrival of the promised descendant to whom the promise was made. The law was enacted through the agency of angels and entrusted to a mediator.

20 A mediator does not represent just one party alone, but God is one.

21 Does the law, then, oppose the promises of God? Certainly not! For if a law had been given that could bring life, then righteousness would indeed have come through the law.

22 The Scripture has declared that all are under the power of sin, so that the promise grounded in faith in Jesus Christ might be given to those who believe.

23 Before the arrival of faith, we were held in custody under the law, confined until the coming faith was to be revealed.

24 Therefore, the law served as our guardian to lead us to Christ, so that we might be made right with God through faith.

25 But now that faith has come, we are no longer under the supervision of a guardian.

26 For you are all children of God through your faith in Christ Jesus.

27 All of you who have been baptized into Christ have clothed yourselves with Christ.

28 In Christ Jesus, there is no distinction between Jew and Gentile, slave and free, male and female, for you are all united as one.

29 If you belong to Christ, then you are Abraham's descendants and heirs according to the promise.
