Psalms Chapter 81

1 (For the music director, to be played on the Gittith; a psalm of Asaph.) Sing joyfully to God, our source of strength; shout triumphantly to the God of Jacob!

2 Take up a song of praise, and bring here the tambourine, the delightful lyre along with the harp.

3 Sound the trumpet at the new moon, at the appointed time, on the day of our sacred festival.

4 This was established as a decree for Israel, a binding ordinance from the God of Jacob.

5 God established this as a decree in Joseph's time, as a lasting testimony, when he went out to rule over the land of Egypt. There I encountered a language I did not understand.

6 I relieved him from the burden on his shoulder; his hands were freed from carrying heavy loads.

7 You called out in distress, and I rescued you; I responded to you from the hidden place of thunder. I tested you at the waters of Meribah. Pause and reflect.

8 Listen, my people, and I will speak to you; O Israel, if you will only pay attention to me.

9 You must not allow any foreign god to dwell within you, nor shall you bow down in worship to any alien deity.

10 I am the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt. Open your mouth wide, and I will fill it.

11 But my people refused to listen to my voice; Israel would have nothing to do with me.

12 Therefore, I allowed them to follow the desires of their own hearts, and they pursued their own plans.

13 If only my people had listened to me, and Israel had followed my ways!

14 I would have quickly brought their enemies under control and directed my power against those who opposed them.

15 Those who oppose the LORD should have humbled themselves before Him, for then their prosperity would have lasted forever.

16 He would have provided them with the finest wheat, and with honey from the rock I would have fully satisfied you.
