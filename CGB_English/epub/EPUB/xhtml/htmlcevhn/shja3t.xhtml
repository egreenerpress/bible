Ezra Chapter 10

1 After Ezra had prayed and confessed, weeping and prostrating himself before the house of God, a very large crowd of men, women, and children from Israel gathered around him. The people were deeply moved and wept bitterly.

2 Shekaniah son of Jehiel, one of the descendants of Elam, responded to Ezra, saying, "We have sinned against our God by marrying foreign women from the peoples of the land. Yet even in this, there is hope for Israel.

3 Therefore, let us now establish a solemn agreement with our God to send away all the foreign wives and the children born to them, in accordance with the advice of my lord and of those who deeply reverence the commandments of our God. Let this be carried out in full compliance with the law.

4 "Get up, for this matter is your responsibility. We will stand with you. Be strong and courageous, and take action."

5 Then Ezra stood up and required the chief priests, the Levites, and all the people of Israel to take an oath, pledging that they would act in accordance with this declaration. And they took the oath.

6 Then Ezra stood up from his place in front of the house of God and went to the room of Johanan, the son of Eliashib. When he arrived there, he refused to eat bread or drink water, for he was deeply grieved over the unfaithfulness of those who had returned from exile.

7 They issued a public announcement throughout Judah and Jerusalem, calling all the exiles to assemble in Jerusalem.

8 Furthermore, it was decreed that anyone who did not present themselves within three days, as advised by the leaders and elders, would have all their possessions confiscated, and they themselves would be excluded from the community of those who had returned from exile.

9 Then all the men of Judah and Benjamin assembled in Jerusalem within three days. It was the ninth month, on the twentieth day of the month, and all the people sat in the open square of the house of God, deeply troubled by the situation and because of the heavy rain.

10 Ezra the priest rose and addressed them, saying, "You have acted unfaithfully by marrying foreign women, thereby adding to the guilt of Israel."

11 Now, therefore, confess your sins to the LORD, the God of your ancestors, and do what pleases him. Separate yourselves from the people of the land and from the foreign wives you have taken.

12 Then the entire assembly responded with a loud voice, saying, "What you have declared, we must do."

13 The crowd is large, and it is the rainy season, so we cannot remain outside. Moreover, this task cannot be completed in just a day or two, for many of us have been involved in this wrongdoing.

14 Let our leaders from the entire assembly now come forward, and let all those who have married foreign women in our towns present themselves at designated times, accompanied by the elders and judges of each city, until the intense anger of our God over this matter is turned away from us.

15 Only Jonathan, the son of Asahel, and Jahaziah, the son of Tikvah, were assigned to oversee this matter. Meshullam and Shabbethai the Levite assisted them in their task.

16 The exiles did as instructed. Ezra the priest, along with certain leaders from the ancestral families, each identified by name, set themselves apart. On the first day of the tenth month, they gathered to carefully investigate the matter.

17 And they completed the process of dealing with all the men who had married foreign wives by the first day of the first month.

18 Among the descendants of the priests, it was discovered that some had married foreign women. Specifically, these were from the sons of Jeshua, the son of Jozadak, and his brothers: Maaseiah, Eliezer, Jarib, and Gedaliah.

19 They pledged by giving their word that they would divorce their foreign wives. Recognizing their guilt, they presented a ram from the flock as a guilt offering for their wrongdoing.

20 And from the descendants of Immer: Hanani and Zebadiah.

21 From the descendants of Harim: Maaseiah, Elijah, Shemaiah, Jehiel, and Uzziah.

22 From the descendants of Pashur: Elioenai, Maaseiah, Ishmael, Nethaneel, Jozabad, and Elasah.

23 Also from the Levites: Jozabad, Shimei, Kelaiah (who is also called Kelita), Pethahiah, Judah, and Eliezer.

24 Among the singers, there was Eliashib. Among the gatekeepers, there were Shallum, Telem, and Uri.

25 Furthermore, from the people of Israel: from the descendants of Parosh, there were Ramiah, Jeziah, Malchiah, Miamin, Eleazar, Malchijah, and Benaiah.

26 From the descendants of Elam: Mattaniah, Zechariah, Jehiel, Abdi, Jeremoth, and Eliah.

27 From the descendants of Zattu: Elioenai, Eliashib, Mattaniah, Jeremoth, Zabad, and Aziza.

28 From the descendants of Bebai: Jehohanan, Hananiah, Zabbai, and Athlai.

29 And from the descendants of Bani: Meshullam, Malluch, Adaiah, Jashub, Sheal, and Ramoth.

30 From the descendants of Pahath-moab: Adna, Chelal, Benaiah, Maaseiah, Mattaniah, Bezalel, Binnui, and Manasseh.

31 From the descendants of Harim: Eliezer, Ishijah, Malchiah, Shemaiah, and Shimeon.

32 Benjamin, Malluch, and Shemariah.

33 From the descendants of Hashum: Mattenai, Mattathah, Zabad, Eliphelet, Jeremai, Manasseh, and Shimei.

34 From the descendants of Bani: Maadai, Amram, and Uel.

35 Benaiah, Bedeiah, Chelluh.

36 Vaniah, Meremoth, Eliashib,

37 Mattaniah, Mattenai, and Jaasau.

38 "And Bani, Binnui, and Shimei,"

39 And Shelemiah, Nathan, and Adaiah...

40 Machnadebai, Shashai, Sharai,

41 Azareel, Shelemiah, and Shemariah.

42 Shallum, Amariah, and Joseph.

43 From the descendants of Nebo: Jeiel, Mattithiah, Zabad, Zebina, Jadau, Joel, and Benaiah.

44 All these men had married foreign women, and some of them had children by these wives.
