Isaiah Chapter 24

1 Look, the LORD is making the earth desolate, laying it waste, overturning it completely, and scattering its inhabitants far and wide.

2 The outcome will be the same for everyone: the people and the priest, the servant and the master, the maid and her mistress, the buyer and the seller, the lender and the borrower, the one who charges interest and the one who pays it. All will face the same fate, without distinction.

3 The land will be completely desolated and utterly laid waste, for the LORD has declared this word.

4 The earth mourns and withers away, the world grows weak and fades, the proud and lofty people of the earth waste away.

5 The earth has been polluted because of its inhabitants; for they have violated the laws, disregarded the statutes, and broken the eternal covenant.

6 As a result, the curse has consumed the earth, and those who live on it are left in ruin. Because of this, the people of the earth have been scorched, and only a small number of individuals remain.

7 The fresh wine dries up, the vine withers, and all who were once joyful now sigh in despair.

8 The joyful sound of tambourines has faded; the clamor of those who celebrate has come to an end; the gladness of the harp has ceased.

9 They will not enjoy wine with singing; strong drink will taste bitter to those who consume it.

10 The chaotic city lies in ruins; every house is sealed shut, so that no one can enter.

11 In the streets, people cry out for wine; all joy has turned to gloom, and the land's gladness has vanished.

12 The city lies in ruins, desolate and abandoned, and its gates are shattered and destroyed.

13 When this occurs in the midst of the land among the people, it will be like the shaking of an olive tree, where only a few olives remain, or like the gathering of leftover grapes after the harvest is finished.

14 They will raise their voices and sing in praise of the LORDâ€™s majesty; they will shout joyfully from the sea.

15 Therefore, honor the LORD in the midst of trials, even the name of the LORD, the God of Israel, in the distant coastlands and islands of the sea.

16 From the farthest corners of the earth, we have heard songs of praise, giving glory to the righteous. But I said, "I am wasting away, I am wasting away! Woe is me! The deceitful have acted deceitfully; indeed, the treacherous have dealt with great treachery."

17 Terror, the pit, and the trap are coming upon you, O inhabitant of the earth.

18 This is what will happen: Those who run away from the terrifying noise will fall into a pit, and those who climb out of the pit will be caught in a trap. For the floodgates of heaven are opened, and the very foundations of the earth tremble.

19 The earth is completely shattered, the earth is thoroughly broken apart, the earth is violently shaken.

20 The earth will stagger back and forth like a drunkard, and it will sway like a fragile hut. Its rebellion will weigh heavily upon it, and it will collapse, never to rise again.

21 And it will happen on that day that the LORD will bring judgment upon the armies of the exalted ones in the heavens and the rulers of the earth below.

22 They will be gathered together like prisoners in a dungeon; they will be confined in a prison and held there for many days. After a long time, they will be brought to account.

23 Then the moon will be dismayed, and the sun will be put to shame, for the LORD of hosts will reign on Mount Zion and in Jerusalem, and in the presence of His elders, He will manifest His glory.
