Lamentations Chapter 3

1 I am the one who has experienced suffering under the rod of His anger.

2 He has guided me and led me into darkness, but not into light.

3 Certainly, He has turned against me; He continually directs His hand against me all day long.

4 He has caused my flesh and my skin to waste away; he has shattered my bones.

5 He has built up obstacles against me and surrounded me with bitterness and hardship.

6 He has placed me in dark, desolate places, like those who have long been dead.

7 He has surrounded me with barriers, so that I cannot escape; he has made my burden heavy.

8 Even when I cry out and plead, he blocks my prayer.

9 He has blocked my paths with cut stone; he has made my ways twisted and difficult.

10 He was like a bear lying in ambush against me, like a lion hiding in secret places.

11 He has diverted my path and shattered me; he has left me in ruin and desolation.

12 He has drawn his bow and made me the target for his arrow.

13 He has made the arrows from his quiver pierce deep into my innermost being.

14 I became an object of mockery to all my people; they sing about me all day long.

15 He has overwhelmed me with bitterness; he has saturated me with the bitterness of wormwood.

16 He has crushed my teeth with gravel; he has covered me with ashes.

17 You have driven my soul far from peace; I have forgotten what prosperity feels like.

18 And I said, "My strength has failed, and my hope in the LORD has vanished."

19 I recall my suffering and distress, the bitterness and anguish I endured.

20 My soul continually remembers these things and is deeply humbled within me.

21 This I bring to mind, and therefore I find hope.

22 We are not utterly destroyed because of the Lordâ€™s steadfast love, for His mercies never come to an end.

23 "Your mercies are renewed each morning; your faithfulness is boundless."

24 The LORD is my allotted portion, declares my soul; therefore, I will place my hope in Him.

25 The LORD is good to those who wait for him, to the one who earnestly seeks him.

26 It is beneficial for a person to maintain hope and patiently await the deliverance of the LORD.

27 It is beneficial for a person to endure hardship and responsibility while they are young.

28 He sits alone and remains silent, for he has endured it upon himself.

29 He humbles himself completely, bowing low to the ground, in the hope that there may yet be a chance for deliverance.

30 He offers his cheek to the one who strikes him; he endures insults to the fullest extent.

31 The Lord will not reject His people forever.

32 Though He may bring sorrow, He will show compassion in accordance with the abundance of His steadfast love.

33 For the Lord does not willingly bring suffering or cause grief to the children of humanity.

34 To trample beneath his feet all those who are held captive in the world.

35 To deny a person their rightful justice in the presence of the Most High.

36 The Lord does not approve of undermining a person's case or perverting justice in their legal matter.

37 Who is it that speaks and makes something happen, unless the Lord has commanded it?

38 Does not both adversity and prosperity come from the mouth of the Most High?

39 Why should a living person complain, a man about the consequences of his sins?

40 Let us carefully examine and test our actions, and return wholeheartedly to the LORD.

41 Let us raise our hearts and hands to God in heaven.

42 We have sinned and rebelled against you, yet you have not forgiven us.

43 You have overwhelmed us with your anger and pursued us relentlessly; you have destroyed us without showing any compassion.

44 You have wrapped yourself in a cloud, so that our prayers cannot reach you.

45 You have made us like the filth and garbage among the nations.

46 All our adversaries have spoken out against us.

47 Terror and a trap have overtaken us, devastation and ruin are upon us.

48 My eyes pour out streams of tears because of the devastation that has come upon the daughter of my people.

49 My eyes pour out tears without stopping; there is no relief or pause.

50 Until the LORD looks down from heaven and takes notice.

51 My eyes grieve my heart because of all the young women of my city.

52 My enemies pursued me relentlessly, like hunters chasing a defenseless bird, though I had done nothing to deserve it.

53 They have brought my life to an end in the pit, and they have thrown a stone over me.

54 The waters rose above my head, and I thought, "I am finished; I am lost."

55 I cried out to you, O LORD, from the depths of despair.

56 You have heard my voice; do not turn your ear away from my plea, from my cry for help.

57 You came close to me on the day I called out to you; you said, "Do not be afraid."

58 O Lord, you have defended my cause; you have rescued and preserved my life.

59 O LORD, you have witnessed the injustice done to me; please uphold my case and bring about justice.

60 You have witnessed all their acts of vengeance and every scheme they have devised against me.

61 You have heard their insults, O LORD, and all their schemes against me.

62 The words spoken by those who oppose me, and their schemes against me throughout the day.

63 Observe their every action, whether they sit or rise; I am the focus of their thoughts and the source of their joy.

64 Give them, O LORD, the repayment they deserve according to what their actions have earned.

65 Grant them deep anguish of heart, and let your curse fall upon them.

66 Pursue them and bring about their destruction in your righteous anger, wiping them out completely from beneath the heavens that belong to the LORD.
