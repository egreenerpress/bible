Psalms Chapter 64

1 (For the music director, a psalm of David.) O God, hear my voice as I pray; protect my life from the terror of my enemies.

2 Protect me from the hidden schemes of the wicked; shield me from the rebellion of those who practice evil.

3 They sharpen their tongues like swords and aim their words like arrows, ready to shoot bitter and hurtful speech.

4 They lie in wait to ambush the innocent, attacking them suddenly without fear.

5 They strengthen themselves in their wicked schemes; they discuss secretly setting traps, saying, "Who will notice what we do?"

6 They thoroughly investigate wrongdoing; they carry out an exhaustive search. Both the innermost thoughts of each one of them and their hearts are profoundly corrupt.

7 But God will shoot them with His arrow; in an instant, they will be struck down.

8 Therefore, their own words will bring about their downfall; all who witness it will flee from them in horror.

9 All people will stand in awe and proclaim the works of God, for they will carefully reflect on His deeds.

10 The righteous will rejoice in the LORD and place their trust in Him; all who are upright in heart will take pride and find their glory in Him.
