Genesis Chapter 8

1 Then God remembered Noah, along with all the living creatures and the livestock that were with him in the ark. God caused a wind to sweep over the earth, and the floodwaters began to recede.

2 The sources of the great deep were sealed, and the floodgates of the heavens were closed. The rain pouring down from the sky ceased.

3 The waters gradually receded from the earth, and after the 150 days had passed, the waters had diminished.

4 The ark came to rest in the seventh month, on the seventeenth day of that month, on the mountains of Ararat.

5 The floodwaters gradually receded until the tenth month. On the first day of that month, the peaks of the mountains became visible.

6 After forty days had passed, Noah opened the window he had built in the ark.

7 Then he released a raven, which flew back and forth until the waters had completely receded from the earth.

8 Then he sent out a dove to see if the waters had receded from the surface of the earth.

9 However, the dove could find no place to rest the sole of her foot, so she returned to him in the ark, for the waters still covered the entire surface of the earth. Then Noah reached out his hand, took hold of her, and brought her back into the ark with him.

10 And he waited another seven days; then he sent the dove out from the ark once more.

11 In the evening, the dove returned to Noah, and behold, it had a freshly plucked olive leaf in its beak. From this, Noah understood that the floodwaters had receded from the earth.

12 Noah waited another seven days and then sent out the dove once more. This time, the dove did not return to him again.

13 In the six hundred and first year, on the first day of the first month, the waters had receded from the earth. Noah then removed the covering of the ark and looked out, and he saw that the surface of the ground was dry.

14 In the second month, on the twenty-seventh day of the month, the earth was completely dry.

15 And God spoke to Noah, saying,

16 "Come out of the ark, you, your wife, your sons, and your sons' wives with you."

17 Take with you every living creature that is with you—all animals, birds, and every creature that moves on the ground—so that they may thrive and increase in number, filling the earth and multiplying abundantly.

18 Noah emerged from the ark, accompanied by his sons, his wife, and his sons’ wives.

19 Every kind of animal, every crawling creature, and every bird—all that move on the earth according to their kinds—came out of the ark.

20 Noah built an altar to the LORD and took some of every clean animal and every clean bird, then presented them as burnt offerings on the altar.

21 The LORD was pleased with the pleasing aroma, and He said to Himself, "I will never again curse the ground because of humankind, even though the inclination of the human heart is evil from youth onward. Nor will I ever again destroy every living creature as I have done."

22 As long as the earth endures, the cycles of planting and harvest, cold and heat, summer and winter, and day and night will never come to an end.
