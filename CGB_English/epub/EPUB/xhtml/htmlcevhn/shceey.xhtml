Matthew Chapter 16

1 The Pharisees, along with the Sadducees, approached Jesus and, testing him, asked that he show them a sign from heaven.

2 He replied to them, "When evening comes, you say, 'The weather will be clear, because the sky is red.'"

3 In the morning, you say, "It will be stormy today, for the sky is red and overcast." You hypocrites! You know how to interpret the appearance of the sky, but you cannot recognize the signs of this present time.

4 A corrupt and unfaithful generation demands a miraculous sign, but no sign will be given to it except the sign of the prophet Jonah. Then He left them and went away.

5 When the disciples reached the other side of the lake, they realized they had forgotten to bring any bread with them.

6 Then Jesus said to them, "Be careful and guard yourselves against the influence of the Pharisees and the Sadducees."

7 And they discussed the matter among themselves, saying, "This is because we did not bring any bread."

8 When Jesus became aware of this, he said to them, "You who have such little faith, why are you discussing among yourselves the fact that you have no bread?"

9 Do you still not understand? Have you forgotten the five loaves that fed the five thousand, and how many baskets of leftovers you gathered afterward?

10 "Don’t you remember the seven loaves that fed the four thousand, and how many baskets of leftovers you gathered afterward?"

11 Why do you not grasp that I was not speaking to you about literal bread when I told you to beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and the Sadducees?

12 Then they realized that he was not warning them to be cautious about the leaven used in bread, but about the teaching of the Pharisees and the Sadducees.

13 When Jesus arrived in the region of Caesarea Philippi, he asked his disciples, "Who do people say that I, the Son of Man, am?"

14 They replied, "Some say you are John the Baptist; others say Elijah; and still others say Jeremiah or one of the prophets."

15 He asked them, "But who do you say that I am?"

16 Simon Peter replied, "You are the Messiah, the Son of the living God."

17 Jesus responded and said to him, "You are blessed, Simon son of Jonah, because this was not revealed to you by human means, but by my Father who is in heaven."

18 And I also say to you, you are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church; and the powers of death will not overcome it.

19 I will give you the authority of the kingdom of heaven. Whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you release on earth will be released in heaven.

20 Then Jesus instructed his disciples not to tell anyone that he was the Messiah.

21 From that time on, Jesus began to explain to his disciples that he must go to Jerusalem, endure great suffering at the hands of the elders, chief priests, and teachers of the law, be put to death, and then be raised to life on the third day.

22 Then Peter took Him aside and began to strongly object, saying, “May this never happen to You, Lord! This must not occur!”

23 But Jesus turned and said to Peter, "Get behind me, Satan! You are a stumbling block to me because your thoughts are not aligned with God’s purposes, but with human concerns."

24 Then Jesus said to his disciples, "If anyone desires to follow me, let them renounce their own self-interest, take up their cross, and follow me."

25 For whoever seeks to save their own life will ultimately lose it, but whoever is willing to give up their life for my sake will truly find it.

26 What benefit does a person gain if they acquire the entire world but forfeit their own soul? Or what can a person offer in return for their soul?

27 For the Son of Man will come in the splendor of his Father, accompanied by his angels, and at that time, he will repay each person according to their deeds.

28 Truly, I tell you, there are some standing here who will not experience death before they see the Son of Man coming in his kingdom.
