Isaiah Chapter 6

1 In the year that King Uzziah died, I saw the Lord seated on a throne, exalted and lofty, and the hem of His robe filled the temple.

2 Above it stood the seraphim, each having six wings: with two wings they covered their faces, with two wings they covered their feet, and with two wings they flew.

3 One called out to another, saying, "Holy, holy, holy is the LORD of hosts; the entire earth is filled with His glory."

4 The doorposts shook at the sound of the one who called out, and the temple was filled with smoke.

5 Then I said, "How terrible it is for me! I am ruined! For I am a man with unclean lips, and I live among a people who also have unclean lips. Yet my eyes have seen the King, the LORD of armies!"

6 Then one of the seraphim flew to me, holding in his hand a burning coal that he had taken with tongs from the altar.

7 He placed it upon my mouth and said, "Behold, this has touched your lips; your guilt is removed, and your sin is atoned for."

8 I also heard the voice of the Lord, saying, "Whom shall I send, and who will go on our behalf?" Then I said, "Here I am; send me."

9 He said, "Go and tell this people: 'You will indeed hear, but you will not understand; you will indeed see, but you will not perceive.'"

10 Make the hearts of this people unresponsive, and dull their hearing, and close their eyes; otherwise, they might see with their eyes, hear with their ears, understand with their hearts, turn back, and be healed.

11 Then I asked, "Lord, how long will this last?" He replied, "Until the cities lie in ruins, empty of inhabitants, and the houses are abandoned, with no one living in them, and the land becomes completely desolate."

12 The LORD will cause people to be taken far away, and there will be a vast desolation throughout the land.

13 Yet a tenth part will remain in it, and it will return and be consumed. Like a terebinth tree or an oak, which retains its core even when its leaves fall, so the holy seed will be the enduring essence within it.
