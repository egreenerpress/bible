I Thessalonians Chapter 4

1 Moreover, we urge you, brothers and sisters, and appeal to you in the name of the Lord Jesus, that just as you have learned from us how you ought to live in order to please God—as indeed you are doing—you should strive to excel even more.

2 For you are aware of the instructions we gave you through the authority of the Lord Jesus.

3 For this is God’s will—your sanctification—that you should keep yourselves from sexual immorality.

4 Each of you should learn to control your own body in a way that is holy and honorable.

5 Not driven by sinful desires, as the Gentiles who do not know God.

6 Do not wrong or take advantage of anyone in any matter, for the Lord will avenge all such actions, as we have already warned and testified to you.

7 God has not called us to live in impurity, but to pursue holiness.

8 Therefore, whoever rejects this message is not rejecting human authority but God himself, who has also given us his Holy Spirit.

9 Concerning brotherly love, there is no need for me to write to you, for you yourselves have been taught by God to love one another.

10 You are already showing this kind of love toward all the fellow believers throughout Macedonia. Yet we urge you, brothers and sisters, to excel even more in this.

11 Strive to live peacefully, attend to your own affairs, and work diligently with your own hands, just as we instructed you.

12 So that you may live with integrity and honor in the presence of those outside the faith, and so that you may lack nothing you need.

13 I do not want you to be unaware, brothers and sisters, about those who have died, so that you do not grieve as others do who have no hope.

14 For if we believe that Jesus died and rose again, then in the same way, God will bring with Him those who have died in faith through Jesus.

15 This is what we declare to you by the authority of the Lord: those of us who are still alive and remain until the coming of the Lord will not precede those who have already died.

16 For the Lord himself will come down from heaven with a commanding cry, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trumpet call of God. And those who have died in Christ will rise first.

17 Then, those of us who are still alive and remain will be taken up together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air. And in this way, we will always be with the Lord.

18 Therefore, encourage and strengthen one another with these truths.
