I Corinthians Chapter 16

1 Regarding the collection for the believers, as I directed the churches of Galatia, you should do the same.

2 On the first day of the week, each of you should set aside and save something, in proportion to how God has blessed you financially, so that there will be no need for collections when I arrive.

3 When I arrive, I will send whomever you approve through your letters to deliver your generous gift to Jerusalem.

4 And if it is fitting for me to go as well, they will accompany me.

5 I will come to you after I have traveled through Macedonia, for I am indeed planning to journey through Macedonia.

6 It is possible that I will stay and even spend the winter with you, so that you may assist me on my journey, wherever I may go.

7 For I do not intend to see you briefly in passing, but I hope to stay with you for some time, if the Lord allows.

8 However, I will remain in Ephesus until the time of Pentecost.

9 A significant and effective opportunity has been made available to me, yet there are also many who oppose me.

10 If Timothy comes, make sure he is with you without any fear, for he is doing the Lord’s work, just as I am.

11 Therefore, let no one look down on him. Instead, send him on his way in peace so that he may come to me, for I am expecting him along with the other believers.

12 Regarding our brother Apollos, I strongly urged him to come to you with the other believers, but he was not at all willing to come at this time. However, he will come when he has the opportunity.

13 Be alert, stand firm in your faith, act with courage, and be strong.

14 Let everything you do be carried out with love.

15 I urge you, my fellow believers—as you are aware, the household of Stephanas was the first in Achaia to embrace the faith, and they have devoted themselves to serving the saints—

16 You should submit yourselves to such individuals, and to everyone who works alongside us and labors with us.

17 I am pleased by the arrival of Stephanas, Fortunatus, and Achaicus, for they have made up for what was lacking on your part.

18 For they have brought comfort and encouragement to both my spirit and yours. Therefore, recognize and honor those who are like this.

19 The churches in Asia send you their greetings. Aquila and Priscilla warmly greet you in the Lord, along with the church that meets in their home.

20 All the fellow believers send their greetings to you. Greet each other with a sacred kiss.

21 This greeting is written by me, Paul, in my own hand.

22 If anyone does not love the Lord Jesus Christ, let that person be accursed. The Lord is coming.

23 May the gracious favor of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you.

24 May my love be with all of you through Christ Jesus. Amen.
