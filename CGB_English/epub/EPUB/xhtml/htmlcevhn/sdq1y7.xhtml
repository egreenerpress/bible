Psalms Chapter 92

1 (A Psalm or Song for the Sabbath day.) It is good to give thanks to the LORD and to sing praises to your name, O Most High.

2 To proclaim your steadfast love in the morning, and your unwavering faithfulness every night.

3 With a ten-stringed instrument, and with the lyre; with the harp playing a reverent melody.

4 For you, LORD, have filled me with joy through your deeds; I will rejoice in the works of your hands.

5 O LORD, how magnificent are your deeds! Your thoughts are exceedingly profound.

6 A senseless person does not comprehend, nor does a foolish individual grasp this truth.

7 When the wicked sprout like grass, and all who do evil thrive, it is only so that they may be utterly destroyed forever.

8 But you, O LORD, are exalted above all forever.

9 Behold, your enemies, O LORD, will surely perish; all who do evil will be scattered and brought to ruin.

10 But you will lift up my strength like the horn of a wild ox; I will be anointed with fresh oil.

11 My eyes will witness the fulfillment of my longing against my enemies, and my ears will hear the outcome I desire concerning the wicked who rise up against me.

12 The righteous will thrive like a flourishing palm tree; they will grow strong and majestic, like the cedars of Lebanon.

13 Those who are established in the house of the LORD will thrive and prosper in the courts of our God.

14 Even in their old age, they will continue to bear fruit; they will remain full of vitality and flourish abundantly.

15 To demonstrate that the LORD is just: He is my foundation, and there is no wrongdoing in Him.
