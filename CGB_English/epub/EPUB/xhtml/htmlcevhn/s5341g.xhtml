Job Chapter 4

1 Then Eliphaz from Teman responded and said,

2 If we attempt to speak with you, will you be offended? Yet who can keep from speaking when compelled to do so?

3 Look, you have taught many people, and you have given strength to those whose hands were weak.

4 Your words have supported those who were stumbling, and you have given strength to the weak and weary.

5 But now it has come upon you, and you are overwhelmed; it has reached you, and you are deeply distressed.

6 Is not your reverence for God your source of strength, your confidence, your hope, and the integrity of your ways?

7 Consider this, I ask you: Has anyone ever been destroyed while being truly innocent? Or have the upright ever been completely wiped out?

8 Just as I have observed, those who cultivate wrongdoing and plant evil will harvest the same.

9 By the power of Godâ€™s breath, they are destroyed, and by the force of His anger, they are utterly consumed.

10 The mighty roar of the lion, the thunderous voice of the fierce lion, and the sharp teeth of the young lions are shattered and broken.

11 The aged lion dies from lack of prey, and the strong lion's cubs are scattered far and wide.

12 Now something was quietly revealed to me, and my ear caught a faint whisper of it.

13 In the midst of night visions, when deep sleep overtakes people,

14 A deep fear came over me, and trembling seized my entire being, causing all my bones to quake.

15 Then a spirit moved before my face, and the hair on my body stood on end.

16 It remained motionless, but I could not make out its appearance. A form was before my eyes, and there was a profound silence. Then I heard a voice speaking, saying,

17 Can a mere human be more righteous than God? Can a person be more pure than their Creator?

18 Look, God places no confidence in His servants; even His angels He accuses of error.

19 How much more fragile are those who dwell in houses of clay, whose foundation is in the dust, who are crushed more easily than a moth?

20 They are utterly destroyed from morning till evening; they perish forever, with no one giving it a second thought.

21 Does not their splendor, which is within them, fade away? They perish, even without gaining wisdom.
