Hosea Chapter 6

1 Come, let us return to the LORD; for though He has torn us, He will heal us; though He has struck us down, He will bind up our wounds.

2 After two days, he will restore us to life; on the third day, he will raise us up, and we will live in his presence.

3 Then we will truly understand, if we persist in seeking to know the LORD: His presence is as certain as the dawn, and He will come to us like the rain, like the autumn and spring rains that refresh the earth.

4 O Ephraim, what should I do with you? O Judah, what should I do with you? For your loyalty is like a morning cloud, fleeting and transient, like the early dew that vanishes with the rising sun.

5 Therefore, I have disciplined them through the prophets; I have brought them to account by the words I have spoken, and my judgments shine forth like the light.

6 For I desired steadfast love, not mere ritual sacrifice, and the true knowledge of God more than burnt offerings.

7 But they, like Adam, have broken the covenant; they have acted deceitfully and betrayed me.

8 Gilead is a city filled with those who commit wickedness, and it is defiled by bloodshed.

9 Like bands of thieves lying in ambush for travelers, so the group of priests conspire together to kill along the way. They carry out their wicked schemes with full intent, for they are deeply involved in shameful acts.

10 I have witnessed a dreadful thing in the house of Israel: Ephraim has committed spiritual prostitution, and Israel has become defiled.

11 Furthermore, O Judah, He has appointed a time of harvest for you, when I restore the fortunes of my people.
