Philippians Chapter 3

1 Finally, my brothers and sisters, find your joy in the Lord. Writing the same things to you again is not burdensome for me, and for you, it serves as a safeguard.

2 Be cautious of those who are like vicious dogs, watch out for those who do evil, and be on guard against those who mutilate the flesh.

3 For we are the true circumcision—those who worship God by the Spirit, take pride in Christ Jesus, and place no trust in human effort or status.

4 Though I could also place my confidence in human achievements, if anyone else thinks they have reason to trust in their own merits, I have even more.

5 I was circumcised on the eighth day, as the law requires. I am descended from the people of Israel, from the tribe of Benjamin, a true Hebrew of Hebrew heritage. In regard to the law, I was a Pharisee.

6 In terms of zeal, I was a persecutor of the church; as for the righteousness that comes through the law, I was faultless.

7 However, the things that I once considered valuable and advantageous, I now regard as worthless and a loss for the sake of Christ.

8 Certainly, and I consider everything as worthless compared to the surpassing greatness of knowing Christ Jesus my Lord. For His sake, I have willingly let go of all things and regard them as mere rubbish, so that I may gain Christ.

9 And may I be found in Him, not possessing a righteousness of my own that comes from following the law, but rather the righteousness that comes through faith in Christ—the righteousness that God provides, received by faith.

10 My desire is to truly know Him—to experience the power of His resurrection and to share in the fellowship of His sufferings, becoming like Him even in His death.

11 If somehow I might reach the goal of being raised from the dead.

12 I do not claim to have already achieved this or to have already reached perfection, but I press on to take hold of that for which Christ Jesus took hold of me.

13 Brothers and sisters, I do not consider that I have already attained perfection, but I focus on this one thing: leaving behind what is past and striving forward toward what lies ahead.

14 I strive toward the goal to win the prize of God’s heavenly calling in Christ Jesus.

15 Therefore, let all of us who are mature in faith adopt this same perspective. And if in any matter you think differently, God will make this clear to you as well.

16 However, in regard to the progress we have already made, let us continue to live by the same principles and remain united in purpose.

17 Brothers and sisters, join together in following my example, and take note of those who live according to the pattern you have seen in us.

18 Many people live in a way that I have often warned you about, and now I tell you again with deep sorrow—they are enemies of the cross of Christ.

19 Their ultimate fate is destruction, their god is their own desires, and they take pride in what should bring them shame. Their thoughts are focused only on earthly matters.

20 Our citizenship is in heaven, and from there we eagerly await the Savior, the Lord Jesus Christ.

21 Who will transform our lowly body, so that it may be conformed to his glorious body, by the power that enables him to bring all things under his authority.
