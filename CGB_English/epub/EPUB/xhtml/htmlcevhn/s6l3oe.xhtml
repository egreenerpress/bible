Psalms Chapter 32

1 (A Psalm of David, for instruction.) Blessed is the one whose rebellion is pardoned, whose sin is fully atoned for.

2 Blessed is the person to whom the LORD does not count their wrongdoing, and in whose heart there is no deceit.

3 When I remained silent, my bones grew weak and weary from my constant groaning throughout the day.

4 For both day and night, your hand pressed heavily upon me; my strength was drained as if by the scorching heat of summer. Selah.

5 I openly admitted my sin to you; I did not conceal my wrongdoing. I declared, "I will confess my rebellious acts to the LORD," and you forgave the guilt of my sin. Selah.

6 Therefore, everyone who is devoted to God will pray to you when you can be found. Even when overwhelming troubles rise like floodwaters, they will not reach him.

7 You are my refuge; you will protect me from distress; you will surround me with songs of rescue. Selah.

8 I will instruct you and teach you in the path you should follow; I will guide you with my watchful care.

9 Do not be like the horse or the mule, which lack understanding and must be controlled with a bit and bridle to keep them from straying. Otherwise, they will not come near you willingly.

10 The wicked will experience many troubles, but those who place their trust in the LORD will be surrounded by His steadfast love.

11 Rejoice in the LORD and be glad, you who are righteous; let all who are pure in heart shout for joy.
