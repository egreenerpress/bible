II Thessalonians Chapter 1

1 Paul, Silas, and Timothy, to the church of the Thessalonians, who belong to God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ:

2 May grace and peace be yours, given by God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

3 We are obligated to continually give thanks to God for you, brothers and sisters, as is fitting, because your faith is growing abundantly, and the love each one of you has for one another is increasing greatly.

4 We ourselves take pride in you among the churches of God because of your steadfast endurance and faith in the midst of all the persecutions and hardships you are suffering.

5 This is clear evidence of Godâ€™s just judgment, so that you may be considered worthy of the kingdom of God, for which you are also enduring suffering.

6 It is just and right in God's sight to repay with affliction those who cause you distress.

7 And to you who are experiencing distress, there will be rest for you alongside us when the Lord Jesus is revealed from heaven with His powerful angels.

8 He will come with blazing fire to bring judgment on those who do not acknowledge God and on those who refuse to obey the good news of our Lord Jesus Christ.

9 Those who will face eternal destruction, being separated from the presence of the Lord and the majesty of His power.

10 When he comes to be glorified among his holy ones and to be marveled at by all who have believed (since our testimony to you was believed) on that day.

11 Therefore, we continually pray for you, asking that our God may consider you worthy of this calling and bring to completion every good purpose of his goodness and the work of faith with power.

12 May the name of our Lord Jesus Christ be honored and praised through you, and may you, in turn, be honored and united with Him, all in accordance with the gracious gift of our God and the Lord Jesus Christ.
