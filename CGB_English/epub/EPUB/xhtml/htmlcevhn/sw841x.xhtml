Psalms Chapter 89

1 (A contemplative song by Ethan the Ezrahite.) I will sing forever of the Lord’s steadfast love; with my mouth, I will proclaim your faithfulness to every generation.

2 For I have declared, "Lovingkindness will be established forever; your faithfulness will be firmly set in the heavens themselves."

3 I have established a covenant with the one I have chosen; I have made a solemn promise to David, my servant.

4 I will establish your descendants forever and secure your throne for all generations. Selah.

5 The heavens will proclaim your marvelous works, O LORD, and your steadfast faithfulness will be declared in the assembly of your holy ones.

6 Who in all of heaven can compare to the LORD? Who among the mighty ones can be likened to the LORD?

7 God is to be deeply revered in the gathering of His holy people, and He is to be held in awe by all who stand in His presence.

8 O LORD, God of armies, who is a mighty LORD like you? Your faithfulness surrounds you completely.

9 You govern the fury of the sea; when its waves rise up, You calm them.

10 You have shattered Rahab like a defeated foe; you have dispersed your enemies with your mighty power.

11 The heavens belong to You, and the earth is Yours as well; the world and all it contains—You established them.

12 You have created both the north and the south; Mount Tabor and Mount Hermon will joyfully praise your name.

13 You possess a powerful arm; your hand is strong, and your right hand is exalted.

14 Righteousness and justice form the foundation of your throne; steadfast love and faithfulness go before you.

15 Happy are the people who recognize the sound of celebration; they will walk, O LORD, in the radiance of your presence.

16 All day long they will celebrate in your name, and through your righteousness they will be lifted up and honored.

17 For you are the source of their strength and honor, and by your favor, our power and dignity will be lifted high.

18 For the LORD is our protector, and the Holy One of Israel is our ruler.

19 Then You spoke in a vision to Your faithful servant and said, "I have bestowed strength upon a mighty one; I have raised up a chosen leader from among the people."

20 I have chosen David, my servant; with my sacred oil I have anointed him.

21 The one with whom I will firmly establish my authority, and whom I will strengthen with my power.

22 The enemy will not overpower him, nor will the wicked oppress him.

23 I will crush his enemies before him and strike down those who oppose him.

24 But my steadfast love and faithfulness will remain with him, and through my authority, his power and honor will be greatly exalted.

25 I will establish his dominion over the sea, and his authority over the rivers.

26 He will call out to me, saying, "You are my Father, my God, and the steadfast foundation of my deliverance."

27 I will also establish him as my firstborn, elevating him above the kings of the earth.

28 I will maintain my steadfast love for him forever, and my covenant with him will remain unshakable.

29 I will ensure that his descendants continue forever, and his throne will endure as long as the heavens exist.

30 If his children abandon my instructions and fail to live according to my decrees;

31 If they violate my decrees and fail to observe my commands,

32 At that time, I will punish their rebellion with the rod, and their wrongdoing with blows.

33 However, I will not completely withdraw my steadfast love from him, nor will I allow my faithfulness to come to an end.

34 I will not violate my covenant, nor will I change the promise that has come from my lips.

35 I have made a solemn oath by my holiness, and I will not deceive David.

36 His descendants will continue forever, and his throne will remain steadfast like the sun before me.

37 It will be established forever, enduring like the moon, and as a trustworthy witness in the heavens. Selah.

38 But you have rejected and despised [your people]; you have been angry with your chosen one.

39 You have nullified the covenant with your servant; you have defiled his honor by casting his crown to the ground.

40 You have demolished all his defenses; you have reduced his fortresses to ruins.

41 All who pass by plunder him; he has become a disgrace to those around him.

42 You have strengthened the power of his foes; you have caused all his enemies to rejoice.

43 You have also blunted the sharpness of his sword and have not allowed him to stand firm in battle.

44 You have brought an end to his splendor and thrown his throne down to the earth.

45 You have cut short the days of his youth; you have overwhelmed him with disgrace. Selah.

46 How long, O LORD? Will you remain hidden forever? Will your anger continue to burn like fire?

47 Consider how brief my life is; why have you created all humanity for such a fleeting purpose?

48 Who among the living can escape death? Can anyone rescue their own life from the power of the grave? Pause and reflect on this.

49 Lord, where are the steadfast acts of love and faithfulness you showed in the past, which you promised to David with your unwavering truth?

50 Lord, remember the disgrace your servants endure—how I carry in my heart the scorn of all these powerful nations.

51 O LORD, how your enemies have mocked and insulted you; how they have scorned and ridiculed the ways of your chosen one.

52 May the LORD be praised forever and ever. So be it, and so it is.
