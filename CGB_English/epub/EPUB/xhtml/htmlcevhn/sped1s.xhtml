I Chronicles Chapter 2

1 These are the sons of Israel: Reuben, Simeon, Levi, Judah, Issachar, and Zebulun.

2 Dan, Joseph, Benjamin, Naphtali, Gad, and Asher.

3 The sons of Judah were Er, Onan, and Shelah. These three were born to him by the daughter of Shua, a Canaanite woman. Er, Judah’s firstborn, was wicked in the eyes of the LORD, so the LORD put him to death.

4 And Tamar, his daughter-in-law, bore him Perez and Zerah. In total, Judah had five sons.

5 The descendants of Perez were Hezron and Hamul.

6 The sons of Zerah were Zimri, Ethan, Heman, Calcol, and Dara—five in total.

7 The sons of Carmi were Achar, who brought trouble upon Israel by committing an offense involving the devoted things.

8 The sons of Ethan were Azariah.

9 The sons of Hezron who were born to him were Jerahmeel, Ram, and Chelubai.

10 Ram became the father of Amminadab, and Amminadab became the father of Nahshon, who was a leader among the people of Judah.

11 And Nahshon became the father of Salma, and Salma became the father of Boaz.

12 Boaz became the father of Obed, and Obed became the father of Jesse.

13 Jesse became the father of his firstborn son Eliab, his second son Abinadab, and his third son Shammah.

14 Nethaneel was the fourth, and Raddai was the fifth.

15 Ozem was the sixth, and David was the seventh.

16 The sisters of Jesse were Zeruiah and Abigail. Zeruiah had three sons: Abishai, Joab, and Asahel.

17 Abigail gave birth to Amasa, and Amasa's father was Jether the Ishmaelite.

18 Caleb, the son of Hezron, had children with his wife Azubah and also with Jerioth. Their sons were Jesher, Shobab, and Ardon.

19 After Azubah died, Caleb married Ephrath, and she bore him a son named Hur.

20 And Hur became the father of Uri, and Uri became the father of Bezalel.

21 Afterward, Hezron married the daughter of Machir, the father of Gilead. He was sixty years old when he took her as his wife, and she bore him a son named Segub.

22 Segub was the father of Jair, who possessed twenty-three towns in the region of Gilead.

23 He captured Geshur and Aram, along with the towns of Jair, and took Kenath and its surrounding settlements—sixty cities in all. All of these territories belonged to the descendants of Machir, the father of Gilead.

24 After Hezron died in Caleb-ephratah, his wife Abiah gave birth to Ashur, who became the father of Tekoa.

25 The sons of Jerahmeel, the firstborn of Hezron, were Ram, his firstborn, followed by Bunah, Oren, Ozem, and Ahijah.

26 Jerahmeel also had another wife named Atarah, who was the mother of Onam.

27 The sons of Ram, the firstborn of Jerahmeel, were Maaz, Jamin, and Eker.

28 The descendants of Onam were Shammai and Jada. The sons of Shammai were Nadab and Abishur.

29 Abishur’s wife was named Abihail, and she gave birth to Ahban and Molid.

30 The sons of Nadab were Seled and Appaim, but Seled died without having any children.

31 The descendants of Appaim were Ishi. The descendants of Ishi were Sheshan. And the descendants of Sheshan were Ahlai.

32 The sons of Jada, the brother of Shammai, were Jether and Jonathan. However, Jether died without having any children.

33 The sons of Jonathan were Peleth and Zaza. These were the descendants of Jerahmeel.

34 Sheshan had no sons, only daughters. He also had an Egyptian servant named Jarha.

35 Sheshan gave his daughter in marriage to Jarha, his servant, and she bore him a son named Attai.

36 And Attai became the father of Nathan, and Nathan became the father of Zabad.

37 Zabad was the father of Ephlal, and Ephlal was the father of Obed.

38 Obed became the father of Jehu, and Jehu became the father of Azariah.

39 Azariah became the father of Helez, and Helez became the father of Eleasah.

40 Eleasah became the father of Sisamai, and Sisamai became the father of Shallum.

41 And Shallum became the father of Jekamiah, and Jekamiah became the father of Elishama.

42 The sons of Caleb, the brother of Jerahmeel, were as follows: Mesha, his firstborn, who was the father of Ziph; and the sons of Mareshah, who was the father of Hebron.

43 The descendants of Hebron were Korah, Tappuah, Rekem, and Shema.

44 Shema became the father of Raham, who was the father of Jorkoam, and Rekem became the father of Shammai.

45 The son of Shammai was Maon, and Maon became the father of Beth-zur.

46 Ephah, Caleb’s concubine, gave birth to Haran, Moza, and Gazez. Haran later became the father of Gazez.

47 The descendants of Jahdai were Regem, Jotham, Geshan, Pelet, Ephah, and Shaaph.

48 Maacah, Caleb's concubine, gave birth to Sheber and Tirhanah.

49 She also gave birth to Shaaph, the father of Madmannah, Sheva, the father of Machbenah, and the father of Gibea. Caleb’s daughter was named Achsah.

50 These were the descendants of Caleb, the son of Hur, who was the firstborn of Ephrathah: Shobal, who became the ancestor of the people of Kiriath-jearim.

51 Salma was the ancestor of Bethlehem, and Hareph was the ancestor of Beth-gader.

52 Shobal, the father of Kirjath-jearim, had sons: Haroeh and half of the clan of Manahath.

53 The families of Kiriath-jearim included the Ithrites, the Puhites, the Shumathites, and the Mishraites. From these families descended the Zareathites and the Eshtaulites.

54 The descendants of Salma included the people of Bethlehem, the Netophathites, Ataroth, the household of Joab, and half of the Manahethites, along with the Zorites.

55 The families of the scribes who lived in Jabez were the Tirathites, the Shimeathites, and the Suchathites. These are the Kenites who descended from Hemath, the ancestor of the house of Rechab.
