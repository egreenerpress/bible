Numbers Chapter 27

1 Then the daughters of Zelophehad came forward. Zelophehad was the son of Hepher, who was the son of Gilead, who was the son of Machir, who was the son of Manasseh, from the clans of Manasseh, the son of Joseph. These are the names of his daughters: Mahlah, Noah, Hoglah, Milcah, and Tirzah.

2 They presented themselves before Moses, Eleazar the priest, the leaders, and the entire community at the entrance to the tent of meeting, and they said,

3 Our father died in the wilderness, but he was not among those who joined together in rebellion against the LORD in Korah’s assembly. Instead, he died because of his own sin, and he left no sons behind.

4 Why should our father’s name be removed from his family simply because he had no son? Grant us, therefore, a share of land among our father’s relatives.

5 Moses presented their case before the LORD.

6 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

7 The daughters of Zelophehad are correct in what they say. You must certainly grant them a share of inheritance among their father’s relatives, and you are to transfer their father’s inheritance to them.

8 You are to speak to the Israelites and say: If a man dies and has no son, you must transfer his inheritance to his daughter.

9 If he has no daughter, then you must give his inheritance to his brothers.

10 If he has no brothers, then you must give his inheritance to his father’s brothers.

11 If the man’s father has no brothers, then you must give his inheritance to the nearest relative in his family, and that person will take possession of it. This will be a permanent legal statute for the people of Israel, as the LORD commanded Moses.

12 The LORD said to Moses, "Go up into this mountain of Abarim, and look upon the land that I have given to the people of Israel."

13 And when you have seen it, you too will be gathered to your ancestors, just as your brother Aaron was gathered.

14 Because you rebelled against my command in the wilderness of Zin, during the dispute of the congregation, and failed to honor me as holy at the waters in their presence—that is, the waters of Meribah at Kadesh in the wilderness of Zin.

15 Moses spoke to the LORD, saying,

16 May the LORD, the God who gives breath and life to all living beings, appoint a leader over the community.

17 Who will go out before them and come in before them, who will lead them out and bring them in, so that the community of the LORD will not be like sheep without a shepherd.

18 The LORD said to Moses, "Take Joshua, the son of Nun, a man in whom the spirit dwells, and place your hand on him."

19 Bring him before Eleazar the priest and the entire assembly, and publicly commission him in their presence.

20 You shall bestow some of your authority upon him, so that the entire community of the Israelites may obey him.

21 He will present himself before Eleazar the priest, who will seek guidance for him through the decision of the Urim in the presence of the LORD. At the priest's instruction, they will go out, and at his instruction, they will come in—both he and all the Israelites with him, the entire community.

22 Moses obeyed the LORD's command. He took Joshua and presented him before Eleazar the priest and in the presence of the entire assembly.

23 He placed his hands on him and formally commissioned him, just as the LORD had instructed through Moses.
