Ecclesiastes Chapter 7

1 A good reputation is more valuable than costly perfume, and the day of one's death is more significant than the day of one's birth.

2 It is more beneficial to attend a house of mourning than to go to a house of celebration, for death is the ultimate destiny of everyone, and the living should take this to heart.

3 Grief is more valuable than joy, for through the sorrow of the face, the heart is refined and improved.

4 The heart of the wise is drawn to the house of mourning, where reflection and understanding are found; but the heart of fools seeks the house of merriment, where thoughtlessness and distraction prevail.

5 It is more valuable to listen to the correction of a wise person than to hear the empty praise of foolish people.

6 Just as the crackling of thorns burning beneath a pot is loud but short-lived, so too is the laughter of a fool—empty and meaningless. This, too, is nothing but futility.

7 Certainly, oppression drives even a wise person to madness, and a bribe corrupts the heart.

8 The outcome of a matter is more valuable than its beginning, and a person who remains patient in spirit is better than one who is prideful in heart.

9 Do not be quick to let your spirit become angry, for anger resides in the hearts of fools.

10 Do not say, "Why were the past days better than these?" for it is not wise to ask such a question.

11 Wisdom is valuable when accompanied by an inheritance, and it brings benefit to those who live under the sun.

12 Wisdom serves as a protection, and wealth also serves as a protection, but the true value of knowledge lies in this: wisdom grants life to those who possess it.

13 Reflect on the actions of God: for who can straighten what he has made crooked?

14 In times of prosperity, rejoice and be glad, but in times of hardship, pause and reflect: God has ordained both to exist in balance, so that humanity may not fully comprehend or predict what lies ahead.

15 In the course of my fleeting life, I have observed many things: there is a righteous person who meets their end despite their integrity, and there is a wicked person who extends their days through their wrongdoing.

16 Do not strive to be excessively righteous, nor make yourself overly wise. Why should you bring harm upon yourself?

17 Do not indulge in excessive wickedness, nor act foolishly. Why should you bring about your own destruction before your appointed time?

18 It is good for you to hold firmly to this principle; indeed, do not let go of it, for those who fear God will ultimately emerge from all these things.

19 Wisdom empowers the wise more effectively than the strength of ten mighty warriors in a city.

20 Indeed, there is no one on earth who is truly righteous, who always does what is good and never sins.

21 Do not pay attention to every word that is spoken, or you may hear your servant cursing you.

22 For you often know in your own heart that you yourself have also cursed others.

23 I have examined all these things through the pursuit of wisdom. I declared, "I will become wise," but true wisdom remained beyond my grasp.

24 What is distant and profoundly mysterious—who can fully comprehend it?

25 I devoted my heart to understanding, to exploring, and to seeking out wisdom, as well as to discerning the meaning behind things. I also sought to recognize the nature of wickedness in folly, even in foolishness and madness.

26 I have found something more bitter than death: a woman whose heart is full of traps and snares, and whose hands are like chains. Whoever pleases God will escape from her, but the sinner will be caught by her.

27 Look, this is what I have discovered," says the Teacher, "as I carefully examined things one by one to understand their meaning.

28 This is what I have sought, but have not yet found: among a thousand men, I have found one who is upright, but among all these women, I have not found one.

29 Here is what I have discovered: God created humanity to be upright and righteous, but people have pursued their own schemes and devious plans.
