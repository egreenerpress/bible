I Timothy Chapter 5

1 Do not harshly rebuke an older man, but appeal to him respectfully as you would a father. Treat younger men as brothers, with kindness and understanding.

2 Treat older women as you would your own mothers, and younger women as your sisters, with absolute purity and respect.

3 Respect and provide for those widows who are truly in need and have no other means of support.

4 However, if a widow has children or grandchildren, let them first learn to practice godliness within their own household and to repay their parents, for this is pleasing and acceptable in the sight of God.

5 A true widow, who is left alone and without support, places her trust in God and devotes herself to earnest prayers and petitions both day and night.

6 But the woman who indulges in self-gratification and lives for pleasure is spiritually dead, even while she is physically alive.

7 Instruct them in these matters, so that they may live without fault or reproach.

8 But if anyone does not provide for their own relatives, and especially for their immediate family, they have rejected the faith and are worse than someone who does not believe.

9 Do not enroll a widow in the official list unless she is at least sixty years old and has been faithful to one husband.

10 She should be well respected for her good deeds, such as raising children, showing hospitality to strangers, serving fellow believers humbly, helping those in distress, and consistently pursuing every kind of good work.

11 However, do not enroll younger widows, for when their natural desires lead them away from their devotion to Christ, they will seek to marry again.

12 They are under condemnation because they have abandoned their initial commitment to faith.

13 Moreover, they become accustomed to idleness, going from house to house without purpose. Not only are they idle, but they also become gossips and meddlers, talking about matters that are none of their concern.

14 I desire that the younger women marry, have children, manage their households well, and avoid giving any opportunity for the adversary to bring criticism or disgrace.

15 Some have already strayed and followed Satan.

16 If any man or woman who believes has widows in their care, they should provide for them and not burden the church, so that the church can assist those who are truly in need as widows.

17 Elders who lead effectively should be considered deserving of special respect and recognition, particularly those who work diligently in teaching and instructing from the Scriptures.

18 For the Scripture says, "Do not muzzle an ox while it is treading out the grain." And, "The worker deserves his wages."

19 Do not accept an accusation against an elder unless it is supported by two or three witnesses.

20 Publicly correct those who commit sin, so that the rest may also be warned and deterred.

21 I solemnly urge you before God, the Lord Jesus Christ, and the chosen angels, to uphold these instructions without showing favoritism or acting out of partiality in any way.

22 Do not hastily appoint anyone to a position of responsibility, nor share in the sins of others. Maintain your own integrity and purity.

23 Stop drinking only water, but instead take a small amount of wine for the sake of your stomach and your frequent ailments.

24 The sins of some people are evident and obvious, leading directly to judgment, while the sins of others become known only later, following them to judgment.

25 In the same way, the good deeds of some people are evident and recognized beforehand, while those whose actions are not immediately apparent cannot remain hidden forever.
