Psalms Chapter 37

1 (A Psalm of David.) Do not let yourself become agitated over those who do wrong, nor envy those who engage in wickedness.

2 For they will quickly be cut down like grass and wither away like green plants.

3 Place your trust in the LORD and commit yourself to doing good; then you will live securely in the land, and you will be provided for with certainty.

4 Find your joy and satisfaction in the LORD, and He will grant you the deepest longings of your heart.

5 Entrust your path to the LORD; place your confidence in Him, and He will bring it to fulfillment.

6 He will bring your righteousness to light, shining brightly for all to see, and your justice will be as clear and undeniable as the midday sun.

7 Find your peace in the LORD and wait patiently for Him. Do not be troubled by those who succeed in their own ways, nor by those who carry out evil schemes.

8 Stop being angry, and abandon your rage; do not let yourself become agitated to the point of doing wrong.

9 Those who do evil will be destroyed, but those who trust in and rely on the LORD will inherit the land.

10 In just a short time, the wicked will be no more; you will carefully look for their place, but it will not exist.

11 However, the humble will inherit the land and take great joy in the abundance of peace.

12 The wicked devise schemes against the righteous and grind their teeth at them in anger.

13 The Lord will laugh at him, for He sees that his day of judgment is approaching.

14 The wicked have unsheathed their swords and strung their bows, aiming to bring down the poor and needy, and to kill those who live with integrity.

15 Their own sword will turn against them, piercing their own hearts, and their bows will be shattered.

16 The modest possessions of a righteous person are of greater value than the abundant wealth of many wicked individuals.

17 The power of the wicked will be shattered, but the LORD sustains the righteous.

18 The LORD is fully aware of the lives of those who live with integrity, and their lasting inheritance will endure forever.

19 They will not be put to shame in times of trouble, and during seasons of scarcity, they will have enough to be fully satisfied.

20 However, the wicked will be destroyed, and the enemies of the LORD will vanish like the fat of lambs burned in sacrifice; they will be consumed and dissipate like smoke.

21 The wicked take out loans and fail to repay them, but the righteous act with compassion and give generously.

22 Those who are blessed by God will inherit the land, but those who are cursed by Him will be utterly destroyed.

23 The LORD directs the path of a righteous person, and He takes pleasure in their way of life.

24 Even if he stumbles, he will not be completely overwhelmed, for the LORD supports him with His hand.

25 I was once young, and now I am old; yet I have never seen the righteous abandoned, nor their children reduced to begging for food.

26 He is always compassionate and generous, lending freely; and his descendants are truly blessed.

27 Turn away from wrongdoing and pursue what is good; then you will live securely forever.

28 For the LORD delights in justice and does not abandon His faithful ones; they are kept secure forever. But the descendants of the wicked will be destroyed.

29 The upright will possess the land and live in it permanently.

30 The mouth of the righteous speaks with wisdom, and their tongue declares what is just and fair.

31 The instruction of his God is deeply rooted within him; his every step will remain steady and secure.

32 The wicked keep a close watch on the righteous, seeking an opportunity to kill them.

33 The LORD will not abandon him to his enemy's power, nor will He condemn him when he faces judgment.

34 Trust in the LORD and remain faithful to His ways, and He will lift you up to possess the land as your inheritance. When the wicked are destroyed, you will witness it.

35 I have observed the wicked flourishing in great strength, thriving and expanding like a lush, evergreen tree.

36 Yet he vanished, and behold, he was no more; indeed, I searched for him, but he could not be found.

37 Observe the blameless person and take note of the upright individual, for the outcome of such a person is peace.

38 However, those who act wickedly will be utterly destroyed; the future of the unrighteous will be brought to an abrupt and complete end.

39 The deliverance of the upright comes from the LORD; He is their refuge and strength in times of distress.

40 The LORD will come to their aid and rescue them; He will free them from the power of the wicked and save them, because they place their trust in Him.
