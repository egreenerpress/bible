I Thessalonians Chapter 3

1 Therefore, when we could no longer endure the situation, we decided it was best to remain behind in Athens alone.

2 We sent Timothy, our brother and fellow worker in the gospel of Christ, who serves as a minister of God, to strengthen and encourage you in your faith.

3 Do not let anyone be shaken or discouraged by these trials, for you know well that we are destined to face such hardships.

4 Certainly, when we were with you, we warned you beforehand that we would face hardships and troubles, just as it has indeed happened, and you are well aware of this.

5 For this reason, when I could no longer endure the uncertainty, I sent to inquire about your faith, fearing that the tempter might have tempted you in some way, and that our efforts might have been for nothing.

6 But now Timothy has come to us from you and has brought us good news about your faith and love, and that you always hold us in your thoughts, longing to see us just as we also long to see you.

7 Therefore, brothers and sisters, we found comfort and encouragement in the midst of all our suffering and hardship because of your steadfast faith.

8 We find life and encouragement now, as long as you remain steadfast and firm in your faith in the Lord.

9 How can we possibly thank God enough for you, considering all the joy we feel because of you in the presence of our God?

10 We pray earnestly, both day and night, longing to see you face to face, so that we may help strengthen and complete what is lacking in your faith.

11 Now may God our Father and our Lord Jesus Christ guide and prepare our path to come to you.

12 May the Lord cause your love for one another and for all people to grow and overflow, just as our love for you does.

13 The purpose is that he may strengthen your hearts, making them blameless and holy in the presence of God, our Father, when our Lord Jesus Christ returns with all his holy ones.
