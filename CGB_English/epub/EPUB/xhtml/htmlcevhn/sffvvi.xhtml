II Samuel Chapter 9

1 David asked, "Is there anyone still remaining from the house of Saul to whom I can show kindness for Jonathan’s sake?"

2 There was a servant from the household of Saul whose name was Ziba. When they summoned him to David, the king asked him, "Are you Ziba?" He replied, "I am your servant."

3 The king asked, "Is there no one left from the house of Saul to whom I may show the kindness of God?" Ziba replied to the king, "Jonathan still has a son, but he is crippled in both feet."

4 The king asked him, "Where is he?" Ziba replied to the king, "He is staying in the house of Machir, the son of Ammiel, in Lo-debar."

5 Then King David sent messengers to bring him from the house of Machir, the son of Ammiel, in Lo-debar.

6 When Mephibosheth, the son of Jonathan and grandson of Saul, came to David, he bowed down to the ground in deep respect. David said, "Mephibosheth." He replied, "Here I am, your servant."

7 David said to him, "Do not be afraid, for I will certainly show you kindness because of your father Jonathan. I will restore to you all the land that belonged to your grandfather Saul, and you will always have a place at my table to eat."

8 He humbled himself and said, "Who am I, your servant, that you should show such kindness to someone as insignificant and unworthy as I am?"

9 Then the king summoned Ziba, Saul’s servant, and said to him, “I have granted to your master’s son everything that belonged to Saul and his entire household.”

10 Therefore, you, your sons, and your servants must work the land for him, and you shall bring in the harvest so that your master’s son may have food to eat. However, Mephibosheth, your master’s son, will always eat at my table. Now Ziba had fifteen sons and twenty servants.

11 Then Ziba said to the king, "Your servant will do everything that my lord the king has commanded." The king added, "As for Mephibosheth, he will eat at my table, just like one of the king's own sons."

12 Mephibosheth had a young son named Micha. All those who lived in Ziba’s household were servants of Mephibosheth.

13 So Mephibosheth lived in Jerusalem, for he always ate at the king’s table. He was crippled in both feet.
