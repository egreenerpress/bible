I Kings Chapter 4

1 So Solomon ruled as king over all of Israel.

2 These were the chief officials he had: Azariah, the son of Zadok, the priest.

3 Elihoreph and Ahiah, the sons of Shisha, served as secretaries; Jehoshaphat, the son of Ahilud, held the position of royal recorder.

4 Benaiah, the son of Jehoiada, was in command of the army, while Zadok and Abiathar served as priests.

5 Azariah, the son of Nathan, was in charge of the district governors, while Zabud, the son of Nathan, served as a chief official and the king’s trusted advisor.

6 Ahishar was in charge of the royal household, and Adoniram, the son of Abda, was in charge of the forced labor.

7 Solomon appointed twelve officials over all of Israel, who were responsible for supplying provisions for the king and his household. Each official was assigned one month of the year to provide the necessary supplies.

8 Here are the names of their leaders: The son of Hur, from the hill country of Ephraim.

9 The son of Dekar was responsible for Makaz, Shaalbim, Beth-shemesh, and Elon-beth-hanan.

10 The son of Hesed, who was in charge of Aruboth, had authority over Sochoh and the entire region of Hepher.

11 The son of Abinadab, who governed the entire region of Dor, was married to Taphath, the daughter of Solomon.

12 Baana, the son of Ahilud, was assigned the region of Taanach and Megiddo, along with all of Beth-shean, which lies near Zartanah below Jezreel. His territory extended from Beth-shean to Abel-meholah, reaching as far as the area beyond Jokneam.

13 The son of Geber was in charge of Ramoth-gilead. He had authority over the towns of Jair, the son of Manasseh, which were located in Gilead. He also governed the region of Argob in Bashan, which included sixty large cities fortified with walls and bronze gates.

14 Ahinadab, the son of Iddo, was in charge of Mahanaim.

15 Ahimaaz was from the region of Naphtali, and he married Basemath, who was Solomon’s daughter.

16 Baanah, the son of Hushai, was assigned to oversee the regions of Asher and Aloth.

17 Jehoshaphat, the son of Paruah, was in charge of the territory of Issachar.

18 Shimei, the son of Elah, from the tribe of Benjamin.

19 Geber, the son of Uri, was appointed over the region of Gilead, which had been the territory of Sihon, king of the Amorites, and Og, king of Bashan. He was the sole official overseeing that land.

20 Judah and Israel were as numerous as the sand by the sea, a vast multitude. They ate, drank, and celebrated with great joy.

21 Solomon ruled over all the kingdoms from the Euphrates River to the land of the Philistines and as far as the border of Egypt. These kingdoms brought tribute and served Solomon throughout his entire reign.

22 Solomon's daily provisions included thirty measures of fine flour and sixty measures of coarse flour.

23 Ten well-fed oxen, twenty oxen from the pastures, and a hundred sheep, in addition to deer, gazelles, roe deer, and fattened poultry.

24 For he ruled over the entire region west of the Euphrates River, from Tiphsah all the way to Gaza, and over all the kings in that territory. He enjoyed peace on every side surrounding him.

25 Judah and Israel lived in safety, with each person resting securely under their own vine and fig tree, from Dan in the north to Beersheba in the south, throughout all the days of Solomon’s reign.

26 Solomon maintained forty thousand stalls for the horses that pulled his chariots, along with twelve thousand cavalrymen.

27 The officials supplied provisions for King Solomon and for all who came to his table, each one in their assigned month. They ensured that nothing was lacking.

28 They also brought barley and straw for the horses and swift camels to the place where the officers were stationed, each man providing according to his assigned duty.

29 God granted Solomon extraordinary wisdom and deep understanding, along with a breadth of insight as vast as the sand on the seashore.

30 Solomon's wisdom surpassed the wisdom of all the people of the East and all the wisdom of Egypt.

31 For he was wiser than all other men—wiser than Ethan the Ezrahite, Heman, Chalcol, and Darda, the sons of Mahol. His reputation for wisdom spread throughout all the surrounding nations.

32 He spoke three thousand proverbs, and his songs numbered one thousand and five.

33 He spoke about all kinds of trees, from the mighty cedar in Lebanon to the small hyssop that grows out of the wall. He also spoke about animals, birds, creeping creatures, and fish.

34 People from every nation came to listen to Solomon’s wisdom, including all the kings of the earth who had heard about his profound understanding.
