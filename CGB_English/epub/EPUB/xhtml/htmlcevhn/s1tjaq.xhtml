Romans Chapter 14

1 Welcome those who are weak in faith, but do not engage in arguments over doubtful matters.

2 For one person believes they may eat anything, while another, who is more sensitive in conscience, eats only vegetables.

3 Do not let the one who eats look down on the one who does not eat, and do not let the one who does not eat pass judgment on the one who eats, for God has accepted them.

4 Who are you to judge someone elseâ€™s servant? Whether they stand or fall is a matter for their own master to decide. Indeed, they will be upheld, for God has the power to make them stand firm.

5 One person considers one day more important than another, while another person regards every day as equal. Each one should be fully convinced in their own judgment.

6 The one who observes a particular day does so in honor of the Lord, and the one who does not observe it also refrains in honor of the Lord. Likewise, the one who eats does so in honor of the Lord, for he gives thanks to God; and the one who abstains from eating also does so in honor of the Lord, and he too gives thanks to God.

7 For none of us lives for ourselves alone, and no one dies for themselves alone.

8 For if we live, we live for the Lord; and if we die, we die for the Lord. So whether we live or die, we belong to the Lord.

9 For this purpose, Christ died, rose again, and came back to life, so that he might exercise authority as Lord over both the dead and the living.

10 Why do you pass judgment on your brother? Or why do you look down on your brother? For we will all stand before the judgment seat of Christ.

11 For it is written, "As surely as I live, declares the Lord, every knee will bow before me, and every tongue will acknowledge God."

12 Therefore, each of us will have to give a personal account of ourselves to God.

13 Therefore, let us no longer pass judgment on one another. Instead, decide this: that no one should place an obstacle or a cause for stumbling in the way of a fellow believer.

14 I am certain and fully convinced through the Lord Jesus that nothing is inherently unclean. However, if someone considers something to be unclean, then for that person, it is indeed unclean.

15 But if your brother is distressed because of what you eat, you are no longer acting in love. Do not let your food cause the ruin of someone for whom Christ died.

16 Do not allow your good deeds to be slandered or spoken of as evil.

17 The kingdom of God is not about food and drink, but about righteousness, peace, and joy that come through the Holy Spirit.

18 For whoever serves Christ in these matters is pleasing to God and respected by others.

19 Therefore, let us pursue what leads to peace and what builds up one another.

20 Do not let food destroy what God has accomplished. While all things are indeed pure, it is wrong for someone to eat in a way that causes others to stumble.

21 It is better not to eat meat, nor to drink wine, nor to do anything that causes your brother or sister to stumble, be offended, or become spiritually weakened.

22 Do you have faith? Keep it as a personal matter before God. Blessed is the one who does not condemn himself by what he approves.

23 But whoever eats while doubting is condemned, because their eating does not arise from faith. For anything that does not come from faith is sin.
