Psalms Chapter 108

1 (A Psalm of David.) O God, my heart is steadfast; I will sing and make music, yes, with all that I am.

2 Rise up, lyre and harp; I will awaken the dawn with my song.

3 I will give you praise, O LORD, before all peoples; and I will sing of your glory among the nations.

4 For your steadfast love is vast, extending beyond the heavens, and your faithfulness reaches to the skies.

5 Rise up, O God, high above the heavens; let your glory be exalted over all the earth.

6 So that those you love may be rescued, save them by your mighty power and answer my plea.

7 God has spoken in His holiness; I will celebrate, I will apportion Shechem, and measure out the Valley of Succoth.

8 Gilead belongs to me; Manasseh is mine; Ephraim is the strength of my authority; Judah is the one who upholds my law.

9 Moab is like a basin for me to wash my feet; over Edom I will toss my sandal in victory; and over Philistia I will shout in triumph.

10 Who will bring me into the fortified city? Who will guide me into the land of Edom?

11 Will you not, O God, who have rejected us, go out with our armies? Will you not, O God, lead us once more into battle?

12 Grant us deliverance from distress, for human assistance is ultimately futile.

13 With God's strength, we will act with courage and power, for He is the one who will crush our enemies underfoot.
