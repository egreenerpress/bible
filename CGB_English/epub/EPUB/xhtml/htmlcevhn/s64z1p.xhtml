Numbers Chapter 23

1 Balaam said to Balak, "Construct for me here seven altars, and prepare here seven bulls and seven rams."

2 Balak did as Balaam instructed, and together they offered a bull and a ram on each altar.

3 Balaam said to Balak, "Stay here by your burnt offering, and I will go. Perhaps the LORD will come to meet me, and whatever He reveals to me, I will tell you." Then he went to a barren height.

4 Then God met Balaam, and Balaam said to Him, "I have set up seven altars and offered a bull and a ram on each altar."

5 The LORD placed a message in Balaam’s mouth and instructed him, “Go back to Balak, and this is what you must say.”

6 He went back to him, and there he was, standing beside his burnt offering, along with all the leaders of Moab.

7 Then he began his oracle, saying: "Balak, the king of Moab, has brought me from Aram, from the eastern mountains, saying, 'Come, curse Jacob for me; come, denounce Israel.'"

8 How can I curse those whom God has not cursed? How can I denounce those whom the LORD has not denounced?

9 From the heights of the rocky cliffs I see him, and from the hills I observe him. Look, this is a people who will live apart, distinct and separate, and they will not be counted among the other nations.

10 Who can possibly count the vast multitude of Jacob’s descendants or number even a fourth of Israel? May I die as the righteous die, and may my final destiny be like theirs!

11 Balak said to Balaam, "What have you done to me? I brought you here to curse my enemies, but instead, you have blessed them completely!"

12 And he replied, "Should I not be careful to speak only what the LORD has placed in my mouth to say?"

13 Balak said to him, "Please come with me to another place, where you can see them. You will only see a portion of them, not all of them, and from there, curse them for me."

14 He then led him to the field of Zophim, up to the summit of Pisgah, where he constructed seven altars. On each altar, he offered a bull and a ram as sacrifices.

15 And he said to Balak, "Stay here by your burnt offering while I go over there to meet with the LORD."

16 The LORD encountered Balaam and placed a message in his mouth, instructing him, "Return to Balak and deliver this message."

17 When Balaam arrived, he found Balak standing beside his burnt offering, accompanied by the leaders of Moab. Balak asked him, "What has the LORD said?"

18 Then he began his oracle and said, "Arise, Balak, and listen; pay attention to me, son of Zippor."

19 God is not a human being, that He should lie, nor a mortal, that He should change His mind. Does He speak and then not act? Does He promise and not fulfill it?

20 Listen, I have been commanded to bless, and He has indeed blessed; I cannot revoke it.

21 God does not see any wrongdoing in Jacob, nor does He observe any deceit in Israel. The LORD, his God, is with him, and the triumphant proclamation of a king is in their midst.

22 God led them out of Egypt; He possesses strength like that of a wild ox.

23 Certainly, no sorcery can prevail against Jacob, nor can any divination succeed against Israel. At this very moment, it will be said of Jacob and of Israel, "What wonders God has accomplished!"

24 Look, the people will rise up like a mighty lion and stand tall like a young lion. They will not rest until they have devoured their prey and drunk the blood of those they have struck down.

25 And Balak said to Balaam, "Do not curse them at all, nor bless them at all."

26 But Balaam replied to Balak, "Did I not tell you, 'Whatever the LORD says, I must do'?"

27 Balak said to Balaam, "Please come with me, and I will take you to another place. Perhaps it will please God that you can curse them for me from there."

28 Balak took Balaam to the summit of Peor, which overlooks the wilderness.

29 Balaam said to Balak, "Construct for me here seven altars, and prepare here seven bulls and seven rams."

30 Balak followed Balaam's instructions and offered a bull and a ram on each altar.
