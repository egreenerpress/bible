Isaiah Chapter 9

1 However, the gloom will not be as severe as it was during her time of distress, when at first He brought a light affliction upon the land of Zebulun and the land of Naphtali, and later inflicted a heavier burden on her—by the way of the sea, beyond the Jordan, in Galilee of the nations.

2 The people who lived in darkness have seen a brilliant light; those who dwell in the land of death's shadow, upon them a light has dawned.

3 You have greatly increased the nation, yet their joy has not grown. They rejoice before you as they do during the harvest, and they celebrate as warriors do when dividing the plunder.

4 For you have shattered the yoke that burdened him, the rod that oppressed his shoulder, and the staff of his taskmaster, just as you did in the day of Midian.

5 Every battle fought by warriors is marked by chaotic clamor and bloodstained garments, but this conflict will be characterized by consuming flames and the fuel of fire.

6 For to us a child is born, to us a son is given; and the authority to rule will rest on his shoulders. He will be called: Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Eternal Father, Prince of Peace.

7 The growth of his authority and the prosperity it brings will never cease. He will reign on David’s throne and over his kingdom, establishing and upholding it with fairness and justice from this time forward and forever. The passionate commitment of the LORD of hosts will accomplish this.

8 The Lord has spoken a message concerning Jacob, and it has come to rest upon Israel.

9 And all the people will come to understand—even Ephraim and the residents of Samaria—who speak with arrogance and stubbornness of heart, saying,

10 The bricks have crumbled, but we will rebuild with finely cut stone; the sycamore trees have been felled, but we will replace them with majestic cedars.

11 Therefore, the LORD will stir up Rezin's adversaries against him and unite his enemies together.

12 The Syrians advance from the front, and the Philistines press in from behind; they will consume Israel with relentless destruction. Despite all this, God's anger has not subsided, and His hand remains outstretched in judgment.

13 The people do not turn back to the one who strikes them, nor do they seek the LORD of armies.

14 Therefore, the LORD will remove from Israel both the leaders and the common people, the influential and the insignificant, all in a single day.

15 The respected and esteemed leader is the head, while the prophet who spreads falsehood is the tail.

16 The rulers of this people lead them astray, and those who follow them are brought to ruin.

17 Therefore, the Lord will take no delight in their young men, nor will He show compassion to their orphans and widows; for everyone is a hypocrite and an evildoer, and every mouth speaks deceit. Despite all this, His anger has not subsided, and His hand remains outstretched in judgment.

18 Wickedness burns like a raging fire; it consumes the briers and thorns and ignites the dense thickets of the forest, causing them to rise in towering columns of smoke.

19 Because of the fierce anger of the LORD of hosts, the land will be shrouded in darkness, and the people will become like fuel for the fire. No one will show compassion even to their own brother.

20 He will seize with his right hand, yet still be hungry; he will devour with his left hand, yet not be satisfied. Each one will consume the flesh of their own kin.

21 Manasseh and Ephraim, and Ephraim and Manasseh, will unite together, and they will stand against Judah. Yet despite all this, God's anger has not subsided; His hand remains outstretched in judgment.
