Numbers Chapter 12

1 Miriam and Aaron spoke against Moses because of the Cushite woman he had married, for he had taken a Cushite woman as his wife.

2 And they said, "Has the LORD spoken only through Moses? Has He not also spoken through us?" And the LORD heard what they said.

3 Now Moses was the most humble man on the face of the earth, more so than any other person.

4 Then the LORD spoke abruptly to Moses, Aaron, and Miriam, saying, "Come out, all three of you, to the tent of meeting." So the three of them went out.

5 The LORD descended in the pillar of cloud and stood at the entrance of the tabernacle. He then called for Aaron and Miriam, and both of them came forward.

6 And the Lord said, "Listen carefully to my words: If there is a prophet among you, I, the LORD, will reveal myself to them through visions and speak to them in dreams."

7 My servant Moses is not like that; he is faithful in all aspects of my household.

8 I will speak with him face to face, clearly and not in riddles; he will see the very form of the LORD. Why then were you not afraid to speak against my servant Moses?

9 And the LORD's anger burned against them, and He withdrew from their presence.

10 Then the cloud lifted from the tabernacle, and suddenly Miriam was afflicted with leprosy, her skin turning as white as snow. When Aaron looked at Miriam, he saw that she was indeed leprous.

11 Aaron said to Moses, "Please, my lord, I beg you, do not hold this sin against us, for we have acted foolishly and have sinned."

12 Do not let her be like a stillborn child, whose flesh is already half decayed when it comes forth from its mother's womb.

13 Moses pleaded with the LORD, saying, "Please, God, heal her now, I beg you."

14 The LORD said to Moses, "If her father had simply spit in her face, would she not be humiliated for seven days? Let her be excluded from the camp for seven days, and after that, she may be welcomed back."

15 Miriam was excluded from the camp for seven days, and the people did not continue their journey until Miriam was brought back into the camp.

16 Afterward, the people departed from Hazeroth and set up camp in the wilderness of Paran.
