Ephesians Chapter 3

1 For this reason, I, Paul, a prisoner of Christ Jesus for the sake of you Gentiles...

2 If you have heard about the stewardship of God’s grace that was entrusted to me for your benefit:

3 He made known to me this mystery through a divine revelation, as I briefly mentioned before.

4 When you read this, you will be able to grasp my understanding of the profound mystery of Christ.

5 This mystery, which was not disclosed to humanity in previous generations, has now been revealed by the Spirit to His holy apostles and prophets.

6 The Gentiles are to be co-heirs, united as one body, and sharers in the promise through Christ, as revealed in the gospel.

7 I was appointed to serve as a minister, in accordance with the gift of God’s grace that was given to me through the powerful working of His might.

8 To me, though I am the least deserving of all God’s people, this grace has been given: to proclaim to the Gentiles the boundless and unfathomable riches of Christ.

9 And to make all people understand the shared participation in the mystery, which from the beginning of the world has been hidden in God, who created all things through Jesus Christ.

10 This was done so that through the church, the multifaceted wisdom of God might now be made known to the rulers and authorities in the heavenly realms.

11 According to the eternal plan that he established in Christ Jesus our Lord:

12 In Him, we have the freedom to approach God with boldness and confidence, made possible through our faith in Him.

13 Therefore, I urge you not to lose heart because of the hardships I endure for your sake, for these trials are a source of honor for you.

14 For this reason, I kneel in reverence before the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ.

15 From whom every family in heaven and on earth derives its name.

16 May he, in accordance with the abundance of his glorious riches, empower you with inner strength through his Spirit in your innermost being.

17 May Christ make His home in your hearts through your faith, so that you, being firmly established and deeply rooted in love,

18 May you be empowered to grasp, together with all God’s people, the vastness of His love—its width, its length, its depth, and its height.

19 And to comprehend the love of Christ, which surpasses all understanding, so that you may be filled with the complete measure of God’s presence and power.

20 Now to Him who is capable of accomplishing far beyond all that we could ever ask or imagine, according to the power that is actively at work within us.

21 May glory be given to Him in the church through Christ Jesus for all generations, forever and ever. Amen.
