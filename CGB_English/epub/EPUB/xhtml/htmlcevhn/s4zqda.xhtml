Genesis Chapter 21

1 The LORD fulfilled His promise to Sarah just as He had declared, and He acted on Sarah's behalf exactly as He had spoken.

2 Sarah became pregnant and gave birth to a son for Abraham in his old age, at the exact time God had promised him.

3 And Abraham named his son, whom Sarah had borne to him, Isaac.

4 Abraham circumcised his son Isaac when he was eight days old, just as God had commanded him to do.

5 Abraham was one hundred years old when his son Isaac was born to him.

6 Sarah said, "God has brought me laughter, so that everyone who hears about this will laugh with me."

7 And she said, "Who could have ever told Abraham that Sarah would nurse children? Yet I have borne him a son in his old age!"

8 The child grew and was weaned, and on the day Isaac was weaned, Abraham held a great feast to celebrate.

9 Sarah saw the son of Hagar the Egyptian, whom Hagar had borne to Abraham, mocking.

10 Therefore, she said to Abraham, "Drive out this slave woman and her son, for the son of this slave woman shall not share the inheritance with my son, with Isaac."

11 The matter weighed heavily on Abraham's heart because it concerned his son.

12 And God said to Abraham, "Do not be distressed because of the boy and because of your servant Hagar. Listen to everything Sarah has said to you, for it is through Isaac that your descendants will be named.

13 I will also make a nation from the son of the servant woman, for he is your offspring.

14 Abraham got up early in the morning, took some bread and a container of water, and gave them to Hagar. He placed them on her shoulder, along with the child, and sent her away. She left and wandered in the wilderness of Beersheba.

15 The water in the skin was completely gone, and she placed the child beneath one of the bushes.

16 So she went and sat down at a distance, about as far as a bowshot away, for she said, "I cannot bear to watch the child die." She sat there, facing him, and lifted up her voice and wept.

17 God heard the boy’s cry, and the angel of God called to Hagar from heaven, saying, “What troubles you, Hagar? Do not be afraid, for God has heard the boy’s voice as he lies there.

18 "Get up, help the boy to his feet, and take him by the hand, for I will make him into a great nation."

19 Then God opened her eyes, and she saw a well of water. She went to it, filled her container with water, and gave the boy a drink.

20 God was with the boy, and he grew up. He lived in the wilderness and became skilled with the bow.

21 He settled in the wilderness of Paran, and his mother arranged for him to take a wife from the land of Egypt.

22 At that time, Abimelech and Phichol, the commander of his army, spoke to Abraham, saying, "God is with you in everything you do."

23 Now, therefore, swear to me here before God that you will not act deceitfully toward me, nor toward my son, nor toward my grandson. Instead, in keeping with the kindness I have shown you, you must treat me and the land where you have lived as a foreigner with the same fairness.

24 And Abraham said, "I will make this solemn promise."

25 Abraham confronted Abimelech concerning a well of water that Abimelech’s servants had seized by force.

26 And Abimelech replied, "I do not know who has done this thing. You did not inform me, nor had I heard anything about it until today."

27 Abraham took sheep and cattle and gave them to Abimelech, and the two of them entered into a formal agreement.

28 Abraham separated seven female lambs from the flock and set them apart by themselves.

29 And Abimelech said to Abraham, "What is the meaning of these seven female lambs that you have set apart by themselves?"

30 And he said, "You must accept these seven female lambs from me as a witness that I have dug this well."

31 Therefore, he named that place Beer-sheba, because there the two of them made a solemn oath.

32 So they established a covenant at Beersheba. Then Abimelech, along with Phicol, the commander of his army, arose and returned to the land of the Philistines.

33 Abraham planted a grove in Beersheba and there called upon the name of the LORD, the eternal God.

34 And Abraham lived as a foreigner in the land of the Philistines for a long time.
