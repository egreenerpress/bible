Galatians Chapter 1

1 Paul, an apostle—not appointed by human authority nor through any human means, but by Jesus Christ and God the Father, who raised Him from the dead—

2 And all the brothers and sisters who are with me send greetings to the churches in Galatia.

3 May grace and peace be yours, given by God the Father and by our Lord Jesus Christ.

4 He gave himself for our sins so that he might rescue us from this present evil age, in accordance with the will of God, who is our Father.

5 To whom belongs eternal glory forever and ever. Amen.

6 I am astonished that you are so quickly turning away from the one who called you by the grace of Christ and are embracing a different message, which is not truly the gospel.

7 This is not a different gospel; but there are some who are causing confusion among you and seeking to distort the true message of Christ.

8 Even if we ourselves, or even an angel from heaven, were to proclaim to you a gospel different from the one we have already preached to you, let that person be condemned.

9 As I have stated before, I now say again: If anyone proclaims to you a gospel different from the one you have received, let that person be condemned.

10 Am I now trying to win the approval of people, or of God? Or am I striving to please others? If I were still seeking to please people, I would not be a servant of Christ.

11 But I want you to be assured, my brothers and sisters, that the gospel I proclaim is not of human origin.

12 For I did not receive this message from any human source, nor was I taught it by anyone, but it came to me through a direct revelation from Jesus Christ.

13 You have heard about my former way of life in Judaism, how intensely I persecuted the church of God and sought to destroy it.

14 I advanced in the Jewish faith far beyond many of my peers in my own nation, being intensely devoted to the traditions handed down by my ancestors.

15 But when God, who set me apart from my mother’s womb and called me by His grace, was pleased to do so...

16 To make His Son known through me, so that I might proclaim Him among the nations, I did not immediately consult with any human being.

17 I did not go up to Jerusalem to meet with those who were apostles before me; instead, I went to Arabia and later returned to Damascus.

18 After three years had passed, I went up to Jerusalem to meet with Peter and stayed with him for fifteen days.

19 However, I did not see any of the other apostles, except for James, the Lord’s brother.

20 The matters I am writing to you about—I assure you before God that I am not lying.

21 Afterward, I went into the regions of Syria and Cilicia.

22 The churches in Judea that belonged to Christ did not recognize me by sight.

23 However, they had only heard this report: "The one who used to persecute us is now proclaiming the faith he once sought to destroy."

24 And they gave praise to God because of what He had done through me.
