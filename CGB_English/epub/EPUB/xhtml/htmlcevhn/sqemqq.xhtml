I Chronicles Chapter 16

1 So they brought the ark of God and placed it inside the tent that David had prepared for it. Then they presented burnt offerings and fellowship offerings before God.

2 After David had finished presenting the burnt offerings and the fellowship offerings, he blessed the people in the name of the LORD.

3 He distributed to every Israelite, both men and women, a loaf of bread, a generous portion of meat, and a measure of wine.

4 He selected some of the Levites to serve before the ark of the LORD, to commemorate, to give thanks, and to praise the LORD, the God of Israel.

5 Asaph was the leader, and alongside him were Zechariah, Jeiel, Shemiramoth, Jehiel, Mattithiah, Eliab, Benaiah, and Obed-edom. Jeiel played the psalteries and harps, while Asaph produced music with cymbals.

6 Benaiah and Jahaziel, the priests, were stationed with trumpets to sound them continually before the ark of the covenant of God.

7 On that day, David first entrusted this psalm of thanksgiving to the LORD into the care of Asaph and his brothers.

8 Give thanks to the LORD; call on his name. Proclaim his mighty works among the nations.

9 Sing to Him; offer songs of praise to Him. Speak of all His marvelous and wondrous deeds.

10 Rejoice in His holy name; may the hearts of those who seek the LORD be filled with gladness.

11 Pursue the LORD and rely on His power; continually seek His presence.

12 Call to mind the extraordinary deeds he has performed, his miraculous acts, and the decrees he has spoken.

13 O descendants of Israel, His servant, O children of Jacob, His chosen people.

14 He is the LORD our God; His rulings extend throughout the entire earth.

15 Always remember His covenant—the promise He established for a thousand generations.

16 He remembered the covenant He established with Abraham and the oath He swore to Isaac.

17 He established it as a decree for Jacob, as an eternal covenant for Israel.

18 He declared, "To you I will give the land of Canaan as your allotted portion and inheritance."

19 When you were few in number—indeed, very few—and were foreigners in the land.

20 And as they journeyed from one nation to another, moving from kingdom to kingdom among different peoples,

21 He did not allow anyone to oppress them; indeed, he rebuked kings on their behalf.

22 He said, "Do not harm those I have chosen, and do not bring injury to my prophets."

23 Sing to the LORD, all the earth; proclaim His salvation day after day.

24 Proclaim His glory among the nations; make known His wondrous deeds to all peoples.

25 For the LORD is great and deserves the highest praise; He is to be revered above all other gods.

26 The gods of the nations are nothing but idols, but the LORD is the one who created the heavens.

27 Splendor and majesty surround Him; power and joy fill His dwelling.

28 Give to the LORD, all you families of the nations, give to the LORD the honor and power that He deserves.

29 Acknowledge the LORD with the honor that is fitting for His name. Present an offering and come into His presence. Worship the LORD in the splendor of His holiness.

30 Revere Him, all the earth; the world will remain firmly established and will not be shaken.

31 Let the heavens be filled with joy, and let the earth celebrate with gladness. Let people declare among all nations, "The LORD reigns as King!"

32 Let the sea thunder and all that fills it; let the fields exult and everything within them.

33 Then the trees of the forest will rejoice aloud before the presence of the LORD, for he is coming to judge the earth.

34 Give thanks to the LORD, for He is good; His steadfast love endures forever.

35 And say, "Save us, O God, our deliverer; gather us and rescue us from the nations, so that we may give thanks to your holy name and take pride in praising you."

36 Praise be to the LORD, the God of Israel, from everlasting to everlasting. And all the people responded, "Amen," and gave praise to the LORD.

37 So he appointed Asaph and his relatives to remain there before the ark of the covenant of the LORD, to serve continually before the ark, carrying out the daily tasks as required.

38 And Obed-edom, along with his relatives, sixty-eight in total; Obed-edom, the son of Jeduthun, and Hosah were appointed as gatekeepers.

39 Zadok the priest, along with his fellow priests, stood before the tabernacle of the LORD at the high place in Gibeon.

40 To present burnt offerings to the LORD on the altar of burnt offering regularly, both in the morning and in the evening, and to carry out everything written in the law of the LORD, which He commanded Israel to follow.

41 Along with them were Heman and Jeduthun, as well as the others who had been specifically chosen and designated by name to give thanks to the LORD, for His steadfast love endures forever.

42 Heman and Jeduthun were also present, equipped with trumpets and cymbals to produce music, along with other instruments dedicated to worship. The sons of Jeduthun were assigned as gatekeepers.

43 Then all the people went back to their own homes, and David returned to his house to bless his household.
