Luke Chapter 15

1 Then all the tax collectors and sinners came close to him to listen to his teaching.

2 The Pharisees and the teachers of the law began to complain, saying, "This man welcomes sinners and even shares meals with them."

3 He told them this parable, saying,

4 Which one of you, if you have a hundred sheep and lose one of them, would not leave the ninety-nine in the open pasture and go after the one that is lost until you find it?

5 And when he finds it, he places it on his shoulders, filled with joy.

6 When he returns home, he gathers his friends and neighbors and says to them, "Celebrate with me, for I have found my sheep that was lost."

7 I tell you that in the same way, there will be great joy in heaven over one sinner who repents, more than over ninety-nine righteous people who do not need to repent.

8 Imagine a woman who has ten silver coins. If she loses one of them, wouldn’t she light a lamp, sweep the entire house, and search carefully until she finds it?

9 When she finds it, she calls together her friends and neighbors, saying, "Celebrate with me, for I have found the coin that I had lost!"

10 In the same way, I tell you, there is great joy before the angels of God when even one sinner turns away from their wrongdoing and seeks forgiveness.

11 He said, "There was a man who had two sons."

12 Then the younger son said to his father, “Father, give me the share of the estate that belongs to me.” So the father divided his property between them.

13 Not long after, the younger son gathered all his belongings and set out for a distant land. There, he squandered his wealth in reckless and extravagant living.

14 After he had used up all his resources, a severe famine swept through that land, and he found himself in desperate need.

15 So he went and attached himself to a citizen of that region, who sent him into his fields to tend pigs.

16 He longed to fill his stomach with the pods that the pigs were eating, but no one gave him anything.

17 When he finally came to his senses, he said, “How many of my father’s hired workers have more than enough food to eat, while I am here starving to death!”

18 I will get up and go to my father, and I will say to him, "Father, I have sinned against heaven and against you."

19 "I am no longer worthy to be called your son. Treat me as one of your hired workers."

20 So he got up and went to his father. But while he was still a long way off, his father saw him and was filled with compassion. He ran to his son, embraced him, and kissed him.

21 The son said to him, “Father, I have sinned against heaven and against you. I am no longer worthy to be called your son.”

22 But the father said to his servants, “Bring out the finest robe and clothe him with it. Place a ring on his finger and sandals on his feet.”

23 "Go and bring the fattened calf here, slaughter it, and let us feast and celebrate together."

24 For this son of mine was dead, but now he is alive again; he was lost, but now he is found. And they began to celebrate.

25 Now, the older son was out in the field. As he returned and approached the house, he heard music and the sound of dancing.

26 He summoned one of the servants and inquired about the meaning of these events.

27 And he said to him, "Your brother has returned, and your father has slaughtered the fattened calf because he has welcomed him back safe and unharmed."

28 The older son became angry and refused to go inside. So his father came out and pleaded with him.

29 The son replied to his father, "Look, for all these years I have served you, and I have never disobeyed your commands. Yet you never gave me even a young goat so that I could celebrate with my friends."

30 But as soon as this son of yours came back—the one who has squandered your wealth with immoral living—you slaughtered the fattened calf for him.

31 And he said to him, "My child, you are always with me, and everything I have belongs to you."

32 It was fitting for us to celebrate and rejoice, because this brother of yours was dead and has come back to life; he was lost and has been found.
