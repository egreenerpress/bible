Psalms Chapter 102

1 (A prayer of someone in distress, when they are overwhelmed and pour out their complaint before the LORD.) LORD, hear my prayer; let my cry for help reach you.

2 Do not turn away from me in my time of distress; listen closely to me. When I cry out to you, respond to me quickly.

3 My days vanish like smoke, and my bones burn like glowing embers.

4 My heart is stricken and dried up like withered grass, to the point that I even forget to eat my food.

5 Because of the sound of my groaning, my bones cling to my flesh.

6 I am like a pelican in the desolate wasteland; I am like an owl in the barren wilderness.

7 I keep watch, and I am like a solitary sparrow perched on the rooftop.

8 My enemies continually taunt me throughout the day; those who rage against me have bound themselves together in hostility toward me.

9 For I have consumed ashes as if they were bread, and mixed my drink with tears.

10 Because of your fierce anger and wrath—for you have raised me up, only to cast me down.

11 My days are like a fading shadow, and I am dried up like withered grass.

12 But you, O LORD, will remain forever; your name and renown will endure through all generations.

13 You will rise up and show compassion to Zion, for the time to show her favor—indeed, the appointed time—has now come.

14 For your servants find joy in her stones, and cherish even the dust of her ruins.

15 Therefore, the nations will revere the name of the LORD, and all the rulers of the earth will stand in awe of your majesty.

16 When the LORD rebuilds Zion, he will reveal himself in his splendor.

17 He will pay attention to the plea of the helpless and will not disregard their cry for help.

18 This will be recorded for the future generation, and the people who are yet to be created will praise the LORD.

19 For the Lord has gazed down from the lofty heights of his holy dwelling; from heaven he has observed the earth.

20 To listen to the cries of those who are imprisoned; to set free those who are destined to die.

21 To proclaim the name of the LORD in Zion and to declare his praise in Jerusalem;

22 When the people assemble and the nations come together to worship the LORD.

23 He has diminished my strength along the journey; he has cut short the length of my days.

24 I cried out, "O my God, do not take me away in the middle of my life! Your years endure through all generations."

25 Long ago, you established the earth's foundation, and the heavens are the work of your hands.

26 They will perish, but you will remain forever; indeed, all of them will wear out like a garment. Like clothing, you will change them, and they will be transformed.

27 But you remain unchanged, and your years will never come to an end.

28 The descendants of your servants will endure, and their offspring will be firmly established in your presence.
