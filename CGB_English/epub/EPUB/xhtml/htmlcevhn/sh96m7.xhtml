Job Chapter 23

1 Then Job replied and said,

2 Even today, my complaint is filled with bitterness; my suffering is far heavier than my groaning can express.

3 If only I knew where to find Him, that I might approach His very presence!

4 I would present my case before Him and prepare my arguments thoroughly, filling my mouth with reasoned appeals.

5 I desire to know the words He would speak in response to me, and to comprehend what He would say to me.

6 Will he argue against me with his immense power? No; instead, he would grant me strength.

7 In that place, the upright could present their case before him; then I would be forever freed from my judge.

8 Look, I move forward, but He is not there; I turn back, yet I cannot find Him.

9 On the left side, where He acts, I cannot perceive Him; He conceals Himself on the right side, so that I cannot see Him.

10 Yet He knows the path I take; when He has tested me, I will emerge as pure gold.

11 I have followed closely in his footsteps, staying true to his path without turning aside.

12 I have not turned away from the command of His lips; I have treasured the words of His mouth more than my daily bread.

13 Yet he remains steadfast in his purpose, and who can alter his will? Whatever his soul desires, that he accomplishes.

14 For He carries out what is determined for me, and many such purposes are within His power.

15 Therefore, I am deeply disturbed in his presence; when I think about him, I am filled with fear.

16 For God causes my heart to grow faint, and the Almighty fills me with distress.

17 Because I was not destroyed before the darkness, nor has He hidden the gloom from my sight.
