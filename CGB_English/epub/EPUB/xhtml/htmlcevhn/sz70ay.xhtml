Matthew Chapter 13

1 On that same day, Jesus left the house and went to sit by the shore of the sea.

2 Large crowds gathered around him, so he got into a boat and sat down to teach, while the entire crowd stood on the shore.

3 He taught them many things using parables, saying, "Listen carefully: A farmer went out to sow his seed."

4 As he was sowing, some seeds fell along the path, and the birds came and ate them up.

5 Some seeds fell on rocky ground, where there was little soil. They sprouted quickly because the soil was shallow.

6 When the sun rose, the plants were scorched by its heat; and since they had no deep roots, they withered and died.

7 Some of the seed fell among thorny plants, and when the thorns grew up, they crowded out and smothered the young plants.

8 But other seeds fell on good soil, and they produced a harvest—some yielding a hundred times as much, some sixty times, and some thirty times what was sown.

9 "Let those who have the ability to understand, pay attention and comprehend."

10 The disciples approached Him and asked, "Why do You speak to them using parables?"

11 He replied to them, "Because it has been granted to you to understand the hidden truths of the kingdom of heaven, but to them it has not been granted."

12 For whoever has something, more will be given to them, and they will have an abundance. But whoever has nothing, even what little they have will be taken away from them.

13 Therefore, I speak to them in parables: for though they see, they do not truly perceive; and though they hear, they do not truly listen, nor do they comprehend.

14 In them, the prophecy of Isaiah is fulfilled, which says: "You will indeed hear, but you will not understand; you will indeed see, but you will not perceive."

15 The hearts of these people have become calloused, and their ears are hard of hearing. They have shut their eyes tightly, so that they might not see with their eyes, hear with their ears, understand with their hearts, and turn back to me—for if they did, I would heal them.

16 But fortunate are your eyes, for they perceive; and your ears, for they listen.

17 Truly, I tell you, many prophets and righteous people longed to see what you now see, but they did not see it, and to hear what you now hear, but they did not hear it.

18 Listen carefully to the meaning of the parable about the sower.

19 When someone hears the message about the kingdom but does not grasp its meaning, the evil one comes and snatches away what was sown in their heart. This is the person who received the seed along the path.

20 But the one who received the seed in rocky soil is like the person who hears the message and immediately receives it with joy.

21 However, he has no deep root within himself and lasts only for a short time. When trouble or persecution arises because of the message, he quickly falls away.

22 The one who received the seed among the thorns represents the person who hears the word, but the worries of this life and the lure of wealth choke the word, making it unfruitful.

23 The one who received the seed in good soil represents the person who hears the message and comprehends it. This individual produces a fruitful harvest, yielding a crop—some a hundred times what was sown, some sixty, and some thirty.

24 Jesus presented another parable to them, saying: "The kingdom of heaven can be compared to a man who sowed good seed in his field."

25 While the people were sleeping, his enemy came and scattered weeds among the wheat, then went away.

26 But when the grain sprouted and produced its crop, the weeds also became visible.

27 So the servants of the landowner came and said to him, "Master, didn’t you sow good seed in your field? Where then did these weeds come from?"

28 He said to them, "An enemy has done this." The servants replied, "Do you want us to go and pull them up?"

29 But he replied, "No, because while you are pulling up the weeds, you might also uproot the wheat along with them."

30 Allow both to grow together until the harvest. At the time of harvest, I will instruct the reapers: First, gather the weeds and tie them into bundles to be burned. Then, gather the wheat and store it in my barn.

31 Jesus presented another parable to them, saying: "The kingdom of heaven is like a mustard seed that a man took and planted in his field.

32 Though it is the smallest of all seeds, when it grows, it becomes the largest of garden plants and turns into a tree, so that the birds of the sky come and make nests in its branches.

33 Jesus told them another parable: "The kingdom of heaven is like yeast that a woman took and mixed into about three measures of flour until it worked its way through the entire batch."

34 Jesus spoke all these things to the crowd using parables, and he did not address them without using a parable.

35 This happened to fulfill what was spoken by the prophet, who said: "I will open my mouth to speak in parables; I will reveal hidden truths that have been concealed since the creation of the world."

36 Then Jesus dismissed the crowd and went into the house. His disciples came to him and said, "Explain to us the meaning of the parable about the weeds in the field."

37 He replied to them, "The one who sows the good seed is the Son of Man."

38 The field represents the world; the good seed symbolizes the people who belong to the kingdom of God, while the weeds represent those who belong to the evil one.

39 The one who sowed them is the enemy, the devil; the harvest represents the culmination of this age; and the reapers are the angels.

40 Just as the weeds are collected and burned in the fire, so it will be at the conclusion of this age.

41 The Son of Man will send out his angels, and they will gather from his kingdom everything that causes sin and all who practice wickedness.

42 And they will be thrown into a fiery furnace, where there will be weeping and grinding of teeth.

43 At that time, the righteous will shine brightly like the sun in the kingdom of their Father. Whoever has the ability to understand, let them pay close attention.

44 Once more, the kingdom of heaven is like a treasure hidden in a field. When someone discovers it, they hide it again, and in their joy, they go and sell everything they own to buy that field.

45 Once more, the kingdom of heaven is like a merchant in search of fine pearls.

46 When he discovered a single pearl of exceptional value, he went and sold everything he owned in order to purchase it.

47 Once more, the kingdom of heaven is like a net that was thrown into the sea and gathered fish of every kind.

48 When the net was full, they pulled it to the shore, sat down, and sorted the fish. They collected the good ones into containers but threw the bad ones away.

49 This is how it will be at the end of the age: the angels will go out and separate the wicked from the righteous.

50 And they will be thrown into the fiery furnace, where there will be weeping and grinding of teeth.

51 Jesus asked them, "Have you understood all these things?" They replied to him, "Yes, Lord."

52 Then Jesus said to them, "Therefore, every teacher of the law who has been trained in the ways of the kingdom of heaven is like the owner of a house who brings out of his storeroom both new treasures and old."

53 After Jesus had finished teaching these parables, he left that place and went on his way.

54 When Jesus returned to his hometown, he taught the people in their synagogue, and they were utterly amazed. They said, "Where did this man get such wisdom and the power to perform these extraordinary deeds?"

55 Isn’t this the carpenter’s son? Isn’t his mother named Mary, and aren’t his brothers James, Joseph, Simon, and Judas?

56 And aren’t his sisters all here with us? So where did this man get all these things?

57 They took offense at him. But Jesus said to them, "A prophet is honored everywhere except in his own hometown and among his own family."

58 And He did not perform many miracles there because of their lack of faith.
