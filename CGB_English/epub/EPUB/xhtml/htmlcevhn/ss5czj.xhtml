Song of Songs Chapter 3

1 During the night, as I lay in my bed, I searched for the one whom my soul loves. I sought him, but I did not find him.

2 I will arise now and walk through the city, in the streets and the open squares, seeking the one whom my soul loves. I searched for him, but I did not find him.

3 The guards who patrol the city found me, and I asked them, "Have you seen the one my heart loves?"

4 I had only gone a short distance from them when I found the one my soul loves. I took hold of him and would not let him go until I had brought him into my mother’s house, into the room of the one who gave me life.

5 I solemnly urge you, O daughters of Jerusalem, by the gazelles and the does of the field, do not disturb or awaken my beloved until he is ready.

6 Who is this coming up from the wilderness, rising like columns of smoke, fragrant with myrrh and frankincense, scented with all the finest spices of the merchant?

7 Look at his bed, which belongs to Solomon; sixty mighty warriors surround it, chosen from the bravest of Israel.

8 They are all skilled in warfare, each one trained in the use of the sword. Every man carries his sword at his side, ready for battle, prepared to face the terrors of the night.

9 King Solomon constructed a chariot for himself using the finest cedar wood from Lebanon.

10 He crafted its pillars from silver, its base from gold, its covering from purple cloth, and its interior was adorned with a mosaic of love, all for the daughters of Jerusalem.

11 Go out, you daughters of Zion, and look upon King Solomon wearing the crown with which his mother crowned him on the day of his wedding, the day of his heart’s greatest joy.
