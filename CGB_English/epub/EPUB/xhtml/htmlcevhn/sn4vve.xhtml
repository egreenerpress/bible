II Timothy Chapter 3

1 Be aware of this as well: in the final days, difficult and dangerous times will arise.

2 For people will be self-centered, greedy, boastful, arrogant, slanderous, rebellious toward their parents, ungrateful, and devoid of moral integrity.

3 They lack natural love, break agreements, slander others, lack self-control, act with cruelty, and show contempt for what is good.

4 Rebellious, reckless, arrogant, and more devoted to seeking pleasure than to loving God.

5 They maintain an outward appearance of religious devotion, yet they reject its true power and essence. Keep your distance from such people.

6 For among them are those who sneak into households and captivate vulnerable women burdened by their sins, swayed by various desires.

7 They are always seeking knowledge, yet they are never able to attain a true understanding of the truth.

8 Just as Jannes and Jambres opposed Moses, so these individuals also oppose the truth. They are people with corrupted minds, disqualified and rejected when it comes to genuine faith.

9 However, they will not advance any further, for their foolishness will be made evident to everyone, just as the folly of those before them was revealed.

10 However, you have thoroughly understood my teaching, my way of life, my intentions, my faith, my endurance, my love, and my steadfastness.

11 The hardships and sufferings that befell me in Antioch, Iconium, and Lystra—the persecutions I endured—yet from all of them, the Lord rescued me.

12 Indeed, all who desire to live a godly life in union with Christ Jesus will face persecution.

13 However, wicked individuals and impostors will grow increasingly corrupt, leading others astray and themselves being led astray.

14 But you must remain steadfast in what you have learned and firmly believe, knowing from whom you received this teaching.

15 From your childhood, you have been acquainted with the sacred Scriptures, which have the power to make you wise for salvation through faith in Christ Jesus.

16 All Scripture is divinely inspired by God and is beneficial for teaching, for exposing error, for correcting faults, and for training in righteous living.

17 So that the servant of God may be fully equipped and thoroughly prepared for every good work.
