Proverbs Chapter 1

1 The proverbs of Solomon, son of David, king of Israel:

2 To gain wisdom and learn discipline; to understand insightful teachings;

3 To gain the teaching of wisdom, along with understanding of justice, sound judgment, and fairness;

4 To grant insight to the inexperienced, and to the young, knowledge and sound judgment.

5 A wise person will listen attentively and grow in knowledge, while someone with understanding will gain access to sound guidance and wise counsel.

6 To grasp the meaning of a proverb and its explanation, along with the teachings of the wise and their profound sayings.

7 Reverence for the LORD is the foundation of true knowledge, but those who are foolish reject wisdom and correction.

8 My child, listen to your father’s teaching, and do not abandon your mother’s guidance.

9 For they will be like a graceful wreath adorning your head and like precious chains around your neck.

10 My child, if those who do wrong try to persuade you, do not give in to them.

11 If they say, "Come with us, let us ambush to shed blood; let us lie in wait to harm the innocent without reason."

12 Let us completely destroy them, as if they were being swallowed alive by the grave; let them be consumed entirely, like those who descend into the pit.

13 We will discover all kinds of valuable treasures; we will fill our homes with plunder.

14 "Join us and share in our fortunes; let us all pool our resources together."

15 My child, do not join them in their ways; keep your steps far from their path.

16 For their feet rush toward evil, and they are quick to shed blood.

17 Certainly, it is pointless to set a trap openly in full view of a bird.

18 They set traps for their own blood; they ambush their own lives.

19 This is the way of all who are greedy for unjust gain; it takes away the life of those who possess it.

20 Wisdom calls out in the open; she raises her voice in the public squares.

21 She calls out in the busiest places, at the entrances of the city gates, and in the heart of the city, proclaiming her message, saying,

22 How long will you who are naive cling to your naivety? How long will mockers take pleasure in their mocking, and fools despise wisdom?

23 Turn back in response to my correction: listen carefully, and I will pour out my spirit upon you; I will make my words known to you.

24 Because I have called out to you, and you refused to listen; I have reached out my hand to you, and no one paid attention.

25 But you have rejected all my advice and refused to accept my correction.

26 I too will laugh at your disaster; I will ridicule you when terror strikes.

27 When your terror arrives like a devastating storm, and your ruin sweeps in like a whirlwind; when distress and anguish overwhelm you.

28 At that time, they will call out to me, but I will not respond; they will earnestly seek me, but they will not find me.

29 Because they rejected wisdom and refused to embrace reverence for the LORD,

30 They rejected all my advice; they scorned every word of my correction.

31 Therefore, they will consume the consequences of their own actions and be fully satisfied with the outcomes of their own schemes.

32 The waywardness of the naive will lead to their downfall, and the complacency of fools will bring about their ruin.

33 But whoever listens to me will live securely and will be at peace, free from the dread of harm.
