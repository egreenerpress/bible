Luke Chapter 4

1 And Jesus, filled with the Holy Spirit, returned from the Jordan and was guided by the Spirit into the wilderness.

2 For forty days, he was tempted by the devil. During that time, he ate nothing, and when the days were over, he was hungry.

3 The devil said to him, "If you are the Son of God, command this stone to become bread."

4 And Jesus responded to him, saying, "It is written: 'One does not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from God.'"

5 Then the devil led him up to a high mountain and showed him, in an instant, all the kingdoms of the world.

6 The devil said to him, "I will give you all this authority and the splendor that comes with it, for it has been handed over to me, and I can give it to anyone I choose."

7 If you are willing to bow down and honor me, everything will be yours.

8 Jesus responded and said to him, "Step back, Satan! For it is written: 'You shall worship the Lord your God, and Him alone shall you serve.'"

9 Then he led him to Jerusalem and placed him on the highest point of the temple. He said to him, "If you are the Son of God, throw yourself down from here."

10 For it is written, "He will command his angels to watch over you and protect you."

11 They will lift you up in their hands, so that you will not strike your foot against a stone.

12 And Jesus responded to him, saying, "It is written, 'You must not put the Lord your God to the test.'"

13 After the devil had finished every attempt to tempt him, he left him for a time.

14 And Jesus, empowered by the Spirit, returned to Galilee. News about Him spread throughout the surrounding region.

15 He taught in their synagogues and was praised by everyone.

16 He came to Nazareth, where he had been raised. As was his usual practice, he entered the synagogue on the Sabbath day and stood up to read.

17 Then the scroll of the prophet Isaiah was handed to him. He unrolled the scroll and found the place where it was written:

18 The Spirit of the Lord is upon me because He has chosen and appointed me to proclaim good news to the poor. He has sent me to bring healing to those whose hearts are shattered, to announce freedom to those held captive, to restore sight to the blind, and to release those who are oppressed and broken.

19 To proclaim the year of the Lord's favor.

20 Then he closed the scroll, handed it back to the attendant, and sat down. The eyes of everyone in the synagogue were fixed intently on him.

21 And he began to say to them, "Today, this scripture has been fulfilled as you hear it."

22 Everyone present acknowledged and marveled at the kind and profound words that came from his mouth. They said, "Isn’t this Joseph’s son?"

23 And He said to them, “You will undoubtedly quote this saying to Me: ‘Doctor, heal yourself.’ Whatever we have heard was done in Capernaum, do the same here in your hometown.”

24 And He said, "Truly I tell you, no prophet is welcomed in his own homeland."

25 But I assure you, there were many widows in Israel during the time of Elijah, when the sky was closed for three and a half years and a severe famine spread across the entire land.

26 However, Elijah was not sent to any of them, except to Zarephath, a town in Sidon, to a woman who was a widow.

27 During the time of the prophet Elisha, there were many lepers in Israel, yet none of them were healed except Naaman the Syrian.

28 When all those in the synagogue heard these things, they were filled with anger.

29 They stood up, forced him out of the city, and brought him to the edge of the hill on which their city was built, intending to throw him off the cliff.

30 But He walked right through the middle of them and went on His way.

31 He went down to Capernaum, a city in Galilee, and taught the people there on the Sabbath days.

32 The people were amazed at his teaching, because he spoke with authority and power.

33 In the synagogue, there was a man who was possessed by an unclean spirit, and he shouted in a loud voice,

34 They cried out, "Leave us alone! What do you want with us, Jesus of Nazareth? Have you come to destroy us? I know who you are—the Holy One of God!"

35 Jesus sternly commanded the unclean spirit, saying, "Be silent, and come out of him!" Then the demon threw the man down in the middle of the crowd, but it came out of him without causing any harm.

36 Everyone was astonished and began talking among themselves, saying, "What kind of statement is this? With such authority and power, He commands the unclean spirits, and they come out!"

37 And his reputation spread throughout every region of the surrounding area.

38 He left the synagogue and went to Simon’s house. Simon’s mother-in-law was suffering from a severe fever, and they pleaded with him on her behalf.

39 He stood beside her, spoke sternly to the fever, and it left her. At once, she got up and began to serve them.

40 As the sun was going down, all those who had people suffering from various illnesses brought them to him. He placed his hands on each one of them and healed them.

41 Demons also came out of many people, shouting and declaring, “You are the Messiah, the Son of God!” But Jesus rebuked them and would not allow them to speak, because they knew he was the Messiah.

42 When morning came, he left and went to a secluded place. But the people searched for him, came to where he was, and urged him not to leave them.

43 And He said to them, "I must proclaim the kingdom of God to other towns as well, because this is the purpose for which I was sent."

44 And he proclaimed his message in the synagogues throughout Galilee.
