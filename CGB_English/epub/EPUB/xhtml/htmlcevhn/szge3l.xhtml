Ezekiel Chapter 10

1 Then I looked, and there, in the expanse above the heads of the cherubim, something like a sapphire stone appeared above them, resembling the form of a throne.

2 Then He spoke to the man dressed in linen and said, "Go between the wheels beneath the cherubim, take burning coals from the space between them, and scatter them over the city." And he went in as I watched.

3 The cherubim were positioned on the right side of the temple as the man entered, and the cloud filled the inner courtyard.

4 Then the glory of the LORD rose from the cherub and positioned itself above the entrance of the temple. The temple was filled with the cloud, and the courtyard was radiant with the brilliance of the LORD's glory.

5 The sound of the cherubim's wings could be heard even in the outer courtyard, like the voice of the Almighty God when He speaks.

6 Then it happened that when he gave the command to the man dressed in linen, saying, "Take fire from between the wheels, from between the cherubim," the man went in and stood beside the wheels.

7 One of the cherubim reached out its hand from among the cherubim to the fire that was between them, took some of it, and placed it into the hands of the man dressed in linen. He took it and went out.

8 And beneath the wings of the cherubim, the shape of a human hand became visible.

9 When I looked, I saw four wheels beside the cherubim, one wheel next to each cherub. The appearance of the wheels was like the gleaming color of a beryl stone.

10 As for their appearance, the four of them looked alike, resembling a wheel within a wheel.

11 As they moved, they went straight forward in any of the four directions without turning aside. They went wherever the front was facing, following its lead without veering to the right or left as they advanced.

12 Their entire bodies, their backs, their hands, and their wings, as well as the wheels, were completely covered with eyes all around—even the wheels belonging to the four of them.

13 As for the wheels, I heard a voice calling out to them, saying, "O wheel!"

14 Each of them had four faces: the first face was that of a cherub, the second was the face of a human, the third was the face of a lion, and the fourth was the face of an eagle.

15 The cherubim were raised up. This is the living being that I had seen by the Kebar River.

16 When the cherubim moved, the wheels moved alongside them. And when the cherubim raised their wings to ascend from the ground, the wheels did not turn away from their position beside them.

17 When the living creatures moved, the wheels moved with them; and when the creatures rose up, the wheels rose as well, for the spirit of the living beings was within the wheels.

18 Then the glory of the LORD moved away from the entrance of the temple and positioned itself above the cherubim.

19 The cherubim raised their wings and ascended from the ground before my eyes. As they departed, the wheels moved alongside them. They came to a halt at the entrance of the east gate of the LORD’s temple, and the glory of the God of Israel rested above them.

20 This is the living being I saw beneath the God of Israel by the Kebar River, and I recognized that they were cherubim.

21 Each of them had four faces and four wings. Beneath their wings, there was something resembling human hands.

22 The appearance of their faces was the same as the faces I had seen by the river Kebar—their forms and their features were identical. Each one moved straight forward.
