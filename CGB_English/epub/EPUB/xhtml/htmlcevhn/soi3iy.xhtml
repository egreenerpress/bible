Hosea Chapter 13

1 When Ephraim spoke with reverence, he held a position of honor in Israel; but when he turned to worship Baal and sinned, he brought about his own downfall.

2 Now they persist in sinning more and more, crafting molten images from their silver—idols shaped by their own imagination, the work of skilled artisans. They even say of these idols, "Let those who offer sacrifices kiss the calves."

3 Therefore, they will be like the morning mist, like the early dew that vanishes, like chaff blown away by the whirlwind from the threshing floor, and like smoke rising from a chimney.

4 Yet I am the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt. You shall acknowledge no god but me, for there is no savior apart from me.

5 I knew you in the wilderness, in a land of severe drought.

6 Because they had abundant pasture, they became satisfied; when they were satisfied, their hearts grew proud. As a result, they have forgotten me.

7 Therefore, I will confront them like a lion; like a leopard lurking by the path, I will watch over them.

8 I will confront them like a bear robbed of her cubs, and I will tear open the covering of their heart. There I will consume them like a lion; the wild beast will rip them apart.

9 O Israel, you have brought about your own downfall; yet in me lies your salvation.

10 I will be your king. Is there anyone else who can save you in all your cities? Where are your judges, of whom you said, "Give us a king and rulers"?

11 I appointed a king for you in my anger, and I removed him in my fury.

12 The guilt of Ephraim is stored up; his sin is kept hidden.

13 The pains of a woman in labor will come upon him; he is a foolish son, for he should not delay in the time of birth.

14 I will rescue them from the grasp of the grave; I will deliver them from death. O death, I will be your plague; O grave, I will be your destruction. Compassion will be hidden from my sight.

15 Even if he flourishes among his brothers, a scorching east wind will arise—the wind of the LORD, blowing from the wilderness. His spring will dry up, and his fountain will run dry. He will plunder the treasure of all his precious possessions.

16 Samaria will be laid waste, for she has rebelled against her God. Her people will fall by the sword, her infants will be brutally killed, and her pregnant women will be torn open.
