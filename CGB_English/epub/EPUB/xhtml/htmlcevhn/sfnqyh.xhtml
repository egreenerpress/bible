Job Chapter 17

1 My life is fading, my days are coming to an end, and the grave awaits me.

2 Are there not mockers among me? And does my eye not continually witness their provocation?

3 "Now, stand as my guarantor; who will pledge themselves on my behalf?"

4 For you have concealed their hearts from understanding; therefore, you will not honor them.

5 Whoever speaks deceitfully to their friends, even the eyes of their children will grow dim.

6 He has made me a subject of ridicule among the people; I have become a mocking song to them. In the past, I was like a tambourine, a source of joy and celebration, but now I am scorned and despised.

7 My vision has grown dim because of my grief, and all my limbs feel as insubstantial as a shadow.

8 Righteous people will be appalled by this, and the blameless will rise up against the hypocrite.

9 The one who is righteous will continue steadfastly on their path, and those with pure hands will grow ever stronger.

10 But as for all of you, turn back and come here now, for I cannot find a single wise person among you.

11 My days have come to an end, my plans have been shattered, and even the deepest desires of my heart have been cut short.

12 They turn night into day, claiming that light is near even in the midst of darkness.

13 If I delay, the grave will become my home; I have prepared my resting place in the realm of darkness.

14 I have declared to decay, "You are my father," and to the worm, "You are my mother and my sister."

15 And now, where is my hope? As for my hope, who can see it?

16 They will descend to the gates of the grave, when our shared rest lies in the dust.
