Job Chapter 14

1 A person born of a woman lives but a short time, and that life is filled with hardship and trouble.

2 He emerges like a flower, only to be cut down; he vanishes like a shadow, and does not endure.

3 Do you really keep your gaze fixed on someone like me? Will you bring me into judgment before you?

4 Who can produce something pure from what is impure? No one can.

5 Since the length of his days is fixed, and the number of his months is determined by you, you have set limits for him that he cannot exceed.

6 Leave him alone, so that he may find rest, until he completes his appointed time, like a hired worker fulfilling his day.

7 For there is hope for a tree, even if it is cut down, that it will sprout again, and its tender shoots will not fail to grow.

8 Even if its root grows old in the earth and its stump dies in the ground,

9 Yet, when it senses the moisture of water, it will sprout again and produce new branches like a young plant.

10 But a person dies and fades away; indeed, a human breathes their last, and where do they go?

11 As the waters vanish from the sea, and the river dwindles and dries up completely,

12 A person lies down in death and does not rise again; until the heavens are no more, they will not awaken or be roused from their sleep.

13 Oh, if only you would conceal me in the grave, hide me away until your anger has passed! If only you would set a specific time for me and then remember me!

14 If a person dies, will they live again? Throughout all the days of my appointed time, I will wait until my transformation comes.

15 You will call, and I will answer you; you will long for the work of your hands.

16 You carefully observe every step I take; do you not also keep watch over my wrongdoing?

17 My wrongdoing is securely stored away, and you carefully preserve my sin.

18 Certainly, even the mountain that crumbles will come to nothing, and the rock will be dislodged from its place.

19 The relentless waters erode even the hardest stones; you sweep away the produce that springs from the earth's soil, and you shatter the hopes of humanity.

20 You always overpower him, and he passes away; you alter his appearance and send him away.

21 His children rise to prominence, but he is unaware of it; they are brought low, yet he does not perceive it concerning them.

22 Yet his body will be racked with pain, and his innermost being will grieve.
