Ezra Chapter 9

1 After these events had taken place, the leaders approached me and said, "The people of Israel, including the priests and the Levites, have not kept themselves separate from the neighboring peoples who practice detestable customs. They have followed the ways of the Canaanites, Hittites, Perizzites, Jebusites, Ammonites, Moabites, Egyptians, and Amorites."

2 For they have married the daughters of these foreign peoples, both for themselves and for their sons, causing the holy descendants to intermingle with the peoples of those lands. Indeed, the leaders and officials have been the primary ones responsible for this unfaithfulness.

3 When I heard this news, I tore my clothes and my cloak, pulled out the hair from my head and beard, and sat down in stunned silence.

4 Then all those who were deeply moved by the words of the God of Israel, because of the unfaithfulness of the exiles, gathered around me. I sat there in stunned silence until the time of the evening sacrifice.

5 At the time of the evening offering, I rose from my state of deep sorrow. After tearing my clothes and my cloak, I knelt down and lifted my hands in prayer to the LORD my God.

6 And he said, "O my God, I am filled with shame and embarrassment to even lift my face toward you, my God, because our sins have piled up over our heads, and our guilt has risen as high as the heavens."

7 From the time of our ancestors until now, we have been deeply guilty of wrongdoing. Because of our sins, we, along with our kings and priests, have been handed over to the power of foreign rulers—subjected to the sword, captivity, plunder, and public disgrace, as is evident today.

8 For a brief time, the LORD our God has shown us grace by allowing a remnant of us to survive and escape. He has given us a secure foothold in His holy place, so that our God might bring light to our eyes and grant us a measure of renewal in the midst of our oppression.

9 For we were once slaves, yet our God did not abandon us in our captivity. Instead, He showed us mercy in the presence of the kings of Persia, granting us renewal, enabling us to rebuild the house of our God, restore its ruins, and provide protection for us in Judah and Jerusalem.

10 And now, O our God, what can we say in light of all this? For we have abandoned your commandments.

11 You have instructed us through your servants, the prophets, saying, "The land you are entering to possess is defiled by the impurity of the people living there, who have filled it from one end to the other with their detestable practices and uncleanness."

12 Therefore, do not give your daughters in marriage to their sons, nor take their daughters for your sons. Do not seek peace or prosperity through alliances with them, so that you may remain strong and enjoy the abundance of the land, leaving it as an everlasting inheritance for your children.

13 After all that has happened to us because of our wicked actions and our great guilt—since you, our God, have punished us less severely than our sins deserve and have granted us this deliverance—

14 Should we once more violate your commandments and form alliances with these detestable peoples? Would you not be furious with us to the point of destroying us completely, leaving no survivors or anyone to escape?

15 O LORD, God of Israel, you are righteous: for we are the few who remain, having escaped destruction to this day. Now we stand before you in our guilt, for because of our sins, we cannot stand in your presence.
