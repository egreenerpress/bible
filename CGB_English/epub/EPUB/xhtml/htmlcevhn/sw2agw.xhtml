Zechariah Chapter 2

1 I raised my eyes once more and looked, and there before me was a man holding a measuring line in his hand.

2 Then I asked, "Where are you going?" And he replied, "To measure Jerusalem, to determine its width and its length."

3 Then, suddenly, the angel who had been speaking with me moved forward, and another angel came out to meet him.

4 Then he said to him, "Go quickly and tell this young man, 'Jerusalem will be so full of people and livestock that it will be like a city without walls.'"

5 For I, declares the LORD, will be to her a wall of fire surrounding her, and I will be the glory within her midst.

6 "Listen! Come out and flee from the land of the north," declares the LORD, "for I have scattered you to the four corners of the heavens," says the LORD.

7 Free yourself, O Zion, you who live among the people of Babylon.

8 For this is what the LORD of hosts says: After the time of glory, He has sent me to the nations that plundered you, for whoever harms you harms the very center of His care and protection.

9 Listen carefully, for I will raise my hand against them, and they will become plunder for those who serve them. Then you will understand that the LORD of hosts has truly sent me.

10 Sing joyfully and celebrate, O people of Zion, for behold, I am coming, and I will live among you, declares the LORD.

11 And in that day, many nations will unite themselves to the LORD and become my people. I will dwell among you, and you will know that the LORD of armies has sent me to you.

12 The LORD will claim Judah as His own possession in the sacred land, and He will once again choose Jerusalem.

13 Be silent, all people, before the LORD, for He has risen from His holy dwelling place.
