Romans Chapter 10

1 Brothers and sisters, my deepest longing and prayer to God for Israel is that they may experience salvation.

2 I can testify that they have a deep passion for God, but it is not guided by true understanding.

3 Because they were unaware of God's way of making people right with Him and sought instead to establish their own standard of righteousness, they did not submit to the righteousness that comes from God.

4 For Christ is the fulfillment of the law, bringing righteousness to everyone who believes.

5 For Moses explains the righteousness that comes from the law in this way: "The person who follows these commandments will find life through them."

6 The righteousness that comes through faith speaks in this manner: Do not say in your heart, "Who will ascend into heaven?" (that is, to bring Christ down from above).

7 Or, who will go down into the depths? (that is, to bring Christ back from the dead.)

8 But what does it say? The message is near you, in your mouth and in your heartâ€”that is, the message of faith that we proclaim.

9 If you openly declare with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and sincerely believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved.

10 For it is with the heart that one believes, leading to righteousness, and with the mouth that one declares faith, resulting in salvation.

11 The scripture declares, "Whoever believes in him will never be put to shame."

12 For there is no distinction between the Jew and the Gentile, since the same Lord is Lord of all and richly blesses all who call on him.

13 For everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be delivered.

14 How, then, can people call on someone they do not believe in? And how can they believe in someone they have not heard about? And how can they hear about him unless someone proclaims the message?

15 And how can they proclaim the message unless they are sent? As it is written: "How beautiful are the feet of those who bring good news, who announce peace and proclaim the joyful message of good things!"

16 However, not everyone has obeyed the good news. As Isaiah says, "Lord, who has believed what we have proclaimed?"

17 Therefore, faith arises from listening, and listening comes through the message of God.

18 But I ask, have they not heard? Indeed they have! Their message has gone out to all the earth, and their words have reached to the farthest corners of the world.

19 But I ask, did Israel not understand? First, Moses declares, "I will make you envious by those who are not a nation; I will provoke you to anger by a foolish people."

20 But Isaiah speaks boldly and says, "I was found by those who were not seeking me; I revealed myself to those who were not asking for me."

21 But concerning Israel, God says, "Throughout the entire day, I have held out my hands to a people who are disobedient and resistant."
