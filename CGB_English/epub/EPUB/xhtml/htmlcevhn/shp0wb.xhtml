II Chronicles Chapter 10

1 Rehoboam went to Shechem, for all Israel had gathered there to proclaim him as king.

2 When Jeroboam, the son of Nebat, who had fled to Egypt to escape King Solomon, heard the news, he returned from Egypt.

3 They sent for him and summoned him. Then Jeroboam and all the people of Israel came and spoke to Rehoboam, saying,

4 Your father imposed a harsh burden on us. Now, lighten the heavy labor and the oppressive yoke your father placed upon us, and we will serve you.

5 He said to them, "Return to me after three days." Then the people left.

6 King Rehoboam sought advice from the elders who had served his father Solomon during his lifetime. He asked them, "What guidance do you offer me on how to respond to this people?"

7 They said to him, "If you are kind to these people, treat them favorably, and speak to them with gracious words, they will remain your loyal servants forever."

8 However, he rejected the advice given to him by the elders and instead sought counsel from the young men who had grown up with him and served in his presence.

9 And he said to them, "What counsel do you offer so that we may respond to this people, who have spoken to me, saying, 'Lighten the heavy burden that your father placed on us'?"

10 The young men who had grown up with him advised him, saying, "This is how you should respond to the people who have spoken to you, saying, 'Your father made our burden heavy, but you should make it lighter for us.' 11 My father imposed a heavy burden on you, but I will make it even heavier. My father disciplined you with whips, but I will discipline you with scorpions.

12 So Jeroboam and all the people returned to Rehoboam on the third day, just as the king had instructed, saying, "Come back to me on the third day."

13 The king responded to them harshly, and King Rehoboam rejected the advice of the elders.

14 He responded to them according to the counsel of the young men, saying, "My father made your burden heavy, but I will make it even heavier. My father disciplined you with whips, but I will discipline you with scorpions."

15 The king did not listen to the people, for this turn of events was from God, so that the LORD might fulfill the word he had spoken through Ahijah the Shilonite to Jeroboam, the son of Nebat.

16 When all Israel saw that the king refused to listen to them, the people responded to the king, saying, "What share do we have in David? We have no inheritance in the son of Jesse. To your tents, O Israel! Now, David, take care of your own house." So all Israel returned to their homes.

17 However, concerning the Israelites who lived in the cities of Judah, Rehoboam ruled as their king.

18 Then King Rehoboam sent Hadoram, who was in charge of the forced labor, but the people of Israel stoned him to death. King Rehoboam, however, quickly mounted his chariot and fled to Jerusalem.

19 And to this day, Israel has remained in rebellion against the house of David.
