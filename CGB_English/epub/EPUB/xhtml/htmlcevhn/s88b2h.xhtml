Hebrews Chapter 11

1 Faith is the confident assurance of what we hope for, the firm conviction of realities we cannot see.

2 For through faith, the people of old received God's approval.

3 By faith, we comprehend that the universe was created by the command of God, so that what is visible came into existence from what is not visible.

4 By faith, Abel presented to God a superior sacrifice compared to Cain. Through this act, he received confirmation that he was righteous, as God bore witness to his offerings. And though he died, his faith still speaks to us today.

5 By faith, Enoch was taken up so that he did not experience death; he could not be found, because God had taken him away. For before he was taken, he had received the testimony that he had pleased God.

6 But without faith, it is impossible to please God, for whoever approaches Him must believe that He exists and that He rewards those who earnestly seek Him.

7 By faith, Noah, having been divinely warned about events that were not yet visible, acted with reverent obedience and built an ark to save his household. Through this act, he condemned the world and became an heir of the righteousness that comes by faith.

8 By faith, Abraham obeyed when he was called to leave for a place that he would later receive as his inheritance. He set out without knowing where he was going.

9 By faith, he lived as a foreigner in the land of promise, as though it were not his own, dwelling in tents with Isaac and Jacob, who were fellow heirs of the same promise.

10 For he was seeking a city with enduring foundations, whose architect and creator is God.

11 By faith, Sarah herself received the ability to conceive, even though she was past the age of childbearing, and she gave birth to a child. This happened because she considered the one who had made the promise to be trustworthy.

12 Therefore, from one man—and he as good as dead—came descendants as numerous as the stars in the sky and as countless as the sand along the seashore.

13 All of these individuals died while still holding firmly to their faith, even though they had not yet received the fulfillment of the promises. Instead, they saw those promises from a distance, welcomed them with confidence, and acknowledged that they were foreigners and temporary residents on the earth.

14 For those who speak in this way make it clear that they are seeking a homeland.

15 Indeed, if they had kept thinking about the land they had left behind, they would have had the chance to go back.

16 But now they seek a better homeland—a heavenly one. Therefore, God is not ashamed to be called their God, for He has prepared a city for them.

17 By faith Abraham, when he was tested, presented Isaac as an offering. He who had received the promises was willing to sacrifice his one and only son,

18 Concerning him, it was declared, "Through Isaac your descendants will be named."

19 Believing that God had the power to raise him from the dead, and in a sense, he did receive him back, as if from death.

20 By faith, Isaac pronounced blessings upon Jacob and Esau regarding their future destinies.

21 By faith, Jacob, as he was nearing death, blessed each of Joseph’s sons and bowed in worship, leaning on the top of his staff.

22 By faith, Joseph, as he was nearing death, spoke about the future exodus of the Israelites from Egypt and gave instructions regarding his bones.

23 By faith, when Moses was born, his parents hid him for three months because they saw that he was a special child, and they did not fear the king’s decree.

24 By faith, when Moses had grown up, he refused to be known as the son of Pharaoh’s daughter.

25 He chose to endure hardship alongside God's people rather than to indulge in the fleeting pleasures of sin.

26 Considering the disgrace associated with Christ to be of greater value than all the wealth of Egypt, because he was focused on the future reward.

27 By faith, he left Egypt, unafraid of the king’s anger, for he persevered as though he could see the One who is invisible.

28 By faith, he observed the Passover and the application of blood, so that the destroyer of the firstborn would not harm them.

29 By faith, they crossed the Red Sea as if it were dry ground. When the Egyptians attempted to do the same, they were drowned.

30 By faith, the walls of Jericho collapsed after the Israelites had marched around them for seven days.

31 By faith, the prostitute Rahab did not perish with those who refused to believe, for she welcomed the spies in peace.

32 What more can I say? Time would not allow me to fully recount the stories of Gideon, Barak, Samson, and Jephthah, nor of David, Samuel, and the prophets.

33 Through their faith, they conquered kingdoms, established justice, received what was promised, and silenced the roar of lions.

34 They extinguished the fury of flames, escaped the threat of the sword, found strength in their weakness, became mighty in battle, and caused foreign armies to flee.

35 Women had their dead restored to life through resurrection. Others endured torture, refusing to accept release, so that they might attain a more glorious resurrection.

36 Others endured harsh ridicule and brutal beatings, and beyond that, they faced chains and imprisonment.

37 They were stoned to death, they were cut in two, they were tested, and they were killed with the sword. They wandered about dressed in sheepskins and goatskins, destitute, oppressed, and mistreated.

38 The world was not worthy of them. They wandered through deserts and mountains, living in holes in the ground and caves of the earth.

39 All these individuals, commended for their faith, did not receive the fulfillment of what was promised.

40 God has prepared something superior for us, so that they would not reach completeness apart from us.
