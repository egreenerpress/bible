Proverbs Chapter 12

1 Whoever values discipline values knowledge, but whoever rejects correction is foolish and senseless.

2 A person of integrity receives favor from the LORD, but He will condemn the one who devises evil.

3 A person cannot secure their position through wickedness, but the foundation of the righteous will remain unshaken.

4 A woman of noble character is a crown to her husband, but one who brings disgrace is like decay in his bones.

5 The intentions of the righteous are upright, but the schemes of the wicked are full of deceit.

6 The words of the wicked are like a hidden trap, seeking to shed blood, but the speech of the upright will rescue them.

7 The wicked are utterly destroyed and cease to exist, but the household of the righteous remains steadfast and endures.

8 A person will be praised according to their wisdom, but one with a corrupt heart will be held in contempt.

9 A person who is looked down upon but has a servant is better off than someone who boasts of their own importance but lacks even bread.

10 A righteous person cares deeply for the well-being of their animals, but even the so-called compassion of the wicked is marked by cruelty.

11 Whoever cultivates their land will have plenty of food, but whoever chases after empty pursuits lacks good judgment.

12 The wicked crave the destructive schemes of evildoers, but the foundation of the righteous produces enduring fruit.

13 The wicked are trapped by the words of their own wrongdoing, but the righteous will escape from distress.

14 A person will be fulfilled by the good that comes from their words, and the reward of their actions will be given back to them.

15 A fool's way seems right in his own eyes, but whoever listens to advice shows wisdom.

16 A fool's anger is immediately evident, but a wise person conceals dishonor.

17 The one who speaks truth reveals righteousness, but a false witness spreads deceit.

18 There are those whose words cut deeply like the thrust of a sword, but the speech of the wise brings healing.

19 A truthful statement will endure forever, but a deceitful tongue lasts only for a moment.

20 Deception resides in the hearts of those who devise wicked plans, but those who promote peace and offer wise counsel will experience joy.

21 No harm will come to the righteous, but the wicked will be overwhelmed with trouble.

22 Dishonest speech is detestable to the LORD, but those who act with integrity bring Him great pleasure.

23 A wise person keeps knowledge to themselves, but the heart of fools broadcasts foolishness.

24 The hand of the diligent will rise to leadership, but the lazy will be forced into servitude.

25 A burdened heart weighs a person down, but an encouraging word brings joy and lifts the spirit.

26 The one who lives righteously is of greater worth than their neighbor, but the path of the wicked leads them astray.

27 The lazy person does not even cook what he has caught in the hunt, but the wealth of a hardworking individual is of great value.

28 The path of righteousness leads to life; and along its course, there is no death.
