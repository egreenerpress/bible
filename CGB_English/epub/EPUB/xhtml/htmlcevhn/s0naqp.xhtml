Jeremiah Chapter 15

1 Then the LORD said to me, "Even if Moses and Samuel were to stand before me, pleading on their behalf, my heart would not be moved in favor of this people. Send them away from my presence and let them go."

2 And this is what will happen: if they ask you, "Where should we go?" then you are to tell them, "This is what the LORD says: Those destined for death, to death; those destined for the sword, to the sword; those destined for famine, to famine; and those destined for captivity, to captivity."

3 The Lord declares: "I will assign four kinds of punishment to them: the sword to kill, the dogs to tear apart, the birds of the sky, and the wild animals of the earth, to devour and destroy them."

4 I will scatter them among all the kingdoms of the earth because of what Manasseh, the son of Hezekiah, king of Judah, did in Jerusalem.

5 Who will show you compassion, O Jerusalem? Who will mourn for you? Who will turn aside to inquire about your well-being?

6 The LORD declares, "You have abandoned me; you have turned away. Therefore, I will raise my hand against you and bring about your destruction. I am weary of holding back my judgment."

7 I will scatter them like chaff at the gates of the land; I will take away their children and bring ruin to my people, for they refuse to turn back from their ways.

8 Their widows have become more numerous to me than the grains of sand by the seas. I have brought against them—against the mother of young men—a destroyer at midday. I have caused him to descend upon them suddenly, bringing terror upon the city.

9 The woman who bore seven children now grows weak; she breathes her last breath. Her sun has set while it was still day; she is disgraced and humiliated. As for the rest of them, I will hand them over to the sword before their enemies, declares the LORD.

10 How terrible it is for me, my mother, that you gave birth to me—a man who causes strife and conflict throughout the land! I have neither borrowed money with interest nor lent it to others at interest, yet everyone curses me.

11 The LORD declared, "Truly, it will go well with those who remain of your people. Indeed, I will ensure that your enemies treat you kindly during times of trouble and distress."

12 Can iron shatter the iron from the north, or break the strength of steel?

13 I will hand over your wealth and your treasures as plunder, without any compensation, because of all your sins, throughout all your territories.

14 I will cause you to march with your enemies into a land you do not know, for a fire has been ignited by my anger, and it will burn against you.

15 O LORD, you know all things. Remember me, and come to my aid. Take action against those who persecute me. Do not let me perish while you patiently endure their wrongdoing. Acknowledge that I have endured this disgrace because of my loyalty to you.

16 When I discovered your words, I eagerly embraced them; your message became a source of joy and delight to my heart, for I bear your name, O LORD, God of all power and might.

17 I did not join the gathering of those who mock, nor did I take pleasure in their company. I chose to sit alone because of your influence upon me, for you have filled me with deep concern and righteous anger.

18 Why is my suffering unending, and my wound beyond healing, refusing to be cured? Will you truly be to me like a deceptive stream, like waters that dry up and fail?

19 Therefore, this is what the LORD says: If you turn back to me, I will restore you, and you will stand in my presence. If you separate what is precious from what is worthless, you will be like my own mouth. Let them come back to you, but you must not go back to them.

20 I will make you like a fortified bronze wall to this people. They will fight against you, but they will not overcome you, for I am with you to save you and to rescue you, declares the LORD.

21 I will rescue you from the grasp of the wicked, and I will redeem you from the power of the ruthless.
