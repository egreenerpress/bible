I Chronicles Chapter 7

1 The descendants of Issachar were Tola, Puah, Jashub, and Shimron—four in total.

2 The descendants of Tola were Uzzi, Rephaiah, Jeriel, Jahmai, Jibsam, and Shemuel. These were the leaders of their ancestral family, the house of Tola. They were mighty and courageous men in their generations. During the time of David, their recorded number was 22,600.

3 The descendants of Uzzi were led by Izrahiah. Izrahiah's sons were Michael, Obadiah, Joel, and Ishiah—five in total. All of them were prominent leaders.

4 And accompanying them, according to their family lines and ancestral households, were organized troops ready for battle, totaling thirty-six thousand men. This was because they had many wives and children.

5 And their relatives from all the families of Issachar were courageous and mighty warriors, recorded in their genealogies as eighty-seven thousand in total.

6 The descendants of Benjamin were Bela, Becher, and Jediael—three in total.

7 The sons of Bela were Ezbon, Uzzi, Uzziel, Jerimoth, and Iri—five in total. They were leaders of their ancestral families, courageous warriors. According to their genealogical records, their descendants numbered 22,034.

8 The sons of Becher were Zemira, Joash, Eliezer, Elioenai, Omri, Jerimoth, Abiah, Anathoth, and Alameth. All of these were the descendants of Becher.

9 The total number of them, according to their genealogical records by their generations, as leaders of their ancestral households and as mighty warriors, was twenty-two thousand.

10 The descendants of Jediael included Bilhan, and the sons of Bilhan were Jeush, Benjamin, Ehud, Chenaanah, Zethan, Tharshish, and Ahishahar.

11 All these were the descendants of Jediael, listed according to their family leaders, mighty warriors of great courage. They numbered seventeen thousand two hundred men, trained and ready for warfare and battle.

12 Shuppim and Huppim, the descendants of Ir, along with Hushim, who were the sons of Aher.

13 The descendants of Naphtali were Jahziel, Guni, Jezer, and Shallum, who were the sons of Bilhah.

14 The descendants of Manasseh included Ashriel, whom his wife bore. However, his concubine, who was an Aramean woman, bore Machir, the father of Gilead.

15 Machir married the sister of Huppim and Shuppim, whose name was Maachah. The name of his second son was Zelophehad, and Zelophehad had daughters.

16 Maachah, the wife of Machir, gave birth to a son, and she named him Peresh. His brother was named Sheresh, and his sons were Ulam and Rakem.

17 The descendants of Ulam included Bedan. These were the descendants of Gilead, who was the son of Machir, the son of Manasseh.

18 And his sister Hammoleketh gave birth to Ishod, Abiezer, and Mahalah.

19 The descendants of Shemidah were Ahian, Shechem, Likhi, and Aniam.

20 The descendants of Ephraim were Shuthelah, his son Bered, his son Tahath, his son Eladah, and his son Tahath.

21 Zabad, his son, and Shuthelah, his son, along with Ezer and Elead, were killed by the men of Gath who were native to that land. They died because they had gone down to seize the Gathites' livestock.

22 And Ephraim, their father, grieved for many days, and his relatives came to console him.

23 When he went in to his wife, she conceived and gave birth to a son. He named him Beriah, because misfortune had come upon his household.

24 His daughter was Sherah, who built both Lower Beth-horon and Upper Beth-horon, as well as Uzzen-sherah.

25 Rephah was his son, and Resheph was Rephah’s son. Telah was Resheph’s son, and Tahan was Telah’s son.

26 Laadan's son was Ammihud, and Ammihud's son was Elishama.

27 Non was his son, and Jehoshua was his son.

28 Their territories and settlements included Bethel and its surrounding villages, Naaran to the east, and Gezer to the west, along with its villages. They also held Shechem and its villages, extending as far as Gaza and its surrounding towns.

29 And along the territory of the descendants of Manasseh were Beth-shean with its surrounding villages, Taanach with its surrounding villages, Megiddo with its surrounding villages, and Dor with its surrounding villages. In these places lived the descendants of Joseph, the son of Israel.

30 The descendants of Asher were Imnah, Isuah, Ishuai, Beriah, and their sister Serah.

31 The sons of Beriah were Heber and Malchiel, who became the father of Birzavith.

32 And Heber became the father of Japhlet, Shomer, Hotham, and their sister Shua.

33 The descendants of Japhlet were Pasach, Bimhal, and Ashvath. These were the children of Japhlet.

34 The sons of Shamer were Ahi, Rohgah, Jehubbah, and Aram.

35 The sons of his brother Helem were Zophah, Imna, Shelesh, and Amal.

36 The descendants of Zophah were Suah, Harnepher, Shual, Beri, and Imrah.

37 Bezer, and Hod, and Shamma, and Shilshah, and Ithran, and Beera.

38 The descendants of Jether were Jephunneh, Pispah, and Ara.

39 The sons of Ulla were Arah, Haniel, and Rezia.

40 All these were the descendants of Asher, leaders of their ancestral families, distinguished and courageous warriors, chiefs among the leaders. The total number of those listed in their genealogy who were fit for military service and battle was twenty-six thousand men.
