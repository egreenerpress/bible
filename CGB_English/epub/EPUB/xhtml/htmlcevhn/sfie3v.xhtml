Matthew Chapter 7

1 "Do not pass judgment on others, so that you may not be judged yourselves."

2 For by the standard you use to judge others, you will be judged; and by the measure you apply to others, it will be measured back to you.

3 Why do you focus on the tiny speck in your brother’s eye, yet fail to notice the large log in your own eye?

4 How can you say to your brother, “Let me remove the speck from your eye,” when there is a large beam in your own eye?

5 You hypocrite, first remove the large plank from your own eye, and then you will see clearly to remove the small speck from your brother’s eye.

6 Do not give what is sacred to dogs, nor throw your pearls before pigs, for they may trample them underfoot and then turn and tear you apart.

7 Ask, and it will be given to you; seek, and you will find; knock, and the door will be opened to you.

8 For everyone who asks receives; and the one who seeks finds; and to the one who knocks, the door will be opened.

9 What father among you, if his son asks for bread, would give him a stone instead?

10 Or if he asks for a fish, would he give him a snake instead?

11 If you, who are flawed and sinful, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your heavenly Father give good things to those who ask Him?

12 Therefore, whatever you would want others to do for you, do the same for them, for this sums up the teachings of the Law and the Prophets.

13 Enter through the narrow gate, for the gate is wide and the road is broad that leads to destruction, and many are those who travel it.

14 For the gate is narrow and the way is difficult that leads to life, and only a few find it.

15 Be on guard against false prophets who approach you appearing harmless, like sheep, but on the inside, they are vicious, predatory wolves.

16 You will recognize them by what they produce. Do people pick grapes from thornbushes, or figs from thistles?

17 In the same way, every healthy tree produces good fruit, but a diseased tree yields bad fruit.

18 A healthy tree cannot produce bad fruit, nor can a diseased tree produce good fruit.

19 Every tree that does not produce good fruit is cut down and thrown into the fire.

20 Therefore, you will recognize them by what they produce.

21 Not everyone who says to me, "Lord, Lord," will enter the kingdom of heaven, but only the one who does the will of my Father who is in heaven.

22 Many will say to me on that day, "Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in your name? Did we not cast out demons in your name? And did we not perform many miracles in your name?"

23 At that time, I will declare to them, 'I never had a relationship with you; leave my presence, you who practice lawlessness.'

24 Therefore, whoever hears these teachings of mine and puts them into practice, I will compare to a wise person who built their house on solid rock.

25 The rain poured down, the floods rose, and the winds blew fiercely, striking against that house. Yet it did not collapse, because its foundation was firmly set on solid rock.

26 Everyone who hears these teachings of mine and does not put them into practice will be compared to a foolish man who built his house on sand.

27 The rain poured down, the floodwaters rose, and the winds howled, striking against that house. It collapsed, and its destruction was complete.

28 When Jesus finished speaking these words, the crowds were amazed at his teaching.

29 He taught them as one who possessed true authority, not like the teachers of the law.
