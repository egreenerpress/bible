Matthew Chapter 9

1 He got into a boat, crossed over the water, and arrived at his hometown.

2 Suddenly, they brought to Him a man who was paralyzed, lying on a mat. When Jesus saw their faith, He said to the paralyzed man, “Take heart, my son; your sins are forgiven.”

3 And then, some of the scribes thought to themselves, "This man is speaking blasphemy."

4 And Jesus, aware of their thoughts, said, "Why are you harboring evil intentions in your hearts?"

5 Which is easier: to say, 'Your sins are forgiven,' or to say, 'Get up and walk'?

6 But so that you may understand that the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins—(then he said to the paralyzed man)—Get up, pick up your mat, and go home.

7 He got up and went back to his home.

8 When the crowds saw this, they were amazed and praised God, who had granted such authority to human beings.

9 As Jesus was leaving that place, he saw a man named Matthew sitting at the tax collector’s booth. He said to him, “Follow me.” Matthew got up and followed him.

10 As Jesus was dining in the house, it happened that many tax collectors and sinners came and joined him and his disciples at the table.

11 When the Pharisees saw this, they said to His disciples, "Why does your Teacher eat with tax collectors and sinners?"

12 When Jesus heard this, he responded, "Those who are healthy do not need a doctor, but those who are sick do."

13 Go and understand what this means: 'I desire compassion, not sacrifice.' For I have not come to call those who consider themselves righteous, but sinners to turn back to God.

14 Then the disciples of John came to Him and asked, “Why is it that we and the Pharisees often fast, but Your disciples do not fast?”

15 Jesus said to them, "Can the wedding guests mourn while the bridegroom is still with them? But the time will come when the bridegroom is taken away from them, and then they will fast."

16 No one sews a patch of new, unshrunk cloth onto an old garment, because the new patch will shrink and pull away from the old fabric, making the tear even worse.

17 People do not pour new wine into old wineskins; otherwise, the skins will burst, the wine will spill out, and the skins will be ruined. Instead, they pour new wine into fresh wineskins, and both are preserved.

18 As Jesus was speaking these things to them, suddenly a prominent leader approached, bowed before him, and said, "My daughter has just died. But if you come and place your hand on her, she will live."

19 Jesus got up and went with him, and his disciples followed as well.

20 Suddenly, a woman who had been suffering from a bleeding condition for twelve years approached from behind and touched the edge of his robe.

21 For she thought to herself, "If I can just touch his clothing, I will be healed."

22 But Jesus turned around, and when He saw her, He said, "Daughter, take heart; your faith has healed you." And from that moment, the woman was completely healed.

23 When Jesus entered the ruler’s house and saw the musicians and the crowd in an uproar,

24 He said to them, "Step aside, for the girl is not dead, but asleep." But they ridiculed him, mocking his words.

25 When the crowd had been sent out, he entered, took her by the hand, and the young girl rose up.

26 The news of this spread throughout the entire region.

27 As Jesus was leaving that place, two blind men followed him, calling out and saying, “You, Son of David, have mercy on us!”

28 When Jesus entered the house, the blind men approached him. Jesus asked them, "Do you believe that I have the power to do this?" They replied, "Yes, Lord."

29 Then He touched their eyes and said, "Let it be done for you according to your faith."

30 Then their eyes were opened, and Jesus strictly instructed them, saying, "Make sure no one finds out about this."

31 But after they left, they spread the news about him throughout the entire region.

32 As they were leaving, suddenly they brought to Him a man who was unable to speak, under the control of an evil spirit.

33 When the demon was driven out, the man who had been unable to speak began to talk. The crowds were astonished and said, "Nothing like this has ever been seen in Israel before!"

34 But the Pharisees said, "He drives out demons by the power of the ruler of the demons."

35 Jesus traveled through all the towns and villages, teaching in their synagogues, proclaiming the good news of the kingdom, and healing every kind of illness and disease among the people.

36 When he saw the crowds, he was deeply moved with compassion for them, because they were weary and disoriented, like sheep without a shepherd.

37 Then he said to his disciples, "The harvest is indeed abundant, but the workers are few."

38 Therefore, pray to the Lord of the harvest, asking Him to send out workers into His harvest field.
