III John Chapter 1

1 From the elder, to my dear friend Gaius, whom I truly love.

2 Dear friend, my greatest desire is that you may thrive and enjoy good health, just as your soul is flourishing.

3 I was filled with great joy when the brothers and sisters came and spoke of your faithfulness to the truth, confirming that you continue to live in accordance with it.

4 There is no greater joy for me than to hear that my children are living in accordance with the truth.

5 Dear friend, you are acting faithfully in everything you do for the fellow believers and for strangers.

6 These individuals have testified to your love and kindness before the church. If you assist them in their journey in a manner worthy of God, you will be doing what is right.

7 Because they went out for the sake of His name, accepting nothing from the Gentiles.

8 We should therefore welcome and support such individuals, so that we may work together in advancing the truth.

9 I wrote to the church, but Diotrephes, who desires to be first among them, does not welcome us.

10 Therefore, if I come, I will bring to mind the actions he is committingâ€”spreading harmful and slanderous words against us. Not only is he not satisfied with this, but he also refuses to welcome the fellow believers himself and even prevents those who wish to do so, expelling them from the church.

11 Dear friends, do not pursue what is evil, but instead pursue what is good. Whoever does what is good comes from God; but whoever does what is evil has not truly known God.

12 Demetrius is well spoken of by everyone, and by the truth itself. Indeed, we also affirm this, and you know that our testimony is true.

13 I have many things to write to you, but I do not wish to write them with ink and pen.

14 I am confident that I will see you soon, and we will speak directly to one another. May peace be with you. Our friends send their greetings to you. Please greet each of our friends by name.
