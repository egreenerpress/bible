John Chapter 2

1 On the third day, there was a wedding in Cana, a town in Galilee, and the mother of Jesus was present there.

2 Both Jesus and his disciples were invited to the wedding celebration.

3 When the wine ran out, Jesus’ mother said to him, “They have no more wine.”

4 Jesus said to her, "Dear woman, why do you involve me in this? My appointed time has not yet arrived."

5 His mother said to the servants, "Whatever he tells you to do, do it."

6 There were six stone water jars placed there, used by the Jews for ceremonial purification, each holding between twenty and thirty gallons.

7 Jesus said to them, "Fill the jars with water." So they filled them to the very top.

8 And he said to them, "Take some now and bring it to the master of the banquet." So they brought it.

9 When the master of the banquet tasted the water that had been turned into wine, he did not know where it had come from (though the servants who had drawn the water knew). So the master of the banquet called the bridegroom aside.

10 He said to him, "Everyone serves the best wine first, and after people have had plenty to drink, they bring out the cheaper wine. But you have saved the best until now."

11 This was the first of the miraculous signs that Jesus performed in Cana of Galilee. Through it, he revealed his divine glory, and his disciples put their faith in him.

12 After this, he went down to Capernaum—he, along with his mother, his brothers, and his disciples. They stayed there for only a few days.

13 The Jewish Passover was approaching, and Jesus traveled to Jerusalem.

14 In the temple, he found people selling cattle, sheep, and doves, as well as money changers seated at their tables.

15 Then, after making a whip from cords, he drove everyone out of the temple, along with the sheep and oxen. He scattered the money of the money changers and overturned their tables.

16 He said to those who were selling doves, "Take these things away from here. Do not turn my Father's house into a marketplace."

17 And his disciples recalled what was written: "Passion for your house will consume me."

18 Then the Jewish leaders responded and said to him, "What sign can you show us to prove your authority, since you are doing these things?"

19 Jesus responded to them, saying, "Tear down this temple, and in three days I will rebuild it."

20 Then the Jewish leaders said, "This temple took forty-six years to build, and you claim you can raise it up in three days?"

21 However, he was referring to the temple of his own body.

22 After Jesus rose from the dead, his disciples recalled that he had spoken these words to them. They then believed both the Scripture and the message Jesus had delivered.

23 While Jesus was in Jerusalem during the Passover festival, many people came to believe in him because they witnessed the miraculous signs he performed. Their faith was inspired by what they saw him do.

24 But Jesus did not entrust himself to them, because he knew all people intimately.

25 He did not need anyone to testify about humanity, for He understood what was within a person.
