Matthew Chapter 3

1 In those days, John the Baptist appeared, proclaiming his message in the wilderness of Judea.

2 And he proclaimed, "Turn away from your sins and change your ways, for the kingdom of heaven is near."

3 This is the one spoken of by the prophet Isaiah, who said: "The voice of one calling out in the wilderness, 'Prepare the way for the Lord; make His paths straight.'"

4 John wore clothing made of camel’s hair and had a leather belt around his waist. His food consisted of locusts and wild honey.

5 Then people from Jerusalem, all of Judea, and the entire region around the Jordan went out to him.

6 And they were baptized by him in the Jordan River, openly acknowledging their sins.

7 When John saw that many of the Pharisees and Sadducees were coming to be baptized by him, he said to them, "You brood of vipers! Who warned you to escape from the coming wrath?"

8 Produce, then, the kind of fruit that demonstrates your genuine repentance.

9 Do not presume to say to yourselves, ‘We have Abraham as our father,’ for I tell you that God can raise up children for Abraham from these stones.

10 And now the axe is already positioned at the root of the trees. Therefore, every tree that does not produce good fruit will be cut down and thrown into the fire.

11 I baptize you with water as a sign of repentance, but the one who comes after me is far greater than I—I am not even worthy to carry his sandals. He will baptize you with the Holy Spirit and with fire.

12 The one who holds the winnowing fork is ready to thoroughly clean his threshing floor. He will gather the wheat into his storehouse, but the chaff he will burn with a fire that cannot be extinguished.

13 Then Jesus came from Galilee to the Jordan River, to John, in order to be baptized by him.

14 But John tried to stop Him, saying, "I am the one who needs to be baptized by You, and yet You come to me?"

15 Jesus responded to him, saying, "Allow this to happen for now, because it is proper for us to fulfill all that righteousness requires." Then he permitted it.

16 As soon as Jesus was baptized, he came up out of the water. At that moment, the heavens opened before him, and he saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove and resting upon him.

17 And behold, a voice came from heaven, saying, "This is my beloved Son, in whom I take great delight."
