Isaiah Chapter 62

1 For the sake of Zion, I will not remain silent, and for the sake of Jerusalem, I will not rest, until its righteousness shines forth like the dawn, and its salvation blazes like a burning torch.

2 The nations will witness your righteousness, and all kings will behold your glory. You will be given a new name, one that the LORD Himself will proclaim.

3 You will also be a glorious crown in the hand of the LORD, and a royal crown in the hand of your God.

4 You will no longer be called "Forsaken," nor will your land be called "Desolate." Instead, you will be called "My Delight Is in Her," and your land will be named "Married." For the LORD takes delight in you, and your land will be united in a sacred bond.

5 Just as a young man marries a virgin, so your descendants will marry you; and as a bridegroom rejoices over his bride, so your God will rejoice over you.

6 I have appointed watchmen on your walls, O Jerusalem, who will never be silent, day or night. You who call upon the LORD, do not remain quiet.

7 Do not allow him any rest until he establishes and makes Jerusalem a source of praise and honor throughout the earth.

8 The LORD has made a solemn promise, swearing by His mighty power and strength: "I will never again allow your grain to be taken as food by your enemies, nor will foreigners drink the wine for which you have worked so hard."

9 However, those who have gathered it will eat it and give praise to the LORD, and those who have collected it will drink it within the sacred courts of my sanctuary.

10 Pass through, pass through the gates; prepare the way for the people. Build up, build up the roadway; clear away the stones; raise a banner to guide the people.

11 Listen, the LORD has declared to the farthest reaches of the earth: Proclaim to the people of Zion, "See, your deliverance is coming! Look, his reward is with him, and his recompense accompanies him."

12 And they will be called "The Holy People," "Those Redeemed by the LORD." And you will be called "Sought After," "A City Not Abandoned."
