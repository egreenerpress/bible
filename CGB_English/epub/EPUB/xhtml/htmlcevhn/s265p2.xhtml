Jonah Chapter 4

1 But Jonah was greatly displeased and became very angry.

2 He prayed to the LORD and said, "Please, LORD, wasn’t this what I said when I was still in my own country? That is why I fled to Tarshish in the first place, because I knew that you are a gracious and compassionate God, slow to anger, abounding in steadfast love, and one who relents from sending disaster."

3 Therefore, Lord, I beg you, take my life from me now, for it is better for me to die than to live.

4 Then the LORD said, "Is it right for you to be angry?"

5 So Jonah left the city and went to the east side of it. There he built a shelter for himself and sat beneath it in the shade, waiting to see what would happen to the city.

6 Then the LORD God caused a plant to grow, and it sprang up over Jonah to provide shade for his head, to relieve him from his distress. Jonah was overjoyed because of the plant.

7 But God appointed a worm at the dawn of the next day, and it attacked the plant so that it withered.

8 When the sun rose, God sent a scorching east wind, and the sun beat down on Jonah’s head so intensely that he grew faint. Overwhelmed, he wished for death and said, “It would be better for me to die than to live.”

9 And God said to Jonah, "Is it right for you to be so angry about the plant?" Jonah replied, "It is right for me to be angry—angry enough to die."

10 Then the LORD said, "You felt compassion for the plant, even though you did not work for it or make it grow. It sprouted in a single night and withered in a single night.

11 Should I not have compassion on Nineveh, that great city, where there are more than a hundred and twenty thousand people who cannot tell their right hand from their left—and also many animals?
