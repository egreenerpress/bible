I Corinthians Chapter 10

1 Furthermore, brothers and sisters, I want you to understand that all our ancestors were under the guidance of the cloud, and all of them passed through the sea.

2 All of them were united with Moses through baptism, symbolized by the cloud and the sea.

3 And all of them ate the same spiritual food;

4 And all of them drank the same spiritual drink, for they drank from the spiritual rock that accompanied them—and that rock was Christ.

5 However, God was not pleased with many of them, and as a result, they perished in the wilderness.

6 These events serve as examples for us, so that we might not desire evil things as they did.

7 Do not become idolaters, as some of them were; for it is written, "The people sat down to eat and drink, and then got up to indulge in revelry."

8 Do not engage in sexual immorality, as some of them did, and in a single day twenty-three thousand fell as a result.

9 Do not test Christ, as some of them did, and were destroyed by serpents.

10 Do not complain or grumble, as some of them did, and as a result, they were destroyed by the destroyer.

11 All these events happened to them as examples, and they were recorded as a warning for us, upon whom the culmination of the ages has come.

12 Therefore, let anyone who believes they are standing firm be careful not to fall.

13 No trial or temptation has overtaken you except what is common to humanity. But God is faithful, and he will not allow you to be tested beyond your ability to endure. With the trial, he will also provide a way of escape, so that you can bear it.

14 Therefore, my dearly beloved, run away from the worship of idols.

15 I am speaking to you as people of understanding; carefully consider what I am saying.

16 The cup of blessing over which we give thanks—is it not a sharing in the blood of Christ? The bread that we break—is it not a sharing in the body of Christ?

17 For though we are many, we are one loaf and one body, because we all share in the same bread.

18 Consider the people of Israel in their physical practices: do not those who eat from the sacrifices share in the altar's significance?

19 What, then, am I saying? Is an idol truly anything significant, or is food sacrificed to idols of any real importance?

20 However, I affirm that when the Gentiles offer sacrifices, they are sacrificing to demons and not to God. I do not want you to participate in fellowship with demons.

21 You cannot drink from the cup of the Lord and also from the cup of demons. You cannot share in the Lord’s table and at the same time partake of the table of demons.

22 Are we provoking the Lord to jealousy? Are we more powerful than He?

23 Everything is permissible for me, but not everything is beneficial. Everything is permissible for me, but not everything builds up.

24 Let no one pursue their own interests alone, but let each person also seek the well-being of others.

25 Eat whatever is sold in the marketplace without raising questions for the sake of your conscience.

26 The earth belongs to the Lord, and everything in it.

27 If any unbelievers invite you to a meal and you choose to attend, eat whatever is served without raising questions for the sake of conscience.

28 However, if someone tells you, "This food was offered as a sacrifice to idols," do not eat it, both for the sake of the one who informed you and for the sake of conscience—because the earth and everything in it belong to the Lord.

29 I am referring to conscience—not your own, but that of the other person. For why should my freedom be judged by someone else’s conscience?

30 If I partake of something by God's grace, why should I be criticized for that which I give thanks?

31 So then, whether you eat or drink, or whatever you do, do everything to honor and glorify God.

32 Do not cause anyone to stumble, whether Jews, Gentiles, or the community of God.

33 Just as I strive to accommodate everyone in every way, not pursuing my own advantage but the benefit of many, so that they may be saved.
