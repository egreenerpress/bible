Leviticus Chapter 15

1 The LORD spoke to Moses and Aaron, saying,

2 Speak to the Israelites and tell them: If any man has a discharge from his body, he is unclean because of this discharge.

3 This will be the condition of his impurity due to his discharge: whether his body continues to flow with the discharge or his body stops the discharge, it remains his impurity.

4 Every bed on which a person with a discharge lies will be unclean, and anything on which they sit will also be unclean.

5 Anyone who touches his bed must wash their clothes, bathe in water, and will remain unclean until evening.

6 Anyone who sits on an object that was used by a person with a bodily discharge must wash their clothes and bathe in water. They will remain unclean until evening.

7 Anyone who touches the body of a person with a discharge must wash their clothes, bathe in water, and will remain unclean until evening.

8 If someone who has a bodily discharge spits on a person who is clean, then the clean person must wash their clothes, bathe in water, and remain ceremonially unclean until evening.

9 Any saddle that a person with a discharge rides on will become unclean.

10 Anyone who touches anything that was under the person who had the discharge will be unclean until evening. Whoever carries any of those items must wash their clothes, bathe in water, and remain unclean until evening.

11 If anyone who has a bodily discharge touches another person, and that person has not washed their hands with water, they must wash their clothes, bathe in water, and will remain unclean until evening.

12 Any clay vessel that someone with a discharge touches must be broken, and every wooden vessel must be thoroughly rinsed with water.

13 When someone who has had a bodily discharge is healed from it, they must count seven days for their cleansing. During this time, they must wash their clothes and bathe their body in fresh, flowing water. After doing this, they will be considered clean.

14 On the eighth day, he shall bring two turtledoves or two young pigeons and present himself before the LORD at the entrance to the tent of meeting. He shall then give them to the priest.

15 The priest shall present them, one as a sin offering and the other as a burnt offering. The priest shall make atonement for him before the LORD to cleanse him from his discharge.

16 If a man has an emission of semen, he must wash his entire body with water and will remain ritually unclean until evening.

17 Any clothing or leather item that has been exposed to semen must be washed with water and will remain ritually unclean until evening.

18 If a man has sexual relations with a woman and there is an emission of semen, both of them must wash themselves with water and will remain ritually unclean until evening.

19 If a woman has a discharge, and her discharge is blood from her body, she shall be set apart for seven days. Anyone who touches her will be unclean until evening.

20 During her time of menstrual impurity, anything she lies on will become unclean, and anything she sits on will also be unclean.

21 Anyone who touches her bed must wash their clothes and bathe in water, and they will remain unclean until evening.

22 Anyone who touches anything she has sat on must wash their clothes and bathe in water, and they will remain unclean until evening.

23 If the discharge is on her bed or on anything she sits on, and someone touches it, they will be unclean until evening.

24 If a man has sexual relations with her during her menstrual period, he will be unclean for seven days, and any bed he lies on will also be unclean.

25 If a woman has a discharge of blood for many days outside the time of her menstrual period, or if her discharge continues beyond the time of her period, all the days of her unclean discharge will be like the days of her menstrual impurity. She will be considered unclean.

26 Every bed on which she lies during the days of her discharge will be like the bed of her menstrual impurity, and anything she sits on will be unclean, just as it is during her time of separation due to uncleanness.

27 Anyone who touches these things will become unclean. They must wash their clothes, bathe in water, and remain unclean until evening.

28 But if she is freed from her discharge, she must count seven days for herself, and after that she will be ritually clean.

29 On the eighth day, she is to take two turtledoves or two young pigeons and bring them to the priest at the entrance of the tent of meeting.

30 The priest shall present one of the birds as a sin offering and the other as a burnt offering. In this way, the priest will make atonement for her before the LORD, cleansing her from the impurity caused by her discharge.

31 In this way, you must set apart the people of Israel from their impurity, so that they do not die because of their uncleanness when they defile my dwelling place, which is among them.

32 This is the regulation concerning anyone who has a bodily discharge, or whose semen flows from him, making him unclean because of it.

33 Concerning a woman who is experiencing her menstrual period, and concerning anyone who has a bodily discharge, whether a man or a woman, and concerning a man who lies with a woman who is ritually unclean.
