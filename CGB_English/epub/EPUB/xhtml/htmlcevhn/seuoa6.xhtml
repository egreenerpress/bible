Amos Chapter 5

1 Listen to this message I bring against you, a solemn lament, O house of Israel.

2 The young woman of Israel has fallen; she will not rise again. She lies abandoned on her land, with no one to lift her up.

3 For this is what the Lord GOD says: The city that sends out a thousand will have only a hundred left, and the town that sends out a hundred will have only ten remaining, among the house of Israel.

4 For this is what the LORD says to the house of Israel: "Seek me, and you will live."

5 Do not go to Bethel for help, nor enter Gilgal, and do not journey to Beersheba, for Gilgal will certainly be taken into exile, and Bethel will come to nothing.

6 Seek the LORD, and you will live; otherwise, He will burst forth like a consuming fire against the house of Joseph, devouring it completely, with no one in Bethel able to extinguish it.

7 You who distort justice into bitterness and cast aside righteousness in the land,

8 Seek the One who created the Pleiades and Orion, who transforms the deepest darkness into dawn and turns day into night; who summons the waters of the sea and pours them out over the face of the earth. The LORD is His name.

9 He empowers the oppressed to stand against the mighty, so that the downtrodden rise up against the stronghold.

10 They despise the one who confronts wrongdoing in public, and they detest the one who speaks with integrity and honesty.

11 Therefore, because you trample on the poor and take from them heavy burdens of grain, you have built houses of finely cut stone, but you will not live in them; you have planted lush vineyards, but you will not drink their wine.

12 I am fully aware of your numerous offenses and your grievous sins. You oppress the righteous, accept bribes, and deny justice to the poor at the city gate, depriving them of their rightful due.

13 Therefore, those who are wise will remain silent in such a time, for it is a time of great trouble and wickedness.

14 Pursue what is good and reject what is evil, so that you may truly live. Then the LORD, the God of heavenly armies, will be with you, just as you have declared.

15 Reject what is evil and embrace what is good, and uphold justice in the public square. Perhaps the LORD, the God of armies, will show mercy to the surviving remnant of Joseph.

16 Therefore, the LORD, the God of all armies, declares this: There will be mourning in every street, and in all the public squares, people will cry out, "Alas! Alas!" They will summon the farmers to grieve and those skilled in lamentation to lead the wailing.

17 And there will be lamentation in every vineyard, for I will pass through your midst, declares the LORD.

18 How terrible it will be for you who long for the day of the LORD! What purpose does it serve for you? The day of the LORD will be a time of darkness, not light.

19 It is as if someone were fleeing from a lion, only to encounter a bear; or if they went into their house, rested their hand against the wall, and were bitten by a snake.

20 Will not the day of the LORD be a time of darkness rather than light? A day of deep gloom, with no brightness at all?

21 I reject and detest your religious festivals; I find no pleasure in your sacred gatherings.

22 Even if you present burnt offerings and grain offerings to me, I will not accept them. Nor will I look with favor on the peace offerings from your well-fed animals.

23 Remove from me the clamor of your songs; I will not listen to the music of your harps.

24 Let justice flow like a river, and righteousness like an ever-flowing stream.

25 Have you presented sacrifices and offerings to me during the forty years in the wilderness, O house of Israel?

26 However, you have carried the shrine of your god Moloch and the pedestals of your idols, the star of your deity, which you crafted for yourselves.

27 Therefore, I will send you into exile beyond Damascus, declares the LORD, whose name is the God of Armies.
