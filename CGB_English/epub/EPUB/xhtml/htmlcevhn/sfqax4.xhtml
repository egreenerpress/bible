Genesis Chapter 30

1 When Rachel realized that she was unable to bear children for Jacob, she became deeply envious of her sister. In her distress, she said to Jacob, "Give me children, or I will die!"

2 Jacob became very angry with Rachel and said, "Am I in the place of God, who has prevented you from bearing children?"

3 And she said, "Here is my servant Bilhah; go to her, and she will bear children on my behalf, so that through her I too may build a family."

4 So Rachel gave her servant Bilhah to Jacob as a wife, and Jacob had relations with her.

5 Bilhah became pregnant and gave birth to a son for Jacob.

6 Rachel said, "God has vindicated me and has listened to my plea, giving me a son." So she named him Dan.

7 And Bilhah, Rachel’s servant, became pregnant again and gave birth to a second son for Jacob.

8 Rachel said, "I have struggled intensely with my sister, and I have overcome." So she named him Naphtali.

9 When Leah realized that she had stopped bearing children, she took her servant Zilpah and gave her to Jacob as a wife.

10 And Zilpah, Leah's servant, gave birth to a son for Jacob.

11 And Leah declared, "Good fortune has come!" So she named him Gad.

12 And Zilpah, Leah’s servant, gave birth to a second son for Jacob.

13 Leah said, "How happy I am! The women will call me blessed." So she named him Asher.

14 During the time of the wheat harvest, Reuben went out into the field and found some mandrakes. He brought them to his mother, Leah. Then Rachel said to Leah, "Please give me some of your son’s mandrakes."

15 Rachel replied, "Is it not enough that you have taken my husband? Now you want to take my son's mandrakes as well?" Leah responded, "Very well, he can sleep with you tonight in exchange for your son's mandrakes."

16 Jacob returned from the field in the evening, and Leah went out to meet him. She said, "You must come to me tonight, for I have secured your company by giving you my son’s mandrakes." So he spent the night with her.

17 God listened to Leah, and she became pregnant and gave birth to Jacob’s fifth son.

18 Leah said, "God has granted me my reward because I gave my servant to my husband." So she named him Issachar.

19 Leah became pregnant once more and gave birth to Jacob's sixth son.

20 Leah said, "God has granted me a precious gift; now my husband will live with me, because I have borne him six sons." So she named him Zebulun.

21 Afterward, she gave birth to a daughter and named her Dinah.

22 Then God took notice of Rachel, listened to her plea, and enabled her to conceive.

23 She became pregnant and gave birth to a son, and she declared, "God has removed my disgrace."

24 She named him Joseph, saying, "May the LORD grant me yet another son."

25 After Rachel gave birth to Joseph, Jacob said to Laban, "Let me go, so I may return to my own home and to my country."

26 "Give me my wives and my children, for whom I have worked for you, and let me leave. You know very well the work I have done for you."

27 Laban said to him, "Please, if I have found favor in your eyes, stay; for I have come to understand through experience that the LORD has blessed me because of you."

28 And he said, "Set the terms of my payment, and I will give it to you."

29 And he said to him, "You know how I have served you and how your livestock has fared under my care."

30 Before I arrived, you had very little, but now your possessions have grown into a great abundance. The LORD has blessed you greatly since I came here. Now, when will I also be able to provide for my own household?

31 And he said, "What shall I give you?" Jacob replied, "You do not need to give me anything. But if you will do this one thing for me, I will continue to tend and watch over your flock."

32 Today, I will go through all your flocks and separate from them every speckled and spotted sheep, every dark-colored lamb, and every speckled and spotted goat. These will be my wages.

33 In the future, my integrity will speak for me when the matter of my wages comes before you. Any goat that is not speckled or spotted, or any sheep that is not brown, if found with me, will be considered stolen.

34 And Laban replied, "Very well, let it be as you have said."

35 On that day, he set apart the male goats that were striped and spotted, as well as all the female goats that were speckled and spotted, and every goat that had any white on it, along with all the dark-colored sheep. He then entrusted them to the care of his sons.

36 He put a distance of three days' travel between himself and Jacob, while Jacob continued to tend the rest of Laban's flocks.

37 Jacob took fresh branches from poplar, hazel, and chestnut trees. He peeled strips of bark from them, exposing the white inner wood of the branches.

38 Jacob placed the peeled branches he had prepared in the troughs where the flocks came to drink, so that when the animals came to drink, they would mate in the presence of the branches.

39 The flocks mated in front of the rods and gave birth to young that were streaked, speckled, and spotted.

40 Jacob separated the lambs and directed the flocks to face the streaked and spotted sheep, as well as all the dark-colored ones in Laban’s flock. He kept his own flocks separate and did not mix them with Laban’s livestock.

41 Whenever the stronger animals were in heat, Jacob would place the striped branches in the troughs in front of them, so that they would mate near the branches.

42 However, when the livestock were weak, he did not place them with the others. As a result, the weaker animals became Laban's, while the stronger ones became Jacob's.

43 The man prospered greatly and acquired an abundance of livestock, along with female and male servants, camels, and donkeys.
