Nehemiah Chapter 1

1 The account of Nehemiah, the son of Hachaliah. It happened in the month of Chislev, in the twentieth year, while I was in the citadel of Susa,

2 Hanani, one of my brothers, arrived with some men from Judah. I questioned them about the Jewish survivors who had escaped the captivity and about the condition of Jerusalem.

3 They told me, "The few who remain from the captivity in the province are enduring great hardship and disgrace. The wall of Jerusalem lies in ruins, and its gates have been destroyed by fire."

4 When I heard these words, I sat down, wept, and mourned for several days. I fasted and prayed before the God of heaven.

5 And he said, "I humbly ask you, O LORD, God of heaven, the great and awe-inspiring God, who faithfully keeps His covenant and shows steadfast love to those who love Him and obey His commandments."

6 Please listen attentively and keep your eyes open, so that you may hear the prayer of your servant, which I now offer before you day and night on behalf of the people of Israel, your servants. I confess the sins of the Israelites, which we have committed against you. Both I and my family have sinned.

7 We have acted very wickedly toward you and have failed to keep the commandments, the statutes, and the ordinances that you gave to your servant Moses.

8 Please remember the words you commanded your servant Moses, saying, "If you act unfaithfully, I will scatter you among the nations."

9 However, if you return to me, obey my commandments, and faithfully carry them out, even if some of you have been scattered to the farthest reaches of the heavens, I will gather you from there and bring you back to the place I have chosen as the dwelling for my name.

10 Here are your servants and your people, whom you have delivered by your mighty power and your strong hand.
