Hebrews Chapter 10

1 The law serves as a shadow of the good things to come, not the actual reality of those things. Therefore, it can never, through the same sacrifices offered repeatedly year after year, make perfect those who come to worship.

2 If those sacrifices had been truly effective, would they not have stopped being offered? For the worshipers, having been cleansed once, would no longer have any consciousness of sin.

3 However, in those sacrifices, there is a repeated reminder of sins year after year.

4 For it is impossible for the blood of bulls and goats to remove sins.

5 Therefore, when he comes into the world, he says, "You did not desire sacrifices and offerings, but you have prepared a body for me."

6 In burnt offerings and sacrifices for sin, you have taken no delight.

7 Then I said, “Behold, I have come—as it is written about me in the scroll of the book—to do your will, O God.”

8 When God previously said, "You did not desire sacrifices, offerings, burnt offerings, or sin offerings, nor did you take pleasure in them"—though these are offered according to the law—

9 Then he said, "Behold, I have come to do your will, O God." He removes the first in order to establish the second.

10 By this will, we have been made holy through the one-time sacrifice of the body of Jesus Christ.

11 Every priest stands day after day performing his duties, repeatedly offering the same sacrifices, which can never remove sins.

12 But this man, after offering a single sacrifice for sins once and for all, took His seat at the right hand of God.

13 From now on, he awaits the time when his enemies will be placed under his feet as a footstool.

14 For through a single sacrifice, he has made perfect forever those who are being made holy.

15 The Holy Spirit also testifies to us about this matter. For after He had previously declared,

16 This is the covenant I will establish with them after those days, declares the Lord: I will place my laws within their hearts, and I will inscribe them upon their minds.

17 And I will no longer recall their sins and wrongdoings.

18 Now, where there is forgiveness for these sins, there is no longer any need for an offering to atone for sin.

19 Therefore, brothers and sisters, since we have confidence to enter the Most Holy Place through the blood of Jesus,

20 By a new and living path, which He has opened for us through the curtain—that is, through His own flesh.

21 And since we have a great high priest who presides over the house of God,

22 Let us approach with sincere hearts, fully confident in our faith, having our inner selves cleansed from a guilty conscience and our bodies purified with clean water.

23 Let us firmly hold on to the declaration of our faith without faltering, for the one who made the promise is trustworthy.

24 Let us thoughtfully consider how we can encourage one another to demonstrate love and to engage in good deeds.

25 Do not abandon gathering together, as some have made a habit of doing, but encourage one another—and all the more as you see the day drawing near.

26 For if we deliberately continue to sin after we have come to know the truth, there no longer remains a sacrifice to atone for sins.

27 But there is a dreadful expectation of judgment and a fierce fire of wrath that will consume those who oppose God.

28 Anyone who rejected the law of Moses was put to death without mercy on the testimony of two or three witnesses.

29 Consider how much more severe a punishment someone deserves who has trampled underfoot the Son of God, who has treated as unholy the blood of the covenant by which they were sanctified, and who has insulted the Spirit of grace.

30 For we know the One who has declared, "Vengeance is mine; I will repay," says the Lord. And again, "The Lord will judge His people."

31 It is a terrifying thing to come under the judgment of the living God.

32 But remember the earlier days, when, after you had received the light of truth, you endured a great struggle with suffering.

33 At times, you were publicly exposed to ridicule and suffering, enduring both insults and hardships. At other times, you stood in solidarity with those who were treated in this way.

34 For you showed compassion toward me in my imprisonment and accepted with joy the confiscation of your possessions, knowing that you yourselves have a better and lasting treasure in heaven.

35 Therefore, do not discard your boldness and assurance, for it carries with it a great reward.

36 You need endurance, so that after you have carried out God’s will, you may receive what He has promised.

37 In just a short time, the one who is to come will arrive and will not delay.

38 Now, the righteous will live by faith; but if anyone turns back, my soul will take no pleasure in them.

39 However, we are not among those who shrink back and are destroyed, but among those who have faith and are saved, preserving their souls.
