Philippians Chapter 2

1 If there is any encouragement in Christ, if there is any comfort found in love, if there is any shared experience of the Spirit, if there is any heartfelt compassion and mercy,

2 Make my joy complete by being united in thought and purpose, sharing the same love, and being in harmony with one another, focused on the same goal.

3 Do not act out of selfish ambition or conceit, but with humility regard others as more important than yourselves.

4 Do not focus solely on your own interests, but also consider the concerns and needs of others.

5 Adopt the same attitude and mindset that was in Christ Jesus.

6 He, who existed in the very nature of God, did not consider equality with God something to be grasped or exploited.

7 He willingly set aside His divine privileges and took on the humble position of a servant, being born in human form and appearing as an ordinary man.

8 And though he existed in the form of God, he took on the nature of a servant, appearing in human likeness. He humbled himself, submitting fully to God’s will, even to the point of death—a death by crucifixion.

9 Therefore, God has also greatly exalted him and bestowed upon him a name that surpasses every other name.

10 So that at the name of Jesus, every knee will bow—those in heaven, those on earth, and those under the earth—

11 And every tongue will openly declare that Jesus Christ is Lord, bringing honor and glory to God the Father.

12 Therefore, my dearly loved ones, just as you have always obeyed—not only when I was with you, but now even more in my absence—continue to pursue your salvation with deep reverence and humility.

13 For it is God who is at work within you, enabling you both to desire and to carry out what pleases Him.

14 Do everything without complaining or arguing.

15 So that you may live without fault, pure and innocent, as children of God, above reproach in the midst of a corrupt and twisted generation. Among them, you shine like bright lights in the world.

16 Proclaiming the message of eternal life, so that I may have cause for joy on the day of Christ, knowing that I have not run my race for nothing nor labored without purpose.

17 Indeed, even if I am poured out as an offering upon the sacrifice and service that arise from your faith, I am glad and rejoice with all of you.

18 For this same reason, you too should be glad and celebrate with me.

19 However, I am confident in the Lord Jesus that I will send Timothy to you soon, so that I too may be encouraged when I learn about your circumstances.

20 For I have no one else who shares my mindset, who will genuinely care about your well-being.

21 For everyone looks out for their own interests, rather than the concerns of Jesus Christ.

22 You are well aware of his proven character, for he has worked alongside me in spreading the gospel, serving faithfully like a son with his father.

23 Therefore, I hope to send him to you soon, as soon as I see how things turn out for me.

24 I am confident in the Lord that I myself will also come to you soon.

25 Yet I considered it essential to send Epaphroditus to you—my brother, co-worker, and fellow soldier, as well as your messenger and the one who attended to my needs.

26 For he deeply desired to see all of you and was greatly distressed because you had heard that he was ill.

27 Indeed, he was so ill that he nearly died. However, God showed him mercy—and not only him but also me, so that I would not be overwhelmed with grief upon grief.

28 Therefore, I am sending him with greater urgency, so that when you see him again, you may be filled with joy, and my own sorrow may be lessened.

29 Welcome him, then, in the Lord with complete joy, and hold people like him in high regard.

30 Because of his dedication to Christ's work, he came close to death, risking his own life to make up for the help you were unable to give me.
