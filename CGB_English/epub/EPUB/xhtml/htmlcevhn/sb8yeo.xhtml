Genesis Chapter 12

1 The LORD had said to Abram, "Leave your country, your relatives, and your father's household, and go to the land that I will show you."

2 I will make you into a great nation, and I will bless you. I will make your name renowned, and you will be a source of blessing to others.

3 I will bless those who bless you, and I will curse those who curse you. Through you, all the families of the earth will be blessed.

4 So Abram set out, just as the LORD had instructed him, and Lot went with him. Abram was seventy-five years old when he left Haran.

5 Abram took his wife Sarai, his nephew Lot, all the possessions they had acquired, and the people they had brought with them in Haran. They set out to journey to the land of Canaan, and they arrived in the land of Canaan.

6 Abram journeyed through the land as far as the place of Shechem, to the oak of Moreh. At that time, the Canaanites were living in the land.

7 The LORD appeared to Abram and said, "I will give this land to your descendants." So Abram built an altar there to the LORD, who had appeared to him.

8 He then moved from that place to a mountain east of Bethel and set up his tent, with Bethel to the west and Ai to the east. There he built an altar to the LORD and called on the name of the LORD.

9 Abram continued his journey, steadily making his way toward the southern region.

10 A severe famine struck the land, and Abram traveled to Egypt to stay there for a time, because the famine was intense and widespread.

11 As they approached Egypt, he said to his wife Sarai, "Listen, I know that you are a woman of great beauty.

12 Therefore, it will happen that when the Egyptians see you, they will say, ‘This is his wife,’ and they will kill me, but they will let you live.

13 Please say that you are my sister, so that things may go well for me because of you, and my life may be spared on your account.

14 When Abram arrived in Egypt, the Egyptians noticed that his wife was exceptionally beautiful.

15 The officials of Pharaoh saw her and praised her to Pharaoh, so the woman was brought into Pharaoh’s household.

16 And he treated Abram well because of her. Abram received sheep, cattle, male donkeys, male and female servants, female donkeys, and camels.

17 The LORD inflicted severe plagues upon Pharaoh and his household because of Sarai, Abram’s wife.

18 Pharaoh summoned Abram and said, "What have you done to me? Why didn’t you tell me that she was your wife?"

19 Why did you say, ‘She is my sister,’ so that I might have taken her as my wife? Now, here is your wife; take her and go on your way.

20 Pharaoh gave orders to his officials concerning him, and they sent him on his way, along with his wife and all that he possessed.
