Proverbs Chapter 10

1 The proverbs of Solomon. A wise son brings joy to his father, but a foolish son is a source of grief to his mother.

2 Wealth gained through wickedness brings no lasting benefit, but living a life of integrity and justice rescues from death.

3 The LORD will not allow the life of the righteous to go hungry, but He will thwart the desires of the wicked.

4 A person who works carelessly and lazily will end up in poverty, but the one who is diligent and hardworking will prosper and gain wealth.

5 A son who gathers during the summer acts wisely, but a son who sleeps during the harvest brings disgrace.

6 Blessings rest upon the upright, but violence overwhelms the speech of the wicked.

7 The memory of the righteous is a blessing, but the reputation of the wicked will decay and come to ruin.

8 A person with a wise heart will accept and follow instructions, but a foolish chatterer will come to ruin.

9 The one who lives with integrity walks securely, but whoever twists their ways will be exposed.

10 The one who schemes with deceitful glances brings about trouble, but the babbling fool will come to ruin.

11 The words of a righteous person are like a fountain of life, but violence overwhelms the speech of the wicked.

12 Hatred provokes conflicts, but love conceals all wrongdoings.

13 A person of understanding speaks with wisdom, but discipline is needed for those who lack understanding.

14 Wise people store up knowledge, but the speech of the foolish brings them close to ruin.

15 The wealth of the rich serves as their fortified stronghold, but the ruin of the poor lies in their destitution.

16 The efforts of the righteous lead to life, while the produce of the wicked results in sin.

17 The one who follows instruction walks the path of life, but whoever rejects correction goes astray.

18 The one who conceals hatred with deceitful words, and the one who spreads slander, is a fool.

19 When many words are spoken, sin is unavoidable; but the one who controls their speech shows wisdom.

20 The speech of the righteous is like refined silver, but the thoughts of the wicked are of little value.

21 The words of the upright provide nourishment and guidance to many, but those who lack understanding perish due to their foolishness.

22 The blessing of the LORD brings true wealth, and He adds no hardship or grief to it.

23 A fool finds amusement in doing wrong, but a person of understanding delights in wisdom.

24 The dread that the wicked fear will overtake them, but the heartfelt longing of the righteous will be fulfilled.

25 Just as a whirlwind sweeps by and is gone, so the wicked are swept away and no longer remain. But the righteous stand firm like an enduring foundation, unshaken and lasting forever.

26 Just as vinegar irritates the teeth and smoke stings the eyes, so is a lazy person to those who send him on a task.

27 Reverence for the LORD extends one's life, but the years of the wicked will be cut short.

28 The hope of the upright will lead to joy, but the expectations of the wicked will come to nothing.

29 The path of the LORD is a source of strength for those who live with integrity, but it brings ruin to those who practice wickedness.

30 The righteous will never be uprooted, but the wicked will not remain in the land.

31 The mouth of the righteous speaks wisdom, but the deceitful tongue will be silenced.

32 The words of the righteous reflect wisdom and discern what is fitting, but the speech of the wicked is filled with perversity and deceit.
