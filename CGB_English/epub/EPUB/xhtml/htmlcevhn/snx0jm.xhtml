John Chapter 10

1 Truly, truly, I tell you, whoever does not enter the sheepfold through the gate but climbs in by some other way is a thief and a robber.

2 But the one who enters through the door is the shepherd of the sheep.

3 The gatekeeper opens the gate for him, and the sheep listen to his voice. He calls his own sheep by name and leads them out.

4 When he leads out his own sheep, he goes ahead of them, and the sheep follow him because they recognize his voice.

5 They will not follow a stranger; instead, they will run away from him, because they do not recognize the voice of strangers.

6 Jesus told them this parable, but they did not understand what He was saying to them.

7 Then Jesus said to them again, "Truly, truly, I tell you, I am the gate for the sheep."

8 Everyone who came before me was a thief and a robber, but the sheep did not listen to them.

9 I am the gate; if anyone enters through me, they will be saved. They will come in and go out freely and find abundant provision.

10 The thief comes only to steal, kill, and destroy. But I have come so that they may have life, and have it in overflowing abundance.

11 I am the good shepherd; the good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep.

12 But the one who is a hired hand, and not the shepherd, who does not own the sheep, sees the wolf coming and abandons the sheep, fleeing for his own safety. Then the wolf attacks and scatters the sheep.

13 The hired worker runs away because he is merely a hired worker and has no genuine concern for the sheep.

14 I am the good shepherd; I know my sheep, and my sheep know me.

15 Just as the Father knows me, I also know the Father; and I give up my life for the sheep.

16 I have other sheep that do not belong to this flock. I must bring them as well, and they will listen to my voice. Then there will be one flock and one shepherd.

17 This is why my Father loves me: because I willingly lay down my life, so that I may take it up again.

18 No one takes it from me, but I willingly lay it down of my own accord. I have the authority to lay it down, and I have the authority to take it up again. This command I have received from my Father.

19 Once again, there was a division among the Jews because of these words.

20 And many of them said, "He is possessed by a demon and out of his mind. Why are you listening to him?"

21 Others remarked, "These are not the words of someone possessed by a demon. Can a demon give sight to the blind?"

22 At that time in Jerusalem, it was the Feast of Dedication, and the season was winter.

23 And Jesus was walking in the temple area, specifically in the section known as Solomon’s Colonnade.

24 Then the Jewish leaders gathered around Him and said, “How long will You keep us in suspense? If You are the Messiah, tell us plainly.”

25 Jesus responded to them, "I have already told you, but you did not believe. The works I do in my Father’s name testify about me."

26 You do not believe because you are not among my sheep, as I have already told you.

27 My sheep listen to my voice, and I know them intimately, and they follow me.

28 I grant them eternal life, and they will never be destroyed. No one will ever snatch them out of my hand.

29 "My Father, who has given them to me, is greater than all; and no one can snatch them out of my Father’s hand."

30 I and the Father are united as one.

31 Then the Jewish leaders picked up stones again, intending to throw them at him.

32 Jesus responded to them, "I have shown you many good works that come from my Father. For which of these works are you trying to stone me?"

33 The Jewish leaders responded to him, saying, "We are not stoning you for any good deed, but for blasphemy, because you, a mere man, are claiming to be God."

34 Jesus responded to them, "Doesn't your law say, 'I declared, "You are gods"'?"

35 If those to whom the word of God was given were called "gods"—and the Scripture cannot be set aside—

36 Do you accuse of blasphemy the one whom the Father has set apart and sent into the world, simply because I said, "I am the Son of God"?

37 If I do not perform the works of my Father, do not believe me.

38 But even if you do not believe me, believe the evidence of the works themselves, so that you may come to understand and believe that the Father is in me, and I am in the Father.

39 So they tried once more to seize him, but he slipped away from their grasp.

40 He then went back across the Jordan to the place where John had been baptizing earlier, and there he stayed.

41 Many people came to him and said, "John performed no miracles, but everything John said about this man was true."

42 And many people in that place put their faith in him.
