Luke Chapter 18

1 Jesus told them a parable to emphasize that people should always pray and not lose heart.

2 He said, "There was a judge in a certain city who neither feared God nor had any respect for people."

3 There was a widow in that city who came to him and pleaded, "Grant me justice against my adversary."

4 At first, he refused. But later, he thought to himself, "Even though I have no reverence for God and no respect for people..."

5 Because this widow keeps bothering me, I will grant her justice, so that she won’t wear me out with her persistent pleas.

6 The Lord said, "Listen to what the unjust judge says."

7 Will not God bring about justice for his chosen ones, who cry out to him day and night, even if he delays in responding to them?

8 I assure you that he will swiftly bring about justice for them. Yet, when the Son of Man returns, will he find faith remaining on the earth?

9 Jesus told this parable to some who were confident in their own righteousness and looked down on others:

10 Two men went up to the temple to pray; one was a Pharisee, and the other a tax collector.

11 The Pharisee stood by himself and prayed like this: "God, I thank you that I am not like other people—greedy, dishonest, immoral—or even like this tax collector."

12 I fast twice a week, and I give a tenth of everything I own.

13 The tax collector, standing at a distance, would not even lift his eyes toward heaven, but instead beat his chest, saying, “God, show mercy to me, a sinner.”

14 I tell you, this man returned to his home justified, unlike the other. For everyone who exalts themselves will be humbled, and whoever humbles themselves will be exalted.

15 They also brought infants to Him, asking that He would touch them. But when His disciples saw this, they scolded those who brought the children.

16 But Jesus called them to him and said, "Let the little children come to me, and do not hinder them, for the kingdom of God belongs to such as these."

17 Truly I tell you, whoever does not welcome the kingdom of God with the humility and trust of a young child will certainly not enter it.

18 A certain leader approached Him and asked, "Good Teacher, what must I do to inherit eternal life?"

19 And Jesus said to him, "Why do you call me good? No one is good except God alone."

20 You are aware of the commandments: Do not commit adultery, do not murder, do not steal, do not give false testimony, and honor your father and mother.

21 And he replied, "I have faithfully kept all these commandments since I was young."

22 When Jesus heard this, he said to him, "You still lack one thing: sell everything you own and give the proceeds to the poor, and you will have treasure in heaven. Then come and follow me."

23 When he heard this, he became deeply distressed, for he was extremely wealthy.

24 When Jesus saw that the man was deeply grieved, He said, "How difficult it is for those who possess wealth to enter the kingdom of God!"

25 It is easier for a camel to pass through the eye of a needle than for a wealthy person to enter the kingdom of God.

26 Those who heard this asked, "Who then can be saved?"

27 He said, "What is impossible for people is possible for God."

28 Then Peter said, "Look, we have left everything behind and have followed you."

29 And He said to them, "Truly, I tell you, there is no one who has left home, or parents, or brothers, or wife, or children, for the sake of the kingdom of God, who will not receive far more in this present time and in the age to come—eternal life."

30 Who will not receive many times more in this present age, and in the age to come, eternal life.

31 Then Jesus took the twelve disciples aside and said to them, "Listen, we are going up to Jerusalem, and everything that has been written by the prophets about the Son of Man will be fulfilled."

32 For he will be handed over to the Gentiles, and he will be mocked, treated with contempt, and spat upon.

33 They will whip him and kill him, but on the third day he will rise again.

34 They did not comprehend any of these things; the meaning of what was said was concealed from them, and they were unaware of the significance of the words that had been spoken.

35 As he approached Jericho, a blind man was sitting by the roadside, begging.

36 When he heard the crowd passing by, he inquired about what was happening.

37 They informed him that Jesus of Nazareth was passing by.

38 And he called out, saying, "Jesus, Son of David, have mercy on me!"

39 Those who were walking ahead scolded him, telling him to be quiet. But he shouted even louder, "Son of David, have mercy on me!"

40 Jesus stood and ordered the man to be brought to him. When the man came near, Jesus asked him,

41 He asked, "What do you want me to do for you?" The man replied, "Lord, I want to regain my sight."

42 And Jesus said to him, "Receive your sight; your faith has made you well."

43 At once, his sight was restored, and he began to follow Jesus, praising God. When all the people witnessed this, they too gave glory to God.
