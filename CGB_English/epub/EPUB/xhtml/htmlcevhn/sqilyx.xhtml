Psalms Chapter 27

1 (A Psalm of David.) The LORD is my light and my deliverance; whom should I fear? The LORD is the stronghold of my life; of whom should I be afraid?

2 When the wicked, my enemies and adversaries, came against me to destroy me, they stumbled and fell.

3 Even if an army surrounds me, my heart will not be afraid; even if war breaks out against me, I will remain steadfast and confident.

4 One thing I have asked of the LORD, and this is what I will seek: that I may live in the house of the LORD all the days of my life, to gaze upon the splendor of the LORD and to seek Him in His temple.

5 For in times of distress, he will conceal me in his shelter; in the hidden place of his dwelling, he will keep me safe; he will place me securely upon a rock.

6 Now my head will be raised high above all my enemies surrounding me. Because of this, I will bring joyful offerings to His dwelling place; I will sing, yes, I will sing praises to the LORD.

7 Listen, O LORD, when I call out to you; show me your mercy and respond to my plea.

8 When you said, "Seek my presence," my heart responded to you, "Your presence, LORD, I will seek."

9 Do not turn your face away from me; do not cast your servant aside in anger. You have been my helper; do not abandon me or forsake me, O God of my salvation.

10 Even if my father and mother abandon me, the LORD will receive me and care for me.

11 Guide me in your ways, O LORD, and direct me along a straight and clear path, for the sake of those who oppose me.

12 Do not hand me over to the desires of my enemies, for false witnesses have risen against me, and those who speak with malice and violence.

13 I would have lost all hope if I had not trusted that I would see the Lord's goodness in the land of the living.

14 Trust in the LORD: be strong and courageous, and he will fortify your heart. Yes, trust in the LORD.
