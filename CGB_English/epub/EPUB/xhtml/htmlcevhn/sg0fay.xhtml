Luke Chapter 21

1 He raised his eyes and observed the wealthy individuals placing their offerings into the temple treasury.

2 And he also noticed a certain poor widow putting in two small copper coins.

3 And He said, "Truly, I tell you, this poor widow has given more than all of them."

4 For all these individuals have contributed to Godâ€™s offerings out of their abundance, but she, out of her poverty, has given everything she had to live on.

5 And as some were speaking about the temple, remarking how it was adorned with beautiful stones and offerings, He said,

6 Concerning these things that you see, the time will come when not one stone will remain standing on another; every one of them will be torn down.

7 They asked Him, saying, "Teacher, when will these things happen? And what sign will there be to show when these events are about to take place?"

8 He said, "Be careful not to be deceived, for many will come claiming to represent me, saying, 'I am the Messiah,' and declaring that the time is near. Do not follow them."

9 When you hear about wars and uprisings, do not be alarmed, for these things must happen first, but the end will not come immediately.

10 Then he said to them, "Nation will rise up against nation, and kingdom against kingdom."

11 There will be massive earthquakes in various places, along with famines and deadly diseases. Terrifying events and extraordinary signs will also appear in the heavens.

12 Before all these things happen, they will seize you and persecute you, handing you over to the synagogues and throwing you into prisons. You will be brought before kings and rulers because of your allegiance to me.

13 And this will serve as evidence for you.

14 Decide firmly in your hearts not to plan ahead how you will respond.

15 For I will grant you the ability to speak with wisdom, which none of your opponents will be able to contradict or oppose.

16 You will be betrayed by your parents, brothers, relatives, and friends, and some of you will be put to death because of this.

17 You will be hated by everyone because of your association with my name.

18 Not a single hair on your head will be lost.

19 Through your endurance, you will gain mastery over your lives.

20 When you see Jerusalem surrounded by armies, then understand that its devastation is near.

21 At that time, those who are in Judea must flee to the mountains, and those who are in the city must leave it. Those who are in the surrounding countryside must not enter the city.

22 For these are the days of retribution, when all that has been written must be fulfilled.

23 But how terrible it will be for those who are pregnant and for those nursing infants in those days! For there will be great suffering in the land, and fierce anger against this people.

24 They will be killed by the sword and taken as captives to all nations. Jerusalem will be trampled underfoot by foreign powers until the appointed time for the Gentiles comes to an end.

25 There will be signs in the sun, the moon, and the stars; and on the earth, nations will be in turmoil and distress, overwhelmed with confusion. The sea and its waves will roar and surge.

26 People will be overcome with terror and dread, their hearts failing them as they anticipate the dreadful events that are about to unfold on the earth. For the very forces of the heavens will be thrown into upheaval.

27 And then they will see the Son of Man coming in a cloud with great power and overwhelming glory.

28 When these events begin to unfold, stand firm and lift your heads with confidence, for your deliverance is near.

29 He told them a parable: "Look at the fig tree and all the other trees.

30 When the trees begin to bud, you can see for yourselves and recognize that summer is near.

31 In the same way, when you see these things happening, understand that the kingdom of God is near.

32 Truly, I tell you, this generation will not pass away until all these things have taken place.

33 Heaven and earth will come to an end, but my words will never cease to be.

34 Be careful and guard yourselves, so that your hearts are not weighed down by excessive indulgence, drunkenness, and the anxieties of daily life. Otherwise, that day will come upon you suddenly, catching you unprepared.

35 For it will come suddenly, like a trap, upon all who live on the face of the entire earth.

36 Therefore, stay alert and pray continually, so that you may be considered worthy to escape all these things that are going to happen and to stand confidently before the Son of Man.

37 During the day, he taught in the temple, and at night he would go out and stay on the hill called the Mount of Olives.

38 And all the people rose early in the morning and gathered in the temple to listen to him.
