Genesis Chapter 42

1 When Jacob learned that there was grain available in Egypt, he said to his sons, "Why are you just standing here looking at each other?"

2 And he said, "Listen, I have heard that there is grain in Egypt. Go down there and buy some for us, so that we may survive and not perish."

3 Joseph’s ten brothers went down to Egypt to buy grain.

4 However, Jacob did not send Benjamin, Joseph’s brother, with his other sons, because he said, “I fear that harm might come to him.”

5 The Israelites went to Egypt to buy grain along with others who had come, for there was a severe famine in the land of Canaan.

6 Joseph was the ruler over the land, and he was the one who sold grain to all the people of the land. When Joseph’s brothers arrived, they bowed down before him with their faces to the ground.

7 Joseph recognized his brothers when he saw them, but he concealed his identity and treated them as strangers. He spoke harshly to them and asked, "Where have you come from?" They replied, "We have come from the land of Canaan to buy food."

8 Joseph recognized his brothers, but they did not recognize him.

9 Joseph recalled the dreams he had about them and said, "You are spies! You have come to uncover the weaknesses of the land."

10 They replied to him, "No, my lord, your servants have come only to buy food."

11 We are all sons of one man; we are honest men. Your servants are not spies.

12 And he said to them, "No, you have come to spy out the land's vulnerabilities."

13 They replied, "We are twelve brothers, the sons of one man in the land of Canaan. The youngest is with our father today, and one is no longer with us."

14 Joseph said to them, "This is exactly what I told you when I said, 'You are spies.'"

15 This is how you will be tested: As surely as Pharaoh lives, you will not leave this place unless your youngest brother comes here.

16 Choose one of you to go and bring back your brother, while the rest of you remain confined in prison. This will test the truth of your words to see if you are being honest. Otherwise, as surely as Pharaoh lives, you are spies.

17 He placed them all in custody for three days.

18 On the third day, Joseph said to them, "Do this, and you will live, for I am a God-fearing man."

19 If you are honest men, let one of your brothers remain confined in the place of your imprisonment. As for the rest of you, go and take grain to provide for the hunger in your households.

20 However, bring your youngest brother to me; in this way, your words will be proven true, and you will not die." And they did as they were instructed.

21 They said to one another, "We are truly guilty concerning our brother, for we saw the distress of his soul when he pleaded with us, and we refused to listen. That is why this trouble has come upon us."

22 Reuben responded to them, saying, "Did I not tell you, 'Do not harm the boy'? But you refused to listen. Now, see, his blood is being demanded from us."

23 They did not realize that Joseph could understand them, for he was speaking to them through an interpreter.

24 Joseph turned away from them and wept. Then he turned back to them and spoke with them again. He took Simeon from among them and had him bound in their presence.

25 Then Joseph gave orders to fill their bags with grain, to return each man’s money into his sack, and to provide them with supplies for their journey. This is what he did for them.

26 They loaded their donkeys with the grain and set out from there.

27 As one of them opened his sack to feed his donkey at the lodging place, he noticed his money—there it was, right at the top of his sack.

28 He said to his brothers, "My money has been returned—look, it’s right here in my sack!" Their hearts sank, and they were filled with fear, saying to one another, "What is this that God has done to us?"

29 They returned to their father Jacob in the land of Canaan and recounted to him everything that had happened to them, saying,

30 The man, who is the ruler of the land, spoke harshly to us and accused us of being spies sent to gather information about the country.

31 We said to him, "We are honest men; we are not spies."

32 We are twelve brothers, sons of our father. One is no longer with us, and the youngest is with our father today in the land of Canaan.

33 The man, who was the ruler of the land, said to us, "This is how I will know that you are honest men: leave one of your brothers here with me, take provisions to relieve the hunger of your families, and then go on your way."

34 Bring your youngest brother to me. Then I will know that you are not spies, but honest men. In that case, I will release your brother to you, and you will be free to trade in the land.

35 As they were emptying their sacks, they discovered, to their astonishment, that each man’s pouch of money was still inside his sack. When both the brothers and their father saw the bundles of money, they were filled with fear.

36 Jacob, their father, said to them, "You have deprived me of my children: Joseph is gone, Simeon is gone, and now you want to take Benjamin away. Everything is working against me."

37 Reuben said to his father, "You may put my two sons to death if I do not bring him back to you. Entrust him to my care, and I will return him to you."

38 And he said, "My son must not go down with you, for his brother is dead, and he alone remains. If any harm comes to him on the journey you are taking, you would bring my gray hair down to the grave in sorrow."
