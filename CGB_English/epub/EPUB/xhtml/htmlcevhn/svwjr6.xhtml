I Chronicles Chapter 14

1 Now Hiram, the king of Tyre, sent messengers to David, along with cedar wood, stonemasons, and carpenters, to construct a house for him.

2 David realized that the LORD had firmly established him as king over Israel, for his reign had been exalted and made great because of his people Israel.

3 David took additional wives in Jerusalem, and he fathered more sons and daughters.

4 Here are the names of his children born to him in Jerusalem: Shammua, Shobab, Nathan, and Solomon.

5 And Ibhar, Elishua, and Elpelet.

6 And Nogah, Nepheg, and Japhia.

7 And Elishama, Beeliada, and Eliphalet.

8 When the Philistines learned that David had been anointed as king over all Israel, they mobilized and went out in search of him. David received word of their advance and went out to confront them.

9 The Philistines advanced and deployed their forces throughout the Valley of Rephaim.

10 David sought guidance from God, asking, "Should I go and attack the Philistines? Will you hand them over to me?" The LORD responded to him, "Go up, for I will surely deliver them into your hands."

11 So they advanced to Baal-perazim, and there David defeated them. Then David declared, "God has broken through my enemies by my hand, like a flood of water bursting forth." Because of this, they named that place Baal-perazim, meaning "The Lord of the Breakthrough."

12 After they had abandoned their idols there, David issued a command, and they were burned in the fire.

13 Once more, the Philistines spread out across the valley.

14 So David inquired of God once more, and God said to him, "Do not go up after them. Turn away from them and circle around, so that you can come upon them opposite the mulberry trees."

15 When you hear the sound of marching in the tops of the balsam trees, then you must advance to battle, for God has gone out ahead of you to strike down the army of the Philistines.

16 David therefore acted in accordance with God's command, and they struck down the army of the Philistines from Gibeon all the way to Gezer.

17 David's reputation spread to every land, and the LORD caused all nations to be in awe of him.
