Isaiah Chapter 55

1 Listen, all who are thirsty! Come to the waters, and you who have no moneyâ€”come, buy, and eat! Yes, come and purchase wine and milk without cost or payment.

2 Why do you spend your money on what does not truly nourish you, and labor for what fails to satisfy? Listen carefully to me, and partake of what is good, so that your soul may delight in abundance.

3 Listen closely and come to me; pay attention, and your soul will find life. I will establish with you an everlasting covenant, the faithful and enduring promises I made to David.

4 Listen carefully, I have appointed him as a witness to the nations, a guide and a ruler for the people.

5 Listen carefully: You will summon a nation you do not know, and nations that do not know you will come running to you. This will happen because of the LORD your God, and because of the Holy One of Israel, for He has honored you.

6 Seek the LORD while He is accessible; call upon Him while He is near.

7 Let the wicked abandon their sinful ways, and the unrighteous person their harmful thoughts. Let them turn back to the LORD, and He will show them mercy; let them return to our God, for He will freely and generously forgive.

8 For my thoughts are not like your thoughts, nor are your ways like my ways, declares the LORD.

9 Just as the heavens are far above the earth, so are my ways far beyond your ways, and my thoughts far beyond your thoughts.

10 For just as the rain and snow descend from the heavens and do not return there without first watering the earth, causing it to produce and sprout, providing seed for the one who sows and bread for the one who eats:

11 This is how my word will be when it goes out from my mouth: it will not return to me empty, but it will achieve the purpose for which I sent it and succeed in what I intended it to do.

12 You will go out with joy and be guided forward in peace. The mountains and hills will burst into song before you, and all the trees of the field will applaud with their branches.

13 Instead of thornbushes, fir trees will grow, and instead of briers, myrtle trees will rise. This will stand as a testimony to the LORD, an enduring sign that will never be destroyed.
