Ephesians Chapter 5

1 Therefore, imitate God, as beloved children;

2 Live your lives in love, just as Christ loved us and gave himself up for us as an offering and sacrifice to God—a pleasing and fragrant act of devotion.

3 Engage in no sexual immorality, impurity, or greed, for such things must not even be mentioned among you, as is fitting for those who are holy.

4 Avoid all obscene speech, foolish chatter, and crude joking, which are out of place, but instead let there be expressions of gratitude.

5 You should understand this clearly: no sexually immoral person, no impure individual, nor anyone driven by greed—which is a form of idolatry—will have any share in the kingdom of Christ and of God.

6 Do not let anyone mislead you with empty or deceptive words, for it is because of such things that God’s wrath comes upon those who are defiant and refuse to obey Him.

7 Therefore, do not join with them or share in their ways.

8 There was a time when you were completely engulfed in darkness, but now you are filled with light because of your connection to the Lord. Live, then, as people who belong to the light.

9 For the produce of the Spirit consists entirely of goodness, righteousness, and truth.

10 Demonstrating what is pleasing and acceptable to the Lord.

11 Do not associate with or participate in the worthless deeds of darkness; instead, expose and confront them.

12 For it is disgraceful even to mention the things that they do in secret.

13 However, everything that is exposed and brought to light becomes visible, for whatever reveals and makes things clear is itself light.

14 Therefore, it is said: "Wake up, you who are asleep, rise from the dead, and Christ will shine his light upon you."

15 Therefore, be careful to live thoughtfully and with purpose, not as those who lack understanding, but as those who are wise.

16 Make the most of every opportunity, because the present times are filled with corruption and moral decay.

17 Therefore, do not act foolishly, but strive to comprehend what the Lord’s will is.

18 Do not become intoxicated with wine, which leads to reckless behavior, but instead be continually filled with the Spirit.

19 Encourage one another with psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs, singing and offering heartfelt praise to the Lord.

20 Always giving thanks to God the Father for everything in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ.

21 Yield to one another out of reverence for God.

22 Wives, place yourselves under the authority of your own husbands, as you would under the authority of the Lord.

23 For the husband holds a position of leadership and responsibility over the wife, just as Christ is the head and authority over the church. Moreover, Christ is the Savior who protects, sustains, and redeems the church, which is His body.

24 Therefore, just as the church submits to Christ, so also wives should submit to their own husbands in all things.

25 Husbands, love your wives just as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her.

26 So that he might set it apart as holy and purify it, cleansing it through the washing of water accompanied by the word.

27 So that he might present to himself the church in all its splendor, without stain, wrinkle, or any flaw, but that it might be holy and blameless.

28 Therefore, men should love their wives as they love their own bodies. The one who loves his wife is, in fact, loving himself.

29 No one has ever hated their own body; instead, they care for it and nurture it, just as the Lord cares for and nurtures the church.

30 For we are part of Christ's own body, united with His flesh and His bones.

31 For this reason, a man will leave his father and mother and be united to his wife, and the two will become one flesh.

32 This is a profound and sacred truth, but I am speaking about Christ and the church.

33 However, each one of you must love his wife as he loves himself, and the wife must respect and honor her husband.
