Numbers Chapter 31

1 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2 "Take vengeance for the Israelites against the Midianites; after that, you will be gathered to your ancestors."

3 Moses addressed the people, saying, "Prepare some of your men for battle, and send them to fight against the Midianites, to execute the LORD's vengeance on Midian."

4 From each tribe, you shall send one thousand men, representing all the tribes of Israel, to join the battle.

5 From the thousands of Israel, a thousand men were chosen from each tribe, totaling twelve thousand warriors prepared for battle.

6 Moses sent them to battle—a thousand men from each tribe, along with Phinehas, the son of Eleazar the priest. They went to war carrying the sacred vessels and the trumpets for sounding the alarm, which Phinehas held in his hand.

7 They fought against the Midianites, just as the LORD had commanded Moses, and they killed all the men.

8 They killed the kings of Midian, in addition to the others who were slain: Evi, Rekem, Zur, Hur, and Reba—five kings of Midian. They also killed Balaam, the son of Beor, with the sword.

9 The Israelites captured all the Midianite women and their children, and they seized as plunder all their livestock—their cattle, flocks, and all their possessions.

10 They set fire to all the cities where they lived and destroyed all their fortified strongholds, burning them completely.

11 They gathered all the plunder and everything they had seized, including both people and animals.

12 They brought the captives, the plunder, and the spoils of war to Moses, to Eleazar the priest, and to the entire community of the Israelites at the camp on the plains of Moab, near the Jordan River, close to Jericho.

13 Moses, along with Eleazar the priest and all the leaders of the community, went out to meet them outside the camp.

14 Moses became angry with the commanders of the army—the officers over thousands and the officers over hundreds—who had returned from the battle.

15 Moses said to them, "Have you allowed all the women to live?"

16 Look, these individuals led the Israelites, following Balaam’s advice, to act unfaithfully against the LORD in the incident at Peor. As a result, a plague broke out among the LORD’s community.

17 Now, therefore, execute every male among the young, and put to death every woman who has had sexual relations with a man.

18 However, all the young women who have not had sexual relations with a man, you may keep alive for yourselves.

19 You must remain outside the camp for seven days. Anyone who has killed someone or has touched a dead body must purify themselves and their captives on the third day and again on the seventh day.

20 Cleanse all your garments, everything made from animal skins, all items crafted from goats' hair, and every object made of wood.

21 And Eleazar the priest said to the soldiers who had gone to battle, "This is the regulation of the law that the LORD commanded Moses."

22 Only the gold, the silver, the bronze, the iron, the tin, and the lead.

23 Everything that can withstand fire, you must pass through the fire, and it will be clean. However, it must also be purified with the water of purification. As for anything that cannot endure fire, you must pass it through the water.

24 On the seventh day, you must wash your clothes, and you will be clean. Afterward, you may enter the camp.

25 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

26 Count the total number of captives taken, both people and animals, you, along with Eleazar the priest, and the leaders of the community.

27 Divide the plunder into two portions: one part for those who engaged in the battle and went out to war, and the other part for the entire community.

28 Take a tribute for the Lord from the soldiers who went out to battle: one out of every five hundred, whether from the people, the cattle, the donkeys, or the sheep.

29 Take a portion from their share and give it to Eleazar the priest as a contribution to the LORD.

30 From the portion belonging to the Israelites, you shall take one out of every fifty—whether from the people, the cattle, the donkeys, the flocks, or any other livestock—and give them to the Levites, who are responsible for the care of the tabernacle of the LORD.

31 Moses and Eleazar the priest carried out everything exactly as the LORD had commanded Moses.

32 The plunder, which was the remainder of the spoils taken by the soldiers, consisted of 675,000 sheep.

33 "And seventy-two thousand cattle,"

34 And sixty-one thousand donkeys,

35 The total number of persons was thirty-two thousand, including the women who had never been intimate with a man.

36 The portion belonging to those who went out to war was 337,500 sheep.

37 The portion set aside for the LORD from the sheep totaled six hundred and seventy-five.

38 The cattle numbered thirty-six thousand, and the Lord's portion from them was seventy-two.

39 The donkeys numbered thirty thousand five hundred, and from these, the portion dedicated to the LORD was sixty-one.

40 The total number of persons was sixteen thousand, and from these, the LORD’s portion was thirty-two persons.

41 Moses presented the tribute, which was the LORD's sacred offering, to Eleazar the priest, just as the LORD had commanded him.

42 And from the portion belonging to the Israelites, which Moses had set apart from the men who had gone to war,

43 Now, the portion that belonged to the community was 337,500 sheep,

44 And thirty-six thousand cattle,

45 And thirty thousand donkeys and five hundred.

46 And there were sixteen thousand people.

47 From the portion belonging to the Israelites, Moses took one out of every fifty, both of people and animals, and gave them to the Levites, who were responsible for the care of the tabernacle of the LORD. This was done just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

48 The commanders who were in charge of the army divisions—the leaders of thousands and the leaders of hundreds—approached Moses.

49 And they said to Moses, "Your servants have counted the men of war under our command, and not a single one of us is missing."

50 We have therefore brought an offering to the LORD, consisting of what each person has acquired—gold jewelry, chains, bracelets, rings, earrings, and ornaments—to seek reconciliation for our lives before the LORD.

51 Moses and Eleazar the priest received the gold from them, including all the finely crafted ornaments.

52 All the gold that was presented as an offering to the LORD by the commanders of thousands and the commanders of hundreds amounted to sixteen thousand seven hundred and fifty shekels.

53 The soldiers had taken plunder, each man keeping what he had seized for himself.

54 Moses and Eleazar the priest collected the gold from the commanders of thousands and of hundreds and brought it into the tent of meeting as a lasting reminder before the LORD for the Israelites.
