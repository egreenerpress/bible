Ephesians Chapter 1

1 Paul, an apostle of Jesus Christ by the purpose and will of God, to the holy and dedicated people who are in Ephesus, and to all who are faithful and committed to Christ Jesus:

2 May grace and peace be yours, given by God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

3 Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has richly blessed us with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly realms through our union with Christ.

4 Just as He chose us in Him before the creation of the world, so that we might be holy and blameless in His presence, living in love.

5 God chose us beforehand to be adopted as His children through Jesus Christ, in accordance with His kind and purposeful will.

6 To the honor and praise of his glorious grace, by which he has shown us favor and brought us into his beloved family.

7 In Him, we have been set free through His blood, receiving the forgiveness of our sins, in accordance with the abundant wealth of His grace.

8 In this, he has richly provided for us with complete wisdom and understanding.

9 He has revealed to us the hidden purpose of his will, in accordance with his kind intention that he planned within himself.

10 In the appointed time of fulfillment, God’s purpose is to bring together all things in Christ—everything in heaven and everything on earth—uniting them under his authority.

11 In Him, we have also received an inheritance, having been predestined according to the plan of Him who accomplishes all things according to the purpose of His own will.

12 We are destined to bring praise to His glory—we who were the first to place our hope in Christ.

13 In Him, you also placed your trust after hearing the message of truth, the good news of your salvation. When you believed in Him, you were marked with the seal of the promised Holy Spirit.

14 This is the guarantee of our inheritance, serving as a pledge until the full redemption of what God has claimed as his own, all to the praise of his glorious grace.

15 Therefore, I too, having heard about your faith in the Lord Jesus and your love for all the saints,

16 I continually give thanks for you, always remembering you in my prayers.

17 I pray that the God of our Lord Jesus Christ, the glorious Father, may grant you the Spirit of wisdom and revelation, so that you may truly know Him.

18 May the eyes of your heart be opened and enlightened, so that you may fully grasp the hope to which He has called you, the glorious wealth of His inheritance reserved for His holy people.

19 And what is the immeasurable greatness of his power toward us who believe, in accordance with the working of his immense strength,

20 This is the power that God demonstrated in Christ when He raised Him from the dead and seated Him at His right hand in the heavenly realms,

21 He is exalted far above every ruler, authority, power, and dominion, and above every name that is invoked, not only in this present age but also in the age to come.

22 And He has placed all things under His authority, appointing Him as the supreme head over everything for the benefit of the church.

23 This is his body, the complete expression of him who fills everything in every way.
