I Corinthians Chapter 15

1 Furthermore, my fellow believers, I want to remind you of the good news that I proclaimed to you, which you embraced and in which you now stand firm.

2 Through this message, you are being saved, provided you hold firmly to the teachings I delivered to you—unless your faith was without genuine purpose.

3 I passed on to you as of primary importance what I also received: that Christ died for our sins in accordance with the Scriptures.

4 And that He was buried, and that He was raised on the third day, in fulfillment of the Scriptures.

5 And He appeared to Cephas, and then to the twelve.

6 Afterward, he appeared to more than five hundred brothers and sisters at the same time. Most of them are still alive today, though some have passed away.

7 Afterward, he appeared to James; then to all the apostles.

8 And finally, he appeared to me as well, like someone born at an unexpected or unusual time.

9 For I am the least worthy of the apostles, unfit even to be called an apostle, because I once persecuted the church of God.

10 By the undeserved favor of God, I am who I am, and His grace given to me was not without effect. I worked harder than all of them—yet not I, but the grace of God that was with me.

11 Therefore, whether it was I or they who preached, this is how we proclaimed the message, and this is how you came to believe.

12 Now if it is proclaimed that Christ has been raised from the dead, how can some among you claim that there is no resurrection of the dead?

13 But if there is no resurrection of the dead, then Christ has not been raised either.

14 If Christ has not been raised from the dead, then our preaching is meaningless, and your faith is also meaningless.

15 Indeed, we would be exposed as false witnesses about God, because we have testified that God raised Christ from the dead. But if the dead are not raised, then Christ was not raised either.

16 If the dead are not raised, then Christ has not been raised either.

17 And if Christ has not been raised, your faith is meaningless; you are still trapped in your sins.

18 Then those who have died in Christ would also be lost.

19 If our hope in Christ is limited to this life alone, we are to be pitied more than anyone else.

20 But now Christ has been raised from the dead and has become the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep.

21 For just as death came through a human being, so too the resurrection of the dead has come through a human being.

22 Just as all people die because of their connection to Adam, so too will all people be brought to life because of their connection to Christ.

23 But each person will be raised in their proper sequence: Christ, as the firstfruits of the resurrection; then, at his coming, those who belong to Christ.

24 Then the end will come, when he hands over the kingdom to God the Father, after he has abolished all dominion, authority, and power.

25 For he must continue to rule until he has completely subdued all his enemies beneath his authority.

26 The final adversary to be vanquished is death.

27 For God has placed all things under his authority. But when it is said that "all things are put under him," it is clear that this does not include God himself, who is the one who subjected all things to him.

28 When all things are brought under his authority, then the Son himself will also submit to the one who placed all things under his rule, so that God may be supreme over everything.

29 Otherwise, what will those who are baptized on behalf of the dead do if the dead are not raised at all? Why then are they baptized on their behalf?

30 Why do we constantly put ourselves in danger every hour?

31 I solemnly declare, by the joy I have in Christ Jesus our Lord, that I face death every day.

32 If, as a mere human, I have fought with wild beasts in Ephesus, what benefit is it to me if the dead are not raised? Let us eat and drink, for tomorrow we die.

33 Do not be misled: harmful influences and associations can corrupt good character and behavior.

34 Wake up to what is right and stop sinning, for some of you lack the knowledge of God. I say this to bring you to a place of shame.

35 But someone might ask, "How are the dead raised? And with what kind of body will they come back?"

36 You fool! What you sow does not come to life unless it first dies.

37 What you sow is not the body that will eventually grow, but a simple seed—perhaps of wheat or some other grain.

38 But God gives it a body as He has chosen, and to each kind of seed its own distinct form.

39 Not all flesh is the same: there is one kind of flesh for humans, another for animals, another for fish, and yet another for birds.

40 There are heavenly bodies and earthly bodies, but the splendor of the heavenly is of one kind, and the splendor of the earthly is of another.

41 The sun has its own unique splendor, the moon its distinct radiance, and the stars their individual brilliance. Even among the stars, each one shines with a glory that sets it apart from the others.

42 The resurrection of the dead follows a similar pattern. The body is buried in a state of decay, but it will be raised in a state of imperishability.

43 It is planted in dishonor, but it is raised in glory. It is planted in weakness, but it is raised in strength.

44 The body is buried as a physical, earthly body, but it is raised as a spiritual, heavenly body. There is an earthly, physical body, and there is also a spiritual, heavenly body.

45 As it is written, "The first man, Adam, became a living being"; the last Adam became a life-giving spirit.

46 However, what comes first is not the spiritual, but the natural; afterward comes the spiritual.

47 The first man is earthly, formed from the dust of the ground; the second man is the Lord, who comes from heaven.

48 Just as the earthly nature defines those who are of the earth, so also the heavenly nature defines those who are of heaven.

49 Just as we have carried the likeness of the earthly, we will also carry the likeness of the heavenly.

50 I want to make this clear, my brothers and sisters: human beings, in their mortal and perishable state, cannot inherit the kingdom of God. What is temporary and subject to decay cannot inherit what is eternal and imperishable.

51 Listen carefully, I am revealing a profound truth to you: Not all of us will die, but all of us will be transformed.

52 In an instant, in the blink of an eye, at the final trumpet blast—for the trumpet will sound, and the dead will be raised imperishable, and we will be transformed.

53 For this perishable body must be clothed with imperishability, and this mortal body must be clothed with immortality.

54 When this perishable body has been clothed with imperishability, and this mortal body has been clothed with immortality, then the words that are written will come to pass: "Death has been swallowed up in victory."

55 O death, where is your power to harm? O grave, where is your triumph?

56 The power of death lies in sin, and the force of sin is derived from the law.

57 But we give thanks to God, who grants us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ.

58 Therefore, my dearly loved brothers and sisters, stand firm and unwavering, always excelling in the work of the Lord, because you know that your efforts in the Lord are not without purpose or reward.
