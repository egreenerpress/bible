Psalms Chapter 63

1 (A Psalm of David, written when he was in the wilderness of Judah.) O God, You are my God; earnestly I seek You. My soul thirsts for You; my whole being longs for You in this parched and weary land, where there is no water.

2 To witness your mighty power and your glorious presence, just as I have beheld you in your holy sanctuary.

3 Because your steadfast love is more precious than life itself, my lips will honor you with praise.

4 Therefore, I will praise you all my life; I will raise my hands in worship to your name.

5 My soul will be fully satisfied, as with the richest and most nourishing food, and my mouth will praise you with lips overflowing with joy.

6 When I think of you as I lie on my bed, and reflect on you during the quiet hours of the night.

7 Because you have been my help, I will rejoice in the shelter of your wings.

8 My soul clings closely to you; your right hand supports me.

9 But those who seek to take my life, to destroy me, will descend to the depths of the earth.

10 They will die by the sword; their bodies will become food for jackals.

11 But the king will find joy in God; all who swear allegiance to Him will take pride, while the mouths of those who speak deceit will be silenced.
