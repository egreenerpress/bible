Matthew Chapter 8

1 When he came down from the mountain, large crowds followed him.

2 Suddenly, a man with leprosy approached and knelt before him, saying, “Lord, if you are willing, you have the power to make me clean.”

3 Jesus reached out his hand and touched him, saying, "I am willing; be clean." And immediately, his leprosy was healed.

4 And Jesus said to him, "Make sure you do not tell anyone about this. Instead, go on your way, present yourself to the priest, and offer the gift that Moses commanded, as a testimony to them."

5 When Jesus entered Capernaum, a centurion approached him, pleading with him,

6 And he said, "Lord, my servant is at home, paralyzed and suffering terribly."

7 And Jesus said to him, "I will go and restore him to health."

8 The centurion replied, "Lord, I am not worthy for you to come under my roof. But simply speak the word, and my servant will be healed."

9 For I am a man who operates under authority, with soldiers under my command. I say to one, ‘Go,’ and he goes; to another, ‘Come,’ and he comes; and to my servant, ‘Do this,’ and he does it.

10 When Jesus heard this, he was amazed and said to those who were following him, "Truly I tell you, I have not found such great faith, not even in Israel."

11 I tell you this: Many will come from the east and the west and will recline at the table with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob in the kingdom of heaven.

12 However, those who are considered heirs of the kingdom will be expelled into the farthest darkness, where there will be intense sorrow and bitter anguish.

13 And Jesus said to the centurion, "Go your way; as you have believed, so let it be done for you." And his servant was healed at that very moment.

14 When Jesus entered Peter’s home, he saw Peter’s mother-in-law lying there, ill with a fever.

15 He reached out and touched her hand, and immediately her fever disappeared. Then she got up and began to serve them.

16 When evening arrived, they brought to Him many who were afflicted by demonic spirits. He drove out the spirits with His command and healed all who were sick.

17 This happened to fulfill what was spoken by the prophet Isaiah, who said, "He took upon himself our weaknesses and carried our diseases."

18 When Jesus noticed large crowds gathering around him, he instructed his disciples to set out for the other side of the lake.

19 A scribe approached Him and said, "Teacher, I will follow You wherever You go."

20 Jesus said to him, "Foxes have dens, and birds of the sky have nests, but the Son of Man has no place to rest his head."

21 Then another of His disciples said to Him, “Lord, let me first go and bury my father.”

22 But Jesus said to him, "Follow me, and let those who are spiritually dead take care of burying their own dead."

23 When he got into the boat, his disciples went with him.

24 Suddenly, a violent storm arose on the sea, so fierce that the waves were crashing over the boat and threatening to engulf it. Yet, Jesus was asleep.

25 Then His disciples approached Him and woke Him, saying, "Lord, save us! We are about to die!"

26 And he said to them, "Why are you afraid, you who have so little trust in me?" Then he stood up and commanded the winds and the sea to be still, and there was a profound calm.

27 The men were amazed and said, "What kind of man is this, that even the winds and the sea obey him?"

28 When Jesus arrived on the other side of the lake, in the region of the Gergesenes, two men who were possessed by demons came out from the tombs to meet him. They were so violent that no one could safely travel along that road.

29 And suddenly, they cried out, saying, “What do you want with us, Jesus, Son of God? Have you come here to torment us before the appointed time?”

30 And at some distance from them, there was a large herd of pigs feeding.

31 The demons pleaded with Him, saying, "If You are going to drive us out, allow us to go into the herd of pigs."

32 He said to them, "Go." And when they came out, they entered the herd of pigs. Suddenly, the entire herd rushed down the steep slope into the sea and drowned in the waters.

33 The men who were tending the pigs fled and went into the city, where they reported everything that had happened, including what had occurred to those who had been demon-possessed.

34 And look, the entire city came out to meet Jesus. But when they saw him, they pleaded with him to leave their region.
