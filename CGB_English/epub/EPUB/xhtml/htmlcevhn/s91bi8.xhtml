Hosea Chapter 11

1 When Israel was young, I loved him, and I called my son out of Egypt.

2 As they were called, they turned away from God; they offered sacrifices to the Baals and burned incense to carved idols.

3 I guided Ephraim, holding them by their arms to help them walk; yet they did not recognize that I was the one who healed them.

4 I led them with gentle cords, with ties of love. I was to them like one who lifts the yoke from their jaws, and I provided food for them.

5 He will not go back to the land of Egypt, but the Assyrian will rule over him as his king, because they refused to turn back [to God].

6 The sword will remain over his cities, destroying their defenses and consuming their outlying towns, all because of their own misguided plans.

7 My people are determined to turn away from me. Even when they are called to turn to the Most High, not one of them seeks to honor or lift him up.

8 How can I abandon you, Ephraim? How can I surrender you, Israel? How can I treat you like Admah? How can I reduce you to the fate of Zeboim? My heart is deeply troubled within me, and my compassion burns intensely.

9 I will not unleash the full intensity of my anger, nor will I return to utterly destroy Ephraim. For I am God, not a mere humanâ€”the Holy One who dwells among you. I will not come in wrath to destroy your city.

10 They will follow the LORD faithfully. He will roar like a lion, and when He roars, His people will come trembling from the west.

11 They will come trembling like a bird fleeing from Egypt, and like a dove returning from the land of Assyria. Then I will settle them securely in their homes, declares the LORD.

12 Ephraim surrounds me with falsehood, and the house of Israel with deception. Yet Judah continues to walk with God and remains faithful to the Holy One.
