Psalms Chapter 17

1 (A Prayer of David.) O LORD, listen to my plea for justice; pay attention to my cry. Hear my prayer, for it comes from sincere lips, not from deceit or falsehood.

2 Allow my judgment to proceed from your presence; may your eyes see what is just and fair.

3 You have examined my heart; you have come to me in the night; you have tested me, and you will find nothing [wrong]. I am determined that my words will not lead me into sin.

4 Regarding the actions of humanity, by following the guidance of Your words, I have kept myself from walking in the ways of the destroyer.

5 Guide my steps along your ways, so that I may walk securely and not stumble.

6 I have called out to you, for you will answer me, O God. Turn your ear toward me and listen to my words.

7 Display your extraordinary steadfast love, O you who deliver by your mighty power those who take refuge in you from those who rise up against them.

8 Guard me as the most precious part of your sight; shelter me in the protective shadow of your wings.

9 From the wicked who oppress me, from my mortal enemies who surround me on every side.

10 They are engulfed in their own abundance; with their mouths they speak arrogantly.

11 They have now surrounded us in our every move; they have fixed their gaze, intent on bringing us low to the ground.

12 Like a lion eager for its prey, or a young lion lying in wait in hidden places.

13 Rise up, O LORD, thwart his plans and bring him low; rescue my soul from the wicked, who serve as your instrument of judgment.

15 As for me, I will gaze upon your face in righteousness; I will be fully content when I awaken, beholding your likeness.
