Psalms Chapter 4

1 (For the director of music, to be accompanied by stringed instruments. A psalm of David.) God, my righteous defender, hear my cry. You have relieved me in times of trouble; show me mercy now and listen to my prayer.

2 O you who are called men, how long will you dishonor my glory? How long will you pursue emptiness and chase after lies? Pause and reflect.

3 But understand this: the LORD has chosen the faithful for himself; the LORD will listen when I call out to him.

4 Be deeply reverent and refrain from sin. Reflect quietly in your heart as you lie on your bed, and remain calm. Pause and consider this.

5 Present your offerings with integrity and sincerity, and place your complete confidence in the LORD.

6 Many people ask, "Who can show us anything good?" LORD, let the light of your presence shine upon us.

7 You have filled my heart with joy, greater than the joy they experience when their grain and wine abound.

8 I will lie down in peace and sleep, for you, LORD, alone make me dwell in safety.
