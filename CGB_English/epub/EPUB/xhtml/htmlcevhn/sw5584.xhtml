I Peter Chapter 1

1 Peter, an apostle of Jesus Christ, to the exiles dispersed throughout Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, and Bithynia,

2 Chosen according to the foreknowledge of God the Father, set apart by the work of the Spirit, for the purpose of obedience and the cleansing power of the blood of Jesus Christ: May grace and peace be abundantly multiplied to you.

3 Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ! In His great mercy, He has given us new birth into a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead.

4 To an inheritance that is imperishable, unstained, and unfading, kept secure in heaven for you.

5 You are protected by the power of God through your faith, leading to the salvation that is prepared to be fully revealed at the final time.

6 You greatly rejoice in this, even though for a little while, if necessary, you may experience grief through various trials.

7 The testing of your faith, which is far more valuable than perishable gold—even though it is refined by fire—will result in praise, honor, and glory when Jesus Christ is revealed.

8 Though you have not seen him, you love him. Even though you do not see him now, you believe in him and are filled with an indescribable and glorious joy.

9 You are receiving the ultimate outcome of your faith, which is the salvation of your souls.

10 Concerning this salvation, the prophets carefully investigated and searched intently, those who foretold of the grace that was destined to come to you.

11 They were seeking to understand what, or what kind of time, the Spirit of Christ within them was indicating when it foretold the sufferings of Christ and the subsequent glory that would follow.

12 It was revealed to them that they were not serving themselves, but you, in communicating these things which have now been proclaimed to you by those who preached the gospel to you through the Holy Spirit sent from heaven. These are matters into which even angels long to look.

13 Therefore, prepare your minds for action, remain clear-headed, and set your hope fully on the grace that will be given to you when Jesus Christ is revealed.

14 As children who obey, do not conform to the desires you once had in your ignorance.

15 But just as the One who called you is holy, you also must be holy in every aspect of your conduct.

16 For it is written: "You must be holy, because I am holy."

17 And if you appeal to the Father, who judges everyone impartially according to their actions, then live out the time of your temporary stay here with reverence.

18 You are aware that you were not redeemed with perishable things, such as silver or gold, from the empty way of life handed down to you from your ancestors.

19 But with the precious blood of Christ, like that of a flawless and unblemished lamb.

20 He was indeed chosen and appointed before the creation of the world, but was revealed in these final times for your sake.

21 Through him, you believe in God, who raised him from the dead and granted him glory, so that your faith and hope might rest in God.

22 Since you have purified your souls by obeying the truth through the Spirit, resulting in a sincere love for your fellow believers, make sure to love one another earnestly and with a pure heart.

23 You have been born anew, not from perishable seed, but from imperishable, through the living and enduring word of God, which remains forever.

24 All humanity is like grass, and all human glory is like the flower of the grass. The grass withers, and its flower falls away.

25 But the word of the Lord remains eternal. And this is the message that has been proclaimed to you through the gospel.
