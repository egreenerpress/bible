Proverbs Chapter 28

1 The wicked flee even when no one is chasing them, but the righteous stand with the boldness of a lion.

2 When a land continually sins, it will have many rulers; but with a person of wisdom and understanding, its stability will endure.

3 A poor person who oppresses others in poverty is like a relentless downpour that destroys the crops and leaves no sustenance behind.

4 Those who abandon the law commend the wicked, but those who uphold the law oppose them.

5 Wicked individuals fail to grasp what is just and right, but those who earnestly seek the LORD comprehend all matters fully.

6 It is better to be poor and live with integrity than to be wealthy and live with dishonesty.

7 A son who obeys the law demonstrates wisdom, but one who associates with reckless and unruly people brings disgrace to his father.

8 The one who increases his wealth through interest and dishonest gain will ultimately gather it for someone who will show compassion to the poor.

9 Whoever refuses to listen to the law, even their prayers will be detestable.

10 Whoever leads the righteous astray onto a wicked path will themselves fall into the pit they have dug; but those who live with integrity will inherit what is good.

11 A wealthy person may consider themselves wise in their own eyes, but a discerning person of humble means will see through their self-deception.

12 When the righteous rejoice, there is great honor and splendor; but when the wicked rise to power, people go into hiding.

13 Whoever conceals their wrongdoing will not succeed, but the one who acknowledges and turns away from their sins will receive compassion.

14 Blessed is the person who lives in constant reverence and awe of God, for they walk in wisdom and security. However, those who stubbornly harden their hearts and refuse to heed Godâ€™s ways will inevitably stumble into trouble and calamity.

15 Like a roaring lion or a prowling bear, so is a wicked ruler over a vulnerable people.

16 A ruler who lacks wisdom and discernment becomes a harsh oppressor, but one who rejects greed and selfish gain will enjoy a long and prosperous life.

17 A person who commits violence against another's life will flee to the grave; let no one stop them.

18 Whoever lives with integrity will be kept safe, but those who are crooked in their ways will suddenly be brought to ruin.

19 The one who cultivates his land will have an abundance of food, but whoever chases after worthless pursuits will end up in poverty.

20 A person who lives with integrity will be richly blessed, but whoever is eager to gain wealth through dishonest means will not remain blameless.

21 Showing favoritism is not right, for even for a small bribe a person may act unjustly.

22 Whoever is eager to gain wealth quickly has a greedy and envious nature, and fails to realize that poverty will eventually overtake them.

23 A person who offers honest correction will ultimately gain more respect and favor than one who speaks only flattering words.

24 Whoever steals from their father or mother and says, "It is not wrong," that person is no better than a destroyer.

25 A person with a proud heart stirs up conflict, but whoever places their trust in the LORD will prosper abundantly.

26 Whoever relies on their own understanding is foolish, but those who live with wisdom will find safety and deliverance.

27 Whoever gives to the poor will never be in need, but whoever turns a blind eye will face many troubles.

28 When the wicked rise to power, people go into hiding; but when the wicked are brought to ruin, the righteous flourish and multiply.
