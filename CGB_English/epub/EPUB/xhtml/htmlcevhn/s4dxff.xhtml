II Chronicles Chapter 27

1 Jotham was twenty-five years old when he became king, and he ruled for sixteen years in Jerusalem. His mother’s name was Jerushah, the daughter of Zadok.

2 He did what was right in the eyes of the LORD, following all the ways of his father Uzziah. However, he did not enter the temple of the LORD. Meanwhile, the people continued to act corruptly.

3 He constructed the grand entrance to the house of the LORD, and he also carried out extensive building work on the wall of Ophel.

4 Furthermore, he constructed cities in the hill country of Judah, and in the wooded areas he built fortresses and watchtowers.

5 He also waged war against the king of the Ammonites and defeated them. That same year, the Ammonites gave him a tribute of one hundred talents of silver, ten thousand measures of wheat, and ten thousand measures of barley. The Ammonites continued to pay this tribute to him in both the second and third years.

6 So Jotham grew powerful because he ordered his life in accordance with the ways of the LORD his God.

7 The remaining events of Jotham’s reign, including all his military campaigns and his accomplishments, are indeed recorded in the Book of the Kings of Israel and Judah.

8 He was twenty-five years old when he became king, and he ruled for sixteen years in Jerusalem.

9 Jotham died and was buried with his ancestors in the City of David. His son Ahaz succeeded him as king.
