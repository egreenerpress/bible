Amos Chapter 7

1 This is what the Lord GOD showed me: He was forming locusts at the time when the late spring crops began to sprout—indeed, it was the growth that comes after the king’s harvest had been gathered.

2 After they had completely consumed the vegetation of the land, I cried out, "O Lord GOD, please forgive! How can Jacob survive? He is so weak and insignificant."

3 The LORD reconsidered this matter and declared, "It shall not happen," says the LORD.

4 This is what the Lord GOD revealed to me: The Lord GOD summoned a judgment by fire, and it consumed the vast depths, devouring a portion of the land.

5 Then I said, "O Lord GOD, please stop! How can Jacob survive? He is so weak and insignificant."

6 The LORD relented concerning this: "This too shall not come to pass," declares the Lord GOD.

7 This is what he showed me: I saw the Lord standing by a wall that had been built true to the plumb line, and in his hand was a plumb line.

8 The LORD said to me, "Amos, what do you see?" I replied, "A plumb line." Then the Lord said, "Look, I am setting a plumb line among my people Israel; I will no longer overlook their actions."

9 The sacred shrines of Isaac will be destroyed, and the holy places of Israel will be left in ruins. I will rise up against the house of Jeroboam and bring judgment upon them with the sword.

10 Then Amaziah, the priest of Bethel, sent a message to Jeroboam, king of Israel, saying, "Amos is plotting against you in the midst of the people of Israel. The land cannot endure all his words."

11 For this is what Amos says: Jeroboam will die by the sword, and Israel will certainly be taken into exile from their own land.

12 Amaziah also said to Amos, "You who claim to be a seer, go and flee to the land of Judah. Earn your living there and prophesy there instead."

13 But do not prophesy anymore at Bethel, for it is the king's sanctuary and the royal residence.

14 Amos responded to Amaziah, saying, "I am not a prophet, nor am I the son of a prophet. I was simply a shepherd and a tender of sycamore fig trees."

15 The LORD called me while I was tending the flock, and the LORD said to me, "Go and proclaim my message to my people Israel."

16 Now then, listen to the word of the LORD: You say, "Do not prophesy against Israel, and do not speak your words against the house of Isaac."

17 Therefore, this is what the LORD says: Your wife will become a prostitute in the city, and your sons and daughters will die by the sword. Your land will be divided and measured out for others, and you yourself will die in an unclean land. Israel will certainly be taken into exile, removed from its own land.
