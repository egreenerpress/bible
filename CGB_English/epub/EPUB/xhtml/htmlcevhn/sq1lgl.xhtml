Romans Chapter 11

1 I ask then, has God rejected his people? Certainly not! For I myself am an Israelite, a descendant of Abraham, from the tribe of Benjamin.

2 God has not rejected his people, whom he chose beforehand. Do you not know what the Scripture says about Elijah? It tells how he pleaded with God against Israel, saying,

3 Lord, they have slain your prophets and demolished your altars; I am the only one remaining, and now they are seeking to take my life.

4 But what does God’s response say to him? "I have kept for myself seven thousand men who have not bowed the knee to Baal."

5 In the same way, even now, there remains a chosen few by God's gracious selection.

6 If it is by grace, then it is no longer based on works; otherwise, grace would no longer be grace. But if it is by works, then it is no longer grace; otherwise, work would no longer be work.

7 What does this mean? Israel has not attained what it was seeking, but those who were chosen have attained it, while the rest were hardened in their hearts.

8 As it is written: "God has given them a spirit of deep sleep, eyes that cannot see, and ears that cannot hear," and this remains true to this very day.

9 And David says, "May their table become a snare, a trap, a stumbling block, and a retribution for them."

10 May their eyes be darkened so they cannot see, and may their backs be bent continually.

11 So I ask, have they stumbled so as to fall completely? Certainly not! Instead, through their failure, salvation has come to the Gentiles, in order to stir them to jealousy.

12 Now if their stumbling has brought riches to the world, and their loss has brought wealth to the Gentiles, how much greater will their full inclusion bring?

13 I am speaking to you who are not Jewish, and since I have been appointed as an apostle specifically to the Gentiles, I take great pride in my role and responsibility.

14 If somehow I might stir up my own people to jealousy and save some of them.

15 If their rejection has led to the reconciliation of the world, what will their acceptance bring but life from the dead?

16 For if the first portion is holy, then the whole batch is also holy; and if the root is holy, so too are the branches.

17 And if some of the branches were broken off, and you, though a wild olive shoot, were grafted in among them and now share in the nourishing root and richness of the cultivated olive tree,

18 Do not boast against the branches. But if you do boast, remember that you do not support the root; rather, the root supports you.

19 You might then say, "The branches were broken off so that I could be grafted in."

20 Very well; they were cut off because of their unbelief, and you stand firm through faith. Do not become arrogant, but be cautious and reverent.

21 For if God did not spare the natural branches, be careful that he does not spare you either.

22 Consider, then, both the kindness and the sternness of God: sternness toward those who have fallen, but kindness toward you—provided you remain in his kindness. Otherwise, you too will be cut off.

23 And if they do not persist in their unbelief, they too will be grafted back in, for God has the power to graft them in once more.

24 For if you were cut off from a wild olive tree, which is naturally uncultivated, and were grafted, contrary to nature, into a cultivated olive tree, how much more will these, who are the natural branches, be grafted back into their own olive tree?

25 Brothers and sisters, I do not want you to be unaware of this divine mystery, so that you do not become arrogant in your own understanding. A partial hardening has come upon Israel until the full number of the Gentiles has come in.

26 And so all of Israel will be saved, as it is written: "The Deliverer will come from Zion, and he will remove ungodliness from Jacob."

27 This is my covenant with them: when I remove their sins.

28 Regarding the gospel, they are opposed to it for your benefit; but concerning God's chosen people, they are dearly loved because of the promises made to their ancestors.

29 For God’s gifts and His calling are irrevocable.

30 In the past, you did not believe in God, but now you have received mercy because of their unbelief.

31 In the same way, these have now also not believed, so that through the mercy shown to you, they too may receive mercy.

32 For God has bound all people in disobedience, so that He may show mercy to all.

33 Oh, the immeasurable depth of the wealth of God’s wisdom and knowledge! How beyond comprehension are His judgments, and how unfathomable His ways!

34 Who has fully understood the thoughts of the Lord? Or who has ever advised Him or guided His decisions?

35 Who has ever given to God first, so that God would owe them anything in return?

36 Everything comes from him, exists through him, and is directed toward him. To him be glory forever. Amen.
