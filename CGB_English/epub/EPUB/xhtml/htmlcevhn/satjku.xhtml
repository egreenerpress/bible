Genesis Chapter 46

1 Israel set out on his journey with all his possessions and arrived at Beersheba. There, he offered sacrifices to the God of his father Isaac.

2 And God spoke to Israel in a vision during the night, saying, "Jacob, Jacob." And he replied, "Here I am."

3 And God said, "I am the Almighty, the God of your father. Do not be afraid to go down to Egypt, for there I will make you into a great nation."

4 I will go down with you to Egypt, and I will certainly bring you back again. Joseph will be the one to close your eyes when you die.

5 Jacob set out from Beersheba, and the sons of Israel transported their father Jacob, along with their children and wives, in the wagons that Pharaoh had provided for the journey.

6 They brought their livestock and all the possessions they had acquired in the land of Canaan, and Jacob, along with all his descendants, entered Egypt.

7 He brought with him into Egypt his sons and his grandsons, his daughters and his granddaughters—indeed, all his descendants.

8 Here are the names of the Israelites who came to Egypt with Jacob, each accompanied by his family: Reuben, Jacob’s firstborn son.

9 The sons of Reuben were Hanoch, Phallu, Hezron, and Carmi.

10 The sons of Simeon were Jemuel, Jamin, Ohad, Jachin, Zohar, and Shaul, whose mother was a Canaanite woman.

11 The descendants of Levi were Gershon, Kohath, and Merari.

12 The descendants of Judah were Er, Onan, Shelah, Perez, and Zerah. However, Er and Onan died in the land of Canaan. The sons of Perez were Hezron and Hamul.

13 The descendants of Issachar were Tola, Puvah, Jashub, and Shimron.

14 The sons of Zebulun were Sered, Elon, and Jahleel.

15 These are the sons of Leah, whom she bore to Jacob in Paddan-aram, along with his daughter Dinah. The total number of his sons and daughters was thirty-three.

16 The descendants of Gad were Ziphion, Haggi, Shuni, Ezbon, Eri, Arodi, and Areli.

17 The sons of Asher were Jimnah, Ishuah, Isui, Beriah, and their sister Serah. The sons of Beriah were Heber and Malchiel.

18 These are the sons of Zilpah, whom Laban gave to his daughter Leah, and these are the children she bore to Jacob, totaling sixteen individuals.

19 The sons of Rachel, Jacob’s wife, were Joseph and Benjamin.

20 In the land of Egypt, Joseph became the father of two sons, Manasseh and Ephraim, who were born to him by Asenath, the daughter of Potiphera, the priest of On.

21 The descendants of Benjamin were Bela, Becher, Ashbel, Gera, Naaman, Ehi, Rosh, Muppim, Huppim, and Ard.

22 These are the sons of Rachel who were born to Jacob: the total number of his descendants through her was fourteen.

23 The descendants of Dan were Hushim.

24 The sons of Naphtali were Jahzeel, Guni, Jezer, and Shillem.

25 These are the sons of Bilhah, whom Laban gave to his daughter Rachel, and she bore them to Jacob. The total number of these descendants was seven.

26 All the descendants of Jacob who went to Egypt, those who were his direct offspring, not including the wives of his sons, numbered sixty-six in total.

27 The sons of Joseph, who were born to him in Egypt, were two in number. All the descendants of Jacob who came to Egypt totaled seventy persons.

28 Jacob sent Judah ahead of him to Joseph, to prepare the way for their arrival in Goshen. When they arrived, they entered the land of Goshen.

29 Joseph prepared his chariot and went up to Goshen to meet his father, Israel. When he arrived, he presented himself to his father. Joseph embraced him, throwing his arms around his father’s neck, and wept for a long time as he held him.

30 And Israel said to Joseph, "Now I can die in peace, for I have seen your face and know that you are still alive."

31 Joseph said to his brothers and to his father’s household, “I will go and inform Pharaoh, telling him, ‘My brothers and my father’s family, who were in the land of Canaan, have come to me.’”

32 The men are shepherds, as their occupation has always been to tend livestock. They have brought their flocks, their herds, and all that they own.

33 And when Pharaoh summons you and asks, "What do you do for a living?"

34 You should say, "Your servants have been involved in raising livestock from our youth up to the present, both we and our ancestors." This way, you may settle in the land of Goshen, because all shepherds are detestable to the Egyptians.
