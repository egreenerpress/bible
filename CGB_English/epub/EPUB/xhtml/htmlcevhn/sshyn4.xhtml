Psalms Chapter 76

1 (For the director of music, to be accompanied by stringed instruments. A psalm or song of Asaph.) In Judah, God is recognized; his name is highly honored in Israel.

2 In Salem, His sanctuary is established, and His dwelling place is in Zion.

3 He shattered the arrows of the bow, the shield, the sword, and the weapons of war. Selah.

4 You are more majestic and splendid than the mountains filled with bounty.

5 The bold and courageous have been plundered; they have fallen into their final sleep. Not one of the mighty warriors can lift a hand to fight.

6 At your rebuke, O God of Jacob, both the chariot and the horse fall into a deep, lifeless slumber.

7 You, indeed You, are to be revered with awe; who can endure Your presence when Your anger is kindled?

8 You caused your judgment to resound from heaven; the earth trembled in awe and fell silent.

9 When God rose up to execute judgment, to deliver all the humble of the earth. Selah.

10 Certainly, human anger will bring you praise; what remains of that anger, you will restrain.

11 Make a vow and fulfill it to the LORD your God; let all who are around Him bring offerings to the One who is worthy of reverence.

12 He will break the power of rulers; he strikes fear into the kings of the earth.
