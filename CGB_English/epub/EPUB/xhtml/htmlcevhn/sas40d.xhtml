Mark Chapter 9

1 And He said to them, "Truly I tell you, there are some standing here who will not experience death until they see the kingdom of God come in its full power."

2 Six days later, Jesus took Peter, James, and John with him and led them up a high mountain by themselves, away from the others. There, in their presence, he was transformed in appearance.

3 His clothing became radiant, intensely white, like no launderer on earth could ever bleach them.

4 Then Elijah appeared to them, accompanied by Moses, and they were speaking with Jesus.

5 Peter responded to Jesus, saying, "Teacher, it is wonderful for us to be here. Let us build three shelters—one for you, one for Moses, and one for Elijah."

6 For he did not know what to say, because they were utterly terrified.

7 A cloud descended and enveloped them, and from the cloud a voice spoke, saying, "This is my beloved Son; listen to him."

8 And suddenly, when they looked around, they saw no one else anymore, except Jesus alone with them.

9 As they were descending the mountain, Jesus instructed them not to tell anyone about what they had witnessed until the Son of Man had risen from the dead.

10 They kept this statement to themselves, discussing among themselves what it could mean to rise from the dead.

11 They asked him, saying, "Why do the teachers of the law say that Elijah must come first?"

12 He replied to them, "Elijah truly comes first and restores all things. And as it is written concerning the Son of Man, he must endure many sufferings and be treated with contempt."

13 But I tell you that Elijah has indeed come, and they have done to him whatever they pleased, just as it is written about him.

14 When he returned to his disciples, he saw a large crowd gathered around them, and the scribes were arguing with them.

15 Immediately, all the people, when they saw him, were utterly astonished. They ran to him and greeted him with great enthusiasm.

16 And he asked the teachers of the law, "What are you arguing about with them?"

17 Then someone from the crowd spoke up and said, "Teacher, I have brought my son to you, for he is possessed by a spirit that makes him unable to speak."

18 Wherever the spirit seizes him, it throws him into convulsions. He foams at the mouth, grinds his teeth, and becomes rigid. I asked your disciples to drive the spirit out, but they were unable to do so.

19 He responded and said, "O unbelieving generation, how long must I remain with you? How long must I endure you? Bring him to me."

20 They brought the boy to Jesus, and as soon as he saw Him, the evil spirit violently convulsed the boy. He fell to the ground and rolled around, foaming at the mouth.

21 The man asked his father, "How long has this been happening to him?" His father replied, "Since he was a child."

22 Many times it has thrown him into the fire or into the water, trying to kill him. But if you are able to do anything, have pity on us and help us.

23 Jesus said to him, "If you are able to believe, everything is possible for the one who has faith."

24 Immediately the father of the child cried out, and with tears said, “Lord, I believe; help me overcome my unbelief!”

25 When Jesus noticed the crowd gathering quickly, he sternly addressed the unclean spirit, saying, "You mute and deaf spirit, I command you: come out of him and never enter him again."

26 The unclean spirit cried out violently, convulsing the man severely, and then came out of him. The man was left as though he were dead, so much so that many who saw it said, "He is dead."

27 But Jesus grasped him by the hand and raised him up, and he stood on his feet.

28 When Jesus entered the house, his disciples asked him privately, "Why were we unable to drive out the demon?"

29 And He said to them, "This kind cannot be driven out except through prayer and fasting."

30 They left that place and traveled through Galilee, but Jesus did not want anyone to know about it.

31 He instructed his disciples, saying to them, "The Son of Man will be handed over to the power of men, and they will put him to death. But after he has been killed, he will rise again on the third day."

32 But they did not comprehend what he meant and were too afraid to ask him about it.

33 When Jesus arrived in Capernaum and was in the house, he asked his disciples, "What were you arguing about among yourselves on the way here?"

34 But they remained silent, because on the way they had been arguing among themselves about who was the greatest.

35 Jesus sat down, called the twelve disciples to him, and said, "If anyone wants to be first, they must become last of all and a servant to everyone."

36 Then Jesus took a child and placed him in the middle of the group. After picking the child up and holding him in his arms, he said to them,

37 Whoever welcomes one of these little children in my name welcomes me; and whoever welcomes me is not merely welcoming me, but the one who sent me.

38 John responded to Him, saying, "Teacher, we saw someone driving out demons in Your name, and he does not follow us. So we tried to stop him, because he is not one of our group."

39 But Jesus said, "Do not stop him, for no one who performs a miracle in my name will quickly turn around and speak evil of me."

40 For whoever is not opposing us is supporting us.

41 Whoever gives you a cup of water to drink in my name, because you belong to Christ, truly I tell you, that person will certainly not lose their reward.

42 And if anyone causes one of these little ones who believe in me to stumble, it would be better for them to have a large millstone tied around their neck and be thrown into the sea.

43 If your hand causes you to sin, cut it off. It is better for you to enter life disabled than to have two hands and go into hell, into the unquenchable fire.

44 In this place, their consuming decay never ceases, and the fire that consumes them is never extinguished.

45 And if your foot causes you to sin, cut it off. It is better for you to enter life crippled than to have both feet and be thrown into hell, into the unquenchable fire.

46 In this place, their consuming destruction never ceases, and the fire that consumes them is never extinguished.

47 If your eye causes you to sin, tear it out. It is far better for you to enter the kingdom of God with only one eye than to have both eyes and be thrown into the fire of hell.

48 In this place, their consuming decay never ceases, and the fire that consumes them is never extinguished.

49 For everyone will be purified by fire, and every offering will be seasoned with salt.

50 Salt is valuable, but if the salt loses its saltiness, how can you restore its flavor? Keep the essence of salt within yourselves, and live in peace with one another.
