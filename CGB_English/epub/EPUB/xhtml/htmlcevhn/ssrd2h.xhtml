Genesis Chapter 33

1 Jacob raised his eyes and looked, and there was Esau approaching, accompanied by four hundred men. Jacob then arranged the children with Leah, Rachel, and the two servant women, positioning them in groups.

2 He positioned the servant women and their children at the front, followed by Leah and her children, with Rachel and Joseph at the very back.

3 He went ahead of them and bowed down to the ground seven times as he approached his brother.

4 Esau hurried to meet him, embraced him, threw his arms around his neck, and kissed him. Then they both wept.

5 He looked up and saw the women and the children, then asked, "Who are these with you?" He replied, "These are the children whom God has generously given to your servant."

6 Then the female servants approached, along with their children, and they bowed down in reverence.

7 Leah, along with her children, approached and bowed down. Afterward, Joseph and Rachel came forward, and they also bowed down.

8 And he asked, "What is the meaning of all these flocks that I encountered?" Jacob replied, "These are to gain favor in the eyes of my lord."

9 And Esau replied, "I have plenty, my brother; keep what is yours for yourself."

10 Jacob replied, "No, please, if I have found favor in your eyes, accept this gift from me. For seeing your face is like seeing the face of God, since you have received me with such kindness."

11 Please accept the gift I am presenting to you, for God has shown me great kindness, and I have all that I need. He insisted, and so he accepted it.

12 And he said, "Let us set out on our journey and proceed, and I will go ahead of you."

13 He replied to him, "My lord knows that the children are young and delicate, and I have with me the flocks and herds that are nursing their young. If the animals are driven too hard for even a single day, the entire flock will die."

14 Please, my lord, go ahead of your servant. I will proceed at a gentle pace, matching the ability of the livestock before me and the children, until I come to my lord in Seir.

15 And Esau said, "Please let me leave with you some of the people who are with me." But Jacob replied, "Why is that necessary? May I simply find favor in your eyes, my lord."

16 So on that day, Esau went back on his journey toward Seir.

17 Jacob traveled to Succoth, where he built a house for himself and constructed shelters for his livestock. Because of this, the place was named Succoth.

18 Jacob arrived at Shalem, a city in the region of Shechem, located in the land of Canaan, after returning from Paddan-aram. He set up his camp near the city.

20 And he built an altar there and named it "El-Elohe-Israel," which means "God, the God of Israel."
