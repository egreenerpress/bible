Acts Chapter 15

1 Some men who had come from Judea began teaching the believers, saying, "Unless you are circumcised according to the custom of Moses, you cannot be saved."

2 When Paul and Barnabas found themselves in a significant conflict and debate with them, they decided that Paul and Barnabas, along with some others, should go up to Jerusalem to present this matter to the apostles and elders for resolution.

3 The church accompanied them on their journey, and as they traveled through Phoenicia and Samaria, they shared the news of the Gentiles turning to faith. This brought immense joy to all the believers.

4 When they arrived in Jerusalem, they were welcomed by the church, as well as by the apostles and elders. They then recounted everything that God had accomplished through them.

5 However, some from the sect of the Pharisees who had become believers stood up and argued, saying, "It is necessary to circumcise them and to instruct them to observe the law of Moses."

6 The apostles and elders gathered together to deliberate on this matter.

7 After much debate, Peter stood up and addressed them, saying, "Brothers, you are aware that some time ago, God chose me from among you so that the Gentiles might hear the message of the gospel through me and come to believe."

8 And God, who knows the hearts of all, confirmed their acceptance by giving them the Holy Spirit, just as he did to us.

9 And He made no distinction between us and them, cleansing their hearts through faith.

10 So now, why are you testing God by placing a heavy burden on the necks of the disciples—a yoke that neither our ancestors nor we have been able to carry?

11 We are convinced that we will be saved through the gracious favor of the Lord Jesus Christ, just as they are.

12 Then the entire assembly fell silent and listened attentively as Barnabas and Paul recounted the great signs and wonders that God had performed among the Gentiles through their ministry.

13 After they had finished speaking, James responded, saying, "My fellow believers, listen to me."

14 Simeon has explained how God first showed His concern for the Gentiles, to choose from among them a people who would belong to Him and bear His name.

15 The words of the prophets align with this, as it is written:

16 After this, I will return and rebuild the fallen dwelling place of David. I will restore its ruins and raise it up again.

17 So that the remaining people may seek the Lord, and all the Gentiles who are called by my name, says the Lord, who accomplishes all these things.

18 God has known all His works from the very beginning of the world.

19 Therefore, my judgment is that we should not burden those who have turned to God from among the Gentiles.

20 However, we should write to them, instructing them to abstain from the defilement of idols, from sexual immorality, from eating the meat of animals that have been strangled, and from consuming blood.

21 For in every city, Moses has had his teachings proclaimed, as his writings are read aloud in the synagogues every Sabbath day.

22 Then the apostles and elders, along with the entire church, decided to select men from among themselves to send to Antioch with Paul and Barnabas. They chose Judas, also called Barsabbas, and Silas, both of whom were respected leaders among the believers.

23 The apostles, elders, and fellow believers wrote letters to the Gentile believers in Antioch, Syria, and Cilicia, sending them this message: Greetings from the apostles, elders, and your brothers and sisters in faith.

24 Since we have learned that some individuals who left our community have unsettled you with their words, disturbing your faith by insisting that you must be circumcised and follow the law—a requirement we never gave them authority to impose—

25 After careful deliberation and being united in purpose, we have decided to send chosen representatives to you, along with our dear brothers Barnabas and Paul.

26 Men who have risked their lives for the sake of the name of our Lord Jesus Christ.

27 Therefore, we have sent Judas and Silas, who will personally explain these matters to you in detail.

28 For it has been decided by the Holy Spirit and by us not to impose on you any greater obligation than these essential requirements.

29 You must refrain from eating food sacrificed to idols, from consuming blood, from eating meat from animals that were strangled, and from sexual immorality. If you avoid these things, you will be doing what is right. Farewell.

30 After they were sent off, they arrived in Antioch. Once they had assembled the entire community, they presented the letter to them.

31 When they read it, they were filled with joy because of the encouragement it brought.

32 Judas and Silas, who were also prophets, encouraged the believers with many words and strengthened their faith.

33 After they had stayed there for a while, the believers sent them on their way in peace to return to the apostles.

34 However, Silas chose to remain there, as he was content to stay.

35 Paul and Barnabas remained in Antioch, teaching and proclaiming the message of the Lord, along with many others.

36 Some time later, Paul said to Barnabas, "Let us return and visit the believers in every city where we have proclaimed the word of the Lord, to see how they are doing."

37 Barnabas decided to take John, also known as Mark, with them.

38 However, Paul did not consider it wise to take him along, since he had left them in Pamphylia and had not continued with them in the work.

39 The disagreement between them became so intense that they decided to part ways. Barnabas took Mark with him and set sail for Cyprus.

40 Paul selected Silas to accompany him and set out on his journey. The believers commended them to the grace and favor of God as they departed.

41 He traveled through Syria and Cilicia, strengthening and encouraging the churches in their faith.
