Jeremiah Chapter 12

1 You are righteous, O LORD, even when I bring my case before you. Yet I wish to discuss your ways of justice with you: Why do the wicked prosper? Why do those who act so deceitfully live in ease and contentment?

2 You have planted them, and indeed, they have taken root; they grow and even produce fruit. You are close to their lips, but far from their hearts.

3 But you, O LORD, know me completely; you have observed me and tested the sincerity of my heart toward you. Tear them away like sheep destined for slaughter, and set them apart for the day of destruction.

4 How long will the land lie in mourning, and the plants of every field wither away, all because of the wickedness of those who live there? The animals and the birds are dying out, for the people have said, "God will not see what becomes of us in the end."

5 If you have grown weary running alongside footmen, how will you compete against horses? And if you falter in a land of peace, where you felt secure, how will you manage in the raging torrents of the Jordan?

6 Even your own brothers and the members of your fatherâ€™s household have betrayed you; they have gathered a crowd against you. Do not trust them, even if they speak kindly to you.

7 I have abandoned my house, I have left my inheritance; I have handed over the one I deeply love into the power of her enemies.

8 My inheritance has become like a lion in the forest to me; it roars against me, and so I have come to despise it.

9 My inheritance has become to me like a brightly colored bird, surrounded by other birds that are hostile to her. Come, gather all the wild animals of the field; come and devour her.

10 Many shepherds have ruined my vineyard; they have trampled my chosen plot underfoot, turning my delightful inheritance into a barren wasteland.

11 They have turned it into a wasteland, and now, in its desolation, it grieves before me. The entire land lies in ruin, for no one takes it to heart or cares about its condition.

12 Invaders have swept across every high place in the wilderness, for the sword of the LORD will consume everything from one end of the land to the other. No one will find peace or security.

13 They have planted wheat, but will harvest thorns; they have labored hard, but will gain nothing. They will be disgraced by their harvests because of the burning anger of the LORD.

14 This is what the LORD says concerning all my wicked neighbors who encroach upon the inheritance I have given to my people Israel to possess: Listen carefully, I will uproot them from their land, and I will also remove the house of Judah from among them.

15 This is what will happen: After I have uprooted them, I will return and show them compassion. I will bring them back, each person to their inheritance and each one to their land.

16 And it will happen that if they diligently learn the ways of my people, pledging loyalty in my name by saying, 'As surely as the LORD lives,' just as they once taught my people to swear by Baal, then they will be established and thrive among my people.

17 But if they refuse to obey, I will completely uproot and destroy that nation, declares the LORD.
