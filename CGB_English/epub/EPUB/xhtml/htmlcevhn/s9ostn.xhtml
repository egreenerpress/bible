I Chronicles Chapter 11

1 Then all Israel assembled before David at Hebron and declared, "We are your own flesh and blood."

2 In earlier times, even when Saul was king, you were the one who led Israel out to battle and brought them back safely. The LORD your God said to you, "You will shepherd my people Israel, and you will be the leader over my people Israel."

3 So all the elders of Israel came to the king at Hebron, and David established a covenant with them there in the presence of the LORD. Then they anointed David as king over Israel, in fulfillment of the LORD’s word spoken through Samuel.

4 David and all of Israel went to Jerusalem, which was also known as Jebus. At that time, the Jebusites, who were the inhabitants of the land, lived there.

5 The people of Jebus said to David, "You will not enter here." Yet David captured the stronghold of Zion, which became known as the City of David.

6 David declared, "Whoever strikes down the Jebusites first will become the commander and leader." So Joab, the son of Zeruiah, went up first and became the chief.

7 David settled in the stronghold, and for this reason, it became known as the City of David.

8 He constructed the city all around, extending from the Millo outward. Joab was responsible for restoring the remaining sections of the city.

9 So David grew increasingly powerful, for the LORD of Heaven’s Armies was with him.

10 These are also the leaders of the mighty warriors who served David, standing firmly with him to establish his rule over the kingdom, alongside all Israel, to make him king in accordance with the word of the LORD concerning Israel.

11 Here is the count of David’s mighty warriors: Jashobeam, the son of Hachmoni, was the leader of the Thirty. He once raised his spear and struck down three hundred men in a single battle.

12 After him came Eleazar, the son of Dodo, from the clan of Ahoah. He was one of the three mighty warriors.

13 He was with David at Pas-dammim, where the Philistines had assembled for battle. In that place, there was a field full of barley, and the people fled from the Philistines.

14 They positioned themselves in the middle of that plot of land, defended it, and struck down the Philistines. The Lord rescued them with a mighty act of salvation.

15 Three of the thirty leading warriors went down to David at the rock, to the cave of Adullam, while the army of the Philistines was camped in the Valley of Rephaim.

16 At that time, David was in the stronghold, and a Philistine garrison was stationed in Bethlehem.

17 David was deeply thirsty and said, "Oh, if only someone would bring me water from the well of Bethlehem, the one by the gate!"

18 The three warriors broke through the Philistine forces, drew water from the well near the gate of Bethlehem, and brought it back to David. However, David refused to drink it. Instead, he poured it out as an offering to the LORD.

19 And he said, "Far be it from me, my God, to do such a thing! Should I drink the blood of these men who risked their lives? For they brought this water at the risk of their own lives." So he refused to drink it. These were the deeds of the three mightiest warriors.

20 Abishai, the brother of Joab, was the leader of the three mighty warriors. He gained renown among them by raising his spear against three hundred men and killing them, earning a distinguished reputation among the three.

21 Among the three, he was the most distinguished of the two, for he was their leader; yet he did not reach the status of the top three.

22 Benaiah, the son of Jehoiada, was the son of a courageous man from Kabzeel, known for his many heroic deeds. He killed two mighty warriors of Moab, who were as fierce as lions. Additionally, he went down into a pit on a snowy day and killed a lion there.

23 He killed an Egyptian, a man of impressive size, standing five cubits tall. In the Egyptian’s hand was a spear as large as a weaver’s beam. Armed only with a staff, he confronted the Egyptian, wrested the spear from his hand, and killed him with his own weapon.

24 Benaiah, the son of Jehoiada, accomplished these deeds and gained renown among the three mighty warriors.

25 Indeed, he was highly esteemed among the thirty mighty warriors, though he did not reach the status of the top three. David appointed him to be in charge of his personal guard.

26 The mighty warriors among the army's ranks included Asahel, the brother of Joab, and Elhanan, the son of Dodo from Bethlehem.

27 Shammoth from Haror and Helez from Pelon.

28 Ira, the son of Ikkesh from Tekoa, and Abiezer from Anathoth.

29 Sibbecai from Hushah, and Ilai from the clan of Ahoah.

30 Maharai from Netophah, and Heled son of Baanah from Netophah.

31 Ittai, the son of Ribai from Gibeah, who was of the tribe of Benjamin, and Benaiah the Pirathonite.

32 Hurai from the valleys of Gaash, and Abiel from Arbah.

33 Azmaveth from Bahurim, Eliahba from Shaalbon.

34 The sons of Hashem the Gizonite, and Jonathan the son of Shage the Hararite.

35 Ahiam, the son of Sacar the Hararite, and Eliphal, the son of Ur.

36 Hepher from Mecherath and Ahijah from Pelon.

37 Hezro from Carmel, and Naarai, the son of Ezbai.

38 Joel, the brother of Nathan, and Mibhar, the son of Haggeri.

39 Zelek from Ammon, Naharai from Beeroth (who was the armor-bearer of Joab, the son of Zeruiah),

40 Ira the Ithrite and Gareb the Ithrite.

41 Uriah the Hittite, and Zabad the son of Ahlai.

42 Adina, the son of Shiza, a leader from the tribe of Reuben, along with thirty men who were with him...

43 Hanan, the son of Maachah, and Joshaphat the Mithnite.

44 Uzziah the Ashterathite, Shama, and Jehiel, the sons of Hothan the Aroerite.

45 Jediael, the son of Shimri, and his brother Joha, who was from the town of Tiz,

46 Eliel the Mahavite, Jeribai, Joshaviah (the sons of Elnaam), and Ithmah the Moabite.

47 Eliel, Obed, and Jasiel the Mesobaite.
