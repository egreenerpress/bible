Mark Chapter 12

1 He began to teach them using parables. "There was a man who planted a vineyard. He put a fence around it, dug a pit for the winepress, and built a watchtower. Then he leased it to tenant farmers and went away to another country.

2 At the appointed time, he sent a servant to the tenants so that he might collect from them some of the fruit of the vineyard.

3 They seized him, beat him, and sent him away with nothing.

4 Once more, he sent another servant to them, but they threw stones at him, struck him in the head, and sent him away, having treated him with disgrace and dishonor.

5 Once more, he sent another servant, but they killed him as well, along with many others—some they beat, and some they killed.

6 Therefore, having one more son, his beloved, he sent him to them last of all, saying, ‘They will respect my son.’

7 But those tenant farmers said to one another, "This is the heir. Come, let us kill him, and the inheritance will be ours."

8 They seized him, killed him, and threw his body outside the vineyard.

9 What, then, will the owner of the vineyard do? He will come and put those tenants to death, and he will give the vineyard to others.

10 Have you not read this Scripture: "The stone that the builders rejected has become the cornerstone"?

11 This is the work of the Lord, and it is astonishing in our sight.

12 They attempted to seize him, but they were afraid of the crowd, because they understood that he had told the parable against them. So they left him and went away.

13 Then they sent some of the Pharisees and Herodians to him, intending to trap him in his own words.

14 When they arrived, they said to Him, "Teacher, we know that You are truthful and show no partiality to anyone, for You do not consider a person’s status but teach the way of God in accordance with the truth. Tell us, is it right to pay taxes to Caesar, or not?"

15 "Should we pay, or should we not pay?" But Jesus, aware of their deceit, said to them, "Why are you testing me? Bring me a denarius so I can look at it."

16 They brought it to him, and he asked, "Whose image and inscription are on this?" They replied, "Caesar's."

17 Jesus responded to them, saying, "Give to Caesar what belongs to Caesar, and to God what belongs to God." And they were amazed at His answer.

18 Then the Sadducees, who deny the resurrection of the dead, came to him and questioned him, saying,

19 Teacher, Moses instructed us that if a man’s brother dies, leaving a wife but no children, the brother should marry the widow and provide offspring for his deceased brother.

20 There were seven brothers. The first one married a wife, but when he died, he left no children.

21 Then the second brother married her, but he also died without having any children. The same thing happened with the third brother.

22 All seven brothers married her, but none of them had children with her. Finally, the woman also died.

23 In the resurrection, when people rise from the dead, whose wife will she be? For all seven of them were married to her.

24 Jesus responded to them, saying, "Are you not mistaken because you do not understand the Scriptures or the power of God?"

25 For when people rise from the dead, they will neither marry nor be given in marriage; instead, they will be like the angels in heaven.

26 Regarding the dead and their resurrection, have you not read in the book of Moses, in the passage about the bush, how God spoke to him, saying, "I am the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob"?

27 He is not the God of the dead, but the God of the living. Therefore, you are greatly mistaken.

28 One of the scribes approached and, after listening to their discussion and observing how well Jesus had answered them, asked him, "Which commandment is the most important of all?"

29 Jesus replied to him, "The most important commandment is this: 'Listen, Israel, the Lord our God is the one and only Lord.'"

30 You must love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, with all your mind, and with all your strength. This is the foremost commandment.

31 The second commandment is similar: You must love your neighbor as yourself. No other commandment is greater than these.

32 The scribe replied to Him, "Well said, Teacher. You have spoken the truth: there is one God, and there is no other besides Him."

33 To love Him with all your heart, with all your mind, with all your being, and with all your strength, and to love your neighbor as yourself, is far greater than all burnt offerings and sacrifices.

34 When Jesus saw that the man had answered wisely, He said to him, "You are not far from the kingdom of God." After that, no one dared to ask Him any more questions.

35 And Jesus responded, as he was teaching in the temple, "Why do the scribes say that the Messiah is the son of David?"

36 David himself, inspired by the Holy Spirit, declared: "The Lord said to my Lord, 'Sit at my right hand until I make your enemies a footstool under your feet.'"

37 David himself refers to him as 'Lord,' so how can he also be his son?" The crowd listened to him with great interest.

38 And He taught them, saying, "Be on your guard against the scribes, who delight in wearing long robes and crave respectful greetings in the marketplaces.

39 And the most honored seats in the synagogues, and the places of highest honor at banquets.

40 These are the ones who exploit widows by seizing their homes, and then, to cover their actions, they offer lengthy prayers as a show of piety. Such people will face a more severe judgment.

41 Jesus sat down near the temple treasury and observed how the people were putting money into it. Many who were wealthy contributed large amounts.

42 A certain poor widow came and put in two small coins, which together amount to a very small sum.

43 Jesus called his disciples to him and said, "Truly, I tell you, this poor widow has put in more than all the others who have contributed to the treasury."

44 For they all gave out of their surplus, but she, in her poverty, gave everything she had—all she had to live on.
