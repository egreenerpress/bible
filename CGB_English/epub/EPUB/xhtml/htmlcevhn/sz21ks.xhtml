Exodus Chapter 35

1 Moses assembled the entire community of the Israelites and said to them, "These are the instructions that the LORD has commanded you to follow."

2 For six days, work is to be done, but the seventh day shall be a sacred day for you, a Sabbath of complete rest dedicated to the LORD. Anyone who does any work on that day must be put to death.

3 You must not light any fire in your dwellings on the Sabbath day.

4 Moses addressed the entire assembly of the Israelites, saying, "This is what the LORD has commanded:

5 Gather from among yourselves an offering to present to the LORD. Let everyone whose heart is willing bring this offering to the LORD: gold, silver, and bronze.

6 And blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, along with fine linen and goats' hair.

7 And rams' skins dyed red, and fine leather, and acacia wood.

8 Provide oil for the lamps, spices for the sacred anointing oil, and for the fragrant incense.

9 And onyx stones, as well as gemstones to be mounted on the ephod and the breastplate.

10 And all those among you who are skilled and wise in heart shall come forward and create everything that the LORD has commanded.

11 The tabernacle, its tent and its covering, its clasps, its frames, its crossbars, its posts, and its bases.

12 The ark, along with its carrying poles, the atonement cover, and the curtain that serves as a partition.

13 The table, along with its carrying poles, all its utensils, and the bread of the Presence.

14 The lampstand for providing light, along with its accessories, its lamps, and the oil needed for the light.

15 The altar for burning incense, along with its carrying poles, the anointing oil, the fragrant incense, and the curtain for the entrance to the tabernacle—

16 The altar for burnt offerings, along with its bronze grating, its carrying poles, and all its utensils, the basin and its stand.

17 The curtains surrounding the courtyard, their supporting pillars, the bases for those pillars, and the screen for the entrance to the courtyard.

18 The tent pegs of the tabernacle, along with the pegs for the surrounding courtyard and their connecting cords.

19 The finely crafted garments for performing sacred duties in the holy place, including the holy vestments for Aaron the priest and the garments for his sons, to serve in their priestly roles.

20 Then the entire assembly of the Israelites left Moses' presence.

21 And they came—everyone whose heart moved them, and everyone whose spirit prompted them willingly—bringing offerings to the Lord for the work of the tabernacle of meeting, for all its service, and for the sacred garments.

22 Both men and women came, all who were willing and eager, bringing bracelets, earrings, rings, and pendants—all kinds of gold jewelry. Every person who presented an offering brought a gift of gold to the LORD.

23 Every person who possessed blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, fine linen, goats' hair, rams' skins dyed red, or badgers' skins brought them as contributions.

24 Everyone who contributed an offering of silver or bronze brought it as an offering to the LORD. Likewise, every person who had acacia wood suitable for any part of the work of service brought it as well.

25 All the women who were skilled and wise in their craft spun with their own hands, and they brought what they had spun: blue, purple, and scarlet yarn, as well as fine linen.

26 And all the women whose hearts were moved with skill and understanding spun the goats' hair.

27 The leaders brought onyx stones and other precious stones to be mounted on the ephod and the breastpiece.

28 And spices, and oil for the lamps, and for the anointing oil, and for the fragrant incense.

29 The Israelites brought a voluntary offering to the LORD—every man and woman whose hearts moved them to contribute for all the tasks that the LORD had commanded to be done through Moses.

30 Moses said to the Israelites, "Take note, the LORD has specifically chosen Bezalel, the son of Uri, the son of Hur, from the tribe of Judah."

31 And God has filled him with His Spirit, granting him wisdom, understanding, knowledge, and skill in every kind of craftsmanship.

32 And to design intricate works, crafting in gold, silver, and bronze.

33 And in the cutting of stones for setting, and in the carving of wood, to craft any kind of skilled work.

34 And He has given him the ability to teach, both him and Oholiab, the son of Ahisamach, from the tribe of Dan.

35 He has filled them with skill and understanding to carry out every kind of craftsmanship: the work of engravers, skilled artisans, and embroiderers in blue, purple, scarlet, and fine linen, as well as weavers—all those who perform any skilled work or design intricate patterns.
