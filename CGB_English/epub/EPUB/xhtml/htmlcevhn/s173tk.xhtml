Acts Chapter 4

1 As they were speaking to the people, the priests, the captain of the temple guard, and the Sadducees confronted them.

2 They were deeply troubled because the apostles were teaching the people and proclaiming in Jesus the resurrection of the dead.

3 They seized them and placed them in custody until the next day, for it was already evening.

4 Nevertheless, many of those who heard the message believed, and the number of men who came to faith was about five thousand.

5 The next day, their leaders, elders, and teachers of the law gathered together.

6 Annas the high priest, along with Caiaphas, John, Alexander, and all who were of the high priest’s family, assembled together in Jerusalem.

7 When they had brought them before the council, they questioned them, saying, "By what authority or in whose name have you done this?"

8 Then Peter, empowered by the Holy Spirit, said to them, "You leaders of the people and elders of Israel, ..."

9 If we are being questioned today about the good deed done to the disabled man, and by what power he has been healed,

10 Let it be clearly understood by all of you and by all the people of Israel that this man stands before you healed and whole by the power of Jesus Christ of Nazareth—the one you crucified, whom God raised from the dead. It is through Him that this healing has taken place.

11 This is the stone that you, the builders, rejected, but it has now become the cornerstone.

12 Salvation is found in no one else, for there is no other name under heaven given to humanity by which we must be saved.

13 When the religious leaders observed the confidence and boldness of Peter and John, and realized that they were ordinary, uneducated men, they were astonished. They recognized that these men had been with Jesus, and this connection was evident in their words and actions.

14 When they saw the man who had been healed standing there with them, they had no way to deny or oppose what had happened.

15 But after they ordered them to step outside the council chamber, they began to deliberate among themselves.

16 They were saying, "What should we do with these men? It is clear to everyone living in Jerusalem that a remarkable miracle has been performed through them, and we cannot deny it."

17 To prevent this teaching from spreading further among the people, we must sternly warn them not to speak to anyone in this name again.

18 They summoned them and ordered them not to speak or teach at all in the name of Jesus.

19 Peter and John responded, saying to them, "You must decide whether it is right in God’s eyes to listen to you rather than to God."

20 We are compelled to speak about what we have witnessed and experienced.

21 After they had issued further threats, they released them, finding no grounds to punish them because of the people, for everyone was praising God for what had taken place.

22 The man was over forty years old when this miraculous healing was performed for him.

23 After being released, they went to their fellow believers and reported everything that the chief priests and elders had said to them.

24 When they heard this, they all joined together in prayer, lifting their voices to God and saying, "Lord, you are the God who created heaven, earth, the sea, and everything in them."

25 You spoke through your servant David, saying, "Why did the nations rage and the peoples plot in vain?"

26 The rulers of the earth rose up, and the leaders assembled together in opposition to the Lord and against His Anointed One.

27 Indeed, against your holy servant Jesus, whom you anointed, both Herod and Pontius Pilate, along with the Gentiles and the people of Israel, came together in opposition.

28 To carry out everything that your hand and your purpose had predetermined should take place.

29 And now, Lord, take notice of their threats, and enable your servants to speak your word with complete confidence and courage.

30 By extending your hand to bring healing, and so that signs and wonders may be performed through the name of your holy servant Jesus.

31 After they had prayed, the place where they were gathered together was shaken, and they were all filled with the Holy Spirit. Then they began to speak the word of God with great courage and confidence.

32 The entire group of believers was united in heart and mind. No one claimed that any of their possessions belonged to them personally, but they shared everything they had in common.

33 The apostles bore powerful testimony to the resurrection of the Lord Jesus, and abundant grace rested upon all of them.

34 No one among them was in need, for those who owned lands or houses sold them and brought the proceeds from what was sold.

35 They placed the proceeds at the apostles' feet, and the resources were distributed to each person according to their specific needs.

36 And Joses, whom the apostles called Barnabas (which means "Son of Encouragement"), a Levite from the region of Cyprus,

37 After selling a piece of land, he brought the money and placed it at the feet of the apostles.
