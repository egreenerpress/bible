Psalms Chapter 94

1 O LORD God, to whom vengeance belongs; O God, to whom justice is due, reveal yourself.

2 Rise up, O Judge of the earth; repay the arrogant what they deserve.

3 LORD, how much longer will the wicked prosper? How much longer will they celebrate their success?

4 How long will they continue to speak harsh and arrogant words, while all who do evil boast in their wickedness?

5 O LORD, they crush your people and oppress those who belong to you.

6 They kill the widow and the foreigner, and take the life of the orphan.

7 Yet they say, "The LORD does not see, nor does the God of Jacob pay any attention."

8 Listen, you senseless ones among the people—you who lack understanding! How long will you remain foolish? When will you finally gain wisdom?

9 The one who created the ear, does he not hear? The one who fashioned the eye, does he not see?

10 The one who disciplines the nations—should he not also correct? The one who imparts knowledge to humanity—should he not also understand?

11 The LORD understands the thoughts of humanity, recognizing that they are ultimately futile.

12 Blessed is the one whom you discipline, O LORD, and instruct through your law.

13 So that you may grant him relief during times of trouble, until a pit is prepared for the wicked.

14 For the LORD will not reject His people, nor will He abandon His chosen possession.

15 However, justice will once again align with what is right, and all those who are sincere and upright in heart will pursue it.

16 Who will take a stand with me against those who do evil? Who will stand by my side to oppose those who practice wickedness?

17 If the LORD had not been my help, my soul would have nearly settled in silence.

18 When I cried out, "I am slipping!" your steadfast love, O LORD, supported me and kept me from falling.

19 Amid the countless thoughts that fill my mind, your consolations bring joy and peace to my soul.

20 Can a throne built on wickedness be in alliance with you—one that enacts injustice under the guise of law?

21 They conspire together against the lives of the righteous and unjustly condemn the innocent to death.

22 But the LORD is my protector; my God is the solid foundation of my safety.

23 He will bring upon them the consequences of their own wrongdoing and will destroy them because of their wickedness. Indeed, the LORD our God will utterly destroy them.
