Genesis Chapter 49

1 Jacob summoned his sons and said, "Come together, so I can tell you what will happen to you in the days to come."

2 Come together and listen, you descendants of Jacob; pay attention to Israel, your father.

3 Reuben, you are my firstborn, my strength, and the first sign of my vigor. You hold the highest honor and the greatest power.

4 You are as unstable as water, and you will not excel, for you went up to your father’s bed and defiled it—he climbed onto my couch.

5 Simeon and Levi are brothers; their weapons are tools of violence.

6 O my soul, do not join in their hidden plans; do not unite with their gathering, my honor. For in their rage they killed a man, and in their arrogance they tore down a wall.

7 May their anger be cursed, for it was intense, and their fury, for it was harsh. I will disperse them among the tribes of Jacob and scatter them throughout Israel.

8 Judah, you are the one your brothers will honor; your hand will grasp the necks of your enemies; your father’s sons will bow down before you.

9 Judah is like a young lion; my son, you have returned from the hunt. He crouches and lies down like a lion, like a mighty lion—who dares to disturb him?

10 The ruling authority will not depart from Judah, nor the staff of leadership from between his feet, until the one to whom it rightfully belongs comes; and to him will the obedience of the peoples be given.

11 He ties his young donkey to the vine, and his colt to the choicest vine. He washes his robes in wine, and his garments in the juice of grapes.

12 His eyes will be glazed from wine, and his teeth will gleam white from milk.

13 Zebulun will settle by the shore of the sea; he will become a safe harbor for ships, and his territory will extend as far as Sidon.

14 Issachar is like a sturdy donkey, lying down between two loads.

15 He recognized that rest was desirable and that the land was delightful; so he submitted himself to labor, bearing the burden, and became a servant, paying tribute.

16 Dan will govern his people, standing as one of the tribes of Israel.

17 Dan will be like a snake by the roadside, a viper along the path, that bites the horse’s heels, causing its rider to tumble backward.

18 I have waited for your deliverance, O LORD.

19 Gad will be attacked by raiding forces, but in the end, he will triumph over them.

20 From the tribe of Asher, his land will produce abundant food, and he will provide delicacies fit for a king.

21 Naphtali is like a swift deer set free; he speaks with eloquence and grace.

22 Joseph is like a thriving tree, a flourishing tree by a spring of water, whose branches extend over the wall.

23 The archers bitterly attacked him, shot their arrows at him, and treated him with hostility.

24 But his bow remained steady, and his arms grew strong through the power of the Mighty One of Jacob—from there comes the Shepherd, the Rock of Israel.

25 By the God of your father, who will support you, and by the Almighty, who will bless you with blessings from the heavens above, blessings from the depths beneath, blessings of fertility and the womb.

26 The blessings of your father surpass the blessings of my ancestors, extending to the farthest reaches of the eternal hills. These blessings will rest upon the head of Joseph, upon the brow of the one who was set apart from his brothers.

27 Benjamin will be like a ravenous wolf: in the morning he will consume his prey, and in the evening he will distribute the plunder.

28 These are the twelve tribes of Israel, and this is what their father said to them when he blessed them. He gave each one a blessing tailored to their unique destiny.

29 Then he gave them these instructions, saying, "I am about to join my ancestors in death. Bury me with my fathers in the cave that is in the field of Ephron the Hittite."

30 In the cave located in the field of Machpelah, near Mamre, in the land of Canaan—the field that Abraham purchased from Ephron the Hittite as a burial site for his possession.

31 There they laid Abraham and his wife Sarah to rest; there they also laid Isaac and his wife Rebekah to rest; and there I buried Leah.

32 The acquisition of the field and the cave within it was made from the descendants of Heth.

33 When Jacob had finished giving his final instructions to his sons, he drew his feet up onto the bed, breathed his last, and passed away, joining his ancestors in death.
