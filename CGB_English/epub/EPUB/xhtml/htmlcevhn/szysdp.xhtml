Ruth Chapter 1

1 During the time when the judges governed, a severe famine struck the land. A man from Bethlehem in Judah, along with his wife and their two sons, decided to leave and settle temporarily in the region of Moab.

2 The man’s name was Elimelech, and his wife’s name was Naomi. His two sons were named Mahlon and Chilion. They were Ephrathites from Bethlehem in Judah. They traveled to the land of Moab and settled there.

3 Elimelech, Naomi’s husband, died, leaving her alone with her two sons.

4 They married women from Moab; one was named Orpah, and the other was named Ruth. They lived there for about ten years.

5 Mahlon and Chilion both died, and the woman was left without her two sons and her husband.

6 Then Naomi got up, along with her daughters-in-law, to leave the land of Moab and return to her homeland. She had heard while in Moab that the LORD had shown kindness to His people by providing them with food.

7 Therefore, she left the place where she had been staying, accompanied by her two daughters-in-law, and they set out on the journey to return to the land of Judah.

8 Naomi said to her two daughters-in-law, "Go, each of you return to your mother’s home. May the LORD show you the same kindness you have shown to the dead and to me."

9 May the LORD enable each of you to find security and peace in the home of your husband. Then she kissed them, and they wept aloud, their voices rising together in sorrow.

10 They replied to her, "We will certainly go back with you to your people."

11 Naomi said, "Go back, my daughters. Why would you come with me? Do I still have sons in my womb who could become your husbands?

12 Return home, my daughters; go your way, for I am too old to have a husband. Even if I were to say, "I have hope," and if I were to have a husband tonight and even bear sons—

13 Would you wait for them until they are grown? Would you refrain from marrying others for their sake? No, my daughters, for I am deeply grieved on your behalf, as the hand of the LORD has turned against me.

14 Then they raised their voices and wept once more. Orpah kissed her mother-in-law goodbye, but Ruth clung to her.

15 And she said, "Look, your sister-in-law has returned to her people and to her gods. You should go back and follow your sister-in-law."

16 Ruth replied, "Do not urge me to leave you or to turn back from following you. Wherever you go, I will go, and wherever you stay, I will stay. Your people will be my people, and your God will be my God."

17 Where you die, I will die, and there I will be buried. May the LORD punish me severely, and even more, if anything but death separates you and me.

18 When she realized that Ruth was determined to go with her, she stopped trying to persuade her otherwise.

19 So the two of them traveled until they reached Bethlehem. When they arrived in Bethlehem, the entire town was stirred because of them, and the people exclaimed, "Is this really Naomi?"

20 She said to them, "Do not call me Naomi; call me Mara, for the Almighty has dealt with me in great bitterness."

21 I left with abundance, but the LORD has brought me back empty. Why should you call me Naomi, when the LORD has spoken against me, and the Almighty has brought me hardship?

22 So Naomi returned, accompanied by Ruth the Moabite, her daughter-in-law, who had come back with her from the land of Moab. They arrived in Bethlehem at the start of the barley harvest.
