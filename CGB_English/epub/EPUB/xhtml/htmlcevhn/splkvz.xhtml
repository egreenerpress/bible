Galatians Chapter 5

1 Therefore, stand firm in the freedom that Christ has given us, and do not allow yourselves to be burdened once more by the yoke of slavery.

2 Listen, I, Paul, declare to you that if you choose to undergo circumcision, Christ will be of no benefit to you.

3 I solemnly declare once more to every man who undergoes circumcision that he is obligated to obey the entire law.

4 Christ has no value for you, whoever among you seeks to be justified by the law; you have turned away from grace.

5 For by the Spirit, we eagerly await the hope of righteousness that comes through faith.

6 In Jesus Christ, neither being circumcised nor being uncircumcised has any value; what truly matters is faith that expresses itself through love.

7 You were making good progress in your faith; who has caused you to turn aside and stop following the truth?

8 This influence does not originate from the one who calls you.

9 A small amount of leaven permeates and affects the entire batch of dough.

10 I am confident in the Lord concerning you, that you will not think differently. However, whoever is causing you trouble will bear the consequences of their actions, no matter who they are.

11 And as for me, my brothers and sisters, if I were still preaching the necessity of circumcision, why would I still face persecution? If that were the case, then the offense of the cross would no longer exist.

12 I wish that those who are causing you trouble would be completely removed.

13 My fellow believers, you have been called to live in freedom; however, do not use this freedom as an opportunity to indulge your selfish desires. Instead, through love, serve one another.

14 The entire law is summed up in a single command: "You must love your neighbor as yourself."

15 But if you attack and harm one another, be careful, or you will end up destroying each other.

16 This is what I say: Live according to the guidance of the Spirit, and you will not carry out the desires of your sinful nature.

17 For the desires of the flesh oppose the Spirit, and the Spirit opposes the desires of the flesh; these are in conflict with each other, so that you are unable to do what you truly desire.

18 But if you are guided by the Spirit, you are not subject to the law.

19 The actions that stem from sinful human nature are evident, and they include the following: sexual immorality, impurity, indecent behavior, and unrestrained lust.

20 Idolatry, sorcery, hostility, discord, jealousy, anger, quarrels, divisions, factions, and false teachings.

21 Jealousy, murders, drunkenness, wild parties, and similar behaviorsâ€”concerning these things, I warn you now, as I have warned you before, that those who practice such things will not inherit the kingdom of God.

22 The result of the Spirit's work in a person's life is evident in these qualities: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control. These virtues reflect the character and influence of the Spirit within those who follow God.

23 Gentleness and self-control: against such qualities, there is no law.

24 Those who belong to Christ have put to death their sinful nature, along with its passions and desires.

25 If we live by the guidance of the Spirit, let us also conduct our lives in step with the Spirit.

26 Let us not seek empty praise or boastful pride, stirring up conflict with one another or harboring jealousy toward each other.
