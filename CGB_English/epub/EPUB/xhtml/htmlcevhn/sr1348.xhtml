Joshua Chapter 11

1 When Jabin, the king of Hazor, heard about these events, he sent messengers to Jobab, the king of Madon, as well as to the kings of Shimron and Achshaph.

2 And to the kings who ruled in the northern hill country, in the lowlands south of Chinneroth, in the valley, and in the regions of Dor to the west,

3 And to the Canaanites in the east and the west, and to the Amorites, the Hittites, the Perizzites, and the Jebusites in the hill country, as well as to the Hivites who dwell below Mount Hermon in the region of Mizpah.

4 They set out, along with all their armies—a vast multitude, as numerous as the grains of sand on the seashore—accompanied by a great number of horses and chariots.

5 When all these kings had gathered together, they came and camped near the waters of Merom to wage war against Israel.

6 The LORD said to Joshua, "Do not be afraid of them, for by this time tomorrow I will hand them over to you, all of them slain before Israel. You are to hamstring their horses and burn their chariots with fire."

7 So Joshua advanced, along with all the warriors under his command, and they launched a surprise attack against them by the waters of Merom. They swiftly descended upon them and struck them down.

8 The LORD gave them over into the hands of Israel, who struck them down and pursued them as far as Greater Sidon, Misrephoth-maim, and the valley of Mizpeh to the east. They defeated them completely, leaving no survivors.

9 Joshua carried out the command of the LORD exactly as he had been instructed: he hamstrung their horses and burned their chariots with fire.

10 At that time, Joshua turned back and captured Hazor, striking down its king with the sword. For Hazor had formerly been the leading city of all those kingdoms.

11 They struck down every person in the city with the sword, completely destroying them. No one was left alive. Then they set Hazor on fire and burned it to the ground.

12 Joshua captured all the cities of those kings and their rulers. He struck them down with the sword and completely destroyed them, just as Moses, the servant of the LORD, had commanded.

13 However, concerning the cities that remained strong and fortified, Israel did not burn any of them, except for Hazor alone; Joshua was the one who burned it.

14 The Israelites took all the plunder from these cities, including the livestock, as their own. However, they struck down every person with the sword, leaving no one alive. They completely destroyed them, sparing no one who could breathe.

15 Just as the LORD had instructed Moses, his servant, Moses in turn directed Joshua, and Joshua carried out everything exactly as commanded. He did not neglect any part of what the LORD had instructed through Moses.

16 So Joshua conquered the entire region—the hill country, the entire southern territory, all the land of Goshen, the western foothills, the Jordan Valley, the mountains of Israel, and their surrounding lowlands.

17 From Mount Halak, which rises toward Seir, all the way to Baal-gad in the Valley of Lebanon below Mount Hermon, he captured all their kings, struck them down, and put them to death.

18 Joshua waged war for an extended period against all those kings.

19 No city made peace with the Israelites except the Hivites, who lived in Gibeon. All the others were conquered through battle.

20 For it was the LORD’s purpose to harden their hearts, so that they would engage Israel in battle, in order to completely destroy them without showing them any mercy. This was done just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

21 At that time, Joshua came and completely eliminated the Anakim from the hill country—from Hebron, Debir, Anab, and all the highlands of Judah and Israel. Joshua utterly destroyed them, along with their cities.

22 In the land belonging to the Israelites, none of the Anakim remained, except in Gaza, Gath, and Ashdod, where some still stayed.

23 Joshua conquered the entire land, just as the LORD had promised Moses. He then distributed it as an inheritance to Israel, assigning portions according to their tribal divisions. After this, the land experienced peace, free from the turmoil of war.
