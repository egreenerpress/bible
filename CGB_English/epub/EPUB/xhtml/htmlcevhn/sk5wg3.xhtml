I Chronicles Chapter 15

1 David built houses for himself in the city of David. He also prepared a place for the ark of God and set up a tent to house it.

2 Then David declared, "No one should carry the ark of God except the Levites, for the Lord has chosen them to carry the ark of God and to serve Him forever."

3 David assembled all of Israel in Jerusalem to bring the ark of the LORD to the place he had prepared for it.

4 David gathered together the descendants of Aaron and the Levites.

5 From the descendants of Kohath: Uriel was the leader, and his relatives numbered one hundred and twenty.

6 From the descendants of Merari, Asaiah was the leader, along with his relatives, numbering two hundred and twenty.

7 From the descendants of Gershom: Joel was the leader, and his relatives numbered one hundred and thirty.

8 From the descendants of Elizaphan, Shemaiah was the leader, and his relatives numbered two hundred.

9 From the descendants of Hebron: Eliel was the leader, and his relatives numbered eighty.

10 From the descendants of Uzziel, Amminadab was the leader, and his relatives numbered one hundred and twelve.

11 David summoned Zadok and Abiathar, the priests, along with the Levites—Uriel, Asaiah, Joel, Shemaiah, Eliel, and Amminadab.

12 He said to them, "You are the leaders of the Levite families. Consecrate yourselves, both you and your fellow Levites, so that you may bring up the ark of the LORD, the God of Israel, to the place I have prepared for it."

13 Because you did not follow the proper procedures from the beginning, the LORD our God brought judgment upon us, for we did not seek Him in the manner He required.

14 So the priests and the Levites purified themselves in order to carry the ark of the LORD, the God of Israel.

15 The Levites carried the ark of God on their shoulders using the poles, just as Moses had commanded in accordance with the instruction of the LORD.

16 David instructed the leaders of the Levites to assign their fellow Levites as musicians, to play instruments of music—harps, lyres, and cymbals—raising their voices joyfully in song.

17 So the Levites designated Heman, the son of Joel; and from his relatives, Asaph, the son of Berechiah; and from the sons of Merari, their relatives, Ethan, the son of Kushaiah.

18 Along with them were their fellow workers of the second rank: Zechariah, Ben, Jaaziel, Shemiramoth, Jehiel, Unni, Eliab, Benaiah, Maaseiah, Mattithiah, Elipheleh, Mikneiah, Obed-edom, and Jeiel, who served as gatekeepers.

19 So the singers—Heman, Asaph, and Ethan—were assigned to play bronze cymbals.

20 Zechariah, Aziel, Shemiramoth, Jehiel, Unni, Eliab, Maaseiah, and Benaiah were to play the psalteries, tuned to the high-pitched Alamoth style.

21 And Mattithiah, Elipheleh, Mikneiah, Obed-edom, Jeiel, and Azaziah were appointed to play harps, using the Sheminith tuning, to lead with skill and excellence.

22 Chenaniah, the leader of the Levites, was in charge of the music. He provided instruction in singing because he was highly skilled.

23 And Berechiah and Elkanah served as guardians of the ark.

24 Shebaniah, Jehoshaphat, Nethaneel, Amasai, Zechariah, Benaiah, and Eliezer, the priests, sounded the trumpets before the ark of God. Obed-edom and Jehiah were stationed as gatekeepers for the ark.

25 So David, along with the elders of Israel and the commanders of thousands, went to bring the ark of the covenant of the LORD from the house of Obed-edom with great rejoicing.

26 When God provided assistance to the Levites who were carrying the ark of the LORD’s covenant, they sacrificed seven bulls and seven rams.

27 David was dressed in a robe made of fine linen, as were all the Levites who carried the ark, the singers, and Chenaniah, the leader of the music, along with the singers. David also wore a linen ephod.

28 So all of Israel carried up the ark of the covenant of the LORD with loud rejoicing, accompanied by the sound of horns, trumpets, and cymbals, and with music from stringed instruments like harps and lyres.

29 When the ark of the covenant of the LORD arrived at the city of David, Michal, the daughter of Saul, looked out of a window and saw King David leaping and dancing with joy. And in her heart, she held him in contempt.
