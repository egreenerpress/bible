I Corinthians Chapter 5

1 It is widely reported that there is sexual immorality among you, and of a kind that is not even tolerated among the Gentiles—specifically, that a man is living with his father’s wife.

2 You are filled with pride, instead of grieving, so that the one who committed this act might be removed from your midst.

3 For I, though physically absent, am present with you in spirit, and I have already passed judgment, as if I were there with you, on the one who has committed this act.

4 In the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, when you are assembled together, and my spirit is present with you, by the authority and power of our Lord Jesus Christ,

5 To hand such a person over to Satan for the destruction of their sinful nature, so that their spirit may be preserved and saved on the day of the Lord Jesus.

6 Your boasting is not commendable. Are you not aware that even a small amount of leaven permeates the entire batch of dough?

7 Therefore, remove the old leaven of sin, so that you may become a fresh, new batch, just as you are meant to be free from corruption. For Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed on our behalf.

8 Therefore, let us celebrate the feast, not with the old yeast of sin, nor with the yeast of malice and evil, but with the unleavened bread of purity and truth.

9 I wrote to you in a letter not to associate with those who engage in sexual immorality.

10 However, I was not referring to associating with the sexually immoral people of this world, or the greedy, or swindlers, or idolaters—for then you would have to completely withdraw from the world.

11 Now, I have written to you instructing that you should not associate with anyone who claims to be a fellow believer but is sexually immoral, greedy, an idolater, verbally abusive, a drunkard, or a swindler. Do not even share a meal with such a person.

12 Why should I be responsible for judging those outside the church? Isn’t it your role to judge those within the church?

13 But those who are outside the faith, God will judge. Therefore, remove the wicked person from among your community.
