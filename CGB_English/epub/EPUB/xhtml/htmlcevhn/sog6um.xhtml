Job Chapter 34

1 Then Elihu continued and said,

2 Listen to my words, you who are wise; pay attention to me, you who possess understanding.

3 The ear tests words, just as the mouth tastes food.

4 Let us carefully consider what is just and right; let us discern together what is truly good.

5 Job has declared, "I am innocent," yet God has denied me justice.

6 Should I falsely deny my innocence? My suffering is severe, though I have done no wrong.

7 Who is like Job, who drinks up mockery as if it were water?

8 Who associates with those who do evil and joins in the company of the wicked.

9 For he has declared, "It does not benefit a person at all to take pleasure in God."

10 Therefore, listen to me, you who have understanding: it is unthinkable that God would act wickedly, or that the Almighty would do wrong.

11 God will repay each person according to their actions and ensure that everyone receives what corresponds to their conduct.

12 Indeed, God would never act wickedly, nor would the Almighty ever distort justice.

13 Who has appointed Him to govern the earth? Or who has entrusted Him with the ordering of the entire world?

14 If God were to fix His attention on humanity, if He were to withdraw His spirit and breath from them;

15 All humanity will perish together, and people will return to dust.

16 If you now possess understanding, listen carefully to this; pay close attention to the meaning of my words.

17 Should someone who despises justice be allowed to rule? And would you condemn the one who is perfectly righteous?

18 Is it appropriate to say to a king, "You are wicked," or to rulers, "You are unrighteous"?

19 How much less should this apply to God, who shows no partiality toward rulers and does not favor the wealthy over the poor? For all of them are the work of His hands.

20 In an instant they will perish, and the people will be struck with terror at midnight, then vanish. The powerful will be removed, not by human hands.

21 For God’s eyes are fixed on the ways of humanity, and He observes every step they take.

22 There is no place of darkness, nor any shadow of death, where those who practice evil can conceal themselves.

23 For God will not impose on anyone more than what is just; no one can contend with Him in judgment.

24 He will shatter powerful men beyond counting and appoint others to take their place.

25 Therefore, He is fully aware of their deeds, and in the night He overturns them, so that they are utterly ruined.

26 He strikes them down as wicked people, openly in the sight of others.

27 Because they turned away from Him and refused to reflect on any of His ways.

28 As a result, they cause the cries of the poor to reach Him, and He listens to the pleas of those who suffer.

29 When God grants peace, who can bring turmoil? And when He withdraws His presence, who can perceive Him? This applies whether it concerns an entire nation or just a single individual.

30 May the hypocrite not rule, so that the people are not trapped.

31 It is indeed right to say to God, "I have endured your discipline; I will not continue to act wrongly."

32 Teach me what I do not understand; if I have done wrong, I will not do it again.

33 Should it be according to your own judgment? He will repay it, whether you reject it or accept it—not I. So declare what you know.

34 Let those who have insight speak to me, and let a person of wisdom listen to what I say.

35 Job has spoken without understanding, and his words lacked true wisdom.

36 My wish is that Job be thoroughly tested to the very end because of his responses, which align him with the ways of the wicked.

37 For he adds rebellion to his sin; he defiantly claps his hands in our midst and endlessly speaks against God.
