Genesis Chapter 16

1 Now Sarai, Abram's wife, had not borne him any children. She had an Egyptian servant named Hagar.

2 Sarai said to Abram, "The LORD has prevented me from bearing children. Please, go in to my servant; perhaps I can build a family through her." And Abram listened to Sarai's request.

3 After Abram had lived in the land of Canaan for ten years, Sarai, Abram’s wife, took Hagar, her Egyptian servant, and gave her to her husband Abram to be his wife.

4 So he went in to Hagar, and she became pregnant. When she realized she was pregnant, she began to look down on her mistress with contempt.

5 Sarai said to Abram, "The wrong done to me is your responsibility. I gave my servant into your embrace, and when she saw that she had conceived, I became despised in her eyes. May the LORD judge between you and me."

6 But Abram said to Sarai, "Look, your servant is under your authority; do with her as you see fit." When Sarai treated her harshly, Hagar fled from her presence.

7 The angel of the LORD came upon her by a spring of water in the wilderness, near the spring on the road to Shur.

8 And he said, "Hagar, Sarai’s servant, where have you come from, and where are you going?" She replied, "I am running away from my mistress Sarai."

9 The angel of the LORD said to her, "Go back to your mistress and place yourself under her authority."

10 The angel of the LORD said to her, "I will greatly increase your descendants so that they will be too numerous to count."

11 The angel of the LORD said to her, "Listen, you are pregnant and will give birth to a son. You are to name him Ishmael, for the LORD has heard about your suffering."

12 He will be a fierce and untamed man; his hand will be raised against everyone, and everyone’s hand will be raised against him. Yet he will live in defiance, dwelling in the presence of all his relatives.

13 She named the LORD who had spoken to her, "You are the God who sees me," for she said, "Have I truly seen the One who watches over me, even here?"

14 Therefore, the well was named Beer-lahai-roi, which means "Well of the Living One who sees me." It is located between Kadesh and Bered.

15 Hagar gave birth to a son for Abram, and Abram named the son whom Hagar bore Ishmael.

16 Abram was eighty-six years old when Hagar gave birth to Ishmael for Abram.
