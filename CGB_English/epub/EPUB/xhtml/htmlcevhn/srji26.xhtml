Habakkuk Chapter 1

1 The message that the prophet Habakkuk received in a vision.

2 O LORD, how long must I cry out to you, and you do not listen? I call out to you about the violence, yet you do not intervene to save!

3 Why do you make me witness wrongdoing and force me to look upon suffering? Destruction and violence are all around me; there are those who stir up conflict and strife.

4 As a result, the law has become ineffective, and justice never prevails. The wicked surround the righteous, and so corrupt judgments are handed down.

5 Look among the nations and observe; be utterly amazed! For I am about to do something in your days that you would not believe, even if you were told.

6 For behold, I am raising up the Chaldeans, that fierce and impetuous nation, who will march across the breadth of the earth to seize dwelling places that do not belong to them.

7 They are fearsome and awe-inspiring; their authority and honor come from their own nature and power.

8 Their horses are faster than leopards and more ferocious than wolves at dusk. Their cavalry will spread out widely, and their riders will come from distant lands. They will swoop down swiftly, like eagles eager to devour their prey.

9 They come with nothing but violence in their hearts; their faces are set like a scorching east wind, and they gather captives as numerous as the sand.

10 They will mock kings and ridicule rulers; they will scorn every fortified stronghold. They will pile up dust and capture it with ease.

11 Then his mindset will shift, and he will advance and act arrogantly, attributing his strength to his own god.

12 Are you not eternal, O LORD my God, my Holy One? We will not perish. O LORD, you have appointed them for judgment; and, O mighty God, you have established them for discipline.

13 Your eyes are too pure to look upon evil, and you cannot tolerate wrongdoing. So why do you watch those who act deceitfully and remain silent when the wicked destroy those who are more righteous than themselves?

14 And do you treat people like the fish of the sea, like crawling creatures that have no one to govern them?

15 They all are caught with the hook; they are ensnared in their net and gathered into their dragnet. Because of this, they rejoice and celebrate.

16 Therefore, they offer sacrifices to their nets and burn incense to their fishing gear, for by these tools their portion is abundant, and their food is plentiful.

17 Should they therefore empty their nets without restraint, continuing to slaughter the nations without mercy?
