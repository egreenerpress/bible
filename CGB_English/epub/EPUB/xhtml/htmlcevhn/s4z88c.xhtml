I John Chapter 4

1 Dear friends, do not automatically trust every spiritual influence, but carefully examine them to determine whether they come from God. For many false prophets have gone out into the world.

2 This is how you can recognize the Spirit of God: Every spirit that acknowledges that Jesus Christ has come in human form is from God.

3 Every spirit that does not acknowledge that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh is not from God. This is the spirit of the antichrist, which you have heard is coming, and is already present in the world.

4 You belong to God, dear children, and you have overcome them, because the one who is in you is greater than the one who is in the world.

5 They belong to the world; that is why they speak from the world's perspective, and the world listens to them.

6 We belong to God. Whoever knows God listens to us, but whoever does not belong to God does not listen to us. This is how we recognize the spirit of truth and the spirit of deception.

7 Dear friends, let us continue to love one another, because love comes from God. Everyone who loves has been born of God and knows God.

8 Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love.

9 This is how God's love for us was revealed: God sent his one and only Son into the world so that we might have life through him.

10 This is the essence of love: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son as the atoning sacrifice for our sins.

11 Dear friends, if God has loved us in this way, we also are obligated to love one another.

12 No one has ever seen God at any time. However, if we love one another, God remains in us, and His love is brought to its full expression within us.

13 This is how we know that we remain in him, and he in us: because he has given us a share of his Spirit.

14 We have witnessed and now declare that the Father sent the Son to be the Savior of the world.

15 Whoever acknowledges that Jesus is the Son of God, God lives in them, and they live in God.

16 We have come to know and firmly believe in the love that God has for us. God is love itself, and whoever lives in love lives in God, and God lives in them.

17 This is how love is brought to its full expression in us: so that we may have confidence on the day of judgment, because just as Christ is, so are we in this world.

18 There is no fear in love; rather, perfect love drives out fear, because fear involves punishment. The one who fears has not been fully perfected in love.

19 We love Him because He loved us first.

20 If someone claims, "I love God," yet harbors hatred toward their brother or sister, they are deceiving themselves. For if someone cannot love their brother or sister, whom they can see, how can they possibly love God, whom they have never seen?

21 This is the command we have received from Him: whoever loves God must also love their brother.
