Genesis Chapter 35

1 God said to Jacob, "Get up, go to Bethel, and settle there. Build an altar there to God, who appeared to you when you were fleeing from your brother Esau."

2 Then Jacob said to his household and to everyone with him, "Get rid of the foreign gods that are among you, purify yourselves, and change your clothes."

3 Let us get up and go to Bethel, where I will build an altar to God, who responded to me in the time of my trouble and has been with me on the journey I have taken.

4 They handed over to Jacob all the foreign gods they had in their possession, along with the earrings they were wearing. Jacob then took these items and buried them beneath the oak tree near Shechem.

5 They continued on their journey, and a profound fear of God came upon the cities surrounding them. Because of this, none of the cities dared to pursue or attack Jacob’s family.

6 So Jacob arrived at Luz, which is in the land of Canaan—that is, Bethel—he and all the people who were with him.

7 He built an altar there and named the place El-beth-el, because it was there that God had revealed Himself to him when he was fleeing from his brother.

8 But Deborah, Rebekah’s nurse, died and was buried below Bethel under an oak tree. The place was named Allon-bachuth, which means "Oak of Weeping."

9 And God appeared to Jacob once more after he had come out of Paddan-aram, and He blessed him.

10 God said to him, "Your name is Jacob, but you will no longer be called Jacob. Your name will now be Israel." So God named him Israel.

11 And God said to him, "I am God Almighty. Be fruitful and increase in number; a nation and a community of nations will come from you, and kings will be among your descendants."

12 The land that I gave to Abraham and Isaac, I will give to you, and I will also give it to your descendants after you.

13 And God departed from him at the place where He had spoken with him.

14 Jacob set up a stone pillar at the place where God had spoken with him. He poured out a drink offering on it and also anointed it with oil.

15 Jacob named the place where God spoke with him "Bethel."

16 They set out from Bethel, and when they were still some distance from Ephrath, Rachel went into labor, and her delivery was extremely difficult.

17 As she was enduring intense labor, the midwife said to her, "Do not be afraid; you will give birth to this son as well."

18 As she was nearing death—for she was dying—she named him Ben-oni, meaning "son of my sorrow." But his father called him Benjamin, meaning "son of the right hand."

19 Rachel died and was buried on the way to Ephrath, that is, Bethlehem.

20 Jacob set up a memorial stone on her grave; that stone remains at Rachel's burial site to this day.

21 And Israel continued his journey, pitching his tent beyond the tower of Eder.

22 It happened while Israel was living in that land that Reuben went and slept with Bilhah, his father’s concubine. Israel heard about it. At that time, Jacob had twelve sons.

23 The sons of Leah were Reuben, Jacob’s firstborn, along with Simeon, Levi, Judah, Issachar, and Zebulun.

24 The children of Rachel were Joseph and Benjamin.

25 The sons of Bilhah, Rachel’s servant, were Dan and Naphtali.

26 The sons of Zilpah, Leah’s servant, were Gad and Asher. These were the sons of Jacob born to him in Paddan-aram.

27 Jacob went to his father Isaac at Mamre, near the city of Arbah, which is Hebron, where Abraham and Isaac had lived as foreigners.

28 Isaac lived for one hundred and eighty years.

29 Isaac breathed his last and died, and he was reunited with his ancestors in death, having lived a long and full life. His sons, Esau and Jacob, buried him.
