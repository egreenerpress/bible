Exodus Chapter 40

1 The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2 On the first day of the first month, you shall erect the tabernacle, the tent of meeting.

3 You shall place the ark of the covenant inside it, and you shall shield the ark with the veil.

4 You shall bring in the table and arrange the items that are to be placed upon it in their proper order. Then you shall bring in the lampstand and light its lamps.

5 You shall place the golden incense altar in front of the ark of the covenant, and then hang the curtain at the entrance of the tabernacle.

6 You are to place the altar for burnt offerings in front of the entrance to the tabernacle, the tent of meeting.

7 You shall place the basin between the tent of meeting and the altar, and you shall put water in it.

8 You shall set up the courtyard all around it and hang the curtain at the entrance to the courtyard.

9 You shall take the anointing oil and anoint the tabernacle and everything in it. You shall consecrate it and all its furnishings, and it will be holy.

10 You shall anoint the altar of burnt offering and all its utensils, consecrating the altar. It will then become a most holy altar.

11 You shall anoint the basin and its stand, and consecrate them.

12 You are to bring Aaron and his sons to the entrance of the tent of meeting and wash them with water.

13 You shall clothe Aaron in the sacred garments, anoint him, and consecrate him, so that he may serve me as a priest.

14 You shall bring his sons and dress them in tunics.

15 You shall anoint them, just as you anointed their father, so that they may serve me in the priestly office. Their anointing will establish for them a permanent priesthood throughout all their generations.

16 Moses did exactly as the LORD had commanded him; he carried out everything according to the instructions he had received.

17 In the first month of the second year, on the first day of the month, the tabernacle was set up.

18 Moses set up the tabernacle, securing its bases in place. He erected its frames, inserted the crossbars, and raised its supporting pillars.

19 He then spread out the tent over the tabernacle and placed the covering of the tent on top of it, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

20 He took the covenant tablets and placed them inside the ark. Then he attached the carrying poles to the ark and set the mercy seat on top of it.

21 He brought the ark into the tabernacle and hung the veil as a partition, shielding the ark of the testimony, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

22 He placed the table inside the tent of meeting, on the north side of the tabernacle, outside the curtain.

23 He arranged the bread in proper order on the table before the LORD, just as the LORD had instructed Moses.

24 He placed the lampstand inside the tent of meeting, opposite the table, on the south side of the tabernacle.

25 And he lit the lamps in the presence of the LORD, just as the LORD had instructed Moses.

26 He placed the golden altar inside the tent of meeting, in front of the veil.

27 And he burned fragrant incense on it, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

28 And he hung the curtain at the entrance of the tabernacle.

29 He placed the altar for burnt offerings near the entrance of the tabernacle, the tent of meeting, and presented on it the burnt offering and the grain offering, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

30 He placed the basin between the tent of meeting and the altar, and filled it with water for washing.

31 Moses, Aaron, and his sons washed their hands and feet there.

32 Whenever they entered the tent of meeting or approached the altar, they would wash themselves, just as the LORD had commanded Moses.

33 He set up the surrounding courtyard around the tabernacle and the altar and hung the curtain for the entrance of the courtyard. In this way, Moses completed the work.

34 Then a cloud enveloped the tent of meeting, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle.

35 Moses was unable to enter the tent of meeting because the cloud had settled upon it, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle.

36 When the cloud lifted from above the tabernacle, the Israelites would set out on their journey, continuing to travel as long as the cloud moved.

37 However, if the cloud did not lift, they would not set out until the day it rose again.

38 For the cloud of the LORD rested over the tabernacle during the day, and fire appeared on it at night, visible to all the people of Israel throughout their entire journey.
