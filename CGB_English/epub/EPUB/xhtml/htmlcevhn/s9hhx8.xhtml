Psalms Chapter 69

1 (For the director of music, to be played on the "Shoshannim" instrument. A psalm of David.) Rescue me, O God, for the floodwaters have risen to my very soul.

2 I am sinking in deep mud, where there is no firm ground to stand on; I have entered deep waters, and the floodwaters overwhelm me.

3 I am exhausted from my weeping; my throat is parched, and my eyes grow weak as I wait for my God.

4 Those who hate me without reason are more numerous than the hairs on my head. My enemies, who seek to destroy me unjustly, are powerful. Yet I have restored what I did not steal.

5 O God, you are fully aware of my foolishness, and my sins are not concealed from you.

6 Do not let those who wait for you, O Lord GOD of hosts, be put to shame because of me; do not let those who seek you be disgraced because of me, O God of Israel.

7 Because of you, I have endured insults and disgrace; humiliation has overwhelmed me.

8 I have become like a foreigner to my own brothers, and a stranger to my mother's children.

9 Because my passion for your house consumes me, and the insults of those who insult you have fallen upon me.

10 When I wept and disciplined my soul through fasting, it became a source of shame for me.

11 I also made sackcloth my clothing, and I became an object of ridicule to them.

12 Those who gather at the city gate speak against me, and I have become the subject of mocking songs among those who are drunk.

13 As for me, I direct my prayer to you, O LORD, at a time when you are pleased to listen. O God, in the abundance of your steadfast love, answer me, and in the faithfulness of your saving power, hear my plea.

14 Rescue me from the mud, and do not let me sink; save me from those who hate me, and from the depths of the waters.

15 Do not let the floodwaters overwhelm me, nor allow the depths to engulf me. May the pit not close its mouth over me.

16 Listen to me, O LORD, for your steadfast love is good; turn to me according to the abundance of your compassionate mercies.

17 Do not turn your face away from your servant, for I am in distress; answer me quickly.

18 Come near to my soul and rescue it; save me because of my adversaries.

19 You are aware of my disgrace, my humiliation, and my dishonor; all those who oppose me are fully known to you.

20 The weight of disgrace has shattered my heart, and I am overwhelmed with sorrow. I searched for someone to show compassion, but there was no one; I sought comforters, but I found none.

21 They also offered me bitter herbs as my food, and when I was thirsty, they gave me sour wine to drink.

22 May their table become a snare before them, and what should have been for their well-being, may it turn into a trap.

23 May their eyes be darkened so they cannot see, and cause their strength to continually falter.

24 Release your fury upon them, and let your fierce anger overtake them.

25 May their dwelling place become deserted, and may no one live in their tents.

26 For they pursue the one you have struck down, and they add to the suffering of those you have wounded.

27 Increase their guilt by adding more sin to their wrongdoing, and do not allow them to enter into your righteousness.

28 May their names be erased from the book of life, and may they not be recorded among the righteous.

29 But I am afflicted and in distress; may your deliverance, O God, lift me up to safety.

30 I will sing praises to the name of God, and I will honor Him with songs of gratitude.

31 This will also be more pleasing to the LORD than the sacrifice of an ox or a bull with horns and hooves.

32 The meek will witness this and rejoice, and those who seek God will find their hearts revived.

33 For the LORD listens to the cries of the needy and does not disregard those who are bound in affliction.

34 Let the heavens and the earth praise him, the seas, and all that moves within them.

35 For God will deliver Zion and rebuild the cities of Judah, so that His people may settle there and claim it as their own.

36 The descendants of his servants will inherit the land, and those who cherish his name will live there.
