Isaiah Chapter 52

1 Awake, awake! Clothe yourself with strength, O Zion; put on your splendid garments, O Jerusalem, the holy city. For from now on, the uncircumcised and the unclean will no longer enter you.

2 Rise up from the dust; stand up and take your place, O Jerusalem. Free yourself from the chains around your neck, O captive daughter of Zion.

3 For this is what the LORD says: You have given yourselves up for nothing, and you will be set free without any payment.

4 For this is what the Lord GOD says: Long ago, my people went down to Egypt to live there as foreigners, but the Assyrians oppressed them without any just cause.

5 So now, what do I have here, declares the LORD, when my people are taken away without cause? Their rulers make them wail in anguish, says the LORD, and my name is constantly blasphemed every single day.

6 Therefore, my people will come to know my name; they will understand on that day that I am the one who declares these things. Behold, it is I who speaks.

7 How lovely on the mountains are the feet of the one who brings good news, who proclaims peace, who announces good things, who declares salvation, who says to Zion, "Your God reigns!"

8 Your watchmen will raise their voices; together they will sing aloud, for they will see clearly with their own eyes when the LORD restores Zion.

9 Shout for joy and sing aloud, you desolate places of Jerusalem! For the LORD has brought comfort to His people; He has redeemed Jerusalem.

10 The LORD has revealed His holy power before the eyes of all the nations, and all the farthest reaches of the earth will witness the salvation brought by our God.

11 Leave at once, leave at once! Get out from there and do not touch anything unclean. Come out from among them and purify yourselves, you who carry the sacred vessels of the LORD.

12 You will not leave in a hurried rush, nor will you flee in panic, for the LORD will march ahead of you, and the God of Israel will guard your rear.

13 Behold, my servant will act wisely; he will be lifted up, honored, and raised to great heights.
