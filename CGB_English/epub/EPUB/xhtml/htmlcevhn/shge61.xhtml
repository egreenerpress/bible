Ephesians Chapter 2

1 And He has made you alive, you who were dead in your transgressions and sins;

2 In the past, you lived according to the ways of this world, following the ruler of the domain of the air—the spirit now at work in those who are disobedient.

3 In the past, all of us lived among them, following the passions and desires of our sinful nature, indulging the cravings of our flesh and our thoughts. By our very nature, we were deserving of God’s wrath, just like everyone else.

4 But God, who is abundant in mercy, because of the immense love with which he loved us,

5 Even when we were spiritually dead because of our sins, God made us alive together with Christ—it is by His grace that you have been saved.

6 And He has raised us up together with Him and seated us together with Him in the heavenly realms in Christ Jesus.

7 This was done so that in the ages to come, He might demonstrate the immeasurable riches of His grace, expressed through His kindness toward us in Christ Jesus.

8 You are saved by grace through faith, and this is not something you have accomplished on your own—it is the gift of God.

9 This is not based on works, so that no one can boast.

10 For we are God’s handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to live out.

11 Therefore, remember that in the past you were Gentiles by birth, referred to as "the uncircumcised" by those who call themselves "the circumcised"—a physical mark made by human hands.

12 At that time, you were separated from Christ, excluded from the citizenship of Israel, and foreign to the covenants of promise. You were without hope and without God in the world.

13 But now, in Christ Jesus, you who were once distant have been brought near through the blood of Christ.

14 For he is our peace, the one who has united both groups into one and has dismantled the dividing wall of hostility between us.

15 By His physical sacrifice, He eliminated the hostility—the law with its commandments and regulations—so that He might create in Himself one new humanity from the two, thereby establishing peace.

16 And so that He might bring both groups into unity with God through one body by means of the cross, putting to death the hostility between them through His sacrifice.

17 He came and proclaimed peace to those who were far away and to those who were near.

18 For through him, both of us have access to the Father by means of one Spirit.

19 Now, you are no longer outsiders or foreigners, but you are fellow citizens with God’s holy people and members of God’s own household.

20 You are being built upon the foundation laid by the apostles and prophets, with Jesus Christ himself as the cornerstone.

21 In Him, the entire structure is joined together harmoniously and grows into a sacred temple in the Lord.

22 In Him, you also are being joined together to become a dwelling place for God through the Spirit.
