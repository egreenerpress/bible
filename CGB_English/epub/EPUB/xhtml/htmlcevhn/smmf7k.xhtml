Isaiah Chapter 25

1 O LORD, you are my God; I will honor you, I will give praise to your name; for you have accomplished extraordinary deeds; your plans from ancient times are steadfast and true.

2 You have turned a thriving city into a heap of rubble; a fortified city into a crumbling ruin. The palace of foreigners is no longer a cityâ€”it will never be rebuilt.

3 Therefore, the mighty nation will honor you, and the city of the powerful nations will stand in awe of you.

4 For you have been a source of strength to the poor, a stronghold for the needy in their distress, a shelter from the storm, a shade from the scorching heat, when the fury of the ruthless strikes like a tempest against a wall.

5 You will silence the clamor of foreign nations, like the scorching heat in a parched land. Even their oppressive power will be subdued, like the relief brought by the shade of a cloud. The pride of the ruthless will be humbled and brought low.

6 On this mountain, the LORD of hosts will prepare for all peoples a lavish banquet, a feast of rich foods, a feast of well-aged wines, of the finest meats and the choicest wines, carefully refined.

7 And on this mountain, He will remove the shroud that covers all peoples, the veil that envelops all nations.

8 He will completely destroy death forever; and the Lord GOD will remove every tear from all faces. He will take away the disgrace of his people from the entire earth, for the LORD has declared it.

9 And on that day, it will be proclaimed: "Behold, this is our God! We have waited for Him, and He will deliver us. This is the LORD; we have waited for Him. Let us rejoice and be glad in His salvation!"

10 For on this mountain the hand of the LORD will remain, and Moab will be crushed beneath Him, just as straw is trampled into the manure pile.

11 He will stretch out his hands in their midst, as a swimmer stretches out his hands to swim, and he will bring down their arrogance along with the plunder they have taken.

12 He will tear down the stronghold of your towering walls, bringing them low and leveling them to the ground, reducing them to dust.
