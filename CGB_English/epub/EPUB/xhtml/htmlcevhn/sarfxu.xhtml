Hebrews Chapter 7

1 This Melchizedek, king of Salem and priest of the Most High God, met Abraham as he was returning from defeating the kings and blessed him.

2 To Abraham, Melchizedek also received a tenth of everything. First, his name means "King of Righteousness," and then he is also the King of Salem, which means "King of Peace."

3 This one has no father, no mother, no genealogy, no beginning of days, nor end of life, but is made like the Son of God, and remains a priest forever.

4 Now reflect on how significant this man was, to whom even Abraham, the patriarch, gave a tenth of the spoils.

5 Indeed, those who belong to the tribe of Levi and are appointed to the priestly office have a command, based on the law, to collect tithes from the people—that is, from their fellow Israelites—even though they too are descendants of Abraham.

6 However, the one who does not trace his lineage from them received tithes from Abraham and blessed him who possessed the promises.

7 Without any doubt, the lesser is blessed by the greater.

8 Here on earth, mortal men receive tithes, but in the case of the one spoken of, it is testified that he lives, and it is he who receives them.

9 To put it another way, even Levi, who receives tithes, paid tithes through Abraham.

10 For he was still within his father’s lineage when Melchizedek encountered him.

11 If, then, perfection could have been achieved through the Levitical priesthood—since it was under this priesthood that the people received the law—what further need would there have been for another priest to arise, belonging to the order of Melchizedek, rather than being designated according to the order of Aaron?

12 Since the priesthood has been changed, it necessarily follows that there must also be a change in the law.

13 The one about whom these things are said belongs to a different tribe, and no one from that tribe has ever served at the altar.

14 It is clear that our Lord descended from Judah, a tribe about which Moses said nothing regarding the priesthood.

15 And this becomes even more evident when we consider that another priest arises in the likeness of Melchizedek,

16 He was appointed not according to the law of a physical, external commandment, but according to the power of an indestructible and eternal life.

17 For it is declared, "You are a priest forever, in the manner of Melchizedek."

18 Certainly, the earlier commandment is set aside because it was weak and ineffective.

19 The law was unable to bring anything to perfection, but the introduction of a better hope has accomplished this; through this hope, we are able to draw near to God.

20 And since he was made a priest not without an oath—

21 The former priests were established without an oath, but this one was appointed with an oath by God, who said to him: "The Lord has sworn and will not change his mind: You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek."

22 Jesus has become the guarantee of a superior covenant.

23 There were indeed many priests, because they were prevented from continuing in office due to death.

24 But this man, because he remains forever, holds a priesthood that is permanent and unchanging.

25 Therefore, he is fully capable of saving completely those who approach God through him, because he always lives to plead on their behalf.

26 We needed a high priest like this—one who is holy, blameless, pure, set apart from sinners, and exalted above the heavens.

27 Unlike those high priests, who need to offer sacrifices daily—first for their own sins and then for the sins of the people—this one did not require such repeated offerings. He accomplished this once and for all when he offered up himself.

28 The law appoints as high priests those who are weak and subject to human limitations. But the word of the oath, which came after the law, appoints the Son, who has been perfected and set apart forever.
