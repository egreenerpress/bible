II Thessalonians Chapter 2

1 We urge you, brothers and sisters, by the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ and by our gathering together to meet him,

2 Do not let your minds be quickly unsettled or alarmed, whether by a spiritual message, a spoken word, or a letter supposedly from us, claiming that the day of the Lord has already come.

3 Do not let anyone deceive you in any way, for that day will not come until the rebellion occurs first, and the man of lawlessness is revealed, the one destined for destruction.

4 This one opposes and exalts himself above everything that is called God or is worshiped, so that he takes his seat in the temple of God, presenting himself as though he were God.

5 Do you not remember that when I was still with you, I told you about these things?

6 And now you understand what is restraining him, so that he may be revealed at the appointed time.

7 The hidden power of lawlessness is already at work, but the one who is currently restraining it will continue to do so until he is removed from the scene.

8 Then the lawless one will be revealed, whom the Lord Jesus will overthrow with the breath of his mouth and annihilate by the splendor of his coming.

9 This refers to the one whose arrival is driven by Satan's influence, accompanied by all kinds of deceptive power, signs, and false miracles.

10 And with every kind of deceitful wickedness among those who are perishing, because they refused to embrace the love of the truth that would have led to their salvation.

11 For this reason, God will send them a powerful delusion, so that they will believe what is false.

12 This is so that all who did not believe the truth but instead took delight in wickedness may be condemned.

13 We are always obligated to give thanks to God for you, brothers and sisters dearly loved by the Lord, because God has chosen you from the very beginning for salvation through the sanctifying work of the Spirit and through your faith in the truth.

14 He called you to this purpose through the message we proclaimed, so that you might share in the glory of our Lord Jesus Christ.

15 Therefore, brothers and sisters, stand firm and hold tightly to the teachings you have received, whether through spoken instruction or through our letter.

16 Now may our Lord Jesus Christ himself, and God our Father, who has loved us and given us eternal encouragement and good hope through his grace,

17 Strengthen and encourage your hearts, and firmly establish you in every good word and deed.
