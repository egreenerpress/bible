Song of Songs Chapter 1

1 The most exquisite of songs, composed by Solomon.

2 Let him kiss me with the kisses of his mouth, for your love is more delightful than wine.

3 Because of the fragrance of your fine perfumes, your name is like a precious oil poured out; this is why young women adore you.

4 Draw me close, and we will eagerly follow you. The king has brought me into his private chambers. We will be filled with joy and delight in you; we will cherish your love more than the finest wine. Those who are sincere and upright truly love you.

5 I am dark-skinned, yet beautiful, O daughters of Jerusalem, like the tents of Kedar, like the splendid curtains of Solomon.

6 Do not look down on me because I am dark, for the sun has tanned my skin. My brothers were angry with me; they made me tend the vineyards, but I could not care for my own.

7 Tell me, you whom I deeply love, where do you graze your flock, where do you let them rest at midday? For why should I be like one who wanders aimlessly among the flocks of your companions?

8 If you do not know, O most beautiful of women, go out and follow the tracks of the flock, and graze your young goats near the shepherdsâ€™ tents.

9 I have likened you, my beloved, to a team of horses harnessed to Pharaoh's chariots.

10 Your cheeks are lovely, adorned with rows of ornaments, and your neck is beautiful with chains of gold.

11 We will adorn you with golden ornaments, embellished with studs of silver.

12 While the king is seated at his banquet table, my spikenard releases its fragrance.

13 My beloved is to me like a bundle of myrrh, resting close to my heart throughout the night.

14 My beloved is to me like a cluster of henna blossoms in the vineyards of En-gedi.

15 Look, you are beautiful, my beloved; truly, you are beautiful. Your eyes are like those of a dove.

16 Look, you are beautiful, my love, truly delightful; and our resting place is lush and flourishing.

17 The timbers of our house are made of cedar, and our rafters are crafted from fir.
