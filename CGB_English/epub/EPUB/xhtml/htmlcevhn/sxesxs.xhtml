James Chapter 4

1 Where do conflicts and quarrels among you come from? Do they not arise from your own desires that wage war within you?

2 You desire and do not have; you kill and covet, yet you cannot obtain what you seek. You quarrel and wage war, but you still do not have, because you do not ask.

3 You ask, yet you do not receive, because you ask with wrong motives, intending to spend what you seek on your own selfish desires.

4 You who engage in unfaithfulness and spiritual adultery, do you not understand that aligning yourselves with the ways of the world puts you in opposition to God? Therefore, anyone who chooses to be a friend of the world makes themselves an enemy of God.

5 Do you believe that the Scripture speaks without purpose when it says, "The spirit that resides within us desires with envy"?

6 But God gives even greater grace. Therefore, it is said, "God opposes the proud but shows favor to the humble."

7 Therefore, place yourselves under God's authority. Stand firm against the devil, and he will retreat from you.

8 Come close to God, and he will come close to you. Wash your hands, you who are sinful, and purify your hearts, you who are divided in your thoughts and loyalties.

9 Humble yourselves in sorrow, and grieve deeply; let your laughter be turned into mourning, and your joy into gloom.

10 Submit yourselves humbly before the Lord, and he will exalt you.

11 Do not speak harshly or critically about one another, my fellow believers. Whoever speaks ill of a brother or sister and passes judgment on them is, in fact, speaking against the law and judging the law itself. But if you presume to judge the law, you are no longer living as one who follows it; instead, you have set yourself up as its judge.

12 There is only one Lawgiver, who has the power to save and to destroy. So who are you to judge someone else?

13 Listen now, you who say, "Today or tomorrow we will go to this or that city, stay there for a year, conduct business, and make a profit."

14 You do not know what will happen tomorrow. For what is your life? It is like a mist that appears for a short time and then vanishes.

15 You should instead say, "If the Lord wills, we will live and do this or that."

16 But now you take pride in your arrogant claims; all such boasting is wrong.

17 Therefore, for anyone who knows what is right to do and fails to do it, for that person it is sin.
