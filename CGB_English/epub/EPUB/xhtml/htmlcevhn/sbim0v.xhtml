Judges Chapter 17

1 There was a man from the hill country of Ephraim whose name was Micah.

2 He said to his mother, "The eleven hundred shekels of silver that were taken from you, the ones you cursed and spoke about in my hearing—look, the silver is with me; I took it." His mother replied, "May the LORD bless you, my son."

3 When he returned the eleven hundred shekels of silver to his mother, she said, "I had completely dedicated this silver to the LORD from my own hand for my son, to make a carved image and a metal idol. But now, I will give it back to you."

4 He returned the silver to his mother, and she took two hundred shekels of silver and gave them to a silversmith. The silversmith used the silver to craft a carved image and a metal idol, which were then placed in Micah’s house.

5 The man Micah had a shrine for his gods. He made a sacred garment and household idols, and he appointed one of his sons to serve as his priest.

6 In those days, Israel had no king, and each person did whatever they considered right in their own eyes.

7 There was a young man from Bethlehem in Judah, from the tribe of Judah, who was a Levite. He was staying there as a temporary resident.

8 The man left the city of Bethlehem in Judah, intending to stay wherever he could find a place. As he traveled, he came to the hill country of Ephraim and arrived at the house of Micah.

9 Micah asked him, "Where do you come from?" He replied, "I am a Levite from Bethlehem in Judah, and I am traveling to find a place where I can stay."

10 Micah said to him, "Stay with me and be a father and priest to me, and I will give you ten shekels of silver per year, a set of clothing, and your food." So the Levite agreed and stayed with him.

11 The Levite agreed to stay with the man, and the young man became like a son to him.

12 Micah appointed the Levite to serve as his priest, and the young man became his priest and remained in Micah's household.

13 Then Micah said, "Now I know that the LORD will bless me, because I have a Levite serving as my priest."
