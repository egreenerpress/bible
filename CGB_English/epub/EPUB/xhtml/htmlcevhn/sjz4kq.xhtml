Matthew Chapter 5

1 When Jesus saw the crowds, he went up on a mountainside. After he sat down, his disciples came to him.

2 Then Jesus began to teach them, saying,

3 Blessed are those who recognize their spiritual poverty and dependence on God, for the kingdom of heaven belongs to them.

4 Blessed are those who grieve, for they will receive comfort.

5 Blessed are those who are humble and gentle, for they will receive the earth as their inheritance.

6 Blessed are those who deeply desire and earnestly seek righteousness, for they will be fully satisfied.

7 Blessed are those who show compassion, for they will receive compassion in return.

8 Blessed are those whose hearts are pure, for they will behold God.

9 Blessed are those who work to bring peace, for they will be recognized as God’s own children.

10 Blessed are those who are persecuted for the sake of righteousness, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.

11 You are blessed when people insult you, persecute you, and falsely say all kinds of evil things about you because of your commitment to me.

12 Be joyful and exceedingly glad, for your reward in heaven is great. In the same way, they persecuted the prophets who came before you.

13 You are the essential seasoning for the world, but if the salt loses its distinctive flavor, how can it be made salty again? It becomes completely useless, fit only to be thrown out and trampled underfoot by people.

14 You are the light of the world. A city built on a hill cannot be hidden.

15 No one lights a lamp and then hides it under a basket. Instead, they place it on a stand so that it gives light to everyone in the house.

16 Let your light shine brightly before others, so that they may see your good deeds and give praise to your Father in heaven.

17 Do not think that I have come to abolish the Law or the Prophets. I have not come to abolish them, but to fulfill them.

18 Truly, I tell you, until heaven and earth pass away, not even the smallest letter or the tiniest stroke of a letter will disappear from the Law until everything is accomplished.

19 Therefore, whoever disregards even the smallest of these commandments and teaches others to do the same will be considered the least in the kingdom of heaven. But whoever practices and teaches these commandments will be called great in the kingdom of heaven.

20 For I tell you this: Unless your righteousness surpasses that of the scribes and Pharisees, you will certainly not enter the kingdom of heaven.

21 You have heard that it was said to those of ancient times, "You shall not murder," and whoever commits murder will be subject to judgment.

22 But I tell you this: Anyone who becomes angry with their brother or sister without just cause will be subject to judgment. And whoever insults their brother or sister by calling them "Raca" (an Aramaic term of contempt) will be answerable to the council. But whoever calls someone "You fool!" will be in danger of the fire of hell.

23 Therefore, when you bring your offering to the altar and remember that your brother or sister has something against you,

24 Leave your offering there at the altar, and go your way. First, make peace with your brother, and then return to present your offering.

25 Settle your dispute with your adversary quickly, while you are still on the way with them. Otherwise, your adversary may hand you over to the judge, and the judge may turn you over to the officer, and you may be thrown into prison.

26 Truly, I tell you, you will certainly not be released from there until you have paid the last penny.

27 You have heard that it was said by those of ancient times, "You shall not commit adultery."

28 But I tell you this: Anyone who looks at a woman with lustful intent has already committed adultery with her in his heart.

29 If your right eye causes you to sin, tear it out and throw it away. It is better for you to lose one part of your body than for your whole body to be thrown into hell.

30 If your right hand causes you to sin, cut it off and throw it away. It is better for you to lose one part of your body than for your entire body to be thrown into hell.

31 It has been said, "Whoever divorces his wife must give her a formal certificate of divorce."

32 But I tell you this: Anyone who divorces his wife, except on the grounds of sexual immorality, causes her to become an adulteress, and whoever marries a divorced woman commits adultery.

33 You have also heard that it was said to the people long ago, "Do not break your oath, but fulfill to the Lord the vows you have made."

34 I tell you, do not swear oaths at all—not by heaven, because it is God’s throne;

35 Do not swear by the earth, for it is merely the footstool beneath His feet; nor by Jerusalem, for it is the city of the great King.

36 You must not swear by your head, for you cannot make even a single hair white or black.

37 Let your words be straightforward: simply say 'Yes' or 'No.' Anything beyond this comes from evil.

38 You have heard it said, "An eye for an eye, and a tooth for a tooth."

39 But I tell you this: Do not resist an evil person. If someone strikes you on the right cheek, turn to them the other cheek as well.

40 If someone takes you to court to sue you and tries to take your tunic, let them have your cloak as well.

41 If anyone forces you to go one mile, go with them two miles.

42 Give to anyone who asks of you, and do not turn away from someone who wishes to borrow from you.

43 You have heard it said, "You shall love your neighbor and hate your enemy."

44 But I tell you this: Love your enemies, speak well of those who insult you, do good to those who hate you, and pray for those who mistreat you and pursue you with harm.

45 This is so that you may truly be children of your Father in heaven. For He causes the sun to rise on both the evil and the good, and sends rain on the righteous and the unrighteous alike.

46 If you love only those who love you, what reward do you deserve? Even the tax collectors do the same, don’t they?

47 And if you greet only your own friends and family, how are you doing anything extraordinary? Don’t even those who don’t follow God’s ways do the same?

48 Therefore, strive to be complete and whole in your character, just as your heavenly Father is complete and perfect in His nature.
