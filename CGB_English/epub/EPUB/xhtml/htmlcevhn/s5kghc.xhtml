Luke Chapter 1

1 Since many have undertaken to compile an orderly account of the events that have been firmly established among us,

2 Just as they handed down to us the accounts from those who were eyewitnesses from the very beginning and became servants of the message;

3 It also seemed fitting to me, since I have carefully investigated everything from the beginning, to write an orderly account for you, most honorable Theophilus.

4 So that you may have a firm understanding and assurance of the truths you have been taught.

5 During the reign of Herod, king of Judea, there was a priest named Zechariah, who belonged to the division of Abijah. His wife, Elizabeth, was also a descendant of Aaron.

6 Both of them were upright and righteous in God’s sight, living in obedience to all the Lord’s commands and decrees without fault.

7 They had no children because Elizabeth was unable to conceive, and both of them were now advanced in age.

8 And it happened that while he was performing his priestly duties before God in the assigned order of his division,

9 In keeping with the established practice of the priestly duties, it fell to him by lot to offer incense when he entered the temple of the Lord.

10 And the entire crowd of people was praying outside during the time when the incense was being offered.

11 Then an angel of the Lord appeared to him, standing at the right side of the altar of incense.

12 When Zechariah saw him, he was deeply disturbed, and fear overwhelmed him.

13 But the angel said to him, "Do not be afraid, Zechariah, for your prayer has been heard. Your wife Elizabeth will bear you a son, and you are to name him John."

14 You will experience great joy and happiness, and many people will celebrate with you at his birth.

15 For he will be highly esteemed by the Lord and will not drink wine or any alcoholic beverage. He will be filled with the Holy Spirit even from the time of his birth.

16 And he will lead many of the people of Israel back to the Lord their God.

17 He will go before the Lord in the spirit and power of Elijah, to turn the hearts of parents toward their children, and to lead the disobedient to embrace the wisdom of the righteous. His purpose will be to prepare a people who are ready for the Lord.

18 Zacharias said to the angel, "How can I be sure of this? For I am an old man, and my wife is advanced in years."

19 The angel replied to him, saying, "I am Gabriel, who stands in the presence of God. I have been sent to speak to you and to bring you this good news."

20 Listen carefully: you will become mute and unable to speak until the day these things take place, because you did not believe my words, which will be fulfilled at the appointed time.

21 The people waited for Zechariah and were astonished that he remained in the temple for such a long time.

22 When he came out, he was unable to speak to them, and they realized that he had seen a vision in the temple. He motioned to them but remained unable to speak.

23 When the time of his priestly service was completed, he returned to his home.

24 After those days, his wife Elizabeth became pregnant and secluded herself for five months, saying,

25 This is how the Lord has treated me during the time when He showed me favor, removing the disgrace I bore among people.

26 In the sixth month, the angel Gabriel was sent by God to a town in Galilee called Nazareth.

27 To a young woman who was pledged in marriage to a man named Joseph, of the lineage of David; and the young woman’s name was Mary.

28 The angel entered her presence and said, "Greetings, you who are greatly favored! The Lord is with you. You are blessed among women."

29 When she saw him, she was deeply disturbed by his words and began to ponder what kind of greeting this could be.

30 The angel said to her, "Do not be afraid, Mary, for you have been shown grace by God."

31 Listen carefully: You will become pregnant and give birth to a son, and you are to name him Jesus.

32 He will be great and will be called the Son of the Most High. The Lord God will give him the throne of his ancestor David.

33 He will rule over the house of Jacob forever, and his kingdom will never come to an end.

34 Then Mary said to the angel, "How can this happen, since I have not been intimate with a man?"

35 The angel replied to her, "The Holy Spirit will come upon you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you. Therefore, the holy one to be born from you will be called the Son of God."

36 And look, your relative Elizabeth has also conceived a son in her old age. This is now the sixth month for her, even though she was once considered unable to have children.

37 For with God, no thing is beyond His power.

38 Mary responded, "I am the Lord’s servant. May it happen to me just as you have said." Then the angel left her.

39 Mary got up during that time and hurried to the hill country, to a town in Judah.

40 She went into Zechariah's house and greeted Elizabeth.

41 When Elizabeth heard Mary’s greeting, the baby in her womb leaped for joy, and Elizabeth was filled with the Holy Spirit.

42 She exclaimed with a loud voice, saying, "You are most blessed among women, and blessed is the child you will bear."

43 How is it that I am so honored, that the mother of my Lord should come to me?

44 For behold, as soon as the sound of your greeting reached my ears, the baby in my womb leaped with joy.

45 And blessed is she who has believed, for there will be a fulfillment of what was spoken to her by the Lord.

46 And Mary said, "My soul glorifies the Lord."

47 And my spirit has found great joy in God, my Savior.

48 For He has looked with favor on the humble state of His servant. Indeed, from now on, all generations will consider me blessed.

49 For the Almighty has done great things for me, and his name is holy.

50 And His steadfast love extends to those who revere Him, from one generation to the next.

51 He has demonstrated His power with His mighty arm; He has dispersed those who are arrogant in the thoughts of their hearts.

52 He has brought down the powerful from their thrones and lifted up the humble.

53 He has satisfied the hungry with abundant blessings, but the wealthy he has sent away with nothing.

54 He has come to the aid of his servant Israel, mindful of his steadfast love and mercy.

55 As He promised to our ancestors, to Abraham and to his descendants forever.

56 Mary stayed with her for about three months and then returned to her own home.

57 When the time came for Elizabeth to give birth, she delivered a son.

58 Her neighbors and relatives heard how the Lord had shown her great mercy, and they celebrated with her in joy.

59 On the eighth day, they came to circumcise the child, and they intended to name him Zechariah, after his father.

60 His mother responded, saying, "No, he will not be called that; his name will be John."

61 They said to her, "There is no one among your relatives who is called by this name."

62 They gestured to his father, asking what he wanted to name the child.

63 He requested a writing tablet and wrote, "His name is John." Everyone was astonished.

64 At that very moment, his mouth was opened, and his tongue was freed. He began to speak, praising God.

65 A sense of awe came upon all who lived in the surrounding areas, and all these events were widely talked about throughout the entire hill country of Judea.

66 All who heard these things pondered them deeply in their hearts, asking, "What kind of child will this be?" And the power of the Lord was upon him.

67 And his father Zechariah was filled with the Holy Spirit and prophesied, saying,

68 Praise be to the Lord, the God of Israel, because He has come to His people and brought them deliverance.

69 And He has raised up a mighty deliverer for us from the lineage of His servant David.

70 As he declared through the words of his holy prophets, who have spoken since the beginning of time:

71 So that we might be rescued from our adversaries and delivered from the power of all who oppose us.

72 To fulfill the compassionate promises made to our ancestors and to honor his sacred covenant.

73 The promise that He made with an oath to our father Abraham.

74 He would grant to us that, having been rescued from the power of our enemies, we might serve Him without fear.

75 In purity and uprightness before Him, throughout all the days of our lives.

76 And you, child, will be called the prophet of the Most High, for you will go before the Lord to prepare the way for him.

77 To impart the understanding of deliverance to His people through the forgiveness of their sins.

78 Through the compassionate mercy of our God, the dawn from heaven has come to visit us.

79 To bring light to those who dwell in darkness and in the shadow of death, and to direct our steps onto the path of peace.

80 The child grew and became strong in spirit, and he lived in the wilderness until the time came for him to be revealed to Israel.
