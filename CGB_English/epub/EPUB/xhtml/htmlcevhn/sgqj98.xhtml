II Corinthians Chapter 4

1 Therefore, since we have been entrusted with this ministry, and because we have received mercy, we do not lose heart.

2 We have rejected all shameful and underhanded ways, refusing to act with deceit or to manipulate the word of God. Instead, by openly declaring the truth, we commend ourselves to the conscience of every person in the presence of God.

3 But if our message of the gospel is concealed, it is concealed from those who are perishing.

4 In this case, the god of this age has blinded the minds of those who do not believe, so that the light of the glorious gospel of Christ, who is the image of God, cannot shine on them.

5 We do not proclaim ourselves, but Christ Jesus as Lord; and we are your servants for the sake of Jesus.

6 For it is God, who said, "Let light shine out of darkness," who has caused his light to shine within our hearts. This light brings us the knowledge of Godâ€™s glory, revealed in the face of Jesus Christ.

7 We hold this precious treasure in fragile, earthly containers, so that the surpassing greatness of the power may clearly be seen as coming from God and not from us.

8 We face challenges on all sides, yet we are not overwhelmed; we are uncertain, but we do not lose hope.

9 We are oppressed, yet not abandoned; struck down, yet not annihilated.

10 We continually carry within our bodies the reality of Jesus' death, so that the life of Jesus may also be visibly revealed in our mortal bodies.

11 For we who are alive are constantly being handed over to death for the sake of Jesus, so that the life of Jesus may also be revealed in our mortal bodies.

12 Therefore, death is at work within us, but life is at work within you.

13 We possess the same spirit of faith, as it is written: "I believed, and so I spoke." In the same way, we also believe, and therefore we speak.

14 We are confident that the one who raised the Lord Jesus from the dead will also raise us with Jesus and bring us into his presence together with you.

15 Everything is done for your benefit, so that the overflowing grace, as it is acknowledged and thanked by many, may bring greater glory to God.

16 Therefore, we do not lose heart. Even though our physical body is wasting away, our inner self is being renewed day by day.

17 Our present troubles, though they may seem significant, are actually brief and light compared to the immense and everlasting glory they are preparing for us.

18 We do not focus on what is visible, but on what is invisible. For what is seen is temporary, but what is unseen is eternal.
