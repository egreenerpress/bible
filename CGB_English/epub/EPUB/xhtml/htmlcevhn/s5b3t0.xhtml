I Thessalonians Chapter 5

1 Concerning the specific times and seasons, my brothers and sisters, there is no need for me to write to you about these matters.

2 You are fully aware that the day of the Lord will come unexpectedly, like a thief in the night.

3 For when people declare, "There is peace and security," then sudden destruction will come upon them, like the pains of labor upon a pregnant woman, and they will not escape.

4 But you, brothers and sisters, are not living in spiritual darkness, so that day will not surprise you like a thief.

5 You are all people of light and children of the day. We do not belong to the night or to darkness.

6 Therefore, let us not be spiritually unaware, as others are; instead, let us stay alert and remain self-controlled.

7 For those who sleep, do so at night; and those who get drunk, become drunk at night.

8 But as for us, who belong to the day, let us remain clear-minded and self-controlled, clothing ourselves with the breastplate of faith and love, and wearing as our helmet the confident hope of salvation.

9 For God has not destined us to experience His wrath, but to receive salvation through our Lord Jesus Christ.

10 He died for us so that, whether we are awake or asleep, we may live together with him.

11 Therefore, encourage and strengthen one another, just as you are already doing.

12 We urge you, brothers and sisters, to recognize and appreciate those who work diligently among you, who lead and guide you in the Lord, and who offer you instruction and correction.

13 Hold them in the highest regard and show them deep affection because of the work they do. Live in harmony with one another.

14 Now we urge you, brothers and sisters, to admonish those who are disorderly, encourage the timid, help the weak, and be patient with everyone.

15 Make sure that no one repays evil with evil to anyone. Instead, always pursue what is good, both among yourselves and toward all people.

16 Be joyful at all times.

17 Pray continually, without interruption or cessation.

18 In all circumstances, give thanks, for this is God’s will for you in Christ Jesus.

19 "Do not suppress or stifle the work of the Spirit."

20 Do not disregard or look down upon the messages of prophecy.

21 Examine everything carefully; hold firmly to what is good.

22 Avoid every form or semblance of wrongdoing.

23 May the God of peace himself sanctify you completely, and may your entire being—spirit, soul, and body—be kept blameless at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ.

24 The one who calls you is trustworthy, and he will surely accomplish it.

25 Brothers and sisters, please pray for us.

26 Greet all the fellow believers with a sacred gesture of affection.

27 I solemnly urge you by the authority of the Lord that this letter be read to all the holy brothers and sisters.

28 May the gracious favor of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you. Amen.
